NASA's Mars Rover Finds Strongest Hints of Ancient Life Signs

2025-09-10 22:48:54 EDT

NASA's Mars rover Perseverance has discovered rocks in a dry river channel that may contain the strongest hints yet of ancient microscopic life, scientists reported. While direct detection of life is not possible for the rover, the collected samples from the Neretva Vallis mudstones show organic carbon and minuscule specks enriched with iron phosphate and iron sulfide. On Earth, these compounds are byproducts of microorganisms consuming organic matter. Although non-biological processes could also be responsible, researchers consider this the most compelling candidate for long-ago life found so far, awaiting in-depth analysis on Earth, a mission currently facing cost and timeline challenges.

China Unveils Record-Breaking Cable-Stayed Mega Bridge Over Yangtze River

2025-09-10 22:00:12 EDT

China has opened the world's longest cable-stayed bridge, the Changtai Yangtze River Bridge, in Jiangsu province, significantly reducing travel time between Changzhou and Taizhou. Stretching 10.3 kilometers with a main span of 1,208 meters, this mega bridge is the first over the Yangtze to integrate an expressway, regular road, and intercity railway on a single structure. A distinctive feature is its asymmetrical lower deck, accommodating a 200km/h railway on one side and a regular road on the other, a pioneering side-by-side traffic layout for a large-span bridge. This engineering feat highlights China's advanced infrastructure capabilities.
