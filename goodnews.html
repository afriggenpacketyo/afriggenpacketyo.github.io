North America Anticipates Spectacular Aurora Borealis Display This Weekend

2026-05-16 14:07:59 EDT

Stargazers across the northern United States and much of Canada are poised to witness a spectacular display of the aurora borealis, also known as the Northern Lights, this Saturday and Sunday night. This dazzling natural phenomenon occurs as energized particles from space violently collide with atoms of gas in Earth's atmosphere, close to its magnetic pole, creating brilliant splashes of green, purple, and pink light. The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) predicts the geomagnetic storm causing this aurora will be strongest on Saturday night, offering the best viewing opportunities from where the aurora is directly overhead or up to 1000 km away. This rare treat for those not living near the poles is most commonly observed in March and November.
