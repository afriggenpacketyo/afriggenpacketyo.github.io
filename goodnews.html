Dirty Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly, Experts Warn

2025-06-10 14:34:21 EDT

Hummingbird feeders, while intended to help these birds, can be harmful or even deadly if not properly maintained. Sugar water left out too long can spoil and attract harmful microbes, leading to infections like candida, which can swell a hummingbird's tongue and make it difficult to eat. Experts recommend cleaning feeders every two to three days in spring and fall, and daily or every other day in the summer. The solution is to mix one part refined white sugar with four parts water, and to regularly clean the feeder with hot water or a vinegar and water mixture.
