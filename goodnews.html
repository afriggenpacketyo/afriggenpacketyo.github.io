Zambia's Ancient Writing System Almost Erased by Colonialism

2025-06-07 19:41:47 EDT

Sona, or Tusona, is an ancient and sophisticated writing system from Zambia that was almost erased by colonialism. This system comes from the Chokwe, Luchazi and Luvale people, and it includes geometric patterns made in the sand, on cloth, and on people's bodies. These patterns and symbols carry mathematical principles, references to the cosmos, and instructions on community life. The original custodians and teachers of Sona were women, and there are still community elders alive who remember how it works.
