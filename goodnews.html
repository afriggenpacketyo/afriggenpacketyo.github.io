2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

An article explains that homeowners who take out a reverse mortgage retain full legal ownership and title of their property. The lender places a lien on the home to secure the loan, but does not take ownership. This financial tool allows seniors aged 62 and older to convert home equity into cash without making monthly payments. The loan balance, including interest and fees, becomes due when the borrower sells the home, moves out, or passes away, at which point the heirs can choose to repay the loan to keep the house or sell it.
