Viral Photos of Horned 'Zombie Rabbits' in Colorado Explained by Wildlife Officials

2025-08-13 20:33:00 EDT

Photos of rabbits with black, horn-like growths circulating online have been confirmed as real by Colorado Parks and Wildlife. The animals are not 'zombie-rabbits' but are suffering from Shope Papilloma Virus, a relatively common and typically harmless disease spread by ticks, fleas, and rabbit-to-rabbit contact. The virus causes keratinous carcinomas to grow on the rabbits' bodies, which usually disappear as their immune system fights off the infection. This disease, which does not affect humans or other pets, is believed to be the origin of the mythical 'jackalope' legend.
