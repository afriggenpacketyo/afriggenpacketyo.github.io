2025-05-19 22:21:00 EDT

The Japanese government is set to commence offshore "blue carbon" research, focusing on capturing carbon dioxide through cultivated sea plants and storing it in the deep sea, with the goal of accelerating decarbonization. The Environment Ministry will collaborate with the Japan Agency for Marine-Earth Science and Technology and Eneos Corp. to study seaweed behavior at significant depths and assess the environmental impact of carbon capture and storage projects. Japan aims to capture 1 million tons of CO2 by fiscal 2035 and 2 million tons by fiscal 2040 through blue carbon initiatives, enhancing its carbon capture efforts to achieve net zero emissions by 2050. This initiative is crucial given the declining CO2 absorption capacity of Japan's aging forests.
