Atmospheric Conditions Prevented Any Atlantic Hurricanes from Making U.S. Landfall This Year

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

For the first time in a decade, the 2025 Atlantic hurricane season is concluding without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States. Meteorologists attribute this unusual phenomenon to a persistent weather pattern known as "recurvature," where storms veered northward and out to sea before reaching the coast. A recurring dip in the jet stream created an area of low pressure over the eastern U.S., which weakened the Bermuda High pressure system that typically steers hurricanes westward. This atmospheric setup repeatedly deflected the 13 named storms of the season away from the U.S. coastline.
