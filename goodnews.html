Key Gaps in Original Medicare Coverage and Supplemental Options

2025-09-10 11:02:53 EDT

Original Medicare, the cornerstone of health insurance for over 68 million retired Americans, contains notable gaps in coverage that often lead to unexpected out-of-pocket expenses. Key services generally not covered include routine dental care, vision and hearing exams (and aids), most outpatient prescription drugs, long-term care like nursing home stays, and healthcare received outside the United States. This forces nearly 90% of Medicare beneficiaries to seek additional coverage. To bridge these gaps, individuals can explore Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans, which often combine hospital, medical, and prescription drug coverage with added benefits, or Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plans, designed to cover deductibles, copayments, and coinsurance. Proactive planning during enrollment periods is essential to ensure comprehensive healthcare protection and avoid financial surprises.
