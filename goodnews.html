HSAs offer a triple tax benefit: pre-tax contributions, untaxed growth, and tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses. While assets are highest in the 60-64 age group, many retirees lack a clear plan for effective use. Notably, there's no statute of limitations on reimbursing past medical expenses with receipts, offering flexibility for tax planning in retirement. However, contributions must cease six months before Medicare enrollment, even if only for Part A. Funds can cover Medicare Part B, Part D, and Advantage Plan premiums, but not Medigap. At 65, non-medical withdrawals are permitted but become taxable, without the 20% penalty applied to younger withdrawals.
