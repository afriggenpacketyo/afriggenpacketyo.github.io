2025-12-14 19:50:29 EST

While the official Met Office definition only requires a single flake of snow at any UK weather station, local snowfall on Christmas Day remains uncommon. Historically, Scotland records the highest number of white Christmases, whereas Northern Ireland has experienced the fewest. This observed decline is largely attributed to climate change, which is leading to warmer and wetter UK winters, suggesting that future white Christmases will become even less probable, though natural weather variability will still bring occasional snowy periods.
