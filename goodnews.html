Brain's Map of Lost Limb Remains Intact, Challenging Prior Beliefs

2025-08-27 06:00:00 EDT

A new study published in Nature Neuroscience challenges previous beliefs about brain reorganization after limb amputation, suggesting that the brain's map of a lost limb remains largely intact. Researchers, using MRI scans before and after amputations, found that the "phantom hand representation" in the somatosensory cortex was similar to the pre-amputated state, even years later. This finding contradicts the idea that neighboring brain areas expand to fill the void and offers significant hope for brain-computer interfaces, which rely on stable neural circuits for controlling prosthetic limbs. The research also proposes that phantom limb pain may stem from the brain still expecting signals from the missing body part, rather than brain map changes.
