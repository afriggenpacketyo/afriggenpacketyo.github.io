2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has become the first to directly observe and measure quantum friction between two solid surfaces. Using folded graphene, they discovered a rare effect where friction did not increase with thickness; in some cases, thicker folds actually slid more easily. This counterintuitive phenomenon was traced to how bending the material altered electron movement, locking them into fixed energy levels and reducing the conversion of motion into heat. The findings could pave the way for creating ultra-efficient, low-wear nanoscale machines and devices.
