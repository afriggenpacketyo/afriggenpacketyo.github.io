US-China Research Team Develops Novel Bubble-Popping Robot Propulsion Method

2025-09-06 23:00:08 EDT

A joint US-China research team has developed an innovative method for launching millimeter-scale robots using the energy from rapidly collapsing vapor bubbles. This novel technique involves rapidly heating light-absorbing materials with a laser, causing water droplets on the surface around the material to boil, which triggers the formation of vapor bubbles. Once these bubbles grow to a certain point, they suddenly collapse, rapidly releasing energy and creating a strong force capable of launching the tiny jumpers up to 1.5 meters (5 feet) into the air. By controlling the flow of light, scientists can change how and where these jumpers launch from a wet surface and can even get the small pieces of material to swim in water, demonstrating cavitation as an efficient propulsion mechanism.
