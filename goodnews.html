Golden Eagles Return to Northern England

2025-05-28 21:44:16 EDT

Golden eagles are beginning to venture back into England from southern Scotland, raising hopes that they may nest in the area. The resurgence is attributed to the South of Scotland Golden Eagle Project (SSGEP), which has translocated juveniles and sub-adults from the Scottish Highlands since 2018. Satellite tagging confirms that some eagles are exploring Northumberland and other areas, marking a potentially significant milestone. Golden eagles were wiped out in England and Wales by the mid-19th century due to persecution, and the project aims to reestablish a population in the region, with conservationists expressing hope that the birds will settle in areas like the Lake District and Northumberland Uplands.
