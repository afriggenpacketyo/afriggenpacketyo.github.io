Syria Navigates Complex and Controversial Post-Dictatorship Elections

2025-09-20 05:37:00 EDT

Syria is conducting its first parliamentary elections after decades of dictatorship, utilizing a complex indirect voting system due to the displacement of millions and fragile infrastructure. The process involves appointed committees selecting an "electoral college" of 6,000 to 7,000 people, who then vote for 121 members of the new People's Assembly. While praised by some for its consultative phases and inclusion of women, the system faces significant criticism for granting the interim president, Ahmad al-Sharaa, the power to directly appoint another 70 members, raising concerns about the potential for manipulation and consolidation of power.

Neuroscientist Explains the Science of Limerence and How to Overcome Romantic Obsession

2025-09-20 05:00:00 EDT

Neuroscientist Tom Bellamy's book "Smitten" explores the psychological state of "limerence," an intense romantic infatuation characterized by obsessive thoughts and emotional highs and lows. Bellamy explains that while limerence is a natural part of early love, it can become a form of "person addiction" if the object of affection is unavailable or sends mixed signals, leading to anxiety. To break the cycle of unhealthy limerence, he recommends strategies such as limiting contact, reframing positive daydreams into negative "daymares," and redirecting energy towards new personal goals to create a sense of purpose.
