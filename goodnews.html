Jazz as Defiance: The 'Swing Youth' Movement in Nazi Germany

2026-05-26 08:02:00 EDT

During Nazi Germany, jazz music became an act of defiance for the "Swing Youth" movement, a counterculture that resisted the regime's oppressive ideology. Originating in Hamburg, these affluent teenagers adopted American fashion and names, gathering in clubs to play swing music, often greeting each other with "Swing Heil!". Despite Nazi condemnation of jazz as "degenerate music" due to its Black American and Jewish roots, it was never fully outlawed. The Swing Youth's cultural resistance, though not overtly political, led to repression, with some members arrested and even sent to concentration camps, yet their spirit of individuality persisted.
