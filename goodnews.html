The wall, built by a settled hunter-gatherer or early Neolithic society, is now submerged under nine meters of water due to sea-level rise. Its construction involved large granite standing stones, suggesting a sophisticated and organized community. Researchers theorize that such submerged structures may have contributed to local legends of sunken cities, reflecting the lasting impact of environmental changes on human memory and culture.
