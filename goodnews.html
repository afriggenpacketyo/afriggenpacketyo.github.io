Greece Acquires Rare WWII Photos of Nazi Executions After eBay Appearance

2026-03-02 06:12:23 EST

Greece has successfully acquired a collection of World War II photographs depicting the final moments of 200 men executed by German soldiers in May 1944, following the assassination of a Nazi general. The trove, comprising 262 photographs, documents, and banknotes, is believed to have been taken by a German soldier and recently appeared on eBay, eliciting strong emotional responses in Greece. Culture Minister Lina Mendoni confirmed the acquisition, highlighting the historical significance of these images, which provide the first photographic evidence of an event previously known only through handwritten notes. The photographs capture the men marching and defiantly facing the camera, offering a poignant visual testimony to their sacrifice.

Clownfish Adapt Stripes Based on Social Cues and Habitat

2026-03-02 06:00:00 EST

Researchers have discovered that tomato clownfish can flexibly adjust the timing of losing their stripes in response to social and environmental cues, as detailed in a new paper in PLOS Biology. Juvenile clownfish, initially sporting two to three stripes, typically lose all but one as they mature and integrate into an anemone's social hierarchy. Experiments revealed that the presence of adult clownfish on a live anemone significantly accelerates this stripe-fading process, suggesting young fish alter their appearance to secure a place within the group. This adaptation, involving changes in gene expression and thyroid hormones, highlights how social pressure and habitat influence physical development and integration within a community.

This accelerated discovery is vital because an estimated 1 million animal and plant species are threatened with extinction, with many vanishing before they are even identified. German Court Hears Climate Lawsuits Against Automakers BMW and Mercedes-Benz

2026-03-02 05:26:00 EST

Germany's Federal Court of Justice (BGH) is hearing two climate lawsuits brought by the German Environmental Aid Association (DUH) against car manufacturers BMW and Mercedes-Benz. The advocacy group seeks a ruling to prohibit the companies from selling new internal combustion engine vehicles from 2030, arguing that they are consuming an excessive share of the global CO2 budget. The lawsuits contend that the automakers' current practices force governments to implement more restrictive CO2 reduction measures, thereby infringing on personal freedoms. The court faces the critical legal question of whether companies can be compelled to adopt climate-protective measures independently of government regulations, following previous rulings against DUH in lower courts.
