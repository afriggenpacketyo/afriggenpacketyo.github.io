2026-06-16 03:03:42 EDT

The summer solstice, occurring on Sunday, June 21st, 2026, will officially mark the start of astronomical summer in the Northern Hemisphere. This event signifies the longest period of daylight and shortest night of the year, occurring at a precise moment when the hemisphere is maximally tilted towards the Sun. The exact time of the solstice is 09:24 BST. The date can vary between June 20th and 22nd due to the Earth's orbital period being slightly longer than a calendar year, necessitating leap days to maintain alignment. The Earth's axial tilt is responsible for the distinct seasons experienced throughout the year.
