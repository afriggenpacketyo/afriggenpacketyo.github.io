2025-09-30 11:01:42 EDT

For the first time, researchers at Oregon Health and Science University have created early-stage human embryos by fertilizing eggs that were engineered using DNA from skin cells. The novel technique involves transferring the nucleus from a skin cell into a donor egg stripped of its own genetics, then inducing the egg to halve its chromosomes before fertilization. While the process, termed "mitomeiosis," is still inefficient and requires significant refinement, it represents a major breakthrough in reproductive science. This technology could one day overcome certain forms of infertility and potentially allow same-sex couples to have children who are genetically related to both partners.
