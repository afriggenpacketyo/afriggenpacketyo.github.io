California, Arizona, and Nevada have jointly proposed voluntary water-saving measures for the next three years to address the critically low levels of the Colorado River's reservoirs. The plan aims to conserve 3.2 million acre-feet of water through cutbacks and an additional 700,000 acre-feet via conservation efforts and infrastructure improvements. This initiative seeks to stabilize water supply while negotiations remain deadlocked among all seven states with legal rights to the river. The proposal requires approval from state water agencies and the Arizona legislature, and cooperation from the federal government, highlighting the urgent need for a unified approach amid ongoing water scarcity. The plan also creates a conservation pool to meet federal trust obligations to tribes.
