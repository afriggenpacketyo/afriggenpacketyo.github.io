This maneuver marks the first crewed lunar flyby in over half a century, since the Apollo 17 mission in 1972, and was confirmed as a "good burn" by mission control. Canadian astronaut Jeremy Hansen shared the crew's "phenomenal" views of Earth, reflecting on humanity's renewed capabilities and hopes for the future of space exploration. The mission also achieves significant milestones by carrying the first Black astronaut, the first woman, and the first non-American on a lunar journey, aiming to set a new human distance record in space and pave the way for future Mars missions.
