The virus, carried by almost every human, causes infected B cells to present viral structures that strikingly resemble a protein found in the myelin sheaths of nerve fibers. This "nasty trick" effectively programs the immune system to mistakenly attack its own nerve tissue, leading to MS. This article highlights that while HLA-DR15 is a crucial risk factor, environmental elements also play a role, and research is now focusing on targeted treatments and early childhood vaccination.
