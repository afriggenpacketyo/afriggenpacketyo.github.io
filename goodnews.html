United Airlines Flight Attendants Ratify New Contract with 31% Raises

2026-05-12 14:45:35 EDT

United Airlines flight attendants have overwhelmingly approved a new five-year labor contract, securing average base pay raises of 31% by August. This landmark agreement also introduces boarding pay, a significant improvement as flight attendants previously only began earning once the aircraft door closed. The deal, which garnered 82% approval from nearly 90% of voting members, marks the first raise for United's 30,000 flight attendants in close to six years. Additionally, it includes $741 million in back pay and quality-of-life enhancements like restrictions on red-eye flights.
