Bipartisan Bill Introduced to Expand Burn Pit Benefits for Civilians

2026-05-14 18:52:00 EDT

A bipartisan group of U.S. lawmakers has unveiled a draft bill aimed at bolstering access to workers' compensation for civilians, including law enforcement agents, who developed illnesses like cancer from toxic burn pit exposure during overseas operations. Named the Kenya Merritt Renewing Our Promise to Address Toxicity Act, the bill seeks to place civilian employees on equal legal footing with military service members. Currently, civilians must prove a direct link between their illness and exposure, a significant hurdle that has led to all related claims being denied by the U.S. Department of Labor. The proposed legislation would create a presumption that certain illnesses are work-related for eligible federal employees, aligning their protections with the Department of Veterans Affairs framework.
