Understanding Rule 523: Debts Not Discharged in Bankruptcy

2026-01-23 10:54:01 EST

Rule 523 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code outlines specific types of debts that cannot be legally eliminated through bankruptcy proceedings, impacting both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 filings. This provision serves as an exception list to debt-relief benefits, often catching filers off guard. Common non-dischargeable debts include most student loans, recent tax debts, child support, alimony, and debts arising from fraud. While limited exceptions exist, such as proving "undue hardship" for student loans, the bar is extremely high. Given bankruptcy's long-term credit consequences, exploring alternatives like debt settlement for unsecured debts is often advisable before filing.
