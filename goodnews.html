Dirty Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly, Expert Warns

2025-06-10 07:30:00 EDT

Wildlife experts warn that dirty hummingbird feeders can harm or kill the birds they are intended to help. Spoiled sugar water in feeders attracts microbes that can cause infections, including candida, which can swell hummingbirds' tongues and lead to starvation. To prevent this, feeders should be cleaned every two to three days with hot water or a vinegar and water mixture. It is also important to use the correct recipe of one part refined white sugar to four parts water, avoiding other sugars, sweeteners, and red food coloring. Planting native hummingbird-friendly wildflowers is also recommended.
