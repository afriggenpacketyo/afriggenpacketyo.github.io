Colorado Addresses Economic Challenges in Electric Vehicle Battery Recycling with New Law

2026-07-13 05:00:00 EDT

The electric vehicle battery recycling industry faces significant economic hurdles, as salvage yards often struggle to profit from or even dispose of old EV batteries, sometimes incurring costs. This issue is exacerbated by the increasing use of cheaper lithium-ion iron phosphate (LFP) batteries, which offer less valuable minerals for recycling. Colorado has responded by enacting a new "producer responsibility" law, mandating that EV manufacturers cover the costs of recycling unwanted batteries. This legislation aims to prevent batteries from becoming hazardous waste and ensures critical minerals are recovered, setting a potential precedent for other states.

European Union Approves Comprehensive New Air Passenger Rights

Airlines will also be required to display fares inclusive of cabin baggage by default and allow passengers to use return flights even if the outbound leg is missed.
