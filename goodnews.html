Seasonal outbreaks in the U.S. often occur in summer, linked to items like lettuce, basil, and berries. Symptoms include watery diarrhea, cramps, and fatigue, lasting days to over a month. While usually not life-threatening, it poses higher risks for those with weakened immune systems and is treated with antibiotics.
