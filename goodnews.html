A key regulation for reverse mortgages, known as the 6-month rule, dictates the timeline for heirs after a borrower passes away or permanently leaves the home. Lenders must issue a "Due and Payable" letter, giving the family six months to either repay the loan balance in full or sell the property to cover the debt. This rule primarily applies to federally insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgages (HECMs). While up to two three-month extensions can be requested from HUD, the deadline underscores the need for borrowers to engage in thorough estate planning with their families.
