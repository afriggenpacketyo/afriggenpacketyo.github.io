Ancient DNA Reveals Gender-Specific Human Sacrifice Rituals in Neolithic China

2025-11-28 23:00:15 EST

A study of ancient DNA from the 4,000-year-old Shimao settlement in northwestern China has revealed evidence of gender bias in human sacrifice rituals. Archaeologists found that victims in mass burials, likely for public ceremonies, were predominantly male. In contrast, individuals buried as attendants alongside deceased elites were overwhelmingly female, suggesting sex-specific roles in sacrificial practices. These findings, published in the journal Nature, provide new insights into the hierarchical and patrilineal structure of this large, state-level society during China's late Neolithic period, pushing back the timeline for known female sacrifice.
