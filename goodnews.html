Total Lunar Eclipse, Known as a 'Blood Moon,' to Be Visible Across Europe, Africa, and Asia

2025-09-05 08:22:00 EDT

A total lunar eclipse is expected on September 7, 2025, offering viewers in Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia the chance to see the moon turn a reddish-orange hue. This phenomenon, often called a "Blood Moon," occurs when the Earth passes directly between the sun and the moon, casting a shadow that covers the lunar surface. The red color is caused by sunlight filtering through Earth's atmosphere, which scatters blue light while allowing red and orange light to pass through. Unlike a solar eclipse, a lunar eclipse is safe to view with the naked eye and can last for a significant duration.
