The institution acquired these first-edition volumes in 1838 for a sum equivalent to a skilled laborer's annual wage. With only approximately 120 complete sets known globally, the book is celebrated as the "absolute pinnacle of ornithological art," showcasing meticulously hand-colored, life-size images of over 400 North American bird species. The exhibition offers a unique opportunity to view this monumental work, while also acknowledging the historical contributions of enslaved and indigenous peoples.
