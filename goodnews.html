Known for his highly percussive and explosive piano technique, Palmieri fused Afro-Caribbean rhythms with jazz stylings to create a new sound in the 1960s with his orchestra, La Perfecta. Born in New York to Puerto Rican parents, he became an elder statesman of the genre, winning multiple Grammy awards, including the first-ever Grammy for a Latin recording in 1975. His work was celebrated for its excitement and historical depth, earning him a place in the Library of Congress' National Recording Registry.
