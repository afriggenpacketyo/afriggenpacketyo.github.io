Tech Worker Advocates for Federal Law to Ban 'Ghost Job' Advertisements

2025-08-25 13:13:51 EDT

Frustrated by the prevalence of "ghost jobs" after losing his own employment, tech worker Eric Thompson is spearheading efforts to pass federal legislation, the "Truth in Job Advertising and Accountability Act." Ghost jobs are job postings made without a genuine intent to hire, often used to collect resumes or signal growth, causing significant time-wasting for job seekers. Thompson's proposed act would mandate transparency in job listings, requiring details like intended hire dates and whether a role is new or a backfill. With support from a working group, he is engaging lawmakers to address this "gray area" in employment practices, aiming for a national ban to ensure honesty in hiring.
