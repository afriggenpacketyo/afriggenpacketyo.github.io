The Evolving Meaning of 'Decimate' Explored in Context of President Trump's Usage

2026-05-01 05:00:00 EDT

The word "decimate," frequently used by President Trump to describe U.S. military actions, has a historical meaning far different from its modern usage. Originating from the Latin "decimatio," it once referred to a brutal Roman army punishment of killing precisely one-tenth of a rebellious unit. Over centuries, its meaning evolved from "taking a tenth" or "leaving only one tenth" to its current, more general sense of "destroy" or "devastate." This semantic shift highlights how language evolves, often to the annoyance of purists, as newer generations adopt broader interpretations.
