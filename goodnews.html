Options for Individuals Facing Debt Collector Lawsuits in March

2026-03-06 10:42:09 EST

With rising credit card balances and delinquency rates, debt collection lawsuits are becoming more frequent, prompting individuals to understand their options when sued. Ignoring a lawsuit can lead to default judgments, wage garnishment, or bank account levies. However, being served doesn't mean a decided outcome; individuals can challenge debt validity if documentation is incomplete or inaccurate, or if statutes of limitations have expired. Negotiating a settlement before judgment is often preferred by collectors, potentially reducing the owed amount by 30-50%. Other options include consulting a debt relief company for negotiation assistance or a consumer law attorney for legal defense, especially for large debts or consumer protection violations. For broader relief, considering Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 bankruptcy can halt collection activity and discharge eligible debts.
