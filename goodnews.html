The development of lab-grown meat, or cultivated meat, is being debated as a possible solution to reduce greenhouse gas emissions associated with traditional meat production. While advocates claim it can meet growing meat demand with less environmental impact, critics argue that the environmental benefits are overstated and that reducing meat consumption should be prioritized. The process involves growing animal cells in vats, but questions remain about the energy intensity and overall climate cost compared to traditional farming. Despite regulatory approvals in some countries, mass production and affordability remain challenges, and some worry about the ultra-processed nature of cultivated food, as well as the environmental impact of the CO2 production in comparison to the methane gas produced by cows.
