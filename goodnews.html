Scientists Uncover Key Mechanism Linking Epstein-Barr Virus to Multiple Sclerosis Development

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Scientists have achieved a significant breakthrough in understanding how the common Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) can trigger multiple sclerosis (MS) in a small subset of infected individuals. The research indicates that in people possessing a specific genetic molecule, HLA-DR15, EBV-infected B cells present viral structures that strikingly mimic a protein found in the myelin sheaths surrounding nerve fibers. This "fatal similarity" causes the immune system to mistakenly attack its own nerve tissue, leading to the development of MS. While HLA-DR15 is identified as the most important genetic risk factor, other elements like the timing of EBV infection and lifestyle factors also contribute. This discovery offers renewed hope for developing targeted treatments and preventive vaccines against MS.
