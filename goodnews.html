Genome Sequencing of Ancient Egyptian Potter Reveals Middle Eastern Ancestry

2025-07-02 11:00:00 EDT

Researchers have successfully sequenced the first whole ancient Egyptian genome from the remains of a man who lived as a potter between 4,500 and 4,800 years ago. The study, which analyzed well-preserved DNA from the man's tooth, revealed he had brown eyes, stood 5.2 feet tall, and lived a physically demanding life. Significantly, the genetic analysis showed that about 20% of his ancestry traced back to the Fertile Crescent in the Middle East, providing the first direct genetic evidence of population mixing between North Africa and the Levant during this critical period of Egyptian history. This breakthrough opens new possibilities for understanding the genetic history of ancient Egypt.
