Experts Explain Why Students Frequently Contract "Freshers' Flu"

2025-10-04 19:05:38 EDT

"Freshers' flu," a prevalent ailment among new university students, is identified by experts as a collection of common cold viruses, not actual influenza. Its rapid transmission is attributed to the convergence of thousands of students from diverse regions into close living and learning environments, exposing them to numerous novel respiratory pathogens. Contributing significantly to this phenomenon are the lifestyle factors common during freshers' week, including sleep deprivation from late-night socializing, compromised diets, heightened stress, and increased alcohol intake. These elements collectively weaken the immune system, creating an optimal breeding ground for infections and making the illness difficult for students to evade.
