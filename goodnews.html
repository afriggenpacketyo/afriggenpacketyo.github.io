The fossil revealed phytoliths, which are microscopic mineral deposits from plants, and small quartz crystals likely used as gastroliths to help grind tough food. This rare find confirms that this particular species was herbivorous, challenging previous assumptions about their diets. The discovery sheds new light on the ecological roles of pterosaurs, the creatures that dominated the skies during the age of dinosaurs.

Astronomers Use James Webb Telescope to Capture First Glimpse of Rocky Planet Formation

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

For the first time, astronomers have directly observed the earliest stages of rocky planet formation around a young, sun-like star named HOPS-315, located 1,370 light-years away. Using the James Webb Space Telescope and the ALMA array, researchers detected silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing in the star's surrounding disk. These are the fundamental building blocks believed to have formed Earth and other terrestrial planets. This landmark discovery provides an unprecedented look into a process that may mirror the dawn of our own solar system and could help determine how common Earth-like worlds are.
