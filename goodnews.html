Study Reveals Human Activity Caused Divergence in Animal Sizes During Middle Ages

2025-09-01 13:05:00 EDT

A new study by French researchers reveals a significant shift in the body size evolution of animals that began in the Middle Ages. By analyzing over 225,000 animal bones from the past 8,000 years, scientists found that wild species like deer and foxes began to shrink, while domesticated species such as cattle and chickens grew larger. This divergence is attributed to increasing human influence, including the fragmentation of wild habitats and the intensification of selective breeding for livestock. The findings demonstrate the profound and lasting impact of human activities on the natural world, altering the evolutionary trajectory of multiple species over the last millennium.
