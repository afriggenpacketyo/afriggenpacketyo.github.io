DNA Study Reveals Ancestral Origins of Indigenous Americans

2025-05-17 02:34:00 EDT

A new genetic study has traced the migration of the first people to colonize the Americas back to modern-day Russia between 20,000 and 30,000 years ago. The study, published in Science, suggests that the languages and traditions of Indigenous groups living in the Americas today can be traced back to these early settlers. Researchers sequenced the genomes of 1,537 individuals from 139 ethnic groups in northern Eurasia and the Americas, finding that the early settlers split into groups that became isolated in different environmental settings, which led to genetic mutations that helped them adapt to extreme environments.
