2026-03-25 16:12:31 EDT

The United Nations General Assembly has passed a resolution, proposed by Ghana, formally recognizing the enslavement of Africans during the transatlantic slave trade as "the gravest crime against humanity." The resolution also encourages UN member states to consider offering apologies for the slave trade and contributing to a reparations fund. Adopted with 123 votes in favor, the United States, Israel, and Argentina voted against it, while the United Kingdom and European Union member states abstained, citing concerns over international law and the concept of reparations for historical wrongs. This non-legally binding resolution aims to safeguard against historical forgetting and address the enduring racial inequalities stemming from slavery.
