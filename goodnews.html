Fossil Discovery in China Reveals Ancient Marine Animals Practiced Social Distancing

2025-07-30 00:39:15 EDT

Chinese paleontologists have discovered that an ancient, clam-like marine animal known as a "lamp shell" may have practiced a form of social distancing to survive. The 436-million-year-old fossils, found in China's Guizhou province, were arranged in a distinct checkerboard-like pattern on the former sea floor. Researchers believe the extinct species, Nucleospira calypta, used hundreds of fine, bristle-like structures to sense their neighbors and maintain optimal spacing. This strategic arrangement likely improved their ability to filter-feed, providing a fascinating insight into the survival behaviors of prehistoric creatures.
