Shingles Vaccine Linked to Lower Heart Disease Risk in Study

2025-05-05 19:05:08 EDT

A recent study published in the European Heart Journal suggests that the shingles vaccine may significantly reduce the risk of cardiovascular events, including stroke, heart failure, and coronary heart disease, for up to eight years. The research, involving over one million people aged 50 or older, found a 23% lower risk of such events in vaccinated individuals. The protective effect was particularly noticeable in men, those under 60, and individuals with unhealthy lifestyles. Researchers believe that preventing shingles through vaccination may reduce blood vessel damage and inflammation that can lead to heart issues.
