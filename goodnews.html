New Blood Test Shows Promise in Predicting Alzheimer's Symptoms Years in Advance

2026-07-15 07:46:00 EDT

Researchers have developed a blood test, p-tau217, that may predict the likelihood of apparently healthy older adults developing Alzheimer's symptoms within five to ten years. The study, published in JAMA, found that very high p-tau217 levels correlated with a 38% risk of cognitive impairment over five years, rising to 78% over ten years. This breakthrough could accelerate drug development by identifying high-risk individuals for clinical trials. While not yet ready for general public use, the test measures a form of tau linked to brain plaques and tangles, offering crucial insights into the disease's progression.
