California's e-bike regulations: A guide to legal classifications and restrictions.

2026-06-10 06:00:00 EDT

California law categorizes electric bicycles into three classes, with specific regulations regarding motor power, speed, and rider age. Class 1 and 2 e-bikes are low-speed and can be ridden by all ages, while Class 3 e-bikes, capable of reaching 28 mph, require riders to be at least 16 and wear a helmet. Vehicles with motors exceeding 750 watts, lacking operable pedals, or offering pedal assistance beyond 28 mph may be classified as motorcycles or mopeds, requiring licenses and registration. Law enforcement is emphasizing public education to prevent accidents and citations related to illegal e-bike use.
