The Origin and Significance of the Name 'Juneteenth'

2025-06-19 10:21:18 EDT

Juneteenth, a portmanteau of June and nineteenth, is a federally recognized U.S. holiday commemorating the end of slavery in the United States. It originated on June 19, 1865, when Union soldiers arrived in Galveston Bay, Texas, announcing the freedom of over 250,000 enslaved people. The name "Juneteenth" pays homage to the date when the Emancipation Proclamation finally took effect in Texas. Celebrations include parades, parties, concerts, and educational workshops, reflecting a joyful anniversary of independence and a remembrance of the country's history of slavery. The holiday signifies freedom and unity for all.
