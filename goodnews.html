Oldest Known Pterosaur in North America Discovered in Arizona

2025-07-07 15:00:04 EDT

Researchers have identified the fossil of North America's oldest known pterosaur, a flying reptile approximately the size of a small seagull that lived 209.2 million years ago. The discovery, detailed in a Smithsonian-led paper, was made from a fragile jawbone unearthed at a fossil site in Arizona's Petrified Forest National Park. The new species, named Eotephradactylus mcintireae, helps fill a significant gap in the fossil record from the late Triassic period. The finding provides valuable new insights into the evolution of pterosaurs, which were close cousins of dinosaurs and the first animals after insects to evolve powered flight.
