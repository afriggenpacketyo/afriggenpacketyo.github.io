Understanding Your Obligation to Pay a Debt After It Has Been Sold to a Collection Agency

2025-11-20 13:29:11 EST

This article explains that a borrower's legal obligation to pay a debt typically remains even after the original creditor sells the delinquent account to a collection agency. The new owner acquires the legal right to collect the balance. However, consumers are protected by the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), which entitles them to receive written validation of the debt and dispute any inaccuracies. It is also crucial to check if the debt is within the state's statute of limitations, as collectors cannot sue for time-barred debts. If the debt is valid, negotiation or professional debt relief services are potential options.
