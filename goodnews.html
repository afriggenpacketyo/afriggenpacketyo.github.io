UK Marine Life Adapts to Warmer Waters

2025-06-18 05:15:59 EDT

Scientists predict that endangered sharks, rays, and native oysters may flourish in UK waters as they adapt to rising ocean temperatures. A study mapped the responses of 19 threatened marine species to climate change, identifying potential biodiversity hotspots in the North Sea within the next 50 years. While mobile species are expected to adapt well, static creatures like sea pens and the ocean quahog clam may struggle, impacting ecosystems and food chains. The findings aim to guide government planning for Marine Protected Areas, emphasizing the need to manage threats like fishing, disease, and pollution to ensure these species thrive in their new habitats.

With branches in New York, London, and Jerusalem, the LBI houses a vast collection of historical objects, books, letters, and artworks, most of which have been digitized and made accessible online. Businesses Employ 'Tariff Engineering' to Avoid Higher Duties

2025-06-18 03:56:58 EDT

Faced with increasing tariffs, businesses are resorting to "tariff engineering" to lower their duty liabilities by strategically altering product classifications. This involves changing an item's materials, dimensions, or composition to fit into a different harmonized system code, which can result in lower tariff rates. While not inherently illegal, this practice requires careful navigation of over 5,000 product classification codes used by U.S. customs authorities. Examples include adding cubic zirconia to lapel pins to classify them as jewelry and adding zippered pockets to women's shirts to qualify for lower duties.
