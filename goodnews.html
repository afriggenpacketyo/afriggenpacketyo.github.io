Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Ancient Cross-Equator Migration Corridor in Pangaea

2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences have identified a new species of dicynodont, an extinct clade of non-mammalian therapsids considered a distant relative of modern mammals, named Dinanomodon guoi. This significant discovery, based on a fossil specimen unearthed in Gulang county, Gansu province, China, marks the first bidentalian genus shared between China and South Africa. The finding strongly suggests the existence of a cross-equator migration corridor during the era of the supercontinent Pangaea, providing invaluable insights into ancient animal migration patterns and the profound interconnectedness of landmasses millions of years ago. Dicynodonts were herbivorous, mostly toothless animals that varied greatly in size, from small burrowers to large grazers.
