Explainer: The Role of the FCC and Its Authority to Revoke Broadcast Licenses

2025-09-19 20:34:12 EDT

Recent threats from the Trump administration to revoke broadcast licenses of networks like ABC have raised questions about the powers of the Federal Communications Commission (FCC). The FCC, an independent agency, grants licenses to local television and radio stations to use public airwaves, requiring them to operate in the "public interest." While it does not directly regulate national networks or cable channels, it can influence them through their affiliated local stations. Experts note that revoking a license is a complex process and is typically related to issues like obscenity or children's programming, not political content, making the current threats largely unprecedented.
