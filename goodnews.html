New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species Discovered in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new dinosaur species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils found in Kalasin Province. This plant-eating dinosaur, estimated to have lived 150 million years ago, featured an unusually long neck and measured up to 20 meters (66 feet) in length. The discovery, detailed in a Nature scientific journal article, marks the first time a Mamenchisauridae family sauropod, known for its extremely long neck, has been identified in Thailand. Unique characteristics, including a Y-shaped bone arrangement and distinct air-cavity structure, differentiate this species from others globally.
