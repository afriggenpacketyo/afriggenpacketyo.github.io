China Completes Major Green Belt Project to Combat Desertification in Inner Mongolia

2025-07-09 00:00:08 EDT

China has successfully completed an 1,856-kilometer (1,153-mile) green belt in Inner Mongolia, effectively enclosing the Badain Jaran, Tengger, and Ulan Buh deserts. This significant environmental project aims to combat desertification and control the movement of sand in the Alxa league region. The method involves using straw checkerboards to stabilize shifting sands, which are then followed by the planting of drought-resistant vegetation like sacsaoul trees. This long-term strategy is part of China's broader "green great wall" initiative, designed to build a robust ecological barrier and mitigate the effects of desert expansion on the country.
