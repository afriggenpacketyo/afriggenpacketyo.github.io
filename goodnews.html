Oldest Known North American Pterosaur Species Discovered in Arizona

2025-07-07 15:00:00 EDT

Scientists have identified a new species of pterosaur from a 209-million-year-old jawbone fossil discovered in Arizona's Petrified Forest National Park. The creature, named Eotephradactylus mcintireae, is now the earliest known flying reptile found in North America. Researchers say the seagull-sized reptile likely fed on hard-shelled, primitive fish in an ancient river system. The discovery is significant because Triassic pterosaur fossils are extremely rare due to their small, hollow bones, and this find helps fill a critical gap in the evolutionary record before a mass extinction event.
