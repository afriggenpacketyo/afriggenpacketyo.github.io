Uruguay Becomes First Latin American Country to Legalize Euthanasia

2025-10-16 02:38:00 EDT

Uruguay's Senate has passed a bill legalizing euthanasia, making it the first country in Latin America to formally enact such a law. The "Dignified Death" bill allows mentally sound adult citizens with terminal, irreversible diseases to choose a medically assisted death. The legislation, which previously passed the lower house with a large majority, does not permit assisted suicide where the patient self-administers medication. This move is consistent with Uruguay's history of socially liberal policies in a predominantly Catholic region, including the legalization of cannabis, same-sex marriage, and abortion. Public support for the measure is strong, with over 60% of Uruguayans in favor, according to recent polls.
