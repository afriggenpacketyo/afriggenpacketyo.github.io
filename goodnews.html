Jackson made history as the first African American to launch a nationwide presidential bid, successfully getting on the ballot in all 50 states. His campaigns were instrumental in mobilizing millions of Black voters, many for the first time, and significantly influenced the Democratic Party to revise its primary rules, transitioning from winner-take-all to proportional delegate allocation. This strategic change proved pivotal for Obama's 2008 victory, cementing Jackson's enduring legacy as a transformative figure in expanding political participation and fostering diversity within American democracy.
