The Historic Introduction of Football's Penalty Shootout in 1970

2026-03-28 02:15:22 EDT

Football's penalty shootout, now a ubiquitous and nerve-wracking decider, was officially introduced in 1970 to replace less sporting methods like coin tosses or drawing lots. The world's first official shootout occurred on August 5, 1970, in Hull, England, during a Watney Cup match between Manchester United and Hull City. This innovative rule change was championed by Israeli Football Association officials after a controversial coin toss eliminated their team from the 1968 Olympics. The article vividly recounts the historic match, highlighting the drama as iconic players like George Best participated in this groundbreaking moment, forever changing how tied matches are resolved.

Chinese Researchers Develop Breakthrough Lithium Battery Electrolyte for EVs

2026-03-28 02:00:14 EDT

Chinese researchers have developed a novel hydrofluorocarbon-based electrolyte for lithium batteries, potentially revolutionizing electric vehicle performance. Crucially, these batteries can also operate efficiently in extreme cold, down to minus 70 degrees Celsius, addressing a major limitation for EVs in harsh climates.
