A Comparison of 401(k) Plans and Annuities for Retirement Savings

2025-07-16 10:17:08 EDT

When planning for retirement, individuals often face a choice between investment vehicles like 401(k) plans and insurance products like annuities. A 401(k) offers growth potential and flexibility, allowing pre-tax or Roth contributions to be invested in markets, though this exposes the saver to market risk. In contrast, an annuity provides a guaranteed income stream, shifting risk to an insurance company but often coming with higher fees and less flexibility. For many, the optimal strategy is not a binary choice but a hybrid approach, using a 401(k) for growth during one's career and then converting a portion to an annuity for a stable income floor in retirement.
