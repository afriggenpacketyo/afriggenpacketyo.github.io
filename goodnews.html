Daylight Saving Time 2026 Begins, Causing Most Americans to "Spring Forward"

2026-03-07 06:00:13 EST

Daylight Saving Time for 2026 is set to begin on Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, causing most Americans to "spring forward" and lose an hour of sleep. This annual tradition effectively shifts an hour of daylight from morning to evening, with the start date consistently on the second Sunday of March since 2007. Historically, it was first adopted in the U.S. in 1918 to conserve fuel during World War I, though subsequent studies have shown minimal energy savings. Only Hawaii and Arizona, with the exception of the Navajo Nation, along with several U.S. territories, do not observe this time change, which has also been linked to some negative health effects on individuals.
