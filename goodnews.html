A Guide to the Key Differences Between ETFs and Mutual Funds for Investors

2025-09-22 07:00:01 EDT

Exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and mutual funds are both popular investment vehicles, but they have key differences in trading, tax efficiency, and cost that investors should understand. ETFs trade like stocks on an exchange throughout the day, while mutual funds are priced once at the end of the day. A significant advantage for ETFs held in taxable accounts is their tax efficiency, as their structure rarely generates the capital gains distributions common with mutual funds. Furthermore, ETFs generally have lower annual fees, or expense ratios, than their mutual fund counterparts, making them a cheaper option for many investors.
