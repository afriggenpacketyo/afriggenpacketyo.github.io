A debt discharge is a legal action, most often occurring after a bankruptcy filing, that permanently relieves a borrower of the obligation to repay certain debts. When a debt is discharged, creditors are legally barred from any further collection attempts. However, not all debts are eligible; unsecured debts like credit cards and medical bills are common candidates, while student loans and recent taxes are typically excluded. The article stresses that while a discharge provides significant relief, the record of the bankruptcy itself remains on a credit report for seven to ten years.
