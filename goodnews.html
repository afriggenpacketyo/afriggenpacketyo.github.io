UK Cyber Chiefs Advocate for Passkeys to Enhance Online Account Security

2026-04-24 10:40:23 EDT

UK cyber chiefs are urging people to adopt passkeys, where available, as a more secure alternative to traditional passwords for online accounts. The National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) is "overhauling decades of security practice" to recommend passkeys, which are a piece of digital information tied to a user's account and unique to each site or app. Unlike passwords, they don't require memorization and use public key cryptography with device-level checks like Face ID or Touch ID. While not a "silver bullet," passkeys are considered less vulnerable to phishing attempts and human error, offering stronger overall resilience for digital services and potentially relieving the burden of remembering complex passwords.
