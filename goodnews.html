Understanding Your Rights When a Creditor Freezes Your Bank Account

2026-03-23 13:17:40 EDT

With credit card balances at record highs and delinquency rates worsening, creditors are increasingly resorting to freezing bank accounts over unpaid debts, causing significant financial stress. This article explains consumer rights in such situations, emphasizing that creditors must provide proper notice both before and after a bank levy occurs. Borrowers have the right to claim exempt funds, such as Social Security or disability benefits, which are protected by federal and state laws from seizure. Additionally, individuals can challenge the freeze in court if proper legal procedures weren't followed or if the debt is incorrect, and they always retain the right to legal representation and negotiation to resolve the issue.
