French Parliament Approves Assisted Dying Legislation for Terminally Ill Adults

2026-07-15 15:27:00 EDT

France's parliament has successfully passed a bill that allows assisted dying for terminally ill adults experiencing constant and unbearable physical suffering. The legislation permits eligible French citizens or legal residents to request a lethal substance, which can be self-administered or administered by a medical professional, following a thorough physician's examination and an expert panel assessment. Although the lower house approved the bill, the government invoked a constitutional rule to bypass the conservative-dominated Senate, sending it directly to the Constitutional Council for a final judicial review. If upheld, France will join a growing number of nations that have legalized some form of assisted dying.
