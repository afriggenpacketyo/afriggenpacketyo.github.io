China Completes Major Sand-Control Belt in Inner Mongolia

2025-07-09 00:00:08 EDT

China has completed a vast 1,856-kilometer (1,153-mile) green belt designed to control the expansion of three major deserts in Inner Mongolia. The project aims to stabilize the sand of the Badain Jaran, Tengger, and Ulan Buh deserts, which cover a combined area of nearly 95,000 square kilometers. Engineers used a method of creating straw checkerboards to lock down shifting sands, which is then followed by planting drought-resistant trees. This large-scale effort is part of China's broader 'green great wall' initiative to combat desertification and build a more effective ecological barrier against sandstorms.
