Researchers analyzed audio recordings of birds in the rainforest and found little support for the 'acoustic transmission hypothesis'. Instead, the study suggests that early morning vocal activity relates to birds marking their territory after being inactive at night and communicating about finding food. The researchers did find relationships between birds who were more territorial and those with an omnivorous diet, with these birds being more vocal at dawn.
