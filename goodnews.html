Japanese Superstitions Impede Sale and Rental of "Stigmatized Properties"

2026-07-18 03:32:00 EDT

In Japan, cultural superstitions surrounding death make "jiko bukken," or stigmatized properties where unfortunate events occurred, extremely difficult to sell or rent. These homes, often associated with suicides, lonely deaths, or fires, can remain vacant for extended periods and require significant rent reductions. Specialized services, like Kachimode Co., have emerged to conduct "ghost investigations" and "cleanse" properties to reassure potential tenants, sometimes even involving Shinto priests. This unique cultural phenomenon contributes to Japan's growing number of vacant homes, highlighting how deeply ingrained beliefs about impurity and misfortune impact the real estate market, creating both challenges and niche business opportunities.

Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A new Chinese fossil find has provided significant insights into the evolutionary transition of birds from their dinosaur ancestors, specifically detailing how they gradually shed their long tails. Published in the journal Science Advances, the research highlights the step-by-step development of the pygostyle, a short, fused skeletal structure found in modern birds that anchors tail feathers and muscles, crucial for flight. Studying this transformation has historically been challenging due to the scarcity of early-diverging bird and bird-like dinosaur fossils. This discovery illuminates a profound morphological change in terrestrial vertebrate history, offering a clearer understanding of avian adaptation for flight.
