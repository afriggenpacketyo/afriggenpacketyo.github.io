Chinese Scientists Create Lab-Grown Heart Pacemaker from Stem Cells

2026-05-22 22:00:07 EDT

Scientists in Shanghai have achieved a significant medical breakthrough by successfully creating the world's first laboratory-grown sinoatrial node, which functions as the heart's natural pacemaker, using human pluripotent stem cells. This autonomously beating organoid represents a major advancement that could revolutionize research into cardiac diseases and drug screening, offering new avenues for understanding and treating heart rhythm disorders. The sinoatrial node is crucial for the heart's continuous and rhythmic beating, dictating when its chambers contract to pump blood efficiently. Its failure can lead to dangerously slow or paused heartbeats, making this engineered biological pacemaker a promising development for controlling abnormal rhythms.
