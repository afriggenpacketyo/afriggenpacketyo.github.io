Chinese scientists have identified a gene in upland rice that obstructs the development of deeper, thicker roots, which are crucial for drought resistance. This discovery could transform widely cultivated irrigated rice into drought-tolerant varieties, enabling it to thrive in water-scarce environments. The findings, published in Molecular Plant, represent a significant step towards enhancing food security.
