The Reverse Mortgage 6-Month Repayment Rule Explained for Homeowners and Heirs

2025-09-16 12:55:29 EDT

A key feature of reverse mortgages is the "6-month rule," which dictates the timeline for repayment after the borrower passes away or permanently leaves the home. Once this qualifying event occurs, the lender issues a "Due and Payable" letter, giving heirs six months to either repay the loan balance in full or sell the property to settle the debt. This rule primarily applies to federally insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgages (HECMs). While up to two three-month extensions can be requested from HUD, this guideline highlights the importance of proactive estate planning for borrowers who wish for their families to retain the home.
