The Role and Jurisdiction of the International Court of Justice Explained

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, is based in The Hague and handles legal disputes between states. Comprised of 15 judges from different countries, its jurisdiction depends on whether nations have agreed to submit to its authority, either through a blanket declaration, mutual consent, or by signing specific treaties like the Genocide Convention. While its rulings are legally binding, the court lacks enforcement power, relying on the cooperation of the involved parties, as seen in ongoing cases involving Ukraine and Israel.
