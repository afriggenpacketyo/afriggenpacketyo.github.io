UK Postal Regulator Approves Royal Mail's Plan to Reduce Second-Class Deliveries

2025-07-10 03:25:21 EDT

Ofcom has approved Royal Mail's plan to reduce second-class letter deliveries to five weekdays, eliminating Saturday service, effective July 28. This significant reform aims to cut costs and ensure the financial sustainability of the Universal Service Obligation amidst declining letter volumes. While first-class deliveries will maintain a six-day-a-week schedule, new regulatory targets include delivering 90% of first-class mail next-day and 95% of second-class mail within three days. Royal Mail welcomed these changes, expecting implementation over 12-18 months, yet consumer groups like Citizens Advice have voiced strong concerns, arguing that merely cutting services and relaxing targets will not inherently improve service reliability or address existing performance issues.
