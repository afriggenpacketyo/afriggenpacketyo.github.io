UK Government Establishes New Unit to Combat Organized Crime in High Street Shops

2026-05-18 17:06:38 EDT

The UK government has unveiled a new £30 million High Street organized crime unit, a direct response to a year-long BBC investigation that exposed widespread illegal activities within seemingly legitimate mini-marts, vape shops, and barbers. This new unit, which will be led by the National Crime Agency (NCA) over the next three years, aims to aggressively target criminal enterprises involved in drug trafficking, child sexual exploitation, money laundering, and immigration offenses. The NCA estimates that at least £1 billion in illicit funds are laundered through these High Street fronts annually. In addition to dedicated police officers in hotspot regions, the initiative includes a crucial funding boost for trading standards, which have experienced substantial resource cuts in previous years. The government also plans to review existing law enforcement powers and consult on extending the duration of closure orders for criminal businesses, signaling a comprehensive effort to disrupt and dismantle organized crime networks impacting local communities.
