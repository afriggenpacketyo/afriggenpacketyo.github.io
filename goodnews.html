Examining the Persistent Myth of Turkey-Induced Sleepiness

2025-11-26 09:00:00 EST

The common belief that eating turkey on Thanksgiving causes drowsiness is a persistent myth that science does not support. While turkey contains the amino acid tryptophan, which can be converted to sleep-related chemicals in the brain, it does not contain unusually high amounts compared to other meats like chicken or cheese. Furthermore, for tryptophan to have a sedative effect, it would need to be consumed in large quantities without other proteins. Experts suggest that post-meal lethargy is more likely caused by the large overall quantity of food, carbohydrate-heavy side dishes, and alcohol consumption rather than the turkey itself.
