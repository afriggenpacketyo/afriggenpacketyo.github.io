Venice Architecture Biennale Focuses on Adapting Cities to Extreme Heat

2025-05-09 02:19:00 EDT

The 19th Venice Architecture Biennale explores how cities can adapt to rising temperatures and extreme weather caused by climate change, which scientists say led to tens of thousands of excess deaths in Europe in recent years. Curator Carlo Ratti emphasizes the need for a collective, interdisciplinary approach to rethink the built environment, incorporating knowledge from construction, architecture, urban planning, and sciences. The biennale highlights solutions like redesigning urban areas with more trees and permeable surfaces to combat overheating and improve water absorption, while also showcasing cities like Paris, Copenhagen and Rotterdam that are pioneering climate adaptation strategies. The exhibition aims to spur a global construction revolution to make cities more resilient and habitable in the face of climate change.
