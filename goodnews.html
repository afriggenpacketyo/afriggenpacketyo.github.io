Daylight Saving Time Ends: Clocks "Fall Back" This Sunday, November 2nd

2025-11-01 19:32:20 EDT

Daylight Saving Time concludes on Sunday, November 2nd, 2025, at 2 a.m., when clocks "fall back" one hour, granting most Americans an extra hour of sleep. This annual change, which shifts darkness to arrive earlier in the evening and sunrise earlier in the morning, has historical roots in World War I energy-saving efforts and was legally mandated in the U.S. by the Uniform Time Act of 1966. While most of the U.S. observes it, Hawaii and Arizona (excluding Navajo Nation), along with several territories, do not. There's ongoing debate, with President Trump having previously called for its elimination.
