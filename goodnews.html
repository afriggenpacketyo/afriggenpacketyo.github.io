Cambodia Honors HeroRAT Magawa with World's First Landmine-Detecting Rat Statue

2026-04-04 21:09:11 EDT

Cambodia has unveiled the world's first statue dedicated to a landmine-detecting rat, honoring Magawa, a heroic African giant pouched rat known for his exceptional service. Magawa, who lived to eight years old, sniffed out over 100 landmines and other explosives in Cambodia during his five-year career, clearing more than 141,000 square meters of land, equivalent to 20 football pitches. Awarded the PDSA Gold Medal – known as the George Cross for animals – for his "life-saving devotion to duty" in 2020, he was the first rat to receive this prestigious honor in the charity's 77-year history. The monument, carved from local stone and unveiled on International Day for Mine Awareness, serves as a poignant reminder to the international community that landmines remain an ongoing risk to Cambodia, which now has a target date of 2030 to become entirely mine-free.
