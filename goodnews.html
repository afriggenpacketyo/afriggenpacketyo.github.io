Brazil Deploys Specially Bred Mosquitoes to Combat Dengue Fever

2025-07-26 09:19:53 EDT

To combat devastating dengue outbreaks, Brazil is releasing millions of mosquitoes engineered with Wolbachia bacteria. This naturally occurring bacterium prevents the dengue virus from replicating inside the Aedes aegypti mosquito, rendering it unable to transmit the disease to humans. The initiative, led by the World Mosquito Program, has shown remarkable success in a trial city, reducing dengue cases by approximately 90%. Now, the Brazilian government is scaling up the project, building a massive new factory to produce billions of these "good mosquitoes" to protect millions of citizens from dengue, Zika, and chikungunya.
