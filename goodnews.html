The Story Behind the Name of Juneteenth

2025-06-18 11:59:05 EDT

Juneteenth, now a federally recognized holiday, commemorates the end of slavery in the U.S. and is celebrated annually on June 19. The name Juneteenth combines June and nineteenth, marking the day in 1865 when enslaved people in Texas were informed of their freedom under the Emancipation Proclamation. Although President Lincoln signed the Proclamation in 1863, it wasn't until Union soldiers arrived in Galveston, Texas, that the news reached the westernmost Confederate state. The holiday symbolizes independence, unity, and resilience, with celebrations including parades, parties, and cultural events.
