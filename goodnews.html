Specialized USPS Facility in Utah Deciphers Illegible Mail Addresses

2025-07-24 20:54:07 EDT

The U.S. Postal Service operates a Remote Encoding Center in Salt Lake City, Utah, dedicated to deciphering poorly handwritten addresses on mail. When automated systems fail to read an address, images are sent to this facility where data conversion operators, or keyers, work around the clock to manually decode them. These experts process approximately 75,000 images of mail daily, with an individual keyer handling about 900 pieces per hour. This specialized center ensures that even mail with the most challenging penmanship has a chance to reach its intended destination, preventing it from being returned to the sender.
