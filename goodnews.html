Tiny Zooplankton Play a Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New scientific research reveals that tiny marine creatures called zooplankton are unsung heroes in the fight against climate change. These animals undertake a massive annual migration in the Southern Ocean, gorging on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface, getting fat, and then sinking deep into the ocean to burn that fat. This process effectively locks away vast amounts of carbon, equivalent to the annual emissions of about 55 million gasoline-powered cars. The study highlights a previously underestimated natural carbon pump, emphasizing the crucial role these small organisms play in regulating the global climate.
