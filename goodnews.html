NASA's Perseverance Rover May Have Detected First Evidence of Lightning on Mars

2025-11-28 10:12:16 EST

Scientists believe they have recorded the first evidence of electrical activity in the Martian atmosphere, suggesting the planet is capable of producing lightning. NASA's Perseverance rover, using its SuperCam instrument, captured audio and electromagnetic signals of small electrical discharges, often associated with dust devils and storms in the Jezero Crater. While researchers find the evidence persuasive, some skepticism remains because the discharges were only heard and not visually confirmed. The discovery has significant implications for understanding Martian atmospheric chemistry and habitability, and scientists hope to send more sensitive instruments to confirm the findings in the future.
