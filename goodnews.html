October's Harvest Moon to be First Supermoon of 2025

2025-10-07 03:55:18 EDT

The first of three consecutive supermoons in 2025 is set to appear on the night of October 7th. Known as the Harvest Moon, it will be the largest and brightest full moon of the year so far, as its peak coincides with its closest approach to Earth, an orbital point called the perigee. A supermoon can appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than a typical full moon. This year's Harvest Moon, the full moon closest to the autumn equinox, is occurring later than usual. Stargazers can look forward to two more supermoons later this year in November and December.
