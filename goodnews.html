This traditional name signifies the time when earthworms begin to emerge from the soil as the weather transitions to warmer spring temperatures. For sky-watchers in Asia, Australia, and parts of the Americas, this full moon coincided with a rare total lunar eclipse, causing the Moon to appear a rusty red, earning it the moniker "Blood Moon." Although totality was not visible in the UK, nearly a third of the global population witnessed this celestial phenomenon.
