You Can Dispute Debts Even After They're Sold to Collection Agencies

2025-12-19 11:41:32 EST

Individuals have the right to dispute a debt, even if it has been sold to a collection agency, under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA). Collection agencies are required to send a validation notice within five days of initial contact, outlining the debt amount, original creditor, and the right to dispute within 30 days. If a written dispute is submitted within this period, collection activities must cease until the debt is verified. Many agencies struggle to validate older debts, especially those sold multiple times, which can render the debt uncollectible if validation fails. If a debt is validated, options like debt settlement or credit counseling can help manage repayment.
