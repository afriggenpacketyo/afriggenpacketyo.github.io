The "4% rule" for annuities is a guideline that helps retirees compare an annuity's guaranteed income stream against the safe withdrawal rate from a traditional investment portfolio. While the original 4% rule suggests withdrawing 4% of savings annually, annuities offer guaranteed lifetime income, protecting against outliving funds. This rule serves as a benchmark for evaluating whether an annuity provides comparable or superior value, highlighting benefits like longevity protection, but also drawing attention to potential drawbacks such as lack of inflation adjustment or flexibility compared to traditional portfolios.
