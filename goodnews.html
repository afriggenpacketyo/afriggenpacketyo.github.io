A blue moon occurs when there are two full moons in a single calendar month, and a micromoon happens when a full moon occurs at its farthest point from Earth. While the blue moon will not appear blue in color, its distance will make it appear slightly smaller and dimmer than an average full moon. For observers in the Southern Hemisphere, the moon may appear to obscure the bright star Antares.
