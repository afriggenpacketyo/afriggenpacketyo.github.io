Researchers are studying the squirting cucumber, a toxic relative of the common cucumber, for its remarkable ability to ballistically disperse its seeds. When ripe, the fruit becomes pressurized with fluid and, upon the slightest touch, explodes to launch its seeds at speeds up to 30 miles per hour. Scientists used high-speed videography and CT scans to discover that the fruit ripens at a perfect ballistic angle of around 50 degrees for maximum distance.
