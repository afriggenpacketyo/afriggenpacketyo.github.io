The Peak District's Evolution from Private Land to Public National Park

2026-04-18 19:36:46 EDT

The Peak District, Britain's first national park, celebrates its 75th anniversary, marking a long history of evolving public access. Initially attracting wealthy gentry, railways in the 19th century opened the area to mass tourism. However, much land remained private, leading to the pivotal 1932 Kinder Scout mass trespass, a protest for roaming rights. This, alongside sustained campaigning, led to the 1949 National Parks and Access to the Countryside Act, establishing national parks and public rights of way. Later legislation further expanded access, transforming the Peak District into a cherished public space for 13 million annual visitors.
