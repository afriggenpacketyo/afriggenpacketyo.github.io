Aurora Borealis colors explained and visibility extended beyond polar regions

2025-12-22 06:55:00 EST

The captivating colors of the aurora borealis and australis are caused by solar particles interacting with atmospheric elements. Oxygen typically produces green light, while nitrogen creates purple, blue, or pink hues, with rare red colors occurring at higher altitudes. Recent solar activity has expanded the visibility of auroras beyond their usual polar regions, with sightings reported in more temperate latitudes. While auroras are generally harmless, research indicates they can cause significant ozone depletion in polar regions. Auroras are also observed on other planets, demonstrating a universal atmospheric phenomenon.
