Viral Photos of Horned "Zombie Rabbits" in Colorado Explained by Wildlife Officials

2025-08-13 20:33:00 EDT

Photos of rabbits with black, horn-like growths on their faces have gone viral, sparking alarm online, but Colorado wildlife officials have a scientific explanation. The animals are infected with Shope Papilloma Virus, a relatively common disease spread by ticks and fleas that causes keratinous tumors to grow on the rabbits' heads. The virus is not transmissible to humans or other pets and is usually harmless to the rabbits unless the growths interfere with their ability to eat. The phenomenon is believed to have inspired the myth of the "jackalope," a fabled rabbit with antelope antlers.
