In younger children, these cells exist as small, easily destroyed clusters, whereas in older individuals, they mature into larger, more durable groups. This discovery offers hope for new drugs, such as the licensed immunotherapy teplizumab, which could delay the disease's onset by allowing beta cells more time to mature.
