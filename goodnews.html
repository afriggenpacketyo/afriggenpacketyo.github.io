El Niño Conditions Expected to Influence 2026 Hurricane Season Patterns

2026-05-15 09:55:00 EDT

The transition into an El Niño climate pattern is anticipated to significantly influence the 2026 hurricane seasons in both the Atlantic and Pacific oceans. El Niño typically fosters warmer ocean waters in the Eastern Pacific, creating favorable conditions for increased hurricane activity and development by reducing vertical wind shear. Conversely, El Niño tends to create cooler waters and more wind shear in the Atlantic basin, statistically leading to a less active hurricane season there. This shift follows a period of La Niña, which was associated with above-average Atlantic hurricane seasons in recent years.
