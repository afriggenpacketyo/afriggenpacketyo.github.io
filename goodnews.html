The Evolving Symbolism of the Statue of Liberty Explored

2025-07-06 09:19:19 EDT

A historical analysis reveals that the Statue of Liberty was not originally intended as a symbol of immigration. Proposed in 1865, it was conceived as a tribute to the end of slavery in the United States, with the statue depicted standing on broken chains and shackles. It was poet Emma Lazarus who, in her 1883 sonnet "The New Colossus," reimagined the statue as a "mother of exiles" welcoming immigrants. This new meaning gained prominence with the opening of Ellis Island in 1892 and was later solidified by President John F. Kennedy's popularization of the phrase "a nation of immigrants."
