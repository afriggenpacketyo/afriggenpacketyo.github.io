Understanding Credit Card Grace Periods

2025-06-25 12:51:58 EDT

A credit card grace period is the time between the end of your billing cycle and your payment due date when you won't be charged interest on new purchases if you pay your full statement balance by the due date. It's essentially a temporary, interest-free loan, typically lasting 21 to 25 days. To maximize the benefits of a grace period, pay your full statement balance by the due date every month, automate payments, track your billing cycle, and avoid cash advances. Understanding and utilizing your credit card's grace period can help you avoid hefty interest charges and make the most of your credit card benefits. (not investment advice)
