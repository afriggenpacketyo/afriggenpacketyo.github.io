Artist JR unveils large-scale installation on Paris's Pont Neuf bridge

2026-06-16 04:03:00 EDT

French street artist JR has opened his latest large-scale installation, titled "La Caverne du Pont Neuf," on Paris's historic Pont Neuf bridge. The artwork, inspired by the quarries from which the bridge's stones were extracted, features images of rugged rock covering the structure. This project is JR's largest to date and aims to transform the way people experience the city, paying homage to the late artists Christo and Jeanne-Claude's "The Pont Neuf Wrapped." The installation is accessible to the public 24 hours a day until June 28, and includes sound design by Thomas Bangalter. JR is known for his large-scale public art projects that engage with architectural landmarks and communities worldwide.

Indonesia struck by 6.7 magnitude earthquake in Central Sulawesi; no immediate reports of major damage

2026-06-16 03:56:00 EDT

A 6.7-magnitude earthquake has struck Indonesia's central Sulawesi Island, according to the U.S. Geological Survey. The shallow quake occurred east-southeast of Palu, the provincial capital, with no immediate reports of casualties or significant damage. While a tsunami risk was ruled out, hospital patients were evacuated as a precautionary measure. The earthquake was followed by several aftershocks. Footage from the affected area has shown some damaged structures, and disaster management agencies are still collecting information on the extent of the impact. Indonesia is situated on the geologically active "Ring of Fire," making it prone to regular seismic activity.

2026-06-16 03:49:20 EDT

The UK government has announced plans to implement a social media ban for individuals under the age of 16, with the regulation set to be introduced by early 2027. This measure aims to enhance online safety for children, with Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer emphasizing its importance in protecting young people. The proposed ban will encompass popular platforms such as TikTok, Snapchat, and X, though a comprehensive list of affected services is yet to be released. The government is also considering additional measures for under-18s, including overnight curfews and restrictions on infinite scrolling. Enforcement will rely on "highly effective age assurance" technologies, and the regulator Ofcom is tasked with studying the best verification methods.
