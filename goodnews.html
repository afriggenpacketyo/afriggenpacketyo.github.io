The Enduring Trend of Cheaper Computers May Be Nearing Its End

2025-12-04 05:00:00 EST

Despite widespread inflation, the price of computers and other electronics has generally decreased over time, a phenomenon largely attributed to Moore's Law. This principle, which observes that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles approximately every two years, has driven down the cost of computing power. However, experts now believe Moore's Law is approaching its physical limits as transistors become so small that further miniaturization is increasingly difficult and costly. This suggests that the era of predictably cheaper and more powerful electronics may be ending, requiring new technological breakthroughs in areas like software efficiency and chip packaging.
