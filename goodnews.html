Viral photos circulating online depict rabbits in Colorado with black, horn-like growths, leading to descriptions like "zombie-rabbits." These growths are real and are caused by the Shope Papilloma Virus, a relatively harmless disease primarily found in the American Midwest. Spread by fleas and ticks, the papillomas typically disappear as the rabbits' immune systems combat the virus. While alarming in appearance, the condition does not usually harm wild bunnies unless it obstructs eating or vision. This natural phenomenon is even believed to have inspired the centuries-old myth of the "jackalope."
