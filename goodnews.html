Nobel Laureate and Physicist Chen-ning Yang Dies at 103

2025-10-18 00:23:49 EDT

Nobel Prize-winning theoretical physicist Chen-ning Yang has died in Beijing at the age of 103 due to sickness. Yang, often considered one of the 20th century's greatest physicists alongside Albert Einstein, was renowned for his co-authorship of the Yang–Mills theory in 1954. This groundbreaking theory provided the mathematical framework for understanding three of nature's four fundamental forces—electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions—in the subatomic world. His work laid the essential foundation for the Standard Model, which is the cornerstone of modern physics, unifying these forces and explaining elementary particle behavior.

2025-10-17 19:58:22 EDT

Stargazers can anticipate a dazzling display next week as the Orionid meteor shower reaches its annual peak around October 22nd, visible until November 7th. Known for their brightness and speed, these meteors originate from debris left by Halley's Comet as Earth passes through its orbit. Traveling at about 41 miles per second, the tiny particles burn up in the atmosphere, creating long streaks of light, with some even appearing as bright "fireballs." Observers can expect around 15 meteors per hour during the peak. For optimal viewing, finding a dark spot away from city lights after midnight and looking towards the constellation Orion is recommended, no binoculars or telescope needed.
