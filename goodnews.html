2025-09-04 16:14:08 EDT

The "$1,000 a month rule" is a guideline designed to help individuals calculate how much they need to save to generate a specific monthly income in retirement. Based on the 4% withdrawal rule, it suggests that for every $1,000 of desired monthly income, a retiree needs between $240,000 and $300,000 in savings. For example, to generate $4,000 per month beyond Social Security, one would need a nest egg of approximately $960,000 to $1.2 million. This framework helps make abstract retirement planning more concrete and underscores the importance of starting to save early. (not investment advice)
