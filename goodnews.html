Filing for Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Possible Even with No Income

2026-01-28 11:16:01 EST

Individuals facing overwhelming debt can file for Chapter 7 bankruptcy even without income, and a lack of income may actually increase eligibility. The "means test" used for Chapter 7 compares income to state median, and those below the threshold automatically qualify. While upfront costs like filing and attorney fees exist, payment installments or waivers may be available for hardship cases. The court also reviews assets and expenses, and unprotected assets could be sold. Other debt relief options like negotiation, debt relief companies, or credit counseling exist, but Chapter 7 provides immediate relief and debt elimination without ongoing payments, making it a viable solution for those with no current income.
