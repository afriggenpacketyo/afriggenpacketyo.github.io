The bill seeks to address the program's outdated benefit levels and resource limits, which have not been meaningfully adjusted since the 1970s, contributing to high poverty rates among beneficiaries. Proposed changes include increasing monthly payments, raising asset limits to allow for savings, and indexing thresholds to inflation.
