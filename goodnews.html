Northern Ireland to Implement Graduated Driver Licensing for Young Drivers

2026-01-26 19:01:23 EST

Northern Ireland is set to become the first UK area to introduce graduated driver licensing for young drivers, aiming to significantly reduce fatal road crashes among 17-23 year olds. These comprehensive reforms, effective in October, will require a six-month wait before a practical test, completion of 14 training modules, and a 24-month restricted period for new drivers. Key restrictions include limiting night-time passengers for new drivers and removing the previous 45mph speed limit. Infrastructure Minister Liz Kimmins and PSNI welcome the changes, believing they will ultimately save lives by providing a more structured and slower learning journey for new drivers.
