Stillaguamish Tribe Restores Wetlands to Revive Salmon Populations and Mitigate Flood Risks

2026-05-03 05:00:00 EDT

The Stillaguamish Tribe in Washington is undertaking a significant environmental restoration project, actively transforming hundreds of acres of farmland back into vital tidal wetlands. This initiative involves purchasing land and strategically removing levees, with the primary goal of reviving endangered Chinook salmon populations. The project is rooted in their ancestral treaty rights to fish and seeks to reverse decades of environmental degradation that have severely impacted salmon runs. Beyond ecological benefits, the restored floodplains also serve a crucial role in mitigating future flood damage to nearby human settlements by allowing river surges to dissipate naturally. This ambitious project, while facing land-use conflicts, represents a powerful blend of cultural preservation and ecological recovery.
