Meteorite Containing 'Alien World Chemistry' Crashes into New Jersey Home

2026-07-16 10:26:00 EDT

A meteorite that struck a New Jersey home in July 2024 has been found to contain prebiotic molecules and other "building blocks of life," including magnesium organic compounds and amino acids. Scientists determined the meteorite is a rare CM1/2 carbonaceous chondrite, making it one of the most scientifically valuable ever recovered. Forensic study revealed the meteorite had been covered in concentrated salty fluids, or brine, on its parent asteroid, indicating the presence of liquid water that later evaporated. This discovery suggests that CM-type carbonaceous chondrite meteorites may have delivered organic materials to early Earth, potentially contributing to the origins of life. Fragments of the meteorite will be preserved at the American Museum of Natural History.
