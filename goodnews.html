Paleontologists Discover 500-Million-Year-Old, Three-Eyed Predator

2025-05-17 16:38:36 EDT

Paleontologists have discovered a 500-million-year-old, three-eyed predator named Mosura fentoni, nicknamed the "sea moth," in the Burgess Shale of the Canadian Rockies. This creature, about the length of an index finger, had a pencil sharpener-like mouth and claws extending from its head. The discovery provides new insights into animal life during the Cambrian period, revealing a more complex structure than previously known in radiodonts. The well-preserved fossil showed the Mosura's open circulatory system and swimming style similar to modern stingrays.
