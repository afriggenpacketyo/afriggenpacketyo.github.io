2025-07-07 23:49:43 EDT

A new clinical trial has demonstrated that a single gene therapy injection can significantly reverse hearing loss in both children and adults with a specific genetic form of deafness. The therapy involves delivering a healthy copy of the OTOF gene to the inner ear, which is crucial for transmitting sound signals to the brain. All ten participants in the small-scale study showed considerable hearing improvement within a month, with one seven-year-old girl regaining almost full hearing. Researchers are hopeful this safe and well-tolerated method can be adapted for other common genetic causes of deafness.
