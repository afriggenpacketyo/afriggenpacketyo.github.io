Indian Researchers Win Ig Nobel Prize for Smelly Shoe Study

2025-09-27 20:25:28 EDT

Two Indian researchers, Vikash Kumar and Sarthak Mittal, have won an Ig Nobel Prize for their study on foul-smelling shoes and their impact on shoe rack user experience. Their research, stemming from observations in university hostels, identified Kytococcus sedentarius bacteria as the primary culprit behind the odor. They discovered that short bursts of ultraviolet light could effectively eliminate the bacteria and the associated smell. This led to the development of a prototype for a UVC light-equipped shoe rack designed not just to store but also to sterilize footwear. The Ig Nobel award, which celebrates unusual but inventive scientific endeavors, has encouraged the researchers to continue exploring overlooked questions in everyday science.
