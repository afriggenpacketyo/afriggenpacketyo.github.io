Genetic Study Fills Gaps in Indigenous Americans' Ancestry

2025-05-17 02:34:00 EDT

A recent DNA study has traced the migration of prehistoric humans from Asia to North and South America, offering insights into the origins of Indigenous groups. The research, published in Science, analyzed genomes from 1,537 individuals across northern Eurasia and the Americas, revealing genetic links between modern Indigenous populations and early settlers. The study also found that early settlers split into isolated groups due to geographical barriers, such as the Amazon rainforest and the Andes, leading to distinct genetic traits, including adaptations to high altitudes. This genetic record is crucial for preserving the legacy of endangered groups like the Kawesqar people of Patagonia.
