Jazz Icon Sonny Rollins, Renowned Saxophonist, Passes Away at 95

2026-05-25 22:50:23 EDT

Sonny Rollins, the legendary tenor saxophonist widely regarded as one of jazz history's most vital and influential artists, has died at his home in Woodstock, N.Y., at the age of 95. Known as "the saxophone colossus," Rollins enjoyed a prolific career spanning over six decades, collaborating with giants like Miles Davis and John Coltrane and releasing over 60 albums. His innovative improvisations, often extended and deeply structural, and his unique interpretations of standard tunes solidified his preeminence in the post-World War II jazz era. Rollins leaves behind a monumental musical legacy, having continued to explore and perform into his 80s before respiratory illness forced his retirement in 2014, with his archives now housed at the Schomburg Center.
