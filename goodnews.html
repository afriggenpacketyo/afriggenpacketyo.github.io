California Residents to Receive Climate Credit on October Electricity Bills

2025-09-25 06:00:00 EDT

Millions of California residents will see a credit on their October electricity bills as part of the state's biannual Climate Credit program. The refund is derived from the state's cap-and-invest program, which requires large greenhouse gas emitters to purchase allowances for their pollution. A portion of the revenue generated from these auctions is then distributed directly to ratepayers. The credit amount varies by utility provider, with customers of PG&E, Southern California Edison, and SDG&E receiving between $56 and $82. This initiative is part of California's broader strategy to combat climate change while mitigating energy costs for consumers.
