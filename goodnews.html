Explainer: Why Widespread Price Decreases Are Rare and Often Signal Economic Trouble

2025-11-20 06:30:00 EST

This article explains that while politicians often promise to lower prices, a general decrease in the overall price level, known as deflation, is rare and typically a sign of a severe economic downturn. Prices for individual goods can fluctuate due to supply and demand, but an economy-wide drop in prices usually indicates collapsing consumer demand, as seen during the Great Depression. Central banks, like the Federal Reserve, intentionally target a small, steady rate of inflation (around 2%) to encourage spending and investment. Therefore, promises to make everything cheaper are generally unrealistic and economically undesirable.
