The finding challenges previous assumptions, as most solar system bodies have not cooled sufficiently fast to form a solid inner core. The distinctive red color is attributed to sunlight reflecting and scattering through Earth's atmosphere, where shorter blue wavelengths disperse more easily, allowing red light to reach the moon.
