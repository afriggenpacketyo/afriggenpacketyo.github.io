Exploring the Origins of "Love" as Zero Points in Tennis Scoring

2026-06-04 05:00:00 EDT

The unique tennis scoring term "love" for zero points has an intriguing, though uncertain, etymology, dating back to the late 18th century and earlier card games. While the sport became official in 1874, its distinct scoring system has largely endured. One prominent theory suggests "love" derives from the French "l'oeuf" (the egg), due to its zero-like shape, similar to "goose egg" in other contexts. A more plausible theory posits it comes from the English idiom "for the love of the game," implying that a player's dedication transcends their score. Despite modern alternatives like "bagel" and "zero," "love" persists, reflecting tennis's deep respect for tradition and unique character.
