Study Suggests Leprosy Existed in the Americas Before European Arrival

2025-06-05 05:30:02 EDT

A new study indicates that leprosy, or Hansen's disease, existed in the Americas long before the arrival of European explorers. Researchers from the Institut Pasteur in Paris found evidence of the bacterium Mycobacterium lepromatosis in ancient human remains from Argentina and Canada, dating back 1,000 years. This discovery challenges the previous belief that leprosy was solely introduced to the Americas by European settlers. The study, which involved collaboration with indigenous communities and various international institutions, analyzed over 800 DNA samples from ancient human remains and recent medical cases showing signs of leprosy. The findings suggest that Mycobacterium lepromatosis spread rapidly throughout the continent and that unknown animal reservoirs may exist.
