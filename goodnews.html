New York City to Use Ranked Choice Voting in Mayoral Primary

2025-06-23 00:04:00 EDT

New York City is implementing ranked choice voting in its Democratic mayoral primary election, a system where voters rank multiple candidates in order of preference. If no candidate receives a majority of first-choice votes, the candidate with the fewest first-choice votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed based on voters' second choices. The process continues until a candidate secures a majority. While proponents say this system prevents wasted votes and ensures broad support for the winner, critics worry about transparency and the potential for unexpected results.
