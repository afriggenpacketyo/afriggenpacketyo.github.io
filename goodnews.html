Financial experts suggest that the best age to take out a reverse mortgage is typically in one's late 60s to early 70s, though the decision depends heavily on individual circumstances. A reverse mortgage allows seniors aged 62 and older to convert home equity into cash without monthly repayments, but the loan balance grows over time. Waiting until an older age generally allows borrowers to access a larger portion of their home's equity, as lenders factor in a shorter life expectancy.
