Historical Context of the U.S. Purchase of Alaska from Russia in 1867

2025-08-12 06:46:14 EDT

As a U.S.-Russia summit is set to take place in Alaska, this article revisits the historical context of the territory's sale in 1867. Facing financial strain after its defeat in the Crimean War, the Russian Empire under Tsar Alexander II sold Alaska to the United States for $7.2 million. At the time, the resource-depleted territory was seen as a liability, and the sale was a strategic move to gain cash and a new ally. The deal, initially mocked in the U.S. as "Seward's Folly," was later vindicated by the discovery of gold and oil, turning it into one of history's greatest bargains and leaving a lasting Russian cultural footprint, such as Orthodox churches, in the state.
