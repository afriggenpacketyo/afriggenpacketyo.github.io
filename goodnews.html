The discovery provides new insights into animal life during the Cambrian period. The creature was equipped with a circular mouth lined with teeth, flaps for swimming, and intimidating claws. The Mosura's body included 16 segments lined with gills, similar to modern arthropods, showcasing its complexity. The fossil also revealed the Mosura's open circulatory system and its swimming style, similar to modern stingrays.
