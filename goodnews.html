China Accelerates Race in Non-Invasive Ultrasound Brain-Computer Interface Technology

2026-03-02 09:18:43 EST

China is rapidly advancing in non-invasive ultrasound brain-computer interface (BCI) technology, with companies like Gestala developing systems to stimulate and study brain activity without surgery. Unlike implant-based BCIs such as Neuralink, ultrasound uses high-frequency sound waves for imaging, tissue destruction, and neural modulation. Gestala's initial focus is chronic pain treatment, with plans for a wearable helmet, and long-term ambitions for depression, Alzheimer's, and stroke rehabilitation. While promising, technical challenges remain, including skull distortion and the slower measurement of blood flow compared to electrical signals, raising new privacy questions for this experimental field.
