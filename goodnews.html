The Importance of the Fiduciary Standard for Trustworthy Financial Guidance

2025-09-24 08:58:09 EDT

To ensure reliable financial advice, individuals should prioritize working with a professional who adheres to a "fiduciary" standard, meaning they are legally bound to act exclusively in the client's best interest. This crucial distinction sets them apart from advisors who only follow a "suitability" standard, where recommendations merely need to be appropriate, not necessarily optimal. Neglecting to engage a fiduciary can lead to detrimental outcomes, such as being steered into high-cost investments or products that primarily benefit the advisor, potentially costing clients tens of thousands of dollars over time. Resources like the SEC's Investment Adviser Public Disclosure site and professional organizations can help identify true fiduciary advisors. (not investment advice)
