This article explains that these unique bonds allow institutional investors to speculate on the occurrence of natural disasters; if a predefined event does not materialize, investors earn a return. However, if specific disaster thresholds are met, funds are directed to insurance companies to cover claims, and investors incur losses. The model is also evolving to directly finance weather resilience upgrades, such as fortified roofs, which demonstrably reduce future damages.
