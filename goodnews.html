Understanding Required Minimum Distributions from Retirement Accounts

2026-05-18 12:35:30 EDT

Retirees with tax-deferred accounts like 401(k)s or traditional IRAs must strategically manage Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) once they reach age 73. The annual RMD amount is calculated by dividing the account balance by a life expectancy factor from the IRS Uniform Lifetime table, meaning the withdrawal increases with age. For a $200,000 account, annual RMDs can range from approximately $7,500 at age 73 to over $9,400 at age 79. Retirees should plan for these distributions and their tax implications, potentially considering diversified investments like gold to offset market volatility. (not investment advice)
