2026-05-17 10:44:00 EDT

Irish researchers have uncovered the oldest surviving English-language poem, "Caedmon's Hymn," within a 9th-century medieval manuscript in a Roman library. This discovery is significant as it places the poem three centuries earlier than previously known copies, shedding new light on the wide diffusion of the English language. The poem, composed in Old English by a Northumbrian agricultural worker in the 7th century, was found within the main body of a Latin text, the "Ecclesiastical History of the English People." The manuscript's complex journey across continents and owners adds to the remarkable nature of its rediscovery.
