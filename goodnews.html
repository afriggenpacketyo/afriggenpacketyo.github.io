Jazz Saxophone Icon Sonny Rollins Passes Away at 95

2026-05-26 05:16:00 EDT

Sonny Rollins, a towering figure in jazz and a legendary tenor saxophonist, has died at 95. Known for his improvisational brilliance and relentless experimentation, Rollins recorded over 60 albums spanning seven decades, defining the bebop era alongside greats like John Coltrane. His career included famous sabbaticals, notably practicing alone on New York's Williamsburg Bridge, which inspired his album "The Bridge." Rollins also contributed to The Rolling Stones' "Tattoo You" and was a vocal advocate for civil rights through his music. He retired from performing in 2014 due to respiratory illness, leaving behind a profound musical legacy.
