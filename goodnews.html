Amid concerns about outliving savings, a retirement income strategy known as the "rule of thirds" is gaining attention as an alternative to traditional guidelines like the 4% rule. This approach involves converting one-third of retirement savings into a guaranteed lifetime income product, such as a fixed annuity, to cover essential expenses. The remaining two-thirds remain invested for growth and flexibility, aiming to provide a balance of security and liquidity. While this strategy can reduce market volatility stress, retirees should consider the trade-offs, including reduced liquidity for the annuitized portion and continued market risk for the invested funds.
