Brain Scans Reveal Structural Differences in Individuals with Psychopathy, Study Finds

2025-07-02 00:47:08 EDT

A new study reveals that individuals diagnosed with psychopathy exhibit shared structural differences in their brains compared to the general population. Researchers found that men with high scores for impulsive and antisocial behavior had significant volume reductions in brain regions associated with behavioral control, emotional processing, and decision-making, such as the pons and thalamus. On average, the brains of psychopathic subjects were found to be about 1.45 percent smaller than those of the control group. These neurological findings could provide a crucial basis for better understanding the biological underpinnings of psychopathy and potentially developing future treatments for the personality disorder.
