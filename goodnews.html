2026-03-06 01:04:05 EST

Scientists have lauded this as an "exceptional" finding of "lazarus taxon," a term used for species that reappear after a prolonged absence from the fossil record. The pygmy long-fingered possum, a striped marsupial, possesses a unique elongated fourth finger for extracting wood-boring insect larvae, its primary food source. The ring-tailed glider, similar to its Australian counterpart, inhabits the hollows of tall trees. This remarkable discovery was made possible through combining fossil analysis, rare photographs, and invaluable insights from local indigenous elders.
