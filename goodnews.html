Understanding the difference between endemic, epidemic, and pandemic diseases

2026-05-11 06:31:00 EDT

The terms endemic, epidemic, and pandemic describe how diseases spread, not necessarily their severity. An endemic disease occurs regularly in a specific region, while an epidemic signifies an unusual increase in cases within a region over a limited time. A pandemic involves the worldwide spread of a disease, requiring international cooperation for control. These distinctions are crucial for understanding public health responses, as highlighted by recent outbreaks and the COVID-19 pandemic.
