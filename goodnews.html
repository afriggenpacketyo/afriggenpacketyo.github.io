New Renters' Rights Bill Becomes Law in England, Overhauling Tenancy Regulations

2025-10-27 14:32:41 EDT

A new law described as the most significant reform to renting in England in three decades has been formally approved, introducing major changes for tenants and landlords. The Renters' Rights Bill abolishes 'no-fault' evictions, requiring landlords to provide a valid reason for repossession, and establishes rolling 'periodic' tenancies instead of fixed-term contracts. The legislation also limits rent increases to once per year, bans rental bidding wars, and makes it illegal for landlords to discriminate against tenants with children or those receiving benefits. These changes will affect nearly 20% of UK households who rent privately.
