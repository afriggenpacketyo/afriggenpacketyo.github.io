2026-07-15 05:24:00 EDT

As many regions, including Germany, face severe drought and dwindling groundwater resources, new strategies for storing water are gaining importance. Groundwater, which accounts for nearly half of the world's drinking water, is being extracted at three times the rate of 50 years ago, exacerbated by climate change and increased paving. Innovations like "Smart Storm Water Storage" and managed aquifer recharge, seen in Munich and California, aim to combine flood protection with drought prevention by channeling purified rainwater into the ground. These methods offer advantages over surface reservoirs, such as reduced evaporation, and help replenish vital water reserves for communities and agriculture.
