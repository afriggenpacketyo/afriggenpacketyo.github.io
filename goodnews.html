France has passed a new sexual consent law, redefining sexual assault as any sexual act occurring without explicit consent. The legislation, approved by the Senate with overwhelming support, mandates that consent must be "freely given, informed, specific, prior and revocable," and cannot be inferred from silence or lack of reaction. This historic step aligns France with other European nations adopting consent-based laws, moving away from a previous definition that required "violence, coercion, threat or surprise." The bill was introduced in January, weeks after convictions in the high-profile sexual assault case of Gisele Pelicot, which highlighted the urgent need for clearer consent definitions and contributed to a significant societal shift towards a culture of consent and greater protection for victims.
