Conversely, at night, when outside air typically cools, opening a window and positioning a fan to draw this cooler air inwards is advised. For personal cooling, it is suggested to direct the fan's airflow broadly across the body, rather than intensely at the face, to optimize the evaporative cooling effect of sweating. The article also debunks the common misconception that fans become ineffective once ambient temperatures exceed 35C, confirming that air movement still significantly aids evaporation at higher temperatures.
