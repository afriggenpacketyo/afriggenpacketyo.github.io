Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly if Not Properly Maintained

2025-06-10 07:30:00 EDT

Hummingbird feeders, intended to aid these tiny birds, can become deadly traps if not properly cleaned and maintained. Spoilage of sugar water in feeders leads to microbial growth, causing infections like candida in hummingbirds, which can lead to tongue swelling and starvation. Wildlife rehabbers report frequently rescuing hummingbirds with feeder-related infections. The solution involves using the correct sugar-to-water ratio (1:4) and cleaning feeders every one to three days, with more frequent cleaning in hot weather, to prevent harmful microbial growth.
