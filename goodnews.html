Understanding the 2-4-6-8 Rule for Subsequent Bankruptcy Filings

2025-12-11 15:42:46 EST

The 2-4-6-8 rule is a guideline that dictates the waiting periods between bankruptcy filings to be eligible for a discharge. It specifies different timeframes based on the type of previous bankruptcy and the intended new filing: 2 years for Chapter 13 to Chapter 13, 4 years for Chapter 7 to Chapter 13, 6 years for Chapter 13 to Chapter 7, and 8 years for Chapter 7 to Chapter 7. This rule is crucial for individuals who need to file for bankruptcy more than once, as it prevents immediate repeat filings without attempting alternative debt relief solutions. Consulting a bankruptcy attorney is recommended to understand the specific implications for individual cases, as the rule primarily governs discharge eligibility rather than the filing itself.
