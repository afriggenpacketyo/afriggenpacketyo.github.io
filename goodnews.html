Unusual Atmospheric Patterns Prevented Any Hurricanes From Making US Landfall This Season

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

For the first time in a decade, the Atlantic hurricane season is concluding without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States. Meteorologists attribute this unusual outcome to a persistent atmospheric pattern known as "recurvature," where storms veered northward and out to sea before reaching the coast. This pattern was caused by a recurring area of low pressure over the eastern U.S., which weakened the western edge of the Bermuda High pressure system. This effectively created a steering current that deflected multiple storms, including four major hurricanes, away from the U.S. coastline, a phenomenon that experts say characterized the entire season.
