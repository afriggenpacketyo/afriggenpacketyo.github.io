The Historic Introduction of Penalty Shootouts in Football

2026-03-28 02:15:22 EDT

This article recounts the world's first official penalty shootout in football, which took place on August 5, 1970, during a Watney Cup match between Hull City and Manchester United. Previously, tied matches were settled by replays, coin tosses, or drawing lots, a system deemed "immoral and cruel" by Israeli FA officials Yosef Dagan and Michael Almog, who proposed the shootout to FIFA in 1969. The historic event saw legendary players like George Best score, while Denis Law became the first to miss and goalkeeper Ian McKechnie the first to save and then famously miss a penalty, forever changing how tied games are decided.
