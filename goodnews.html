Tropical Storms Humberto and Imelda May Interact in Rare Fujiwhara Effect

2025-09-25 14:42:41 EDT

Meteorologists are monitoring Tropical Storm Humberto and a nearby developing system, likely to be named Imelda, for a potential interaction known as the Fujiwhara effect. This rare phenomenon occurs when two tropical cyclones get close enough to orbit a common center, which can alter their paths and intensity. While experts consider a full merger unlikely due to the size difference between the storms, the possibility of them "dancing" around each other exists. Humberto is expected to strengthen into a hurricane over the Atlantic, but the potential interaction with the second, weaker storm adds a layer of complexity to forecasting its ultimate track.
