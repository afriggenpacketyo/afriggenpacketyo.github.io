Understanding Social Security Protections Against Debt Garnishment

2026-05-07 14:09:27 EDT

Many older Americans are carrying significant debt into retirement, leading to concerns about the security of their Social Security income. This article clarifies that federal law generally protects Social Security retirement or disability benefits from garnishment by most private creditors, including those for credit card, medical, and unsecured personal loan debts. While creditors may still pursue lawsuits and attempt to levy bank accounts, federal rules typically shield up to two months' worth of electronically deposited Social Security benefits. However, crucial exceptions exist: federal debts like taxes and student loans, as well as child support and alimony, can lead to garnishment, underscoring the importance of understanding these distinctions for retirees managing their finances.
