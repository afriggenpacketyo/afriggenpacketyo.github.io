The Word 'OK' Traces Its Origins to a 19th-Century Joke and a Presidential Campaign

2025-07-23 17:27:00 EDT

The globally recognized word "OK" has a uniquely American origin, starting as a joke in the 1830s and popularized by a presidential campaign. Scholars trace its first appearance to a Boston newspaper in 1839 as a playful, misspelled abbreviation of "all correct" ("oll korrect"). The term gained widespread use during President Martin Van Buren's 1840 reelection campaign, where his nickname "Old Kinderhook" conveniently aligned with the abbreviation, leading to the formation of "O.K. Clubs." From this political beginning, the simple and versatile word spread worldwide, becoming a universal term for agreement and understanding.
