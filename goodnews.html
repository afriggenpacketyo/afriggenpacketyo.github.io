China Achieves Tamper-Proof Quantum Communication Over 100km Using Single Atoms

2026-02-05 14:00:10 EST

Chinese researchers have successfully demonstrated tamper-proof quantum communication over 100 kilometers using single atoms, marking a significant breakthrough in quantum key distribution (QKD). This innovative method, known as device-independent QKD (DI-QKD), establishes secure quantum links between atoms using single light particles, or photons, to generate identical encryption keys. Its inherent security is derived from the quantum-mechanical behavior of entangled atoms, making it robust even if the communication devices are flawed or have been tampered with. This study helps bridge the gap between theoretical proof-of-principle experiments and practical, real-world applications for highly secure communication systems.
