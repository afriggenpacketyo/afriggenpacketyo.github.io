Exercise Shown to Improve Cancer Survival Rates

2025-06-02 06:04:32 EDT

A major international trial has demonstrated that an exercise program for colon cancer patients can reduce the risk of dying by a third. The three-year program, which started soon after chemotherapy, aimed to have participants exercise at double the recommended amount for the general population, such as brisk walking for 45-60 minutes three to four times a week. Results showed that 80% of people exercising remained cancer-free after five years, compared to 74% in the other group, with a 37% lower risk of death eight years after initial treatment. Scientists are now investigating whether similar exercise regimes could improve survival for people with other diseases, such as breast cancer.
