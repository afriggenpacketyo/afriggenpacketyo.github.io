The Evolution of "Synergy": From Religious Debates to Corporate Buzzword

Its earliest appearances trace back to religious texts and 17th-century Christian theological debates, later transitioning into 19th-century medicine and toxicology to describe cooperative effects. Mid-20th century saw its application in psychology and by intellectuals like Buckminster Fuller, who explored its implications in group dynamics and geometry. However, its widespread adoption as a ubiquitous corporate buzzword during the 1980s and 90s, particularly within the context of business mergers and acquisitions, ultimately led to its overuse and a consequent dilution of its precise meaning, cementing its status as "the ultimate buzzword."
