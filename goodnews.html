Aviation Pioneer Wally Funk Dies at 87, Celebrated for Breaking Barriers

2026-07-09 18:48:10 EDT

Wally Funk, a pioneering aviator and the oldest woman to journey into space, has died peacefully at 87 in Grapevine, Texas. Despite her exceptional performance in the 1961 Mercury 13 program, she was initially denied the opportunity to become a NASA astronaut due to gender restrictions. Funk dedicated over seven decades to aviation, serving as a flight instructor, the first female FAA inspector, and an air safety investigator. Her lifelong ambition was fulfilled in 2021 when she flew with Jeff Bezos on Blue Origin, becoming a global symbol of perseverance and inspiring countless individuals, particularly young women, in science and exploration.
