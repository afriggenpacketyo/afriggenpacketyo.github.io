Aligning Exercise with Body Clock May Boost Health Benefits, Study Suggests

2026-04-14 19:54:08 EDT

Health researchers suggest that synchronizing exercise with an individual's natural body clock, or chronotype, could enhance health benefits, particularly for those at risk of heart disease. A study in Pakistan found that participants who exercised according to their chronotype (morning for 'larks,' evening for 'night owls') showed greater improvements in sleep quality, blood pressure, and metabolic markers compared to those who exercised at non-optimal times. The findings indicate that a 'one-size-fits-all' approach to exercise timing is less effective, and mismatches between biological and social schedules, known as 'social jetlag,' can increase health risks.
