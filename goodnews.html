2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team in China has become the first to directly observe and measure friction between solid materials at the quantum level. Using folded graphene, the scientists discovered that, contrary to classical physics, friction did not always increase as the material got thicker; in some cases, thicker folds actually slid more easily. The team traced this unusual effect to how the material's bending changed electron movement, locking them into fixed energy levels and reducing the conversion of motion into heat. This breakthrough could aid in the design of ultra-efficient, durable nanodevices and advanced materials.
