2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Astronomers have for the first time observed the earliest signs of rocky planet formation around a young, sun-like star named HOPS-315, located about 1,370 light-years away. Using the James Webb Space Telescope and ALMA array, researchers detected condensing silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas within the star's protoplanetary disk. This discovery, published in *Nature*, offers an unprecedented glimpse into the cosmic nursery where Earth-like planets are born, suggesting this process may be common. The findings could help determine how frequently Earth-like worlds occur in the universe.
