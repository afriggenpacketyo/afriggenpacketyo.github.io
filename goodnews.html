Robot Discovers Ancient Artifacts in France's Deepest Shipwreck

2026-04-29 14:39:51 EDT

A remotely operated underwater robot has discovered cannons and ceramics dating back five centuries from what is believed to be France's deepest shipwreck, located off the coast of Ramatuelle. The 16th-century merchant vessel, accidentally found during a seabed survey, was reportedly sailing from northern Italy laden with ceramics. The French navy and the culture ministry's underwater archaeology department are conducting missions to inspect the artifacts at a depth of over 1.5 miles.\n\nNicknamed "Camarat 4," the wreckage site is being kept secret by the navy. The robot, equipped with cameras and pincers, has captured extensive footage and is being used to create a 3D model of the site. Archaeologists are excited by the clarity of the water, which allows for excellent visibility of the findings, including hundreds of pitchers and plates adorned with various motifs.\n\nThese discoveries are considered invaluable for understanding 16th-century trade routes and maritime history, as detailed textual records from that period are scarce. The careful recovery of artifacts, like a jug that was gently lifted by the robot, is crucial to preserving these historical treasures. This mission represents one of the deepest artifact recoveries ever made from a shipwreck in French waters.
