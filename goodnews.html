DNA Test Could Spare Millions of Breast Cancer Patients from Chemotherapy

2026-05-30 09:14:42 EDT

An international study, spearheaded by University College London, indicates that millions of breast cancer patients could safely avoid chemotherapy due to a newly developed DNA test called Prosigna. This innovative test measures the activity of 50 genes linked to breast cancer growth, effectively identifying patients who would achieve similar outcomes with hormone therapy alone. The trial, encompassing over 4,000 participants, demonstrated a 93.7% five-year survival rate for the two-thirds of patients who skipped chemotherapy, aligning closely with those who received it. These "practice-changing" findings, to be presented at a major cancer conference, offer significant hope for reducing the burden of chemotherapy's severe side effects for a large patient population.
