NASA Van Allen Probe Safely Re-enters Earth's Atmosphere After Extended Mission

Most of the probe was expected to burn up during re-entry, with NASA assessing a low, one-in-4,200 chance of harm from any surviving components. The spacecraft, along with its twin, was part of a mission to gather unprecedented data on Earth's two permanent radiation belts, known as the Van Allen belts. Originally designed for two years, the mission extended to nearly seven, providing crucial insights into how these belts shield Earth from harmful cosmic radiation, solar storms, and streaming solar wind.
