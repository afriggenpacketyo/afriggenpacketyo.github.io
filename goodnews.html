2026-01-20 20:19:40 EST

A significant UK study, ELSA, indicates that universal screening for type 1 diabetes in children, using a simple finger-prick blood test, could be highly effective. Study Suggests Diverse Exercise Routines Lead to Longer, Healthier Lives

2026-01-20 20:17:52 EST

A 30-year study of 110,000 Americans suggests that engaging in a variety of physical activities, rather than focusing on just one, is key to boosting health and living longer. Researchers found that active individuals who diversified their exercise routines were 19% less likely to die during the study period. While the total amount of exercise remains important, combining activities with complementary benefits, such as resistance training and aerobic exercise, offers enhanced health outcomes. Experts recommend adults aim for strengthening activities at least twice a week and 150 minutes of moderate or 75 minutes of vigorous activity, spread across several days.
