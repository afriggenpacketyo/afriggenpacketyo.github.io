Oxfordshire dinosaur trackway identified as world's longest continuous sauropod site

2026-06-10 01:03:32 EDT

Researchers have identified a dinosaur trackway in Oxfordshire, consisting of 200 footprints dating back 166 million years, as the largest of its kind globally. The site at Dewars Farm Quarry reveals the movements of Cetiosaurus, a large sauropod, and potentially other dinosaur species like Megalosaurus. Paleontologists suggest the varied sizes of the sauropod tracks could indicate a family herd or individuals of different ages. The discovery provides significant insights into the prehistoric environment of Oxfordshire, which was once a tropical landscape with islands, and offers clues about predator-prey interactions.
