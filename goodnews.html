Psychologists Explain Why Sexual Desire Often Wanes in Long-Term Relationships

2025-10-19 00:56:00 EDT

It is common for sexual frequency and desire to decrease after the initial passionate phase of a long-term relationship, according to psychologists. This shift is partly hormonal, as the bonding hormone oxytocin, which becomes more prominent over time, can dampen the hormones that drive initial sex drive. Research also highlights a discrepancy between physical and psychological arousal, particularly in heterosexual women, whose satisfaction is often not prioritized. Experts emphasize that open communication about needs, maintaining physical affection, and prioritizing intimacy are key for couples to navigate this natural change and maintain a satisfying relationship.
