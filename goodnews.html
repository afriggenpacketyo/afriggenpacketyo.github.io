Businesses Employ 'Tariff Engineering' to Avoid Higher Duties

2025-06-18 03:56:58 EDT

Companies are increasingly using "tariff engineering" to skirt higher duties imposed by the U.S. government by altering materials or dimensions of products to fit into lower-tariff categories. This practice involves strategic design choices to reclassify products under different harmonized system codes, which can result in significant duty savings. Examples include adding cubic zirconia to lapel pins to classify them as jewelry and adding fuzzy-felt fabric to sneaker soles to classify them as slippers. While generally legal, companies must avoid fraudulent misrepresentation of product classifications, as illustrated by Ford's case involving Transit Connect vans.
