Understanding and Reducing "Phantom Energy" to Save Money and Emissions

2025-09-03 11:04:07 EDT

"Phantom energy," also known as vampire energy, describes the electricity consumed by electronic devices when they are plugged in but not actively in use, contributing to approximately 5% to 10% of total home energy consumption. This often-overlooked drain on power grids contributes to climate change by increasing demand for electricity, often sourced from power plants that release planet-warming emissions. Experts identify smart televisions and appliances with constant displays, like microwaves, as significant culprits, consuming substantial energy even in standby mode. Simple, actionable steps such as unplugging chargers once devices are fully charged or disabling unnecessary smart features can lead to significant reductions in both emissions and utility costs, fostering a collective shift towards more sustainable daily habits.
