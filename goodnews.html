Google's Debug Program Proposes Releasing Sterile Mosquitoes to Combat Disease

2026-06-03 09:26:00 EDT

Google's Debug program plans to release millions of male mosquitoes infected with Wolbachia bacteria in Florida and California to combat disease-spreading Aedes aegypti mosquitoes. These sterile males mate with wild females, preventing eggs from hatching and effectively reducing the next generation's population without increasing human bites. Scientists advocate for this intervention, as Aedes aegypti is an invasive species responsible for diseases like dengue, yellow fever, and Zika, posing a significant global health risk. The program aims to shrink the disease-carrying mosquito population, with similar efforts by the World Mosquito Program showing success in reducing outbreaks in other regions.
