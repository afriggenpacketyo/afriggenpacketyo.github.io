Effective Parenting Involves Avoiding Certain Phrases

2025-06-22 19:56:15 EDT

According to research on parent-child relationships, parents who have an easy time getting their children to listen avoid threats, bribes, or harsh consequences. Instead, they use language that encourages cooperation by honoring a child's autonomy while setting boundaries. Phrases like "Because I said so," "If you don't listen, you'll lose [X privilege]," and "Stop crying. You're fine" can trigger a child's fight-or-flight response. Alternative phrases that promote respect, emotional safety, and involvement can lead to better communication and cooperation, such as "I know you don't like this decision.
