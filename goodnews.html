Russia and Ukraine Agree to Prisoner Exchange After Negotiations

2025-06-02 11:03:00 EDT

Ukraine and Russia have reached an agreement to exchange prisoners of war, focusing on those severely wounded, under the age of 25, and the remains of deceased soldiers, following negotiations in Istanbul. Ukraine's Defense Minister Rustem Umerov stated that the agreement includes an all-for-all exchange of seriously wounded and sick prisoners, as well as young soldiers aged 18 to 25, and the return of 6,000 bodies of fallen soldiers. Vladimir Medinsky, the lead Russian negotiator, confirmed the agreement and indicated Russia plans to swap at least 1,000 soldiers. Russia also proposed a two to three-day ceasefire in specific areas to allow commanders to collect the bodies of their soldiers.
