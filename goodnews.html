Northern Lights Visible Across Much of the UK Following Strong Solar Storm

2025-09-02 02:02:00 EDT

The Northern Lights, or aurora borealis, were observed across large parts of the UK on Monday night, providing a spectacular display from northern Scotland down to Norfolk and Kent. This rare and widespread visibility was attributed to a strong solar storm, specifically a coronal mass ejection from the Sun, which caused increased solar energy to interact with Earth's oxygen and nitrogen. Space weather forecasters had accurately predicted the aurora's appearance, confirming a "moderate geomagnetic storm" (G2) in the early hours of Tuesday. The Met Office Space Weather Centre suggests the solar activity could intensify further to a G4 Severe storm on Tuesday night, significantly increasing the chance for the aurora to be visible across the entire UK, potentially even with the naked eye.
