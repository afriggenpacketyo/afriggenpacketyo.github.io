Exploring the Surprising and Evolving History of the Word 'Dude'

2025-07-30 05:00:00 EDT

The term "dude" has a rich history that traces back to the 1880s in New York City, where it was used to describe overly fashionable, effeminate young men. The word likely referenced "Yankee Doodle" and was popularized by a poem meant to mock the trend. It later evolved to describe East Coast city dwellers visiting western "dude ranches" in elaborate cowboy attire. By the mid-20th century, the term was adopted by Black and Mexican American communities as a marker of solidarity before entering mainstream surfer and skater culture, eventually becoming a widely used, gender-neutral term of familiarity.
