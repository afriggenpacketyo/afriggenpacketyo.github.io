Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A new Chinese fossil find offers crucial insights into the evolutionary transition of birds from their dinosaur ancestors, specifically how they shed their long tails. Researchers published in Science Advances explain that modern birds possess a pygostyle, a fused tail bone structure essential for flight-adapted tail feathers, unlike most non-avian dinosaurs. This discovery helps overcome the 'exceeding rarity' of early-diverging bird and birdlike dinosaur fossils, shedding light on the profound morphological changes involved in developing the flight-adapted bird body plan. The findings demonstrate a step-by-step process in the evolution of this aerodynamically favorable structure.
