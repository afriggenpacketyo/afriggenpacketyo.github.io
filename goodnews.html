2025-07-08 10:18:00 EDT

Astronomers have identified a third known interstellar object, a comet named 3I/ATLAS, that has entered our solar system, but they assure it poses no danger to Earth. The comet, estimated to be about 20 kilometers wide, was spotted by a telescope in Chile and is currently 670 million kilometers from the sun. At its closest approach in October, it will still be more than 1.5 times the distance from the Earth to the sun. Scientists will continue to monitor the object to learn more about its composition and origins from outside our solar system.
