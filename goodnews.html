Understanding Jewish Funeral Traditions and Mourning Rituals

2025-10-15 10:07:00 EDT

Jewish mourning rituals primarily focus on eternal memory among the living, with less emphasis on an afterlife. Jewish law mandates quick, simple burials, with bodies given a ritual bath and placed in plain wooden caskets or shrouds, without embalming. The article explains that close family members perform K'riah, a ritual tearing of clothing, as an expression of pain. After burial, a seven-day period of intense mourning, called shiva, is observed, during which mourners refrain from work and grooming. The communal Mourner's Kaddish prayer, recited with a minyan, provides support and honors the deceased's memory.
