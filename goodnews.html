FDA Encourages Bayesian Methods in Drug Trials to Accelerate Development

2026-01-21 08:30:00 EST

The FDA is now encouraging drug companies to use Bayesian statistical methods in clinical trials, a significant shift from the traditional "blank slate" approach. For over 60 years, trials had to independently prove a drug's efficacy, ignoring prior research. Bayesian methods allow formal incorporation of existing knowledge from earlier studies, related drugs, or real-world evidence, which is crucial for rare diseases with limited patient populations, or for testing drugs in children after adult trials. This change promises to make drug development faster and more efficient, as demonstrated by the HEALEY ALS Platform Trial, which tested multiple drugs simultaneously.

This obituary commemorates Claudette Colvin, a pivotal yet under-recognized figure in the U.S. civil rights movement, who has died aged 86. In March 1955, at 15, Colvin was arrested for refusing to give up her bus seat to a white passenger in Montgomery, Alabama, nine months before Rosa Parks' more famous protest. She later became one of four plaintiffs in Browder v Gayle, the federal lawsuit that successfully challenged bus segregation laws. Despite her groundbreaking act and legal courage, Colvin faced societal judgment and limited opportunities, eventually moving to New York and working as a nurse's aide. Her role received greater recognition in later life, with her juvenile record expunged in 2021.
