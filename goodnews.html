Buried as an unknown, Duquesne's remains were disinterred in 2011, but DNA and dental records yielded no match. Investigators successfully used craniofacial superimposition, comparing his wartime photo to the skull, with a distinctive smile and shadow providing "compelling evidence." This case underscores the value of refining older forensic techniques and bringing closure to families of fallen soldiers, even after many years.
