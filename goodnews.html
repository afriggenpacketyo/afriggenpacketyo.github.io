Ancient Western Xia Dynasty Tombs in China Added to UNESCO World Heritage List

2025-07-12 05:00:08 EDT

A vast complex of imperial tombs from the Western Xia dynasty (1038–1227) has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage site. Located near Yinchuan in northwest China, the 40-square-kilometer site includes nine imperial mausoleums and over 270 subordinate tombs belonging to the Tangut people. The World Heritage Committee recognized the site as a significant testament to the cultural fusion and interaction among diverse traditions along the ancient Silk Road. The protected area also contains extensive historical flood control structures and a large architectural complex used for worship and site management.
