2026-02-05 06:00:00 EST

Traditionally, one growth ring per year was assumed for dinosaurs, similar to tree rings. However, researchers found up to five growth marks in two-year-old crocodiles, suggesting that some dinosaur specimens might have been younger than previously thought. This finding, alongside similar results in other reptiles and birds, indicates that these marks may represent cycles of growth rather than strict annual increments, prompting a cautionary tale for paleontologists.
