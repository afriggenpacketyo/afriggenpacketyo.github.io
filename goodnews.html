Rembrandt's 'Young Lion Resting' Sells for $18 Million to Benefit Big Cat Conservation

2026-02-07 04:00:57 EST

A rare chalk drawing by Rembrandt, "Young Lion Resting," fetched a record-setting $18 million at a Sotheby's auction in New York City, with all proceeds dedicated to big cat conservation. Billionaire philanthropist Thomas Kaplan and his associate Jon Ayers, who sold the drawing, are channeling the funds to their charity, Panthera, which focuses on protecting 40 species of wild cats and their habitats. This sale shattered the previous record for a Rembrandt drawing at auction, underscoring the significant value placed on both art and wildlife preservation. The 17th-century work, depicting a leashed lion, offers a unique glimpse into Rembrandt's artistic genius and commitment to a vital cause.
