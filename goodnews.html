Study Reveals Immigrants Face Significant Disadvantages in Germany's Housing Market

2026-05-16 09:59:00 EDT

A recent study by Germany's Expert Council on Integration and Migration (SVR) reveals that immigrants and individuals with immigrant backgrounds face significant disadvantages in the nation's housing market. Amidst a shortage of 1.4 million affordable apartments, these groups are more likely to live in smaller, often overcrowded rental units and are less likely to be homeowners, allocating a greater portion of their income to rent. Structural challenges such as lower incomes and language barriers are exacerbated by racial discrimination, as illustrated by a court case where a woman was denied an apartment viewing due to her name. The SVR recommends expanding social housing and implementing anonymous application processes to combat these disparities.
