Kanchha Sherpa, the last surviving member of the historic 1953 expedition that first successfully scaled Mount Everest, has died in Kathmandu at the age of 92. Sherpa was just 19 when he joined the 35-member team led by Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay as a porter. Despite lacking prior mountaineering experience, he played a crucial role, carrying supplies up to base camp, and was one of only three Sherpas to ascend past 8,000 meters. His passing marks the end of an era for those directly involved in one of humanity's greatest exploratory achievements, leaving behind a legacy of endurance and contribution to mountaineering history.
