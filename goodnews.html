Bernard LaFayette, Key Selma Voting Rights Organizer, Dies at 85

2026-03-06 19:17:58 EST

Bernard LaFayette, a pivotal figure in the Selma voting rights campaign that led to the 1965 Voting Rights Act, has died at 85. As director of the Alabama Voter Registration Campaign, LaFayette quietly laid the groundwork in Selma, building local leadership and momentum despite initial skepticism. He was a co-founder of SNCC and a participant in the Freedom Rides, enduring beatings and an assassination attempt, always adhering to nonviolence. LaFayette later became a global advocate for nonviolence, working with Martin Luther King Jr. and pursuing his mission to institutionalize the movement internationally.
