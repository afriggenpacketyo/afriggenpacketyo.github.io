This maneuver, often psychologically damaging for the player on the receiving end, has historical roots in the 17th-century Dutch spice trade and 19th-century American peddlers notorious for selling fake wooden nutmegs, thus associating the term with trickery. Although linguists identify a notable century-long gap in its usage before its resurgence in soccer in the late 1960s, the connotation of outsmarting an opponent endured.
