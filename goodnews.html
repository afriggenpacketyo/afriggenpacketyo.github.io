2025-11-18 02:00:08 EST

A Chinese research team has developed a highly efficient iron-based nanoparticle catalyst that converts syngas, derived from coal, into olefins. This breakthrough process couples two reactions, yielding various carbon-based compounds essential for pharmaceuticals, plastics, and other synthetics, without relying on petroleum. Published in the journal Science, this innovation significantly enhances the hydrogen atom economy for syngas conversion. Olefins, such as ethylene and propylene, are crucial building blocks typically produced from petroleum or methanol, often with limited efficiency and substantial carbon dioxide by-products. This new method offers a more sustainable alternative for producing these vital industrial chemicals.
