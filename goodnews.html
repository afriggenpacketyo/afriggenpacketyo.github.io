Navigating Life Insurance Options for Seniors as Age Limits and Costs Rise

2025-10-30 16:17:46 EDT

As life expectancies increase, more seniors are considering life insurance, but they face narrowing options and rising costs as they age. While there is no universal age cutoff, most insurers stop offering term life policies around age 75 or 80, and permanent policies like whole life are typically available up to age 85 or 90. For older adults, policies such as final expense or guaranteed issue insurance become more common, offering smaller coverage amounts for funeral costs and small debts without extensive medical exams. Experts advise seniors to shop around, consider their specific needs, and apply as early as possible to secure affordable coverage.
