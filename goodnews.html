2025-09-21 00:00:13 EDT

Published in the peer-reviewed National Science Review, this finding challenges the long-held conventional wisdom that the moon's internal geological processes had largely ceased. By comparing numerous before-and-after images of the moon's least stable regions, researchers confirmed that lunar seismic activity, or moonquakes, are actively triggering these landslides.
