Often ranked alongside Albert Einstein, Yang was a towering figure in 20th-century physics, whose contributions fundamentally reshaped our understanding of the universe. In 1954, he co-authored the groundbreaking Yang–Mills theory with Robert Mills, which described how the electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions operate in the subatomic world.
