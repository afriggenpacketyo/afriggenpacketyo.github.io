Six subtle signs of identity theft that may go unnoticed

2026-06-02 13:00:12 EDT

Identity theft often begins with subtle signs that can be easily overlooked before significant damage occurs. These include small, recurring credit card charges that may be test transactions, missing mail indicating a change of address by a thief, and unexpected IRS letters suggesting someone has used your Social Security number. Changes in credit reports, such as new accounts or inquiries you didn't authorize, are also major red flags. Additionally, receiving Explanation of Benefits statements for medical care you never received or unrequested multifactor authentication prompts can signal compromised accounts. Early detection through vigilance and security measures is crucial for mitigating the impact of identity theft.
