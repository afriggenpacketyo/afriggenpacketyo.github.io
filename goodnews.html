Mutual Funds vs. ETFs: Understanding the Differences

2025-06-12 10:46:07 EDT

Exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and mutual funds are common ways to invest in a diversified basket of stocks or bonds and both can be actively or passively managed. ETFs are traded like individual stocks, and prices fluctuate during trading hours while mutual funds must be purchased and sold directly and are priced at the end of the market day. ETFs tend to have lower expense ratios than mutual funds because when investors buy mutual fund shares, there's more work that goes into managing the transaction, which leads to higher costs for the mutual fund company. It's important to consider fund costs since they can ultimately take a bite out of overall returns. (not investment advice)
