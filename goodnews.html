UK Competition Watchdog Recommends Vets Publish Prices Amid Soaring Costs

2025-10-15 02:39:27 EDT

The UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has proposed that veterinary practices be mandated to publish price lists and disclose ownership by larger groups. This recommendation follows a CMA investigation that found pet owners pay 16.6% more at large group-owned vets, attributing soaring prices—up 63% in seven years—to a lack of competition. The CMA's provisional findings also suggest price caps on medicines and a ban on bonuses tied to specific treatments. The regulator argues the current system, established in 1966, fails to regulate the businesses owning most practices, leading to insufficient transparency for consumers.
