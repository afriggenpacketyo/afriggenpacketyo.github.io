Experimental Drug Nearly Doubles Survival Rates for Advanced Pancreatic Cancer Patients

2026-06-01 20:08:49 EDT

A new experimental drug, daraxonrasib, has shown significant promise by nearly doubling overall survival rates for patients with advanced pancreatic cancer, a notoriously deadly disease. The daily pill blocks a mutated protein driving tumor growth in over 90% of cases, a target previously considered "undruggable." In a study, patients on daraxonrasib lived a median of 13.2 months compared to 6.7 months for those on chemotherapy, experiencing fewer severe side effects and improved quality of life. The FDA plans to expedite review and has allowed early access, offering a turning point for pancreatic cancer treatment.
