WHO Approves First Malaria Drug Specifically Designed for Infants

2026-04-24 19:30:00 EDT

The World Health Organization (WHO) has approved the first malaria drug specifically formulated for infants weighing less than 5 kilograms, a significant breakthrough ahead of World Malaria Day. This new combination of artemether and lumefantrine addresses a critical gap, as babies previously relied on drugs for older children, risking dosing errors and toxicity. The approval enables countries to authorize the drug and UN agencies to distribute it in malaria-endemic areas, particularly Sub-Saharan Africa, which accounts for 90% of cases. This development offers hope in combating a disease that disproportionately affects children under five and causes millions of cases annually.
