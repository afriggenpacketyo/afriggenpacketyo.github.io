New DNA Study Suggests Horses Originated in North America, Reached Europe via China

2026-06-19 23:22:54 EDT

A groundbreaking fossil DNA study challenges long-held beliefs about equine origins, suggesting that horses actually originated in North America millions of years ago, rather than being a European import to the Americas. The research indicates that an extinct lineage, known as the Dalian horse from northeastern China, served as a crucial "genetic middleman." This Dalian horse, possessing distinctive American ancestry, transmitted its genetic heritage to ancient horse populations in Siberia. Subsequently, these Siberian bloodlines then contributed to the genetic makeup of modern European horses, redefining their historical migratory path.
