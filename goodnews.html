Understanding the Rules for Filing a Tax Extension

2025-07-21 11:37:28 EDT

Filing for a tax extension with the IRS provides an additional six months to submit your tax return, but it does not delay the deadline for payment. Taxpayers must still estimate their tax liability and pay what they owe by the original April 15 deadline to avoid penalties and interest. The late payment penalty is typically 0.5% of the unpaid amount per month, while interest accrues daily on the outstanding balance.
