Study Suggests Weight Loss Drugs May Help Prevent Obesity-Related Cancers

2025-05-22 17:00:03 EDT

A new study suggests that GLP-1 agonists, commonly known as weight loss drugs, may modestly reduce the risk of obesity-related cancers, particularly colorectal cancer. The observational study compared the effects of GLP-1 drugs, like Ozempic and Wegovy, to dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DDP-4) inhibitors in adults with obesity and diabetes. Researchers found that patients taking GLP-1 receptor agonists had a 7% lower risk of developing an obesity-related cancer and an 8% lower risk of death from any cause. The authors hope to continue their research with larger populations and those without diabetes.
