Medical experts describe CVI as a common circulatory condition, affecting up to 40% of the U.S. population, where damaged veins in the legs struggle to return blood to the heart. This can lead to symptoms like varicose veins, swelling, and in severe cases, skin discoloration or ulcers. Treatment is typically non-surgical, focusing on compression stockings, exercise like walking to strengthen calf muscles, and lifestyle changes to manage the benign condition.
