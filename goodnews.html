South African Scientists Use Radioactive Isotopes in Rhino Horns to Combat Poaching

2025-08-01 01:20:58 EDT

A new anti-poaching initiative in South Africa, known as the Rhisotope Project, is embedding radioactive material into rhino horns to deter trafficking. Launched by the University of the Witwatersrand and conservation partners, the project injects a small, harmless amount of radioactive isotope into the horns. This substance is undetectable to the rhino but can be easily identified by radiation detectors at borders and airports, making the horns a significant liability for smugglers. The innovative method aims to disrupt the black market and protect the country's declining rhino population, which is heavily targeted by poachers.
