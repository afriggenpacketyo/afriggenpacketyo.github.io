Chinese Researchers Achieve 100-Fold AI Speed Boost with Optical Interconnect System

2026-07-13 02:00:12 EDT

Researchers at Peking University in China have unveiled a groundbreaking all-optical interconnect system that significantly boosts artificial intelligence (AI) distributed inference speeds by over 100 times, while remarkably utilizing only one-ninth of the conventional computational resources. Published in the journal National Science Review, this innovative system integrates standard electronic chips, specifically field-programmable gate array (FPGA) devices, with highly optimized algorithms. The core of this breakthrough lies in two custom-designed communication hardware components, including a silicon photonic transceiver chip operating at 400 gigabits per second, which efficiently converts electrical signals to optical and vice versa. This development presents a radically different and highly energy-efficient approach to scaling AI computational power, moving beyond the traditional reliance on ever-increasing numbers of GPUs and massive data centers.
