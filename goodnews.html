Astronomers Witness Earliest Stages of Rocky Planet Formation for the First Time

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb Space Telescope, astronomers have for the first time directly observed the initial stages of rocky planet formation around a young, sun-like star named HOPS-315. The discovery provides an unprecedented look into a process similar to how Earth may have formed over 4.5 billion years ago. Researchers detected silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing in the star's surrounding gas disk, the fundamental building blocks of terrestrial planets. This observation helps confirm that the conditions for creating Earth-like worlds may be a common occurrence in the universe's earliest phases of star system development.
