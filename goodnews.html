Study Suggests Vitamin A Byproduct May Impair Immune Response to Cancer

2026-01-20 15:06:23 EST

New research indicates that a byproduct derived from vitamin A, retinoic acid, may inadvertently hinder the immune system's ability to combat cancer. Published in Nature Immunology, the study suggests that blocking the effects of this substance could potentially enhance existing cancer immunotherapy treatments. Scientists at Princeton University discovered that dendritic cells, crucial for activating the body's defenses, naturally produce retinoic acid, which subsequently weakens their capacity to stimulate cancer-killing T cells. Collaborative research also identified promising small molecules that selectively block retinoic acid production, offering a novel and controlled approach to improving cancer treatment strategies in preclinical models.
