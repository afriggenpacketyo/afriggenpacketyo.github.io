First Dinosaur Bone from Antarctica Identified After Decades in Storage

2026-06-29 01:10:46 EDT

A fossil initially unearthed in Antarctica in 1985 and subsequently forgotten in a drawer for 40 years has now been identified as the continent's first dinosaur bone. Palaeontologists confirmed the specimen is a tail bone from a Titanosaur, a group known for containing Earth's largest dinosaurs. This significant discovery, made by Dr. Mark Evans at the British Antarctic Survey, provides crucial insights into how these massive creatures thrived in a region now considered uninhabitable. The find helps to expand our understanding of ancient ecosystems at the bottom of the world approximately 80 million years ago, despite the challenges of Antarctic fossil exploration.

Chinese Technology Achieves Cost-Effective, Zero-Energy Seawater Desalination

2026-06-29 01:00:05 EDT

A groundbreaking Chinese prototype has demonstrated year-long stability in desalinating seawater with zero utility energy costs, making freshwater production cheaper than bottled water. This innovative technology utilizes a new photothermal material, achieving a remarkable solar absorption rate of over 90% and significantly reducing the energy required for evaporation by nearly 46%. Successful trials at a small site irrigated five square meters of farmland using only natural sunlight, without requiring any external power infrastructure. Projecting over two years of operation, the economic advantage is expected to become even more pronounced with larger scale implementation, offering a promising solution to global water scarcity.
