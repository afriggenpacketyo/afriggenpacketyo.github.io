Ancient China-West connections influenced modern science, including AI

2026-06-22 21:00:06 EDT

The development of artificial intelligence (AI) has roots in a profound, historical exchange between China and the West, particularly involving Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz and the I Ching. Leibniz, the inventor of the binary numeral system fundamental to modern computing, found a surprising parallel in the 64 hexagrams of the I Ching, China's ancient text. Jesuit missionary Father Joachim Bouvet recognized this connection and informed Leibniz, who then published a paper on binary arithmetic in 1703, explicitly linking it to the Chinese figure Fu Xi. This historical link highlights a deeper, often overlooked, relationship between Chinese civilization and Western scientific thought that shaped modern scientific advancements.
