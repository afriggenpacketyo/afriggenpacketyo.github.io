This disease, prevalent in the American Midwest, causes these distinctive growths, which are generally benign unless they obstruct the rabbits' ability to eat or see. The virus primarily spreads through fleas, ticks, or direct rabbit-to-rabbit contact, posing no threat to humans or other animal species. Once the affected rabbits' immune systems successfully combat the virus, the growths naturally recede, a phenomenon believed to have inspired the centuries-old myth of the 'jackalope.'
