World's Oldest Cave Art, a Hand Stencil, Discovered in Indonesia

2026-01-21 13:09:24 EST

Scientists have announced the discovery of the world's oldest known cave art in Indonesia, a red stencil of a human hand dating back at least 67,800 years. Found in caves on Muna island in Sulawesi province, this ancient rock art, likely made with red ochre, features unique claw-shaped fingers suggesting a deep cultural meaning. Researchers used a precise uranium-thorium dating technique on calcite formations to determine its age, making it significantly older than previously known cave art. This discovery provides crucial evidence for understanding early Homo sapiens migration routes from Asia to Australia, reinforcing the northern route theory.
