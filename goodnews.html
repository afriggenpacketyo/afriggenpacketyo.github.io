Rules for Garnishing Social Security Benefits for Debt Explained

2026-05-13 12:28:04 EDT

However, federal debts operate under different regulations: the IRS can levy up to 15% of monthly benefits for back taxes without a court order, with no minimum benefit floor. For federal student loan defaults, up to 15% can be garnished, but the remaining benefit cannot fall below $750 per month. Court-ordered obligations like child support and alimony can result in 50-65% of benefits being withheld. While direct-deposited benefits are protected for two months' worth of funds in bank accounts, some states offer additional safeguards, emphasizing the need for beneficiaries to understand these complex rules.
