Understanding the Summer Solstice: The Longest Day of the Year

2026-06-16 03:03:42 EDT

The summer solstice, marking the official start of astronomical summer, will occur on Sunday, June 21, at 09:24 BST in the northern hemisphere. This phenomenon, which can also fall on June 20 or 22 due to Earth's orbital variations and leap years, signifies the moment the hemisphere is maximally tilted towards the Sun. Earth's axial tilt is the fundamental reason for distinct seasons, as it dictates the amount of sunlight different regions receive throughout the year. The term "solstice" derives from Latin, meaning "sun stand still," referring to the Sun's apparent pause in movement across the sky.
