Indonesia considers acquiring advanced policing technology from China

2026-06-05 18:00:14 EDT

Indonesia is reportedly exploring the purchase of high-tech policing equipment from China, with a specific interest in counterterrorism technology. At a trade show in Beijing, the Indonesian National Police outlined their requirements, which include intelligence technology, tactical weapons, bomb disposal gear, and specialized vehicles. These acquisitions are intended to bolster the capabilities of units like the Anti Terror Special Detachment 88 and the Mobile Brigade Corps. The police are also seeking intelligence and surveillance systems, such as intercept and tracking systems, thermal imaging drones, and bomb disposal robots. While the list includes tactical armored vehicles and protective gear, the Indonesian force prioritizes domestic procurement, though recent transfers of advanced drug detection equipment from China indicate a willingness to engage with Chinese suppliers.

Iran's World Cup soccer team granted visas to compete in the United States

2026-06-05 17:57:34 EDT

Players from Iran's World Cup soccer team have successfully obtained visas, allowing them to enter and participate in the tournament held in the United States. The U.S. Secretary of State had previously indicated that visa issuance would not be an issue, provided the Iranian delegation did not include individuals with ties to the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC). U.S. Embassy officials in Ankara were recognized for their work in processing these visas. Despite receiving visas to play in the U.S., the Iranian team is expected to be based in Mexico due to complexities related to U.S. sanctions. The team's participation follows a period of heightened tensions and a ceasefire in the region.
