Producer Prices Show Softer Inflation, Retail Sales Increase in November

2026-01-14 11:19:17 EST

Economic data for November indicates that wholesale inflation was milder than anticipated, while consumer spending remained robust. The producer price index (PPI) rose by 0.2% for the month, below expectations, and core PPI remained flat. Annually, the PPI increased by 3%, still above the Federal Reserve's target. Concurrently, retail sales surged by 0.6% in November, exceeding forecasts, with broad-based gains across various retail sectors. This mixed economic picture suggests moderating inflationary pressures at the wholesale level alongside sustained consumer demand.
