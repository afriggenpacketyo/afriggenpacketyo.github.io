Stillaguamish Tribe Restores Wetlands to Revive Salmon Populations in Washington

2026-05-03 05:00:00 EDT

The Stillaguamish Tribe in Washington is undertaking a significant ecological restoration project, purchasing hundreds of acres of farmland to remove levees and re-establish tidal marshes. These newly flooded wetlands serve as critical nurseries for young Chinook salmon, a federally threatened species vital to the tribe's cultural heritage and sustenance. This initiative aims to reverse decades of environmental degradation, acknowledging the complex history of land treaties where the tribe now buys back territory it once ceded. Beyond salmon recovery, these restored floodplains are also expected to reduce future flood damage for surrounding communities, offering a dual benefit of ecological revival and increased resilience against natural disasters.
