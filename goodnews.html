Bipartisan Bill Aims to Simplify Charitable Donations from 401(k)s

2026-05-18 11:42:33 EDT

A new bipartisan bill, the Charity Parity Act, seeks to allow retirees aged 70½ or older to make qualified charitable distributions (QCDs) directly from 401(k)s and similar workplace retirement plans. Currently, QCDs are only permitted from IRAs, requiring an extra rollover step that this legislation aims to eliminate. This change would modernize retirement planning by offering tax benefits, such as excluding donations from income and counting them towards required minimum distributions, without increasing adjusted gross income. The proposal is seen as a practical update, aligning with the evolving role of 401(k)s that increasingly offer features to retain retiree funds.

Can Bankruptcy Halt Social Security Garnishment for Retirees?

2026-05-18 11:01:05 EDT

Many retirees rely on Social Security benefits but also carry debt, leading to concerns about garnishment. This article explores whether filing for bankruptcy can protect these benefits. While an automatic stay in bankruptcy can temporarily halt most collection activities, its effectiveness against Social Security garnishment depends on the debt type and creditor. Regular creditors generally cannot directly garnish identifiable Social Security funds, but federal entities can offset benefits for student loans or tax debts. Discharging federal student loans in bankruptcy is challenging, requiring proof of "undue hardship," though recent guidance has made it slightly more accessible. Other debt relief options like settlement or management plans are also available.
