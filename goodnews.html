Germany Consecrates First Catholic Bishop of Indian Heritage, Signaling Church Diversity

2026-03-15 02:11:00 EDT

Germany's Catholic Church has made a historic step by consecrating Carmelite Father Joshy Pottackal, originally from India, as its first bishop of non-European heritage. This appointment is seen as a "strong signal" acknowledging the growing diversity within the German Catholic Church, where millions of faithful have migration backgrounds. Pottackal, who has lived in Germany for over 20 years and holds a German passport, emphasizes the church's reflection of Germany as a country shaped by immigration. His consecration, following similar appointments by Pope Leo XIV, is hoped to promote greater flexibility and highlight the international character within church life, celebrating integration and belonging.

UK Government Proposes Granting Police-Style Powers to Environment Officers to Combat Fly-Tipping

2026-03-14 23:09:22 EDT

The UK government is proposing to grant police-style powers to Environment Agency officials in England to enhance their ability to crack down on fly-tipping and illegal waste dumping. These expanded powers could include the authority to search premises without a warrant, seize assets, and arrest individuals suspected of environmental criminality. Under new legislation, those caught transporting or dealing with waste illegally could face up to five years in prison. Additionally, the Department for Environment, Food and Affairs is considering penalty points on driving licenses for fly-tipping offenses, aiming to deter waste criminals and reduce cleanup costs for councils.
