Understanding Your Obligations Regarding a Deceased Relative's Debt

2026-07-14 12:13:51 EDT

Generally, individuals are not legally responsible for a deceased relative's debt, as it typically becomes a claim against the deceased person's estate, which is settled using their assets. However, exceptions exist if one co-signed a loan, was a joint account holder, or inherited secured property like a mortgaged home. Debt collectors are usually limited to contacting the estate's executor, and misrepresenting a relative's personal liability is prohibited under federal law. The article advises confirming legal responsibility before making any payments and explores various debt relief options for those who are indeed liable. (not investment advice)
