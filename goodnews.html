The annual presidential pardon of a turkey is a well-known but often misunderstood American tradition ahead of Thanksgiving. While presidents have received turkeys as gifts since the 19th century, these were historically intended for the dinner table. The modern ceremony originated with the National Turkey Federation in 1947, and the first informal sparing of a bird is credited to President John F. Kennedy in 1963. President George H.W. Bush formalized the event in 1989, turning it into the public ritual it is today, where two turkeys are officially pardoned and sent to live out their days.
