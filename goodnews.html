2025-06-19 09:27:31 EDT

Juneteenth, officially recognized as a federal holiday in 2021, commemorates the end of slavery in the United States. The name combines "June" and "nineteenth," marking the day in 1865 when enslaved people in Texas learned of their freedom under the Emancipation Proclamation. While the Emancipation Proclamation was issued in 1863, it wasn't until Union soldiers arrived in Galveston, Texas, that the news reached the westernmost Confederate state. Celebrations vary across the U.S. and include parades, parties, concerts, and educational events, with many viewing the holiday as a joyful anniversary of independence.
