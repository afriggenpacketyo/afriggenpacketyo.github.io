2025-12-03 05:00:00 EST

The word "ideology" has evolved from a neutral term for the study of ideas into a common political pejorative used to dismiss opponents' beliefs. Coined during the French Enlightenment, it was quickly turned into a derogatory label by Napoleon and later used by Karl Marx to describe a "false consciousness." Today, political figures across the spectrum use the term to attack worldviews they oppose, with conservatives targeting concepts like "gender ideology" and liberals critiquing systems like "Christian nationalism."
