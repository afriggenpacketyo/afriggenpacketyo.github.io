Understanding Federal Limits on Wage Garnishment for Credit Card Debt

2025-10-17 11:20:16 EDT

For individuals with delinquent credit card debt, wage garnishment is a serious potential consequence that allows creditors to take a portion of earnings directly from a paycheck. Federal law limits this amount to the lesser of 25% of disposable income or the amount by which weekly earnings exceed 30 times the federal minimum wage. However, many states have enacted stricter rules that offer greater protection for debtors, with some even prohibiting wage garnishment for unsecured debts entirely. The article advises that addressing delinquent debt through negotiation, debt management plans, or, as a last resort, bankruptcy is crucial to avoid this outcome.
