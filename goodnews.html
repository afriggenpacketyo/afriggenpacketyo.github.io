Claudette Colvin, a significant but often overlooked figure in the U.S. civil rights movement, has passed away at the age of 86. At just 15 years old in 1955, Colvin made history by refusing to give up her seat to a white woman on a segregated bus in Montgomery, Alabama, an act that occurred months before Rosa Parks' similar protest. Her courageous defiance led to her arrest for disturbing public order, and she later became a plaintiff in a landmark lawsuit that successfully challenged bus segregation nationwide. Though initially ostracized and uncelebrated for decades, her legacy of courage was increasingly recognized later in life, with her 1955 arrest record finally expunged in 2021.
