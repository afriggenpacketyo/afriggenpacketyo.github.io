The discovery provides new insights into animal life during the Cambrian period, revealing that the creature was more complex than previously understood. Mosura, nicknamed the "sea moth," possessed flaps for swimming, claws for predation, and a segmented body with gills, similar to modern arthropods. The finding contributes to a better understanding of the diversity of life on Earth before the Cambrian explosion.
