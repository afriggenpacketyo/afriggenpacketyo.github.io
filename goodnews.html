Paleontologists Discover New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Paleontologists in Thailand have announced the exciting discovery of a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, identified from fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This sauropod, distinguished by an unusually long neck and an estimated length of up to 20 meters, is believed to have roamed the Earth approximately 150 million years ago during the Late Jurassic period. The discovery, particularly a unique dorsal vertebra exhibiting distinctive air-cavity structures, marks the first time a dinosaur from the Mamenchisauridae family has been found in Thailand. This finding significantly expands the known geographical range of these ancient giants, offering new insights into prehistoric ecosystems.
