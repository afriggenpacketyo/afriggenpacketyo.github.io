Geological Experts Clarify "Mini-Volcanoes" on California Coast Are Spontaneous Combustion Events

2026-04-24 18:18:57 EDT

Recent fire incidents in Santa Barbara, locally dubbed the "Hope Ranch Volcano," are not volcanic activity but rather instances of spontaneous combustion, according to geologists. Professor James Boles explains that the interaction of oxygen with unstable compounds like iron sulfides in cliffside rock layers generates intense heat, causing organic materials to ignite. Similar phenomena have been observed along Ventura County's coast. While visually resembling eruptions, these events are purely geological reactions, not indicative of underlying volcanic activity. Fire crews have responded to these flare-ups for centuries, preventing structural damage.
