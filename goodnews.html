Understanding the Home Equity Requirements for a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-06 15:11:59 EDT

For senior homeowners, a reverse mortgage can be a valuable tool for accessing retirement income, but it comes with specific equity requirements. Most lenders require homeowners to have at least 50% to 60% equity in their property to qualify for a Home Equity Conversion Mortgage (HECM), the most common type. The actual amount that can be borrowed depends on factors like the borrower's age, current interest rates, and FHA lending limits. If a homeowner falls short of the equity threshold, options include paying down the existing mortgage, waiting for equity to build, or downsizing to a less expensive home.
