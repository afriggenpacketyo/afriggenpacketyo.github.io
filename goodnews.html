Neuroscientist Explains Memory Is Less a Record of the Past and More a Tool for the Present

2025-10-19 06:30:00 EDT

Neuroscientist Dr. Charan Ranganath argues that the common understanding of memory as a faithful filing cabinet is incorrect. In his book "Why We Remember," he explains that memory's primary function is not to store every detail, but to help us make sense of the present and plan for the future. He distinguishes between episodic memory (specific events), which declines with age, and semantic memory (facts and knowledge), which often remains strong. Ranganath suggests that because memory is a reconstructive process, like a painting rather than a photograph, our relationship to past events, including trauma, can change over time.
