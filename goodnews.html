2025-09-03 08:41:00 EDT

Initially, children in art were often limited to portrayals of Jesus or angels, or as miniature adults in noble portraits designed for political purposes like marriage alliances. The Renaissance and Baroque periods saw a shift towards individual, often idealized, portraits among the wealthy bourgeoisie. Over centuries, the artistic lens expanded to include peasant children and eventually, with the advent of photography, captured the spontaneous and unburdened moments of modern childhood.
