2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

Urban heat islands are metropolitan areas significantly warmer than their rural surroundings, a phenomenon intensified by buildings, paved surfaces, and human activities. This effect can raise urban temperatures by 10-15 degrees Celsius, making residents vulnerable during heatwaves. The article explains that dark concrete and asphalt absorb heat, tall buildings trap air, and pollution creates a greenhouse layer. Solutions include greening cities with drought-resistant trees, implementing "cool roofs" that reflect sunlight, and using permeable "cool pavements." These strategies aim to reduce urban temperatures, lower air pollution, and decrease energy demand for cooling.
