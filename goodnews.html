A near-total eradication of bullfrogs from habitats within Yosemite National Park led to the striking recovery of the Northwestern pond turtle. Researchers conducted night surveys to remove adult bullfrogs and day surveys to target egg masses, removing an estimated 16,000 bullfrogs across two sites. After several years, they observed the first small pond turtle hatchlings and juveniles, along with a comeback of certain snake and newt species, suggesting a potential tool for reversing species decline and restoring freshwater ecosystems.
