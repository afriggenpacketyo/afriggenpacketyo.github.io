Native American Women Revitalize Facial Tattoos as Cultural Expression

2025-05-04 07:00:06 EDT

Native American women, particularly from south-eastern Indigenous nations, are reclaiming the tradition of facial tattoos as a form of cultural revitalization and personal commitment. The Inchunwa Project, a community collective, supports this movement through research, education, and tattooing sessions. The practice, which was once common but faded due to colonization, now represents a connection to ancestors, a commitment to community, and a pathway for future generations. The traditional process differs from western tattooing, often involving hand-poked designs without stencils, emphasizing a deeper connection between the tattooer and the person receiving the tattoo.
