Ancient Chinese Silk Manuscripts Return to Beijing After Decades in US

2025-05-18 06:10:34 EDT

Two volumes of the 2,300-year-old Zidanku Silk Manuscripts, considered the earliest known in China, have been returned to Beijing from the National Museum of Asian Art, part of the Smithsonian Institution in the United States. These manuscripts, dating back to the Warring States Period around 300BC, are historically significant and predate the Dead Sea Scrolls. The repatriation follows diplomatic efforts by China's National Cultural Heritage Administration, enabled by the Smithsonian's new ethical returns policy. The manuscripts were illegally excavated in 1942 and acquired by an American collector in 1946 before being gifted to the museum in 1992.
