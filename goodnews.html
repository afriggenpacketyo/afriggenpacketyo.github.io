Understanding Which Debts Are Forgiven After a Person's Death

2026-06-23 10:36:55 EDT

The question of what happens to debt after death is increasingly relevant as household debt reaches record highs, leaving many older Americans with significant financial obligations. While debt generally belongs to the borrower and creditors primarily seek repayment from the deceased's estate, not all debts are forgiven. Unsecured debts like credit card balances and personal loans without co-signers are often discharged if the estate lacks sufficient assets. Federal student loans are typically forgiven upon death, and some private ones may be too, but secured loans and co-signed debts usually remain. Proactive debt management is crucial to protect surviving family members from financial burdens.
