Research Uncovers Reason for More Aggressive Type 1 Diabetes in Young Children

2025-11-12 21:48:00 EST

Scientists have made a significant breakthrough, discovering why Type 1 diabetes manifests more severely and aggressively when it develops in very young children. Research published in Science Advances reveals that the pancreas's insulin-producing beta cells are still in a developmental stage in early childhood, particularly under the age of seven, making them highly vulnerable to immune system attacks. In contrast, older individuals possess more mature and durable beta cell clusters, allowing for some insulin production even after an attack. This crucial insight offers new hope for delaying the disease's onset through emerging immunotherapy drugs, potentially allowing children's pancreases more time to mature.
