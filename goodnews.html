2025-09-01 07:00:48 EDT

Researchers found that adults with hearing loss who began wearing hearing aids before the age of 70 had a 61% lower risk of all-cause dementia compared to those with untreated hearing loss. This benefit was not observed in individuals who started using hearing devices at age 70 or older. The findings underscore the importance of early diagnosis and intervention for hearing impairment as a modifiable risk factor for cognitive decline.
