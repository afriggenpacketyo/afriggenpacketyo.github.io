Conservation Mission Launched to Save Endangered Polymita Snails

2025-08-03 20:46:34 EDT

A new conservation mission has been launched to protect the endangered Polymita tree snails, widely considered the "world's most beautiful" due to their exceptionally vibrant and intricately patterned shells. These unique snails, endemic to Eastern Cuba, are rapidly disappearing from their natural forest habitats, primarily driven towards extinction by the illegal shell trade catering to collectors. Biologists from Cuba and the University of Nottingham in the UK are collaborating to study the snails' evolution and genetic makeup, undertaking challenging captive breeding initiatives despite logistical hurdles like power outages. The project aims to gather vital data to inform effective conservation strategies, highlighting the paradox where the snails' beauty is also their greatest vulnerability.
