2025-09-26 12:57:13 EDT

A retirement strategy known as the "rule of thirds" is gaining traction as an alternative to traditional withdrawal methods like the 4% rule. This approach involves converting one-third of a retiree's savings into a guaranteed lifetime income product, typically a fixed annuity, to cover essential expenses. The remaining two-thirds of the portfolio can then be invested for growth or used for flexible spending and unexpected costs. By blending the security of a pension-like income stream with the flexibility of market investments, the strategy aims to reduce stress from market volatility and provide a stable financial foundation for retirees.
