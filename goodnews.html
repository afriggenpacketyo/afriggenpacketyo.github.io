Exploring the Origins and Growing Popularity of the Term 'Neurodivergent'

2025-10-29 05:00:00 EDT

The term "neurodivergent" has gained widespread use since 2020 to describe individuals whose brains process information differently from what is considered typical. Coined in the late 1990s by activist Kassiane Asasumasu, it serves as a broad umbrella for various neurological differences, including but not limited to autism, ADHD, depression, and conditions resulting from brain injuries. The term is distinct from "neurodiversity," which encompasses the entire spectrum of human brain variations. Its rise in popularity reflects a growing movement toward more inclusive language and a desire for solidarity among people with different ways of thinking, allowing for self-identification without disclosing specific medical diagnoses.
