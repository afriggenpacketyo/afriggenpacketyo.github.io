Social Security Reduces Benefit Clawback Rate to 50%

2025-05-05 06:30:01 EDT

The Social Security Administration has reduced the default withholding rate for new overpayment notices from 100% to 50% for Title II benefits, which include retirement, survivors, and disability insurance. This change comes after criticism of the initial 100% rate, though experts still warn that even a 50% clawback can cause significant financial hardship for affected beneficiaries. Overpayments can occur for various reasons, such as unreported changes in circumstances or agency errors. Beneficiaries have 90 days to request a lower withholding rate or a waiver of recovery, otherwise, the agency will withhold up to 50% of their benefits until the overpayment is recovered.
