After 72 weeks, 62.9% of the semaglutide group experienced reduced inflammation and fat accumulation in their livers, while 36.8% saw improvements in liver fibrosis, suggesting semaglutide could be an effective tool in treating advanced liver disease. Adverse effects like nausea and diarrhea were more common in the semaglutide group.
