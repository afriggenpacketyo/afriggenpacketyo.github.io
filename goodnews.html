Atmospheric Conditions Spared US Mainland from Hurricane Landfall for First Time in a Decade

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

The 2025 Atlantic hurricane season concluded without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States, a first in ten years. Meteorologists attribute this unusual outcome to a persistent weather pattern known as "recurvature," where storms veered northward and back out to sea before reaching the coast. This was primarily caused by an area of low pressure, or a trough, over the eastern U.S., which weakened the western edge of the Bermuda High pressure system. This atmospheric setup effectively steered 13 named storms, including four major hurricanes, away from the U.S. mainland, a stark contrast to recent, more destructive seasons.

The Presidential Turkey Pardon: A Look at the History of a White House Thanksgiving Tradition

2025-11-25 10:29:59 EST

The annual White House tradition of a president "pardoning" a turkey for Thanksgiving has a long and informal history. While the practice was officially established by President George H.W. Bush in 1989, its origins can be traced back to President Abraham Lincoln. In 1863, Lincoln reportedly granted clemency to a turkey at the behest of his son, Tad. It was also Lincoln who, in that same year, issued the proclamation that established Thanksgiving as a national holiday. Over the decades, the ceremony has evolved from a simple presentation of a turkey from the poultry industry into a widely publicized media event symbolizing the start of the holiday season.
