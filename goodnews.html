NATO's Purpose and Collective Defense Clause Explained

2025-06-24 02:21:00 EDT

NATO, the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, was established in 1949 to deter Soviet expansion in Europe and foster political integration on the continent. The alliance functions as a collective security organization, providing mutual defense through military and political means if a member state is threatened. Article 5 of the NATO charter, the collective defense clause, states that an attack on one member is considered an attack on all, prompting a collective response. The article has only been invoked once, by the United States after the 9/11 attacks, and the organization continues to evolve its role in response to contemporary security challenges.
