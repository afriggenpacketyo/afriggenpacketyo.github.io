Scientists Uncover Genetic Mechanism for Regenerating a Whole Plant from a Single Cell

A collaborative team of scientists from China and the Netherlands has discovered the genetic process that allows a single plant cell to regenerate into a complete new plant. Their research, published in the journal Cell, identifies two genetic switches that can revert a differentiated somatic cell back to an embryonic state, a phenomenon known as somatic embryogenesis. This ability was previously thought to be exclusive to fertilized eggs.
