Fossil Discovery in China Suggests Some Pterosaurs May Have Been Herbivores

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

A rare pterosaur fossil found in northeastern China is challenging previous assumptions about the diet of these ancient flying reptiles. The fossil of a Sinopterus atavismus contained stomach contents with phytoliths, which are microscopic mineral deposits from plants. Researchers also found small quartz crystals, likely gastroliths or "stomach stones" used to help grind tough plant matter, a feature seen in modern birds and lizards. This is the first time such evidence has been found in a pterodactyloid pterosaur, providing the first direct confirmation of herbivory in this group.

Astronomers Directly Observe Earliest Stages of Rocky Planet Formation for the First Time

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb Space Telescope and the ALMA array, astronomers have captured the first direct evidence of rocky planet formation around a very young, sun-like star named HOPS-315. Located 1,370 light-years away, the infant star's surrounding gas disk showed signs of silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing, the essential building blocks for planets like Earth. This discovery provides an unprecedented look into the processes that may have occurred at the beginning of our own solar system. The finding suggests that the initial steps of planet formation could be a common process in the universe.
