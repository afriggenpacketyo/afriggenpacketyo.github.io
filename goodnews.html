A long-standing guideline known as the "85th percentile rule" is being reconsidered by some U.S. states for setting speed limits. This rule suggests that the posted speed limit should match the speed of the 15th-fastest car out of 100, assuming most drivers naturally travel at a safe speed. However, critics argue this creates a feedback loop where speeding leads to higher limits, especially in urban areas with pedestrians. In response to rising road fatalities, the Federal Highway Administration has clarified that other factors like road use and crash frequency should be considered, prompting a shift toward more context-sensitive policies.

China Proposes Hypersonic Drone Carrier Design Featuring a Pivoting 'Scissor Wing'

2025-08-16 22:00:14 EDT

Chinese researchers have proposed a design for a hypersonic drone carrier that revives NASA's "scissor wing" concept. The design features a single, centrally mounted wing that rotates up to 90 degrees to optimize performance across different speeds. At low speeds, the wing is perpendicular to the fuselage for maximum lift, while at transonic speeds, it pivots to 45 degrees to mitigate shock waves. For hypersonic flight at Mach 5, the wing aligns parallel with the fuselage, turning the entire aircraft into a "waverider" for high efficiency. This innovative approach aims to solve the aerodynamic compromises inherent in aircraft designed for both low and high-speed flight.
