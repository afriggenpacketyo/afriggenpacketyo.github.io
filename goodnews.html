Jakob Fugger: The World's First Global Trader Who Revolutionized Finance

2025-12-14 12:45:00 EST

Jakob Fugger, a 16th-century German merchant, is recognized as the world's first global trader and one of history's wealthiest individuals. He revolutionized commerce by adopting double-entry bookkeeping from Venice and establishing an extensive intelligence network. Fugger became indispensable to the Habsburg monarchy by lending aggressively, securing access to lucrative silver and copper mines. His influence extended to persuading the Pope to relax the ban on charging interest, paving the way for modern lending practices. Fugger's legacy includes the Fuggerei, the world's first social housing complex, still operating today.
