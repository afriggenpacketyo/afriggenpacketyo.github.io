Civil Rights Leader Bernard LaFayette, Selma Voting Rights Organizer, Dies at 85

2026-03-05 22:45:33 EST

Bernard LaFayette, a pivotal civil rights leader and architect of the Selma voting rights campaign, has passed away at 85 from a heart attack. LaFayette's quiet groundwork in Selma, Alabama, was instrumental in setting the stage for the 1965 Voting Rights Act, two years before "Bloody Sunday." A co-founder of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee, he championed nonviolent civil disobedience and later worked with Martin Luther King Jr.'s Poor People's Campaign. His life was dedicated to institutionalizing and internationalizing the nonviolence movement, a mission he pursued globally after King's assassination.
