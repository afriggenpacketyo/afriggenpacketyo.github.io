2025-12-04 20:06:02 EST

An increasing number of families in the UK and other Western countries are choosing to have only one child, contributing to record-low fertility rates. This trend is driven by a "fertility gap," where people desire more children than they actually have, often due to financial pressures, the high cost of childcare, and career demands. While the proportion of one-child families in England and Wales has grown, research has debunked old stereotypes of only children being spoiled or maladjusted. In fact, studies suggest they often have academic advantages due to greater parental resources, a phenomenon known as "resource dilution."
