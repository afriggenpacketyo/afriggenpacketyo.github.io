2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

This article explains the phenomenon known as the "urban heat island effect," which causes cities to be significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas. This occurs because dark, hard surfaces like asphalt roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, releasing it slowly as heat. The effect is compounded by a lack of cooling elements like trees and water, as well as waste heat generated by vehicles and air conditioning. As climate change makes heatwaves more frequent and intense, urban populations are becoming increasingly vulnerable to extreme heat.
