Fossil Discovery in China Suggests Some Pterosaurs Were Herbivores

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

A rare pterosaur fossil discovered in northeastern China contains the first-ever evidence of stomach contents, revealing that the ancient flying reptile was an herbivore. The fossil of a Sinopterus atavismus, a type of pterodactyloid, had microscopic plant mineral deposits called phytoliths in its stomach. Researchers also found small quartz crystals, likely gastroliths swallowed to help grind tough plant matter, similar to the function of a gizzard in modern birds. Published in Science Bulletin, this discovery challenges previous assumptions about the diets of these dinosaur-era creatures and renews a contentious debate among paleontologists about pterosaur feeding habits.
