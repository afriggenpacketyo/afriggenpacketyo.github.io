Hundreds of Ancient Species Discovered in China, Revealing Post-Extinction Life

2026-01-28 15:21:40 EST

Scientists have discovered nearly a hundred new animal species in a Chinese quarry, offering a rare glimpse into life immediately following a mass extinction event 513 million years ago. The site yielded over 50,000 fossil specimens, including ancient relatives of worms, sponges, jellyfish, and arthropods, many with preserved soft parts. This "Huayuan biota" represents the first major discovery of soft-bodied fossils from directly after the Sinsk event, which ended the Cambrian explosion. The findings suggest early animals were capable of wide dispersal by ocean currents and provide crucial insights into evolution and recovery after catastrophic events.
