New Report Suggests Salton Sea Shoreline is Not a Major Source of Regional Air Pollution

2025-08-15 20:39:10 EDT

A new study indicates that dust from the exposed shoreline of the shrinking Salton Sea contributes less than 1% of the small particle air pollution in California's Coachella and Imperial valleys. This finding challenges the long-held assumption that the lake bed is the primary cause of the region's severe air quality issues and high asthma rates. The report, authored by the Pacific Institute, suggests that pollution sources are varied, including agricultural chemicals, diesel exhaust, and mining debris. Researchers now recommend shifting focus from shoreline dust control to protecting residents through indoor air filtration and public health alerts.
