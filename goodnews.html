Chronic Fatigue Syndrome Affects Millions, Often Undiagnosed

2025-12-20 12:03:40 EST

Chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS), also known as myalgic encephalomyelitis (ME), affects approximately 3.3 million people in the United States, yet an estimated 85% remain undiagnosed. This debilitating condition, often triggered by infections like COVID or mononucleosis, causes severe, persistent fatigue that significantly impairs daily activities, alongside symptoms like post-exertional malaise and unrefreshing sleep. Experts highlight CFS as a poorly understood immune disorder, predominantly affecting women, with diagnosis relying on symptom evaluation and exclusion of other conditions. Promisingly, researchers are developing AI-based tools like BioMapAI to identify the condition with high accuracy by analyzing biological markers, offering hope for future diagnosis and management plans.
