2025-09-20 14:39:07 EDT

Great Smoky Mountains National Park is home to a variety of owl species, though their nocturnal and stealthy habits make them difficult for visitors to spot. Ornithologists note that the barred owl is the most common, while the Eastern screech-owl and great horned owl are also resident breeders. These predators are uniquely adapted for nighttime hunting with exceptional vision and hearing that allows them to pinpoint prey in the dark. While reintroduction efforts for the barn owl in the 1990s were not demonstrably successful, the park remains a crucial habitat for these birds, whose biggest threats are habitat loss and vehicle collisions.
