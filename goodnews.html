2026-03-29 09:00:13 EDT

Tuberculosis, often called "the white plague," has seen a concerning rise in the U.S. since the pandemic, with over 10,600 cases confirmed in 2024—the highest annual count since 2013. Health experts attribute this increase to a "rebound effect" from undetected latent infections during COVID-19 and increased international travel from high-burden countries. While US rates remain low globally, the uptick strains healthcare systems and highlights the importance of early detection and treatment. TB is curable with antibiotics, but untreated cases can be fatal and contribute to drug-resistant strains.
