Palaeontologists Uncover New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new plant-eating dinosaur species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, found in Kalasin Province. This sauropod, estimated to be 20 meters long and to have lived approximately 150 million years ago, is characterized by its unusually long neck and a unique Y-shaped arrangement of supporting bones with distinctive air cavities in its dorsal vertebra. The discovery marks the first Mamenchisauridae family fossil found in Thailand, a family previously thought to be exclusive to China. This significant finding, published in Nature, expands the understanding of dinosaur distribution and ancient ecosystems in Southeast Asia, thrilling the research team.
