Explainer on Recognizing and Responding to Heat Exhaustion and Heat Stroke

2025-07-01 07:40:00 EDT

As heatwaves become more common, it is crucial to understand the difference between heat exhaustion and the more dangerous heat stroke. Heat exhaustion occurs when the body overheats, causing symptoms like headache, heavy sweating, and dizziness, and should be treated by moving to a cool place and rehydrating. If left untreated, it can progress to heat stroke, a life-threatening emergency where the body's temperature regulation system fails, sweat production stops, and body temperature can rise rapidly. Recognizing the signs and taking immediate action, including seeking emergency medical help for heat stroke, can be life-saving.
