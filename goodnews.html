The Evolution and Overuse of the Business Buzzword 'Synergy'

2026-01-14 05:00:00 EST

The word "synergy," stemming from Greek for "working together," has transitioned from its religious and medical roots to an often-disliked corporate buzzword. Initially used in Christian theological debates and 19th-century medicine to describe combined positive effects, its widespread adoption in business literature, notably after Igor Ansoff's 1965 "Corporate Strategy," solidified its corporate status. Sociolinguists observe that its overuse during the 1980s and 90s, especially in mergers and acquisitions, caused it to lose precise meaning. Despite its positive connotations, many now perceive "synergy" as vague, overused jargon that sounds smart but lacks clarity.
