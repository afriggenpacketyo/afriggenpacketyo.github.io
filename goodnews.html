Ancient Western Xia Dynasty Tombs in China Granted Unesco World Heritage Status

2025-07-12 05:00:08 EDT

A vast burial site from the Western Xia dynasty, located near Yinchuan in northwest China, has been added to the Unesco World Heritage list. The 40-square-kilometer complex includes nine imperial mausoleums and over 270 associated tombs, dating back to a period between 1038 and 1227. The World Heritage Committee recognized the site as a significant testament to the cultural fusion and interaction among diverse traditions along the ancient Silk Road. This designation highlights the historical importance of the Tangut people who ruled the dynasty.
