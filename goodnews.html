The Surprising Origin of Barbie: Inspired by a German Pin-Up Doll

2025-08-13 08:30:00 EDT

The iconic Barbie doll was surprisingly inspired by Bild Lilli, a German pin-up doll created in 1952 for the Bild tabloid newspaper, initially targeting male readers. Lilli, described as a "sensual" and "sassy secretary" (interpreted by some as a high-class call girl), quickly became a popular plastic doll sold in tobacco shops and bars, not originally intended for children. Mattel co-founder Ruth Handler discovered Lilli in Switzerland in 1956 and subsequently bought the rights, transforming Lilli's aesthetic into the world-renowned Barbie in 1959. This origin reveals Barbie's roots in a sexualized caricature before becoming a dominant children's toy and cultural phenomenon.
