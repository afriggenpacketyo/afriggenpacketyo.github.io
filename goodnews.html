Despite its widespread adoption, DST is not universally observed, with notable exceptions like Hawaii and Arizona. The practice has also accumulated various myths, including Benjamin Franklin's supposed invention and its benefit to farmers. Debates continue regarding its impact on sleep, health, and productivity, with recent legislative efforts in the US to make DST permanent.
