The Powerful Link Between Scents, Emotions, and Memories Explored

2025-12-21 02:19:00 EST

This article explores the profound connection between scents, emotions, and memories, particularly during times like Christmas. Olaf Conrad, an otorhinolaryngologist, explains that smells are processed in the brain's paleocortex, close to the amygdala (emotions) and hippocampus (memories), making them potent triggers for vivid recollections. Unlike other sensory inputs, important memories linked to smells are "saved without a time-stamp," causing them to feel like current experiences. The article also touches on Highly Sensitive Persons (HSPs) who experience scents with much greater intensity, sometimes leading to physical or emotional distress. Aromatherapy in palliative care is noted as a positive application of this sensory link.
