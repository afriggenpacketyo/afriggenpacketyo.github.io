Semaglutide Shows Promise in Reversing Liver Disease in Clinical Trial

2025-05-03 05:00:56 EDT

A new clinical trial published in The New England Journal of Medicine reveals that semaglutide injections can reverse life-threatening cases of liver disease. The drug, traditionally used for type 2 diabetes, was administered to participants diagnosed with metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH). After 72 weeks, 62.9% of participants experienced reduced inflammation and fat accumulation in their livers, compared to only 34.3% in the placebo group. While some adverse effects like nausea and diarrhea were more common in the semaglutide group, researchers are optimistic about its potential as a new treatment option for fatty liver disease.
