EU Implements Comprehensive Ban on BPA in Food Packaging

2026-06-19 13:09:00 EDT

The European Union is implementing a comprehensive ban on Bisphenol A (BPA) and other bisphenols in food packaging, effective July 2026, establishing the strictest regulation globally. BPA, an industrial chemical found in various plastics and epoxy resins, can leach into food and poses significant health risks, including altering immune systems, increasing cardiovascular disease risk, and interfering with hormone systems. Studies have consistently detected BPA in the majority of human bodies. While some exceptions and transition periods apply, the ban aims to substantially reduce exposure, with advice for consumers to prioritize fresh foods, glass containers, and products explicitly labeled bisphenol-free.
