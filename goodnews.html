Antidepressants Can Intensify Heat Sensitivity, Posing Challenges in Rising Temperatures

2026-07-11 13:18:00 EDT

This article explains that people taking antidepressants often experience heightened heat sensitivity, making soaring temperatures particularly challenging. Medications like SSRIs (e.g., sertraline) and tricyclic antidepressants (e.g., amitriptyline) can affect the hypothalamus, a brain gland regulating body temperature, leading to symptoms like dizziness, nausea, and dehydration. Some antidepressants may inhibit sweating, while others increase it, further complicating the body's cooling mechanisms and potentially reducing thirst sensation. Experts advise patients to stay hydrated and avoid direct sunlight, emphasizing that medication should never be stopped without consulting a doctor, especially as climate change brings more frequent heatwaves.
