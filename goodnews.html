Harvard Law Library's Medieval Manuscript Identified as Original Magna Carta

2025-05-15 02:23:48 EDT

A seemingly ordinary manuscript housed in Harvard Law School's library for eight decades has been identified as an original 1300 version of the Magna Carta, a foundational document of modern liberties. Two British academics, David Carpenter and Nicholas Vincent, made the discovery while researching digital images of the manuscript. The document, purchased for a mere $27.50 after World War II, is now recognized as one of only seven originals from that date still in existence. This discovery underscores the importance of preserving and studying historical documents, especially in times when fundamental freedoms are under threat.
