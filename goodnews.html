Understanding Medicare's 3-Day Rule and Its Impact on Skilled Nursing Coverage

2025-09-11 11:08:05 EDT

Medicare's "3-day rule" is a critical regulation that can significantly affect out-of-pocket healthcare costs for seniors. The rule requires a beneficiary to have a medically necessary inpatient hospital stay of at least three consecutive days before Medicare Part A will cover subsequent care in a skilled nursing facility. Time spent under "observation status" does not count, which can lead to unexpected bills for patients who believe they qualify. Understanding this rule, along with its exemptions through plans like Medicare Advantage, is essential for navigating post-hospital care coverage.
