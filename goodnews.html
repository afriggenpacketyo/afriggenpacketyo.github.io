Businesses Employ 'Tariff Engineering' to Avoid Higher Duties

2025-06-18 03:24:46 EDT

Businesses are increasingly using "tariff engineering" to reduce the impact of tariffs imposed by the U.S. government. Tariff engineering involves altering a product's materials, dimensions, or composition to fit into a different harmonized system code with lower duty rates. While most new tariffs are broad-based, exemptions exist, allowing companies to benefit from strategic design choices. For example, manufacturers bundled steel and aluminum into products to qualify for lower duties, but later removed them when tariffs on those materials increased. Experts say that tariff engineering is a legitimate strategy to reduce duty liability, as there are over 5,000 product classification codes used by U.S. customs authorities.
