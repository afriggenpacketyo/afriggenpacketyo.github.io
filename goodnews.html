New Review Finds No Link Between Acetaminophen Use in Pregnancy and Autism or ADHD

2026-01-17 11:16:21 EST

A significant new review, published in The Lancet Obstetrics, Gynecology & Women's Health, concludes that using acetaminophen as directed during pregnancy does not increase a child's risk of autism, ADHD, or intellectual disability. This meta-analysis of about 60 studies, including sibling-comparison designs, found no causal link, contradicting earlier mixed results and some public claims. Medical experts reiterate that acetaminophen remains the safest first-line option for pain or fever during pregnancy, as untreated symptoms could pose risks to both mother and fetus. The article emphasizes that previous associations might have stemmed from underlying maternal conditions rather than the medication itself.
