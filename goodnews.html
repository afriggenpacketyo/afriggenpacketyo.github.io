When deciding between pretax (traditional) and Roth 401(k) contributions, financial planners suggest looking beyond your marginal tax rate to your effective tax rate. Your marginal rate is the tax on your last dollar earned, while your effective rate is the total tax paid as a percentage of your total income, which is often much lower. Since your effective tax rate in retirement may be lower than your current marginal rate, making pretax contributions during high-earning years could be more advantageous than commonly believed. This approach encourages a multi-year tax planning perspective, aiming to pay taxes when your overall rate is lowest. (not investment advice)
