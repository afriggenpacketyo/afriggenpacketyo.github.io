Understanding Endemic, Epidemic, and Pandemic Disease Spread

2026-05-11 06:31:00 EDT

The terms endemic, epidemic, and pandemic describe the geographical spread and frequency of diseases, not necessarily their severity. A disease is endemic when it occurs regularly in a specific region with a relatively constant number of cases over time, such as malaria. An epidemic occurs when a disease's incidence in a region rises unusually above its expected level, often for a limited period. A pandemic signifies a disease spreading across continents and countries, requiring international cooperation for control, as seen with influenza. These distinctions are crucial for public health understanding and response.
