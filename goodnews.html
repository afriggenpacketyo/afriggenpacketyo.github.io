2025-08-16 06:00:00 EDT

New research on wild chimpanzees in Uganda suggests that young chimps acquire their combined vocal and gestural communication patterns through social learning from their mothers, not through genetics. Scientists observed that individuals related through their maternal line exhibited similar communication styles, while no such pattern was found with paternal relatives. This finding mirrors how human infants learn from their primary caregivers.
