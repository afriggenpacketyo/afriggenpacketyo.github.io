2026-01-08 06:53:37 EST

Newly dated fossils discovered in a Moroccan cave provide strong evidence that Homo sapiens originated in Africa, dating back over 773,000 years to a period when Earth's magnetic field flipped. These findings help fill a significant gap in the African fossil record, previously dominated by European Homo antecessor fossils from around 800,000 years ago. The Moroccan fossils exhibit a mix of traits from Homo erectus and later hominins, suggesting a complex evolutionary divergence. Researchers believe this discovery supports the hypothesis of early hominin exchanges between North Africa and southwestern Europe. The precise dating, made possible by geological markers from the magnetic field reversal, offers a clearer timeline for human evolution. This scientific breakthrough sheds new light on our species' origins and early migrations.
