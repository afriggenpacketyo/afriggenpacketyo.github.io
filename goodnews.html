It details how Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz, the 17th-century German polymath, developed the binary numeral system—the foundational bedrock of modern computing. Crucially, Leibniz discovered the identical conceptual principles of his system in the ancient Chinese I Ching (Book of Changes) through Jesuit missionary Father Joachim Bouvet. This revelation, received by Leibniz in 1703, prompted him to publish a second paper on binary code, explicitly linking his work to the ancient Chinese figure of Fu Xi, underscoring a deep cross-cultural influence on scientific advancement.
