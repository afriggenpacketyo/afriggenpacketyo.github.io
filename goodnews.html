2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences‚Äô Institute of Vertebrate Palaeontology and Paleoanthropology have identified a remarkable new species of dicynodont, an extinct clade of non-mammalian therapsids considered a "cousin" to today's mammals. This discovery, based on a specimen unearthed in Gulang county, Gansu province, China, establishes Dinanomodon guoi as the first bidentalian genus to be shared between China and South Africa. This significant finding provides compelling evidence of an ancient cross-equator migration corridor that existed within the supercontinent Pangaea, offering crucial insights into the geographical distribution and evolutionary patterns of prehistoric life.
