Antidepressants Can Exacerbate Heat Sensitivity, Posing Risks During Hot Weather

2026-07-11 13:18:00 EDT

This article highlights how individuals taking antidepressants, particularly selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) and tricyclic antidepressants, can experience significantly heightened heat sensitivity, rendering soaring temperatures more challenging. These medications can impact the hypothalamus, a brain gland regulating body temperature, thereby impairing the body's natural temperature regulation and sweating mechanisms. This can lead to various uncomfortable symptoms such as dizziness, nausea, headaches, and dehydration. While some antidepressants inhibit sweating, others paradoxically increase it. Medical professionals strongly advise patients against abruptly discontinuing medication, instead recommending increased hydration and avoidance of direct sunlight, especially as climate change brings more frequent heatwaves.
