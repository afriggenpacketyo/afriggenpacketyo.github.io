Arizona and Hawaii Remain Exempt from Daylight Saving Time, Citing Unique Reasons

2026-03-06 11:47:09 EST

As daylight saving time approaches, most of the U.S. will adjust clocks, but Arizona (with the exception of the Navajo Nation) and Hawaii, along with five U.S. territories, remain exempt from the practice. Arizona made this decision in 1968, primarily due to its intense desert heat, aiming to prevent later sunsets that would keep people outdoors longer and increase energy consumption during hot months. Hawaii's consistent daylight hours near the equator rendered the time change unnecessary, leading to its exemption in 1967. Public sentiment across the U.S. indicates a strong desire to end the twice-annual clock changes, with ongoing legislative efforts at both state and federal levels.
