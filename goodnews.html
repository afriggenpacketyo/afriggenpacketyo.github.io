NASA's DART Mission Reveals Asteroid Deflection Is More Effective Than Previously Thought

2025-09-14 12:07:00 EDT

Data from NASA's Double Asteroid Redirection Test (DART) mission has provided crucial new insights for planetary defense strategies. When the DART spacecraft intentionally collided with the asteroid moonlet Dimorphos, it ejected an astonishing 35.3 million pounds of rock and dust into space. This massive plume of debris acted like a rocket thruster, giving the asteroid a much stronger push than the impact alone and significantly altering its orbit by 33 minutes. The findings confirm that Dimorphos is a loosely bound 'rubble pile,' making it more susceptible to this type of kinetic impact. This 'ejecta bonus' is now a critical factor for scientists designing future missions to deflect potentially hazardous near-Earth asteroids.

This lack of visibility makes it difficult for lenders to accurately assess a person's total debt and overall credit risk.
