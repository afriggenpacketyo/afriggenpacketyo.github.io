Understanding Aortic Dissections: Insights Following Senator Graham's Death

2026-07-12 22:44:55 EDT

Following Senator Lindsey Graham's death from an aortic dissection, this article provides crucial medical information about the life-threatening condition. An aortic dissection occurs when a tear in the aorta's inner layer allows blood to flow between its layers, potentially rupturing the outer layer. Symptoms often manifest suddenly as severe chest or back pain, resembling a heart attack, requiring immediate emergency medical care for improved survival outcomes. While rare, it is more common in men in their 60s and 70s, with risk factors including smoking and high blood pressure, emphasizing the importance of managing these conditions for prevention.
