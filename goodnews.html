Comparing Debt Relief Programs and Credit Counseling for Managing Finances

2025-09-26 16:38:06 EDT

With U.S. credit card debt surpassing $1.2 trillion, many Americans are exploring options beyond minimum payments to manage their finances. Two common approaches are debt relief programs and credit counseling, which operate differently. Debt relief programs, such as debt settlement, aim to reduce the total amount owed by negotiating with creditors, but this can negatively impact credit scores. In contrast, credit counseling focuses on creating a manageable debt management plan to repay the full amount over time, often with lower interest rates, which generally has a milder effect on credit. The best choice depends on an individual's financial situation, debt load, and risk tolerance.
