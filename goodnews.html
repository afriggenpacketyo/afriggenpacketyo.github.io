Studies suggest that the unique acceleration and deceleration characteristics of EVs, particularly the regenerative braking systems, contribute to this effect. Regenerative braking converts kinetic energy back into battery power when slowing down, creating low-frequency deceleration that can provoke queasiness. This issue presents a new challenge for automotive engineers focused on improving passenger comfort in the next generation of vehicles.
