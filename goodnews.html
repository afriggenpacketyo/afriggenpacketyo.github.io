New dinosaur species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, discovered in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Paleontologists in Thailand have discovered a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils found in Kalasin Province. This dinosaur, estimated to have lived 150 million years ago, possessed an exceptionally long neck and measured up to 20 meters in length. The discovery is significant as it's the first of its kind from the Mamenchisauridae family found in Thailand, with most other fossils of this group originating from China. Unique features of its vertebrae, including a distinctive air-cavity structure, set it apart from known species.
