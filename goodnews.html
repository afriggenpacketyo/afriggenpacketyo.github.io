The Story Behind the Name Juneteenth

2025-06-18 11:59:05 EDT

Juneteenth, a federally recognized U.S. holiday, commemorates the end of slavery in the U.S. after the Civil War and is also known as Freedom Day, Emancipation Day, or America's second Independence Day. The name Juneteenth is a portmanteau that combines the words June and nineteenth, dating back to June 19, 1865, when the last group of people enslaved in the southern U.S. were informed of their freedom under the Emancipation Proclamation. Celebrations vary, but often include parades, parties, concerts, educational workshops, and other cultural events centered on art and cuisine.
