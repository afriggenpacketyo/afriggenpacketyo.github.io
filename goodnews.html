French Parliament Approves Assisted Dying Bill for Terminally Ill Adults

2026-07-15 15:27:00 EDT

France's parliament has approved a bill legalizing assisted dying for terminally ill adults, a significant legislative milestone years in the making and a promise from President Emmanuel Macron. The law permits adults with life-threatening, advanced illnesses causing constant physical suffering to request a lethal substance, which can be self-administered or given by a doctor. Crucially, it excludes psychological suffering alone and requires patients to express free, informed choice, subject to physician and expert panel assessment. While the lower house passed it, the government bypassed the conservative-dominated Senate, sending the bill to the Constitutional Council for final approval, potentially aligning France with other European nations.
