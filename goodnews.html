Historical Practice of Turkey Droving to London for Christmas Uncovered

2025-12-24 00:32:22 EST

Before modern transportation, Christmas turkeys were herded on foot from Norfolk and Suffolk farms to London markets in a practice known as "turkey droving." Thousands of birds, some wearing tiny leather boots or coated in tar and sand for protection, embarked on a three-month journey, covering about three miles daily. Drovers would forage for food along the way, with birds roosting in trees at night. This tradition, which ended with the advent of railways, highlights a unique aspect of British agricultural history and the efforts made to save breeds like the Norfolk Black.
