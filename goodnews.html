2026-06-08 05:00:00 EDT

A new FDA-approved vibrating belt, Osteoboost, is being explored as a method to combat osteopenia, a condition of low bone density. The device mimics exercise by delivering low-magnitude vibrations to the spine and hips, stimulating bone-building cells. Clinical trials suggest that users experienced significantly less bone strength loss in the spine compared to a placebo group. However, some scientists caution that the study's findings may not fully support its prescription as a treatment for osteopenia, which is a systemic disease. Experts recommend using such devices as a supplementary tool alongside established bone-health practices like weight-bearing exercise and adequate nutrition.
