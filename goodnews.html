Soybeans have become a cornerstone of the modern global food system, yet their primary role is often misunderstood by consumers. The vast majority of soy, about 77 percent, is not used for tofu or soy milk but is cultivated as the main protein source for livestock to support the world's meat-heavy diets. This has concentrated production in the Americas, making the U.S. and Brazil agricultural superpowers and turning the soybean into a key commodity in international trade, particularly with China. The recent U.S.-China trade war has highlighted the geopolitical significance of this humble bean and its central, if inefficient, role in feeding the planet.
