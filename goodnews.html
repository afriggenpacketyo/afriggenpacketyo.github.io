Scientists Discover Iberian Harvester Ants Reproduce by Cloning Another Species' Males

2025-09-13 07:30:06 EDT

Researchers have discovered a bizarre reproductive strategy in Iberian harvester ants (Messor ibericus), where queens produce offspring of a completely different species to create their workforce. The queens, having lost the ability to produce their own workers, mate with males of another species, Messor structor, to create hybrid worker ants. More remarkably, to ensure a supply of these males without needing to find them in the wild, the queens can clone the Messor structor males using only their stored sperm. This unique phenomenon, termed "sexual domestication," represents a newly identified mode of reproduction and is changing how scientists understand evolution and genetics in social insects.
