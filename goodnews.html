UN General Assembly Declares Transatlantic Slave Trade a 'Gravest Crime Against Humanity'

2026-03-27 21:06:54 EDT

The United Nations General Assembly overwhelmingly passed a resolution declaring the transatlantic slave trade as "the gravest crime against humanity." UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres emphasized that the wealth of many Western nations was built on "stolen lives and stolen labour," highlighting the deep and often unrecognized wounds. The non-legally binding resolution, supported by African and Caribbean nations, is seen as a significant political step that could bolster discussions on reparations. While 123 countries voted in favor, 52 abstained, including the UK and EU states, and the US, Argentina, and Israel voted against it. This vote adds political legitimacy to the global reparations movement.
