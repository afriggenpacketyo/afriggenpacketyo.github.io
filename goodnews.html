2025-12-14 12:45:00 EST

His success was fueled by innovations like double-entry bookkeeping learned in Venice and an extensive intelligence network. Fugger aggressively lent to Habsburg emperors, securing lucrative mining rights for silver and copper, which were essential European exports to Asia. His enduring legacy includes the Fuggerei, the world's oldest social housing complex, founded in 1521, and his influence on modern lending practices by persuading the Pope to relax the ban on charging interest.
