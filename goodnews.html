Fact Check Debunks Misleading Comparisons of European Sexual Assault Statistics

2025-10-25 14:58:00 EDT

A viral bar chart claiming the UK is the "sexual assault capital of Europe" is misleading, according to a DW fact check. While UK police-recorded figures appear alarmingly high, cross-country comparisons are problematic due to vast differences in legal definitions, reporting practices, and cultural factors. For instance, the UK's 2003 law shift to consent-based definitions and its method of counting multiple offenses per perpetrator inflate its statistics compared to other nations. Criminologists recommend victim surveys for a more accurate measure, as police statistics only show "above sea level" crimes. Furthermore, claims linking uncontrolled mass migration to increased sexual assault rates are explicitly debunked as false and unsupported by evidence.
