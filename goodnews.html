Three-Eyed Ancient Predator Discovered in Canada

2025-05-20 07:07:12 EDT

Scientists have discovered a fossil of a three-eyed predator, named Mosura fentoni, in Canada's Burgess Shale, dating back over 500 million years. This creature, about the size of an index finger, possessed spiny jointed claws, a circular mouth with teeth, and swimming flaps. Mosura, part of the extinct radiodont group, is described as unlike any living animal, with a unique abdomen-like body region. The discovery provides insights into the early evolution of arthropods and their adaptations, including efficient respiration.
