Astronomers Witness Comet Reversing Its Spin, a First Observation

2026-03-27 10:38:09 EDT

Astronomers have made a groundbreaking observation, witnessing a comet, 41P/Tuttle-Giacobini-Kresák, reversing its direction of rotation, a phenomenon never before documented. Archival data revealed that during its 2017 pass around the sun, the comet's spin dramatically slowed, then reversed as it approached the sun. Scientists attribute this unusual event to "jets of gas" released from melting ice on the comet's surface, which act like small thrusters, effectively pushing against and then reversing its original motion. This discovery, described as "like pushing a merry-go-round," highlights the dynamic and unpredictable nature of comets. Researchers anticipate that continued rotational changes might lead to the comet's eventual instability, potentially resulting in its fragmentation or disintegration, underscoring the importance of long-term astronomical observations.
