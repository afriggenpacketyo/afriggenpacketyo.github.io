An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Jurisdiction

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), located at the Peace Palace in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial organ of the United Nations. Composed of 15 judges from different nations, the court adjudicates legal disputes between states, distinct from the International Criminal Court which tries individuals. Its jurisdiction applies when states consent to it, either through a blanket declaration or on a case-by-case basis, or when a dispute falls under a specific UN convention like the Genocide Convention. While its rulings are legally binding, the ICJ lacks an enforcement mechanism, depending on the cooperation of the involved parties.
