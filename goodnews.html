Scientists Discover Hedgehogs Possess Ultrasound Hearing Beyond Human Range

2026-03-10 20:24:19 EDT

Scientists have discovered that hedgehogs can hear sounds at frequencies up to 85kHz, far beyond the human range of 20kHz, suggesting they may communicate using ultrasound. This "game-changer" discovery, made by measuring brain responses of anesthetized hedgehogs, could significantly aid conservation efforts for the declining species. Researchers hope to develop targeted sound repellents to deter hedgehogs from dangerous machinery like cars and strimmers, which cause thousands of deaths annually. The unique stiff chain of bones in a hedgehog's ear, similar to echolocating bats, facilitates this high-frequency hearing, opening new avenues for understanding their audio world and protecting them from stressful noises.
