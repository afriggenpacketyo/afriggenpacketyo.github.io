Understanding Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) for Retirement Accounts

2026-05-05 14:08:58 EDT

Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) are mandatory withdrawals from certain retirement accounts, such as traditional IRAs and 401(k)s, typically beginning at age 73. The amount is calculated based on the account balance and IRS life expectancy factors, increasing as one ages. For a $500,000 account, RMDs can range from approximately $18,868 at age 73 to over $24,752 at age 80. Failing to take RMDs can result in a significant penalty of up to 25% of the under-withdrawn amount.
