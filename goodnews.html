Unclean Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly to Birds

2025-06-10 14:34:21 EDT

Hummingbird feeders, while intended to help these birds, can become deadly if not properly maintained. Sugar water left out too long can spoil and grow harmful microbes, leading to infections in hummingbirds. A common infection is candida, which causes swollen tongues and starvation. To prevent this, feeders should be cleaned every two to three days with hot water, or more frequently in hot weather, using a mixture of vinegar and water. Planting native wildflowers is also a good way to support local hummingbird populations.
