Van Allen Probe Spacecraft Safely Re-enters Earth's Atmosphere

2026-03-12 07:23:36 EDT

A 1,300lb Nasa spacecraft, the Van Allen Probe, successfully re-entered Earth's atmosphere over the Eastern Pacific region, nearly 14 years after its launch. Most of the probe was expected to burn up during re-entry, with Nasa estimating a low one-in-4,200 chance of harm from any surviving components. The mission, originally designed for two years, lasted almost seven, collecting unprecedented data on Earth's two permanent radiation belts. These Van Allen belts are crucial for shielding Earth from harmful cosmic radiation and solar storms, making the probe's data vital for understanding space weather.
