Study Suggests Ancestor Cell of Animals and Plants Used Hydrogen, Not Oxygen

2025-05-20 07:00:10 EDT

A Chinese study published in Nature suggests that the last common ancestor of eukaryotes, the foundation of complex life, lived around 2.72 billion years ago in oxygen-deficient conditions. Researchers from East China Normal University and Shenzhen University traced the ancestor of animals and plants to a hydrogen-metabolising cell that existed before the Great Oxidation Event. This finding supports the hydrogen hypothesis for the origin of eukaryotes, challenging earlier findings about the lineage of this common ancestor. Understanding the origins of eukaryogenesis could provide insights into the evolution of life and its potential existence in outer space.
