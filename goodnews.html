Humpback whales achieve record-breaking long-distance swims between Australia and Brazil

2026-05-20 06:53:00 EDT

Two humpback whales have been documented making unprecedented long-distance migrations between Australia and Brazil, marking the longest lifetime journeys ever recorded for the species. Researchers used extensive photo-identification data of whale flukes to track these individuals across approximately 14,000 to 15,000 kilometers. These findings expand the known migratory limits for southern hemisphere humpbacks and support the 'Southern Ocean Exchange' hypothesis, suggesting rare inter-basin movements can occur, potentially aiding genetic diversity. Scientists also suggest climate change may be contributing to an increase in such extraordinary migrations.
