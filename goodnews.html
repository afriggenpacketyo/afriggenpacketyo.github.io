"Bridge Ties" Offer a Path to Healing Political Division, Sociologist Argues

2026-02-22 07:00:37 EST

Sociologist Eva M. Meyersson Milgrom argues that "bridge ties"—loose social connections that cross boundaries of age, gender, culture, and politics—are crucial for healing political division and fostering resilience. Unlike close-knit groups that reinforce sameness, bridge ties expose individuals to diverse perspectives and allow for disagreement without unraveling social networks. These connections, often found through shared interests rather than beliefs, cultivate familiarity across differences, promoting tolerance and political resilience. Examples include interfaith collaborations in Minneapolis supporting immigrants, and human rights organizations protecting Palestinian farmers, demonstrating how these ties facilitate the flow of essential resources and understanding between profoundly different social worlds.
