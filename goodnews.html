2025-09-07 19:13:39 EDT

Stargazers across Asia, as well as parts of Europe and Africa, were treated to a total lunar eclipse on Sunday, resulting in a phenomenon known as a "Blood Moon." This event occurs when the Earth passes directly between the sun and moon, casting a shadow that gives the lunar surface a distinct reddish hue. The color is caused by sunlight scattering through the Earth's atmosphere, which filters out blue light and allows red light to pass through and reflect off the moon. The eclipse was most visible in Asia, including India and China, while observers in the Americas missed the event.
