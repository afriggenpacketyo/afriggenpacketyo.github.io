Potential Radiation Release from Bunker Buster Strike on Iranian Nuclear Site Assessed

2025-06-18 16:34:36 EDT

Experts assess the potential risks of radiation contamination if the U.S. were to use a "bunker buster" bomb on Iran's Fordo Fuel Enrichment Plant. Fordo processes uranium isotopes, primarily in the form of uranium hexafluoride, a heavy gas molecule unlikely to travel far. Experts believe any contamination would likely remain localized to the site. Although uranium isotopes are at the low end of hazard for radioactive materials, uranium hexafluoride gas can react with water to form hydrofluoric acid, an acutely hazardous material. The underground location of the facility would likely inhibit any significant environmental release.
