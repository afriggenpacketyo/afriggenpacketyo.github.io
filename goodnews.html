Study Finds Ancient Horses Migrated Between Asia and North America

2025-05-15 22:00:07 EDT

A new study reveals that ancient horses repeatedly migrated between North America and Eurasia during the late Pleistocene epoch, approximately 50,000 to 13,000 years ago. These migrations occurred via the Bering land bridge when sea levels were lower, connecting the two continents. The study examined fossils, including those of Dalianensis horses from northeastern China, which showed mixed ancestry from both Eurasian and American populations. The research, published in the journal Science, indicates that genetic exchanges between the continents happened in both directions over multiple migrations, contributing to the understanding of horse evolution and distribution across the globe.
