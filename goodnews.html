California's 'Mini Volcanoes' Revealed to Be Natural Spontaneous Combustion Events

2026-04-24 18:18:57 EDT

An investigation by The Times clarifies that the "mini volcanoes" observed along California's coast, such as the "Hope Ranch Volcano," are not geological volcanoes but rather natural spontaneous combustion events. Professor James Boles explains that these flare-ups occur when earth layers shift, allowing oxygen to interact with unstable compounds like iron sulfides in the rocks. This oxidation process generates significant heat, causing organic materials to ignite. While these phenomena have been recorded since the 1800s and can resemble volcanic activity, they are distinct geological processes that pose localized fire risks but do not involve magma.
