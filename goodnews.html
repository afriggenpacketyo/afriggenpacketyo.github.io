He proposes three key strategies for improved back health, particularly relevant as many return to work or study. Firstly, posture is dynamic, not static; constant movement and regular breaks are crucial, as even the best ergonomic setup won't prevent pain if one remains still for hours. Secondly, building strength, especially in the core and back, through simple exercises like arm raises and head turns, is more effective than seeking stiffness.
