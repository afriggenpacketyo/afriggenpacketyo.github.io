2025-10-14 21:26:52 EDT

Study Finds Group Singing Effectively Treats Postnatal Depression in New Mothers

2025-10-14 19:32:02 EDT

A major three-year study has concluded that specially designed group singing classes are a clinically effective treatment for mothers experiencing postnatal depression. The research, published in the British Journal of Psychiatry, found that participants in the 'Melodies for Mums' program showed sustained improvement in their symptoms for up to six months after the sessions ended. The program is designed to foster social connection and relaxation through music, providing a valuable and potentially cost-effective alternative to traditional therapies. Researchers believe the act of singing itself, combined with peer support, contributes to the lasting positive impact.
