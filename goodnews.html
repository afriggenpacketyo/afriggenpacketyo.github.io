2025-11-25 10:29:59 EST

The White House tradition of the presidential turkey pardon, officially started by President George H.W. Bush, has a long and informal history dating back to Abraham Lincoln. In 1863, Lincoln reportedly spared a turkey intended for Christmas dinner at the behest of his son, Tad, marking one of the earliest known instances of presidential clemency for a bird. The modern annual ceremony, where two turkeys are presented by the National Turkey Federation and sent to a farm, became a regular practice during the Reagan administration. This tradition complements the national Thanksgiving holiday, which Lincoln formally established during the Civil War.
