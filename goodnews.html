Aviation Pioneer Wally Funk, Oldest Woman in Space, Dies at 87

2026-07-09 18:48:10 EDT

Wally Funk, a trailblazing aviation pioneer and the oldest woman to journey into space, has passed away at the age of 87. Despite being denied the chance to become a NASA astronaut in the 1960s as part of the Mercury 13 program due to her gender, Funk steadfastly pursued her passion, excelling as a flight instructor and federal aviation inspector. At 82, she ultimately fulfilled her lifelong dream, traveling to space aboard Jeff Bezos's Blue Origin rocket in 2021. Her extraordinary life story is widely celebrated as a powerful testament to unwavering determination and perseverance, continuing to inspire generations, particularly young girls, to pursue careers in science, aviation, and space exploration, leaving an indelible legacy.
