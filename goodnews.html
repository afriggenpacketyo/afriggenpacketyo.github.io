2025-05-14 22:59:32 EDT

Scientists at the Field Museum of Chicago have uncovered new details about a well-preserved Archaeopteryx fossil, revealing insights into early bird evolution. Using UV light and CT scans, the article reports that researchers identified soft tissues and anatomical traits indicating that Archaeopteryx, while capable of flight, likely spent more time on the ground and could climb trees. The fossil showed the presence of tertials, specialized feathers crucial for flight, suggesting their specific evolution for aerial locomotion.
