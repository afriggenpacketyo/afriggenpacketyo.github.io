2026-01-29 02:00:14 EST

Recent archaeological findings of 5,100-year-old dams in China are challenging the long-held Western theory of "Oriental Despotism," which posited that large-scale hydraulic projects in Eastern societies necessitated a powerful, centralized government. A Chinese scholar argues that the study reveals prehistoric communities across China initially collaborated on smaller, clan-based water management projects like ponds for irrigation and flood control. This suggests that complex water systems did not inherently lead to despotic rule, offering a counter-narrative to the theory popularized by historian Karl August Wittfogel in 1957. The discovery prompts a re-evaluation of historical political development in China.
