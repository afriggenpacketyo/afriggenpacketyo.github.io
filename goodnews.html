The Ingenious Water-Flow Model of Economist Bill Phillips

2026-04-07 06:30:00 EDT

This article details the ingenious approach of economist Bill Phillips, creator of the "Phillips Curve," who famously modeled the British economy using a complex system of flowing water in his garage. Post-World War II, Phillips constructed this hydraulic apparatus to visually demonstrate macroeconomic principles, such as how government spending and taxation influenced economic activity, ultimately impressing skeptical academics at the London School of Economics. His subsequent analysis of U.K. wage and unemployment data led to the development of the Phillips Curve, which illustrates the inverse relationship between unemployment and inflation. This curve became an essential tool for central bankers in managing modern fiat currencies, guiding policies to stabilize economies.
