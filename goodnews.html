New Drug Improving Walking Ability for MS Patients Approved for NHS in England

2026-07-22 19:38:58 EDT

The first drug specifically designed to help individuals with multiple sclerosis improve their walking is now accessible through the NHS in England. Fampridine works by boosting nerve signals, which facilitates the transmission of electrical messages from the brain to the body, thereby making movement easier for some patients and enhancing their independence. Clinical trials demonstrated that approximately four out of ten people taking the drug experienced significant improvements in both their walking speed and the duration they could walk. This landmark decision, expected to benefit up to 5,000 people annually, marks a crucial advancement for MS treatment, especially since the drug was previously rejected by the NHS for not being considered value for money.
