Businesses Employ 'Tariff Engineering' to Reduce Duty Liabilities

2025-06-18 23:15:04 EDT

Faced with increasing tariffs, businesses are resorting to "tariff engineering," a legal practice involving altering product materials, dimensions, or compositions to fit into lower-duty categories. Customs lawyers and supply chain experts explain that this strategy allows companies to benefit from exemptions carved out by the U.S. government. For example, some manufacturers bundle steel and aluminum elements into final products to qualify for a lower 25% duty, while others remove such elements to avoid higher tariffs on steel and aluminum products. Companies like Columbia Sportswear and Converse have also successfully implemented strategic product tweaks to save on tariff costs. The practice, though legal, requires careful navigation to avoid misrepresenting product classifications.
