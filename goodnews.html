Eli Lilly's Retatrutide Shows Strong Results in Late-Stage Diabetes Trial

2026-03-19 07:52:43 EDT

Eli Lilly's investigational obesity drug, retatrutide, successfully completed its first late-stage trial for Type 2 diabetes patients, demonstrating significant reductions in blood sugar levels and an average weight loss of 16.8% at the highest dose over 40 weeks. This article highlights the drug's "triple G" mechanism, which mimics three hunger-regulating hormones, appearing more potent than existing treatments. While not yet filed for approval, these positive results position retatrutide as a potential key addition to Lilly's obesity and diabetes portfolio, with findings from seven additional phase three trials expected by year-end. The company expressed satisfaction with the relatively low discontinuation rates due to side effects, making it a promising development in metabolic health.
