The U.K. Parliament has enacted the House of Lords (Hereditary Peers) Act of 2026, officially abolishing the right of aristocrats to inherit seats in its upper chamber. This historic reform dismantles a feudal system dating back to 1066, under which 92 lords held legislative power purely through lineage. While some current hereditary peers will retain their positions until death, they are prohibited from passing them to their descendants.
