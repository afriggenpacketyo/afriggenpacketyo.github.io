A central Berlin street, formerly known as Mohrenstrasse, has been officially renamed Anton-Wilhelm-Amo-Strasse after a prolonged legal battle, honoring the first Africa-born scholar to receive a European doctorate. The change, upheld by the Higher Administrative Court, addresses the street's historical ties to the racist practice of "court moors" and Germany's colonial past. Anton Wilhelm Amo, enslaved as a boy from Ghana, became a philosopher and legal scholar whose work on the rights of Black people was largely erased from European intellectual history. This renaming, occurring on International Day for the Remembrance of the Slave Trade and its Abolition, symbolizes a significant act of decolonization and a greater appreciation for Black history and resilience in Germany.
