India's Brush with Presidential Rule During Indira Gandhi's Emergency

2025-06-08 18:02:57 EDT

During Indira Gandhi's Emergency in the mid-1970s, India explored shifting from a parliamentary democracy to a presidential system, according to historian Srinath Raghavan. Inspired by Charles de Gaulle's France, Gandhi's aides proposed a strong presidency to bypass parliamentary constraints and an "obstructionist" judiciary. While the shift never fully materialized, the Forty-second Amendment Act in 1976 expanded Parliament's powers and limited judicial review, reflecting a move towards a powerful executive. After Gandhi's defeat in 1977, subsequent amendments reversed some of these changes, but the idea resurfaced in 1982, highlighting the ongoing debate about India's governance structure.
