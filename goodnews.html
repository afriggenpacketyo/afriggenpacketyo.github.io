2025-05-22 00:03:20 EDT

Astronomers Konstantin Batygin and Fred Adams based their findings on the study of the orbital motions of Jupiter's moons, Amalthea and Thebe. Their research supports the bottom-up method of planet formation for the gas giant. This discovery provides a valuable benchmark for understanding the evolution of Jupiter and its role in the Solar System's development. The team's work gives us a new tool for understanding Jupiter and its role in the Solar System, where it is thought to have played a vital part in stabilizing the planets enough so that life could emerge on Earth.
