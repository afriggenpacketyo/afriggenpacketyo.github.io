Aviation Pioneer Wally Funk, Oldest Woman in Space, Dies at 87

Wally Funk, a trailblazing aviation pioneer and the oldest woman to travel to space, has died peacefully at 87 in Grapevine, Texas. Funk, a member of the Mercury 13 program, outscored many male astronauts but was initially barred from space due to her gender. She went on to break numerous barriers, becoming the first female flight instructor at Fort Sill, the first female FAA inspector, and the first female NTSB air safety investigator. At 82, she finally achieved her lifelong dream of space travel with Blue Origin, setting a Guinness World Record.
