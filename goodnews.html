Study Suggests Early Gut Bacteria Can Protect Infants From Lung Infections

2025-06-04 20:48:13 EDT

UK scientists have discovered that the first bacteria to colonize a newborn's gut may protect against dangerous infections. The study found that infants with the bacteria Bifidobacterium longum in their gut had half the risk of being admitted to the hospital with lung infections. Researchers at University College London and the Sanger Institute analyzed stool samples from 1,082 newborns and tracked their health for two years. The protective bacteria were more commonly found in babies born via vaginal delivery rather than cesarean section.
