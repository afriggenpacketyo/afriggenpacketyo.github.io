Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is marking a major meteorological milestone: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation. This unbroken sequence of handwritten data is the longest of its kind in the UK and Ireland, predating even the modern naming system for clouds. The records, which are still maintained by a human observer today, provide an invaluable history of the region's climate. The observatory's work also led to key scientific developments, such as the invention of the four-cup anemometer for accurately measuring wind speed in the 19th century.
