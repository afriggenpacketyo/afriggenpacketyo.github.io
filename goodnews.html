While a full merger is unlikely, Humberto's presence has already influenced Imelda's track, possibly steering it away from the U.S. mainland. The interaction depends on the storms' relative strengths, with forecasters noting that a stronger storm can absorb a weaker one, though they more commonly "dance" around each other before separating.

2025-10-01 13:39:24 EDT

As the U.S. federal government enters its first shutdown since 2018, historical precedent shows that prolonged closures have become more common in recent decades. The longest shutdown in history was 35 days in 2018-2019 over border wall funding. Other significant shutdowns include a 21-day impasse in 1995-1996 and a 16-day closure in 2013 over the Affordable Care Act. The practice of shutting down the government is a relatively recent phenomenon, stemming from a 1980 legal opinion on the Antideficiency Act that prohibited federal agencies from operating without appropriated funds, transforming budget disputes into high-stakes political standoffs.
