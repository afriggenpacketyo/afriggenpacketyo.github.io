Nocturnal insects, specifically beetles equipped with specialized heat-sensing antennae, could detect this infrared glow from a distance. Experiments using 3D-printed heated cones confirmed that the infrared signal alone was sufficient to attract numerous pollinators. This discovery suggests that infrared radiation might be the oldest known pollination signal, highlighting a unique and ancient co-evolutionary relationship between plants and insects.
