Social Security Benefits to Increase by 2.8% in 2026

2025-10-24 10:37:50 EDT

The Social Security Administration has announced a 2.8% cost-of-living adjustment (COLA) for 2026, which will increase the average monthly retirement benefit by approximately $56 starting in January. This annual adjustment is calculated based on inflation to ensure that the purchasing power of benefits for about 75 million recipients does not erode over time. The 2026 increase is slightly higher than the 2.5% adjustment in 2025 and is in line with the 20-year average. Beneficiaries will be notified of their new payment amounts by mail in early December.
