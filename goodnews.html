Health Experts Explain Physiological Reasons for Decreased Alcohol Tolerance with Age

2025-12-08 05:00:00 EST

As people enter middle age, they often find their tolerance for alcohol decreases, a phenomenon explained by several physiological changes. Medical experts note that the liver enzymes responsible for metabolizing alcohol become less efficient over time. Additionally, the natural loss of muscle mass and its replacement with fatty tissue means there is less water in the body to dilute alcohol, leading to a higher blood alcohol concentration from the same number of drinks. For women in perimenopause, alcohol can also exacerbate symptoms like mood swings and sleep disruption. Health professionals advise that this reduced tolerance serves as a natural prompt to cut back on drinking, which carries increased health risks with age.
