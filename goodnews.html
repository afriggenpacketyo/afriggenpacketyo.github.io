Coastal Rail Service Between San Diego and Orange Counties to Resume After Landslide Risk Mitigation

2025-05-29 21:20:44 EDT

Coastal rail service connecting Orange and San Diego counties is scheduled to resume on June 7 after nearly six weeks of construction to reinforce tracks vulnerable to landslides and coastal erosion. Crews placed 5,900 tons of boulders and will add 240,000 cubic yards of sand to stabilize the rail line. The Orange County Transportation Authority estimates the project's total cost at $300 million. The rail line, serving Metrolink and Amtrak's Pacific Surfliner, has faced recurring interruptions due to eroding bluffs.
