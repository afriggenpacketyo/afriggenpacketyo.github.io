Ancient Chinese Tool Discovery Suggests Early Technological Innovation

2026-01-27 11:01:08 EST

A new study suggests China was at the forefront of Stone Age technology up to 160,000 years ago, with the discovery of sophisticated hafted tools at the Xigou archaeological site. These composite tools, used for cutting, piercing, and sawing, represent the earliest evidence of their kind in eastern Asia. The findings challenge previous assumptions that hominin technologies in the region lacked innovation during the Middle Pleistocene. This research indicates that early hominins in China possessed advanced cognitive and technical abilities comparable to their counterparts in Africa and Eurasia, reshaping understanding of human evolution.
