Physicists Unravel the Science Behind Draining Liquids to the Last Drop

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Two physicists at Brown University, Thomas Dutta and Jay Tang, have applied fluid mechanics to answer an everyday kitchen question: how long it takes to drain the last drops from bottles. Their research, published in Physics of Fluids, used Navier-Stokes equations and experiments with various liquids like milk, olive oil, and maple syrup. They found that low-viscosity liquids such as milk drain in about 30 seconds, while thicker olive oil takes over nine minutes, and cold maple syrup several hours. This study highlights how scientific inquiry can demystify common phenomena and offer practical insights, even for tasks like optimizing the drying time for a cast-iron wok.
