2025-09-13 06:39:00 EDT

An exhibition in Potsdam, Germany, is revisiting the complex legacy of the Plattenbau, the prefabricated concrete housing blocks that defined much of East Germany's urban landscape. These buildings were initially promoted as a socialist utopian solution to post-war housing shortages but later became symbols of social decay and extremism following German reunification. The exhibition, "Wohnkomplex: Art and Life in Plattenbau," explores this divisive architectural heritage through artworks that reflect collective memories and the painful transformations of the era. Curators aim to foster a nuanced discussion about belonging and community, moving beyond simple nostalgia for the former East Germany.
