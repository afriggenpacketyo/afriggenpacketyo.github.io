NASA Deactivates Instrument on Voyager 1 to Extend Historic Mission Life

2026-04-19 01:40:28 EDT

NASA has deactivated one of Voyager 1's science instruments, the Low-energy Charged Particles experiment (LECP), as a critical measure to conserve power and extend the spacecraft's operational life. Launched in 1977 for a projected five-year mission, Voyager 1 is now nearly 49 years old and over 15 billion miles from Earth, holding the distinction as the most distant human-made object. Its radioisotope thermoelectric generator power source is steadily diminishing, compelling engineers to make strategic decisions to keep its remaining vital instruments, including plasma wave and magnetic field detectors, functioning into the 2030s. This deliberate shutdown aims to preserve the historic interstellar mission for as long as feasible.
