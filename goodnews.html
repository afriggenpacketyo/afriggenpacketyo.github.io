2025-10-15 05:00:00 EDT

The term 'broadcasting,' now synonymous with radio and television, surprisingly originated in agriculture, describing the scattering of seeds over a wide area. This method of planting, first recorded in the 18th century, remains in use today, even for activities like lawn seeding. The word's modern media application emerged in the early 1920s with the advent of commercial radio, notably Frank Conrad's broadcast of presidential election results. By the 1930s, the Communications Act officially cemented its new meaning, shaping how Americans consumed news and entertainment and influencing major historical events.
