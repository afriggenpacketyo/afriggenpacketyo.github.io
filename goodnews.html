Chinese Scientists Use AI to Map Moon's Far Side Chemical Makeup

2026-03-07 08:00:08 EST

Chinese scientists have successfully utilized an AI model to determine the chemical composition of the moon's far side, providing new insights into a long-standing lunar mystery. This breakthrough enabled the creation of the first high-precision global distribution map of major oxides on the moon, a region that had previously remained chemically unmapped. Researchers from SITP, an affiliate of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, collaborated with Tongji University and other institutes for this study, published in Nature Sensors. The findings reveal exposure characteristics of deep materials in the South Pole-Aitken basin and compositional patterns of far-side lunar terrains, advancing understanding of lunar geology.
