2026-03-08 01:18:54 EDT

Daylight Saving Time for 2026 is set to commence early Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, requiring most Americans to "spring forward" and consequently lose an hour of sleep. This annual tradition effectively shifts an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening, and will remain in effect until November 1. Historically adopted during wartime to conserve fuel, subsequent studies have indicated minimal actual energy savings, and the time change has been associated with some negative health effects.
