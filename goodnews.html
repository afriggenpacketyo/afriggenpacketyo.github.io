US Overdose Deaths Fell Sharply in 2024

2025-05-14 13:58:44 EDT

US drug overdose deaths fell 27% in 2024, marking the largest one-year decline ever recorded, with an estimated 80,000 deaths compared to 110,000 in 2023. Experts attribute the reduction to increased availability of naloxone, expanded addiction treatment, shifts in drug use, and the impact of opioid lawsuit settlement money. Despite the significant drop, annual overdose deaths remain higher than pre-pandemic levels, and some worry that recent declines could be slowed or stopped by reductions in federal funding and the public health workforce.
