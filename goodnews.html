Berlin's pioneering queer history in the 1920s highlights early LGBTQ+ activism and community.

2026-04-11 02:34:00 EDT

During Germany's Weimar Republic, Berlin emerged as a global hub for queer life and LGBTQ+ research, despite Paragraph 175 criminalizing sexual acts between men. Physician Magnus Hirschfeld founded the Scientific-Humanitarian Committee in 1897 and the Institute for Sexual Science in 1919, challenging prevailing norms and fostering an open atmosphere for artists. This vibrant era ended abruptly with the Nazi rise to power in 1933, leading to the destruction of Hirschfeld's institute and brutal persecution under Paragraph 175. Today, Berlin has regained its status as a prominent center of queer culture, with commemorative sites marking its rich and turbulent past.
