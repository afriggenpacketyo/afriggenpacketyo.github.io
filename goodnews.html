2025-12-08 05:00:00 EST

Many people find their tolerance for alcohol decreases as they enter middle age, a phenomenon explained by physiological changes. According to medical experts, the liver enzymes responsible for breaking down alcohol become less efficient over time, and the body's composition shifts from muscle to fat. Since muscle contains water that dilutes alcohol, having less muscle mass results in a higher blood alcohol concentration from the same amount of drink. This can also exacerbate symptoms like mood swings and poor sleep, particularly for women experiencing perimenopause.
