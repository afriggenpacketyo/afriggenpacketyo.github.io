Social Security benefits generally protected from medical debt collection

Social Security benefits are largely protected from garnishment by private creditors, including those attempting to collect unpaid medical debt. Federal law generally prevents hospitals, medical providers, and collection agencies from directly seizing Social Security checks for consumer debts. While benefits are protected, significant unpaid medical debt can still damage credit scores and lead to lawsuits. If a creditor obtains a judgment, funds already deposited into a bank account may face complications, though banks are required to protect a portion of directly deposited benefits. Certain federal debts, like taxes or child support, are exceptions, but standard medical debt typically does not fall into this category. Exploring debt relief options like negotiation or settlement is advised for unmanageable medical debt.
