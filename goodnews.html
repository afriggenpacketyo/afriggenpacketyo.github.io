Golden Eagles Return to Northern England After Long Absence

2025-05-28 21:44:16 EDT

Golden eagles are increasingly venturing into England from southern Scotland, raising hopes that they may establish nesting sites after being absent for years. This resurgence is largely attributed to the South of Scotland Golden Eagle Project (SSGEP), which has successfully translocated juvenile and sub-adult eagles from the Scottish Highlands. While it is still early to determine if they will settle permanently, the birds have been spotted in Northumberland, the North Pennines, the Lake District, and as far as Lancashire and Yorkshire. The last resident golden eagle in England disappeared from the Lake District in 2015, but conservationists are optimistic about their return.
