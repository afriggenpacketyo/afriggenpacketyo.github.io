Understanding the Air Quality Index and Its Impact on Public Health

2025-06-03 11:48:41 EDT

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a nationwide system used to measure air pollution severity and associated health risks, utilizing six color-coded categories ranging from 'good' to 'hazardous'. The Environmental Protection Agency calculates the AQI based on five major pollutants regulated by the Clean Air Act, including ground-level ozone and particulate matter. Higher AQI values indicate greater health risks, with 'very unhealthy' levels posing risks to the general public and 'hazardous' levels indicating an air quality emergency. Real-time AQI data is available through the EPA's AirNow.gov site, providing information on pollutant levels, at-risk populations, and safe activity recommendations for specific areas.
