Happy, the Bronx Zoo Elephant Known for Self-Recognition, Euthanized at 55

2026-05-28 01:56:33 EDT

Happy, an Asian elephant at the Bronx Zoo renowned for her self-recognition in mirror tests and as the subject of a prominent animal rights case, has been euthanized at the age of 55. Zoo officials cited age-related conditions, including a decline in kidney or liver function, arthritis, and uterine tumors, as reasons for the decision. Happy had lived at the zoo for nearly 50 years and was known for her engagement with keepers and her participation in research demonstrating elephant self-awareness. She was the last elephant on exhibit in New York City, as the zoo's parent institution phased out elephant exhibits. The Nonhuman Rights Project had previously sued the zoo seeking Happy's transfer to a sanctuary, arguing she was unlawfully deprived of liberty, but the New York high court rejected the claim. Her passing marks the end of an era for the zoo and raises further questions about elephant captivity.
