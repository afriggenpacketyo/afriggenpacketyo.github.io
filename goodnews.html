UK Sky-Watchers Anticipate Stunning 'Blood Moon' Total Lunar Eclipse This Evening

2025-09-07 01:54:00 EDT

Sky-watchers across much of the UK are set to witness a spectacular total lunar eclipse this evening, which will transform the full Moon into a striking "Blood Moon" as it passes through Earth's shadow. The full eclipse is scheduled from 18:30 to 19:52 BST, with specific visibility depending on local moonrise times across the country. This distinctive reddish hue is a result of Rayleigh scattering, the same atmospheric phenomenon that creates red sunsets, where Earth's atmosphere refracts longer red wavelengths onto the lunar surface. Dr. Edward Bloomer of the Royal Observatory Greenwich encourages viewing this event as a tangible demonstration of the solar system's mechanics, noting the next UK-visible lunar eclipse is in August 2026.
