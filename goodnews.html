Tiny Zooplankton Play Oversized Role in Carbon Sequestration, Study Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny, migrating zooplankton in the Southern Ocean play a crucial role in combating climate change by locking away vast amounts of carbon. These minuscule creatures gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface in the spring, grow fat, and then sink deep into the ocean for the winter, where they burn this fat. This process effectively transports and stores an estimated 65 million tonnes of carbon annually, equivalent to the emissions of 55 million cars. The discovery underscores the vital, and previously underestimated, climate-regulating service provided by these often-overlooked marine animals.
