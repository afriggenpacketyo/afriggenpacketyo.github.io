Archaeologists Believe They Have Solved Mystery of Peru's 'Band of Holes'

2025-11-27 12:01:32 EST

Researchers may have finally solved the long-standing mystery of Peru's "Band of Holes," a series of over 5,200 man-made pits in the Pisco Valley. Led by archaeologist Charles Stanish, a team used advanced drone mapping and microbotanical analysis to study the site, known locally as Monte Sierpe. Their findings suggest the holes were created in the pre-Inca period as a marketplace and later adapted by the Inca civilization into a sophisticated storage and accounting system for agricultural goods. The layout mirrors the structure of Incan khipus (knotted-string records), and sediment analysis revealed traces of crops like maize and plants used for packaging.
