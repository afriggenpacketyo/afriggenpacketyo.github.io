Key signs to watch for include increased frequency of urination, difficulty starting, a weak flow, and blood in urine or semen. While these can be caused by other conditions, a medical check-up is important. Diagnosis typically involves a combination of a PSA blood test, an MRI scan, and a biopsy, as no single test is definitive. Risk factors include being over 50, having a family history of the disease, and being of Black ethnicity.
