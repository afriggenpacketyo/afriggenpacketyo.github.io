Tiny Zooplankton Play Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny marine creatures called zooplankton are "unsung heroes" in the fight against climate change, locking away vast amounts of carbon in the deep ocean. These animals gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface in the spring, grow fat, and then sink hundreds of meters to hibernate, where they burn the fat and release carbon dioxide. This process, known as the seasonal vertical migration pump, stores as much carbon as the annual emissions of 55 million cars. The discovery highlights the crucial but often overlooked role of these small organisms in regulating the global climate.
