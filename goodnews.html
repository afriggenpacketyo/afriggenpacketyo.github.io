Britain's Iconic Cerne Abbas Giant Undergoes Restoration Amidst Climate Change Challenges

2026-05-27 19:05:48 EDT

The Cerne Abbas Giant, a 55-meter naked, club-wielding chalk figure in Dorset, is undergoing restoration by the National Trust to maintain its prominent outline. Changing weather patterns, including heavier winter rains and mild, damp conditions, are making it harder to preserve the figure, causing chalk erosion and algae growth. National Trust staff and volunteers are packing 17 tonnes of fresh chalk onto the steep hillside in a physically demanding effort that could take up to 15 days. This work comes after public donations helped acquire additional land around the Giant, and recent scientific analysis suggests its origins date back to the late Saxon period, much later than previously imagined.
