Oldest Pterosaur in North America Discovered in Arizona

The flying reptile, named Eotephradactylus mcintireae, was approximately the size of a small seagull and lived around 209 million years ago. This significant finding, detailed in a Smithsonian-led study published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, helps to fill a crucial gap in the fossil record from the late Triassic period. The discovery provides invaluable new insights into the evolution of pterosaurs, which were the first vertebrates after insects to develop powered flight, and the ecosystem they inhabited before a major extinction event.
