2025-10-19 06:30:00 EDT

According to neuroscientist Dr. Charan Ranganath, our common understanding of memory as a faithful filing cabinet of the past is incorrect. Instead, memory functions as a dynamic resource that helps us interpret the present, plan for the future, and construct a meaningful personal narrative. He explains that forgetting is a crucial part of this process, and what we recall is often a condensed "highlight reel" shaped by emotion and subsequent reflection, not a perfect recording of experience. This malleability means that our relationship to past events, including traumatic ones, can change over time, and it also makes both individual and collective memory susceptible to manipulation.
