Research Identifies Four Key Conditions for Effective Cash Transfer Programs to Improve Health

2026-02-28 08:38:25 EST

Research across 37 countries reveals that cash transfer programs significantly improve health outcomes when four specific conditions are met, a success largely absent in most US pilots. Effective transfers must be substantial enough to alter daily realities, directly address specific health barriers, achieve a large population scale, and integrate into existing social infrastructure. While modest sums can be life-saving in low-income countries, typical US pilot amounts often fall short of covering high living costs. The federal SNAP program, however, embodies these conditions by providing meaningful support, targeting food security, reaching millions, and linking with other public systems, demonstrating a successful model for the US.
