Fact Check Finds No Scientific Evidence Linking Sunscreen Use to Increased Cancer Risk

2025-07-19 08:54:00 EDT

Contrary to claims circulating on social media, there is no scientific evidence that using sunscreen increases the risk of skin cancer. A DW fact-check debunks these assertions, explaining that rising skin cancer diagnoses are linked to factors like better detection, increased sun exposure, and climate change, not sunscreen use. While countries with high sunscreen use, like Australia, also have high skin cancer rates, this is due to high UV radiation levels and population genetics. Health authorities continue to strongly recommend sunscreen as a crucial tool for filtering harmful UV rays and preventing melanomas.
