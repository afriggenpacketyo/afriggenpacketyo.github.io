Rare first edition of 'Birds of America' displayed in Glasgow exhibition

2026-05-01 03:56:32 EDT

An exhibition at the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Glasgow is showcasing two volumes of John James Audubon's 'Birds of America,' one of the world's most valuable books. The college acquired these first-edition volumes in 1838, and they contain life-size, hand-colored illustrations of over 200 North American bird species from the early 19th century. Each page measures nearly a meter tall, requiring two people to turn, and only one plate can be displayed at a time. The exhibition highlights the book's artistic and ornithological significance, noting its expensive production and Audubon's efforts to promote it in the UK. The college also acknowledges the contributions of enslaved and indigenous people in its creation, recognizing historical harms.
