Philosophical Inquiry Explores the Core Appeal of Games and Pitfalls of Gamification

2026-02-15 06:45:00 EST

Philosopher C. Thi Nguyen, in his book "The Score," argues that games offer a profound window into human agency, revealing why people voluntarily undertake unnecessary obstacles to overcome them. He distinguishes between "achievement play" (winning as the point) and "striving play" (struggle for absorption), exemplified by rock climbing and fly-fishing. Nguyen contends that game rules, like yoga poses, create freedom by forcing new ways of acting. However, he warns that the "gamification" of modern life, where scores and metrics are tied to real-world outcomes like grades or salary, can lead to "value capture," replacing richer human judgment with simplified, unchosen goals. This insightful analysis challenges conventional views on play and purpose.
