Klamath River Reemerges After Historic Dam Removal, Offering New Recreational Opportunities

2025-06-07 11:00:08 EDT

After the removal of four hydroelectric dams, the Klamath River is experiencing a rebirth, revealing 45 miles of previously submerged river between the Keno and Iron Gate dams. This restoration marks the end of a decades-long campaign led by Indigenous tribes, environmental groups, and fishing advocates, aiming to revitalize the river's ecology and cultural significance. As the river reclaims its natural course, new recreational opportunities like rafting and hiking are emerging, with five public recreation sites planned for completion by August 1. The recovery of the Klamath serves as a hopeful example of how diverse interests can align to enact positive change, even amidst climate challenges.
