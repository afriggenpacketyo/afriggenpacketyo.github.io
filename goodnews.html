2025-05-03 05:00:56 EDT

A new clinical trial published in The New England Journal of Medicine reveals that semaglutide injections can reverse life-threatening cases of liver disease, specifically metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH). The study involved 800 participants across 37 countries who received weekly semaglutide injections or a placebo. After 72 weeks, 62.9% of the semaglutide group showed less inflammation and fat accumulation in their livers, compared to 34.3% in the placebo group. Man's Snake Venom Injections Lead to 'Unparalleled' Antivenom

2025-05-03 02:29:15 EDT

Scientists have developed an "unparalleled" antivenom using antibodies from the blood of Tim Friede, a man who deliberately injected himself with snake venom for nearly two decades. Friede's blood contains antibodies that protect against fatal doses from a wide range of venomous snake species. This could lead to a universal antivenom, which is significant because current therapies must match the specific snake species. The research identified two broadly neutralising antibodies that could target two classes of neurotoxin, offering protection against 13 of 19 deadly elapid species in mice experiments.
