Chinese Scientists Develop E. coli-Based Therapy to Combat Breast Tumors in Mice.

2026-03-18 02:00:25 EDT

Chinese scientists have successfully engineered a strain of E. coli bacteria, specifically Nissle 1917 (EcN), to target and colonize breast tumor cells in mice. This innovative therapy enables the bacteria to produce and deliver an existing cancer drug directly from within the tumor, significantly reducing the toxic side effects commonly associated with traditional chemotherapy. Published in the peer-reviewed journal PLOS Biology, the research demonstrates EcN's remarkable capacity to accumulate and proliferate within solid tumors, establishing it as a highly promising "live vector" for bacterial cancer therapy. This breakthrough offers a novel pathway for developing more precise and effective targeted cancer treatments in the future.
