Study Highlights Potential Rise in Children's Cavities if Fluoride Removed from Water

2025-05-30 11:00:19 EDT

A new study published in Jama Health Forum estimates that removing fluoride from public drinking water supplies in the U.S. could result in an additional 25.4 million cavities in children over five years. This would represent a 7.5% increase in cavities, costing $9.8 billion and resulting in the loss of 2.9 million quality-adjusted life years. The study emphasizes that fluoride is highly effective in preventing tooth decay and that the additional cases would disproportionately affect children most at-risk for tooth decay. The findings counter arguments made by some officials who advocate for ending water fluoridation.
