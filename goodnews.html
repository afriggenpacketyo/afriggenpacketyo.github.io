Opinion: China's Economic Leapfrog Explained by "Privilege of Historic Backwardness"

2026-07-07 21:30:07 EDT

This opinion piece by Alex Lo explores Leon Trotsky's theory of "the privilege of historic backwardness" as a framework to understand China's remarkable economic and technological leapfrog into the 21st century. The theory posits that a backward country can assimilate the advanced countries' achievements, selectively adopting innovations and skipping intermediate developmental stages. Lo argues that China, despite the devastation of the Mao era, successfully utilized this "privilege" by building modern frameworks that enabled rapid growth, much like some African economies adopting 5G without prior infrastructure. This perspective offers a compelling and unique analysis of China's accelerated developmental trajectory.
