2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

Following a major earthquake in Russia that triggered Pacific-wide alerts, this article explains the science of how tsunamis are formed and what to do if one occurs. A tsunami is typically generated by a strong underwater earthquake, at least magnitude 6.5 to 7, that causes vertical movement of the sea floor. While challenging to predict accurately, scientists use tide gauges and deep ocean pressure sensors to forecast their path and potential impact. For safety, officials advise moving immediately to higher ground—at least 30 to 100 feet above sea level—upon receiving a warning, as the waves can travel at jet-speed and are extremely powerful.
