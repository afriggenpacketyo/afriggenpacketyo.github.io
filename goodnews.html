Research Explores the Complex Role of Oxytocin in Human Social Bonds

2026-02-14 05:37:00 EST

Oxytocin, frequently dubbed the "love hormone," is an ancient chain of amino acids, present across many species and known for its role in childbirth. Early research, including a prominent 2005 trust study, suggested it significantly influenced human bonding and trust, sparking widespread public and commercial interest. Scientists now understand oxytocin's function is more nuanced, amplifying existing social emotions, whether positive or negative, rather than acting as a simple "love potion."
