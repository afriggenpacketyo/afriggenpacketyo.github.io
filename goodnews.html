FDA Approves First New Sunscreen Ingredient in Over 25 Years

2026-06-09 18:59:01 EDT

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration has approved bemotrizinol, the first new sunscreen ingredient to receive approval in over 25 years, making it available to American consumers. This chemical, already in use in Europe and elsewhere, has met FDA standards for safety and efficacy in protecting against dangerous ultraviolet rays with minimal irritation or skin absorption. Bemotrizinol will be initially marketed as Parsol Shield and is noted for its ability to protect against both UVA and UVB rays without leaving a white residue, unlike mineral-based sunscreens. This approval comes after decades of delays in the FDA's review process for new sunscreen ingredients, with bemotrizinol being the first to benefit from a streamlined process established by Congress in 2020.
