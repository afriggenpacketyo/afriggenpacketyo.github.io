Federal Judge Mandates Improved Conditions for California ICE Detainees

2026-02-11 18:44:29 EST

A federal judge has issued an order compelling the U.S. government to ensure adequate medical care, timely access to medications, and legal representation for detainees at a California immigration detention facility. The ruling, stemming from a lawsuit by seven detainees, also mandates "temperature-appropriate clothing and blankets" and at least one hour of daily outdoor access. Detainees had previously reported severe conditions, including denied medications and insufficient food, at the CoreCivic-operated center. The judge further ordered an independent monitor to oversee medical records, conduct inspections, and interview staff and patients, aiming to address the alleged mistreatment and ensure due process.
