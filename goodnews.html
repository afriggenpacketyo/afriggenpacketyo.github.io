Debunking Brain Training: Evidence-Based Strategies for Cognitive Health

2026-04-02 16:00:00 EDT

Despite numerous self-help promises, experts largely dismiss the idea that specific "brain training" tasks or games can significantly improve long-term general intelligence. Research indicates negligible benefits from such targeted cognitive exercises. Instead, evidence-based strategies for maintaining cognitive health as one ages focus on holistic well-being: a healthy diet, regular exercise, stress reduction, and adequate sleep are foundational. Beyond these, cultivating curiosity, learning new skills with "desirable difficulty," and fostering social connections and a sense of purpose are crucial. These practices contribute to overall flourishing and are linked to less cognitive decline, shifting the focus from "training" to nurturing the mind.

Strategies to Prevent the IRS from Offsetting Your Tax Refund

2026-04-02 15:14:53 EDT

For taxpayers with outstanding debts, the IRS can legally intercept tax refunds to cover federal or state taxes, defaulted student loans, child support, or unemployment compensation. To prevent this, individuals should address their debts before filing, such as paying off overdue amounts or entering an installment agreement with the IRS. Spouses filing jointly can protect their portion of a refund by submitting an injured spouse allocation form. If the debt is disputed, contacting the reporting agency is crucial. Additionally, adjusting tax withholding can reduce the amount at risk in future years. Various tax relief strategies, like an Offer in Compromise, can also help manage debt.
