Scientists Discover Transmissible Skin Cancer Spreading Among Catfish in Lake Memphremagog

2026-07-22 13:33:15 EDT

Scientists have identified a rare transmissible skin cancer, melanoma, spreading among brown bullhead catfish in Lake Memphremagog, Vermont. First observed in 2013, the black blotches on the fish are not caused by environmental pollutants or viruses, but by cancer cells that appear to transmit directly from fish to fish. Genetic analysis revealed the tumors were clones, suggesting they originated in one fish and have been spreading since. Researchers suspect transmission may occur during spawning, when fish aggregate and scratch each other. This discovery adds catfish to a short list of animals with transmissible cancers and offers new avenues for studying cancer metastasis.
