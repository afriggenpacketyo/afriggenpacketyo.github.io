Despite being available for two decades, in-flight Wi-Fi remains inconsistent, caught between being a miraculous convenience and a frustrating failure. While satellite technology is superior and becoming more common, many regional and short-haul jets still use the older, less reliable air-to-ground service. Airlines are slowly upgrading their entire fleets, a complex and costly process, but promise that consistent, free, high-speed internet should be standard by 2026.
