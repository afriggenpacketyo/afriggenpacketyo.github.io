The planet was found using gravitational microlensing, a method based on Albert Einstein's theory of relativity that has only been successfully used three times before. This technique relies on a massive celestial object passing in front of a more distant star, causing the star's light to bend and magnify, revealing the object's presence. The discovery, first spotted in 2021, required extensive data analysis and highlights a powerful, albeit challenging, method for detecting planets that are otherwise invisible to more common techniques.
