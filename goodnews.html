Astronomers Witness Earliest Stages of Rocky Planet Formation

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb and ALMA telescopes, astronomers have observed the earliest known signs of rocky planet formation around a young, sun-like star named HOPS-315, located 1,370 light-years away. The discovery provides a direct glimpse into a process similar to how Earth may have formed over 4.5 billion years ago, detecting silicate minerals condensing in the star's gas disk. This breakthrough suggests that the initial steps of planet formation could be a common occurrence in the universe. The findings, published in Nature, offer new opportunities to study how frequently Earth-like worlds might develop.
