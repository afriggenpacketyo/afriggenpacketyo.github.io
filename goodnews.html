In the canals of Xochimilco, Mexico City, scientists are using an innovative technique to track the critically endangered axolotl, a unique species of salamander. Researchers from Mexico’s National Autonomous University are filtering water to detect environmental DNA (eDNA), or genetic traces left behind by the elusive creatures. This method has become crucial as traditional net surveys often come up empty, with the wild population having plummeted from an estimated 6,000 per square kilometer in 1998 to just 36 in 2014. The eDNA has confirmed their presence in protected refuge areas, offering a sliver of hope for the species' resilience if its habitat can be restored.
