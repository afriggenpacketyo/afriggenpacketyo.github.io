2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Paleontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new dinosaur species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils found in Kalasin Province. This plant-eating dinosaur, believed to have lived around 150 million years ago, possessed an unusually long neck, reaching up to 20 meters in length. Belonging to the Mamenchisauridae family, known for their elongated necks, this discovery marks the first of its kind in Thailand. Unique features, including a distinctive air-cavity structure in its vertebrae, distinguish it from other known dinosaurs.
