Britain's Oldest Prehistoric Rock Art Confirmed in Welsh Cave

2026-06-01 17:01:43 EDT

Painted rock art found in Gower's Bacon Hole cave in Wales has been definitively confirmed as the oldest prehistoric art in Britain, with scientific dating placing its creation at least 17,100 years ago. The series of 10 red horizontal stripes, initially discovered in 1912 but subsequently dismissed as natural mineral seepage, has been re-examined using advanced uranium-thorium dating techniques. This makes the artwork the oldest in north-western Europe. Archaeologist George Nash suggests the art may have functioned as a "communication system" for hunter-gatherers who inhabited the then-cold, treeless landscape of the region. The significant rediscovery underscores the rich prehistoric heritage of the Gower Peninsula and has prompted calls for the cave to be designated a scheduled monument for its protection.
