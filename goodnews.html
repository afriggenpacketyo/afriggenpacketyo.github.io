Understanding the "609 Letter" for Disputing Inaccurate Credit Report Debt

2026-02-03 14:41:35 EST

A "609 letter" is a formal request under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) for credit bureaus to verify specific items on a credit report, often used to challenge inaccurate or unverifiable debts. If bureaus cannot provide adequate documentation within 30 days, the item must be removed. This strategy is effective for genuine errors like accounts never opened or incorrect payment histories, but not for legitimate debts owed. For actual debt problems, alternative strategies like debt settlement, management plans, or balance transfer cards are recommended over solely relying on a 609 letter. (not investment advice)
