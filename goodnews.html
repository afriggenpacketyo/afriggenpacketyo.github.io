James Watson, DNA Double Helix Co-Discoverer, Dies at 97

2025-11-07 15:28:35 EST

James D. Watson, Nobel laureate and co-discoverer of DNA's double-helix structure, has passed away at 97. His groundbreaking 1953 discovery with Francis Crick and Maurice Wilkins revolutionized medicine, crimefighting, and genealogy, providing crucial insight into how hereditary information is stored and duplicated. Despite his immense scientific contributions, Watson faced widespread condemnation and professional censure late in life for making offensive and racist remarks about intelligence and race. His legacy remains complicated, marked by both unparalleled scientific achievement and deeply regrettable public statements that were unsupported by science, leading to the revocation of several honorary titles.
