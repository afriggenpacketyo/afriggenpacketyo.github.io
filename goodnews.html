Financial Experts Explain Why a Credit Card Charge-Off is Generally Worse Than Debt Settlement

2025-10-28 13:03:43 EDT

When dealing with overwhelming credit card debt, a charge-off is typically more damaging to one's credit than a debt settlement. A charge-off occurs when a creditor gives up on collecting a debt after about six months of nonpayment and it remains on a credit report for seven years, signaling a serious default to future lenders. In contrast, debt settlement is a proactive negotiation to pay less than the full amount owed. While both negatively impact a credit score, settlement demonstrates a resolution and offers more control over the financial recovery process.
