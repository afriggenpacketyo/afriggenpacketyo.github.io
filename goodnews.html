The Word 'Gossip' Transformed from 'Godparent' to Negative, Gendered Term

2026-07-16 05:00:00 EDT

The word "gossip" has undergone a fascinating and significant transformation from its Old English root, "godsibb," which originally meant "sponsor or godparent." It later evolved in Middle English to describe a close friend or neighbor, particularly women who gathered socially. However, by the 16th century, the term acquired a distinctly negative and gendered connotation, becoming primarily associated with women who spread rumors. Feminist writers argue this derogatory shift reflected broader societal efforts to devalue and control women's conversations and social standing. Today, some advocate for reclaiming "good gossip" as a valuable form of female connection and truth-telling.
