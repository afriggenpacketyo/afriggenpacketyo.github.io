Examining the Health and Beauty Claims Surrounding Sea Moss

Sea moss, a red seaweed often touted as a "superfood" by wellness influencers, is credited with numerous health and beauty benefits, from enhancing gut health to promoting radiant skin. Despite its long history as a food additive and traditional medicine, scientific evidence to substantiate these broad claims in humans remains scarce, primarily stemming from small cell or animal studies. While whole sea moss does provide beneficial fiber, antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, these nutrients are readily available from a diverse diet. However, potential risks include the accumulation of heavy metals if sourced from contaminated waters, and excessive iodine intake, which can negatively impact thyroid function. Experts advise caution, recommending consumption in whole or gel form rather than unregulated supplements, and emphasize that a balanced diet remains the most scientifically backed path to good health.
