Guide to Viewing the "Blood Moon" Total Lunar Eclipse Across the UK

2025-09-07 04:56:28 EDT

A total lunar eclipse, creating a "Blood Moon" effect, will be visible across much of the United Kingdom this evening. The phenomenon occurs when the Earth passes directly between the Sun and the Moon, casting a shadow that gives the lunar surface a deep red hue. While the full eclipse lasts for over an hour, visibility in the UK is limited to the period after moonrise, with eastern parts of the country getting the best and longest view. Astronomers advise finding a location with a clear view of the horizon to witness the event, which is safe to observe without special equipment and represents the mechanics of the solar system in action.
