Three-Eyed Ancient Predator Discovered in Canada

The fossil species, resembling the Japanese kaiju Mothra, was about the size of an index finger with spiny claws and a circular mouth. Mosura was a member of radiodonts, an extinct group distantly related to modern moths and arthropods like spiders and crabs. Researchers said it was "unlike any living animal" and had a unique abdomen-like body region with multiple segments. Scientists suspect this adaptation could be related to a specific habitat requiring more efficient respiration.
