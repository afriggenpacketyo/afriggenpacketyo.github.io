The method involves transferring the nuclear DNA from the parents' embryo into a donor egg with healthy mitochondria, the cells' energy sources. This prevents the transmission of incurable mitochondrial diseases, which can cause severe disability and are often fatal. The results, published in The New England Journal of Medicine, are being hailed as a major breakthrough, offering hope to families affected by these genetic conditions.
