Understanding the FCC's Role and Authority to Revoke TV Network Licenses

Following President Trump's suggestions that critical TV networks should lose their licenses and the suspension of Jimmy Kimmel, questions arise about the Federal Communications Commission's (FCC) power. The FCC, established in 1934, primarily regulates local TV and radio stations, not national networks directly, and sets rules for obscenity and decency. While it can influence mergers by denying license transfers, the FCC is legally barred from preventing specific viewpoints. Experts note that despite broad rules allowing significant leeway, enforcement of licensing regulations has decreased since the 1980s, though the theoretical power to rescind licenses remains.
