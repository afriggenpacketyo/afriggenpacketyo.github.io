2026-04-16 09:04:00 EDT

The Court of Justice of the European Union (ECJ) has ruled that users of online gambling services within the EU can sue betting operators for reimbursement of losses if the gambling was prohibited in their home country at the time of the bets. This ruling allows for legal action even if the country's laws have since changed to permit such activities. The ECJ clarified that member states can outlaw or restrict online gambling for consumer protection and social order reasons, citing the particular risks associated with online platforms due to constant access, player isolation, and anonymity. The decision could pave the way for numerous EU residents to reclaim funds lost through illegal online gambling.
