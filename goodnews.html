Chinese Desalination Plant Produces Fresh Water Cheaper Than Tap, Generates Green Hydrogen

2025-12-07 11:00:11 EST

A groundbreaking Chinese desalination plant in Rizhao has achieved a world-first by producing fresh water cheaper than tap water, while also generating green hydrogen, marking a significant environmental and technological leap. This innovative facility is powered entirely by seawater and low-grade waste heat from nearby steel and petrochemical plants, eliminating the need for costly desalination or precious freshwater resources. Operating continuously for over three weeks, the system processes 800 tonnes of seawater annually, yielding 450 cubic meters of ultra-pure fresh water ideal for industrial or residential use, 192,000 standard cubic meters of green hydrogen (enough to power 100 buses for 3,840km annually), and 350 tonnes of mineral-rich brine. This "one-in, three-out" circular economy model represents a substantial advancement in sustainable water and energy production.
