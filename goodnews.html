Daylight Saving Time 2026 Begins This Weekend, Shifting Clocks Forward

2026-03-07 06:00:13 EST

Daylight saving time for 2026 is scheduled to begin on Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, prompting most Americans to adjust their clocks forward by one hour. This shift, colloquially known as "springing forward," effectively moves an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening, resulting in a temporary loss of sleep. The practice, which has been consistently observed since the 1960s, originated during World War I to conserve fuel, though modern studies suggest its energy-saving impact is minimal. Only Hawaii and Arizona, along with several U.S. territories, do not participate in the annual time change.
