Japanese Superstitions Impede Sale and Rental of "Stigmatized Properties"

2026-07-18 03:32:00 EDT

In Japan, "jiko bukken," or stigmatized properties where deaths or unfortunate events occurred, are notoriously difficult to sell or rent due to deep-seated cultural superstitions linking death to impurity and misfortune. Real estate agents are legally obligated to disclose a property's history, often leading to significant rent reductions or prolonged vacancies, with some homes remaining empty for over 1,000 days. Specialist companies, like Kachimode, offer unique "ghost investigations" and "cleansing" services, sometimes involving Shinto priests, to reassure potential tenants or buyers. This cultural phenomenon contributes significantly to Japan's growing number of vacant homes, highlighting a unique challenge in the nation's housing market.
