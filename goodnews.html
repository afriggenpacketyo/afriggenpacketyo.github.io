Scientists Use Environmental DNA to Track Endangered Axolotls in Mexico City

2025-08-11 19:37:29 EDT

In Mexico City's Xochimilco canals, scientists are using environmental DNA (eDNA) to search for the critically endangered axolotl. With the salamander's population having plummeted from thousands to only a few dozen per square kilometer, traditional net surveys often come up empty. By filtering water and analyzing the genetic particles left behind by the creatures, researchers can detect their presence even without physically capturing them. This innovative technique offers a new sign of hope for the species' resilience and provides crucial data for conservation efforts aimed at restoring the axolotl's unique and polluted habitat.
