Understanding and Reducing "Phantom Energy" Waste in Homes

2025-09-03 11:04:07 EDT

"Phantom energy," or vampire energy, describes the electricity consumed by electronic devices that remain plugged in but are not actively in use, typically constituting 5% to 10% of household energy consumption. This constant, hidden drain exacerbates climate change by increasing demand for electricity, often sourced from emission-generating power plants. Common culprits include smart televisions with wake features and appliances with digital clocks. Experts advocate for simple solutions such as unplugging chargers, disabling unnecessary smart features, or using power strips to cut power. These small individual actions, when adopted widely, can collectively lead to substantial reductions in U.S. emissions and promote broader sustainable behaviors.
