Tiny Zooplankton Play a Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New scientific research reveals that tiny marine animals called zooplankton are 'unsung heroes' in the fight against climate change due to their significant role in carbon storage. These creatures, including copepods, gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the ocean's surface in Antarctica before migrating hundreds of meters deep to hibernate. During this deep-sea hibernation, they burn their fat reserves, effectively locking away vast amounts of carbon that would otherwise enter the atmosphere. The study quantifies this process, estimating it sequesters as much carbon annually as the emissions from 55 million cars, highlighting a crucial natural mechanism for mitigating global warming.
