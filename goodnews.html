Retail companies are increasingly using a B2B2C (business-to-business-to-consumer) model to reduce the impact of U.S. tariffs on consumer prices. In this strategy, a middleman company acts as the official importer, purchasing goods from the retailer at a wholesale price and paying the tariff on that lower value before the final sale to the consumer. This "tariff arbitrage" can significantly cut duty costs, allowing brands to avoid passing the full expense of the trade war onto their customers. While some see it as a creative adaptation, logistics experts warn it may not be a scalable long-term solution.
