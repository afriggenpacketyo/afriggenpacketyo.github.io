Understanding the Fiduciary Standard for Trustworthy Financial Advice

2025-09-24 08:58:09 EDT

To find trustworthy financial advice, individuals should ask if their advisor is a fiduciary, meaning they are legally obligated to act in the client's best interest. Unlike professionals adhering only to a "suitability" standard, fiduciaries must prioritize client needs and disclose any conflicts of interest, including how they are compensated. Failing to work with a fiduciary can lead to higher fees and misaligned advice, potentially costing tens of thousands over time. Resources like the SEC's Investment Adviser Public Disclosure site and organizations like the CFP Board can help identify fiduciary advisors. It is crucial to confirm an advisor is a fiduciary 100% of the time and get it in writing.
