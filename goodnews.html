Study Suggests Pigeons Use Magnetic Sensors in Livers for Navigation

2026-05-29 07:49:00 EDT

New research from the University of Bonn and the Max Planck Institute of Animal Behavior suggests that homing pigeons may use superparamagnetic macrophages in their livers for magnetoreception, a crucial component of their extraordinary navigational abilities. These immune cells, which accumulate iron during the breakdown of red blood cells, appear to transmit magnetic data to the pigeons' brains via nearby nerve cells. The study found that without these cells, pigeons lost their orientation capabilities in overcast conditions, highlighting a previously unknown and surprising mechanism for magnetic perception in animals and advancing our understanding of how birds navigate across vast distances.
