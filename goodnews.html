Fish-Rice Farming Could Combat Disease and Boost Income in Senegal

2026-07-12 06:49:40 EDT

An ambitious experiment in Senegal is exploring the integration of fish into rice farming to address food insecurity, poverty, and schistosomiasis, a debilitating disease. Scientists are introducing tilapia and African bonytongue fish into rice fields, with tilapia providing fertilizer through their waste and the bonytongue acting as snail predators to combat the disease's life cycle. This integrated approach aims to increase rice yields, boost farmer income, and reduce the incidence of schistosomiasis, which is prevalent in the region due to dam construction affecting snail populations. The project, involving local farmers, seeks to scale up this sustainable aquaculture practice, which has centuries of success in Asia but is less common in Africa. Early results suggest increased yields and income, offering a promising solution for local communities.
