Study Finds Group Singing Effectively Treats Postnatal Depression in New Mothers

2025-10-14 19:32:02 EDT

A three-year study has found that specially designed group singing classes are a clinically effective and potentially cost-effective treatment for mothers with postnatal depression. The research, published in the British Journal of Psychiatry, showed that while symptoms improved for all mothers in the study, those in the singing group sustained their improvement for six months after the sessions ended. Participants reported feeling a strong sense of community and safety, and the act of singing itself was found to have a relaxing effect. The program, Melodies for Mums, is now being considered for expansion.
