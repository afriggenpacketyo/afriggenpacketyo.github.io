Chinese Scientists Observe Quantum Friction Effect for the First Time

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has made a groundbreaking discovery by directly observing quantum friction between solid materials for the first time. Using folded graphene, the scientists found that, contrary to classical physics, friction did not always increase with thickness and that some thicker folds slid more easily. This unexpected effect is attributed to changes in electron movement within the bent material, which limits the conversion of motion into heat. The findings, published in Nature Communications, could pave the way for creating ultra-efficient nanodevices with minimal wear.
