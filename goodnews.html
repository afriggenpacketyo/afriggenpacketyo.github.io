House passes bill to expedite union contract negotiations, faces Senate hurdles

2026-06-09 19:10:59 EDT

The House of Representatives has approved the Faster Labor Contracts Act by a vote of 230 to 193, aiming to significantly reduce the time it takes for newly unionized workers to reach their first contract. The bill mandates employers to begin negotiations within 10 days and allows for federal mediation and binding arbitration if an agreement isn't reached within 90 days. Twenty Republicans joined Democrats in supporting the measure, which labor leaders hail as a landmark protection for workers. However, the bill faces a tougher path in the Senate, with opponents arguing it constitutes government overreach and could negatively impact employers and the economy.
