2025-12-14 19:50:29 EST

An 'official white Christmas' is defined by snow falling at any Met Office station within the 24-hour period. While the UK has experienced fewer widespread white Christmases in the past two decades, technological advancements in recording have increased the likelihood of a declared white Christmas. Scientists suggest that warmer winters due to climate change will make snowy Christmases less frequent in the future, with a noticeable decline in frost and snow days already observed. Scotland generally sees more white Christmases than Northern Ireland, which has not recorded one in 14 years.
