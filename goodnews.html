Spectacular Orange 'Buck Moon' Set to Grace UK Skies This Week

2025-07-09 05:22:02 EDT

The "buck moon" is set to appear as a spectacular orange sight over UK skies on Thursday, July 10th, rising at 22:09 BST and visible through the night. This phenomenon, influenced by a "major lunar standstill" that occurs roughly every 18.6 years, means the moon will be low in the sky and appear larger due to a visual illusion. Its reddish-orange hue is caused by sunlight scattering through Earth's atmosphere. Named by Algonquin tribes for the period when buck deer grow new antlers, this event is also known as the 'hay moon' or 'mead moon' in Europe, offering a unique astronomical viewing opportunity for observers.
