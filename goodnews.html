Gustav Klimt's "Portrait of Elisabeth Lederer" Sells for Record $236.4 Million

2025-11-19 01:04:00 EST

Gustav Klimt's "Portrait of Elisabeth Lederer" sold for $236.4 million at Sotheby's in New York, becoming the second most expensive artwork ever sold at auction and the most expensive by Sotheby's worldwide. This 1914-1916 portrait of a patron's daughter is one of only two full-length Klimt portraits still privately owned and notably helped save its Jewish subject during the Holocaust by allowing her to claim Klimt, a non-Jew, as her father. The painting was part of a collection looted by Nazis but deemed "too Jewish" to steal, and its sale highlights its historical significance and artistic value, as five Klimt pieces from the same collection fetched a total of $392 million.
