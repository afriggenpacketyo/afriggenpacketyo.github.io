Historical Tradition of Turkey Droving to London for Christmas Revealed

2025-12-24 00:32:22 EST

Before modern transportation and refrigeration, thousands of turkeys were historically "droved" on foot for three months from Norfolk and Suffolk farms to London markets for Christmas. This unique tradition, dating back to the 1500s, involved herding birds, some even fitted with tiny leather boots or protective tar-and-sand coated feet, along rough roads. The Norfolk Black breed, once central to this practice, was nearly lost to history before being preserved by the Peele farming family. The arduous turkey drives to the capital eventually ceased with the advent of steam engines and railways in the late 1800s, fundamentally changing how Christmas turkeys reached urban tables.
