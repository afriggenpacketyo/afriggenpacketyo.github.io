Hubble Telescope Captures New Image of Interstellar Comet Visiting Solar System

2025-08-08 11:29:17 EDT

The Hubble Space Telescope has captured the clearest image to date of 3I-Atlas, the third known interstellar object to pass through our solar system. The comet, discovered last month, is traveling at 130,000 mph and poses no threat to Earth, as its trajectory will keep it farther away than Mars. Hubble's observations have helped refine the size of the comet's icy core to no more than 3.5 miles in diameter and revealed a teardrop-shaped dust plume and tail. The comet will make its closest approach to the sun in late October and should be visible to telescopes again in December after passing behind the sun.
