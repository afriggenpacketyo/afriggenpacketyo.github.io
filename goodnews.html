Fossil Find in China Suggests Some Pterosaurs May Have Been Herbivores

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

A rare pterosaur fossil discovered in northeastern China is challenging long-held beliefs about the diet of these ancient flying reptiles. The fossil of a Sinopterus atavismus, a type of pterodactyloid, contained the first-ever evidence of stomach contents for its kind. Researchers found phytoliths, microscopic mineral deposits from plants, and small quartz crystals, which were likely ingested as gastroliths to help grind tough plant matter. This discovery, published in Science Bulletin, provides the first direct confirmation of herbivory in this group of pterosaurs, shedding new light on the diverse ecosystems of the dinosaur era.

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Located 1,370 light-years away, the infant star is only 100,000 to 200,000 years old. Researchers detected silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing in the star's surrounding disk, the fundamental building blocks of planets like Earth. This groundbreaking observation provides an unprecedented glimpse into the dawn of our own solar system and suggests that the initial steps of planet formation may be a common process.
