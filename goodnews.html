Study Suggests Elevated Body Temperature Directly Combats Some Viruses

2025-11-27 14:00:00 EST

New research published in the journal Science indicates that the heat from a fever can, by itself, help the body fight off certain viral infections. Scientists at the University of Cambridge conducted a study on mice using two strains of influenza, one of which was modified to be heat-tolerant. When mice with simulated fevers were infected, they effectively fought off the normal flu strain but succumbed to the heat-resistant version. This suggests that elevated temperature is a direct defense mechanism, not just a byproduct of the immune response, though its effectiveness against viruses less sensitive to heat remains an open question.

2025-11-27 12:01:32 EST

Using drone mapping and microbotanical analysis, Stanish and his team concluded the site was a sophisticated accounting and storage system for agricultural goods, first developed in the pre-Inca period. The layout mirrors the knotted-string khipus used by the Inca for record-keeping, and sediment analysis revealed traces of crops and reeds used for packaging. This evidence counters more speculative theories, including those involving extraterrestrial origins.
