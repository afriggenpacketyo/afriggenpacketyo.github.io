Understanding What Constitutes Harassment by a Debt Collector Under Federal Law

2026-03-17 13:56:19 EDT

As more Americans face debt due to high borrowing costs and rising prices, this article clarifies what actions by debt collectors qualify as harassment under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA). Prohibited behaviors include repeated or excessive phone calls intended to annoy, contacting individuals at inappropriate times (before 8 a.m. or after 9 p.m.), using threats or intimidation, and employing obscene or abusive language. Publicly exposing debt information or continuing contact after a written cease-communication request also violates federal law. The article advises documenting all interactions, sending a formal cease-communication letter, and filing complaints with regulatory bodies, or considering debt relief options to address the underlying issue.
