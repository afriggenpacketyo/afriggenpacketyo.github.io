Six Key Features Enhancing Safety and Performance in Olympic Skiing

2026-02-15 04:00:00 EST

Olympic skiing incorporates several specialized features to enhance both safety and performance for athletes. Blue dye lines, introduced in 2001, help racers navigate courses at high speeds, especially in poor visibility, by providing visual reference points. The courses themselves are often sprayed with water to freeze them into challenging sheets of ice, demanding razor-sharp ski edges. Safety measures include A-nets and B-nets, rigid and temporary fencing systems designed to prevent skiers from dangerous off-course excursions and absorb kinetic energy during falls. Additionally, racers are now mandated to wear vests with automatic airbags for crash protection and cut-resistant underwear to prevent injuries from ski edges.
