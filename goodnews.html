Palaeontologists Announce Discovery of New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have unveiled the discovery of a new plant-eating dinosaur species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, identified from fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This sauropod, estimated to have lived approximately 150 million years ago, possessed an exceptionally long neck and could reach lengths of up to 20 meters. The finding is particularly significant as it marks the first Mamenchisauridae family member discovered in Thailand, a group predominantly found in China. Unique characteristics, including a distinct air-cavity structure and Y-shaped supporting bones, differentiate this species from all others, providing valuable insights into prehistoric life.
