Explainer Details the Science Behind Tsunamis and Safety Measures

2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

Following a major earthquake in Russia's Far East that triggered tsunami alerts across the Pacific, this article explains the science behind these powerful natural events. Tsunamis are typically generated by strong, shallow, underwater earthquakes of at least magnitude 6.5 that cause vertical shifts in the seafloor. The resulting waves can travel at jet-speed in the deep ocean and grow dangerously high as they approach shallower coastal waters. Experts advise that if you are in a coastal area and receive a warning, you should immediately move to higher ground, ideally at least 100 feet above sea level, and wait for an official all-clear.
