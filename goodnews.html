The article proposes a "work vs. gym" framework for deciding whether to use AI for a task. If a task is primarily about achieving an outcome (work), using AI is efficient. The author argues that AI-generated writing assignments for students are detrimental as they bypass the essential learning process. This distinction also applies to creative fields, where AI can handle "work" tasks (e.g., generating images for manuals) but not "gym" tasks (e.g., creating art). The framework encourages individuals and organizations to consciously wall off cognitive "gym" tasks from AI to preserve human skills and understanding.
