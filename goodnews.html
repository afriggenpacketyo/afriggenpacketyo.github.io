China Develops Jam-Proof Satellite Network Using Coded Light Signals

2026-03-29 22:01:05 EDT

Tsinghua University researchers in China have developed an innovative, jam-proof satellite network that utilizes coded light signals from "beacon" satellites, marking a significant advancement over traditional radio wave-based positioning systems like GPS and BeiDou. This cutting-edge system places powerful light sources on satellites, enabling ground receivers to calculate their position with high precision by detecting the light's direction and the satellites' known locations. Crucially, because light beams are narrow and travel in straight lines, the network is far more resistant to interference than radio signals, offering enhanced accuracy and reliability for navigation in various applications, from vehicles to spacecraft.
