Scotland Initiates Universal Newborn Screening for Spinal Muscular Atrophy

2026-03-22 22:55:52 EDT

Scotland has become the first part of the UK to implement universal newborn screening for Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA), a rare genetic condition causing progressive muscle weakness. This two-year pilot program utilizes a heel prick test for early detection, enabling immediate treatment that can significantly prolong life and prevent severe symptoms. The initiative follows extensive campaigning by parents, including Carrie and Tony Pearson, whose daughter Grayce was diagnosed too late for optimal gene therapy.
