2025-12-09 10:43:00 EST

The International Criminal Court (ICC) has sentenced Ali Muhammad Ali Abd-Al-Rahman, a notorious leader of Sudan's Janjaweed militia, to 20 years in prison. He was convicted on 27 counts of war crimes and crimes against humanity for atrocities committed in the Darfur region, which began in 2003. The court found that Abd-Al-Rahman not only ordered but also personally participated in brutal attacks, torture, and killings, primarily targeting the Fur tribe. This conviction is the first by the ICC for the Darfur conflict, where the government-backed Janjaweed militias are estimated to have killed 300,000 people.
