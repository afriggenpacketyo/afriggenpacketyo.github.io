2025-06-27 07:44:00 EDT

Researchers have potentially identified the oldest rocks on Earth, located in Quebec's Nuvvuagittuq Greenstone Belt. While previous studies yielded conflicting ages, a new analysis using two different dating techniques on a new sample produced a consistent age of approximately 4.16 billion years. Earth formed about 4.5 billion years ago, and rocks from its earliest period are exceedingly rare due to geological recycling. Studying these ancient formations could provide invaluable insights into the planet's early conditions, such as the formation of tectonic plates and the origins of life, though access to the site is currently restricted by the local Inuit community to prevent damage.
