Lilli was depicted as a sensual, witty, and independent woman, often in suggestive situations designed to appeal to a male readership. In 1955, she was turned into a plastic doll that became a popular gag gift for men. Mattel co-founder Ruth Handler discovered the Lilli doll in Switzerland, purchased the rights, and in 1959, launched a modified version named Barbie, which went on to become one of the best-selling toys in history.
