Nutritional Experts Advise on How to Evaluate High-Protein Snack Claims

2025-07-19 06:00:00 EDT

As high-protein snacks flood the market, nutrition experts caution consumers to look beyond marketing claims. Many products labeled as "protein-packed" also contain high levels of sugar and processed ingredients, diminishing their health benefits. Experts suggest a simple calculation to assess a snack's quality: multiply the grams of protein by four and divide by the total calories to find the percentage of calories from protein, with 30% or more being a good benchmark. They also recommend prioritizing whole foods like Greek yogurt, nuts, and cottage cheese, which provide essential nutrients without the excessive additives often found in packaged snacks.
