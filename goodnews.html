Chinese Researchers Develop Infrared Contact Lens for Night Vision

2025-06-01 08:43:00 EDT

Researchers in China have created an infrared contact lens that enables humans to see in the dark by converting infrared light into visible light. The lenses combine conventional soft contact lenses with nanoparticles of gold, sodium gadolinium fluoride, ytterbium, and erbium ions. These upconversion contact lenses (UCLs) convert infrared light with wavelengths between 800 and 1,600 nanometers into visible light. While the resulting images are somewhat blurred, the lenses allow users to recognize patterns, letters, and flashing infrared signals, even with closed eyes. Potential applications include surgical procedures, encryption, and rescue operations in low-visibility conditions.
