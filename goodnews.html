University of Colorado football coach Deion Sanders recently underwent a neobladder reconstruction after being treated for bladder cancer. The procedure involves removing the diseased bladder and constructing a new one from a segment of the patient's own small intestine. Surgeons reshape the intestinal tissue into a sphere and connect it to the kidneys and urethra, creating a new, functional organ. Because it uses the patient's own tissue, there is no risk of rejection, though patients must retrain themselves to control urination, as the neobladder lacks nerve connections to the brain.
