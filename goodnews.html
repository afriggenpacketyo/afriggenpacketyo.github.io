Paris Reopens Seine River for Public Swimming After Century-Long Ban

2025-07-05 02:35:00 EDT

For the first time in over 100 years, Paris has officially reopened sections of the Seine River for public swimming. Swimming had been banned since 1923 due to severe pollution and safety hazards. Three supervised swimming zones have been established with amenities like changing rooms and lifeguards, allowing Parisians and tourists to cool off in the iconic river amid a summer heatwave. Officials will continuously monitor water quality to ensure safety.
