Successful Kelp Forest Restoration Off California Coast Achieved by Culling Sea Urchins

2025-08-31 12:00:34 EDT

A major kelp restoration project off the coast of Southern California has successfully revived a vital marine ecosystem that had declined by 80%. Led by the Bay Foundation, the effort involved divers manually smashing nearly 6 million voracious purple sea urchins, whose populations had exploded and created "urchin barrens" on the seafloor. This culling allowed the giant Pacific kelp, known as the "sequoias of the sea," to regrow rapidly, restoring critical habitat for over 800 species and enhancing carbon storage. The project is now being viewed as a potential model for restoring other struggling kelp forests around the world.
