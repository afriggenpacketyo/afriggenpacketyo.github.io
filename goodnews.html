2025-12-28 01:51:32 EST

Although the FDA mandates separate labeling for added sugars, food companies have adapted by substituting traditional sweeteners with alternatives not yet categorized as "added sugars," paradoxically leading to an overall increase in food sweetness. Health experts strongly recommend limiting added sugar intake to below 5% of daily caloric intake, a more stringent guideline than the current FDA suggestion of 10%. Consumers are urged to meticulously examine nutrition labels and choose plain, unsweetened products to gain better control over their sugar consumption.
