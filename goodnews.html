Palaeontologists Discover New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species in Thailand

2026-07-10 17:30:57 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have discovered a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils found in Kalasin Province. Dating back approximately 150 million years to the Late Jurassic period, this sauropod measured up to 20 meters long and possessed an unusually elongated neck. A CT scan of a recovered dorsal vertebra revealed unique air-cavity structures and a Y-shaped bone arrangement, distinguishing it from other known Mamenchisauridae family members. This marks the first discovery of its kind in Thailand, significantly adding to the region's paleontological record and expanding our understanding of dinosaur diversity.
