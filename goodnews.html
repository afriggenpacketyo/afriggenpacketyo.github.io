Fact Check Debunks Claim That 85% of Global Refugees Are Muslim

2025-11-21 10:59:00 EST

A widely circulated social media claim stating that 85% of the world's refugees are Muslim and that they avoid seeking asylum in Muslim countries has been determined to be false. The UN's refugee agency (UNHCR) does not collect data on refugees' religious affiliations, making the 85% figure unverifiable and highly improbable. Experts note that Ukrainians and Venezuelans, who are from majority-Christian nations, constitute nearly a third of all refugees. Furthermore, data shows that many of the largest refugee-hosting countries, such as Turkey, Pakistan, and Iran, are predominantly Muslim nations, directly contradicting the second part of the claim.
