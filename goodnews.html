China Develops "Lighthouses in Space": A Jam-Proof Satellite Network Using Coded Light Signals

2026-03-29 22:01:05 EDT

Tsinghua University researchers in China have developed a groundbreaking satellite network utilizing coded light signals, dubbed "lighthouses in space," to provide jam-proof and highly precise positioning. Unlike traditional GPS or BeiDou systems that rely on radio waves, this new system places powerful light sources on satellites to beam narrow, straight-line signals to Earth. Ground receivers then use these signals and the satellites' known positions to calculate location with greater accuracy. This innovation addresses vulnerabilities of radio-based systems to interference and offers enhanced precision compared to star-based navigation, marking a significant advancement in global positioning technology for various applications, from vehicles to spacecraft.
