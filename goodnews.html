Skeleton in Magdeburg Cathedral Identified as Likely Remains of Otto I

2026-03-18 14:19:00 EDT

Scientists have announced with "probability bordering on certainty" that a skeleton discovered in a simple wooden coffin within Magdeburg Cathedral's sarcophagus belongs to Otto I, also known as Otto the Great. Analysis of the 1,050-year-old remains indicates a well-fed man who died around 60, consistent with historical records, and showed signs of an active, possibly combat-involved, lifestyle. Genetic evidence further links the skeleton to a blood relative of Heinrich II, an Ottonian dynasty emperor. This significant archaeological finding confirms the resting place of the East Frankish king and Holy Roman Emperor, whose legacy shaped early European history.
