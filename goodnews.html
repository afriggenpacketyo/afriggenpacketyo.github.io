Understanding the Air Quality Index and Its Impact on Public Health

2025-06-03 11:26:12 EDT

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a tool used to measure and communicate the severity of air pollution and its associated health risks. The AQI uses a color-coded system ranging from green (good) to maroon (hazardous), with corresponding numerical values indicating the level of pollution. The index is calculated based on five major pollutants, including ground-level ozone and particulate matter, which pose the greatest threat to human health. As air quality worsens, the public is at risk of experiencing health impacts ranging from headaches and fatigue to more serious conditions like heart attack or stroke. Residents can use resources like AirNow.gov to find the AQI for their area and understand potential health risks and recommended precautions.
