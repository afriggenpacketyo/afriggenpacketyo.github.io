NASA deactivates Voyager 1 instrument to conserve power for extended mission

2026-04-19 01:40:28 EDT

NASA has announced the deactivation of one of Voyager 1's science instruments, the Low-energy Charged Particles experiment (LECP), to conserve dwindling power reserves. Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 is the most distant human-made object and is now operating on borrowed time due to its aging radioisotope thermoelectric generator. The shutdown of the LECP, which has provided crucial data on interstellar space, is intended to extend the mission's operational life. Engineers are also planning a larger power conservation effort, known as 'the Big Bang,' to keep at least one instrument active on each Voyager spacecraft into the 2030s.
