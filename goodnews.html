2026-02-10 14:22:10 EST

The beetle mimics ant nest-mates by turning off its own pheromones and smearing itself with ant chemicals, allowing it to be fed and accepted, even consuming ant eggs. However, this adaptation makes the beetle dependent on the ants for survival, as it cannot reactivate its own protective pheromones if separated. This "Catch-22" suggests that once an intimate symbiotic relationship forms, it becomes irreversible, providing a new model for understanding evolutionary dead ends.
