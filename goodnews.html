Millions Eligible for Compensation Over Mis-Sold UK Car Finance Deals

2026-03-30 08:14:58 EDT

Millions of UK drivers are now expected to receive compensation for mis-sold car finance agreements that were taken out between April 2007 and November 2024. The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) banned "discretionary commission arrangements" in 2021, a practice where car dealers received commission from lenders based on higher interest rates charged to customers without their knowledge. Average payouts are estimated at approximately £700 per mis-sold agreement, with the total compensation potentially reaching £8.2 billion across 14 million car loans. The FCA advises affected individuals to contact their car loan provider directly, warning against scammers, and has published guidance on how to complain, outlining a scheme for reviewing cases and distributing payments despite some industry pushback.
