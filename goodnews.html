The Oxford English Dictionary (OED) has officially added ten Welsh words in its latest update, including 'poody,' which means to sulk or pout. The word gained widespread recognition after contestants on the BBC show 'Race Across the World' used it, baffling the show's producers. 'Poody' is considered a 'boomerang word,' having originated from the English 'pout,' been adopted into Welsh as 'pwdu,' and then re-borrowed back into English.
