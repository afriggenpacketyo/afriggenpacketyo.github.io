Study Reveals Human Influence Caused Divergence in Animal Sizes During Middle Ages

2025-09-01 13:05:00 EDT

A new study from the University of Montpellier reveals a significant divergence in the body size evolution of wild and domestic animals beginning in the Middle Ages. Researchers analyzed over 225,000 animal bones from southern France, spanning 8,000 years, to reach their conclusions. The findings indicate that while domesticated species like chickens and sheep grew larger due to selective breeding, wild species such as foxes and deer shrank. This shift is attributed to increased human control over livestock and the fragmentation of wild habitats due to expanding human settlements and hunting.
