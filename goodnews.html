China Activates Record-Breaking Particle Accelerator for Scientific Research

2026-07-22 21:00:07 EDT

China has successfully brought online the High Intensity Heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) in Huizhou, Guangdong province, a record-breaking particle accelerator that cost 2.6 billion yuan. This advanced facility is now capable of producing the world’s most intense beams of charged atoms, significantly surpassing the previous world record by packing up to eight times as many atoms into each pulse. The HIAF provides scientists with an unprecedented tool to create previously unknown atomic nuclei and to advance crucial research in fields such as cancer treatment and the development of new spacecraft materials, marking a major scientific and technological milestone for the nation.

New MS Drug to Improve Walking Now Available on NHS in England

2026-07-22 19:38:58 EDT

Fampridine, the first drug specifically designed to help individuals with multiple sclerosis (MS) improve their walking ability, is now being made available on the NHS in England. This "signal booster" medication works by enhancing nerve signals, thereby making movement easier for eligible patients and potentially fostering greater independence. Previously rejected for NHS use in England due to cost-effectiveness concerns, its current availability is celebrated as "great news" by both patients and advocacy groups, with up to 5,000 individuals annually expected to benefit. Clinical trials have demonstrated significant improvements in walking speed and duration for a substantial portion of users, offering a life-changing impact on their mobility and overall quality of life.
