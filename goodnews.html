Fossil DNA Study Reveals Horses Originated in North America, Migrating to Europe via China

2026-06-19 23:22:54 EDT

A new fossil DNA study challenges the long-held belief that horses were introduced to the Americas by European conquistadors, revealing instead that they originated in North America millions of years ago. The research indicates that an extinct lineage, the Dalian horse in northeastern China, served as an unexpected genetic intermediary. This Chinese crossroads facilitated the transmission of American horse ancestry to ancient horse populations in Siberia, ultimately contributing to the bloodlines that gave rise to modern European horses. This discovery fundamentally redefines the historical migration patterns of equids across continents, offering fresh insights into evolutionary history.
