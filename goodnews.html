The rule suggests that a road's speed limit should be set at the speed that 85% of drivers travel at or below, effectively letting drivers determine the limit. Critics argue this creates a dangerous feedback loop where speeding leads to higher limits, ignoring the safety of pedestrians and cyclists. The Federal Highway Administration has recently clarified that this is not a strict rule, encouraging communities to also consider factors like road use and crash frequency.
