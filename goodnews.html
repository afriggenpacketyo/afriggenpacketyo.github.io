New US Study Suggests Coffee May Protect Against Irregular Heartbeats

2025-11-10 05:00:04 EST

A new US study, the Decaf clinical trial, challenges conventional wisdom by suggesting that coffee consumption may actually protect people against irregular heartbeats, specifically atrial fibrillation (A-fib). The six-month trial found that patients with persistent A-fib who were allocated to coffee consumption had a significantly lower risk of the condition recurring (47%) compared to those abstaining (64%). While it has been common for medical providers to advise A-fib patients to avoid caffeinated coffee due to concerns about elevated heart rate, this research indicates a potential protective quality. Though the study had some limitations, cardiologists are now suggesting that moderate coffee intake might be acceptable for A-fib patients, prompting a reconsideration of long-held medical advice regarding caffeine and heart health.
