New research has found that wild chimpanzees in Uganda and Côte d'Ivoire consume a significant amount of alcohol through their diet of ripe, fermented fruit. Scientists measured the ethanol content in fruits eaten by the chimps and calculated that their daily intake is equivalent to about 14 grams of ethanol, or nearly two alcoholic beverages for a human. This study provides the first direct chemical evidence of ethanol consumption in wild chimps' diets. The findings lend support to the "drunken monkey" hypothesis, which suggests that human attraction to alcohol may have evolutionary roots in our primate ancestors' fruit-eating habits.
