Understanding the Fiduciary Standard is Key to Trustworthy Financial Advice

2025-09-24 08:58:09 EDT

A critical question to ask any financial professional is whether they are a fiduciary, which legally obligates them to act in their client's best interest. This contrasts with the "suitability" standard, which only requires that a recommendation be appropriate, not necessarily the best available option. Working with a non-fiduciary can lead to being sold high-cost products that benefit the advisor more than the client, potentially costing investors significantly over time. Consumers can verify an advisor's fiduciary status through SEC filings and by seeking out fee-only certified financial planners.
