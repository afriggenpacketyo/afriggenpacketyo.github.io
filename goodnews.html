The spectacle occurs when the Earth passes directly between the Sun and the Moon, casting its shadow on the lunar surface. The reddish hue is caused by sunlight refracting through Earth's atmosphere, the same phenomenon that creates red sunsets. Astronomers advise finding a clear view of the horizon around moonrise to witness the event, which does not require special eye protection.
