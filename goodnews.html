Exploring the Shared Historical Roots of St. Martin's Day and Carnival in Germany

2025-11-10 10:51:00 EST

The German traditions of St. Martin's Day and the start of Carnival, both observed on November 11, share a common medieval origin. St. Martin's Day marked the end of the agricultural year, a time for final feasts before a six-week period of fasting for Advent began. Carnival celebrations on the same day emerged as a final opportunity for exuberant indulgence in food, drink, and festivities before this period of religious austerity. The number 11 itself, positioned between the sacred numbers 10 and 12, became associated with fools and jesters, cementing the date's dual identity of solemn charity and boisterous celebration.
