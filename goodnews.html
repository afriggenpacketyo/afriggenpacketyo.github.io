Russia's presence began in the mid-18th century, driven by the lucrative fur trade, and was consolidated by the Russian-American Company. By the mid-19th century, following its defeat in the Crimean War, Russia viewed the remote and costly colony as a liability and sought a buyer. The United States purchased Alaska for $7.2 million in 1867, a deal initially mocked as "Seward's Folly" but later seen as a historic bargain after the discovery of gold and oil.
