Scientists Identify Bacterium as Cause of Decade-Long Sea Star Wasting Disease

2025-08-04 15:35:49 EDT

Researchers have identified the specific pathogen responsible for the sea star wasting disease that has killed billions of the creatures along North America's West Coast since 2013. A study published in Nature Ecology & Evolution pinpoints the bacterium Vibrio pectinocida as the culprit. The epidemic was particularly devastating for the sunflower sea star, a keystone species whose population collapse led to an explosion of sea urchins and the destruction of vast kelp forests. This breakthrough allows for more targeted conservation efforts, including developing rapid diagnostic tests and breeding disease-resistant sea stars to help restore critical marine ecosystems.
