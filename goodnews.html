Understanding Endemic, Epidemic, and Pandemic: Distinguishing Disease Spread

2026-05-11 06:31:00 EDT

The article clarifies the distinct epidemiological terms: endemic, epidemic, and pandemic, which describe how a disease spreads rather than its severity. An endemic disease, like malaria, is consistently present in a region at a stable rate. An epidemic signifies an unusual increase in cases within a specific area and time, often due to pathogen changes or new introductions. A pandemic, such as influenza outbreaks, indicates global spread across countries and continents, necessitating international cooperation for control, exemplified by the recent hantavirus outbreak.
