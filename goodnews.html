Summer Solstice 2026: Marking the Longest Day of the Year

2026-06-16 03:03:42 EDT

The summer solstice on Sunday, June 21, marks the official start of astronomical summer in the Northern Hemisphere, signifying the longest day and shortest night of the year. This celestial event occurs when the hemisphere is maximally tilted towards the Sun. The precise moment of the solstice is 09:24 BST. Variations in the solstice date (June 20-22) are due to the Earth's orbit taking slightly longer than a calendar year, with leap days accounting for this discrepancy. The Earth's axial tilt is responsible for the distinct seasons experienced throughout the year.
