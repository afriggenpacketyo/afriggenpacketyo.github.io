New Chinese Fossil Reveals Ancient Cross-Equator Migration Route in Pangaea

2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences have identified a new species of dicynodont, named Dinanomodon guoi, based on a fossil discovered in Gansu province, China. This significant paleontological finding establishes the first bidentalian genus shared between China and South Africa, providing crucial evidence for a cross-equator migration corridor during the supercontinent Pangaea. Dicynodonts, an extinct clade of non-mammalian therapsids, are considered "cousins" to today's mammals, and this discovery sheds new light on ancient animal distribution and continental drift patterns. The research was published in the peer-reviewed journal Cladistics.
