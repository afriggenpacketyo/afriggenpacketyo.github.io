Despite Paragraph 175 criminalizing male homosexual acts, figures like Magnus Hirschfeld championed LGBTQ+ rights, founding the Scientific-Humanitarian Committee and the Institute for Sexual Science. This era fostered an atmosphere of relative tolerance where artists openly expressed their identities, leading to vibrant queer spaces like the Eldorado nightclub. The Nazi regime's rise in 1933 brutally ended this period, destroying Hirschfeld's institute and persecuting gay men. However, Berlin's historical legacy continues to influence its contemporary status as a leading queer-friendly city.
