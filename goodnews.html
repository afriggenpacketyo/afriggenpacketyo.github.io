Understanding the Air Quality Index

2025-06-03 11:48:00 EDT

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a tool used to measure air pollution levels and their associated health risks. The AQI uses a color-coded system ranging from good (green) to hazardous (maroon) to categorize air quality. The index is calculated based on five major pollutants regulated by the Clean Air Act: ground-level ozone, particulate matter, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide. The EPA's AirNow.gov site provides the latest AQI status for specific areas, including information on the pollutant causing issues and recommendations for activities.
