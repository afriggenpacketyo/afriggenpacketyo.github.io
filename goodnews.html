Explaining the Official Start Dates for the Winter Season

2025-11-23 05:15:38 EST

Despite recent freezing temperatures and snow, the UK is officially still in autumn according to the meteorological calendar. There are two primary methods for defining seasons: meteorological and astronomical. Meteorologists use a fixed three-month system for easier year-on-year data comparison, with winter always beginning on December 1st. In contrast, the astronomical winter starts on the winter solstice, the shortest day of the year, which falls on December 21st. The meteorological definition aligns with the three statistically coldest months, which is supported by historical data showing the UK's record low temperatures consistently occur in December, January, and February.
