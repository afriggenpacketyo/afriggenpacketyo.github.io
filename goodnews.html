The Ancient Origins and Evolution of New Year's Resolutions

2025-12-31 05:01:00 EST

New Year's resolutions, a tradition for many, have a history stretching back thousands of years, far beyond their modern secular form. This article traces the concept to 2000 B.C. with Babylonian festivals, where people vowed to return borrowed farming equipment. Ancient Romans further solidified the practice by making promises and offerings to Janus, the god of beginnings, on January 1st. The tradition evolved through medieval chivalric vows and spiritual reflections, becoming more widespread and secular in the 20th century with the rise of public celebrations and commercialization. Today, resolutions largely focus on self-improvement, though the underlying impulse to reflect and set goals remains constant.
