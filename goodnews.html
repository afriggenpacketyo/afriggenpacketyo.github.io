Study Challenges Theory on Why Birds Sing at Dawn

2025-06-20 05:00:00 EDT

A new study challenges the long-held belief that birds sing at dawn because sound travels farther and more clearly at that time. Researchers from the Cornell Lab of Ornithology analyzed audio recordings of birds in an Indian rainforest and found little support for the "acoustic transmission hypothesis." The study suggests that the dawn chorus is more likely related to birds marking their territory after being inactive at night and communicating about finding food. The researchers examined recordings from 43 locations in the Western Ghats mountain range, focusing on 69 different bird species to compare vocal activity at dawn and dusk.
