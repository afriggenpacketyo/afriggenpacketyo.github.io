This groundbreaking discovery significantly pushes back the species' estimated arrival date in eastern Asia by approximately 600,000 years. The findings suggest that Homo erectus may have spread across continents earlier and more successfully than scientists previously believed, offering new perspectives on early human migration patterns. The research, published in the peer-reviewed journal Science Advances, employed a dating method capable of accurately assessing materials up to 5 million years old, considerably extending the scope beyond the limited timespan of traditional carbon-14 dating techniques.
