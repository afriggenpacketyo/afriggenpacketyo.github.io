Tiny Zooplankton Play Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny marine creatures called zooplankton are "unsung heroes" in the fight against global warming due to their epic annual migration in the Southern Ocean. These animals feast on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface, grow fat, and then sink deep into the ocean to burn this fat, locking away vast amounts of carbon. Scientists calculate this process sequesters as much carbon as the annual emissions of 55 million cars, a far greater amount than previously understood. This discovery highlights the crucial but often overlooked role of these small organisms in regulating the planet's climate.
