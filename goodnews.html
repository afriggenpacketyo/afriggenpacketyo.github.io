China Activates Powerful Particle Accelerator for Atomic Research

2026-07-22 21:00:07 EDT

China has activated a record-breaking High Intensity Heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) in Huizhou, Guangdong province, enhancing its capabilities in atomic research. This 2.6 billion yuan facility, developed over 16 years, can produce the world's most intense beams of charged atoms, packing up to eight times more atoms per pulse than the previous record holder in Germany. HIAF combines a linear accelerator, a circular accelerator, and storage rings into a single complex. This new tool will enable scientists to create previously unknown atomic nuclei and advance research in crucial areas like cancer treatment and spacecraft materials.
