Debts Not Subject to Social Security Garnishment: A Guide for Retirees

Retirees carrying significant debt are reminded that most private creditors cannot directly garnish Social Security benefits. This protection applies to debts such as credit card balances, medical bills, and unsecured personal loans. While these creditors may pursue legal action, federal law generally shields Social Security income from direct seizure. However, funds deposited into bank accounts alongside other income may be vulnerable under certain conditions. Federal taxes, federal student loans, child support, and alimony are exceptions where garnishment is permissible. Understanding these distinctions is crucial for retirees to safeguard their primary income source and manage their financial obligations effectively.
