Fossil Discovery in China Suggests Ancient 'Lamp Shells' Practiced Social Distancing

2025-07-30 00:39:15 EDT

Chinese paleontologists have discovered compelling evidence that an ancient marine invertebrate, known as a "lamp shell," may have practiced a form of social distancing to improve its chances of survival. An analysis of exceptionally preserved 436-million-year-old brachiopod fossils from Guizhou province revealed that the creatures were arranged in a highly regular, checkerboard-like pattern across the ancient sea floor. The researchers hypothesize that the animals used hundreds of flexible, hair-thin bristles to sense their neighbors and maintain optimal spacing, which likely enhanced their ability to filter feed without competition or interference from others in their population.
