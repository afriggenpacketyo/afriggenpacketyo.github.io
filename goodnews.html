Understanding Income Thresholds for Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 Bankruptcy Filings

2026-01-06 10:46:53 EST

There's no universal maximum income that automatically disqualifies individuals from filing for bankruptcy; eligibility depends on the type of bankruptcy and specific financial circumstances. Chapter 7, which liquidates unsecured debts, typically requires passing a "means test" comparing income to state median, but also considers allowable expenses. Chapter 13, which restructures debt into a repayment plan, has no income ceiling and often requires a steady income. Courts evaluate factors like household size and cost of living. Debt relief alternatives, such as debt settlement or management programs, offer options for those not suited for bankruptcy, emphasizing that the best choice depends on individual debt load and financial goals.
