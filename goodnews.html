This effect, where a neutral particle collision with an atomic nucleus causes a secondary electronic recoil, could be a crucial mechanism for detecting dark matter. The breakthrough, achieved by a team from the University of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, offers "unprecedented insights" into one of physics' most profound unsolved mysteries. This observation validates a long-standing theory and opens new avenues for dark matter research.
