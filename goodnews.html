Finland Nears Completion of World's First Permanent Underground Nuclear Waste Repository

2025-08-30 06:10:00 EDT

Finland is set to become the first country to implement a permanent solution for nuclear waste by burying spent fuel rods deep underground in a facility named Onkalo. Developed over two decades, the repository is designed to safely store all the high-level nuclear waste from the country's five power plants for millennia. The project uses a multi-barrier system, encapsulating the waste in copper canisters, surrounding them with clay, and placing them in stable bedrock nearly 1,500 feet deep. Hailed as a game-changer by the International Atomic Energy Agency, Onkalo represents a major step forward in responsible and sustainable nuclear energy management.
