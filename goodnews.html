Limestone Application in Agriculture Offers Breakthrough for Carbon Emission Reduction

2025-09-14 17:00:00 EDT

Researchers have identified a promising method to combat agricultural carbon emissions by spreading crushed limestone across farmlands. A Yale-led study, published in Nature Water, reveals that limestone reacts with atmospheric carbon dioxide to form bicarbonate ions, which are then washed into waterways and transform into calcium carbonate, effectively storing carbon for millions of years. This discovery is significant because agriculture accounts for approximately 10% of global annual carbon emissions, making this inexpensive and sustainable solution a vital tool. While it doesn't prevent emissions from being produced, it offers a long-term sequestration mechanism. Experts stress that modifying liming practices should be a priority, contributing to broader efforts to remove carbon from the atmosphere, as halting emissions alone is deemed insufficient to meet climate targets.
