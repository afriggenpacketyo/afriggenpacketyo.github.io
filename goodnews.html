UK Study Recommends Universal Type 1 Diabetes Screening for Children

2026-01-20 20:19:40 EST

A major UK study, ELSA, suggests that offering universal screening for type 1 diabetes to all British children using a simple finger-prick blood test would be highly effective. Early identification through such screening could prevent life-threatening diabetic ketoacidosis, a common and dangerous complication, and allow for earlier intervention with treatments to control blood sugar. The study has already screened 17,000 children, with one participant, 12-year-old Imogen, benefiting from early diagnosis and access to the new immunotherapy drug teplizumab, which has shown promise in delaying diabetes onset by about three years. Diabetes charities and experts emphasize that early detection provides families with crucial time to prepare and access vital treatments.

Diverse Exercise Regimen Linked to Longer Life and Better Health

2026-01-20 20:17:52 EST

A comprehensive 30-year US study involving 110,000 adults suggests that engaging in a variety of physical activities, rather than specializing in just one, is a key factor in boosting overall health and achieving a longer life. Researchers discovered that active individuals who diversified their exercise routines were 19% less likely to die during the study period compared to those who focused on a single activity. While the total amount of exercise remains crucial, combining activities that offer complementary health benefits, such as resistance training and aerobic exercise, is particularly effective. This approach can significantly enhance physical and mental well-being, reduce the risk of numerous diseases, and potentially extend one's lifespan, with optimum benefits observed around six hours of moderate or three hours of vigorous activity weekly.
