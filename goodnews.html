Study Links Dog Owner's Work Stress to Anxiety in Their Pets

2025-05-24 22:10:33 EDT

A new study in Scientific Reports reveals that dog owners who ruminate about work stress may inadvertently pass anxiety to their pets. The research found that dogs of owners with high job stress exhibited more stress-related behaviors, such as whining and pacing. This connection is attributed to "crossover," where stress spills over from one individual to another, and "emotional contagion," where dogs pick up on human feelings through subtle cues. The study suggests that employed dog owners could improve their pets' well-being by avoiding work-related thoughts when at home.
