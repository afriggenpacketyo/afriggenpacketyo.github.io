UK Government Expands Energy Bill Support for Intensive Industries

2026-04-15 18:33:35 EDT

The UK government is expanding its British Industrial Competitiveness Scheme (BICS) to provide enhanced energy bill support to approximately 10,000 energy-intensive manufacturers, an increase from the initial 7,000 firms. Starting April 2027, eligible businesses in critical sectors such as automotive, aerospace, steel, and pharmaceuticals could see their electricity bills reduced by up to 25%. Additionally, qualifying companies will receive a one-off payment in 2027 to cover the support they would have received from April 2026. This initiative, funded through energy system adjustments and government expenditure without impacting domestic bills, aims to bolster Britain's economic security and competitiveness amidst global instability and fluctuating oil and gas prices.
