This finding, detailed in the journal *Science*, significantly challenges the long-held "central dogma of biology" which posits that genetic information flows from DNA to RNA to protein, but not in reverse from protein to DNA. Biochemist Alex Gao's team identified Drt3b while investigating how bacteria defend against viruses, revealing that the protein utilizes its own intricate shape as a mold to precisely assemble DNA building blocks. While the exact function of this newly formed DNA remains a mystery, this discovery profoundly expands our understanding of DNA synthesis mechanisms and holds potential for future applications in building custom DNA molecules, though practical tools are not yet realized.
