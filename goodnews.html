While this low dose is not enough to cause intoxication, the steady consumption supports the "drunken monkey" hypothesis, which suggests that human attraction to alcohol may have evolutionary roots in ancestral fruit-eating habits. The study provides the first direct chemical measurements of ethanol in fruits routinely consumed by wild chimps.
