The rock is estimated to sell for between $2 million and $4 million. It is believed to have been ejected from the Martian surface by an asteroid impact before crashing into the Sahara desert, where it was discovered in 2023. Analysis confirmed its origin by comparing its chemical composition to data from the 1976 Viking space probe. This single meteorite represents nearly 7% of all known Martian material currently on our planet.
