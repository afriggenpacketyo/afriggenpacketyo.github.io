Focal therapy for prostate cancer shows fewer side effects with comparable effectiveness

2026-07-16 19:36:40 EDT

A 10-year NHS study suggests that focal therapy, a less invasive treatment for prostate cancer, is as effective as surgery or radiotherapy but with a significantly lower risk of side effects like incontinence and loss of sexual function. The study, involving nearly 3,500 men with intermediate or high-risk prostate cancer, found excellent long-term cancer control. Researchers are advocating for wider availability of focal therapy, which currently treats only about 1,000 men annually in the UK despite up to 15,000 potentially benefiting. The findings could influence clinical practice and patient conversations regarding treatment options.
