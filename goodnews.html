UK Boy Undergoes Pioneering Surgery to Lengthen Leg Using Magnetic Nail Technology

2026-02-17 01:16:51 EST

A nine-year-old boy has become the first in the UK to undergo pioneering surgery to lengthen his leg, which was over an inch shorter due to fibular hemimelia. Experts at Liverpool's Alder Hey Children's Hospital implanted a motorized telescopic nail on the surface of his thigh bone, gradually extending it by 1mm daily using magnets, a method previously unavailable for younger children. The procedure, developed in the US, allowed him to gain 3cm, with a vastly superior experience compared to traditional external fixators. This successful operation has enabled him to run normally and play basketball, and the technique is now being adopted by other specialist centers.
