The History of Monchhichi Dolls and Their East German Counterpart, Tiemi

2025-07-29 03:29:00 EDT

Amid the current craze for collectible toys like Labubu, this article explores the history of a similar vintage doll, the Monchhichi. Created in Japan in 1974, the monkey-like doll became a global phenomenon in the 1980s, especially in Europe. Interestingly, due to Cold War restrictions, capitalist West Germany's popular Monchhichi was not available in communist East Germany. Instead, a state-owned enterprise in the toy-making town of Sonneberg produced a nearly identical version called the Tiemi, which became popular throughout the Eastern Bloc. Today, both original Monchhichis and the rarer Tiemi dolls are sought-after collector's items for nostalgic adults.
