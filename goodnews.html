Shingles Vaccine Linked to Lowered Heart Disease Risk in New Study

2025-05-05 19:05:08 EDT

A recent study published in the European Heart Journal suggests that the shingles vaccine may offer protection against cardiovascular events such as stroke, heart failure, and coronary heart disease for up to eight years. The research, involving over one million people aged 50 or older, found a 23% lower risk of such events in those who received the vaccine, with the protective effect being more pronounced in men, individuals under 60, and those with unhealthy lifestyle habits. Researchers suggest that preventing shingles through vaccination may reduce blood vessel damage, inflammation, and clot formation, which can contribute to heart disease, while also noting that further research is needed on recombinant vaccines like Shingrix to confirm similar benefits. The CDC recommends the two-dose shingles vaccine starting at age 50, given the increased risk of shingles with age.
