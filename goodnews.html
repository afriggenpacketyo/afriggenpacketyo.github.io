Understanding Home Ownership Dynamics with a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

This article clarifies the ownership structure of a home when a reverse mortgage is taken out, a popular option for homeowners over 62. It explains that the homeowner retains the title and legal ownership of the property, while the lender places a lien on it to secure the loan. The loan balance, which includes interest and fees, grows over time and becomes due when the owner sells, moves out, or passes away. At that point, the owner's heirs can choose to repay the loan to keep the house or sell the property and retain any equity remaining after the loan is paid off.
