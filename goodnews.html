Natural "White Hydrogen" Emerges as Potential Game-Changer for Clean Energy

2026-03-13 07:33:00 EDT

Geologists are exploring "white hydrogen," a naturally occurring gas that could revolutionize clean energy production, forming naturally in the Earth's crust through ancient geological processes. Unlike manufactured hydrogen, which heavily relies on fossil fuels, white hydrogen offers a third, potentially renewable, option. Researchers estimate trillions of tons exist in the Earth's crust, with just 2% capable of covering global demand for 200 years, making it a significant potential resource. Despite its promise, pioneers face legal and investment hurdles due to its nascent status as a recognized natural resource. This article highlights a significant, yet challenging, frontier in sustainable energy.

Medical experts explain that the liver's unique ability to renew its cells means it is biologically only a few years old, regardless of the donor's chronological age.
