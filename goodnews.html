When deciding between traditional (pretax) and after-tax Roth 401(k) contributions for retirement savings, financial experts advise considering your effective tax rate in retirement, rather than solely focusing on your current marginal rate. Your marginal rate applies to the highest bracket of your taxable income, whereas your effective tax rate represents the total taxes paid as a percentage of your overall gross income. Understanding this distinction is crucial because your effective rate in retirement may be significantly lower than your working-year marginal rate, potentially making pretax contributions more advantageous for long-term tax optimization. This strategic approach aims to maximize savings by paying taxes when rates are projected to be the lowest. (not investment advice)
