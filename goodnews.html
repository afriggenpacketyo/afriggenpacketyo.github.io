Chinese Scientists Cultivate Beating Human Heart Structure Within Pig Embryo

2025-07-03 22:00:13 EDT

A team of Chinese scientists has successfully grown a human-cell-based heart structure inside a pig embryo, which continued to beat unaided for 21 days. The research, led by the Guangzhou Institutes of Biomedicine and Health, represents a significant step in the field of chimera research and organ generation. To achieve this, scientists reprogrammed human stem cells with specific genes to prevent cell death and improve their viability within the pig host. These modified stem cells were then implanted into pig embryos at an early stage, which were subsequently transferred to surrogate sows for gestation.
