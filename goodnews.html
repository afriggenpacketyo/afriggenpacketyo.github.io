The Ubiquitous Word 'OK' Traces Its Origins to a 19th-Century American Joke

2025-07-23 08:08:13 EDT

The word "OK" is one of the most universally understood terms globally, originating as a linguistic joke in 1839 Boston. It began as an abbreviation for "oll korrect," a playful misspelling of "all correct," which was part of a slang fad at the time. Its popularity surged during President Martin Van Buren's 1840 reelection campaign, where his supporters formed "O.K. Clubs," referencing his nickname "Old Kinderhook." The word's simplicity and versatility made it ideal for the telegraph age and its global spread was later accelerated by American soldiers in the World Wars.
