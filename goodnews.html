2025-07-13 00:00:09 EDT

A new study offers initial evidence that China's large dams on the Brahmaputra river in Tibet may not cause the droughts in India that were previously feared. By analyzing data from two existing dams, Zangmu and Jiacha, researchers found that downstream water flow during the dry season has actually increased. Specifically, low flows in February were observed to be up by more than 50%, suggesting the dams help regulate the river by storing water during wet seasons and releasing it during dry periods. This finding provides a new perspective on the potential transboundary water impacts of China's dam projects.
