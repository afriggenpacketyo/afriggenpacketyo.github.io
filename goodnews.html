New Moon Discovered in Orbit Around Uranus by James Webb Space Telescope

2025-08-19 17:40:00 EDT

NASA's James Webb Space Telescope has identified a new, small moon orbiting Uranus, the 29th known satellite of the ice giant. The moon, designated S/2025 U1, is approximately six miles in diameter, and its diminutive size is the likely reason it was not previously detected by telescopes or the Voyager 2 spacecraft during its 1986 flyby. The discovery was led by researchers from the Southwest Research Institute, who noted the complex relationship between Uranus's many small inner moons and its faint rings. This finding could indicate the presence of additional undiscovered moons, offering new insights into the planet's evolutionary history.
