Understanding the Nuances and Misuse of the Term 'Gaslighting'

2026-03-25 05:00:00 EDT

The term "gaslighting," derived from the 1944 film "Gaslight," has become a ubiquitous buzzword, but its meaning is often diluted. Experts define gaslighting as a deliberate effort to undermine another person's reality, making them doubt their perceptions and sanity, rather than simply disagreeing or lying. While the film depicts overt manipulation, real-life gaslighting is often more subtle, serving as a defense mechanism for the manipulator to wield power. Therapists emphasize the importance of precise usage to avoid trivializing this serious, "soul-destroying" form of psychological abuse and to empower victims in their healing process.
