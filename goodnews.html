WHO Approves First Malaria Drug Specifically Formulated for Infants

2026-04-24 19:30:00 EDT

The World Health Organization (WHO) has approved the first malaria drug specifically designed for infants, a combination of artemether and lumefantrine, safe for babies under 5 kilograms. This approval addresses a critical medical gap, as infants previously relied on adult-dosed drugs, risking errors and toxicity. The new formulation will allow UN agencies to procure and distribute the treatment in malaria-endemic areas, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa, where children under five account for 70% of malaria deaths. This significant advancement marks a positive step in global efforts to combat malaria, which still causes over half a million deaths annually.
