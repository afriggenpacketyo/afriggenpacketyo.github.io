RSPB Advises Against Feeding Garden Birds During Warmer Months to Prevent Disease Spread

2026-04-09 20:59:31 EDT

The Royal Society for the Protection of Birds (RSPB) is advising the public to stop feeding garden birds between May 1 and October 31, citing concerns that feeders can become disease hotspots during warmer months. The charity highlights the spread of trichomonosis, a parasite that has significantly impacted species like greenfinches and chaffinches, leading to population declines. The RSPB recommends "feed seasonally, feed safely," emphasizing hygiene and regular cleaning of feeders to prevent contamination. While acknowledging potential public resistance, the organization aims to curb disease transmission and protect bird populations. The pet food industry has expressed concerns about the abrupt change to long-established advice.
