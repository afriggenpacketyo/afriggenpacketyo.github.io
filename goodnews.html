Explainer: Understanding Grace Periods for Credit Card Payments

2025-07-22 11:26:03 EDT

This article clarifies the concept of grace periods for credit card payments, which are often misunderstood by consumers. Most credit cards offer a grace period on new purchases, allowing cardholders to avoid interest if they pay their balance in full by the due date. However, this protection typically disappears if a balance is carried over from the previous month. Crucially, there is no legally required grace period for late payments; once the due date passes, cardholders risk immediate late fees, penalty APRs, and damage to their credit score. The article advises contacting the issuer immediately if a payment will be late to potentially mitigate these consequences. (not investment advice)
