Understanding Which Debts Are Forgiven Upon a Person's Death

2026-06-23 10:36:55 EDT

When a person dies, their debts typically become the responsibility of their estate, not their surviving relatives, though not all debts are forgiven. Unsecured debts like credit card balances and personal loans without co-signers are often discharged if the estate lacks sufficient assets to cover them. Federal student loans are discharged upon death, and some private lenders offer similar provisions. Medical debt is generally not the responsibility of relatives unless they assumed it, and may go uncollected if the estate is exhausted. This article emphasizes the importance of addressing high-rate debt during one's lifetime to protect heirs and simplify estate planning. (not investment advice)
