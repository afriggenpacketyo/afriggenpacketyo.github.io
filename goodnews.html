Scotland Pioneers Water Cremations as an Environmentally Friendly Funeral Option in the UK

2026-03-02 01:10:44 EST

Scotland has made history by becoming the first country in the UK to legalize water cremations, also known as alkaline hydrolysis, offering an innovative and environmentally friendly alternative to traditional burial or cremation. This process involves gently dissolving body tissue in a pressurized chamber with hot water and chemicals, leaving only bone fragments, which are then returned to relatives as powdered remains in an urn. Touted as a "green cremation," it boasts significantly lower carbon dioxide emissions compared to conventional methods. While hydrolysis equipment needs to be built and approved by Scottish Water, the Scottish government anticipates the first procedures to commence by summer, providing greater choice for grieving families.
