Wealthy Investors Utilize ETFs to Defer Capital Gains Taxes

2025-08-21 17:06:58 EDT

A financial strategy known as a 351 conversion is gaining traction among wealthy investors as a way to defer capital gains taxes on highly appreciated assets. The method involves exchanging stocks from a portfolio for shares in a new exchange-traded fund (ETF) before it launches, which legally delays the tax bill until the ETF shares are sold. To qualify, the contributed assets must meet strict diversification rules, ensuring no single stock is over-weighted. While the strategy offers significant tax advantages, financial advisors caution that it can limit investment flexibility, as the investor's capital is then tied up in that specific ETF.
