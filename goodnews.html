Essential Wage Garnishment Rules for Borrowers Amid Rising Debt

2026-03-19 11:14:12 EDT

Amid record-high debt, this article details critical wage garnishment rules that borrowers should understand. Federal law limits garnishment to 25% of disposable earnings or 30 times the federal minimum wage, though exceptions exist for federal student loans, taxes, and child support, which may have higher limits. A court judgment is usually required for most consumer debts, but not always. State laws can offer additional protections, and certain income types like Social Security are exempt. Borrowers have the right to notice and can challenge garnishment, with options available even after it begins.
