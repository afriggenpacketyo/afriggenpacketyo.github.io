Astronomers Witness Earliest Stages of Rocky Planet Formation for the First Time

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb Space Telescope, astronomers have captured the first direct evidence of rocky planet formation around a very young, sun-like star named HOPS-315. Located 1,370 light-years away, the infant star's surrounding gas disk revealed the presence of silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing, the fundamental building blocks of planets like Earth. This observation provides an unprecedented glimpse into the processes that likely occurred at the dawn of our own solar system. The discovery suggests that the formation of Earth-like planets could be a common process, offering new avenues to explore how prevalent such worlds are in the universe.
