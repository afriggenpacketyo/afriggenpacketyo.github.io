Scientists and Startups Explore "Phytomining" by Harvesting Metals from Plants

2025-09-23 05:47:00 EDT

A novel process called phytomining is being developed to extract valuable metals like nickel from the soil by using "hyperaccumulating" plants. These species naturally absorb high concentrations of metals from the ground and store them in their leaves and shoots. After harvesting, the plants are burned to create a "bio-ore," which is then processed to yield pure metal sulfate, a component needed for electric vehicle batteries. Proponents highlight phytomining as an environmentally friendly alternative to conventional mining, as it can rehabilitate contaminated land with near-zero carbon emissions. However, its scalability remains a major challenge, positioning it as a supplementary method.
