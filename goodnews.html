Tracing the Linguistic Journey of "Kid" from Young Goat to Child

2026-02-04 05:00:00 EST

The word "kid" transitioned from solely referring to a young goat to also meaning a child or young adult around the 17th century, according to etymology experts like Rob Watts and Doug Harper. Originating from Old Norse during the Viking settlements in England, the term for a goat's offspring eventually supplanted the Old English "ticcen." Its adoption for children likely stemmed from the shared characteristics of being sprightly, energetic, curious, and even mischievous. The article also explores related terms, noting "kidnap" originally meant snatching children for indentured servitude, and the verb "to kid" evolved from fooling someone to playfully joking, adding rich context to this common word.
