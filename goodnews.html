Study Suggests Brief Activity Breaks May Lower Cancer Mortality Risk

2026-07-11 16:13:10 EDT

A new observational study from the University of Glasgow suggests that regularly interrupting prolonged sitting with physical activity is linked to a lower risk of cancer death. The research, involving over 91,000 UK participants, found that each additional hour of prolonged sedentary behavior increased cancer death risk by 10%. Conversely, replacing sitting with light, moderate, or vigorous activity was associated with a reduced risk. Experts emphasize that while the article doesn't prove causality, it reinforces the importance of movement for cancer prevention.

Antidepressants Can Exacerbate Heat Sensitivity, Posing Risks in Soaring Temperatures

2026-07-11 13:18:00 EDT

For individuals on antidepressants, summer heat can intensify discomfort due to how these medications affect the body's temperature regulation and sweating. Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) and tricyclic antidepressants can impact the hypothalamus, leading to increased heat sensitivity, dizziness, and dehydration. Some medications may also reduce the sensation of thirst or increase sweating. Experts advise patients not to stop medication abruptly but to stay hydrated and avoid direct sunlight, as climate change promises more frequent high temperatures.
