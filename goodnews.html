New Study in Mice Suggests Link Between Soybean Oil and Obesity

2025-11-30 13:06:02 EST

A recent study published in the Journal of Lipid Research indicates a potential link between soybean oil, America's most consumed cooking oil, and obesity. Researchers found that mice on a high soybean oil diet gained significant weight, an effect tied to how their bodies metabolize linoleic acid into molecules called oxylipins. Genetically modified mice with a reduced ability to produce these oxylipins gained much less weight on the same diet. The authors caution that these findings are from animal models and may not directly apply to human metabolism.
