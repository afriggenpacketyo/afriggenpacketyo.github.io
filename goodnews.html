Health Savings Accounts Can Create Unexpected Tax Burdens for Non-Spouse Heirs

2026-05-09 09:30:01 EDT

Health Savings Accounts (HSAs), recognized for their tax-free growth and withdrawals for qualified medical expenses, can unfortunately present substantial tax challenges for non-spouse heirs following the account holder's passing. Unlike surviving spouses, who inherit HSAs without immediate tax consequences, non-spouse beneficiaries are required to include the entire account balance as taxable income in the year of death, which could potentially elevate them into higher tax brackets. Financial planners recommend strategies such as spending down large HSA balances, donating them to charitable organizations, or distributing the inheritance among multiple individuals to mitigate the tax impact. Additionally, heirs can reduce their tax liability by using the HSA funds to cover the deceased's unpaid medical expenses within a 12-month period. (not investment advice)
