New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species Discovered in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the significant discovery of a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This long-necked sauropod, estimated to be up to 20 meters long, is believed to have lived approximately 150 million years ago during the Late Jurassic period. A detailed CT scan of a unique dorsal vertebra revealed its distinctive characteristics, including a Y-shaped bone arrangement and a unique air-cavity structure, setting it apart from any other known dinosaur globally. This marks the first discovery of a Mamenchisauridae family member in Thailand, significantly contributing to palaeontological understanding in Southeast Asia.
