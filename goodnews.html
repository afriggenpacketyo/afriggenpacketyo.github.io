French Scientists Discover New Blood Type in Guadeloupe Woman

2025-06-21 11:50:03 EDT

French scientists have identified a new blood type, dubbed "Gwada negative," in a woman from Guadeloupe. The discovery, made by the French Blood Establishment (EFS), marks the 48th blood group system recognized by the International Society of Blood Transfusion. The unusual antibody was initially detected in 2011, but the genetic mutation was only identified in 2019 through high-throughput DNA sequencing. The woman, now 54, is believed to be the only known carrier of this blood type, inherited from both her parents, and scientists hope to find others with the same rare blood group to improve care for patients with rare blood types.
