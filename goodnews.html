Nuremberg Trials' Legacy Examined as a Milestone in International Criminal Law

2025-11-20 02:58:00 EST

On November 20, 1945, the Nuremberg Trials began, establishing a groundbreaking precedent by holding high-ranking state officials personally accountable for war crimes and crimes against humanity. The trials prosecuted 24 top Nazi leaders, resulting in twelve death sentences and marking the first time individual guilt was a central tenet of international justice. Despite its historical significance, the German public initially viewed it as "victor's justice." The principles established at Nuremberg laid the groundwork for modern institutions like the International Criminal Court, though the enforcement of international criminal law remains a significant challenge today.
