New Dicynodont Fossil in China Reveals Ancient Cross-Equator Migration Corridor in Pangaea

2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers at the Chinese Academy of Sciences' Institute of Vertebrate Palaeontology and Paleoanthropology have unveiled a new species of dicynodont, an extinct clade of non-mammalian therapsids, named Dinanomodon guoi. This remarkable fossil, discovered in Gulang county, Gansu province, China, signifies the first bidentalian genus found in both China and South Africa. The discovery offers compelling evidence for a cross-equator migration corridor during the era of the supercontinent Pangaea, shedding new light on the ancient distribution patterns of terrestrial vertebrates. Dicynodonts, often described as "cousins" to today's mammals, were a diverse group of herbivorous animals.
