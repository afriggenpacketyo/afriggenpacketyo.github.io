Dozens of New Ancient Animal Species Discovered in Chinese Quarry

2026-01-28 15:21:40 EST

Scientists have discovered almost a hundred new animal species in a Chinese quarry, offering a rare glimpse into life immediately following a mass extinction event 513 million years ago. The "Huayuan biota" fossils, found in Hunan province, represent the first major discovery of soft-bodied animals from directly after the Sinsk event, which brought a sudden end to the Cambrian explosion. Many fossils show soft parts like gills, guts, and eyes. The discovery also revealed species previously only known from Canada's Burgess Shale, suggesting ancient animals could travel globally via ocean currents, surprising researchers and opening a new window into early evolution.
