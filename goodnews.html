The Surprising and Evolving History of the Word 'Dude'

2025-07-30 05:00:00 EDT

The word 'dude,' often associated with 1980s surfer culture, actually originated a century earlier in New York City as a term for overly fashionable, effeminate young men. The term likely referenced "Yankee Doodle Dandy" and was initially used to mock those perceived as trying too hard to appear sophisticated. It later evolved to describe East Coast city dwellers visiting western "dude ranches" in elaborate cowboy attire. In the mid-20th century, it was adopted by Black and Mexican American communities as a term of solidarity before being popularized by California subcultures and becoming the familiar, versatile greeting it is today.
