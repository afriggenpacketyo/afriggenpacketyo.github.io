Specialized USPS Facility in Utah Deciphers Illegible Mail

2025-07-24 20:54:07 EDT

When automated systems at U.S. Postal Service facilities cannot read an address due to poor handwriting, the mail is not returned to sender but is instead sent digitally to a unique center in Salt Lake City, Utah. This Remote Encoding Center (REC) employs hundreds of 'data conversion operators' who work around the clock to manually decipher these difficult addresses. These specialists, known as keyers, process approximately 900 images of mail per hour, ensuring that millions of otherwise undeliverable letters reach their intended destinations. If a keyer cannot decode an address, it is sent for a final hands-on inspection as a last resort.
