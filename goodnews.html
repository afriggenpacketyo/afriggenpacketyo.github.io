Buildings Increasingly Adopt 'Ice Batteries' for More Sustainable Air Conditioning

2025-10-07 09:11:19 EDT

A growing number of buildings, from hospitals to data centers, are installing thermal energy storage systems, also known as "ice batteries," to provide sustainable air conditioning. This technology freezes large tanks of water at night when electricity is cheaper and demand on the grid is low. During the day, the melting ice is used to chill water that circulates through the building, providing cooling without drawing significant power during peak hours. This load-shifting approach reduces planet-warming emissions, lowers electricity costs, and eases strain on the power grid, offering a viable solution to meet rising cooling demands driven by climate change.
