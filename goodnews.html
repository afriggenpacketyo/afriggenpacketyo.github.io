2025-07-11 13:30:00 EDT

The widely accepted fitness goal of walking 10,000 steps per day originated not from scientific research but from a 1960s Japanese marketing campaign for a pedometer. While the number itself is somewhat arbitrary, health experts note that it coincidentally aligns with public health guidelines for physical activity, equating to about five miles of walking. Recent studies suggest health benefits, such as reduced cardiovascular risk, can begin at lower step counts like 2,500 to 3,000 per day, with significant gains plateauing around 7,500 steps. The consensus is that any movement is beneficial, and using the goal as a motivator is positive.
