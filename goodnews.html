Man's Blood Yields Potent Antivenom Effective Against 19 Deadly Snakes

2025-05-05 05:00:37 EDT

Fox News reports on the development of a potentially universal antivenom derived from the blood of Tim Friede, a man who intentionally subjected himself to numerous snake bites over 18 years. Friede's blood contained antibodies that reacted with neurotoxins from 19 of the world's deadliest snakes. Researchers combined two of these antibodies to create an antivenom that proved protective in mouse trials. The hope is that this research could lead to a broad-spectrum antivenom that can be administered in rural settings, offering a more effective and accessible treatment for snake bites globally.
