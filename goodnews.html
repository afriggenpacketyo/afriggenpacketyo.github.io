Understanding IRS Offer in Compromise for Tax Debt Relief

2025-06-20 12:35:51 EDT

The Offer in Compromise (OIC) program is a tax relief option offered by the IRS that allows eligible taxpayers to settle their tax debt for a lower amount than what they originally owe. To qualify, taxpayers must demonstrate an inability to pay their full tax bill without facing financial hardship. The IRS assesses eligibility based on factors such as the taxpayer's ability to pay, income, expenses, and asset equity, and the application process involves substantial paperwork and documentation, including financial statements and supporting documents. The IRS accepts less than half of all applications, making it essential for applicants to be honest, patient, and well-prepared, and it is advisable to consult with a tax professional to navigate the complexities of the process.
