Why American and European Chocolate Have Distinctly Different Flavors

2025-07-07 03:03:00 EDT

The difference in taste between American and European chocolate stems from distinct production priorities and regulations. U.S. chocolate, like Hershey's, often includes butyric acid, a preservative that extends shelf life but imparts a slightly sour tang that can be unfamiliar to European palates. American recipes also tend to have a higher sugar content. In contrast, EU regulations mandate higher cocoa solid percentages and the use of cocoa butter as the main fat, while traditional processes like conching create a finer texture. These varying approaches reflect different consumer preferences and historical developments in the global chocolate industry.
