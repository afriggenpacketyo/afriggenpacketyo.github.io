Scientists Investigate Efficacy of Cannabis in Reducing Alcohol Consumption

Scientists are actively investigating whether cannabis can help reduce alcohol consumption, with new research providing some of the strongest data supporting the "California sober" approach. A study from Brown University, designed to mimic real-life drinking scenarios, found that participants consuming higher potency cannabis drank 27% less alcohol compared to a placebo group, also delaying their initial drink. While these findings suggest cannabinoids could play a therapeutic role in alcohol use disorder, researchers emphasize caution.
