New York City Implements Ranked Choice Voting in Mayoral Primary

2025-06-23 00:04:00 EDT

New York City is utilizing ranked choice voting in its Democratic mayoral primary, a system where voters rank multiple candidates in order of preference. If no candidate secures a majority, the candidate with the fewest first-choice votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed based on voters' second choices. This process continues until a candidate reaches a majority, ensuring broad support for the winner. Preliminary results based on first choices will be available shortly after polls close, but the ranked choice tabulations will begin on July 1, with official certification on July 15. This is the second time New York City has used ranked choice in a mayoral primary, with officials hoping for a smoother process than in 2021.
