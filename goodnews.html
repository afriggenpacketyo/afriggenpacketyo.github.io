Influential German Philosopher Jürgen Habermas Dies at 96

2026-03-14 10:39:00 EDT

Jürgen Habermas, the world-renowned German philosopher and sociologist, has passed away at the age of 96 in Starnberg near Munich. As one of the most influential thinkers of the 20th century, Habermas remained intellectually active, consistently advocating for an open, rigorous democracy and a unified European Union against rising nationalism. His seminal work, "The Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere," explored the evolution and importance of public opinion in democratic societies. A key figure of the Frankfurt School, Habermas dedicated his life's work to fostering communication models based on consensus, aiming to prevent historical barbarism. His theories profoundly influenced post-war Germany's self-image and the 1968 student protests.
