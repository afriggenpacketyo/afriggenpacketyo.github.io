Explainer on President Trump's Chronic Venous Insufficiency Diagnosis

2025-07-18 20:08:30 EDT

President Trump was recently diagnosed with chronic venous insufficiency (CVI) after experiencing mild swelling in his lower legs, a condition his physician described as "benign and common." CVI occurs when veins, particularly in the legs, struggle to return blood to the heart, causing it to pool and lead to swelling, aching, or heaviness. The condition affects about one in three U.S. adults and is more common with age and in those who sit or stand for long periods. Treatment typically involves lifestyle changes like compression stockings, leg elevation, and exercise, with minimally invasive procedures available for more serious cases.
