UK Clocks to Revert to GMT on October 26, Marking End of British Summer Time

2025-10-11 19:32:29 EDT

The UK clocks will go back by one hour on the last Sunday of October, specifically at 02:00 BST on October 26, 2025, marking the end of British Summer Time (BST) and a return to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). This annual shift, easily remembered by the phrase "spring forward, fall back," grants most people an extra hour but extends night shifts. The practice, initiated by Germany in 1916 during WWI to save fuel, has continued with ongoing debates about whether the UK should adopt year-round BST. Arguments for permanent BST include potential economic boosts and reduced evening road accidents, while critics highlight the very gloomy winter mornings it would create, particularly in Scotland.
