2025-11-30 17:00:52 EST

Completed in 1613, its existence was previously known only through engravings made by other artists. Initially suspected to be from one of Rubens' many workshops, a hunch led to scientific analysis by the Centrum Rubenianum, which definitively confirmed its authenticity. The microscopic examination revealed blue and green pigments consistent with the Baroque master's treatment of human skin, adding a significant and well-preserved piece to art history.
