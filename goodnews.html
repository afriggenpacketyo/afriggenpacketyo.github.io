Chinese Researchers Successfully Grow Beating Human Heart Structure in Pig Embryo

2025-07-03 22:00:13 EDT

In a significant scientific advance, a team of Chinese scientists has successfully grown a beating heart structure containing human cells within a pig embryo. The researchers reprogrammed human stem cells to improve their survival and then implanted them into pig embryos, which were transferred to surrogate sows. The resulting chimeric heart structure continued to develop and beat for 21 days, marking a major step forward in the field of interspecies organogenesis. This research, which follows the team's earlier success with growing human kidneys in pigs, could have long-term implications for organ transplantation.
