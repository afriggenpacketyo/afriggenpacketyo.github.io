The term 'hocus pocus' itself is believed by some to be a satirical corruption of the Latin Eucharistic prayer, 'Hoc est enim corpus meum.' Over time, the magician's famous incantation became a general term for trickery and deception. By the early 1800s, the phrase was shortened through common speech to 'hoax,' solidifying its meaning as a deliberate fraud or swindle, from simple pranks to grand deceptions.
