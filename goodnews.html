NASA Launches Twin TRACERS Satellites to Study 'Space Weather'

2025-07-23 19:48:00 EDT

NASA has successfully launched the TRACERS mission, sending two identical satellites into orbit aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket. The twin spacecraft will fly in tandem to study how the solar wind, a stream of charged particles from the sun, interacts with Earth's magnetic field. This interaction creates "space weather," which can produce spectacular auroras but also disrupt satellites, GPS signals, and power grids on the ground. By taking precise, simultaneous measurements from two points, scientists hope to better understand and eventually predict these potentially dangerous space weather events, helping to protect critical infrastructure.
