The Evolution of "Broadcasting" from Agricultural Practice to Mass Media Term

2025-10-15 05:00:00 EDT

The word "broadcasting," commonly associated with radio and television, surprisingly originated in agriculture, describing the method of scattering seeds widely across a field. This historical journey saw the term adopt its media meaning in the early 20th century, notably with Frank Conrad's 1920 presidential election broadcast from KDKA, electrifying the nation and ushering in the radio age. The Communications Act of 1934 legally cemented its new definition, and by the 1930s, "broadcasting" universally meant radio. Even today, as technology evolves, the concept of broadly disseminating information persists, underscoring its enduring importance in shaping culture, news, and democratic understanding.
