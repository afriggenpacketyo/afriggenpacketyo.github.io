Scientists Propose New Theory on the Origin of Giant Exploding Craters in Siberia

2025-09-23 04:25:25 EDT

Researchers have presented a new theory explaining the formation of giant gas emission craters (GECs) in Western Siberia, a mystery that has persisted for over a decade. The study suggests these craters form from a combination of deep underground heat and gas rising to the surface, which thins the permafrost seal from below. This internal pressure, combined with accelerated surface thawing due to climate change, leads to an explosive collapse of the ground. This model aims to explain why these massive craters, which can be over 160 feet deep, have only been observed in the Yamal and Gydan peninsulas, areas with vast natural gas reserves.
