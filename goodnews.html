Explainer: The Science Behind the Urban Heat Island Effect

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As a heatwave impacts the UK, this article explains the phenomenon known as the urban heat island effect, which causes cities to be significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. The primary cause is the prevalence of dark, hard surfaces like asphalt and concrete, which absorb and retain the sun's heat more than natural landscapes. This stored heat is released slowly, keeping urban temperatures elevated, especially at night. The effect is compounded by waste heat from vehicles and buildings, and a lack of cooling vegetation and water, making city dwellers more vulnerable to the impacts of climate change.
