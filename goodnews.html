Filing a Tax Extension Provides More Time to File, But Not to Pay

2025-07-21 11:37:28 EDT

This article clarifies a common misconception about filing for a tax extension with the IRS. While requesting an extension grants an additional six months to submit a tax return, it does not postpone the deadline for paying any taxes owed. The IRS still requires taxpayers to estimate their liability and pay it by the original April deadline to avoid penalties and interest charges. For those unable to pay their full tax bill, the IRS offers several options, including installment plans and, in cases of significant financial hardship, an Offer in Compromise.
