2026-06-29 01:10:46 EDT

Palaeontologists confirmed it as a tail bone from a Titanosaur, a group that included the largest dinosaurs to ever walk the Earth. This discovery, made within the British Antarctic Survey's geology collection, offers crucial new insights into how these enormous creatures lived in a part of the world where the fossil record is sparse. It reveals that an area now thought uninhabitable was once a vibrant ecosystem about 80 million years ago, significantly contributing to prehistoric knowledge.
