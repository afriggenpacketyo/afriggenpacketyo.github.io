Understanding Haboobs: Dust Storms Triggered by Thunderstorms

2025-08-26 15:00:47 EDT

This article explains "haboobs," massive dust storms caused by outflowing winds from thunderstorms, such as the one that recently impacted the Phoenix, Arizona area, disrupting travel and power. Derived from the Arabic word "habb," haboobs are a common occurrence in arid and semi-arid regions worldwide, including the American Southwest. They form when strong downdrafts from building thunderstorm clouds hit the earth's surface, pushing outward and kicking up dust and debris from dry soils. Haboobs significantly reduce visibility, making driving extremely hazardous, and the National Weather Service strongly advises drivers to pull their vehicles off the pavement, stop, turn off lights, and set emergency brakes for safety during these events.
