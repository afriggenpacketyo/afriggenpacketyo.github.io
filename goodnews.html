The Soviet-era Venera mission probe Cosmos 482, which failed to leave Earth's orbit in 1972, is expected to re-enter Earth's atmosphere. Designed to withstand Venus's harsh atmospheric conditions, the lander may partially survive its re-entry, posing a potential threat if it lands on ground. While most space debris burns up, Cosmos 482 is relatively large, weighing 495 kilograms, and its descent is uncontrolled, but the risk of human injury is low. Russia plans to launch a new mission to Venus, Venera-D, in the early 2030s, signaling a renewed interest in exploring the planet.
