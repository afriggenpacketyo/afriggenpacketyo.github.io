China Completes Major Green Belt to Combat Desertification in Inner Mongolia

2025-07-09 00:00:08 EDT

China has completed a 1,856-kilometer "green great wall" in Inner Mongolia, a massive environmental project designed to control the spread of three major deserts. The green belt runs along the southern edges of the Badain Jaran, Tengger, and Ulan Buh deserts, which cover a combined area of nearly 95,000 square kilometers. The project utilizes techniques like straw checkerboards to stabilize shifting sands, followed by the planting of drought-resistant trees such as sacsaoul. This effort is a key part of China's broader strategy to build an effective ecological barrier and combat the advance of desertification.
