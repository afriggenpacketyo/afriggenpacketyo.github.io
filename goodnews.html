Chinese Scientists Discover Evidence of Recent Landslides on the Moon Triggered by Moonquakes

2025-09-21 00:00:13 EDT

A team of Chinese researchers has identified 41 new landslides on the moon that have occurred since 2009, challenging the long-held belief that the moon is geologically inactive. By comparing high-resolution images taken years apart, the scientists found evidence that these landslides were triggered by moonquakes, indicating ongoing endogenic activity. Published in the National Science Review, their findings have significant implications for future lunar missions and the establishment of permanent bases on the moon. The research underscores the need to assess geological hazards, like seismicity, when planning for long-term human presence on the lunar surface.
