American Football's Origins Traced Back to Violent Medieval English Games

2026-01-28 05:00:00 EST

Despite its strong American identity, both the term "football" and the foundational concepts of the modern game trace their origins to medieval England, where rudimentary and often violent games were widely played. Historically, "football" referred to any game played on foot with a ball, with written records appearing as early as 1409. These medieval contests were largely unregulated, involving numerous players and frequently leading to severe injuries or even fatalities, prompting royal decrees against their brutality. The game gradually evolved and was brought to New England by English settlers. The formal differentiation between soccer and rugby was established in 19th-century England, while American colleges subsequently developed their distinct version, which, despite less emphasis on kicking, retained the traditional "football" designation.
