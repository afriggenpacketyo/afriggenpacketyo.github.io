2025-12-06 08:00:01 EST

Blue jeans, a quintessential American garment, originated during the California gold rush when tailor Jacob Davis added copper rivets to denim work pants to enhance their durability. He partnered with his fabric supplier, Levi Strauss, and on May 20, 1873, they patented the design, creating an icon of workwear for miners and cowboys. Initially confined to labor, jeans transitioned into casual fashion through "dude ranch" vacations in the 1930s and became a mainstream staple in the post-World War II era of leisure clothing. Today, denim is a more than $100 billion global market, with its enduring appeal rooted in its remarkable continuity from an 1873 design.
