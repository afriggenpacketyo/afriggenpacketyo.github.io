2025-05-20 11:33:22 EDT

Researchers used machine learning to analyze over 86,000 satellite images of slope streaks and recurring slope lineae (RSLs). The analysis found that the streaks were not generally associated with factors suggesting a liquid or frost origin, but were more likely found in areas with above-average wind speeds and dust deposition. The study casts doubt on the possibility of habitable environments in those regions.
