Rock-Climbing Fish Discovered Scaling 50-Foot Waterfalls in Congo

2026-04-05 07:00:00 EDT

Researchers have formally documented a remarkable phenomenon in the Democratic Republic of Congo: shellear fish (Parakneria thysi) climbing 50-foot waterfalls. This behavior, previously unrecorded in Africa, involves thousands of small fish using their pelvic fins for support and tiny hooks on their pectoral fins to grip rocks. The fish ascend in the splash zone, wriggling vertically and pausing frequently to recover energy, a journey that can take up to ten hours. Scientists believe this vertical migration is crucial for connecting upstream and downstream populations, emphasizing the importance of protecting the waterfall habitat from disruptions like dam construction for biodiversity and conservation efforts.
