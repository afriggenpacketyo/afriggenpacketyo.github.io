Nobel Prize in Chemistry Awarded for Development of Metal-Organic Frameworks

2025-10-08 06:08:02 EDT

The 2025 Nobel Prize in chemistry has been jointly awarded to Susumu Kitagawa, Richard Robson, and Omar M. Yaghi for their pioneering work in the design and synthesis of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs). These are highly porous, crystalline materials constructed from metal ions linked by organic molecules, creating vast internal surface areas. The Nobel committee highlighted the enormous potential of MOFs, which can be used for applications such as capturing carbon dioxide, storing gases, and harvesting water from desert air. Their research has opened up a new field of chemistry, enabling the creation of custom-made materials with novel functions.
