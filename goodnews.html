2025-10-11 19:32:29 EDT

The clocks in the UK will go back by one hour on Sunday, October 26, marking the end of British Summer Time (BST) and a return to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). This annual shift, remembered by the phrase "spring forward, fall back," grants most people an extra hour of sleep but means an additional hour for night shift workers. The practice, initiated by Germany in 1916 during World War One to maximize evening daylight and reduce fuel consumption, has continued for over a century. Debates persist about whether the UK should permanently adopt summer time, weighing potential boosts in economic productivity and reduced road accidents against very gloomy winter mornings, especially in Scotland.
