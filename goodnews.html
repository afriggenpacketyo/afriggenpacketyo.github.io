Andy Hill, a former UCLA basketball player under John Wooden, created the plus-minus stat, now widely used in basketball to measure a player's impact on the team's score while on the court. Hill developed the concept in 1976 while coaching at Santa Monica College, seeking a way to reward players who contributed to winning beyond scoring. Coaches such as Mick Cronin and Lakers coach JJ Redick use plus-minus to assess player performance, though some, like Ken Pomeroy, caution against overreliance on the stat.
