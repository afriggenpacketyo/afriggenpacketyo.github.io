Civil Rights Pioneer Claudette Colvin Dies at 86

2026-01-13 20:13:57 EST

Claudette Colvin, a significant but often overlooked figure in the civil rights movement, has passed away at the age of 86. Her death was confirmed by the Claudette Colvin Legacy Foundation. Nine months prior to Rosa Parks' renowned act, Colvin, then a 15-year-old, was arrested in Montgomery, Alabama, in March 1955 for refusing to surrender her seat on a segregated bus. Her courageous defiance played a crucial role in sparking the modern civil rights movement and served as a foundational element for the landmark lawsuit that ultimately desegregated Montgomery's bus system. Montgomery Mayor Steven Reed acknowledged her profound, though often unrecognized, bravery.
