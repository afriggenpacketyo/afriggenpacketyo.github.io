New Research Challenges Long-Held Belief That Dieting Causes Overeating and Weight Gain

2026-03-13 10:30:00 EDT

This article critically examines how contemporary scientific evidence is now challenging a deeply ingrained, 50-year-old dieting myth which posits that intentionally restricting food intake invariably results in overeating and subsequent weight gain. Early psychological studies, such as the influential 1970s "milkshake study," appeared to suggest that dieting unleashed a "hidden hunger," significantly shaping eating disorder treatments and fueling the anti-diet movement. However, more recent and rigorous randomized, controlled trials have demonstrated that effective dietary restriction can, in fact, reduce instances of binge eating and enhance body image for many individuals. The evolving scientific consensus is more nuanced, indicating that while extreme restriction remains unhealthy, a measured approach to dietary restraint can potentially prevent eating disorders in high-risk populations.
