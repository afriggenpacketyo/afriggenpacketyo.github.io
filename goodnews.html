2025-09-10 12:08:00 EDT

NASA's Perseverance rover has collected rock samples from a dry river channel on Mars that contain the strongest indications yet of potential ancient microbial life. While scientists emphasize that the findings are not definitive proof of life, the samples contain organic carbon along with iron phosphate and iron sulfide compounds. On Earth, these chemicals are often byproducts of microorganisms consuming organic matter. The rover is drilling and storing these samples for a future, though currently delayed, mission to return them to Earth for in-depth analysis. This discovery marks a significant step in the ongoing quest to determine if life ever existed on Mars.
