Comparing Returns: CDs Often Outperform High-Yield Savings and Money Market Accounts

2026-05-20 12:38:07 EDT

This article compares the interest-earning potential of a $2,500 deposit across Certificates of Deposit (CDs), high-yield savings accounts, and money market accounts over various periods. It finds that CDs generally offer higher returns, particularly over six months and one year, due to their locked interest rates, unlike the variable rates of the other two options. For instance, a one-year CD at 4.10% would yield $102.50, slightly more than a high-yield savings account at 4.03% ($100.75) or a money market account at 3.90% ($97.50).
