Understanding Jewish Funeral Traditions and Mourning Rituals

2025-10-15 10:07:00 EDT

Jewish funeral traditions prioritize quick, simple burials and focus on eternal memory among the living, rather than an afterlife. Bodies undergo a ritual bath (Taharah) and are buried in plain wooden caskets or shrouds, without embalming or public viewing. Mourners perform K'riah, a ritual tearing of clothing, and observe shiva for seven days, refraining from work and grooming. The Mourner's Kaddish, an ancient Aramaic prayer, is recited in communal settings to honor the deceased and provide support, with gravestones typically added a month to a year after burial.
