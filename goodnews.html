Viral Photos of 'Zombie Rabbits' in Colorado Explained by Wildlife Officials

2025-08-13 20:33:00 EDT

Photos of rabbits with black, horn-like growths circulating online have prompted concern, but Colorado wildlife officials have a scientific explanation. The animals are suffering from Shope Papilloma Virus, a relatively common and typically harmless disease spread by fleas, ticks, and rabbit-to-rabbit contact. The growths, or papillomas, are usually not harmful unless they interfere with the rabbit's ability to eat, and they disappear once the animal's immune system fights off the virus. The disease, which cannot spread to humans or other pets, is believed to be the origin of the mythical "jackalope" creature.
