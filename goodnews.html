2025-10-29 05:00:00 EDT

The term "neurodivergent" has entered mainstream vocabulary as an inclusive umbrella term for people whose brains process information in ways that differ from societal norms. Coined in the late 1990s by activist Kassiane Asasumasu, it was intended to create solidarity among people with various neurological differences, including but not limited to autism, ADHD, depression, or brain injuries. Its popularity surged during the COVID-19 pandemic amid broader conversations about mental health and accessibility, offering a way for individuals to describe their experiences without disclosing specific medical diagnoses.
