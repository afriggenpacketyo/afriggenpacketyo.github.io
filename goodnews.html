2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb Space Telescope, astronomers have captured the first direct evidence of rocky planet formation around a very young, sun-like star named HOPS-315. Researchers detected silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing in the star's surrounding disk, the fundamental building blocks of planets like Earth. This discovery provides an unprecedented look into the dawn of a new solar system, suggesting that the processes which formed our own planet may be common throughout the universe. The findings could help answer longstanding questions about the prevalence of Earth-like worlds.
