2025-07-24 05:00:00 EDT

Researchers compared daily calorie expenditure across diverse populations, from hunter-gatherers to office workers, and found that total energy burn is surprisingly similar regardless of activity levels. This suggests our bodies compensate for energy spent on exercise by reducing it elsewhere. The findings provide strong evidence that the shift in diet, particularly the rise of ultra-processed foods, is the principal factor behind increasing obesity rates, rather than a collective decline in physical activity.
