New Dinosaur Species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, Discovered in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Paleontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, based on fossils found in Kalasin Province. This long-necked sauropod is estimated to have lived around 150 million years ago and measured up to 20 meters in length. The discovery, made from a vertebra with unique air-cavity structures, marks the first of its kind from the Mamenchisauridae family found in Thailand. The find adds to the growing understanding of dinosaur diversity in Southeast Asia, with the Phu Noi site yielding numerous fossils from the Late Jurassic period.
