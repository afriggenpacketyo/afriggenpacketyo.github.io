Scientists Uncover How Epstein-Barr Virus Triggers Multiple Sclerosis in Genetically Susceptible Individuals

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Scientists have made a significant breakthrough in understanding how the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) contributes to multiple sclerosis (MS) in genetically predisposed individuals. The research reveals that when B cells infected with EBV also possess the HLA-DR15 molecule, the virus manipulates these cells to present myelin protein, which is typically found in nerve fibers. This "fatal similarity" causes the immune system to mistakenly attack the body's own nerve tissue, essentially programming it for self-destruction. This discovery identifies HLA-DR15 as a crucial genetic risk factor and opens new avenues for potential early childhood vaccinations and targeted treatments for MS patients who carry this genetic package.
