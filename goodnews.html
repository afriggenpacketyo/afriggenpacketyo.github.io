2025-09-01 13:40:00 EDT

A new study published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences reveals a significant divergence in the body size evolution of wild and domestic animals, starting in the Middle Ages. Researchers analyzing thousands of animal bones from southern France found that as human settlements expanded and hunting intensified, wild species such as foxes and deer became smaller. In contrast, domesticated animals like cattle, sheep, and chickens grew larger due to increased human control and selective breeding for specific traits. The findings underscore the profound and lasting impact of human activities on the natural world over the past millennium.
