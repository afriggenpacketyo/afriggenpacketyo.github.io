NATO's Purpose, Creation, and Collective Defense Explained

2025-06-24 02:21:00 EDT

NATO, the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, was established in 1949 primarily to deter Soviet expansion in Europe after World War II, and also to prevent nationalist resurgence and foster political integration on the continent. The alliance operates as a collective security organization, providing mutual defense through military and political means if a member state is threatened by an external country. Article 5 of the charter, the collective defense clause, states that an attack against one member is considered an attack against all, and has been invoked once, by the United States after the 9/11 attacks. Currently with 32 members, NATO continues to adapt to evolving security challenges, with countries like Finland and Sweden recently joining.
