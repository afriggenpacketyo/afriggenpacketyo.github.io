Chinese Researchers Develop Groundbreaking AI Chip Storing Data with a Single Electron

2026-07-23 05:00:12 EDT

Researchers at Fudan University in Shanghai have achieved a significant breakthrough in AI memory technology with their new chip, Guiyi, which can store a single bit of information using just one electron. This innovation dramatically reduces the electron requirement compared to current advanced dynamic random access memory (DRAM) chips, which utilize approximately 200,000 electrons per bit, effectively addressing a major memory bottleneck in artificial intelligence. The Guiyi device impressively amplifies a faint, fleeting signal from mere tens of millivolts to a robust 0.5 volts, marking a tenfold improvement over any prior single-electron storage attempts. This development holds immense potential for advancing AI computing efficiency and data density.
