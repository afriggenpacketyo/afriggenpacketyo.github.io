Biological differences explain why women get drunk faster than men

2025-12-22 04:27:00 EST

Scientific research indicates that women often feel the effects of alcohol more rapidly and intensely than men due to biological factors, not simply tolerance. Differences in alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH) enzymes, body composition with lower water content, and hormonal influences like estradiol amplify alcohol's effects. Women also exhibit "telescoping," a faster progression from alcohol use to dependence. These biological distinctions mean women can experience higher blood alcohol levels and more pronounced brain responses, even when consuming the same amount of alcohol as men. Understanding these differences is crucial for health awareness and responsible drinking.
