Discovery of 17th-Century Map Pinpoints Shakespeare's London House

2026-04-15 20:08:54 EDT

A newly discovered 17th-century map has precisely located William Shakespeare's only purchased home in London, resolving a long-standing mystery. The map, found by Shakespeare scholar Lucy Munro at the London Archives, details the playwright's substantial L-shaped dwelling near the Blackfriars Theatre, which he bought in 1613. While historians knew he owned property in the area, its exact location remained unknown, with previous markers being imprecise. The discovery offers new insights into Shakespeare's life in the city, suggesting he may have spent more time in London towards the end of his life and potentially worked on his final plays there. The house was destroyed in the Great Fire of London in 1666.
