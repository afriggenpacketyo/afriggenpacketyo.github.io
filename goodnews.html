Social Security Benefits Can Be Garnished After 70 Under Specific Conditions

2026-05-28 12:19:33 EDT

Social Security benefits can indeed be garnished after age 70, primarily for federal debts such as income tax, defaulted federal student loans, or child support obligations. Age alone does not provide an exemption. While private creditors generally cannot directly garnish these payments, they can freeze or levy bank accounts where benefits are deposited after obtaining a court judgment. Federal rules offer some protection by requiring banks to shield a two-month equivalent of Social Security deposits from such levies.
