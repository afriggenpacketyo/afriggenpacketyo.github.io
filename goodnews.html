California Introduces Nation's Strictest Wildfire Safety Rules for Homes After Devastating Fires

2026-01-06 07:24:27 EST

Following devastating fires like the Eaton and Palisades Fires, California is drafting the nation's most stringent wildfire safety rules, mandating homeowners to clear vegetation within five feet of their homes in high-risk areas. Known as "Zone Zero," these regulations aim to prevent embers from igniting flammable items and spreading flames to structures, a primary cause of home destruction in wildfires. Despite scientific evidence supporting the measure, the proposals face significant public pushback over concerns about losing greenery, shade, and the costs associated with landscaping changes. The rules, once finalized, could set a precedent for other Western states grappling with increasing wildfire threats.
