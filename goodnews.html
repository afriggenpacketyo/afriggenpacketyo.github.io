The Evolution and Impact of U.S. College Rankings

2025-10-02 17:38:08 EDT

U.S. college rankings, now a cultural staple, originated as controversial experiments, as detailed in an article. Early attempts, like Edwin Embree's 1934 list and Kendric Babcock's 1911 federal study, faced immense backlash and suppression due to their perceived recklessness and social biases. The modern era of rankings, spearheaded by U.S. News & World Report in the 1980s, emerged as higher education became a mass-consumer good, providing a "map of the American meritocracy." While methods have evolved, rankings continue to shape public perception, influence admissions, and prompt institutions to adapt their strategies, despite ongoing debate about their validity and impact.
