2026-02-21 07:55:00 EST

Ecuadorian authorities released 158 juvenile tortoises, aged 8 to 13, as the initial phase of a larger plan to resettle 700 animals, aiming to restore the island's ecosystem. These juveniles carry significant genetic links to the original "Chelonoidis niger" species that disappeared in the 19th century due to human exploitation and invasive mammals. Officials hope this long-term rewilding effort will eventually restore the island's original species profile, with seasonal rains aiding the tortoises' adjustment to their new environment, despite ongoing challenges from invasive species.
