An FDA-approved vibrating belt, Osteoboost, is showing potential in slowing bone density loss for post-menopausal women with osteopenia. The device delivers low-magnitude vibrations to the spine and hips, mimicking some effects of exercise and stimulating bone-building cells. Clinical trials suggest an 83% reduction in bone strength loss in the spine over 12 months compared to a placebo group. While researchers highlight its potential as a supplementary treatment, some scientists caution that it targets a specific site and may not fully address osteopenia as a systemic disease. The device costs approximately $1,000 and is available by prescription only, with insurance not covering it.
