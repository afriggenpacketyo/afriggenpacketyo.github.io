2025-07-08 18:01:41 EDT

A new tax and spending package signed by President Trump does not eliminate federal income taxes on Social Security benefits, contrary to some claims. Instead, the legislation introduces a new, temporary "bonus" tax deduction of up to $6,000 for individuals aged 65 and older with incomes below a certain threshold. While this will provide tax relief for some seniors, policy experts note it does not change the fundamental way Social Security benefits are taxed. The deduction will primarily benefit higher-income seniors, while many low-income recipients who already pay no federal income tax will see no change.
