Why Red Wine May Be More Likely to Cause Headaches

2025-05-09 07:01:16 EDT

Researchers are investigating why red wine is more likely to cause headaches than white wine, focusing on a flavanol called quercetin found in higher concentrations in red wines. Quercetin, a healthy antioxidant in grapes, can interfere with alcohol metabolism, leading to an accumulation of acetaldehyde, a toxin that can cause facial flushing, nausea, and headaches. Certain individuals, particularly those of East Asian descent, may have an enzyme variant that hinders the elimination of acetaldehyde, making them more susceptible to red wine headaches. The way grapes are grown and wine is made can also affect quercetin levels.
