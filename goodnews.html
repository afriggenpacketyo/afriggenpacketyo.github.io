Key Differences Between ETFs and Mutual Funds for Investors

2025-09-22 07:00:01 EDT

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) and mutual funds, while both professionally managed and offering diversification, have key distinctions important for investors. ETFs trade like stocks throughout the day, providing immediate pricing, unlike mutual funds which price at day's end. ETFs also offer significant tax efficiency due to "in-kind" transactions, rarely distributing capital gains to shareholders in taxable accounts, a major advantage over mutual funds. Furthermore, ETFs generally have lower expense ratios, making them a cheaper investment option. However, mutual funds still offer a wider selection and are more common in 401(k) plans.
