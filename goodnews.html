Catastrophe Bonds Gain Popularity as a Tool to Manage Natural Disaster Insurance Costs

2025-09-30 20:11:39 EDT

As insurance premiums rise in disaster-prone areas, a Wall Street financing tool known as a catastrophe bond is becoming more vital for keeping coverage affordable. These bonds allow insurance companies to transfer risk to institutional investors, who receive a return if no major disaster occurs but lose their principal if a specified event, like a major hurricane, triggers a payout. This mechanism provides insurers with a large capital pool to pay claims after a catastrophe. A novel program in North Carolina is even using a portion of investor profits from these bonds to fund home resilience projects, such as fortified roofs, to reduce future losses.
