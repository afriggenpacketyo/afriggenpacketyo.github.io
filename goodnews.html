2025-10-21 07:13:58 EDT

When exposed to a lethal fungus, the ants build more compartmentalized and less interconnected nests, with entrances spaced farther apart. These structural changes create longer travel routes, which reduces interactions and helps insulate healthy colony members from infected individuals. This behavior, termed "architectural immunity," is a sophisticated form of social defense that demonstrates how a species can manipulate its environment to protect the community from epidemics.

Exploring the Theory of 'Enshittification' and the Decline of Internet Platforms

2025-10-21 06:30:00 EDT

Internet activist Cory Doctorow's term "enshittification" describes a three-stage process by which online platforms degrade over time. Initially, platforms attract users with good services, then they pivot to benefit business customers at the users' expense. Finally, once both groups are locked in, the platform squeezes value from everyone, causing the quality of the service to plummet. Doctorow argues this pattern, visible in companies like Amazon and Facebook, is driven by a lack of competition and regulation, which allows platforms to prioritize profits over user experience without fear of losing their customer base.
