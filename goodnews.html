2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

A new study reveals that tiny marine creatures called zooplankton are crucial in combating global warming by locking away vast amounts of carbon. These organisms gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the ocean surface in Antarctica, grow fat, and then sink deep into the ocean to hibernate, effectively sequestering the carbon for centuries. Researchers calculated this process, known as the seasonal vertical migration pump, stores about 65 million tonnes of carbon annually, equivalent to the emissions of 55 million cars. This discovery highlights the underappreciated but vital role these small creatures play in regulating the planet's climate.
