Physiological Changes Explain Diminished Alcohol Tolerance with Age

2025-12-08 05:00:00 EST

As individuals age, their ability to tolerate alcohol often decreases due to distinct physiological changes, leading to more severe hangovers from smaller quantities. Experts explain that liver enzymes responsible for metabolizing alcohol become less efficient over time, and a natural loss of muscle mass reduces the body's water content, which would otherwise dilute alcohol in the bloodstream. For women in perimenopause, alcohol can also exacerbate symptoms like mood swings and sleep disruption. Health professionals advise that this reduced tolerance serves as a natural deterrent, encouraging moderation, which is beneficial as the health risks of alcohol consumption increase with age.
