Chinese Scientists Unlock Genetic Secret for Drought-Tolerant Rice

2025-05-07 07:00:12 EDT

Chinese scientists have discovered genetic secrets in the roots of upland rice that are linked to drought resistance, potentially transforming widely cultivated irrigated rice into drought-tolerant varieties. They have identified a gene, HMGB1, that obstructs the development of deeper, thicker roots in upland rice, which are crucial for drought resistance. By removing this gene, irrigated rice developed longer roots and greater drought tolerance, similar to upland rice, enabling it to thrive in water-scarce environments. The findings, published in Molecular Plant, could significantly improve food security in regions facing water scarcity.
