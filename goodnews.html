Understanding Required Minimum Distributions from Retirement Accounts

2026-05-07 10:50:10 EDT

As individuals approach age 73, they become subject to Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) from traditional retirement accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s. The annual withdrawal amount is calculated based on the account balance and the IRS's life expectancy factor, which increases with age. These RMDs are taxed as ordinary income and can impact tax brackets, Social Security benefits, and Medicare premiums. Failing to take RMDs can result in a significant penalty of up to 25% of the undistributed amount.
