Creditors' claims on estate assets depend on ownership and probate status

2026-07-10 11:33:25 EDT

When settling an estate, creditors generally have the right to claim assets that pass through probate, such as real estate owned solely by the deceased, and bank or investment accounts without designated beneficiaries. Personal property and business interests can also be subject to creditor claims if not otherwise protected. Assets like life insurance proceeds, retirement accounts with beneficiaries, and jointly owned property typically bypass probate and are therefore shielded from estate creditors. Understanding how assets are titled and whether they are subject to probate is crucial for heirs to determine what remains after debts are settled.
