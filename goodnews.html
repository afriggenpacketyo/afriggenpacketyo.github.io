Scientists Identify Bacterial Culprit Behind Devastating Sea Star Wasting Disease

2025-08-04 15:35:49 EDT

Researchers have identified the pathogen responsible for the sea star wasting disease that has killed billions of the creatures along North America's West Coast since 2013. A new study has pinpointed a specific bacterium, *Vibrio pectinocida*, as the cause of the epidemic, which was particularly devastating to the sunflower sea star. The collapse of the sunflower sea star population, a keystone predator, led to an explosion of sea urchins that have decimated vital kelp forests. This discovery is a critical breakthrough, enabling more targeted conservation efforts, including developing diagnostic tests and breeding disease-resistant sea stars.
