Study Shows Plastic Bag Bans and Fees Effectively Reduce Litter

A new study published in Science reveals that state and local plastic bag regulations significantly reduce plastic bag litter along U.S. shorelines. Researchers reviewed over 600 bag policies and 45,000 shoreline cleanups from 2017 to 2023, finding that plastic bag litter decreased by 25% to 47% in areas with bag policies compared to those without. The study highlights the effectiveness of these policies in curbing plastic pollution and its threats to marine environments. The findings suggest that fees on plastic bags may be more effective than bans, particularly partial bans, in limiting shoreline litter. The results offer a positive outlook on environmental conservation efforts and the potential for policy interventions to mitigate plastic pollution.
