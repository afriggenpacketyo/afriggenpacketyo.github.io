Understanding the 7-Year Rule for Credit Card Debt and Its Impact on Credit Reports and Legal Liability

2025-09-30 13:48:22 EDT

The "7-year rule" for credit card debt is often misunderstood but has significant implications for consumers. Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, most negative information, such as unpaid debts and late payments, must be removed from a credit report after seven years from the first delinquency. Separately, each state has a statute of limitations, often around seven years, which limits the time a creditor can sue to collect a debt. However, the debt itself does not disappear and collectors may still attempt to contact you. Making a payment on old debt can sometimes restart the statute of limitations, so understanding these distinctions is crucial for managing long-term debt.
