The Shared Medieval Origins of Germany's St. Martin's Day and Carnival Celebrations

2025-11-10 10:51:00 EST

In Germany, the seemingly unrelated traditions of St. Martin's Day parades and the start of Carnival season both occur on November 11 and share a common historical origin. St. Martin's Day, which honors the charitable 4th-century saint, marked the end of the agricultural year in the Middle Ages and was a time for feasting before a six-week period of fasting for Advent began. The start of Carnival on November 11 at 11:11 a.m. evolved as a final day of exuberant celebration before this period of religious abstinence. The two feasts complement each other, one celebrating charity and light, the other celebrating revelry and life.
