This significant finding pushes back the timeline for controlled fire-making, which was previously dated to around 50,000 years ago in northern France. The controlled use of fire was a landmark in human evolution, profoundly improving diets, providing essential warmth for survival in colder environments, and fostering crucial social evolution around communal campfires, contributing to early language and storytelling.
