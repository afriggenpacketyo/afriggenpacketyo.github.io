2025-10-25 11:52:15 EDT

The Octagon, one of Washington D.C.'s oldest homes, holds a unique place in U.S. history, having served as a temporary residence for President James Madison and First Lady Dolley Madison after the White House was burned by the British in 1814. Designed by the U.S. Capitol's first architect, the home was spared destruction because a French ambassador was staying there, allowing the French flag to fly over it. During their six-month stay, President Madison signed the Treaty of Ghent in the house's circular office, officially ending the War of 1812. The building is now a museum managed by the Architects Foundation.
