Brazil is undertaking a massive public health initiative by breeding and releasing millions of mosquitoes engineered to combat the spread of dengue and other viruses. These "Wolbitos," developed by the nonprofit World Mosquito Program, are infected with Wolbachia bacteria, which prevents the dengue virus from replicating inside them. When these mosquitoes mate with the wild population, they pass the bacteria on, effectively making the local mosquito population harmless to humans. A pilot program in the city of Niterói led to a 90% drop in dengue cases, prompting the Brazilian government to build a new factory to produce billions of the mosquitoes for nationwide deployment.
