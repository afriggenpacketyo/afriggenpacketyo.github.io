Scientists Uncover Genetic Link Between Epstein-Barr Virus and Multiple Sclerosis

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Scientists have made a significant breakthrough in understanding how the ubiquitous Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) contributes to the development of multiple sclerosis (MS) in a small fraction of infected individuals. New research indicates that in people possessing a specific genetic molecule, HLA-DR15, EBV-infected B cells can cunningly manipulate the immune system. This manipulation causes the immune system to mistakenly attack myelin proteins, which are crucial insulating layers around nerve fibers, leading to MS. While HLA-DR15 is identified as the most important genetic risk factor, other elements like diet, vitamin D deficiency, and the timing of initial EBV infection also play contributing roles, with early childhood vaccination against EBV being explored as a preventative measure.
