CDC Study Suggests Pepto Bismol May Not Prevent Traveler's Diarrhea

2025-06-03 11:35:00 EDT

A recent CDC study has found that bismuth subsalicylate, the active ingredient in Pepto Bismol, may not be effective in preventing traveler's diarrhea. The study, which involved 270 participants traveling to regions in Asia and Africa, found no significant difference in diarrhea symptoms between those who took Pepto Bismol and those who took a placebo. While bismuth subsalicylate has been approved for treating diarrhea since 1939, its off-label use for prevention may not be as beneficial as previously thought. The authors suggest that further research is needed to determine if certain traveler groups or circumstances might benefit from the medication, even though the current study did not reach its target sample size.
