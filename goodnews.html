Experts Explain the Science Behind 'Old People Smell'

The phenomenon often described as "old people smell" is a real biological process, not just a stigma, according to experts. The distinct musty or greasy scent is largely attributed to the compound 2-nonenal, which is produced as lipids on the skin break down and oxidize with age. This compound is not easily removed by regular washing due to its fatty composition. Experts suggest that managing this age-related odor involves a holistic approach, including an antioxidant-rich diet, proper hydration, and antioxidant skincare, while also noting that sudden changes in body odor could indicate underlying health issues.
