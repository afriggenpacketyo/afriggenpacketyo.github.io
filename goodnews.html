Despite Widespread Inflation, Computer Prices Have Continued to Fall Due to Moore's Law

2025-12-04 05:00:00 EST

In an era of rising costs for most goods, the price of computers and many electronics has consistently decreased, a trend largely attributed to Moore's Law. This principle, observed by Intel co-founder Gordon Moore, posits that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles approximately every two years, making computing power exponentially cheaper and more powerful over time. However, this long-standing trend may be nearing its end as the physical limits of miniaturization are approached. Experts suggest future cost and efficiency gains will need to come from other innovations, such as improved software and advanced chip packaging techniques.
