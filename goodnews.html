CDC Study Finds Pepto Bismol Ineffective in Preventing Travelers' Diarrhea

2025-06-03 11:35:00 EDT

A recent study by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) has found that bismuth subsalicylate, the active ingredient in Pepto Bismol, is not effective in preventing travelers' diarrhea compared to a placebo. The study, involving 270 participants traveling to Southeast Asia, south central Asia, North Africa, or sub-Saharan Africa, found no significant difference in symptoms of loose stool or diarrhea between those who took Pepto Bismol preventatively and those who took a placebo. While bismuth subsalicylate is FDA-approved for treating diarrhea, heartburn, indigestion, nausea, and upset stomach, its off-label use for prevention, especially among international travelers, has been questioned by this new data. Further studies are needed to determine if there is a benefit in certain traveler groups or under specific circumstances.
