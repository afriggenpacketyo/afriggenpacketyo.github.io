Jane Goodall, Revolutionary Primatologist and Conservationist, Dies at 91

2025-10-01 14:08:15 EDT

Jane Goodall, the pioneering naturalist whose groundbreaking work with chimpanzees transformed human understanding of the species, has died at 91. Goodall, a protégé of Louis S.B. Leakey, made history in 1960 by discovering that chimpanzees made and used tools, a trait previously thought exclusive to humans. Her meticulous observations at Tanzania’s Gombe Stream National Park revealed complex chimp behaviors, including emotions, hunting, and even warfare. Goodall's legacy extends to tireless advocacy for conservation and inspiring women in STEM, through the Jane Goodall Institute and its youth programs.
