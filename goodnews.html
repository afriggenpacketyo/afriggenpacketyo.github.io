The Historical Roots of Turkey as the Centerpiece of Thanksgiving

2025-11-26 06:45:00 EST

Contrary to popular belief, historical accounts of the first Thanksgiving do not mention turkey; the only confirmed food was venison. The tradition of eating turkey became widespread much later, largely due to the bird being an inexpensive and abundant option for a festive meal. The modern Thanksgiving holiday was established during the Civil War, promoted by writer Sarah Hale as a day for national unity, with President Lincoln signing it into law in 1863. The industrialization of food production and marketing campaigns by companies further cemented turkey, along with side dishes like pumpkin pie and cranberry sauce, as staples of the celebration.
