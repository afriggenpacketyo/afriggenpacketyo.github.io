Earl Richardson, Educator Who Championed Funding Equity for HBCUs, Dies at 81

2025-09-13 17:21:45 EDT

Earl Richardson, the former president of Morgan State University, has passed away at the age of 81. He was a pivotal figure in a 15-year lawsuit against the state of Maryland that resulted in a historic $577 million settlement for four of the state's historically Black colleges and universities (HBCUs). The lawsuit argued that Maryland had systematically underfunded its Black colleges while developing similar programs at nearby predominantly white institutions. Richardson's relentless advocacy brought national attention to funding disparities for HBCUs and led to a period of significant growth and renewal at Morgan State, often called "Richardson's Renaissance."
