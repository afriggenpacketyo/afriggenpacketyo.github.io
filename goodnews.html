Scientists Uncover Fundamental Sound Motifs Underlying Birds' Diverse Songs

2026-07-23 19:55:29 EDT

French scientists have identified eight fundamental sound "motifs" that form the basis of the spectacular diversity in birdsong worldwide. Analyzing over 100,000 songs from 3,000 species, researchers found these motifs — including trills, whistles, chaotic notes, and harmonies — combine to create unique compositions. A key finding reveals that a bird's environment, such as tropical or temperate forests, influences how these building blocks are used. Simpler motifs are common in dense tropical areas for better transmission, while complex songs appear in temperate regions for closer communication.
