This find, predating Roger Payne's discovery of whale song by nearly two decades, offers invaluable insights into the significantly quieter ocean soundscape of the past. Scientists hope this historical audio will aid in protecting vulnerable whale populations, as sound-making is crucial for their survival, socialization, and navigation, and the recording is hailed as a beautiful and inspiring window into ocean life.
