Montreux Convention Limits NATO's Naval Presence in the Black Sea

2026-07-20 10:10:00 EDT

The 90-year-old Montreux Convention significantly restricts NATO's naval operations in the Black Sea, making Turkey a powerful gatekeeper for access through the Dardanelles and Bosporus straits. This treaty, established in 1936, allows Black Sea states relatively free passage for warships but imposes strict fleet size, tonnage, and duration limits on non-Black Sea navies during peacetime. During wartime, if Turkey is neutral, belligerent nations' warships are largely prohibited from passage, a clause Turkey invoked during the Ukraine war to prevent Russia from reinforcing its fleet. The convention's enduring relevance, despite its archaic language, is maintained due to concerns that renegotiation could destabilize regional security and open a "Pandora's box" of issues.

When a borrower dies, the executor or court-appointed administrator of their estate is typically responsible for notifying creditors and managing outstanding debts during the probate process. This involves identifying all debts, providing a death certificate, and validating claims against the estate, which are usually paid from estate assets before distribution to heirs. While family members may inform lenders, they are generally not personally liable for the deceased's debts unless they were a co-signer, joint account holder, or spouse in certain states.
