Record Puffin Population Count on Skomer Island for Second Consecutive Year

2026-04-22 06:07:03 EDT

Skomer Island, off the coast of Pembrokeshire, has recorded its highest puffin count for the second year in a row, with 52,019 puffins documented this year, an increase of 8,000 from the previous year. This news comes as a "very pleasant surprise" for the Wildlife Trust of South and West Wales (WTSWW), especially following a tragic number of seabird deaths across southern Europe earlier in the year. Skomer Island is an internationally important seabird habitat, also home to significant populations of Manx shearwaters, guillemots, and razorbills. The long-term monitoring of puffin populations is crucial for understanding seabird population changes and identifying threats like pollution and bird flu. The thriving puffin numbers on Skomer contrast with declines seen at many other UK sites, highlighting the island's role as a vital refuge.
