The Aeronautical Reason for Aircraft Carrier Superstructures Being on the Right Side

2025-10-06 12:00:00 EDT

The superstructure, or "island," on an aircraft carrier is almost always located on the starboard (right) side of the flight deck for a critical aerodynamic reason related to propeller-driven aircraft. The clockwise rotation of most Western aircraft engines creates a natural tendency for planes to yaw to the left upon takeoff and landing due to phenomena like the P-factor and spiraling slipstream. Placing the island on the right side provides pilots with a clear visual reference for landings without obstructing the natural leftward drift of the aircraft. This design choice, established early in carrier history, has become a standard for naval aviation.
