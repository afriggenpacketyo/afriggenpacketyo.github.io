Skeleton in Magdeburg Cathedral Identified as Otto the Great

2026-03-18 14:19:00 EDT

A skeleton discovered in a simple wooden coffin within Magdeburg Cathedral's sarcophagus has been identified with "probability bordering on certainty" as Otto I, also known as Otto the Great, the 10th-century East Frankish king and Holy Roman Emperor. Analysis revealed the remains belonged to a well-fed, active man who died around 60, consistent with historical records, and showed signs of past injuries indicating combat experience and regular horse riding. Genetic evidence further supported the identification, linking the skeleton to a blood relative, Heinrich II, the last Holy Roman Emperor of the Ottonian dynasty. This significant archaeological finding provides direct insight into one of Europe's most influential historical figures, whose victory at the Battle of Lechfeld and imperial coronation shaped medieval Europe.

Rhinos Return to Uganda's Kidepo Valley National Park After 40-Year Absence

2026-03-18 13:36:05 EDT

Rhinos have returned to Uganda's Kidepo Valley National Park for the first time in over four decades, following their extinction in the wild due to rampant poaching. Two southern white rhinos were introduced as the first of eight animals planned to re-establish the population, with the last rhino in the park having been killed in 1983. The Uganda Wildlife Authority (UWA) stated that this period saw hunters wipe out around 700 rhinos across Uganda's national parks. This reintroduction initiative, guided by habitat suitability and security studies, includes a secure rhino sanctuary outfitted with perimeter fencing, ranger facilities, and monitoring technology, marking a significant step in restoring the park's natural heritage and combating the ongoing challenge of poaching.
