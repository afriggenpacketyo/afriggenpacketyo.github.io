The Value of Boredom for Mental Well-being and Purpose

2026-03-22 10:15:25 EDT

Author Arthur C. Brooks argues that the modern habit of constantly resolving boredom with digital devices has inadvertently diminished our capacity for abstract thinking and self-reflection. He explains that boredom activates the brain's 'Default Mode Network,' crucial for mind-wandering and contemplating life's larger questions. Over-reliance on phones prevents this network from engaging, potentially leading to feelings of emptiness, anxiety, and depression. Brooks suggests simple rules, like phone-free walks or meals, to reintroduce silent spaces, allowing the brain to function as designed and fostering a richer sense of purpose and peace. This article advocates for embracing boredom as a pathway to deeper personal meaning.
