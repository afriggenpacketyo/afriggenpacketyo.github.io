Ancient Roman Artworks Unearthed in London Demolition Site

2025-06-20 05:02:00 EDT

Archaeologists in London have discovered a large collection of painted Roman wall plaster at "The Liberty" development site, offering insights into ancient Londinium. The fragments, discarded during Roman-era demolition before A.D. 200, have been meticulously reassembled by the Museum of London Archaeology (MOLA). The artworks feature bright yellow and black panel designs adorned with birds, fruit, flowers, and lyres, with influences from other parts of the Roman Empire. The find includes what appears to be the first known example of a painter's signature in Roman Britain and ancient graffiti, including a complete Greek alphabet. The research into each plaster piece is ongoing, with plans for eventual public display.
