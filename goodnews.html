2026-02-23 02:06:38 EST

Chinese and Australian researchers have successfully engineered a gene-edited tomato that surprisingly emits the sweet scent of buttered popcorn. This innovative development was achieved by simultaneously silencing two specific genes responsible for suppressing aromatic compounds within the tomato. The research team, led by Xu Shengchun of Xianghu Laboratory, drew inspiration from the popular aroma of fragrant rice rather than focusing on traditional tomato flavors. This unique creation represents a novel approach in agricultural biotechnology, potentially influencing future consumer preferences and the development of new food products with distinct sensory profiles.

Magnitude 3.5 Earthquake Occurs Off Catalina Island Near Los Angeles

2026-02-23 01:59:22 EST

A magnitude 3.5 earthquake was reported late Sunday night, occurring approximately 13 miles off Catalina Island, near Rancho Palos Verdes in the Greater Los Angeles area. The tremor, which took place at a depth of 6.8 miles, is the only earthquake of magnitude 3.0 or greater recorded in the immediate vicinity over the past ten days. On average, the Greater Los Angeles area experiences about five earthquakes with magnitudes between 3.0 and 4.0 annually. The U.S. Geological Survey encourages residents who felt the event to report their experiences, contributing to ongoing seismic monitoring and preparedness efforts.

Remains of Saint Francis of Assisi Displayed Publicly for First Time in Centuries

2026-02-22 21:42:00 EST

The remains of Saint Francis of Assisi are currently on public display in his namesake basilica in Assisi, Italy, marking the first full public viewing in nearly 800 years. The stone sarcophagus was ceremoniously transferred from the crypt to the lower church for a month-long exhibition, expected to draw nearly 400,000 visitors from around the world. Born around 1181, Saint Francis founded the Franciscan Order, dedicating his life to aiding the poor. This rare public display commemorates the 800th anniversary of his death, with October 4, his feast day, reinstated as a public holiday in Italy.
