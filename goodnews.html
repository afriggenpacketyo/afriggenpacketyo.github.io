New California Law Standardizes Food Date Labels to Reduce Consumer Confusion and Waste

2026-06-26 06:00:00 EDT

A new California law, Assembly Bill 660, will standardize food date labels to just two phrases: "BEST if Used by" (for quality) and "USE by" (for safety). This aims to eliminate consumer confusion caused by over 50 different labels, which currently leads to significant unintentional food waste. Californians discard 2.5 billion meals' worth of unspoiled food annually, contributing to methane emissions. The law, effective July 1, prohibits "sell by" dates and seeks to empower consumers to make better economic and safety decisions, though enforcement and public education remain key challenges.
