Experts Advise Against Daily Electrolyte Drinks for Most Exercise

2026-01-01 19:48:12 EST

Experts, including Professor Graeme Close from Liverpool John Moores University, advise that most people do not need electrolyte drinks for everyday exercise, as the body naturally maintains stable electrolyte levels through a regular diet. While these minerals are crucial for nerves, muscles, and fluid balance, sufficient amounts are typically obtained from food. Electrolyte supplements are only genuinely beneficial for long, intense periods of exercise, particularly in hot conditions, where significant sweating occurs. For moderate activity, plain water is sufficient, and expensive products are unnecessary. A simple homemade solution of water, fruit juice, and a pinch of salt can serve as an effective alternative for those specific situations.
