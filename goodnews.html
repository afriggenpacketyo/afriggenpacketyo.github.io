The Peak District's Evolution from Ancient Wonder to National Park, Shaped by Access Rights and Railways

2026-04-18 19:36:46 EDT

The Peak District, the UK's first national park, celebrates 75 years, with its history as a tourist destination stretching back centuries. Early attractions included the "Devil's Arse" cave, later renamed due to Victorian sensibilities, which still draws visitors with its unique acoustics during floods. The arrival of railways in the 19th century democratized access for mass tourism, but private land ownership limited public roaming. The 1932 Kinder Scout mass trespass was a pivotal moment in the fight for access rights, contributing to the 1949 National Parks Act and the eventual creation of the Countryside and Rights of Way Act in 2000.
