A meteorite identified as the largest piece of the planet Mars on Earth has been sold for nearly $5.3 million at a Sotheby's auction in New York. The meteorite, named NWA 16788, weighs over 54 pounds and represents about 6.5% of all known Martian material on our planet. It is believed to have been dislodged from Mars by an asteroid strike before traveling 140 million miles and crashing in the Sahara Desert, where it was discovered in 2023. Martian meteorites are exceptionally rare, constituting only about 0.6% of all meteorites found on Earth, making this a significant acquisition for the unidentified buyer.
