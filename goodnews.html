TSA PreCheck Introduces Touchless ID for Faster Airport Security Amid Growing Wait Times.

2026-03-18 20:42:08 EDT

As security lines at U.S. airports continue to lengthen, the Transportation Security Administration (TSA) is promoting its PreCheck Touchless ID program, a biometric verification process designed to expedite identity checks. This system, which uses facial identification technology, promises "faster, more efficient identity verification" and provides dedicated, typically shorter, security lanes at the 65 participating airports. To be eligible for enrollment, travelers must already possess TSA PreCheck status and maintain an active frequent flyer profile with one of the five participating airlines—Alaska, American, Delta, Southwest, or United—having uploaded valid passport information to their profile. Each airline offers a slightly different, straightforward opt-in process to utilize this time-saving technology.

2026-03-18 20:04:46 EDT

The UK government has unveiled ambitious plans to significantly boost its domestic steel production, setting a target for the country to manufacture half of the steel it consumes, an increase from the current 30%. To achieve this, new measures include substantially reducing imported steel quotas by 60% from current arrangements, with any steel brought in above these lowered limits subject to a new 50% tariff. Business Secretary Peter Kyle presented these initiatives as a defense against anti-competitive behavior from abroad, rather than protectionism. The UK steel industry, which has faced severe financial difficulties due to high energy prices and global oversupply, welcomed the measures. Critics, however, warn that these tariffs could increase costs for manufacturers and ultimately for UK consumers, potentially impacting the wider economy.
