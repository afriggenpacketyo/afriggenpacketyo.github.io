Key exceptions include situations where individuals have co-signed a loan, are joint account holders, or reside in community property states. Mortgages on inherited real estate are also distinct, requiring heirs to either assume the payments or sell the asset to cover the loan. The article advises that if an estate proves insolvent, debts may ultimately be written off, and beneficiaries are generally not held personally responsible.
