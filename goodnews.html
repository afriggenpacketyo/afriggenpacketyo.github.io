Scientists Discover "Jumping Genes" in Junk DNA Act as Genetic Switches

2025-07-26 11:15:10 EDT

An international research team has discovered that so-called "junk" DNA, which constitutes nearly half of the human genome, plays a crucial role in gene regulation. The study, published in Science Advances, focused on a family of "jumping genes" called MER11, which are ancient retroviruses that integrated into our ancestors' DNA millions of years ago. Researchers found that these sequences can act like "genetic switches," influencing which genes are turned on or off without altering the underlying DNA code. This finding challenges the notion that these elements are non-functional and suggests they have evolved to perform important regulatory functions.
