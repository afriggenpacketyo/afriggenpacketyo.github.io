NY Fed President Suggests Technical Factors Skewed November CPI Data

2025-12-19 10:47:07 EST

New York Federal Reserve President John Williams stated that November's Consumer Price Index (CPI) reading was likely distorted downward by specific "technical factors" related to data collection. He explained that the Bureau of Labor Statistics faced challenges in gathering data for October and early November, leading to data collection primarily in the latter half of November, a period characterized by widespread sales. This timing, Williams noted, probably pushed the CPI reading lower by about a tenth of a percentage point. Despite these identified distortions, Williams found some encouraging signs of cooling price pressures in other economic sectors that were unaffected, suggesting a continuation of the disinflationary process. The full impact of these technical factors is anticipated to be more accurately assessed with the release of December's data.
