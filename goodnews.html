Distinguishing Between Normal Aging and Undiagnosed Adult ADHD in Midlife

2026-02-25 13:58:44 EST

As the U.S. aging population experiences cognitive changes, many are questioning whether symptoms like forgetfulness and difficulty concentrating are normal aging or undiagnosed Adult ADHD. Historically associated with childhood, ADHD is now recognized in approximately 3% of people over 50, with diagnoses rising in previously overlooked populations. Diagnosing ADHD in midlife is complex, as its symptoms can overlap with normal brain shrinkage, psychiatric conditions like depression, or early-onset dementia. Increased awareness and upcoming clinical guidelines for adult ADHD are crucial, as a midlife diagnosis and treatment, even with stimulants, can significantly improve quality of life and potentially impact future brain health.
