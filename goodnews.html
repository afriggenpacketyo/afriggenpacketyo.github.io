Exploring the Sacred Traditions and Rituals of Jewish Funerals

2025-10-15 10:07:00 EDT

Jewish mourning rituals primarily focus on eternal memory among the living, with less emphasis on an afterlife. The article explains that burial, a mitzvah, must occur as quickly as possible, though not on the Sabbath or major holidays. Practices include a ritual bath (Taharah), burial in a simple wooden casket or shroud without worldly belongings, and no public viewing. Mourners observe K'riah, tearing clothing as an expression of pain, and a seven-day shiva period of intense mourning, refraining from work and grooming. The Mourner's Kaddish, an ancient Aramaic prayer, is recited communally to exalt God and honor the deceased's memory.
