The system uses a helicopter-towed array to generate magnetic pulses, which then induce eddy currents in conductive materials. By analyzing the resulting secondary magnetic field, scientists can identify submerged objects and their depth. The research, published in a Chinese journal, addresses significant engineering challenges in stabilizing such airborne systems for geophysical surveys.
