Chinese Scientists Directly Observe Quantum Effect Crucial for Dark Matter Detection

2026-01-16 06:00:11 EST

Chinese scientists have made a significant breakthrough by directly observing the Migdal effect, a quantum phenomenon theorized almost 90 years ago. This effect describes the secondary electronic recoil produced when a neutral particle, such as dark matter, collides with an atomic nucleus. Published in the peer-reviewed journal Nature, this first-ever direct observation was achieved using advanced equipment by a team led by the University of the Chinese Academy of Sciences. This discovery is crucial as it provides a potential new method for detecting dark matter, an invisible component that holds the universe together and remains one of physics' most profound unsolved mysteries.
