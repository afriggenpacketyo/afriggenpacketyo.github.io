Civil Rights Pioneer Claudette Colvin Dies at 86

2026-01-14 01:29:00 EST

Claudette Colvin, a pivotal figure in the US civil rights movement, has died at the age of 86. At just 15, she bravely defied segregation laws in Montgomery, Alabama, on March 2, 1955, by refusing to surrender her bus seat to a white woman, an act that preceded Rosa Parks' more widely publicized protest. Her arrest led her to become one of four plaintiffs in a successful lawsuit that challenged the legality of bus segregation, profoundly impacting public transportation across the United States. Although initially overshadowed and ostracized, Colvin gained significant recognition later in life for her enduring legacy of courage and her instrumental role in changing American history.
