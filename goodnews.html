New York City to Use Ranked Choice Voting in Mayoral Primary

2025-06-23 00:04:00 EDT

New York City will utilize ranked choice voting in its upcoming Democratic mayoral primary election. Voters will rank up to five candidates in order of preference, and if no candidate secures a majority, the candidate with the fewest votes will be eliminated, and their votes redistributed based on voters' second choices. This process continues until a candidate reaches a majority. The system aims to ensure that the elected candidate has broad support and to prevent candidates with fringe views from winning in a crowded field. The tabulation process will begin on July 1, with official results certified on July 15.
