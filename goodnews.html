Chinese Scientists Observe Quantum Friction Effect for the First Time

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team in China has become the first to directly observe and measure quantum friction between two solid surfaces. The scientists at the Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics made the discovery while studying folded graphene, a single-atom-thick layer of carbon. They observed that friction did not always increase with thickness and that some thicker folds slid more easily, a counterintuitive effect traced to how bending altered electron movement. This breakthrough could pave the way for developing ultra-efficient, low-power nanodevices with minimal wear and tear, revolutionizing tiny machines.
