Scientific Explainer Details the Mechanics of Tsunami Formation

2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

Following the major earthquake off Russia's coast, this article explains the science behind tsunamis. These powerful waves are typically generated by large, shallow underwater earthquakes, particularly those with strong vertical motion that displaces the seafloor. The recent 8.8-magnitude quake possessed all these characteristics, warranting immediate Pacific-wide warnings. Tsunamis travel at jet-plane speeds in the deep ocean but slow down and build in height as they approach shallower coastal waters, where they become most dangerous. While forecasting remains challenging, scientists use a network of tide gauges and deep-ocean sensors to better predict their arrival and potential impact.
