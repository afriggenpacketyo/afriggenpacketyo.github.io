Film 'Pressure' Highlights Critical Role of WWII Meteorologists in D-Day Planning

2026-05-27 06:00:00 EDT

The film 'Pressure' dramatizes the pivotal role of meteorologists in the D-Day invasion, showcasing the shift from outdated trend-based forecasting to modern, real-time atmospheric measurements. Starring Andrew Scott as James Stagg and Brendan Fraser as Gen. Dwight D. Eisenhower, the movie emphasizes the immense pressure on meteorologists to provide accurate forecasts. It illustrates how European scientists' reliance on upper-atmosphere data correctly predicted a storm, leading to the invasion's postponement, a contrast to the American method that focused on past patterns. The film underscores how advancements in weather science were crucial for Allied success in World War II.
