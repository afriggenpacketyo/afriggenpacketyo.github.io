Premier League Goal Disallowed Due to Attacking Player's Proximity to Defensive Wall

2025-08-17 10:06:13 EDT

A free-kick goal scored by Crystal Palace's Eberechi Eze against Chelsea was disallowed following a VAR review due to a lesser-known rule infringement. The decision was made because a Crystal Palace player was standing less than one meter away from the Chelsea defensive wall when the kick was taken. According to the International Football Association Board's Law 13, which was introduced in 2019, all attacking players must maintain at least a one-meter distance from a wall formed by three or more defenders. The rule was implemented to prevent time-wasting caused by attackers positioning themselves within the wall.
