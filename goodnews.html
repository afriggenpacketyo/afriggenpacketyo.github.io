NASA Concludes MAVEN Mars Orbiter Mission After Communications Loss

2026-06-04 08:27:35 EDT

NASA has officially concluded the mission of its MAVEN (Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution Mission) orbiter, after losing contact with the spacecraft six months ago during a routine occultation behind Mars. Launched in 2013, the $582 million probe exceeded its planned one-year mission, providing unprecedented insights into how the Martian atmosphere has been eroded by solar wind over billions of years, a key factor in understanding Mars's transition from a potentially habitable world. Although efforts to restart the spacecraft's computer were unsuccessful, MAVEN's legacy includes major discoveries like the "sputtering" escape process and its role in relaying data from Mars rovers.
