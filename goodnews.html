2025-08-22 18:28:30 EDT

Archaeologists and divers have recovered ancient artifacts, including a sphinx statue and Roman coins, from Abu Qir Bay near Alexandria, Egypt. These relics are believed to be from a sunken city, possibly an extension of the ancient city of Canopus, dating back over 2,000 years to the Ptolemaic and Roman eras. Many statues are notably missing their heads and feet, a type of damage attributed by officials to a natural disaster such as a tsunami or earthquake. The discoveries confirm the site was once a thriving residential city and port, also underscoring Alexandria's vulnerability to rising sea levels, which are projected to render one-third of the city uninhabitable by 2050.
