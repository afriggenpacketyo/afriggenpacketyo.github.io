Jesse Jackson's Vision Shaped Modern Democratic Party

2026-02-21 07:00:07 EST

This article explores how Reverend Jesse Jackson's "radically inclusive" vision profoundly shaped the modern Democratic party, following his death on February 17. Through his National Rainbow Coalition, launched after his 1984 presidential campaign, Jackson advocated for a progressive agenda that brought marginalized groups to the forefront. His efforts revitalized Black voter participation, particularly in the South, and he was an early and vocal supporter of gay rights, becoming the first to use "lesbian" and "gay" in a Democratic convention speech.
