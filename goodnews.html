Space Telescope Captures Warped View Through an Einstein Ring

Day 7 of the 2025 Space Telescope Advent Calendar reveals a stunning image of two galaxies, showcasing a rare astronomical phenomenon known as an Einstein Ring. The photograph captures a distant spiral galaxy appearing significantly warped and distorted. This distortion is caused by gravitational lensing, where the immense gravity of a massive, much closer galaxy, part of the SMACSJ0028.2-7537 cluster, bends the light from the background galaxy. The resulting circular shape of the distant galaxy's image is precisely what Albert Einstein predicted in 1912. This visual marvel offers a unique glimpse into the profound effects of gravity on the fabric of spacetime.

Chinese Desalination Plant Produces Fresh Water Cheaper Than Tap Water and Green Hydrogen

2025-12-07 11:00:11 EST

A groundbreaking Chinese desalination plant in Rizhao has achieved a world-first by producing fresh water at a cost lower than tap water, alongside green hydrogen. This innovative facility operates entirely on seawater and low-grade waste heat from nearby industrial plants, eliminating the need for costly desalination or freshwater resources. Annually, it processes 800 tonnes of seawater to yield 450 cubic meters of ultra-pure fresh water, 192,000 standard cubic meters of green hydrogen, and 350 tonnes of mineral-rich brine. This "one-in, three-out" circular economy model provides enough hydrogen to power 100 buses for thousands of kilometers, marking a significant advance in sustainable resource management and clean energy production.
