Bobby Whitlock, Co-Founder of Derek and the Dominos, Dies at 77

2025-08-10 15:26:42 EDT

Bobby Whitlock, the soulful keyboardist, singer, and songwriter who co-founded the legendary blues-rock band Derek and the Dominos with Eric Clapton, has passed away at the age of 77. Whitlock was a crucial contributor to the band's iconic 1970 album, "Layla and Other Assorted Love Songs," co-writing half of its tracks, including the classic "Bell Bottom Blues." His career began at Stax Records and flourished when he joined Delaney & Bonnie and Friends, where he connected with Clapton and George Harrison. An influential session musician who played on albums by the Rolling Stones and Dr. John, Whitlock continued to release solo material and collaborate with his wife, CoCo Carmel, throughout his life.
