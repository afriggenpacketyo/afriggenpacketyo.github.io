Debate Grows Over Decades-Old '85th Percentile Rule' for Setting US Speed Limits

2025-08-17 00:00:53 EDT

A long-standing traffic engineering guideline known as the '85th percentile rule' is facing increased scrutiny across the United States. The rule suggests that speed limits should be set based on the speed that 85% of drivers naturally travel, under the assumption that this is the safest pace. However, safety advocates argue this creates a dangerous feedback loop where speeding drivers lead to higher speed limits, especially in urban areas with pedestrians. In response to rising road fatalities, some states and the Federal Highway Administration are beginning to modify guidelines to consider factors beyond driver speed, such as road use and crash frequency.
