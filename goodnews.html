2026-04-28 01:20:05 EDT

Archaeologists at the ancient Roman site of Pompeii have successfully used artificial intelligence for the first time to digitally reconstruct the face of a man killed during the AD 79 eruption of Mount Vesuvius. His remains were discovered as he attempted to flee the city towards the coast, having died early in the disaster from heavy volcanic debris. The reconstruction, developed in collaboration with the University of Padua, depicts him using a terracotta mortar as a shield, carrying an oil lamp, a small iron ring, and ten bronze coins. This innovative project aims to offer new ways to understand the catastrophe, making archaeological research more accessible and emotionally engaging for the public while upholding scientific rigor.
