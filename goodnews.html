2025-07-31 21:07:58 EDT

A new study published in the journal Cell has uncovered the surprising evolutionary origin of the potato, tracing it back to a tomato ancestor. Researchers discovered that the modern potato emerged from a hybridization event around nine million years ago between a non-tuber-producing wild potato species and an ancient tomato. This interbreeding provided the crucial genes necessary for the potato to develop its characteristic starchy tubers, solving a long-standing botanical puzzle. The findings, based on the analysis of over 500 potato genomes, demonstrate how hybridization between different species can lead to the evolution of entirely new traits and the emergence of new plant varieties.
