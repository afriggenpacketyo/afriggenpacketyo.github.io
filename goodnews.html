US and India Announce Framework for Interim Trade Agreement to Lower Tariffs

2026-02-07 01:50:27 EST

India and the United States have unveiled a framework for an interim trade agreement aimed at reducing tariffs on Indian goods. This development follows President Trump's earlier plan to cut import tariffs, specifically after India agreed to cease purchasing Russian oil. The deal, described as "reciprocal and mutually beneficial," intends to lower tariffs on Indian goods to 18% from 25%, while India will reduce or eliminate tariffs on various U.S. industrial, food, and agricultural products. Indian opposition parties have criticized the agreement, arguing it disproportionately favors the U.S. and could harm sensitive domestic sectors, though India's Trade Minister Piyush Goyal asserts it protects these areas and will open significant market opportunities.
