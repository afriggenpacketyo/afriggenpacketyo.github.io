Generally, surviving spouses are not automatically liable for these debts, as they typically become part of the deceased's estate. However, exceptions exist, such as if the spouse signed as a guarantor, used a joint credit card, or resides in a community property state where debts incurred during marriage are shared. The article advises requesting debt validation and exploring options like negotiation or debt relief if liability is confirmed.
