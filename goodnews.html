Fossil Find in China Suggests Some Pterosaurs Were Herbivores

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

The discovery of a pterosaur fossil in northeastern China is challenging long-held beliefs about the diet of these ancient flying reptiles. The fossil, a species named Sinopterus atavismus, contained microscopic plant mineral deposits called phytoliths in its stomach. This finding provides the first direct evidence of stomach contents for this type of pterosaur and strongly suggests it was a herbivore. The fossil also contained small quartz crystals, likely gastroliths, which are stones swallowed by many modern animals to help grind tough plant matter, further supporting the theory of a plant-based diet for this creature.

Astronomers Observe Earliest Signs of Rocky Planet Formation Around Sun-Like Star

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb Space Telescope, astronomers have for the first time captured the earliest known evidence of rocky planet formation around a very young, sun-like star named HOPS-315. Located about 1,370 light-years away, the infant star's surrounding gas disk showed signs of silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing. These are the fundamental building blocks believed to have formed Earth and other rocky planets in our own solar system. This groundbreaking observation provides a direct glimpse into the initial stages of planet creation, suggesting that the process that formed Earth may be common throughout the universe.
