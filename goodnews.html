Authorized Users Generally Not Liable for Unpaid Credit Card Debt

2026-07-20 11:01:05 EDT

Authorized users on credit card accounts are generally not legally liable for unpaid debt, even if they made purchases, as they do not have a contractual agreement with the card issuer. The primary account holder, who applied for and signed the agreement, bears the sole legal responsibility for repayment. While debt collectors might contact authorized users, they typically cannot sue them directly for the balance. Joint account holders, conversely, share full legal responsibility for the debt. For primary cardholders facing financial hardship, exploring options such as debt settlement, debt management plans, or consolidation loans early can help prevent the situation from escalating to collections or litigation.
