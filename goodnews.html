The discovery provides new insights into animal life during the Cambrian period. The creature, about the length of an index finger, had a circular mouth lined with teeth and claws extending from its head. Researchers noted that the Mosura's body included 16 segments lined with gills, similar to modern arthropods, and had an open circulatory system. The discovery of the Mosura gives scientists better insight into how diverse life on Earth was even prior to the Cambrian explosion.
