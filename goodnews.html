World's Largest Whale Graveyard Discovered in Indian Ocean, Teeming with Deep-Sea Life

2026-06-10 15:00:00 EDT

Chinese scientists have discovered the world's largest and oldest whale graveyard in the Indian Ocean, with some fossils dating back 5.3 million years. The extensive site, spanning 1,200 kilometers and reaching depths of 7,000 meters, contains nearly 500 skeletons and supports vibrant ecosystems of deep-sea creatures, some believed to be new to science. Researchers were astonished by the scale and age of the discovery, which they attribute to the area being a popular foraging habitat with a trench that funnels carcasses to the seafloor. This "whale fall" phenomenon provides a significant food source and may act as evolutionary hotspots and biogeographic stepping stones for deep-sea fauna.

Vast Whale Graveyard Dating Back 5.3 Million Years Discovered in Indian Ocean

2026-06-10 14:24:09 EDT

An extensive whale graveyard, approximately 1,200 kilometers long and located 7 kilometers deep in the Diamantina fracture zone of the Indian Ocean, has been discovered by researchers. Some of the remains date back 5.3 million years, making it a significant paleontological find. The underwater necropolis, teeming with organisms, some potentially new to science, includes fossils of an extinct beaked whale, Pterocetus benguelae, and a newly identified species, Pterocetus diamantinae. The discovery highlights the role of whale falls in supporting deep-sea ecosystems and offers insights into whale evolution and distribution over geological time.
