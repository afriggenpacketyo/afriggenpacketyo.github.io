2025-09-07 19:13:39 EDT

Stargazers across Asia, significant portions of Europe, and Africa were treated to the captivating sight of a "Blood Moon" during a total lunar eclipse on Sunday night. This celestial event, where the sun, Earth, and moon perfectly align, causes Earth's shadow to envelop its satellite, giving it an eerie, deep red coloration. Astrophysicists explain that this red appearance is a result of sunlight being reflected and scattered through Earth's atmosphere, with shorter blue wavelengths dispersing more readily than longer red ones. While Asia, including India and China, offered the prime viewing locations, observers in Europe and Africa also caught a glimpse of a partial eclipse during the early evening moonrise.
