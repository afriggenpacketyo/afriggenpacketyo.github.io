New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species Discovered in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, identified from fossils in Kalasin Province. This long-necked sauropod, estimated to be 20 meters long and to have lived approximately 150 million years ago, belongs to the Mamenchisauridae family. Its unique air-cavity structure in a dorsal vertebra distinguishes it from other known dinosaurs. This significant finding marks the first discovery of its kind in Thailand, expanding the known geographical range for this dinosaur family, which was previously mostly found in China, and was published in the prestigious Nature scientific journal.
