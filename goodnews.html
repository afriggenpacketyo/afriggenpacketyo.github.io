Physicists Explore Fluid Dynamics to Determine Optimal Liquid Drainage Times

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Two Brown University physicists, Thomas Dutta and Jay Tang, investigated the optimal waiting time for liquids to drain from containers, inspired by everyday kitchen observations. Their research, published in Physics of Fluids, utilized Navier-Stokes equations and practical experiments to measure drainage rates for various liquids like milk, olive oil, and maple syrup. The findings revealed that low-viscosity liquids like water drain in seconds and milk in about 30 seconds, while thicker olive oil takes over nine minutes and cold maple syrup requires several hours. This scientific inquiry also determined that a cast-iron wok needs approximately 15 minutes for 90% of its residual water to collect, a revelation that surprised Professor Tang, highlighting the practical applications of fluid mechanics in daily life.
