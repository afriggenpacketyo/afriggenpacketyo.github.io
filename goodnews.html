Identifying Trustworthy Financial Advisors: The Fiduciary Standard

2025-09-24 08:58:09 EDT

To ensure trustworthy financial advice, individuals should ask prospective advisors if they operate as a fiduciary. A fiduciary is legally obligated to act in the client's best interest, unlike those adhering only to a "suitability" standard, which merely requires recommendations to be appropriate. Failing to work with a fiduciary can lead to higher fees and misaligned advice, potentially costing clients tens of thousands of dollars over time. Fiduciaries must disclose all conflicts of interest, including compensation structures. Experts recommend verifying an advisor's fiduciary status through resources like the SEC's Investment Adviser Public Disclosure site and seeking written confirmation of 100% fiduciary commitment.
