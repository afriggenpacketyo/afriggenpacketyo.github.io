Ancient Western Xia Dynasty Tombs in China Added to UNESCO World Heritage List

2025-07-12 05:00:08 EDT

A complex of ancient imperial mausoleums and tombs in northwest China, dating back to the Western Xia dynasty (1038–1227), has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage site. The site, located near the city of Yinchuan, spans 40 square kilometers and includes nine imperial mausoleums and over 270 subordinate tombs. The Western Xia dynasty was ruled by the Tangut people, an ancient branch of the Qiang ethnic group. The World Heritage Committee recognized the site as a testament to the cultural fusion and interaction between diverse traditions along the historic Silk Road.
