Chen-ning Yang, a Nobel Prize-winning theoretical physicist, passed away in Beijing at the age of 103, marking the end of a remarkable life dedicated to scientific advancement. Often ranked alongside Albert Einstein, Yang was celebrated for his groundbreaking contributions to the field of physics. In 1954, he co-authored the Yang–Mills theory with Robert Mills, a set of equations that proved as pivotal to physics as Einstein’s theory of relativity.
