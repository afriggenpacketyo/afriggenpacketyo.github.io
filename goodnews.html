Scientists Discover New Massive Dinosaur Species in Thailand, "The Last Titan"

2026-05-15 12:57:59 EDT

Scientists have identified a new massive dinosaur species, Nagatitan chaiyaphumensis, from remains discovered in Thailand, estimated to have weighed as much as nine adult elephants. This long-necked herbivore, stretching 88 feet, roamed the region 100-120 million years ago and is the largest ever found in Southeast Asia. Dubbed "the last titan" due to its discovery in one of the youngest rock formations, it likely faced few predators at its full size. A life-size reconstruction is now on display in Bangkok.
