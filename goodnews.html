MOCA Acquires Kara Walker's Transformed Confederate Statue, "Unmanned Drone"

2026-02-24 10:00:00 EST

The Museum of Contemporary Art (MOCA) has acquired Kara Walker's sculpture "Unmanned Drone," a central piece from its "Monuments" exhibition. This 13-foot-tall bronze work was created by Walker from a decommissioned statue of Confederate Gen. Stonewall Jackson, originally from Charlottesville, Virginia. Walker dramatically reconfigured the statue, removing Jackson's face and emphasizing his horse, Little Sorrel, to de-venerate the Confederate figure and critique obtuse white supremacy. The acquisition highlights MOCA's commitment to expanding its collection with works that critically engage with historical narratives and contemporary social issues.
