European Court of Justice Rules EU States Must Recognize Transgender Identity on IDs

2026-03-12 12:40:00 EDT

The European Court of Justice (ECJ) has issued a landmark ruling, mandating that European Union nations must provide transgender citizens with identification documents that accurately reflect their "lived gender" rather than their gender assigned at birth. This decision originated from a case referred by Bulgaria's Supreme Court, which questioned its legal authority to amend documents for an individual identifying as a woman after being born a man. The ECJ underscored that discrepancies between lived gender and official identification can lead to "considerable inconveniences" for individuals, particularly during travel within the bloc. This ruling reinforces the supremacy of EU law over national legislation, setting a precedent for member states, including Hungary and Slovakia, that have resisted such changes.
