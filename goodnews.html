Chinese Study Challenges Darwinism, Suggests Acquired Traits Can Be Inherited

2025-05-27 03:00:08 EDT

A study by Chinese scientists has challenged Darwinian evolution by demonstrating that rice plants can pass on adaptive traits acquired through environmental stress without DNA changes. The research, published in Cell, showed that rice plants exposed to cold stress developed tolerance to low temperatures that was passed down through five generations. This finding supports the 19th-century theory of Lamarckism, which posits that organisms can pass on learned survival traits. The study identifies heritable DNA methylation changes as the mechanism driving cold adaptation, suggesting a potential reconciliation of epigenetics with evolutionary principles.
