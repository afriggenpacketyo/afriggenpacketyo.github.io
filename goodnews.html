Doctor Advises Caution with Popular Lip Oils Due to Potential for Hyperpigmentation

2025-09-13 11:00:01 EDT

A clinical professor of dermatology is advising consumers to pay close attention to the ingredients in popular lip oils, as some may cause adverse skin reactions like hyperpigmentation. The thin skin on lips is highly susceptible to sun damage, and oils without broad-spectrum SPF can magnify UV exposure, leading to darkened skin. Irritants found in some plumping ingredients and fragrances like peppermint or citrus can also cause dermatitis, which may result in post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. To use lip oils safely, consumers should look for products with SPF and hydrating ingredients like hyaluronic acid while avoiding fragrances and certain oleic acids.
