Challenges Persist in Keeping King Charles III England Coast Path Fully Open

2026-04-20 01:10:36 EDT

Maintaining the entirety of the King Charles III England Coast Path as a continuous route presents significant challenges, according to Natural England. Sections of the 2,689-mile path are currently inaccessible due to recent landslips and erosion, particularly along the Dorset coast. Landowner agreements, public objections regarding wildlife impact, and the abrupt end of the path at Southampton Water due to ferry service suspension also create access issues. Natural England highlights that the path's flexibility to reroute allows for adjustments not possible with standard footpaths, but seasonal restrictions and ongoing negotiations with landowners are necessary to balance access with environmental protection.
