Scientists Uncover "Last Titan" Dinosaur in Thailand, Weighing Nine Elephants

2026-05-15 12:57:59 EDT

Scientists have announced the discovery of a colossal new dinosaur species, Nagatitan chaiyaphumensis, from skeletal remains unearthed in Thailand. This long-necked herbivore is estimated to have measured 88 feet in length and weighed approximately 27 tons, an equivalent heft to nine adult Asian elephants, establishing it as the largest dinosaur ever found in Southeast Asia. The finding, located in one of the region's youngest rock formations, has led researchers to dub it "the last titan" before the area transformed into a shallow sea. At its full adult size, Nagatitan likely faced very few threats from predators, even from the formidable Carcharodontosaurus relative of its era.
