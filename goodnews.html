Evaluating the Necessity of Supplemental Coverage for Original Medicare Recipients

2025-10-03 14:24:43 EDT

Many retirees are surprised to find that Original Medicare does not cover all medical expenses, leaving them responsible for about 20% of costs with no out-of-pocket maximum. This exposure to potentially high and unpredictable costs is a primary reason why supplemental coverage, or Medigap, is often considered necessary. Medigap plans help cover deductibles and coinsurance, providing financial stability as healthcare needs typically increase with age. While Medicare Advantage plans are an alternative, Medigap paired with Original Medicare offers greater flexibility, allowing beneficiaries to see any provider nationwide who accepts Medicare without network restrictions.
