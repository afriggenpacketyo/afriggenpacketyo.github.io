Historical Practice of Walking Turkeys to London for Christmas Revealed

2025-12-24 00:32:22 EST

Thousands of birds, some wearing tiny leather boots or having tar-coated feet, were herded up to three miles daily to Smithfield Common. This tradition, central to the Norfolk Black breed's history, ended with the advent of railways in the late 1800s. The article highlights the efforts of the Peele family, who have farmed turkeys since the 1800s and are credited with saving the Norfolk Black breed from extinction, preserving a unique cultural heritage.
