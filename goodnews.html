The Historical Connection Between St. Martin's Day and Carnival in Germany

2025-11-10 10:51:00 EST

Two distinct German celebrations occurring on November 11, St. Martin's Day and the start of Carnival season, share a common historical origin. St. Martin's Day, marked by children's lantern parades, honors the charitable saint and traditionally signified the end of the agricultural year and a final feast. This date also became the last day of revelry before the start of a six-week Advent fasting period that was once observed by the Church. Consequently, Carnival celebrations, with their focus on exuberant feasting and jesters, also kick off on this day as a final indulgence before a period of solemnity.
