Wealthy Investors Utilize ETF Conversion Strategy to Defer Capital Gains Taxes

2025-08-21 14:22:00 EDT

A financial strategy known as a 351 conversion is gaining traction among wealthy investors as a way to defer capital gains taxes on highly appreciated assets held in taxable accounts. The process involves exchanging stocks from a separately managed account for shares in a new or existing exchange-traded fund (ETF) before it is publicly launched. This allows the investor to avoid triggering a taxable event while gaining the benefits of the ETF structure, which can rebalance its holdings more tax-efficiently. To qualify, the contributed portfolio must meet strict diversification rules, and while the strategy is effective, it can also limit an investor's future flexibility. (not investment advice)
