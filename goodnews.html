Researchers found that while the rate of cancer diagnoses has nearly doubled since 1990, the mortality rate for the eight cancers studied has remained flat. This suggests that improved screening and detection technologies are identifying more cases, including some that might never have become clinically significant. The findings add nuance to the narrative of rising cancer rates, though experts caution that certain cancers, like colorectal, are genuinely increasing in incidence and mortality.
