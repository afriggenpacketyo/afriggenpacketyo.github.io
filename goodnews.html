Endangered Mexican axolotl discovered by a child in Wales

A 10-year-old girl named Evie has discovered an endangered Mexican axolotl under a bridge near the River Ogmore in Bridgend, Wales. This marks the first documented wild discovery of the species in the UK, with only an estimated 50 to 1,000 individuals remaining globally. The axolotl, named Dippy, was found with damage to its tail and stomach, and Evie's quick action in rescuing it likely saved its life. The discovery highlights the growing popularity of axolotls as pets, partly due to their appearance in video games, which has raised concerns among animal welfare organizations about the challenges of proper care. Experts have advised that such discoveries should be reported to the government.
