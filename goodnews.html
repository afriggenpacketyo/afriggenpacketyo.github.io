Rise in Organ Donation After Circulatory Death Raises Ethical and Procedural Questions

2026-06-30 06:00:00 EDT

The number of organ donations in the US has surged, largely due to "donation after circulatory death" (DCD), where organs are recovered after the heart stops, rather than brain death. This method, now accounting for nearly half of all deceased donors, has saved thousands of lives but introduces complex ethical challenges. The process requires setting donation in motion while a patient is still alive but life support is withdrawn, blurring the lines between end-of-life care and organ recovery. New technologies like normothermic regional perfusion (NRP) further complicate these boundaries, prompting calls for standardized protocols and careful management of public trust.
