Digital Archive Launched to Preserve History of Chavez Ravine's Mexican American Community

2026-07-23 17:30:49 EDT

A new digital archive, "Chavez Ravine: An Unfinished Story," has been launched to preserve the community-curated histories, images, and memories of the Mexican Americans forcibly evicted from Chavez Ravine in the 1950s. The area, once home to thousands of working-class families in neighborhoods like Palo Verde, La Loma, and Bishop, was demolished under eminent domain, initially for a public housing project, eventually becoming the site of Dodger Stadium. This archive, co-founded by activist Carol Jacques and Professor Priscilla Leiva, aims to ensure these stories are not forgotten, serving as an accessible "archival home" for descendants. It documents the rich life and culture that existed before the evictions, pushing back against historical narratives that unjustly labeled the communities as "slums," providing a vital resource for future generations and researchers.
