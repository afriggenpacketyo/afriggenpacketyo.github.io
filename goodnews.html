Lending Experts Advise Caution on Maxing Out Home Equity Lines of Credit

With home equity at record highs, Home Equity Lines of Credit (HELOCs) are popular for home improvements or debt consolidation, but experts warn against maxing them out due to significant risks. HELOCs feature variable interest rates, meaning payments can become unaffordable if rates rise, straining household budgets and potentially leading to foreclosure. Maxing out a HELOC also negatively impacts credit scores and debt-to-income ratios, complicating future loan applications.
