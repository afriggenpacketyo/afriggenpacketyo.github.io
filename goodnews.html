2025-12-17 05:00:00 EST

The tradition of kissing under the mistletoe has roots in ancient solstice celebrations and Norse mythology, evolving from a plant whose name literally means "bird poop on a twig." The etymology stems from the way birds disperse its seeds after consuming its berries. Historically, mistletoe was associated with fertility and rebirth, used by druids for medicinal and ritualistic purposes. Its evergreen nature symbolized life during winter, connecting it to themes of renewal. The custom of kissing under the mistletoe, documented since the late 18th century, gained popularity over time, becoming a cherished holiday tradition that transforms its somewhat unromantic origins into a symbol of festive affection and celebration.
