DNA Analysis of 4,500-Year-Old Remains Reveals Life and Ancestry of Ancient Egyptian Potter

2025-07-02 11:00:00 EDT

Researchers have sequenced the first whole ancient Egyptian genome from the Old Kingdom period, revealing details about a potter who lived between 4,500 and 4,800 years ago. The DNA, extracted from the man's well-preserved remains found in a ceramic pot, showed he had brown hair and eyes and lived a hard life, with skeletal markings indicating extensive pottery work. The genetic analysis also provided a significant breakthrough, showing that about 20% of his ancestry traced back to the Fertile Crescent, offering new evidence of population mixing between North Africa and the Middle East during that era.
