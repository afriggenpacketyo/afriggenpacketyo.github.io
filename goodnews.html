Understanding UV radiation: Risks and protective measures explained

2026-05-26 06:17:03 EDT

UV (ultraviolet) radiation from the sun is essential for vitamin D production but poses significant risks, including skin cancer and premature aging, when exposure is excessive. The UV Index (UVI) measures radiation levels, with higher numbers indicating greater potential for harm. Health authorities advise using sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher and at least 4-star UVA protection, reapplying regularly. Covering up with clothing, wearing sunglasses, and seeking shade during peak sun hours (11:00-15:00) are also crucial protective measures. Tanning without protection is not considered safe.
