Two Species Thought Extinct for Millennia Rediscovered in Remote West Papua

2026-03-06 01:04:05 EST

Two species, a pygmy long-fingered possum and a ring-tailed glider, believed to be extinct for 6,000 years, have been rediscovered alive in West Papua's remote rainforests. Scientists describe finding living examples of these "lazarus taxon" as a remarkable and exceptional scientific discovery. The pygmy long-fingered possum, a striped marsupial weighing about 200g, features an extra-long fourth finger on each hand, aiding its hunt for wood-boring insect larvae. The ring-tailed glider, similar to its Australian cousin, inhabits the hollows of tall trees. These discoveries were made through a combination of fossil analysis, rare photos, and crucial assistance from local Tambrauw and Maybrat elders, who hold the glider sacred.
