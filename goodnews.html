2025-11-20 16:08:00 EST

New research published in Science proposes that Earth's moon originated from a collision with a protoplanet, Theia, that was a 'chemical twin' and formed close to our planet. The study, led by the Max Planck Institute, analyzed elemental isotopes in Earth, the moon, and meteorites, finding near-identical compositions between Earth and the moon. This challenges previous theories suggesting Theia originated further away and provides compelling evidence that the early Earth and Theia shared the same isotopic building blocks. The findings reshape our understanding of the moon's formation and the early solar system's history.
