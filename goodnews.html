Understanding the Nuances of Credit Card Payment Grace Periods

2025-07-22 11:26:03 EDT

This article clarifies the common misunderstanding of credit card grace periods, explaining that they typically apply to new purchases, not late payments. Most cards offer a window of 21-25 days after a billing cycle ends, during which a balance paid in full incurs no interest. However, this grace period is often voided if a balance is carried over from the previous month. For late payments, issuers are not required to offer a grace period, and missing a due date can result in immediate fees, penalty APRs, and eventual damage to one's credit score.
