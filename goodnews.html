Unique 'Smiling' Fossil Discovered on Holy Island, Northumberland

2026-02-19 00:07:07 EST

Christine Clark discovered a unique "smiling" fossil during a Boxing Day walk on Holy Island, Northumberland, which was later identified as a larger part of a crinoid by the British Geological Survey (BGS). Crinoids are ancient marine animals, existing for over 500 million years, and this particular fossil is estimated to be around 350 million years old. The unusual "mouth-like" shape comes from a lengthways split and curved columnal of ossicles. This discovery continues a local tradition of finding "Cuddy's beads," or crinoid stem parts, on the island, linking modern finds to historical spiritual beliefs.
