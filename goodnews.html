France's parliament passes assisted dying law for terminally ill adults

2026-07-15 15:27:00 EDT

France's parliament has approved a bill that would permit assisted dying for terminally ill adults under strict conditions. The law allows for a lethal substance to be requested by an adult experiencing constant physical suffering from a life-threatening illness. Key requirements include the patient's free and informed choice, medical examination, and assessment by an expert panel. While the National Assembly has passed the bill, the Constitutional Council will have the final say on its legality. If enacted, France would join other European nations that have legalized assisted dying.
