New California Law Standardizes Food Date Labels to Reduce Confusion and Waste

2026-06-26 06:00:00 EDT

California's new Assembly Bill 660 will standardize food date labels to just two phrases, 'BEST if Used by' and 'USE by,' aiming to reduce consumer confusion and food waste. With over 50 distinct labels currently in use, many consumers prematurely discard unspoiled food, contributing to 2.5 billion meals' worth of waste annually in California alone. This waste generates significant methane emissions and impacts household budgets. The law, effective July 1, 2026, seeks to clarify quality versus safety dates, prohibiting 'sell by' dates on consumer packaging. This initiative is seen as a crucial step towards better economic and safety decisions for consumers, addressing a long-standing national issue of inconsistent labeling.
