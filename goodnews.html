2025-09-24 08:58:09 EDT

Financial experts emphasize the importance of asking a simple question to determine if a financial advisor can be trusted: "Are you a fiduciary?" A fiduciary has a legal duty to act in their client's best interest, whereas other professionals may only be held to a "suitability" standard, meaning their recommendations only need to be appropriate, not necessarily optimal. Working with a non-fiduciary can lead to being sold high-cost products that benefit the advisor more than the client. Consumers are advised to verify an advisor's status through the SEC and seek professionals who commit to being a fiduciary at all times.
