Study Links Inhaler Propellants to Significant Carbon Emissions, Worsening Respiratory Conditions

2025-10-07 16:58:55 EDT

New research published in the Journal of the American Medical Association reveals that inhalers for asthma and COPD contribute significantly to greenhouse gas emissions, equivalent to 530,000 gas-powered cars annually. Metered-dose inhalers, which use hydrofluoroalkane propellants, account for 98% of these emissions. This creates a concerning cycle, as these emissions exacerbate climate change, which in turn worsens respiratory conditions for millions of Americans. The study highlights a "tremendous opportunity" to adopt lower-emission alternatives, such as dry powder or soft mist inhalers, to protect both patient health and the planet, urging a shift in healthcare practices.
