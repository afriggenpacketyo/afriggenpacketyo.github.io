Japan's Jobless Rate Declines Amid Labor Shortage

2025-05-01 21:13:00 EDT

Japan's average unemployment rate in fiscal year 2024 fell to 2.5 percent, marking the first improvement in two years amid a labor shortage. The number of unemployed people decreased by 30,000 to 1.75 million, while the number of those with jobs grew by 370,000 to 67.93 million, the highest level since 1953. The number of people dismissed by employers fell, while the number of individuals who left their jobs voluntarily remained unchanged. Separate data showed the average job availability ratio decreased slightly to 1.25, indicating 125 jobs available for every 100 job seekers.
