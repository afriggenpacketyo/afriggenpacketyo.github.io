This annual shift, easily remembered by the phrase "spring forward, fall back," grants most people an extra hour. The practice, which began in Germany in 1916 during World War One to conserve fuel, has continued with ongoing debates about its permanent implementation. Arguments for year-round BST include potential economic boosts and a reduction in evening road accidents, but concerns persist about the impact of very gloomy winter mornings, particularly in Scotland, where sunrise could be as late as 9:45 AM, affecting commuters and schoolchildren.
