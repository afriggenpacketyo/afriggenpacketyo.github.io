Simple Brick Stacking Method Could Reduce Air Pollution in Bangladesh

2025-05-18 05:00:00 EDT

A new study suggests that altering the way bricks are stacked in kilns in Bangladesh could significantly reduce air pollution. The traditional method of stacking bricks tightly restricts airflow, leading to inefficient burning and increased emissions. By implementing a zig-zag pattern, airflow increases, leading to more efficient combustion and reduced fuel consumption. The study found that this simple intervention could decrease fuel use by 23% and reduce carbon dioxide and particulate matter emissions by about 20%, improving both worker conditions and profits for kiln owners. The research team is now working with the Bangladeshi government to expand this training, offering a hopeful solution for a region plagued by severe air pollution.
