Understanding Required Minimum Distributions from Retirement Accounts

2026-05-06 10:58:18 EDT

Retirees with traditional IRAs or 401(k)s must begin taking Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) at age 73, with the amount calculated based on account balance and IRS life expectancy factors. For a $250,000 account, annual RMDs can range from approximately $9,434 at age 73 to over $12,376 by age 80. These distributions are taxed as ordinary income and can impact tax brackets and Medicare premiums. Failure to take the correct RMD can result in a significant penalty. The article also suggests investment options like high-yield savings accounts, precious metals, and dividend-paying stocks for remaining funds.
