China's Chang'e-6 Mission Finds Moon's Far-Side Mantle Likely Altered by Ancient Asteroid Impact

2025-07-09 11:00:08 EDT

Samples returned by China's Chang'e-6 lunar mission suggest a massive asteroid impact over 4 billion years ago significantly altered the moon's far-side mantle. The collision, which created the solar system's largest crater, appears to have stripped the deep interior of key chemical elements. Analysis published in the journal Nature reveals the volcanic rock samples are the most chemically depleted ever studied, lacking elements like titanium, thorium, and water compared to rocks from the moon's near side. This mission marks the first successful sample return from the far side of the moon, providing unique insights into its geological history.
