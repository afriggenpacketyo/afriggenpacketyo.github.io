French Parliament Approves Strict Assisted Dying Legislation

2026-07-15 13:29:26 EDT

France's National Assembly has passed a bill creating a right to assisted dying under stringent conditions, following years of extensive debate and revisions. The legislation, which previously faced rejection by the Senate, was approved by a vote of 291 to 241. Prime Minister Sébastien Lecornu is expected to refer parts of the bill to the Constitutional Council for review before it can become law. It would permit assisted dying for adults with a "serious and incurable" life-threatening illness causing unbearable and untreatable suffering, requiring a free and clear patient intention verified by a doctor after a 15-day consultation period. The patient would self-administer the lethal substance, or a medical professional would assist if they are unable.
