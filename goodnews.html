UK Implements Ban on Junk Food Advertising to Combat Childhood Obesity

2026-01-04 10:45:59 EST

A new UK-wide ban on junk food advertising on television before 9 PM and at all times online is set to take effect, aiming to tackle childhood obesity. The ban targets foods high in fat, salt, and sugar (HFSS), including soft drinks, chocolates, pizzas, and certain cereals, identified as major contributors to childhood obesity. While manufacturers can still promote healthier versions, firms failing to comply risk action from the Advertising Standards Authority. This measure, which the government estimates will prevent around 20,000 cases of childhood obesity, is seen by health experts as a "long overdue" and positive step towards public health, despite industry commitments to healthier choices.
