First-Ever CCTV Footage of a Supershear Earthquake Captured and Analyzed

2025-07-22 15:04:20 EDT

Scientists have analyzed what is believed to be the first-ever video recording of a supershear earthquake, providing unprecedented visual data on tectonic motion. The footage, captured by a CCTV camera during a magnitude 7.7 earthquake in Myanmar, shows the ground suddenly splitting and shifting horizontally. Researchers used pixel cross-correlation to analyze the clip, finding the fault slipped 8.2 feet in just 1.3 seconds, confirming a pulse-like rupture. The video also provided real-time evidence of a slightly curved slip path, a key observation that helps deepen the understanding of the physics behind large, destructive earthquakes.
