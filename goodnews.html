California's "Mini-Volcanoes" Identified as Spontaneous Combustion Events, Not Volcanic

2026-04-24 18:18:57 EDT

Fire crews in Santa Barbara recently responded to flames and smoke erupting from the "Hope Ranch Volcano," a phenomenon locally known for recurring flare-ups along the cliffs. However, experts, including UC Santa Barbara's James Boles, clarify that these are not actual volcanoes but rather instances of spontaneous combustion. This occurs when oxygen interacts with unstable compounds in the cliffside rocks, such as iron sulfides, generating intense heat that ignites nearby organic materials. Similar events have been recorded along California's coast since the 1800s, highlighting unique geological processes rather than true volcanic activity, despite the misleading local nomenclature.
