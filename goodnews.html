EFL Championship Clubs Vote to Expand Play-Offs to Six Teams for 2026-27 Season

2026-03-05 06:05:16 EST

English Football League (EFL) Championship clubs have voted in favor of expanding their play-off system from four to six teams, a change set to be introduced for the 2026-27 season. This new format, which mirrors that used in the National League, will see teams finishing from third to eighth in the league table compete for promotion to the Premier League. Clubs finishing third and fourth will progress directly to the semi-finals, while a one-legged quarter-final will be played with the fifth-placed team at home to the eighth, and the sixth hosting the seventh. Semi-finals will remain two legs, and the final will still take place at Wembley. The EFL aims to increase competitive fixtures and offer more clubs a genuine opportunity for promotion.
