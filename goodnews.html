An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Limitations

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), located in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial body of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between states. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, the court's jurisdiction applies when states agree to submit to its authority or when a case is brought under a specific UN convention, such as the Genocide Convention. Recent cases include South Africa's suit against Israel and Ukraine's against Russia. However, the ICJ's power is limited, as it has no enforcement mechanism, making it dependent on the cooperation of the involved parties to comply with its rulings.
