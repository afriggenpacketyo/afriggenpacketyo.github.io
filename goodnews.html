Mosquitoes Discovered in Iceland for the First Time, Raising Scientific Questions

2025-10-22 08:37:43 EDT

Mosquitoes have been discovered in Iceland for the first time, marking a significant biological shift for one of the world's few mosquito-free nations. An entomologist from the Natural Science Institute of Iceland identified three Culiseta annulata mosquitoes near Reykjavik, collected from moth-attracting wine ropes. This initial sighting is considered the first record of mosquitoes occurring in Iceland's natural environment. While their presence could indicate a recent introduction, possibly via international ships or containers, researchers currently do not believe climate change is the primary cause, as this specific species is well-adapted to colder climates. Further monitoring in spring will be crucial to determine their spread and potential long-term establishment.
