Understanding the 5-Year Rule for Annuities

2025-06-04 13:34:59 EDT

The five-year rule is an IRS regulation that dictates how non-spouse beneficiaries must take distributions from an inherited annuity when the original contract owner dies. This rule mandates that the entire value of the annuity be distributed within five years of the owner's death, either in a lump sum or multiple withdrawals. The rule primarily applies to non-qualified annuities funded with after-tax dollars and only affects non-spouse beneficiaries. Understanding this rule is crucial for estate planning, as it can significantly impact how beneficiaries manage inherited annuities and the associated tax implications.
