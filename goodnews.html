A long-standing traffic engineering guideline known as the "85th percentile rule" is facing increased scrutiny and modification across the United States. This rule suggests speed limits should be set based on the speed that 85% of drivers naturally travel, effectively letting the fastest drivers determine the legal limit. Critics argue this creates a dangerous feedback loop where speeding leads to higher limits, especially in urban areas with pedestrians and cyclists. In response to rising road fatalities, the Federal Highway Administration has clarified that other factors like road use and safety risks should be considered, prompting some states and cities to adopt new, safety-focused approaches.
