Museums Redefining Themselves Through Community Engagement

2026-05-16 08:46:00 EDT

Museums are undergoing a profound shift, moving from a focus on objects to fostering community engagement and participation, as recognized by the International Council of Museums' new definition. This trend, particularly strong in Latin America, emphasizes social relevance and community involvement. European museums are also embracing this, with funding increasingly tied to social impact. Examples include museums offering dance classes for seniors, hosting poetry readings, and participating in "museums on prescription" programs. Initiatives like citizen-led exhibitions and involving "museum outsiders" in staff roles are breaking down traditional barriers, highlighting the need for deep and continuous participation to remain relevant to diverse audiences.

Johnson & Johnson Bets Big on New Psoriasis Pill, Icotyde

2026-05-16 08:33:22 EDT

Johnson & Johnson (J&J) is banking on its new psoriasis pill, Icotyde, to become a major revenue driver, potentially exceeding $11 billion annually, replacing sales from the now-off-patent Stelara. Icotyde is the first oral treatment targeting the IL-23 receptor, similar to popular injectables like J&J's Tremfya. While some analysts question the pill's advantage over injectables, J&J believes Icotyde offers a much-needed alternative and fits a "sweet spot" as a first systemic choice for patients. The company is also studying Icotyde for Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis, conditions that significantly contributed to Stelara's success. Payer access and widespread insurance coverage are seen as critical for Icotyde's adoption.
