Explaining Medicare's 3-Day Rule and Its Impact on Skilled Nursing Coverage

2025-09-11 11:08:05 EDT

Medicare's "3-day rule" is a critical regulation that can significantly impact seniors' healthcare costs after a hospitalization. The rule requires a beneficiary to have a medically necessary inpatient hospital stay of at least three consecutive days before Medicare Part A will cover subsequent care in a skilled nursing facility (SNF). Time spent under "observation status" in a hospital does not count toward this requirement, a distinction that can leave patients with unexpected and substantial bills for their rehabilitation care. The article explains how the rule works, its financial implications, and potential exemptions, such as waivers available through some Medicare Advantage plans.
