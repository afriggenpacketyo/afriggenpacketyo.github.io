Exploring the Origins of the White House Thanksgiving Turkey Pardon Tradition

2025-11-25 10:28:23 EST

The annual White House tradition of pardoning a turkey for Thanksgiving has a long and varied history, officially starting with President George H.W. Bush. The practice's roots trace back to President Abraham Lincoln, who established Thanksgiving as a national holiday in 1863 and reportedly granted clemency to a Christmas turkey at his son's request. While presidents occasionally received and spared turkeys for decades, the formal ceremony and practice of sending the pardoned birds to a farm to live out their days became a consistent public event during the Reagan administration in the 1980s.
