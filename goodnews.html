2026-03-08 23:27:45 EDT

2026-03-08 20:17:54 EDT

Spain's government has announced a new amnesty scheme aimed at legalizing the status of an estimated 500,000 to 1.1 million undocumented migrants. The program will offer a one-year, renewable residency visa to individuals who have resided in Spain for at least five months and possess no criminal record. Citing both humanitarian reasons and significant economic benefits, Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez highlighted migrants' crucial role in the country's economic growth and social security system. While welcomed by migrants, the initiative faces criticism from conservative and far-right parties concerned about a "pull effect" and has drawn caution from the European Commission.
