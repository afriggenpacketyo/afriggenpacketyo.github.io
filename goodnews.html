Pompeii Excavation Uncovers Secret of Ancient Rome's Self-Healing Concrete

2025-12-22 19:56:14 EST

Archaeologists excavating a Pompeii construction site, preserved since the 79 AD Vesuvius eruption, have discovered the secret to ancient Roman concrete's remarkable longevity. Researchers found that Romans employed a "hot mixing" technique, adding quicklime to water and volcanic materials, which created lime clasts. These clasts could dissolve and reform into calcium carbonate when exposed to water, allowing the concrete to naturally heal and seal damage over time. This discovery offers valuable insights for modern construction, potentially informing the development of more durable and sustainable materials.
