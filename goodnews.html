New Research Reveals Complex Two-Tone Nature of Horse Whinnies

2026-02-25 06:00:00 EST

New research published in Current Biology reveals that horse whinnies are complex, two-toned vocalizations produced by distinct parts of their vocal tract simultaneously. Animal behavioral scientist Elodie Briefer initially noticed two frequencies, one high and one low, leading to experiments with cameras and excised larynges. The study found that vocal fold vibrations produce the low frequency, while cartilage constriction above the larynx creates a whistle for the high frequency. This unique blending appears to encode different emotional information, with the high frequency indicating pleasantness/unpleasantness and the low frequency indicating intensity, advancing understanding of animal communication.
