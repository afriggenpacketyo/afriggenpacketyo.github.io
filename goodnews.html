Humpback Whales Documented Making Record-Breaking Lifetime Swims Between Australia and Brazil

2026-05-20 06:53:00 EDT

Two humpback whales have been documented making unprecedented lifetime migrations between breeding grounds off Australia and Brazil, marking the first recorded instances of such vast distances. One whale traveled 14,200 kilometers from Queensland to Brazil, while another covered 15,100 kilometers in the opposite direction over 22 years. Identified through unique tail patterns, these rare journeys support the "Southern Ocean Exchange hypothesis," suggesting individuals may follow different routes from shared Antarctic feeding grounds. Scientists believe climate change and warming oceans could increase the frequency of these inter-basin exchanges, impacting genetic diversity and even "whale songs."
