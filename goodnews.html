Researchers Target Dormant "Sleeper Cells" to Prevent Breast Cancer Recurrence

2025-10-22 20:50:00 EDT

Researchers at the University of Pennsylvania are conducting a clinical trial to identify and target breast cancer "sleeper cells" to prevent recurrence. These cells can break away from the main tumor and resurface later to spread cancer throughout the body, making them a key target for treatment. The trial involves identifying sleeper cells in a patient's bone marrow and administering drugs to attack them, with promising early results. After seven years, only two out of 51 women in the trial experienced cancer recurrence, significantly below the expected rate. This research offers hope for preventing cancer recurrence and improving outcomes for breast cancer patients.
