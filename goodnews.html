Tiny Zooplankton Play a Major, Previously Underestimated Role in Carbon Sequestration

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny marine creatures called zooplankton are crucial allies in the fight against climate change, locking away vast amounts of carbon in the deep ocean. These animals gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton near the surface in Antarctica, grow fat, and then sink to the depths to burn this fat over winter. This process, known as the seasonal vertical migration pump, sequesters an estimated 65 million tonnes of carbon annually, equivalent to the emissions of 55 million cars. The discovery highlights the underappreciated importance of these creatures to the planet's climate regulation.
