Sweden to Lease Prison Cells in Estonia to Alleviate Overcrowding

2025-06-04 08:19:34 EDT

Sweden has reached an agreement to send up to 600 criminals to serve their sentences in an Estonian prison, in an effort to reduce pressure on the country's overcrowded jails. Starting in July of next year, Sweden will be able to transfer male prisoners convicted of various crimes to a prison in Tartu, Estonia. Sweden will pay 8,500 euros per inmate per month, which is less than the average cost of 11,500 euros per month in Sweden. The Swedish prison system is currently facing significant pressures due to tougher laws and sentencing related to gang-related violence.
