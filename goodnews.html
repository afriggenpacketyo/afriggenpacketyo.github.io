Aviation Pioneer Wally Funk Dies at 87, Celebrated for Breaking Barriers

2026-07-09 13:21:29 EDT

Wally Funk, a pioneering aviator and the oldest woman to journey into space, has passed away at 87 in Grapevine, Texas. Renowned for over seven decades dedicated to aviation, Funk was a key member of the Mercury 13 program in 1961, where she excelled in rigorous astronaut testing, outperforming many male counterparts despite being barred from NASA due to her gender. She later became the first female flight instructor at Fort Sill and the first female air safety investigator for the NTSB. Her lifelong dream of space travel was realized in 2021 at age 82 with Blue Origin, making her a Guinness World Record holder and the only Mercury 13 member to reach space.
