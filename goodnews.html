2025-05-17 02:34:00 EDT

The study, published in Science, analyzed the genomes of 1,537 individuals from 139 ethnic groups in northern Eurasia and the Americas. It revealed that these early settlers split into distinct groups that became isolated in different environmental settings, contributing to the diverse genetic makeup of present-day South American communities. The findings also highlight the importance of preserving the cultural heritage of endangered Indigenous groups, such as the Kawesqar people of Patagonia.
