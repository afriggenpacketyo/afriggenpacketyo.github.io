Understanding Chapter 13 Bankruptcy: Income and Repayment Plans

2025-10-22 12:07:07 EDT

Chapter 13 bankruptcy, a "wage earner's plan," does not take all of a filer's income but rather a portion known as "disposable income" after essential living expenses are covered. This type of bankruptcy allows individuals to create a three-to-five-year repayment plan to creditors while maintaining a basic standard of living. It's often suitable for those with regular income who are behind on secured debts or have assets they wish to protect, and it can be an alternative to Chapter 7 or debt settlement. Consulting a debt relief expert is recommended to determine the best path for individual financial situations. (not investment advice)
