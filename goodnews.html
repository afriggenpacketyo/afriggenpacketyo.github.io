Scientists Uncover Why Type 1 Diabetes is More Severe in Young Children

2025-11-12 21:48:00 EST

Scientists have discovered that type 1 diabetes is more aggressive in young children due to the vulnerability of their still-developing pancreas. Research published in Science Advances shows that in childhood, particularly before age seven, beta cells in the pancreas exist in small clusters, making them more susceptible to immune system attacks. As people age, these cells mature into larger, more durable groups, allowing for some insulin production even after an attack. This finding offers hope for new immunotherapy drugs, like teplizumab, which could potentially delay the disease's onset by giving the pancreas time to mature.
