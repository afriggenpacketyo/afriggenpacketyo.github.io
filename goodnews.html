Climate Change Rewriting Polar Bear DNA, Offering Adaptation Clues

2025-12-16 06:15:00 EST

Researchers have identified changes in polar bear DNA that suggest adaptation to warmer climates, marking the first statistically significant link between rising temperatures and genetic shifts in a wild mammal. Analyzing blood samples from polar bears in Greenland, scientists found altered gene activity related to heat stress, aging, and metabolism, particularly in bears from warmer regions. These 'jumping genes' may be a survival mechanism against melting sea ice and changing diets. While this offers hope for understanding polar bear resilience, scientists stress the critical need to reduce fossil fuel burning to slow temperature increases and prevent extinction. Further research will examine other polar bear populations globally.
