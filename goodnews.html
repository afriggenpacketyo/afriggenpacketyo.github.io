UK Government Plans to Lower Voting Age to 16, Prompting Focus on Social Media Campaigning

2025-07-18 05:53:11 EDT

The British government is set to introduce a bill that would lower the voting age to 16 for all UK elections, potentially adding 1.5 million young people to the electorate. This represents the most significant change to the franchise since 1969 and aligns national elections with rules already in place in Scotland and Wales. The move is expected to force political parties to intensify their outreach on social media platforms like TikTok, which are primary news sources for teenagers. Analysts suggest that while the Labour Party is expected to benefit most, the right-wing Reform UK party also has a strong social media presence that could attract this new voter base.
