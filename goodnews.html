NASA Rover Discovery Suggests Mars Was Predominantly a Desert Planet

2025-07-02 15:12:50 EDT

A recent discovery by NASA's Curiosity rover offers new insight into why Mars is uninhabitable, suggesting it was always destined to be a mostly desert world. The rover found rocks rich in carbonate minerals, which trap carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, a key element for planetary warming. Unlike Earth, which has a balanced climate cycle involving volcanic activity, Mars's feeble volcanic outgassing was insufficient to replenish its atmospheric CO2. This imbalance meant that any periods of liquid water on Mars were likely brief, sporadic oases, followed by millions of years of barren, cold conditions inhospitable to life.
