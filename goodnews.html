2025-05-29 11:11:54 EDT

Astronomers searching for the elusive "Planet Nine" have instead discovered a new dwarf planet, named 2017 OF201, in the outer reaches of the solar system. The object, about 430 miles across, is located three times farther from Earth than Neptune and has an extremely elongated orbit that takes 25,000 years to complete. The discovery challenges the Planet Nine theory, as the dwarf planet's orbit does not follow the clustered trend of other objects in the Kuiper Belt. While the Vera Rubin Observatory is expected to shed more light on the mystery, the discovery highlights the potential for open science and the use of archival data in making significant astronomical findings.
