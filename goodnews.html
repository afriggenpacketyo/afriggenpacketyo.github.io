Pompeii Excavation Uncovers Roman Self-Healing Concrete Secret

2025-12-22 19:56:14 EST

Archaeologists excavating a Pompeii site have uncovered the secret behind the remarkable longevity of ancient Roman structures: a unique self-healing concrete mixture. A recent study, published in Nature Communications, revealed that Romans used a "hot mixing" technique, adding quicklime to water, volcanic rock, and ash, which created lime clasts. These clasts could dissolve and reform into calcium carbonate when water seeped in, effectively sealing and repairing damage over time. This discovery, found at a construction site preserved by the 79 AD Mount Vesuvius eruption, offers ancient wisdom with potential benefits for modern, durable, and low-carbon concrete development.
