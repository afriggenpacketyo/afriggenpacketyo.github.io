Psychologists suggest that watching characters navigate high-pressure situations induces a form of "vicarious stress," a fight-or-flight response that can feel good because it carries no real-world risk for the viewer. These shows often use hyperrealistic, real-time filmmaking techniques that create a strong sense of empathy and immersion. For many viewers, particularly younger audiences accustomed to constant real-world anxiety, watching relatable characters overcome mundane but intense challenges can be a cathartic and validating experience.
