Physicists Investigate Optimal Drainage Times for Liquids in Everyday Contexts

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Two physicists from Brown University, Thomas Dutta and Jay Tang, have scientifically explored the optimal time required for liquids to drain from containers, a common household inquiry. Their research, published in Physics of Fluids, utilized Navier-Stokes equations and practical experiments to measure the drainage rates of various liquids such as milk, olive oil, and maple syrup. Findings revealed water drains quickly, milk in about 30 seconds, and olive oil in over nine minutes, while thick maple syrup takes hours. This "ludic science" approach, which explores quirky everyday questions, also determined that a wok requires approximately 15 minutes to drain 90% of its residual water, challenging common assumptions.
