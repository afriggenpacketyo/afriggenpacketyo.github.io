Donation After Circulatory Death Transforms Organ Transplantation

2026-07-23 06:20:00 EDT

Donation after circulatory death (DCD) has revolutionized organ transplantation in the U.S., now accounting for nearly half of all organ donors, a dramatic increase from its previous rarity. This method allows organs to be recovered from patients whose hearts have stopped beating, expanding the donor pool significantly. While DCD has saved thousands of lives by providing more organs, it introduces ethical complexities by requiring the donation process to begin before death is fully declared. New technologies like organ care systems and normothermic regional perfusion (NRP) are extending organ viability but also raising questions about the definition of death and the potential for unintended blood flow to the brain. Standardization of DCD protocols and safeguards remains a critical focus.
