Supreme Court Ruling in Hencely v. Fluor Corporation on Terrorism and Preemption

2026-04-22 17:35:00 EDT

The Supreme Court's decision in Hencely v. Fluor Corporation allows a lawsuit by an Army Specialist injured in a 2016 Afghanistan bombing to proceed, ruling that federal law does not preempt state law in this instance. The case centered on whether Fluor Corporation, a contractor employing the bomber, could be sued under South Carolina law for negligence in supervising the individual. Justice Thomas, writing for the majority, argued that preemption is only justified when federal law directs a contractor to perform the exact action forbidden by state law. This ruling suggests a narrower view of preemption among some justices, potentially benefiting consumers and civil rights advocates.
