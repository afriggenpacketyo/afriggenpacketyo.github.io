Businesses are leveraging a decades-old piece of legislation known as the "first sale rule" to minimize the impact of tariffs. This rule allows U.S. importers to calculate customs duties based on the initial sale price from the factory to a vendor, rather than the inflated price from an intermediary. The first sale rule gained renewed attention during the Trump administration's tariff regime, and requires that there be at least two sales involved, the sales must be carried out at arm's length by independent parties, there must be proof that the item was destined for the U.S., and there must be documentation of the first sale price.
