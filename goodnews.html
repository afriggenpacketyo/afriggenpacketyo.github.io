Long-Lost Rembrandt Painting, "Vision of Zacharias in the Temple," Authenticated After 65 Years

2026-03-02 11:54:40 EST

A long-lost painting by Rembrandt, "Vision of Zacharias in the Temple" from 1633, has been rediscovered and authenticated by experts after being excluded from official lists for decades. The masterpiece resurfaced when its private owners presented it to the Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam, which conducted a two-year examination. The museum confirmed its authenticity based on paint analysis, stylistic similarities, and the painting technique, all consistent with Rembrandt's early works. Director Taco Dibbits praised the painting's "incredible power" and high quality, noting its innovative depiction of the Biblical scene. Its value is currently unknown, though other Rembrandt works have fetched millions at auction.
