Daylight Saving Time 2026 Begins Sunday, Shifting Clocks Forward

2026-03-08 01:18:54 EDT

Daylight saving time for 2026 commences on Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, requiring most Americans to "spring forward" and consequently lose an hour of sleep. This annual adjustment shifts an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening, and will remain in effect for 238 days until November 1. Historically, the practice was introduced during World Wars to conserve fuel, though contemporary research indicates minimal energy conservation benefits and potential negative health impacts. Notably, only Hawaii and Arizona, with the exception of the Navajo Nation, do not observe this nationwide time change.
