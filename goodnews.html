Dividends offer a consistent cash flow, making them appealing to income-dependent investors like retirees, though they are subject to taxation. Conversely, stock buybacks reduce the number of outstanding shares, which typically boosts earnings per share and carries a lower tax burden for shareholders, but the return is less direct.
