Bipartisan Bill Seeks to Expand IRA Charitable Donation Options for Retirees

2026-03-24 09:51:06 EDT

A new bipartisan Senate bill aims to expand options for retirees making qualified charitable distributions (QCDs) from their individual retirement accounts. Currently, individuals aged 70½ and older can make direct transfers from IRAs to eligible nonprofits, counting towards required minimum distributions and excluding the amount from taxable income. The proposed legislation would allow these QCDs to be directed to donor-advised funds (DAFs), which are charitable giving accounts managed by public nonprofits. Critics, however, express concern that allowing QCDs to DAFs without distribution requirements could lead to wealth hoarding in these funds, as DAFs are not subject to minimum distribution rules.
