Understanding the Origins and History of Daylight Saving Time

2026-03-06 18:35:19 EST

As clocks "spring forward" this Sunday, the origins of daylight saving time remain a complex topic, often mistakenly attributed to farmers. While Benjamin Franklin proposed early rising in 1784, the concept gained traction with New Zealand entomologist George Hudson in 1895 and British builder William Willet in 1907, who advocated for advancing clocks to save energy and promote activity. Germany first adopted it in 1916 during World War I to conserve fuel, with the U.S. following in 1918. The practice has been debated and modified over the decades.
