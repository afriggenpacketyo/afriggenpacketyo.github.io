Humpback Whales Achieve Record-Breaking Trans-Oceanic Journeys Between Australia and Brazil

2026-05-19 20:41:53 EDT

Two individual humpback whales have set new migration records, making separate crossings between eastern Australia and Brazil, a distance of approximately 9,000 miles (14,500 kilometers). Identified by unique tail markings, these whales traveled in opposite directions, covering farther distances than any previously recorded humpback. This rare event challenges existing knowledge about humpback population separation and their typically predictable migration patterns. Scientists suggest the whales might have met others in shared feeding grounds before diverging from their usual return paths, emphasizing the vast range of these marine animals and the importance of tracking their movements amid warming oceans and changing krill habitats due to climate change.
