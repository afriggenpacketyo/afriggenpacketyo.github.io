NASA's Perseverance Rover Finds Potential Biosignatures of Ancient Martian Life

2025-09-10 14:35:00 EDT

NASA's Perseverance rover has identified rock samples in Mars's Jezero Crater containing minerals that could be signs of ancient microbial life. The minerals, vivianite and greigite, were discovered in mudstones from an ancient river channel and are known on Earth to be byproducts of microbial metabolism acting on organic matter. While scientists emphasize this is not definitive proof and that non-biological processes cannot be ruled out, NASA's science chief called it the closest the agency has come to such a discovery. Final confirmation would require returning the samples to Earth for detailed analysis in laboratories.
