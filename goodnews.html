While both exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and mutual funds offer diversified investment baskets, they have key differences in trading, tax efficiency, and cost. ETFs trade like stocks throughout the day, whereas mutual funds are priced once at the end of the day. A significant advantage for ETFs is their tax efficiency, as their "in-kind" transaction structure rarely generates the capital gains taxes that are common with mutual funds held in taxable accounts.
