Nearly 3,000-Year-Old Human Remains Discovered in El Salvador, Offering New Mesoamerican Insights

2026-07-22 09:30:42 EDT

Archaeologists in El Salvador have exhumed human remains believed to be nearly 3,000 years old, dating back to approximately 850 BC. Discovered in a burial site at Antiguo Cuscatlan, the find includes a small Usulutan-style ceramic vessel and preserved cassava branches beneath volcanic ash. The remains, found face down, will undergo radiocarbon dating and DNA analysis to determine age and sex. This discovery could represent one of Mesoamerica's oldest pre-Hispanic burials, potentially providing crucial new information about the ancient populations that inhabited the region over two millennia ago, building on previous significant finds in El Salvador.
