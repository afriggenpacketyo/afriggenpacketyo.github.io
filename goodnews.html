Chinese Study Finds Gut Microbes Essential for Apple Antioxidant's Cancer-Fighting Effects

2025-11-23 05:00:10 EST

A research team from Shandong University has discovered that the cancer-fighting potential of quercetin, an antioxidant found in foods like apples and onions, is dependent on gut microbes. Through experiments with mice, the study found that gut bacteria break down quercetin into a metabolite called DOPAC, which in turn activates immune cells to inhibit tumor growth. When the mice were given antibiotics, the anticancer effects of quercetin were blocked, indicating that the antioxidant does not act directly on tumors. This finding highlights the crucial role of the gut microbiome in mediating the health benefits of dietary nutrients and suggests DOPAC could be a promising candidate for cancer immunotherapy.
