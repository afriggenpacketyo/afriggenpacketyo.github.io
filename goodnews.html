The Enduring Fascination with Mars and Its Origins in 19th-Century Pseudoscience

2025-08-15 06:00:00 EDT

Humanity's long-standing obsession with Mars, now championed by figures like Elon Musk, has its roots in a 19th-century astronomical misinterpretation. The belief in an intelligent Martian civilization began when Italian astronomer Giovanni Schiaparelli's term "canali" (channels) was mistranslated as "canals," suggesting artificial construction. This idea was popularized by astronomer Percival Lowell, who theorized a vast irrigation network built by a wise, dying race, capturing the public imagination during a time of societal despair. Though later disproven, this early craze fueled generations of science fiction and inspired the very space exploration that continues today.
