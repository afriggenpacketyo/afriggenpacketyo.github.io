The law, which went into effect in 2021, aims to improve traffic flow and safety by preventing drivers from coasting in the left lane. Violating this rule is a civil offense that can result in a $25 fine, though no court costs are added. There are several exceptions, such as when traffic congestion makes it impractical to move right, during inclement weather, or when preparing for a left-side exit, ensuring the rule is applied with practical considerations for road conditions.
