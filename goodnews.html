Chinese Crystal Breakthrough Advances GPS-Free Thorium Clock Navigation

2026-04-11 10:00:09 EDT

A Chinese research team has developed a novel fluorinated borate compound that could significantly advance GPS-free thorium clock navigation by pushing laser light to a record 145.2 nanometres. This specific wavelength is short enough to meet a crucial requirement for these ultra-precise, portable nuclear clocks, which are currently under development in the United States, China, and other nations. The new crystal, detailed in Advanced Materials, surpasses previous benchmarks set by potassium beryllium fluoroborate, an older Chinese crystal, by achieving the necessary 148.3nm target. This breakthrough offers a new pathway for designing next-generation deep-ultraviolet materials and is considered to "pave the way for the practical development of the thorium-229 nuclear clock," promising enhanced navigation capabilities.
