Explainer: Why Cities Experience Higher Temperatures During Heatwaves

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

This article explains the science behind the "urban heat island" effect, a phenomenon where cities and built-up areas become significantly hotter than surrounding rural landscapes. The primary cause is the prevalence of hard, dark surfaces like asphalt roads and buildings, which absorb and retain the sun's energy instead of reflecting it. This stored heat is slowly released, raising ambient temperatures, especially overnight. As climate change makes heatwaves more frequent and intense, this effect makes growing urban populations particularly vulnerable to extreme heat.
