Businesses Circumvent Tariffs Using Legal Loophole

2025-05-27 08:45:47 EDT

Businesses are minimizing the impact of tariffs by leveraging the "first sale rule," a decades-old U.S. customs law. This rule allows importers to calculate duties based on the initial sale price between a manufacturer and a vendor, rather than the inflated price from an intermediary. To apply the rule, there must be at least two sales by independent parties, proof the item was destined for the U.S., and documentation of the first sale price. While this process can be complex and requires trust between parties, the potential cost savings make it worthwhile, especially for higher-value consumer goods and luxury products.
