NASA Satellite Re-enters Earth's Atmosphere After Extended Mission

2026-03-11 18:20:47 EDT

A 1,300-pound NASA satellite, Van Allen Probe A, successfully re-entered Earth's atmosphere over the eastern Pacific Ocean after completing an almost 14-year mission. Launched in August 2012, the probe and its twin spacecraft were dedicated to exploring Earth's permanent radiation belts, crucial zones of charged particles. While most of the probe was expected to incinerate upon re-entry, some components were projected to survive, though NASA assessed the risk of harm to individuals on Earth as very low. The mission significantly surpassed its original two-year lifespan, contributing hundreds of publications and notable discoveries, including the existence of a third radiation belt.
