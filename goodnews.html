Scientists Develop Novel Robot Launch Method Using Laser-Induced Cavitation Bubbles

2025-09-06 23:00:08 EDT

A joint team of US and Chinese researchers has developed an innovative method to launch small, millimeter-scale robots by harnessing the energy from collapsing vapor bubbles. The process, known as cavitation, involves using a laser to rapidly heat light-absorbing materials on a wet surface, causing water droplets to boil and form bubbles. When these bubbles collapse, they release a powerful burst of energy capable of launching tiny jumpers up to five feet into the air. This technique offers a new, efficient launching mechanism that can be precisely controlled by manipulating the laser's light.
