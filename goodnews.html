Sun Pharma to acquire U.S. firm Organon for $11.75 billion

2026-04-26 22:29:23 EDT

Indian drugmaker Sun Pharmaceutical Industries has announced an all-cash deal to acquire U.S.-based Organon & Co for $11.75 billion, including debt. This acquisition will elevate Sun Pharma into the top 25 global pharmaceutical companies, with combined revenues projected at $12.4 billion. Organon, spun off from Merck in 2021, specializes in women's health and biosimilars and boasts a portfolio of over 70 products sold in 140 countries. The deal is part of Sun Pharma's strategy to strengthen its global business and scale its medicine products, particularly in the key U.S. market. The acquisition is expected to significantly boost Sun Pharma's innovative medicines segment, increasing its contribution to the company's topline.

China's industrial profits surge in March driven by AI and chip sectors

2026-04-26 22:25:38 EDT

Profits for China's industrial firms experienced their most significant growth in six months during March, increasing by 15.8% year-on-year. This acceleration, up from 15.2% in the first two months, marks the strongest start to a year since 2017. The surge is largely attributed to the booming equipment and high-tech manufacturing sectors, with profits in these areas soaring by 21% and 47.4% respectively in the first quarter. The artificial intelligence and semiconductor boom significantly boosted subsectors like optical fiber, optoelectronics, and display devices. Despite rising global oil prices and potential risks to raw material costs, China's industrial profits have shown robust growth, indicating strong domestic demand in key technological areas.

Chinese EVs gain significant market share in South Korea

2026-04-26 22:00:24 EDT

Sales of Chinese-made electric vehicles (EVs) in South Korea have surged by 286.1% in the first quarter of 2026, reaching 25,000 units. This rapid growth has led to Chinese EVs capturing a substantial market share, rising from 4.7% in 2022 to 33.9% in 2025, while Korean EV sales, though higher in volume, grew at a slower pace. Tesla's Shanghai-produced models are a major driver of this trend, with the company offering them at lower prices in South Korea. Experts suggest that Chinese firms are increasingly looking to overseas markets for growth due to slowing EV demand domestically. This shift indicates a growing competitiveness of Chinese EV manufacturers in traditionally strong automotive markets.
