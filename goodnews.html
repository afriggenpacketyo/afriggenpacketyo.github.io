Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is marking a major scientific milestone: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation. This unbroken sequence of handwritten data, which began on July 14, 1795, is the longest of its kind in the UK and Ireland, providing an invaluable record of climate change. The tradition continues today with a human observer manually recording data, a practice now rare in an era of automated stations. The observatory's long history has contributed significantly to meteorology, including the development of the cup anemometer for measuring wind speed. The data clearly shows a warming climate, with current observer Shane Kelly noting that the distinct seasons of the past have blurred.
