Gene Therapy Jab Shows Breakthrough in Reversing Hearing Loss

2025-07-06 23:10:30 EDT

A breakthrough gene therapy involving a single injection has shown remarkable success in reversing hearing loss in children and adults with congenital deafness caused by mutations in the OTOF gene. Clinical trials, detailed in Nature Medicine, demonstrated that injecting a synthetic, harmless version of the adeno-associated virus with a functional OTOF gene into the inner ear significantly improved hearing in all 10 participants. One seven-year-old girl quickly regained almost full hearing, able to hold daily conversations within four months. Researchers noted considerable improvement in average perceptible sound volume and confirmed the treatment's safety, marking a significant step towards treating genetic deafness and offering hope for broader applications in the future.
