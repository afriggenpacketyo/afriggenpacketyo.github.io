2025-10-28 09:07:22 EDT

These specimens of Edmontosaurus annectens, initially thought to be fossilized skin, are actually delicate clay molds formed by microbes during decay, protected by thin layers of clay and sandstone. The findings include the first subadult dinosaur mummy with a fully preserved fleshy outline and the earliest known tetrapod with hooves, marking the first reptile with hooved feet. This remarkable discovery provides unprecedented insights into dinosaur soft tissue, external appearance, and evolutionary traits, challenging historical restorations.
