Understanding Jewish Funeral Rituals and Mourning Traditions

2025-10-15 10:07:00 EDT

Jewish funeral traditions, known as mitzvahs, prioritize a quick, simple burial conducted with utmost dignity, focusing on eternal memory among the living rather than an afterlife. Bodies undergo a ritual purification bath, Taharah, and are typically buried in plain wooden caskets or shrouds, without embalming or public viewing, emphasizing humility. Close family members observe K'riah, a ritual tearing of garments, and a seven-day mourning period called shiva, refraining from work and grooming. The Mourner's Kaddish, an ancient Aramaic prayer, is recited to exalt God and honor the deceased, often for 11 months for parents, emphasizing communal support during grief.
