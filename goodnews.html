Massive Martian Meteorite, One of the Largest on Earth, to Be Auctioned by Sotheby's

2025-07-15 16:49:31 EDT

Sotheby's is auctioning NWA 16788, the largest known piece of Mars on Earth, with an expected price of $2 million to $4 million. The meteorite, weighing over 54 pounds, represents about 6.5% of all known Martian material on our planet. It is believed to have been ejected from Mars by an asteroid strike before traveling 140 million miles and landing in the Sahara Desert, where it was discovered in 2023. Such finds are exceptionally rare, with only about 0.6% of all meteorites found on Earth originating from the red planet, making this a significant item for collectors and scientists.
