France's Parliament Approves Assisted Dying Law After Years of Intense Debate

2026-07-15 13:29:26 EDT

After years of extensive and often contentious debate, France's National Assembly has approved a landmark bill establishing a right to assisted dying under a stringent set of conditions. The legislation, passed by a vote of 291 to 241, permits assisted dying for French adults suffering from a "serious and incurable" life-threatening illness that has reached an advanced or terminal stage, causing unbearable or treatment-resistant physical or psychological suffering. Patients must freely express their intention to a doctor, who then makes a decision within 15 days, with the patient typically administering the lethal substance themselves, or with assistance if physically unable. This vote places France among several other European nations that have decriminalized assisted dying in various forms.
