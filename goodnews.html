2025-08-27 06:00:00 EDT

A new study published in Nature Neuroscience challenges the long-held belief that the brain dramatically reorganizes its body map after limb amputation. Researchers, using MRI scans before and after amputations on three individuals, found that the phantom hand representation remained strikingly similar to the pre-amputated hand, even up to five years later. This finding contradicts decades of research and suggests that phantom limb pain might occur because the brain still expects signals from the missing body part, rather than a remapping of brain areas.
