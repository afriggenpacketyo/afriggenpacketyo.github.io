This rare species, often called a "living fossil" due to its 125-million-year lineage, is characterized by its unique slingshot jaws and horn-like rostrum equipped with electro-sensory organs. The confirmed sightings occurred near Jarvis Island in the Central Pacific and along the Tonga Trench, vastly extending the known geographic range of these elusive creatures to the Pacific, Atlantic, and Indian Oceans. This groundbreaking video evidence provides crucial insights into the goblin shark's life and habitat, informing future conservation efforts as deepwater exploration and commercial activities increase.
