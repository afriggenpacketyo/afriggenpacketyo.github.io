China Utilizes Price-Setting Deals to Mitigate International Trade Tensions

2026-02-28 03:00:09 EST

China and South Korea have successfully resolved a trade dispute over hot-rolled steel coils by implementing a price-undertaking deal, a method previously utilized in China's electric vehicle stand-off with the European Union. This agreement involves Chinese exporters voluntarily raising their export prices, thereby avoiding the imposition of anti-dumping duties and safeguarding their access to crucial markets. Analysts view such deals as a beneficial solution for China, as they allow the price premium to remain with the exporting companies rather than being captured as punitive duties by importing nations. China's Ministry of Commerce welcomed this "soft landing" for its role in enhancing the stability and predictability of bilateral steel trade relations.
