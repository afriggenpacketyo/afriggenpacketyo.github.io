Scientific Community Debates the Reason for Red Autumn Leaves

2025-11-05 12:03:44 EST

Scientists remain divided on the exact evolutionary reason why some trees produce vibrant red pigments in their leaves just before they fall. While the loss of green chlorophyll unmasks existing yellow pigments, the red color comes from newly created chemicals called anthocyanins. One leading theory, the photoprotection hypothesis, suggests these pigments act as a sunscreen, protecting the leaf as it transfers valuable nutrients back to the tree. Another hypothesis argues that the red color co-evolved as a warning signal to deter insects like aphids from laying their eggs on the tree for the winter.

Explainer on the Timeline from Credit Card Charge-Off to Collections

2025-11-05 11:16:19 EST

This article details the process of what happens to a credit card account after a borrower falls significantly behind on payments. A "charge-off" is an accounting measure taken by a creditor, typically after 180 days of non-payment, to classify the debt as unlikely to be collected. However, this does not eliminate the borrower's obligation to pay. Following the charge-off, the original creditor may continue internal collection efforts for a period, or they may sell the debt to a third-party collection agency, which often occurs within 30 to 90 days, at which point the borrower must deal with the new agency.
