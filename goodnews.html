2025-11-02 00:01:01 EST

Daylight Saving Time officially ended for 2025 on Sunday, November 2, at 2 a.m., when clocks were set back to 1 a.m., providing most Americans with an extra hour of sleep. This annual tradition, which dates back to Germany's World War I effort to conserve energy, was formalized in the U.S. by the Uniform Time Act of 1966. While widespread, states like Hawaii and Arizona, along with several U.S. territories, do not observe it. The practice remains a subject of debate, with polls showing strong public support for changing the current system. A 2022 Senate bill to make daylight saving time permanent failed, and former President Trump has expressed a desire to eliminate the time change entirely.
