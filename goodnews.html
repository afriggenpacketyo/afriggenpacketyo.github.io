France's Parliament Passes Assisted Dying Law

2026-07-15 15:27:00 EDT

France's parliament has approved a bill allowing assisted dying for terminally ill adults, a move years in the making and an election promise by President Emmanuel Macron. The law permits adults with a life-threatening illness causing constant physical suffering to request a lethal substance, either self-administered or by a doctor. It specifically excludes psychological suffering or neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's. Patients must express a free and informed choice, undergo physician examination, and have their case assessed by experts. The bill will now proceed to France's highest constitutional authority for a final decision.
