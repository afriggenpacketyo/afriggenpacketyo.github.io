Expert Advice: Electrolyte Drinks Unnecessary for Most Everyday Exercise

2026-01-01 19:48:12 EST

Professor Graeme Close, a prominent sports nutrition researcher, highlights that electrolyte drinks are largely unnecessary for individuals engaging in moderate, everyday exercise, such as gym sessions or 5k runs. He explains that the human body efficiently regulates its electrolyte balance, and a standard, healthy diet adequately supplies essential minerals like sodium, potassium, and magnesium. Electrolyte supplementation becomes relevant only during prolonged and intense physical activity, especially in hot environments where substantial fluid loss through sweat occurs. For most people, simply sipping water is sufficient for hydration, while a homemade mix of water, fruit juice, and a pinch of salt offers a practical alternative for specific needs.
