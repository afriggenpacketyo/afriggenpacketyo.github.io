Research Reveals Why Type 1 Diabetes is More Severe in Young Children

2025-11-12 21:48:00 EST

Scientists have uncovered why type 1 diabetes manifests more aggressively in young children, particularly those under seven. The research, published in Science Advances, indicates that the pancreas is still developing in early childhood, making its insulin-producing beta cells more vulnerable to immune system attacks. In younger children, these cells exist in smaller, less durable clusters, which are more easily destroyed compared to the larger, more mature islets found in older individuals. This significant finding offers hope for new immunotherapy drugs, like teplizumab, that could potentially delay the disease's onset by allowing the pancreas more time to mature.
