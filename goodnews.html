Paris Reopens Seine River for Public Swimming After a Century-Long Ban

2025-07-05 02:35:00 EDT

For the first time in over 100 years, Paris has reopened the Seine River to public swimmers, a legacy project of the 2024 Olympics. The city has established three supervised outdoor pool zones, complete with amenities, which will be free to access until late August. The original ban, enacted in 1923, was due to severe pollution from sewage and industrial waste, but a €1.4 billion investment in water quality improvement, including a massive underground storage tank, has made swimming safe again. This initiative, championed by Mayor Anne Hidalgo, aims to enhance the quality of life for Parisians and adapt the city to climate change, fulfilling a long-held promise.

Chinese Scientists Discover 'Genetic Switch' for Organ Regeneration in Mice

2025-07-05 01:00:08 EDT

Scientists in China have successfully restored damaged outer ear tissue in mice by activating a 'genetic switch,' a discovery that could have future implications for human organ regeneration. The study, published in the journal Science, found that mice lost their regenerative ability due to insufficient production of retinoic acid, a key compound in tissue repair. By turning on an 'evolutionarily disabled' genetic pathway, the researchers enabled the complete restoration of lost cartilage and tissue in the mice's ears. While acknowledging the long road to human application, the Beijing-based team is hopeful, as their findings suggest this regenerative mechanism might also be present in other organs.
