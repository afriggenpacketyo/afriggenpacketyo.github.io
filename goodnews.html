Raccoon Roundworm, a Brain-Infecting Parasite, Detected in San Fernando Valley Dog

2026-01-17 22:44:06 EST

Public health officials in Los Angeles County have reported the first formal case of Baylisascaris, commonly known as raccoon roundworm, detected in a healthy dog within the San Fernando Valley. This parasite, typically found in raccoons, poses a risk to humans by potentially infecting the brain, spinal cord, and eyes, leading to severe neurological issues and eye disease. Transmission primarily occurs when people or pets ingest food or water contaminated with raccoon feces containing mature eggs. Officials strongly emphasize preventive measures, including prompt cleanup of raccoon waste, thorough handwashing, and maintaining a safe distance from wildlife. The risk to the public is considered extremely low, with fewer than 10 human cases in California since 1993.
