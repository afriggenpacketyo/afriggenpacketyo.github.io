Explainer: Understanding Chronic Venous Insufficiency, President Trump's Recent Diagnosis

2025-07-17 18:31:24 EDT

Chronic venous insufficiency (CVI), the condition President Trump was recently diagnosed with, is a common circulatory issue affecting up to 40% of the U.S. population. It occurs when valves in the leg veins are damaged, causing blood to pool and leading to symptoms like varicose veins, leg swelling, and skin discoloration. According to vascular surgeons, the condition is typically not serious and is often managed with non-surgical treatments like compression stockings and lifestyle changes such as regular walking. While genetics and aging are risk factors, the condition itself is considered benign and treatable for most patients.
