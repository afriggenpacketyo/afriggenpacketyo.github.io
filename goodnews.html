New Dicynodont Fossil in China Reveals Ancient Cross-Equator Migration Corridor

2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences have identified a new species of dicynodont, an extinct relative of mammals, based on a fossil discovered in Gansu province, China. Named Dinanomodon guoi, this finding marks the first bidentalian genus shared between China and South Africa, suggesting a cross-equator migration corridor during the supercontinent Pangaea. Dicynodonts were herbivorous, mostly toothless animals ranging from small burrowers to large grazers. This significant paleontological discovery provides crucial insights into the ancient distribution and evolutionary history of these unique creatures.
