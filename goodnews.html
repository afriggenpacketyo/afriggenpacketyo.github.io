The History Behind Indianapolis 500 Winners Drinking Milk in Victory Lane

2025-05-22 04:00:52 EDT

The tradition of Indianapolis 500 winners drinking milk started in 1936 when Louis Meyer, after winning his third Indy 500, drank buttermilk, a drink his mother told him would refresh him on a warm day. A cameraman captured the moment, and a dairy industry executive requested that milk be given to every winner. From 1947 to 1954, winners were handed cold water in a silver chalice, but the milk tradition returned in 1956. Since then, 69 consecutive winners have enjoyed milk in the victory circle.
