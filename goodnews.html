Wealthy Investors Utilize ETF Conversions to Defer Capital Gains Taxes on Appreciated Assets

2025-08-21 17:06:58 EDT

A financial strategy known as a '351 conversion' is gaining popularity among wealthy investors as a way to defer taxes on large investment profits. The process allows individuals to contribute highly appreciated stocks from a taxable account into a newly forming exchange-traded fund (ETF) in exchange for shares in that fund. This transaction defers the capital gains tax that would normally be due from selling the original assets.
