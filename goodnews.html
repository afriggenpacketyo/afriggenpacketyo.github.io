Physicists Unravel the Science Behind Draining Liquids in Everyday Life

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Two physicists at Brown University investigated the fluid dynamics of draining liquids, answering a common kitchen question about how long it takes to get the last drop out of bottles. Their research, published in Physics of Fluids, used Navier-Stokes equations and experiments to calculate drainage times for various liquids. They found water drains in seconds, milk in about 30 seconds, olive oil in over nine minutes, and cold maple syrup in hours. The study, inspired by everyday observations, highlights how fundamental physics can explain seemingly simple phenomena, with one physicist even discovering he needed more patience with his wok.
