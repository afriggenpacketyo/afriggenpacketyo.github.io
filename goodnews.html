Experts Explain the Decline of Sexual Desire in Long-Term Relationships

2025-10-19 00:56:00 EDT

It is common for sexual desire to wane in long-term relationships after the initial passionate phase, a phenomenon driven by hormonal changes. According to psychologist Andrea Seiferth, as relationships mature, the bonding hormone oxytocin becomes more prominent, which can dampen sex drive hormones. Research also highlights a discrepancy between physical and psychological arousal, particularly in heterosexual women, which can be influenced by societal messaging and a lack of prioritization of female pleasure. Experts emphasize that open communication, mutual affection, and prioritizing intimacy are key for couples to navigate these changes and maintain a satisfying sexual connection.
