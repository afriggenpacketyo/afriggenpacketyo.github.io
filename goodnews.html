Behavioral Economics Concept 'The Winner's Curse' Explained

2025-11-11 06:30:00 EST

This article explores "The Winner's Curse," a concept in behavioral economics pioneered by Nobel laureate Richard Thaler, which posits that the winner of a competitive auction often overpays for the item. The phenomenon occurs because the winner is typically the person with the most optimistic, and often inaccurate, valuation, while all other bidders value the item for less. Researchers have identified this effect in various markets, from oil drilling rights and book publishing deals to professional sports drafts and corporate acquisitions. To avoid the curse, Thaler advises bidders to consider whether they would still be happy with their purchase if they knew they had paid the highest price.
