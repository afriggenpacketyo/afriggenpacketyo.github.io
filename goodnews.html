2025-09-10 22:48:54 EDT

NASA's Perseverance rover has collected rock samples from a dry Martian river channel that contain the most compelling potential signs of ancient microscopic life found to date. The samples contain organic carbon along with iron phosphate and iron sulfide compounds, which on Earth are byproducts of microorganisms consuming organic matter. Scientists stress that these features could also be formed by non-biological processes and that conclusive analysis must await the samples' eventual return to Earth. Nevertheless, the discovery is considered a significant step forward in the search for extraterrestrial life and helps answer the question of whether we are alone in the universe.
