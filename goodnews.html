High Income Does Not Disqualify Individuals from Medicare Eligibility

2025-09-09 12:50:31 EDT

Eligibility for Medicare is based on age (65 or older) or disability status, not income, meaning you cannot make too much money to qualify for the program. However, higher earners are subject to an Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount (IRMAA), which increases the monthly premiums for Medicare Part B (medical insurance) and Part D (prescription drugs). These surcharges are based on modified adjusted gross income from two years prior. Financial planning strategies, such as strategic retirement account withdrawals and Roth conversions, can help manage these additional costs for retirees.
