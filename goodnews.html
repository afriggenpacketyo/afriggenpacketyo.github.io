Debate Arises Over Origins of American Cowboy After Rubio Credits Spain, Mexico Disagrees

2026-02-23 06:00:00 EST

A debate has ignited over the origins of the American cowboy after Secretary of State Marco Rubio credited Spain, prompting disagreement from Mexico and others. While Spanish and Portuguese explorers introduced horses and cows to the Americas in the 16th century, experts emphasize that the cowboy tradition evolved significantly through the innovations of Mexican vaqueros. These vaqueros, including people of European, Indigenous, and mixed-race heritage, developed crucial riding and roping techniques, gear, and apparel in the New World's distinct environment. Despite their fundamental contributions, the Mexican vaquero is often erased from popular U.S. depictions, even though Spanish-origin terminology persists in cowboy culture.

Chef Joseph ‘Joe’ Randall, “Dean of Southern Cuisine” and Champion of Black Chefs, Dies at 79

2026-02-23 06:00:00 EST

Chef Joseph G. Randall, revered as the "Dean of Southern Cooking" and a tireless champion of Black chefs, has died at 79. A second-generation hospitality professional, Randall mentored young talent and founded the African American Chefs Hall of Fame in 1993 to recognize overlooked culinary professionals. He co-authored "A Taste of Heritage: The New African American Cuisine," a foundational cookbook, and his work was featured at the Smithsonian Museum. Randall sought to differentiate Southern cuisine from soul food, emphasizing the broader influence of African Americans. His legacy of joy, mentorship, and dedication to his craft lives on through his family and the many he inspired.
