New Study Explores Period Blood Test as a Convenient Alternative for Cervical Cancer Screening

2026-02-04 19:48:42 EST

Researchers are investigating the potential of using period blood for cervical cancer screening, proposing it as an accurate and more convenient alternative to current NHS tests, which a significant number of women fail to attend. A study published in The BMJ demonstrated that testing blood collected on a standard sanitary pad performed nearly as well as clinician-collected samples in detecting the disease. This less invasive method could help overcome common barriers to screening, such as fear, pain, discomfort, and cultural factors, thereby improving access for diverse demographics. While promising, experts emphasize the need for further research in more varied populations, noting that this test would primarily benefit menstruating individuals.

Trump Administration Vows Federal Takeover to Accelerate California Wildfire Rebuilding Permits

2026-02-04 19:31:33 EST

During a visit to Pacific Palisades, top White House officials pledged federal intervention to expedite building permits for rebuilding efforts in areas affected by the Los Angeles wildfires. President Trump's recent executive order allows fire victims utilizing federal emergency funds to bypass "unnecessary, duplicative, or obstructive" state and local permitting processes through a "self-certification" tool. This federal initiative highlights a divergence in perspectives, as state and local officials argue that the primary obstacles to rebuilding are insufficient capital and delayed insurance payouts, rather than permitting delays. Governor Gavin Newsom criticized the federal approach, advocating for the release of crucial federal aid funding instead of preempting local permit approval.
