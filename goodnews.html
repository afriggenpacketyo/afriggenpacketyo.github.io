Environmental advocacy group River Action, which initiated the complaint in 2023, characterized the scheme's marketing as "greenwashing" and urged supermarkets to reconsider their reliance on it. Conversely, Red Tractor's CEO Jim Mosley strongly refuted the ruling as "fundamentally flawed," asserting that the scheme's core purpose centers on food safety, animal welfare, and traceability, with environmental standards constituting only a small component overseen by the Environment Agency.

2025-10-14 21:26:52 EDT
