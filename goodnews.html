Scientists Discover Thriving Ecosystem in Deepest Ocean Trenches

2026-05-27 22:00:06 EDT

An international research team, supported by China's crewed submersible Fendouzhe, has uncovered a rich and previously unknown ecosystem thriving on rocks in ocean trenches deeper than 9 kilometers (5.6 miles). This discovery challenges previous beliefs that only sparse life forms like anemones, sponges, or bacteria could survive the extreme conditions of perpetual darkness, near-freezing temperatures, and immense pressure. The team, led by Professor Peng Xiaotong, investigated seven hadal trenches in the Indo-Pacific region between 2020 and 2024, revealing diverse faunas inhabiting these extreme depths. This finding significantly expands understanding of life's adaptability in Earth's most unexplored environments.
