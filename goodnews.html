Explainer: Understanding Debt Cancellation and Who Is Eligible for Relief

2025-10-28 15:14:54 EDT

Debt cancellation, also known as debt forgiveness, is the elimination of a legal obligation to repay some or all of what is owed to a creditor. This form of relief is not widely available but can be accessed through specific programs, legal proceedings like bankruptcy, or direct negotiations. For example, federal student loan forgiveness programs may cancel debt for public service workers, while hospitals sometimes offer charity care to forgive medical bills for low-income patients. Eligibility varies greatly depending on the type of debt and the specific program, often requiring proof of financial hardship, a history of qualifying payments, or meeting certain income thresholds.
