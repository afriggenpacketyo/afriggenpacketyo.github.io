2025-08-23 18:42:59 EDT

Researchers in Tamil Nadu, India, in collaboration with the UK's Face Lab, have digitally reconstructed the faces of two 2,500-year-old male skulls excavated from Kondagai, an ancient burial site near Keeladi. This effort aims to understand the appearance and ancestry of early inhabitants of a previously unknown urban civilization in southern India, dating back to 580 BC. The reconstructions reveal features of Ancient Ancestral South Indians with traces of Middle-East Eurasian and Austro-Asiatic ancestries, suggesting global migration and population mixing. This discovery challenges traditional narratives about the origins of Indian urbanization, which have historically focused on the north.
