Greenland's Size: A Map Distortion Explained

2026-01-07 14:01:59 EST

Amid renewed discussions of President Trump potentially annexing Greenland, this article clarifies the island's actual size and how map projections can be misleading. The common Mercator projection, used in tools like Google Maps, significantly distorts the size of landmasses at higher latitudes, making Greenland appear comparable to Africa, when Africa is vastly larger. While globes offer a more accurate representation, different map projections serve different purposes. The piece explains that while Greenland is the world's largest island, its perceived size on many maps is a cartographic artifact, not a reflection of its true scale relative to other continents.
