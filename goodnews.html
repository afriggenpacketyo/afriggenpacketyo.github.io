Upcoming Total Lunar Eclipse to Create "Blood Moon" Phenomenon

2025-09-05 08:22:00 EDT

A total lunar eclipse, also known as a "blood moon," will be visible on September 7, 2025, for observers in Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia. This celestial event occurs when the Earth passes directly between the sun and the moon, casting a shadow that gives the moon a reddish hue. Unlike a solar eclipse, a lunar eclipse is safe to view with the naked eye. The red color results from sunlight filtering through Earth's atmosphere, which scatters blue light and allows red light to pass through and illuminate the moon's surface.
