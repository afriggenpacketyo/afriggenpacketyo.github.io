An aortic dissection involves a tear in the inner layer of the aorta, the body's main artery, allowing blood to flow between its layers and potentially rupturing. Symptoms often include sudden, severe chest or back pain, described as ripping or stabbing. Rapid diagnosis and emergent surgery are crucial for survival, with treatment varying based on the dissection's location and complications.
