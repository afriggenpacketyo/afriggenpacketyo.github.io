Tiny Zooplankton Play Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Reveals

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research highlights the crucial role of tiny marine animals called zooplankton in combating global warming. These creatures gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the ocean's surface in Antarctica, grow fat, and then sink hundreds of meters into the deep ocean to hibernate. This process effectively locks away vast amounts of carbon, equivalent to the annual emissions of about 55 million cars, preventing it from re-entering the atmosphere. This "seasonal vertical migration pump" is a far more significant climate-regulating mechanism than previously understood, underscoring the importance of these overlooked organisms.
