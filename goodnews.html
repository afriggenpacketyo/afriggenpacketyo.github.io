UK Government Proposes Major Housing Reform to Replace Leasehold with Commonhold Ownership

2026-07-07 19:13:38 EDT

The UK government is proposing a major housing reform to replace new leasehold flats with commonhold, a system where residents collectively own and manage their buildings. This initiative aims to empower homeowners by giving them greater control over shared facilities and costs, addressing the "feudal" nature of the current leasehold system. While commonhold models succeed globally, its previous introduction in England saw limited adoption, partly due to lender reluctance. Significant challenges remain, including compensating existing freeholders and ensuring a cultural shift for residents to become "stewards" of their properties.
