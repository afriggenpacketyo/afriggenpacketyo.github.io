Certain widely prescribed medications can interfere with the body's natural ability to regulate temperature, increasing the risk of heat-related illness during hot weather. Drugs such as specific antidepressants (SSRIs and TCAs), antipsychotics, and heart medications like beta-blockers and diuretics can disrupt processes like sweating and fluid balance. For example, some drugs may reduce sweating, making it harder to cool down, while others can cause dehydration. People taking these medications, especially older adults and those with chronic conditions, are advised to stay hydrated, avoid peak heat, and be aware of the warning signs of heatstroke without discontinuing their prescriptions.
