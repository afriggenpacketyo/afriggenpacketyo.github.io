"Tariff Engineering" Gains Traction as Businesses Seek to Avoid Higher Duties

2025-06-18 23:15:04 EDT

With U.S. President Donald Trump imposing tariffs, businesses are increasingly employing "tariff engineering" to legally reduce their duty liabilities. This practice involves modifying an item's materials, dimensions, or composition to fit into a different harmonized system code with lower tariff rates. Examples include manufacturers bundling steel and aluminum, or hoodie makers switching to cotton to save on duty costs. The practice can be traced back to 1882, and has been ruled legal as long as goods are honestly exposed to customs officers; however, some sectors face challenges due to extensive regulations and the need for certifications and validation.
