New Small Moon Discovered Orbiting Uranus by James Webb Space Telescope

2025-08-19 17:40:00 EDT

NASA has announced the discovery of a new, tiny moon orbiting the planet Uranus, provisionally named S/2025 U1. The satellite, which is only about six miles in diameter, was identified by the James Webb Space Telescope and is the 29th known moon of the ice giant. Its small size is likely why it was missed by previous observations, including the Voyager 2 flyby in 1986. Researchers suggest the discovery could hint at the existence of more small moons, offering new insights into the planet's potentially chaotic evolutionary history. The moon will be officially named later by the International Astronomical Union.
