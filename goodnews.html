Understanding the 7-Year Rule for Credit Card Debt on Your Credit Report

2025-09-30 13:48:22 EDT

The commonly cited "7-year rule" for credit card debt primarily refers to the period that negative information, such as late payments or charge-offs, can remain on your credit report under the Fair Credit Reporting Act. After seven years from the first delinquency, this information must be removed, which can help improve your credit score. However, this rule does not erase the debt itself. Separately, states have statutes of limitations, often around seven years, that prevent creditors from suing you to collect old debts. It is important for consumers to understand that while these protections exist, the underlying financial obligation may technically still exist.
