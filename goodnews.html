Scientists identify key mechanism linking Epstein-Barr virus to Multiple Sclerosis

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Researchers have identified a crucial link between the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) and Multiple Sclerosis (MS), particularly in individuals with the HLA-DR15 gene. The study reveals that EBV can trick the immune system by presenting viral proteins that mimic nerve tissue proteins, leading the immune system to attack myelin sheaths. In individuals with HLA-DR15, the virus can even cause B cells to present myelin protein, effectively programming the body to attack itself. This discovery offers new avenues for understanding and potentially treating MS.
