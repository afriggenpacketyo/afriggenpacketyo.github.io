Understanding eligibility for Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI).

2026-06-10 14:08:30 EDT

Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) provides a financial safety net for workers unable to work due to a qualifying disability. To be eligible, individuals must have a medically determinable condition expected to last at least 12 months or result in death, and be unable to perform substantial gainful activity. Applicants also need sufficient work credits earned through employment. The Social Security Administration uses a strict definition of disability, and eligibility is assessed based on medical condition, work history, and the ability to earn above a certain income threshold. Comprehensive medical documentation and thorough application completion are crucial for a successful claim, and appeals are common after initial denials.
