2026-06-26 21:00:08 EDT

China is simplifying its trade relations with African nations by offering continent-wide market access for coffee, chillies, and cashews, bypassing lengthy bilateral negotiations. This 'green channel' approach, announced by China's General Administration of Customs, applies uniform sanitary and phytosanitary standards across all 53 African countries with diplomatic ties to Beijing. Products meeting baseline requirements for pest risks and safety can now be imported without individual country-specific trade deals. The initiative, launched in May with dried chillies, is driven by growing demand in China for these products and aims to deepen economic ties between China and Africa. This move could set a precedent for how other governments engage in trade with African nations.
