California's 'Mini-Volcanoes' Explained as Spontaneous Combustion, Not Volcanic Activity

2026-04-24 18:18:57 EDT

Reports of "Hope Ranch Volcano" and "Rincon Volcano" along California's coast, where flames and smoke appear to erupt from cliffsides, are clarified by experts as spontaneous combustion, not actual volcanic activity. James Boles, a professor emeritus at UC Santa Barbara, explains that the phenomenon occurs when earth layers shift, allowing oxygen to interact with unstable compounds like iron sulfides in rocks. This oxidation process generates intense heat, causing organic materials in the area to spontaneously ignite. While locals and even firefighters use volcanic terms like "solfatara," geologists emphasize there is no underlying volcano, making the designation geologically inaccurate, despite the recurrent flare-ups since the 1800s.
