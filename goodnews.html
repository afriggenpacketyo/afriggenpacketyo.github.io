Shanghai Scientists Engineer World's First Lab-Grown Heart Pacemaker Using Stem Cells

2026-05-22 22:00:07 EDT

Scientists in Shanghai have achieved a monumental medical breakthrough by successfully creating the world's first laboratory-grown sinoatrial node, which functions as the heart's natural pacemaker, utilizing human pluripotent stem cells. This remarkable, autonomously beating organoid holds immense potential to transform cardiac disease research and significantly advance drug screening methodologies. The sinoatrial node, nestled within the right atrial chamber, is absolutely crucial for maintaining a continuous and rhythmic heartbeat, dictating the contraction of the heart's chambers to ensure efficient blood circulation. This engineered biological pacemaker offers a highly promising new avenue for effectively controlling abnormal heart rhythms and could eventually provide an alternative to traditional electronic pacemakers.
