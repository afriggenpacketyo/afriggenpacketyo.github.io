US-China Research Team Develops Novel Robot Launch Method Using Bubble Cavitation

2025-09-06 23:00:08 EDT

A collaborative US-China research team has developed an innovative method for launching millimeter-scale robots by harnessing the energy from rapidly collapsing vapor bubbles. This groundbreaking technique involves rapidly heating light-absorbing materials with a laser, which causes water droplets on the surface to boil, triggering the formation of vapor bubbles. Once these bubbles grow to a critical point, they suddenly collapse, releasing energy and creating a strong force capable of propelling tiny jumpers up to 1.5 meters into the air. Published in the peer-reviewed journal "Science," the study demonstrates that cavitation can serve as an incredibly efficient launching mechanism for micro-robotics.
