2025-09-26 13:38:00 EDT

The Dutch government has announced it will return over 28,000 fossils, including parts of the historic "Java Man" collection, to Indonesia. These fossils, discovered in 1891 by Dutch anthropologist Eugene Dubois on the island of Java, are among the first known remains of Homo erectus and are crucial for research into human evolution. The decision follows a recommendation from the Dutch Independent Colonial Collections Committee, which concluded the items were likely removed against the will of the local population. This repatriation is part of a broader effort by the Netherlands to return culturally significant items obtained during its colonial era.

2025-09-26 12:57:13 EDT

A retirement income strategy known as the "rule of thirds" is gaining traction as an alternative to traditional methods like the 4% withdrawal rule. This approach involves converting one-third of one's retirement savings into a guaranteed lifetime income product, such as a fixed annuity, to cover essential expenses. The remaining two-thirds of the savings are kept liquid for investment growth, flexible spending, or unexpected costs. The goal is to create a balanced financial foundation that combines the security of a pension-like income stream with the flexibility and growth potential of market investments.
