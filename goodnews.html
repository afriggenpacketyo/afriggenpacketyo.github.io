Physiological Changes Explain Why Alcohol Tolerance Decreases with Age

2025-12-08 05:00:00 EST

Health experts explain that alcohol tolerance diminishes with age due to natural physiological changes in the body. As people get older, liver enzymes that metabolize alcohol become less efficient, and muscle mass, which stores water that dilutes alcohol, is replaced by fatty tissue. These factors cause alcohol to remain in the bloodstream longer and at higher concentrations, leading to more intense effects and worse hangovers from the same amount of drink. For women in perimenopause, alcohol can also exacerbate symptoms like mood swings, anxiety, and sleep disruption.
