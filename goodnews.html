Explainer: Understanding Chronic Venous Insufficiency, President Trump's Recent Diagnosis

2025-07-17 18:31:24 EDT

Following the White House announcement of President Trump's diagnosis, this article explains chronic venous insufficiency (CVI). CVI is a common circulatory condition, particularly in adults over 70, where damaged veins in the legs struggle to return blood to the heart, causing swelling and varicose veins. While generally not a serious threat, it can be painful and lead to complications like skin ulcers if left untreated. According to medical experts, treatment typically involves lifestyle changes like exercise and leg elevation, compression stockings, and in some cases, minimally invasive procedures to close off problematic veins.
