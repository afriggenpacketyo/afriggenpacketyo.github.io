NASA Declares End of Mission for MAVEN Mars Orbiter After Loss of Contact

2026-06-03 17:26:30 EDT

NASA has officially declared the end of its highly successful Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution Mission (MAVEN) after losing contact with the orbiter six months ago. The $582 million probe, launched in 2013, had operated exceptionally well beyond its initial one-year mission, providing extensive and crucial insights into how the solar wind erodes Mars's atmosphere. Despite concerted efforts to remotely reset the spacecraft's computer and reestablish communication, no signal has been received since December 2025. Fragments of recovered data suggest the spacecraft began rotating unexpectedly, which would have led to an immediate power loss. While the mission's operational end is regrettable, MAVEN delivered invaluable data about Mars's past as a warmer, wetter world.
