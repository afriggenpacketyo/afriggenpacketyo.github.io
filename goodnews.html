2025-09-17 19:56:08 EDT

New research has found that wild chimpanzees in Uganda and Côte d'Ivoire consume fruit with a low alcohol content, amounting to the equivalent of nearly two human cocktails per day. Scientists from UC Berkeley measured the ethanol in figs and plum-like fruits favored by the chimps, discovering an average alcohol content of about 0.3% by weight. This study provides the first direct chemical evidence supporting the "drunken monkey" hypothesis, which suggests that human attraction to alcohol may have evolutionary roots in primate fruit-eating habits. The steady, low-dose exposure does not intoxicate the animals but may act as an aperitif, encouraging higher rates of feeding.
