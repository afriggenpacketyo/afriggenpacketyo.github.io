South African Scientists Use Radioactive Isotopes in Rhino Horns to Deter Poaching

2025-08-01 01:20:58 EDT

A new anti-poaching initiative in South Africa, named the Rhisotope Project, involves injecting rhino horns with harmless radioactive isotopes. Led by the University of the Witwatersrand, the project aims to make the horns easily detectable by radiation scanners at borders and airports, thereby disrupting trafficking networks. Researchers have confirmed the process is safe for the animals but effective in triggering security alarms, even when horns are hidden in large shipping containers. This innovative strategy hopes to protect the country's dwindling rhino population, which is heavily targeted for the illegal horn trade.
