A charge-off is merely an accounting entry for the lender, not a legal release of debt, meaning the balance is still owed. Creditors may continue collection efforts in-house, sell the debt to third-party collectors, or ultimately sue for repayment, potentially leading to wage garnishment. The ability to sue depends on the state's statute of limitations, typically 3-6 years, which can be reset by acknowledging the debt.
