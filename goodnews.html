Ancient Silk Road Tombs in China Added to UNESCO World Heritage List

2025-07-12 05:00:08 EDT

A collection of ancient tombs located along the historic Silk Road in northwest China has been officially recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site. The site, situated near the city of Yinchuan, dates back to the Western Xia dynasty, which was ruled by the Tangut people between 1038 and 1227. The vast 40-square-kilometer area includes nine imperial mausoleums and over 270 subordinate tombs. According to the World Heritage Committee, the site serves as a significant testament to the cultural fusion and interaction among diverse traditions that characterized the era, preserving a unique piece of history.
