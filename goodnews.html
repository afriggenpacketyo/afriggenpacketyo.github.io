In an interview, Mollick explained that the primary challenge is not learning to use AI, but developing the expertise needed to oversee it, as traditional apprenticeship paths may disappear. A Historical and Cultural Look at the Evolution of Fountains

This article explores the long and varied history of fountains, from their practical origins in ancient civilizations to their role as modern urban spectacles and cooling systems. Fountains first appeared nearly five millennia ago for practical and ritualistic purposes in Minoan Crete and ancient Egypt. During the Renaissance and Baroque periods in Europe, they evolved into grand symbols of power and artistry, such as Bernini's Fountain of the Four Rivers in Rome. Today, while some fountains are massive tourist attractions, many cities are adapting them with misting nozzles to provide relief from increasingly intense heatwaves, blending ancient purpose with modern necessity.
