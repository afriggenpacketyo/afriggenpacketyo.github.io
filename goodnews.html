2025-11-24 08:00:01 EST

Portfolio strategists are cautioning savers that while holding cash can feel secure, it fails to grow wealth and loses purchasing power over the long term due to inflation. Financial planners advise that while an emergency fund is essential, excess cash should be invested to outpace inflation and achieve long-term goals like retirement. Financial Advisors Highlight Common and Costly Mistakes with Year-End Retirement Withdrawals

2025-11-24 07:00:01 EST

As the Dec. 31 deadline approaches, financial experts are reminding retirees about the importance of correctly taking their required minimum distributions (RMDs) from pretax retirement accounts. Failing to withdraw the full, calculated amount can result in a significant IRS penalty of up to 25% of the shortfall. Common mistakes include waiting until the last minute, forgetting about old 401(k) or inherited IRA accounts, and miscalculating the withdrawal amount. Advisors also note that many retirees underutilize qualified charitable distributions (QCDs), a strategy that allows direct tax-free donations to nonprofits while satisfying RMD requirements.
