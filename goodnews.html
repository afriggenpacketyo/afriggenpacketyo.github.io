Explaining the Mechanics and Consequences of a U.S. Government Shutdown

2025-10-13 08:59:25 EDT

A government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass the necessary appropriations bills to fund federal agencies before the fiscal year begins on October 1. This forces all non-essential government functions to cease until a funding agreement is reached, as federal law prohibits spending without congressional authorization. Essential employees, such as military personnel and air traffic controllers, must continue to work but do not receive pay until the shutdown ends. Programs with permanent funding, like Social Security and Medicare, continue to issue payments, but administrative services can be delayed due to staff furloughs, affecting the public in various ways.
