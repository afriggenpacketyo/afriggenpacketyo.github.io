New York City Implements Ranked Choice Voting in Mayoral Primary

2025-06-23 00:04:00 EDT

New York City is using ranked choice voting in its Democratic mayoral primary, a system where voters rank multiple candidates in order of preference. This system ensures that even if a voter's top choice doesn't win, their other rankings still influence the outcome. The process involves eliminating the candidate with the fewest first-choice votes in rounds and redistributing those votes to the voters' next choices until a candidate secures a majority. While this system aims to promote broader support for the winner and prevent fringe candidates from winning with a simple plurality, it also introduces complexity and potential transparency concerns. Results may take longer to finalize due to the need to tabulate mail-in ballots and conduct multiple rounds of analysis, potentially leading to questions about the accuracy of the vote sorting software.
