Pearl Harbor Sailor Accounted For Eight Decades After Attack

2026-03-14 11:40:29 EDT

The remains of U.S. Navy Seaman 1st Class Clyde C. McMeans, who tragically died aboard the USS California during the 1941 attack on Pearl Harbor, have been officially identified over eight decades later. McMeans, aged 26, was one of 103 crewmen lost when the battleship was torpedoed and bombed, catching fire and slowly sinking. His identification through DNA and forensic analysis brings long-awaited closure to his family, who expressed profound sentiment, stating they "loved him forever, without ever knowing him." A funeral service with full military honors is scheduled for May 1 in Corpus Christi, Texas, honoring his sacrifice.
