2025-07-29 20:08:07 EDT

Firefighters place victims with dangerously high core temperatures into large plastic bags filled with ice and water to rapidly cool them down. This technique, originally developed by the military, can cool patients three to five times faster than traditional methods, potentially preventing brain damage and organ failure. The program's expansion, which saw a drop in heat-related deaths last year, is setting a national example for emergency response in a warming climate.
