UK Clocks to Go Back on October 26, Ending British Summer Time.

2025-10-11 19:32:29 EDT

The clocks in the UK are scheduled to go back by one hour on Sunday, October 26, 2025, signaling the end of British Summer Time (BST) and a return to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). This annual shift, occurring at 02:00 BST, grants most people an extra hour but extends night shifts. The change brings earlier sunsets, making nights draw in noticeably faster. Originating during World War One to conserve fuel, the practice continues to be debated, with arguments for permanent summer time citing economic benefits and reduced accidents, while opponents highlight the extremely dark winter mornings, especially in Scotland.
