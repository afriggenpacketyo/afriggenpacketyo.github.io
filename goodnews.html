China cultivates giant wheat-rye hybrids in desert regions for resilience

2026-05-29 00:00:08 EDT

China is cultivating triticale, a hybrid of wheat and rye, in its western deserts to overcome challenging environmental conditions. Triticale is noted for its tolerance to poor soil, cold, drought, and salinity, making it more adaptable than traditional wheat. Beyond human consumption, its stalks and leaves can be used for animal feed. This initiative aims to make agriculture profitable in newly reclaimed desert lands, where ordinary wheat struggles to sprout.
