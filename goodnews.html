Scientists Transplant Crossbred Corals in Miami to Combat Climate Change Effects

2025-07-04 12:54:01 EDT

In an innovative effort to save Florida's devastated reefs, scientists are transplanting fragments of crossbred coral onto a reef near Miami. The project, a collaboration between the University of Miami, The Florida Aquarium, and a Honduran organization, involves crossbreeding Florida's native Elkhorn corals with a more resilient strain from the warmer waters of Honduras. The goal is to create a more "thermally tolerant" coral population capable of surviving rising ocean temperatures that have caused widespread bleaching and killed over 95% of Florida's Elkhorn corals. This marks the first time an international crossbreeding effort has been permitted for planting in the wild.
