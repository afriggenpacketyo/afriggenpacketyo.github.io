Peak of Perseid Meteor Shower in August to be Hindered by Bright Moon

2025-07-29 06:00:00 EDT

The annual Perseid meteor shower, one of the most popular celestial events, is expected to peak on the night of August 11-12. However, this year's viewing experience will be significantly compromised by a bright, waning gibbous moon, which will be about 92% full. The moonlight will wash out the fainter meteors, drastically reducing the visible count from the typical 50 to 100 per hour. Stargazers are still encouraged to seek out dark sky locations away from city lights, with the best hope for a good show resting on the possibility of seeing fireballs, which are larger and brighter meteors characteristic of the Perseids.
