The word "hoax" has its origins in the magician's incantation "hocus pocus," a phrase popularized in the 1620s as the stage name of a performer named William Vincent. The term "hocus pocus" itself may be a deliberate, playful corruption of the Latin Eucharistic prayer, "Hoc est enim corpus meum." Over time, the phrase became a shorthand for magic and trickery, and the shortened form "hoax" emerged around 1800 to describe a deception or fraud, evolving from stage magic to elaborate public deceptions.
