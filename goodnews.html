Understanding the Income Requirements for Filing Chapter 7 Bankruptcy

2025-10-21 14:43:58 EDT

Qualifying for Chapter 7 bankruptcy, which can eliminate unsecured debts, is primarily determined by an individual's income relative to their state's median for their household size. If an applicant's income is below this median, they typically pass the "means test" automatically. However, those with income above the median may still qualify after a more detailed calculation that subtracts allowable living expenses to determine disposable income. For individuals who do not qualify, alternatives like Chapter 13 bankruptcy, debt settlement programs, or debt management plans offer other paths to financial relief without the same long-term credit impact.
