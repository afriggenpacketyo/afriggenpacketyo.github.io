Viral Images of 'Zombie-Rabbits' with Horn-like Growths Are Real, Caused by Shope Papilloma Virus

2025-08-13 20:33:00 EDT

Viral photos of rabbits in Colorado with black, horn-like growths have sparked alarm, but these "zombie-rabbits" are real and afflicted by the relatively harmless Shope Papilloma Virus. Colorado Parks and Wildlife explains the growths, or papillomas, are common in the Midwest and spread by fleas, ticks, or rabbit-to-rabbit contact, but not to humans or pets. The papillomas are generally not harmful unless they impede eating or vision, and disappear once the rabbit's immune system fights the virus. This condition is believed to have inspired the mythical "jackalope" and has contributed to scientific understanding of virus-linked cancers.
