Palaeontologists Discover New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species in Thailand

2026-07-10 17:30:57 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, identified from fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This long-necked sauropod, estimated to have measured up to 20 meters (66 feet) in length, is believed to have lived approximately 150 million years ago. The discovery, originating from a substantial fossil collection at the Phu Noi site, was confirmed through a distinctive dorsal vertebra. A CT scan revealed its unique air-cavity structure and Y-shaped supporting bones, differentiating it from other members of the Mamenchisauridae family, predominantly found in China. This finding represents a significant paleontological advancement for Thailand.
