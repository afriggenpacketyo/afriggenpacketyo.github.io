Civil Rights Pioneer Claudette Colvin Dies at 86

Claudette Colvin, a pivotal yet often overlooked figure in the US civil rights movement, has passed away at the age of 86. At just 15 years old, she bravely refused to yield her seat to a white woman on a segregated bus in Montgomery, Alabama, in March 1955, an act of defiance that occurred several months before Rosa Parks' more widely publicized protest. Her courageous stand led to her arrest and subsequent involvement as one of four Black female plaintiffs in a successful lawsuit that ultimately challenged and overturned bus segregation laws across the US. Despite being marginalized early in her life, Colvin eventually received belated recognition for her significant contributions, with her 1955 arrest record expunged in 2021.
