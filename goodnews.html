Oldest Pterosaur Fossil in North America Discovered in Arizona

2025-07-07 15:00:00 EDT

Researchers have identified the fossil of a new pterosaur species in Arizona's Petrified Forest National Park, which is now the oldest known flying reptile ever found in North America. The creature, named Eotephradactylus mcintireae, lived approximately 209 million years ago during the late Triassic period. The fossil, a fragile jawbone, was discovered among thousands of others at a rich bone bed. Scientists say the pterosaur was about the size of a small seagull and likely fed on hard-shelled prey. The discovery helps fill a significant gap in the fossil record and provides new insights into the evolution of these early flying animals.
