Authorized Users Not Legally Responsible for Primary Credit Card Holder's Debt

2026-07-20 11:01:05 EDT

It is generally the case that an authorized user on a credit card cannot be legally held responsible for the primary cardholder's unpaid debt, as they do not sign the contractual agreement with the issuer. The primary account holder assumes sole contractual obligation to repay the balance, meaning creditors typically pursue them, not the authorized user, when debts lead to collections or lawsuits. For primary cardholders struggling with payments, exploring debt relief options such as debt settlement, consolidation loans, or even bankruptcy early on can help prevent escalation and mitigate the financial impact on all parties connected to the account.
