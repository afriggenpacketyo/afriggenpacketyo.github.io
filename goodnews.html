Bernard LaFayette, Pivotal Selma Voting Rights Organizer and Nonviolence Advocate, Dies at 85

2026-03-06 19:17:58 EST

Bernard LaFayette, a key figure in the Selma voting rights campaign that led to the 1965 Voting Rights Act, has passed away at 85 from a heart attack. As an advance man, LaFayette laid the groundwork for the movement in Selma, facing dangers including an assassination attempt. He was a co-founder of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee and a close associate of Martin Luther King Jr., dedicating his life to institutionalizing and internationalizing the nonviolence movement. His quiet, persistent work and commitment to human spirit over retaliation were foundational to significant civil rights advancements.
