New Study Suggests Elevated Body Temperature Alone Can Help Combat Viruses

2025-11-27 14:00:00 EST

A recent study provides new evidence on how fevers help the body fight infections, a topic debated since ancient times. Researchers found that in mice, elevated ambient temperature alone was sufficient to combat an influenza virus that was not adapted to heat. To isolate the effect of heat from the immune response, they used a heat-tolerant bird flu virus as a control. The findings, published in Science, support the hypothesis that the heat of a fever directly harms some pathogens, rather than only stimulating the immune system. This reinforces the idea that fever is an evolved defense mechanism.

Archaeologist Believes Decades-Old Mystery of Peru's 'Band of Holes' is Solved

2025-11-27 12:01:32 EST

A long-standing archaeological puzzle, the 'Band of Holes' in Peru's Pisco Valley, may finally be solved. Archaeologist Charles Stanish proposes that the thousands of man-made pits were initially a pre-Inca marketplace and later adapted by the Inca civilization as a sophisticated agricultural storage and accounting system. Using drone mapping and microbotanical analysis of sediment, his team found that the holes were arranged in structured patterns mirroring Incan counting devices and contained fossilized seeds of crops and reeds used for packaging. This evidence strongly suggests a human-made, logistical purpose, debunking more fanciful theories of their origin.
