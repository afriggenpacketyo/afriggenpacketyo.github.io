China's Price-Undertaking Deals Mitigate Trade Disputes with South Korea and EU

2026-02-28 03:00:09 EST

China is effectively utilizing price-undertaking agreements to de-escalate international trade tensions, most recently resolving a dispute with South Korea concerning hot-rolled steel coils, mirroring a similar successful deal with the European Union on electric vehicles. This strategy involves Chinese exporters committing to increase their export prices, which in turn helps to circumvent the imposition of punitive anti-dumping duties and preserves crucial market access for Chinese goods. Analysts, including John Gong, view these "soft landing" solutions as beneficial because they allow the price premium to remain with the exporting firms rather than being absorbed by importing nations as tariffs, thereby fostering greater stability and predictability in bilateral trade relations.
