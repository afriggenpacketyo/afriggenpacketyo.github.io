Neuroscientist Explains the Science of Limerence and How to Overcome Romantic Obsession

2025-09-20 05:00:00 EDT

Neuroscientist Tom Bellamy's work explores "limerence," a term for intense romantic infatuation characterized by obsessive thoughts and emotional highs and lows. While this state can be a normal part of early love, it can become a detrimental "person addiction" when a relationship is uncertain or unattainable, leading to anxiety and craving. Bellamy suggests that overcoming unhealthy limerence involves recognizing it as an internal mental state and taking active steps to break the cycle. Strategies include limiting contact, reframing positive daydreams into negative scenarios, and focusing on new personal goals to create a sense of purpose outside the obsession.
