Chinese Study Develops Low-Cost Method to Convert Plastic Waste into Jet Fuel

2026-07-19 22:00:08 EDT

A joint team from the Shanghai Advanced Research Institute and Fudan University in China has developed a low-cost method to convert plastic waste into jet fuel. Their research focuses on the hydrogenolysis of polyolefins, which constitute over 60% of plastic waste and are notoriously difficult to break down. This innovative reaction operates under relatively mild conditions, allowing for tunable product selectivity to steer the conversion towards desired hydrocarbon ranges. The breakthrough utilizes metal catalysts to selectively cleave and rearrange carbon-carbon bonds, addressing a fundamental challenge in plastic recycling and offering a sustainable fuel source with significant environmental benefits.
