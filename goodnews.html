The U.S. has confirmed its first human case of New World screwworm, a flesh-eating parasite, in a Maryland patient who traveled from El Salvador. While the immediate public health risk is low, this parasitic fly, whose larvae feed on warm-blooded animals, poses a significant threat to livestock. Historically eradicated from the U.S. in 1966 using a sterile insect technique, the screwworm has recently spread northward through Central America and Mexico, prompting renewed federal action. The USDA plans to build a sterile fly production facility in Texas and increase border surveillance to prevent a potentially devastating economic impact on the cattle industry, estimated at over $10.6 billion.
