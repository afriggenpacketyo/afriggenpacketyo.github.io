Rare Blue Micromoon to Appear This Weekend, Not Returning Until 2028

2026-05-28 10:24:57 EDT

A rare blue micromoon will be visible this weekend, offering a celestial spectacle not to be seen again until the end of 2028. This event combines two astronomical phenomena: a blue moon, which is the second full moon in a single calendar month, and a micromoon, which occurs when a full moon is at its farthest point from Earth. While the moon will not appear blue in color, its occurrence is notable. For observers in the Southern Hemisphere, the moon may appear to obscure the bright star Antares, adding an extra layer of visual interest. The subtle differences in size and brightness compared to an average full moon may be difficult for casual observers to notice.
