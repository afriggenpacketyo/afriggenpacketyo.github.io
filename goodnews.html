New Chinese Fossil Discovery Illuminates Avian Tail Evolution

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A significant fossil discovery in China is shedding new light on the evolutionary transition of birds from dinosaurs, specifically detailing how they gradually lost their long tails. Researchers published findings in Science Advances, illuminating the step-by-step development of the pygostyle. This unique skeletal structure, formed by fused tail vertebrae, is crucial for modern bird flight as it serves as an anchor for tail feathers and muscles. Understanding this transformation has been challenging due to the scarcity of early bird and bird-like dinosaur fossils. This breakthrough enhances our comprehension of one of the most profound morphological changes in terrestrial vertebrate history, offering critical insights into avian body plan assembly.
