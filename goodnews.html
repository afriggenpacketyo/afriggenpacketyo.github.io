Constitutional Experts Debate Presidential War Powers in Light of Iran Strikes

2025-06-23 18:05:51 EDT

President Trump's decision to launch airstrikes on Iran's nuclear facilities without congressional approval has reignited the debate over the division of war powers between the executive and legislative branches. Article I of the Constitution grants Congress the power to declare war, while Article II designates the president as Commander in Chief. While presidents have historically engaged in military actions without formal declarations of war, the War Powers Resolution of 1973 sought to reassert congressional authority, though its effectiveness has been debated. Some legal scholars argue the recent strikes against Iran were unconstitutional without congressional authorization, while others suggest that informing Congress fulfills the resolution's requirements.
