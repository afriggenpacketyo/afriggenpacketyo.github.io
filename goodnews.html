Understanding Monthly Payments in Chapter 13 Bankruptcy

2026-02-02 12:04:30 EST

Chapter 13 bankruptcy offers individuals with steady income a court-supervised plan to restructure and repay debts over three to five years, allowing them to retain assets. Monthly payments are highly variable, typically ranging from $500 to $600 for moderate debt, but can be as low as $200-$300 for limited incomes or exceed $1,500-$3,000 for higher earners or those with substantial mortgage arrears. Key factors determining the payment include disposable income, the type and amount of debt (secured, priority, unsecured), the value of non-exempt assets, and the overall length of the repayment plan.
