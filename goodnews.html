New Report Highlights Financial Burden of Urban Sprawl on American Cities

2026-06-03 08:30:00 EDT

A recent report from the Pew Charitable Trusts and other organizations argues that American cities incur significant, often underappreciated, costs due to sprawling housing development. The research, which simulated construction scenarios across ten diverse states, found that infill development within existing communities requires approximately $21,000 less in upfront infrastructure expenses per home compared to building at the urban fringe. Furthermore, ongoing maintenance for infrastructure was about 50% less for infill, while these communities generated 13% more in property taxes per acre. The article advocates for policies that encourage denser, more fiscally efficient growth to help cities remain solvent and potentially lower property taxes.
