Helsinki Achieves Zero Traffic Fatalities Through Smart Urban Planning and Reduced Speed Limits

2025-08-18 03:29:00 EDT

The city of Helsinki, Finland, has successfully eliminated traffic-related deaths over the past year, becoming one of the largest cities to achieve this milestone. A key factor in this success has been the widespread implementation of a 30 km/h (18.6 mph) speed limit on more than half of the city's streets, particularly around schools. This initiative is part of the European Union's "Vision Zero" program, which aims to virtually eliminate road fatalities by 2050. Helsinki's strategy also involves data-driven infrastructure improvements, enhanced speed enforcement with fixed cameras, and promoting public transportation to make streets safer for all residents.
