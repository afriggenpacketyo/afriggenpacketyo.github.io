Scientists Uncover Mechanism Linking Epstein-Barr Virus and Multiple Sclerosis Development

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Scientists have made significant progress in understanding how the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) contributes to multiple sclerosis (MS) in certain individuals, despite nearly everyone being infected with EBV. The research indicates that in people with a specific genetic molecule, HLA-DR15, EBV-infected B cells can present viral structures that mimic a protein found in nerve fiber myelin sheaths. This "fatal similarity" causes the immune system to mistakenly attack its own nerve tissue. While HLA-DR15 doesn't guarantee MS, it is identified as the most crucial genetic risk factor, with infections during adolescence and environmental factors also playing roles. Efforts are now focused on developing targeted treatments and early childhood vaccinations to prevent the disease.
