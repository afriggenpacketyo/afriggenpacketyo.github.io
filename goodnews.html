Cultural and Legal Differences Explain Why American Bread Differs from European Counterparts

2025-10-20 08:00:00 EDT

The perception that bread in Europe, particularly France, tastes better than in the United States is rooted in differing production methods and legal standards. In 1993, France passed a law dictating that bread sold in a traditional boulangerie must be made fresh daily with only four ingredients: flour, water, leavening, and salt. In contrast, mass-produced American bread, like Wonder Bread, often contains over 30 ingredients, including conditioners and preservatives, to facilitate rapid, industrial-scale production. This focus on speed and convenience in the U.S. comes at the expense of the complex flavors that develop through slower, traditional fermentation processes.
