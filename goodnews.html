The word "hoax," used to describe grand deceptions and frauds, has a fascinating origin rooted in the world of magic. Etymologists trace the term to a clipped version of "hocus pocus," an incantation popularized in the 1620s by a magician named William Vincent, who used it as his stage name. The phrase "hocus pocus" itself is theorized to be a deliberate, playful corruption of the Latin Eucharistic prayer, "Hoc est enim corpus meum" ("This is My body"), reflecting the anti-Catholic sentiment in England at the time. Over time, the magician's brand name became shorthand for trickery, eventually evolving into the modern word "hoax" around the 19th century.
