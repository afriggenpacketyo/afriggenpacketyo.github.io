Long-Lost Rubens Masterpiece "Christ on the Cross" Sells for $2.7 Million

2025-11-30 18:29:37 EST

A Peter Paul Rubens painting, "Christ on the Cross," lost for over 400 years, sold for 2.3 million euros ($2.7 million) at an Osenat auction in Versailles. Completed in 1613, its existence was known only through engravings until auctioneer Jean-Pierre Osenat discovered it in a Paris townhouse in September 2024 during a routine inspection. Initially suspected to be from a Rubens workshop, scientific analysis and expert authentication by the Centrum Rubenianum confirmed it as an original masterpiece. The painting, described as being in "very good condition," was last known to be owned by 19th-century French painter William Bouguereau, marking a significant and exciting rediscovery in the art world.
