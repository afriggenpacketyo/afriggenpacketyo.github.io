New Fossil DNA Study Suggests Horses Originated in North America and Migrated Via China to Europe

2026-06-19 23:22:54 EDT

A new fossil DNA study challenges the traditional belief that horses were a European import to the Americas, instead suggesting they originated in North America millions of years ago. The research indicates that an extinct lineage, the Dalian horse found in northeastern China, served as an unexpected genetic intermediary in their global spread. This Dalian horse, once considered a local oddity, possessed distinctive American ancestry and subsequently passed it on to ancient horse populations in Siberia. Consequently, the bloodlines that later gave rise to modern European horses acquired their American roots through this Chinese crossroads, significantly revising established evolutionary narratives about equine migration.
