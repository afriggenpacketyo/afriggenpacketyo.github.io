First Bacteria Encountered at Birth Can Protect Against Hospitalization, Study Finds

2025-06-04 20:48:13 EDT

UK scientists have discovered that the first bacteria our bodies encounter after birth can protect against dangerous infections. The study showed that good bacteria, specifically Bifidobacterium longum, can halve the risk of young children being admitted to the hospital with lung infections. Researchers at University College London and the Sanger Institute analyzed stool samples from 1,082 newborns and tracked their health for two years. Babies with B. longum were significantly less likely to require hospitalization for lung infections. The protective bacteria were found only in babies delivered vaginally, suggesting early microbial encounters are crucial for immune system development.
