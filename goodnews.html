Financial Experts Suggest Strategies for Retirement Withdrawals When the Money Isn't Needed

2025-08-12 09:15:01 EDT

Retirees who must take required minimum distributions (RMDs) from their retirement accounts but do not need the income have several strategic options. Financial planners suggest reinvesting the funds in a taxable brokerage account, often using tax-efficient assets like exchange-traded funds (ETFs). Another popular option for those aged 70½ or older is a qualified charitable distribution (QCD), which allows a direct, tax-free transfer to a nonprofit that can satisfy the RMD. For those focused on legacy planning, using the RMD to contribute to a 529 college savings plan can also provide state tax benefits. (not investment advice)
