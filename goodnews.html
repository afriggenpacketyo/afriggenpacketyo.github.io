China Cultivates Resilient Wheat-Rye Hybrids in Western Deserts for Food Security

2026-05-29 00:00:08 EDT

China is actively cultivating triticale, a resilient wheat-rye hybrid, in its western deserts to bolster national food security. This synthetic hybrid demonstrates superior adaptability to challenging environmental conditions, including poor soil, cold, drought, salinity, and wind and sand. Triticale can produce significant forage yields, up to 4 tonnes per mu annually, making it an ideal crop for newly reclaimed desert saline-alkali land where traditional wheat struggles to grow profitably. First bred in the late 19th century, triticale provides both food for humans and valuable animal feed from its stalks and leaves, offering a sustainable and productive solution for agricultural expansion in arid regions.
