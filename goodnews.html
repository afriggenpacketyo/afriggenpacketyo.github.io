Long-Lost Rembrandt Painting Authenticated After 65 Years

2026-03-02 08:03:32 EST

Rembrandt's "Vision of Zacharias in the Temple," painted in 1633, has been rediscovered and authenticated by experts after being lost to public knowledge for 65 years. Excluded from the Dutch master's official works in 1960 and sold to a private collector in 1961, the painting resurfaced when its owners presented it to the Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam for examination. A two-year analysis confirmed its authenticity through material analysis, stylistic and thematic similarities, and the artist's signature. The article notes the painting's high quality and innovative depiction of the biblical scene, marking it as a significant work from Rembrandt's early career.
