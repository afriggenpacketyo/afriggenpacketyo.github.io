Health officials and scientific studies confirm there is no evidence to support this assertion. The rising global incidence of skin cancer is attributed to factors like better diagnostics, increased sun exposure, and historically inadequate sun protection products, not the use of modern sunscreens. While countries with high sunscreen use, like Australia, also have high melanoma rates, this correlation is explained by other factors, including greater awareness and a sun-intensive climate, rather than a causal link to sunscreen itself.
