Germany Considers Easier Process for Declaring Countries Safe for Asylum Seekers

2025-06-04 15:29:00 EDT

The German government is exploring a simplified process to designate countries of origin as safe, aiming to decrease the number of asylum seekers. Chancellor Merz's Cabinet has approved a draft policy that would allow a country to be declared safe through a simple statutory order, without requiring the consent of the Bundesrat. Interior Minister Alexander Dobrindt stated that this measure is part of a broader set of national initiatives to implement an "asylum turnaround." Under German asylum law, safe countries of origin are those where individuals are not subject to political persecution or inhuman punishment, and migrants from these countries are less likely to have their asylum applications approved.
