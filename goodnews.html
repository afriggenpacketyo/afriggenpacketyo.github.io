Explainer: The Science Behind How Tsunamis Form and Travel

2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

Using the recent 8.8-magnitude earthquake off Russia's coast as an example, this article explains the science of tsunamis. These powerful waves are typically generated by large, shallow underwater earthquakes that cause vertical movement of the seafloor, displacing massive volumes of water. Tsunamis travel at jet-plane speeds in the deep ocean but slow down and increase dramatically in height as they approach shallower coastal waters, which is when they become most dangerous. While forecasting remains challenging, scientists use a network of tide gauges and deep-ocean pressure sensors to better predict a tsunami's arrival time and potential impact, urging people to always heed official warnings.
