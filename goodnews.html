The Tradition of Drinking Milk in the Indianapolis 500 Victory Circle

2025-05-22 04:00:52 EDT

The tradition of Indianapolis 500 winners drinking milk began in 1936 when Louis Meyer drank buttermilk after his third victory, a moment captured by a cameraman and promoted by the dairy industry. While winners from 1947 to 1954 were handed water, the milk tradition returned in 1956, and since then, 69 consecutive winners have enjoyed milk in the victory circle. The tradition reflects a blend of personal preference, historical accident, and marketing savvy, solidifying milk as an iconic symbol of the Indy 500 celebration. The winner of the race will be given an ice-cold bottle of milk in the victory circle.
