2025-10-16 06:31:50 EDT

Uruguay's senate has passed a law to decriminalize euthanasia, making it the first country in Latin America to do so via legislative action. The law permits a healthcare professional to perform euthanasia for mentally competent adult patients suffering from an incurable illness that causes "unbearable suffering," without requiring a terminal diagnosis. This move reinforces Uruguay's reputation as one of the most socially liberal nations in the region, having previously legalized recreational marijuana, same-sex marriage, and abortion. While the Catholic Church voiced strong opposition, the country's secular culture allowed the bill to pass with a significant majority after five years of debate.
