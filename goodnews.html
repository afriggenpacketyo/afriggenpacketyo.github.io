Scotland Legalizes Water Cremations, Offering Environmentally Friendly Funeral Alternative

2026-03-02 01:10:44 EST

Scotland has made history by becoming the first country within the UK to legalize water cremations, formally known as alkaline hydrolysis, thereby offering families an environmentally conscious alternative to conventional burial or cremation. This innovative process entails dissolving body tissue in a pressurized chamber containing hot water and chemicals, which accelerates decomposition and leaves behind only bone fragments. These fragments are then rinsed, dried, and pulverized into a coarse powder, which is returned to relatives in an urn. Public Health Minister Jenni Minto lauded this as an "environmentally friendly alternative," assuring that it will adhere to the same stringent regulatory requirements as existing methods. The first procedures are anticipated to commence by summer, following the necessary equipment installation.
