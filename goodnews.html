The Science and Lore Behind the Aurora Borealis and Australis

2025-12-29 03:25:00 EST

The captivating aurora borealis and australis, vibrant lights dancing across polar skies, are caused by solar storms ejecting charged particles that interact with Earth's atmosphere. Different atmospheric elements, primarily oxygen (green) and nitrogen (purple, blue, pink), determine the colors observed, with rare interactions creating intense scarlet hues. While usually confined to polar regions, recent solar maximums have expanded their visibility significantly. Research also investigates potential ozone depletion from these solar energy particles, though the damage is temporary. Auroras have inspired diverse myths and legends across cultures for millennia, from Norse Valkyries to Finnish fox fire.
