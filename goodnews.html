2025-07-10 21:11:51 EDT

Sotheby's is auctioning the largest known piece of Mars on Earth, a meteorite named NWA 16788, with an expected price of up to $4 million. The 54-pound rock, which represents about 6.5% of all known Martian material on our planet, was discovered in Niger in 2023 after traveling 140 million miles through space. Martian meteorites are exceedingly rare, comprising only about 0.6% of all meteorites found on Earth. The auction house is promoting the object not just as a collector's item but as a significant dataset that could help scientists better understand the red planet.
