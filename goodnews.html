Debunking the Myth: Debts Are Not Automatically Forgiven After Seven Years

2025-11-10 11:41:43 EST

This article clarifies a common misconception about debt, explaining that debts are not legally forgiven after seven years. The seven-year timeline pertains to the Fair Credit Reporting Act, which requires most negative items like late payments and collections to be removed from a consumer's credit report. However, the underlying debt remains legally valid, and creditors can continue to pursue collection efforts. The article distinguishes this from the statute of limitations, which varies by state and determines the period during which a creditor can sue a borrower over unpaid debt.
