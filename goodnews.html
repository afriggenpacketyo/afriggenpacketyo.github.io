2026-04-29 14:45:22 EDT

The UK has abolished the right of hereditary peers to sit in the House of Lords, ending a 700-year-old system. While most hereditary peers lost their seats in 1999, the remaining 92 have now been removed by a new law, fulfilling a Labour election manifesto commitment. Lord Forsyth of Drumlean, the Lord Speaker, thanked them for their service, acknowledging their "ethos of service" and independence of mind.
