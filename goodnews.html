New Guidelines Recommend One-Time Lipoprotein(a) Test for Heart Disease Prevention

2026-03-13 16:56:19 EDT

New guidelines from the American Heart Association and American College of Cardiology recommend a one-time lipoprotein(a) blood test for all adults to assess genetic risk for heart disease. This aims to expand cardiovascular risk assessment tools, as elevated lipoprotein(a) levels signal an inherited risk for heart attacks and strokes. The guidelines also advocate for wider use of coronary calcium scoring and the PREVENT risk assessment tool, encouraging earlier and more aggressive treatment, including statins, even for those with relatively low risk but high lifetime risk profiles. This "sea change" could lead to millions more being treated, potentially reducing cardiovascular disease.
