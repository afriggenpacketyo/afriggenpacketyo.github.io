Understanding Phantom Energy and Its Environmental Impact

2025-09-03 11:04:07 EDT

Phantom energy, also termed vampire energy, describes the electricity consumed by devices when they are plugged in but not actively in use. This silent drain can account for 5% to 10% of a home's total energy consumption, originating from common items like phone chargers, microwaves, and smart televisions with constant connectivity features. Experts highlight that reducing this wasted electricity, by simply unplugging devices or disabling smart features, offers a dual benefit: significant savings on individual utility bills and a collective contribution to reducing planet-warming emissions. Such small, consistent actions, when adopted widely, are crucial for fostering more sustainable energy practices and combating climate change.
