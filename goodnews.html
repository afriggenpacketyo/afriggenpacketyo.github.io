Inspired by personal experiences of trying to extract the last drops of milk or olive oil, and a professor's curiosity about his cast-iron wok, their research utilized Navier-Stokes equations to precisely measure fluid motion. Experiments validated their hypotheses, demonstrating that low-viscosity liquids such as milk drain within approximately 30 seconds, whereas thicker olive oil requires over nine minutes, and cold maple syrup can take several hours.
