These units were then subsequently occupied by individuals moving from even lower-cost homes, with the chain extending to potentially creating vacancies in homeless shelters. This "trickle-down" phenomenon, also observed in other international studies, indicates that market-rate construction not only contributes to public coffers through taxes but also increases overall housing mobility.
