NASA Deactivates Instrument on Voyager 1 to Extend Mission Life

2026-04-19 01:40:28 EDT

NASA has deactivated one of Voyager 1's science instruments, the Low-energy Charged Particles (LECP) experiment, to conserve power and extend the iconic probe's nearly 50-year mission. Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 is the most distant human-made object, now over 15 billion miles from Earth, operating on diminishing radioisotope thermoelectric generator power after far exceeding its initial five-year design. This strategic shutdown, part of a long-term conservation plan, aims to keep at least one instrument operational into the 2030s, allowing the spacecraft to continue sending invaluable data from interstellar space, a scientific feat unmatched by any other mission. Engineers are also developing a "Big Bang" plan to further optimize power.
