The 2025 Nobel Prize in chemistry has been awarded to Susumu Kitagawa, Richard Robson, and Omar M. Yaghi for their pioneering work in the development of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs). The Nobel committee highlighted the vast potential of MOFs, which can be custom-designed for a wide range of applications. These uses include capturing carbon dioxide, harvesting water from desert air, storing toxic gases, and catalyzing chemical reactions, offering innovative solutions to significant global challenges.
