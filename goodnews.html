A new law described as the most significant reform of England's private rental sector in a generation has been formally approved, aiming to provide greater security and rights for millions of tenants. The Renters' Rights Bill abolishes "Section 21 no-fault evictions," meaning landlords can no longer evict tenants without a specific reason. It also ends fixed-term tenancies, replacing them with rolling periodic agreements that tenants can end with two months' notice. The law will also ban rent bidding wars, limit rent increases to once per year, and make it illegal for landlords to discriminate against tenants with children or those receiving benefits.
