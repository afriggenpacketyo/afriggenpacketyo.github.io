The Unexpected Benefits of Embracing Boredom for Mental Well-being and Purpose

2026-03-22 10:15:25 EDT

Author Arthur C. Brooks argues that while humans have largely eliminated boredom through constant device use, this has inadvertently created a "major crisis" by suppressing the brain's "Default Mode Network." This network is crucial for mind-wandering, abstract thinking, and contemplating life's meaning. Constant distraction, he explains, leads to a sense of emptiness, a lack of purpose, and can contribute to anxiety and depression. Brooks suggests reintroducing silent spaces into daily life—like walking without a phone or having phone-free mealtimes—to reactivate this network. He posits that embracing boredom, initially challenging, ultimately fosters calmness, peace, and a richer sense of purpose, making life more interesting.
