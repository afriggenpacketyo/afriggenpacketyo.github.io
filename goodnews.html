Vinyl Records Exempt from New Tariffs, Aiding Music Industry's Physical Media Boom

Vinyl records, CDs, and cassettes have been spared from the Trump administration's rollback of the "de minimis" exemption, which now applies tariffs to many imported goods valued under $800. The exemption for physical music is due to the Berman Amendment, a Cold War-era law that protects the flow of "informational materials." This is welcome news for consumers and the music industry, as vinyl sales have surged in recent years, now accounting for nearly three-quarters of all physical music revenue. The decision prevents a potential price hike on records, which could have dampened the format's popular resurgence.
