Focal Therapy Shows Fewer Side Effects for Prostate Cancer, Study Suggests

2026-07-16 19:36:40 EDT

A 10-year NHS study involving nearly 3,500 men suggests that focal therapy for prostate cancer is as effective as surgery or radiotherapy but with significantly fewer side effects, such as urinary incontinence and loss of sexual function. The treatment, which uses high-intensity ultrasound or cryotherapy to target cancerous tissue, is not suitable for all patients. Researchers advocate for wider availability of focal therapy, noting that current access is limited, creating a "postcode lottery" for patients.
