The Peak District: From Devil's Arse to National Park, 75 Years of Tourism

2026-04-18 19:36:46 EDT

The Peak District, Britain's first national park, celebrates 75 years, with its history as a tourist attraction dating back centuries. Early visitors were well-heeled gentry, but the advent of railways in the 19th century opened the area to mass tourism, making it accessible for day-trippers from cities. A pivotal moment was the 1932 Kinder Scout mass trespass, a protest for public access to private moorland, which significantly contributed to the eventual creation of the 1949 National Parks and Access to the Countryside Act. Today, the National Trust is the largest landowner, and the park, welcoming about 13 million visitors annually, continues to balance conservation with public enjoyment.
