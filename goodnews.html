A University of Georgia geologist examined fragments of the space rock, which originated from a larger meteor that exploded over the state in June. The analysis suggests the meteorite belongs to a group of asteroids from the main belt between Mars and Jupiter, likely from a larger asteroid breakup 470 million years ago. Scientists have proposed naming it the McDonough Meteorite, marking the 27th such recovery in Georgia.
