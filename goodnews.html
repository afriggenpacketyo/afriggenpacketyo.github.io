Researchers Investigate Why Electric Vehicles May Cause More Motion Sickness

2025-06-29 22:00:13 EDT

As electric vehicles (EVs) become more common, a growing number of passengers are reporting motion sickness, a phenomenon researchers are now studying. Studies suggest that the unique acceleration and deceleration characteristics of EVs are more likely to induce queasiness than conventional cars. A key factor is the regenerative braking system, which converts kinetic energy back into electrical energy when slowing down. This process can result in low-frequency deceleration that is known to provoke the uncomfortable symptoms of motion sickness in passengers.
