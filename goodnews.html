Supreme Court Limits Liability for Internet Providers in Copyright Infringement Cases

2026-03-25 12:27:16 EDT

The Supreme Court unanimously ruled that internet service providers (ISPs) are generally not liable for contributory copyright infringement, even if they know users are downloading copyrighted material. This decision overturned a $1 billion verdict against Cox Cable, which had been sued by Sony for music piracy. Justice Clarence Thomas stated that merely providing a service to the public, with knowledge of some users infringing copyrights, is insufficient to establish liability. The ruling differentiates from past cases against services like Grokster and Napster, emphasizing that ISPs do not intend for their service to be used for infringement nor do they tailor it for such purposes, potentially impacting future legal challenges against online platforms.
