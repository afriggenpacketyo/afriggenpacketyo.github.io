Understanding Pentecost: The Christian holiday's origins and traditions

2026-05-22 11:56:00 EDT

Pentecost, also known as Whitsun or Trinity Sunday, marks the descent of the Holy Spirit upon Jesus' Apostles 50 days after Easter, symbolizing the birth of the Christian Church. Its roots lie in the Jewish Feast of Weeks (Shavuot), a harvest festival. The event is described as a 'miracle of understanding' where disciples, speaking different languages, could communicate with each other, inspiring thousands to be baptized. Traditions associated with Pentecost include the color red symbolizing the Holy Spirit's fire, and in some cultures, the scattering of rose petals from church ceilings to represent tongues of flame. The holiday's abstract nature and lack of tangible secular traditions make it less widely known than other Christian celebrations.
