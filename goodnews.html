Explainer: Why Cities Experience Higher Temperatures During Heatwaves

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As a heatwave affects the UK, this article explains the "urban heat island effect," the phenomenon causing cities to be significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. Hard, dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain solar energy, releasing it slowly and elevating temperatures, especially at night. The lack of vegetation and water, which provide natural cooling in the countryside, exacerbates the effect. With climate change making heatwaves more frequent and intense, the growing populations of urban centers are particularly vulnerable to extreme heat and its associated health risks.
