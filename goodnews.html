Understanding Rule 523: Debts Not Discharged in Bankruptcy Proceedings

Rule 523 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code specifies certain debts that cannot be legally eliminated through bankruptcy, applying to both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 filings. Common non-dischargeable debts include most student loans, recent tax debts, child support, alimony, and debts from fraud or willful misconduct, setting a high bar for exceptions like "undue hardship" for student loans. Given these limitations and the long-term credit impact of bankruptcy, individuals are encouraged to explore alternatives like debt settlement for unsecured debts such as credit card balances and medical bills.
