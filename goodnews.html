Influential German Philosopher Jürgen Habermas Dies at 96

2026-03-14 10:39:00 EDT

Jürgen Habermas, the world-renowned German philosopher and sociologist, has died at 96, leaving behind a legacy as one of the 20th century's most influential thinkers. Active until his final years, Habermas championed cosmopolitan ideals, advocating for asylum rights and a unified European Union against nationalism. His groundbreaking work, "The Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere," explored the evolution of public opinion in democracies. Habermas's theories, which emphasized communication and consensus, profoundly shaped post-war German self-image and influenced movements like the 1968 student protests, promoting open and rigorous democratic debate.
