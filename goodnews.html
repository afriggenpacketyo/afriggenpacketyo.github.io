Library of Congress Unveils Rare Draft of Declaration of Independence in New Exhibit

2026-07-11 22:30:23 EDT

A new Library of Congress exhibit, "The Declaration's Promise," showcases a rare original draft of the Declaration of Independence, penned by Thomas Jefferson. Visitors can observe significant edits by Benjamin Franklin and John Adams, such as changing "subjects" to "citizens," reflecting the nation's foundational break from monarchy. The exhibit highlights the evolution of core principles, including "all men are created equal," acknowledging its initial limited application while emphasizing its enduring power to inspire movements for equality. The collection also features historical documents from Abraham Lincoln, Susan B. Anthony, and Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., illustrating America's ongoing journey toward realizing its ideals.
