Pioneering Black Journalist John Edward Bruce to Receive Headstone 101 Years After Burial

2025-08-04 20:06:00 EDT

John Edward Bruce, a prominent Black journalist and Pan-Africanist pioneer born into slavery, will finally receive a headstone at his unmarked grave in Yonkers, New York. Known as "Bruce Grit," he founded several newspapers and was a leading voice in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, but died without the funds for a proper grave marker in 1924. A century later, a coalition of private and public donors, organized by the urban think tank RiseUp, has funded a black granite headstone to honor his legacy. The effort aims to rectify a historical oversight and celebrate a figure who dedicated his life to preserving and acknowledging Black history and culture.
