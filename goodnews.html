Physics Study Reveals Optimal Waiting Times for Draining Liquids from Containers

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Two physicists from Brown University have investigated the everyday question of how long one needs to wait to drain the last drops of liquid from containers, publishing their findings in Physics of Fluids. Their experiments and calculations, using Navier-Stokes equations, determined the time required for various liquids like milk, olive oil, and water to drain from an inclined surface. The study found that thicker liquids like olive oil require significantly longer waiting periods, and even residual water in a wok can take up to 15 minutes to collect sufficiently for draining. The research highlights the application of fluid mechanics in common household scenarios.
