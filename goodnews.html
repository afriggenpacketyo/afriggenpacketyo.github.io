Palaeontologists Announce Discovery of New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species in Thailand

2026-07-10 17:30:57 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new dinosaur species, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This plant-eating dinosaur, estimated to have lived 150 million years ago, featured an unusually long neck and measured up to 20 meters. The identification was made from a distinctive dorsal vertebra, revealing it belongs to the Mamenchisauridae family, known for their elongated necks. This finding marks the first of its kind in Thailand, with unique characteristics, including a specific air-cavity structure, setting it apart from other known dinosaurs.
