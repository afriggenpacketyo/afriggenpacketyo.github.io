Claudette Colvin, Civil Rights Pioneer Who Preceded Rosa Parks, Dies at 86

2026-01-21 08:28:01 EST

Claudette Colvin, a pivotal yet largely unsung figure in the U.S. civil rights movement, has died at 86. In March 1955, at age 15, she was arrested for refusing to give up her bus seat to a white passenger in Montgomery, Alabama, nine months before Rosa Parks' more widely recognized protest. Colvin later became one of four plaintiffs in the landmark Browder v. Gayle federal lawsuit, which ultimately led to the Supreme Court upholding the unconstitutionality of bus segregation. Her courage laid crucial groundwork for the Montgomery Bus Boycott, though she received less public recognition due to societal perceptions at the time.
