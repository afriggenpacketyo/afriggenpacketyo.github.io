The Genesis of Football's Penalty Shootout in 1970

2026-03-28 02:15:22 EDT

The article revisits the historic introduction of football's first official penalty shootout on August 5, 1970, during a Watney Cup match between Manchester United and Hull City. Prior to this, tied games were resolved by coin tosses or replays, a system deemed "immoral and cruel" by Israeli Football Association official Yosef Dagan, who proposed the shootout to FIFA. The inaugural event saw legendary players like George Best and Denis Law participate, with Hull City's goalkeeper Ian McKechnie becoming the first to save and then miss a penalty, a moment that stayed with him for life. This innovation transformed how tied matches were decided, adding a new layer of nerve-shredding drama.
