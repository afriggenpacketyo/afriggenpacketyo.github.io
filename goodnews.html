NASA's Van Allen Probe A Re-enters Earth's Atmosphere After Extended Mission

2026-03-11 18:20:47 EDT

A 1,300-pound NASA satellite, Van Allen Probe A, successfully re-entered Earth's atmosphere over the eastern Pacific Ocean on Wednesday, nearly 14 years after its initial launch. The probe, launched in August 2012, far exceeded its planned two-year mission, operating for almost seven years to explore Earth's permanent radiation belts. Most of the spacecraft was anticipated to incinerate upon re-entry, with NASA assessing a very low risk of harm to individuals on Earth. Throughout its extended mission, the probe yielded significant discoveries, notably confirming the existence of a third radiation belt, profoundly advancing our understanding of space weather.
