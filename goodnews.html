Pine Martens Reintroduced to Exmoor National Park After a Century-Long Absence

2025-10-07 01:17:55 EDT

For the first time in 100 years, pine martens are once again present in Exmoor National Park following a successful reintroduction effort by the Two Moors Pine Marten Project. Conservationists released 19 of the mammals, sourced from Scottish populations, at undisclosed locations within the park last month. Each pine marten is fitted with a radio collar to allow researchers to monitor their progress and adaptation to the new environment. The project is seen as a hopeful sign for nature restoration, as the native species plays a critical role in balancing the woodland ecosystem.

2025-10-06 22:07:49 EDT

California has passed a new law that mandates streaming services like Netflix, Hulu, and YouTube to regulate the volume of their commercials. The legislation requires that advertisements do not play at a significantly louder level than the program content, extending a rule that has applied to broadcast and cable television since 2010. The bill was inspired by a legislative aide's complaint about loud ads waking his sleeping baby. Since many major streaming platforms are based in California, this state law could effectively set a new national standard for ad volume, bringing what Governor Gavin Newsom called "much-needed peace and quiet" to households.
