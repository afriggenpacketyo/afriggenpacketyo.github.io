For homeowners looking to borrow $150,000, the initial monthly payments for a home equity loan and a home equity line of credit (HELOC) are currently almost identical due to similar average interest rates. However, the long-term cost can differ significantly because home equity loans have fixed rates, providing predictable payments, while HELOCs have variable rates that fluctuate with market conditions. A drop in market rates could make a HELOC cheaper over time, whereas a rise would increase payments.
