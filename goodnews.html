China Completes 'Green Great Wall' Project to Combat Desertification in Inner Mongolia

2025-07-09 00:00:08 EDT

China has completed a massive 1,856-kilometer "green great wall" in Inner Mongolia, a significant milestone in its fight against desertification. The project, finalized on Sunday, creates a green belt that effectively contains the Badain Jaran, Tengger, and Ulan Buh deserts. Chinese engineers used methods like creating straw checkerboards to stabilize shifting sands, followed by planting drought-resistant vegetation like sacsaoul trees. This long-term ecological engineering project is a key part of China's broader strategy to build a robust sand prevention and control system and mitigate the environmental impact of its vast deserts.
