Understanding the Distinctions Between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 Bankruptcy

2026-02-24 11:51:47 EST

This article elucidates the core distinctions between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy, the primary legal avenues for individuals grappling with unmanageable debt. Chapter 7, often termed "liquidation bankruptcy," typically facilitates the discharge of most unsecured debts within three to six months, subject to a means test and potential liquidation of non-exempt assets. In contrast, Chapter 13, known as "repayment bankruptcy," involves restructuring debt into a court-approved three-to-five-year plan, which allows individuals to safeguard assets like their homes and address secured debt arrears. The optimal choice hinges on an individual's income, asset protection priorities, and the specific nature of their outstanding debts. (not investment advice)
