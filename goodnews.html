US Transportation Department Endorses More Realistic Female Crash Test Dummy

2025-11-21 03:57:03 EST

The U.S. Department of Transportation has endorsed a new crash test dummy designed to more accurately represent the female body, a move advocates say will significantly improve car safety for women. Data shows women are 73% more likely to be injured and 17% more likely to be killed in a car crash than men, partly because standard dummies are based on an average male physique. The new design incorporates differences in the neck, pelvis, and overall body shape, and is equipped with over 150 sensors. Once a final rule is adopted, the new dummy will be considered for use in the government's five-star vehicle safety ratings.
