Aurora Borealis Expected to Recur Across UK Following Strong Solar Activity

2026-03-21 04:57:17 EDT

The Northern Lights, also known as the Aurora Borealis, created a spectacular display across the UK on Friday night, captivating observers with its vibrant colors. This rare visibility, extending as far south as Norfolk, was a direct consequence of a powerful geomagnetic storm caused by a coronal mass ejection from the Sun. As charged particles interacted with Earth's atmosphere, oxygen and nitrogen atoms produced the stunning green, red, blue, and pink hues. Forecasters are optimistic about another chance to witness this natural phenomenon on Saturday night, with geomagnetic activity expected to remain strong across Scotland, Northern Ireland, and northern England and Wales, potentially even further south.
