Tiny Zooplankton Play a Major Role in Storing Atmospheric Carbon in the Deep Ocean

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny marine creatures called zooplankton are crucial in mitigating global warming by locking away vast amounts of carbon. These animals gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the ocean's surface in Antarctica, grow fat, and then migrate deep into the ocean to hibernate, burning the fat and releasing carbon dioxide far from the atmosphere. This seasonal migration process sequesters an estimated 65 million tonnes of carbon annually, equivalent to the emissions of 55 million cars. The study highlights the underappreciated but vital role these small organisms play in regulating the planet's climate.
