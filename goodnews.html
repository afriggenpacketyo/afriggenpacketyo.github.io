This community science effort, which complements technology-based street tree counts, involves volunteers meticulously identifying species, measuring trunks, and grading tree health. The data collected is crucial for informing urban planning initiatives, such as increasing canopy cover to 30% by 2035 and mitigating heat burdens in disadvantaged neighborhoods. The census also tracks progress in diversifying tree species, a vital strategy against infestations and environmental disturbances, fostering community engagement and environmental stewardship and ensuring each tree is managed individually.

U.S. Banking Sector Shrinks, Raising Questions About Stability and Competition

2025-08-28 09:20:56 EDT

The U.S. banking industry is shrinking, with nearly half of FDIC-insured banks disappearing since 2005 due to large bank mergers, raising concerns about market domination. In contrast, Canada's highly concentrated banking system, with only 79 banks, was named the soundest in the world during the 2008 financial crisis. While Canada's large banks benefit from collective economic strength, former FDIC chair Sheila Bair cautioned that market domination can stifle competitive pricing, innovation, and spirit. With the Federal Reserve proposing lower capital requirements for big banks, further consolidation in the U.S. is anticipated, prompting a reevaluation of banking stability and competition, and a look at lessons from Canada's model.
