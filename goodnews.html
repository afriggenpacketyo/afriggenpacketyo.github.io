The first of three supermoons for the year is set to appear, offering a celestial event where the moon looks slightly larger and brighter than usual. This astronomical phenomenon occurs when a full moon coincides with its closest approach to Earth in its elliptical orbit, a point known as the perigee. According to NASA, this proximity can make the moon appear up to 14% bigger and 30% brighter than when it is at its farthest point in orbit. The spectacle is visible worldwide to the naked eye, provided the skies are clear, with the next two supermoons expected in the subsequent months of the year.

2025-10-06 07:17:35 EDT

The 2025 Nobel Prize in medicine has been awarded to Mary E. Brunkow, Fred Ramsdell, and Dr. Shimon Sakaguchi for their groundbreaking discoveries related to peripheral immune tolerance. Their collective work identified and explained the function of regulatory T cells, or T-regs, which act as a crucial check on the immune system to prevent it from attacking the body's own tissues. Dr. Sakaguchi first discovered this T cell subtype, while Brunkow and Ramsdell later identified the Foxp3 gene that controls their development. This research has opened a new field in immunology and is now being used to develop innovative treatments for autoimmune diseases and cancer.
