The Value of Therapy Sessions When There's 'Nothing to Talk About'

2026-04-10 06:30:00 EDT

Sessions with a therapist that feel uneventful can still be incredibly insightful and beneficial, according to experts. These seemingly quiet appointments can strengthen the therapeutic bond, allow therapists to observe clients during periods of calm, and uncover underlying issues that may have been overlooked. Therapists are trained to guide conversations even when clients feel they have nothing to discuss, using subtle cues to explore deeper concerns. Maintaining regular sessions, even the less turbulent ones, is crucial for overall mental well-being and can help prevent minor issues from escalating into larger problems.

AI Chatbots for Psychiatric Medication Refills Spark Debate on Healthcare Access and Safety

2026-04-10 06:26:30 EDT

A pilot program in Utah is utilizing AI chatbots to facilitate the renewal of certain psychiatric medications, aiming to speed up the process and reduce costs. The system, from Legion Health, only renews low-risk, previously prescribed medications for stable patients, with safeguards including human review for concerning responses. However, many psychiatrists express concerns about the reliance on self-reported data and whether AI can truly handle the nuances of psychiatric care. Supporters highlight the potential to improve access in underserved areas, while critics worry about the depersonalization of care and the risk of reducing a deeply personal process into a software-driven transaction.
