However, Colorado Parks and Wildlife officials have clarified that the animals are not "zombies" but are suffering from Shope Papilloma Virus. The disease, spread by ticks and fleas, causes keratinous tumors that are usually harmless unless they impede eating or vision. The virus is not transmissible to humans or other pets and is believed to have inspired the myth of the "jackalope." The growths typically disappear once the rabbit's immune system fights off the infection.
