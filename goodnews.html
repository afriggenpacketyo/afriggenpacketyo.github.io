This initiative, inspired by "Benedict's Law" after five-year-old Benedict Blythe died from an allergic reaction at school, aims to improve allergy safety and ensure consistent protections. While some schools already have plans, a 2024 freedom of information request revealed 70% lacked recommended safeguards. The government hopes these compulsory measures will save lives, keep more children in school, and foster a collective responsibility for allergy management.
