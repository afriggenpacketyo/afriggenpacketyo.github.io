Rare Blue Micromoon to Appear This Weekend, Not Returning Until 2028

2026-05-28 10:24:57 EDT

A rare blue micromoon will be visible this weekend, offering skywatchers a celestial event not to be seen again until December 2028. A blue moon is defined as the second full moon within a single calendar month, and this particular one is also a micromoon, meaning it is at its farthest point from Earth in its orbit, appearing smaller and dimmer. While the moon will not appear blue in color, the occurrence of two full moons in one month is uncommon. For observers in the Southern Hemisphere, the moon will also appear to obscure the bright star Antares. The subtle differences in size and brightness may be difficult for casual observers to notice without specialized viewing.
