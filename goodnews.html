2026-04-21 06:30:00 EDT

This article delves into the concept of monopsony power, where a single buyer (employer) dominates a market and can suppress wages. Economist Joan Robinson coined the term in the 1930s, but it remained a fringe idea until recent research by Arindrajit Dube highlighted its widespread presence. Dube argues that factors like employer concentration, search frictions for workers, and job differentiation contribute to monopsony power, leading to stagnant wages and increased income inequality.
