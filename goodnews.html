Study Finds Young Chimpanzees Learn Communication Patterns Primarily from Their Mothers

2025-08-16 06:00:00 EDT

New research on wild chimpanzees in Uganda reveals that young chimps acquire their patterns of combining vocalizations and gestures predominantly from their mothers and maternal relatives. The study, published in PLOS Biology, found no similar correlation with paternal communication styles, suggesting the behavior is learned through social interaction rather than genetics. This maternal transmission of communication traits mirrors how human children learn from their primary caregivers. Researchers suggest this capacity for social learning in communication may be an ancient trait, dating back at least to the last common ancestor of humans and chimpanzees.
