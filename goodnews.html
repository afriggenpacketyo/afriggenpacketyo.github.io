UK Clocks to Go Back on Last Sunday of October, Ending British Summer Time

2025-10-11 19:32:29 EDT

The clocks in the UK will go back by one hour on the last Sunday of October, marking the end of British Summer Time (BST) and the return to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). This annual shift, which occurs at 02:00 BST on October 26, grants most people an extra hour. The practice of Daylight Saving Time, initiated by Germany in 1916 during World War One to save fuel, has continued in the UK, though debates persist about making BST permanent. Arguments include economic boosts and reduced road accidents, but also concerns about gloomy winter mornings, particularly in Scotland.
