California Beaches Prepare for Annual Grunion Spawning Phenomenon

2026-07-14 18:11:20 EDT

California's beaches are set to host the annual grunion run, a unique natural wonder where small, silver fish come ashore to spawn. Occurring over four-day periods from March to August, usually around new and full moons, female grunion bury themselves in the sand to lay eggs while males fertilize them. This open-air mating ritual, a summer tradition for many, is a rare spectacle, with the second and third nights of the run typically offering the best viewing opportunities. Conservation efforts protect the species, with a catch season currently open allowing hand-only collection for licensed individuals.
