Yangtze River Fishing Ban Leads to Biomass Surge and Porpoise Recovery

2026-02-20 23:00:09 EST

A comprehensive fishing ban implemented on China's Yangtze River in 2021 has yielded remarkable environmental benefits, leading to a significant surge in biomass and a notable recovery for the critically endangered finless porpoise population. The initiative involved the monumental task of recalling over 111,000 fishing vessels and resettling 231,000 fishers, supported by an investment exceeding US$2.7 billion. This conservation effort was further strengthened by the Yangtze River Protection Law, which introduced stricter regulations on water quality, sand mining, and shoreline restoration. This success highlights a powerful example of large-scale ecological restoration in one of the world's most vital and biodiverse river systems.
