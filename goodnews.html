Research Reveals Tiny Zooplankton Play a Major Role in Storing Atmospheric Carbon

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research indicates that tiny marine creatures called zooplankton are "unsung heroes" in the fight against climate change, locking away vast amounts of carbon in the deep ocean. These animals feast on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface in Antarctica before migrating deep underwater, where they burn their fat reserves, effectively sequestering the carbon for centuries. Scientists calculate this process removes as much carbon from the atmosphere as the annual emissions of 55 million cars, a far greater amount than previously understood. The discovery highlights the critical but often overlooked role of these creatures in regulating the global climate.
