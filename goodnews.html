NASA Sets March 6 for Historic Artemis II Crewed Moon Orbit Mission

2026-02-20 11:11:18 EST

NASA is targeting March 6 (March 7 in the UK) for the launch of its Artemis II mission, which will send four astronauts on a 10-day journey around the Moon. This mission marks the first time humans will orbit the Moon in over 50 years, and it is designed to pave the way for future lunar landings as part of the broader Artemis program. The launch date was confirmed following a successful "wet dress rehearsal," a crucial pre-launch test involving fueling the rocket and running through the countdown sequence at Kennedy Space Center in Florida. This ambitious undertaking represents humankind's furthest trip into space to date, signifying a major step in lunar exploration.
