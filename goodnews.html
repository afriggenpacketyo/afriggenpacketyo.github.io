Archaeologists Discover 3,500-Year-Old Rice Remnants in Guam

2025-07-14 14:03:58 EDT

Archaeologists have uncovered the earliest known evidence of rice in Remote Oceania, finding 3,500-year-old remnants in Guam. The discovery was made at the Ritidian Site Complex in the form of phytoliths, which are fossilized plant parts, found exclusively on ancient earthenware pottery. This finding suggests the rice was part of specialized cultural rituals within a cave that also contained rare rock art and formal burials. The evidence indicates that the first settlers of Guam successfully transported and cultivated rice after a record-setting ocean migration of over 2,300 kilometers.
