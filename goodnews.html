Chinese Researchers Make First Direct Observation of Quantum Friction Effect

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A team of researchers in China has for the first time directly observed quantum friction between two solid surfaces, a breakthrough with significant implications for materials science. The experiment, conducted with folded graphene, revealed a surprising effect where friction did not increase with thickness and, in some cases, decreased. This phenomenon was traced to changes in electron movement within the bent material, which made it harder for motion to be converted into heat. The findings, published in Nature Communications, could pave the way for designing ultra-efficient, low-power nanodevices with minimal wear and tear.
