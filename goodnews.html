Chinese Researchers Make First Direct Observation of Quantum Friction in Graphene

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A team of scientists from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has become the first to experimentally observe quantum friction between solid surfaces. While studying folded graphene, they discovered a counterintuitive effect where friction did not increase with the material's thickness, and in some cases, thicker folds slid more easily. This phenomenon was traced to how bending the material altered its electronic properties, restricting the conversion of motion into heat. The breakthrough, published in Nature Communications, could pave the way for developing ultra-efficient nanodevices with minimal wear.

2025-07-24 20:54:07 EDT

The center processes images of about three million letters daily, with operators keying in corrected address information at a rate of 900 pieces per hour. This crucial human intervention ensures that even the most illegibly addressed mail has a chance of reaching its destination.
