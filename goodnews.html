Pakistan's Murree Brewery Resumes International Beer Exports After Nearly 50-Year Ban

2026-01-31 05:03:00 EST

Murree Brewery, Pakistan's oldest and most renowned brewery, has recommenced exporting beer after a nearly 50-year prohibition on alcoholic exports was lifted. Founded in the 19th century and acquired by a Parsi family in 1947, the company navigated decades of domestic alcohol bans for Muslims, continuing sales to religious minorities and foreigners with permits. The export ban was finally rescinded in 2022 due to a significant change in Pakistan's export policy, allowing alcohol exports to countries outside the Organisation of Islamic Cooperation, a move primarily driven by the government's need for increased revenue. Initial shipments have successfully reached the UK, Portugal, and Japan, with the brewery now looking to expand its international sales to other major markets, including the United States and Canada, marking a notable economic and cultural shift for the nation.
