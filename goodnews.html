Infrared Contact Lens Enables Humans to See in the Dark

2025-06-01 08:43:00 EDT

Chinese researchers have developed an infrared contact lens that converts infrared light into visible light, enabling humans to see in the dark. The lenses combine conventional soft contact lenses with nanoparticles consisting of gold, sodium gadolinium fluoride, ytterbium, and erbium ions. The upconversion contact lenses (UCLs) convert infrared light with wavelengths between 800 and 1,600 nanometers into visible light. While the resulting images are somewhat blurred, the lenses allow users to recognize patterns, letters, and flashing infrared signals in the dark, even with closed eyes. This innovation holds potential applications in surgical procedures, encryption, and rescue operations. The innovation provides new possibilities for human vision.
