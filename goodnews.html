US teen birth rates reach historic low in 2025, CDC reports

2026-04-09 00:01:00 EDT

The teenage birth rate in the United States continued its decades-long decline, reaching a historic low in 2025 with a 7% decrease from the previous year, according to a report by the National Center for Health Statistics. The rate for females aged 15 to 19 was 11.7 births per 1,000, a significant drop from 61.8 in 1991. The overall birth rate also saw a slight decrease, while preterm birth rates remained stable. The report notes an increase in cesarean delivery rates. Experts attribute the decline in teen births to factors such as increased contraceptive use, lower sexual activity among youth, and continued access to abortion care. While a declining birth rate can be viewed positively, experts emphasize the need for continued support for young parents.
