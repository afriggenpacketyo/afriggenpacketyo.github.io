New Dicynodont Fossil in China Reveals Ancient Cross-Equator Migration Route

2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences have identified a novel species of dicynodont, an extinct non-mammalian therapsid, which they named Dinanomodon guoi. Discovered in Gulang county, Gansu province, China, this fossil represents the first bidentalian genus found in both China and South Africa. This remarkable discovery provides compelling evidence for an ancient cross-equator migration corridor that existed during the era of the supercontinent Pangaea. Dicynodonts, considered evolutionary "cousins" to modern mammals, were a diverse group of mostly toothless, herbivorous animals. This finding significantly enhances understanding of prehistoric animal distribution and continental drift.
