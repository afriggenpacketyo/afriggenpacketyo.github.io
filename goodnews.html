Created on October 11, 1974, the preserve was the result of a compromise among conservationists, hunters, and local Indigenous tribes to protect the 729,000-acre swamp from a proposed jetport development. The area is a crucial watershed for the Everglades and is home to diverse wildlife, including the endangered Florida panther. This unique designation allows for both environmental protection and specific recreational activities, drawing approximately one million visitors each year.
