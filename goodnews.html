Berlin's Weimar Era: A Hub for Early Gay Rights and Queer Culture

2026-04-11 02:34:00 EDT

Berlin in the 1920s, during the Weimar Republic, was a significant global center for early LGBTQ+ research, activism, and community building, shaping modern understandings of sexuality and gender. This era saw the rise of Magnus Hirschfeld, a physician who founded the Scientific-Humanitarian Committee in 1897, advocating for gay rights and challenging Paragraph 175, which criminalized sexual acts between men. Hirschfeld's Institute for Sexual Science further advanced progressive ideas on sexuality and gender identity. Despite legal risks and prejudice, Berlin's vibrant queer scene, with its clubs and publications, flourished until the Nazi rise to power in 1933, which led to the destruction of Hirschfeld's institute and the persecution of gay men.
