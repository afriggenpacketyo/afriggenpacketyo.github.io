Young students show gains in reading and math, but older students lag

2026-06-10 00:01:00 EDT

New federal test scores indicate that younger students are demonstrating improvements in reading and math after a period of decline, offering a hopeful outlook for educational progress. The long-term trend (LTT) report from the National Center for Education Statistics shows that 9-year-olds have made solid gains across the board, including lower-performing students. This improvement may be linked to their experiencing more in-person schooling during critical early years. However, the results for 13-year-olds present a less optimistic picture, with no significant improvement in reading or math scores compared to the previous assessment. Reading scores for this age group remain below pre-pandemic levels, and math progress has been largely erased. The report also highlights a significant decrease in the number of students reading for pleasure. These findings underscore a growing disparity in educational outcomes between younger and older students.
