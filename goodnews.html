Australia Seizes Over 100,000 Illegal Exotic Cockroaches Valued at $200,000

2026-06-04 21:26:28 EDT

Australian authorities have seized more than 100,000 illegal exotic cockroaches, with an estimated value of $AU200,000, from a commercial breeder located in Bathurst, New South Wales. The significant haul included Madagascar hissing cockroaches and dubia cockroaches, both of which are prohibited from import, keeping, breeding, or selling in Australia due to their potential to spread disease and harm native wildlife and agriculture. This operation represents the largest seizure of illegal exotic invertebrates to date. The Department of Climate Change, Energy, Environment and Water announced that the seized insects, often used as food for pet reptiles, will be destroyed, and warned pet businesses and owners of federal penalties for possessing or trading such species.
