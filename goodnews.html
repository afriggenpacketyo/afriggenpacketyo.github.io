Homeowners can simultaneously have both a home equity line of credit (HELOC) and a home equity loan, provided they have sufficient equity and meet lender requirements. This strategy allows borrowers to combine the predictable, fixed-rate lump sum of a home equity loan with the flexible, variable-rate access of a HELOC. Lenders typically cap the combined loan-to-value ratio at 80-90% and require strong credit to manage both payment obligations. This approach can be strategic for homeowners who need to consolidate debt with a fixed loan while maintaining a flexible credit line for future or unexpected expenses.
