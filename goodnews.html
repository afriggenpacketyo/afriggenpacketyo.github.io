South African Scientists Use Radioactive Isotopes in Rhino Horns to Deter Poaching

2025-08-01 06:00:37 EDT

In a novel anti-poaching effort, South African scientists are injecting rhino horns with a harmless radioactive material to make them easily detectable by customs officials worldwide. The initiative, known as the Rhisotope Project, aims to disrupt the smuggling of horns, which are often exported to Asian markets for use in traditional medicine. After six years of research, tests on 20 rhinos confirmed the process is safe for the animals but effective enough for detection even inside large shipping containers. This innovative technique offers a new tool in the fight to protect South Africa's rhino population, the largest in the world.
