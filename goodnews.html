Explainer: Understanding Credit Card Payment Grace Periods

2025-07-22 11:26:03 EDT

This article clarifies the concept of grace periods for credit card payments, noting a common misunderstanding among consumers. Most credit cards offer a grace period on new purchases, which is the time between the end of a billing cycle and the due date, during which no interest is charged if the balance is paid in full. However, this does not typically extend to late payments; once a due date is missed, fees and penalty APRs can be applied immediately. While some issuers may offer a brief, informal window before applying penalties, it is not guaranteed. The article advises consumers to contact their issuer immediately if they anticipate being late.
