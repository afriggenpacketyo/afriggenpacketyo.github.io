Clinical trials demonstrated the injection's ability to virtually eliminate new HIV infections when taken every six months, proving to be more effective than daily pills. Gilead has also granted licenses to generic manufacturers to produce lower-cost versions for low- and lower-middle-income countries, committing to supply doses for up to 2 million people at no profit before generic versions become available, with the hope of bending the arc of the epidemic.
