The U.S. government is scaling up a program to combat the New World screwworm fly, a pest whose flesh-eating larvae pose a severe threat to livestock, wildlife, and even pets. The plan involves breeding billions of male flies, sterilizing them with radiation, and releasing them from aircraft over Mexico and southern Texas. These sterile males then mate with wild females, whose eggs subsequently fail to hatch, causing the population to collapse over time. This sterile insect technique successfully eradicated the pest from the U.S. decades ago and is being revived as the fly migrates north from Panama.
