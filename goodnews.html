Understanding and Avoiding 'The Winner's Curse' in Competitive Bidding

2025-11-11 06:30:00 EST

Nobel laureate Richard Thaler's concept of 'The Winner's Curse' describes how winners in competitive auctions often overpay for what they acquire. This phenomenon, first observed by oil companies bidding for drilling rights, suggests that the highest bid, and thus the winning bid, frequently exceeds the true value of the item. Researchers have since identified this curse in various domains, including book publishing, free agent markets in baseball, and NFL draft picks. Thaler advises bidders to be cautious, especially in crowded auctions, and to question if they would be happy with the purchase if they win, recognizing that being the outlier often means making a mistake.
