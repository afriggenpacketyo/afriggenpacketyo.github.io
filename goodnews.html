Breakthrough Research Explains Why Type 1 Diabetes Is More Severe in Young Children

2025-11-12 21:48:00 EST

Scientists have uncovered why Type 1 diabetes manifests more aggressively in young children, particularly those under seven, compared to teenagers or adults. The research indicates that the pancreas's beta cells, responsible for insulin production, are still developing in early childhood, making them more vulnerable to immune system attacks. In mature pancreases, beta cells form larger, more durable clusters, allowing for some insulin production even after an attack. This discovery offers hope for new immunotherapy drugs like teplizumab, which could delay the disease's onset by giving the pancreas time to mature.
