2026-01-20 05:04:47 EST

The Northern Lights, also known as the Aurora Borealis, created a stunning visual spectacle across the UK with vibrant hues of pink and green. This natural phenomenon occurs when solar eruptions send particles towards Earth, interacting with atmospheric gases to produce the colorful display. While typically visible near the Arctic Circle, intense solar activity allowed them to be seen much further south, including in parts of England, Scotland, Northern Ireland, and Wales. The recent severe geomagnetic storm, caused by a coronal mass ejection from the sun during a period of high solar activity, was responsible for this extraordinary event. The aurora is best viewed under clear skies, often appearing as a faint glow to the north, and can be enhanced when viewed through a camera lens. The article notes that while geomagnetic storms can interfere with technology, they are safe for humans due to Earth's atmosphere. The current solar maximum in the sun's 11-year cycle increases the frequency of such events. The display was a rare and beautiful occurrence for many observers across the United Kingdom.
