Understanding the 6-Month Rule for Reverse Mortgages and Its Implications for Heirs

2025-09-16 12:55:29 EDT

A key regulation for reverse mortgages, known as the "6-month rule," dictates the timeline for settling the loan after the borrower passes away or permanently leaves the home. Once a qualifying event occurs, the lender issues a "Due and Payable" letter, giving heirs six months to either repay the loan balance in full or sell the property to cover the debt. This rule primarily applies to federally insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgages (HECMs). While extensions are possible with HUD approval, the deadline emphasizes the need for borrowers to engage in thorough estate planning and communicate clearly with their families to avoid foreclosure and ensure a smooth transition.
