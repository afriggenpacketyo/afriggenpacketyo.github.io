Daylight Saving Time 2026: Key Details on Upcoming Time Change

2026-03-06 06:00:16 EST

Daylight saving time for 2026 is officially set to begin on Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, which will cause most Americans to "spring forward" and consequently lose an hour of sleep. This annual ritual effectively shifts an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening, a practice that was first adopted in the U.S. in 1918 as an effort to conserve fuel during World War I. Despite its long history, studies conducted by the Transportation and Energy Departments have found only minimal benefits in energy conservation and have even linked the time shift to some negative health effects. Only Hawaii and Arizona, along with several U.S. territories, currently do not observe this time change.
