Poland's Supreme Administrative Court Rules to Recognize Same-Sex Marriages from EU States

2026-03-20 11:48:00 EDT

Poland's Supreme Administrative Court has ruled that same-sex marriages performed in other EU countries must be recognized, a landmark decision for LGBTQ+ rights in the socially conservative nation. This ruling, stemming from a case involving a Polish couple married in Germany, dictates that such recognition does not threaten Poland's legal order or national identity, despite the constitution defining marriage as between a man and a woman. The court's decision, met with applause from activists, could allow formal recognition for 30,000 to 40,000 Polish citizens married abroad. However, legal clarity is still needed for couples who have not lived long-term in other EU states, as Poland remains one of few European countries without domestic same-sex union recognition.

China Expands Digital Yuan System by Integrating 12 New Banks

2026-03-20 11:00:14 EDT

China is significantly expanding its digital yuan (e-CNY) system by integrating 12 additional commercial banks, a move consistent with Beijing's five-year plan to "steadily develop the digital yuan." This expansion follows a draft Finance Law that officially recognizes the digital currency as fiat currency, equivalent to physical cash, alongside a new framework enacted in January to support broader adoption. The newly added institutions include seven national joint-stock commercial banks and five city commercial banks, substantially increasing the network from the initial ten state-owned banks. These commercial banks will manage the e-CNY's daily operations and compliance, including anti-money laundering and identity verification, under central bank oversight.
