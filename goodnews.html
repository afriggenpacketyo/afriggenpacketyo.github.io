2025-11-11 20:34:28 EST

Discovered in Queensland, these eggshells belonged to long-extinct mekosuchine crocodiles, which predated modern Australian species. Paleontologists hypothesize some mekosuchines, growing up to five meters, were semi-arboreal, hunting like leopards from trees. This finding from one of Australia's oldest fossil sites in Murgon adds a bizarre and fascinating new dimension to our understanding of ancient crocodile behavior and the prehistoric ecosystems of the continent.
