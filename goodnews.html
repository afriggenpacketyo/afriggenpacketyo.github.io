Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly If Not Properly Maintained

2025-06-10 07:30:00 EDT

Hummingbird feeders, while intended to help these tiny birds, can become deadly traps if not regularly cleaned. Sugar water left out too long spoils, attracting harmful microbes that can cause infections in hummingbirds, such as candida, which causes their tongues to swell and makes it difficult to eat. Wildlife rehabbers emphasize the importance of cleaning feeders every two to three days in spring and fall, and daily in the summer. The solution involves mixing one part refined white sugar with four parts water, avoiding other sugars, sweeteners, and red food coloring, and regularly cleaning the feeder with hot water or a vinegar and water mixture to prevent mold growth.
