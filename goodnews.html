Los Angeles County Sees First Decade-Long Decline in Deaths Among Unhoused Population

2026-03-10 17:48:45 EDT

Los Angeles County reported a 10% decrease in deaths among unhoused people in 2024, marking the first decline in a decade. Despite this progress, over 2,200 unhoused individuals still died, averaging more than six fatalities daily. Drug overdoses remained the leading cause, though fatalities from this cause decreased by 21%, driving the overall reduction. Conversely, traffic deaths and suicides among the unhoused increased, with 80 suicides being a decade-high. Officials attribute the overall reduction to prevention efforts but warn of impending budget challenges for homeless services, risking future progress.
