Severe Drought in Iraq Reveals 40 Ancient Tombs in Receding Reservoir

2025-08-30 10:18:49 EDT

Archaeologists in northern Iraq have uncovered approximately 40 ancient tombs, believed to be over 2,300 years old, after severe drought caused water levels to drop in the Mosul Dam reservoir. The discovery in the Khanke region of Duhok province was made possible by the receding waters, which have plagued the country for five consecutive years. The tombs are thought to date back to the Hellenistic or Hellenistic-Seleucid period. While the drought has had devastating impacts on agriculture and electricity, it has paradoxically created opportunities for archaeological excavation, revealing ruins from thousands of years ago as the landscape changes.

Three Scottish Brothers Set World Record for Fastest Unsupported Row Across the Pacific Ocean

2025-08-30 09:10:34 EDT

Jamie, Ewan, and Lachlan Maclean of Scotland have set a new world record by completing the fastest unsupported row across the Pacific Ocean, traveling over 9,000 miles from Peru to Australia. They finished the grueling journey in 139 days, arriving in Cairns on Saturday and breaking the previous record of 160 days. The brothers endured severe weather, injuries, and near-starvation while raising over £700,000 for clean water projects in Madagascar through their charity, the Maclean Foundation. Their arrival was celebrated by more than 50 family members and supporters at the Cairns Marlin Marina.
