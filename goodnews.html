Yangtze River Fishing Ban Leads to Significant Ecological Recovery

2026-02-20 23:00:09 EST

A comprehensive 10-year fishing ban on China's Yangtze River has resulted in a substantial surge in biomass and a boost for the endangered finless porpoise population. The initiative, which began in 2021, involved recalling over 111,000 fishing vessels and resettling 231,000 fishers with an investment exceeding $2.7 billion. Reinforced by the Yangtze River Protection Law, the ban also introduced stricter regulations on water quality, sand mining, and shoreline restoration. This ambitious environmental undertaking demonstrates significant progress in restoring the ecological health of one of the world's most biodiverse rivers.
