FDA Approves First Gene Therapy to Restore Hearing in Congenital Deafness

2026-04-23 16:46:49 EDT

The Food and Drug Administration has approved the first gene therapy designed to restore hearing for individuals born with a rare form of genetic deafness. This milestone treatment involves infusing a virus carrying a healthy gene for the otoferlin protein into the patient's ears, enabling the brain to receive sound signals. Clinical trials showed significant hearing restoration in most patients, with some achieving normal hearing. While currently applicable only to a rare genetic defect, researchers are hopeful for similar advancements for other forms of hearing loss. The therapy will be offered for free in the U.S. by Regeneron Pharmaceuticals. Some critics express concern that such treatments could stigmatize deafness.

Marijuana Rescheduling by Trump Administration Offers Potential Investor Benefits

2026-04-23 16:46:26 EDT

The Trump administration's move to reclassify marijuana to Schedule III under the Controlled Substances Act is seen as a significant step for investors in the cannabis industry. This demotion from Schedule I status, which previously grouped marijuana with heroin, recognizes its accepted medical uses and could alleviate substantial tax burdens on cannabis firms due to the removal of the 280E tax code. While not legalizing marijuana federally, the change may encourage investment and facilitate access to traditional banking services. Industry leaders anticipate better cash flow and balance sheets for cannabis companies, though challenges remain, and regulatory reform is expected to drive stock price movements.
