Natural Mineral Magnetite Identified as Cause of Black Sand on Michigan Beaches

2025-09-21 04:01:31 EDT

The National Park Service has clarified that the black streaks often found in the sand at Michigan beaches, such as Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore, are not pollution. The dark coloration is caused by magnetite, a naturally occurring iron-based mineral, which can be separated from the lighter quartz sand with a magnet. This phenomenon is distinct from the "stamp sands" in other parts of Michigan, which are remnants of historical copper mining operations. The presence of magnetite is a common and harmless geological feature of the region's diverse mineral landscape.
