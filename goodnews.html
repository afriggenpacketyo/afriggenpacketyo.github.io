Understanding Haboobs: The Science Behind Arizona's Towering Dust Storms

2025-08-26 20:02:42 EDT

Meteorologist Mark O'Malley details how these towering walls of dust are generated by powerful winds from thunderstorms, which force air downwards and forwards, subsequently picking up vast quantities of dust and debris. Predominantly occurring in hot, dry regions, haboobs typically last between 10 to 30 minutes but can severely reduce visibility to as little as a quarter-mile, posing significant hazards, particularly for drivers. The recent Phoenix haboob, accompanied by intense thunderstorms, caused widespread power outages and temporary flight delays, underscoring the disruptive and dangerous nature of these formidable natural events.
