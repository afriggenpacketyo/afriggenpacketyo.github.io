Explainer on Credit Card Charge-Offs and the Seven-Year Rule for Consumers

2025-10-27 13:45:06 EDT

This article explains the financial concept of a credit card charge-off, which occurs when a creditor deems a debt uncollectible, typically after 180 days of non-payment. While the issuer writes off the debt for accounting purposes, the borrower still legally owes the money, and the account is often sold to a collection agency. A charge-off remains on a consumer's credit report for seven years from the date of the first missed payment, significantly damaging their credit score during that period. The article outlines options for dealing with a charge-off, such as negotiating a settlement with a debt relief company, which can help consumers recover financially faster than simply waiting for it to expire.
