US-China Research Team Develops Robot Launch Method Using Cavitation Bubbles

2025-09-06 23:00:08 EDT

A collaborative team of scientists from the U.S. and China has developed a novel method for launching small robots by harnessing the energy from collapsing vapor bubbles. The process, known as cavitation, involves using a laser to rapidly heat light-absorbing materials, which boils nearby water droplets and creates bubbles. When these bubbles collapse, they release a powerful force capable of launching millimeter-scale objects several feet into the air. This innovative technique could have future applications in robotics, allowing for controlled jumping or even swimming on wet surfaces.
