Record-Breaking Humpback Whale Journeys Documented Between Australia and Brazil

2026-05-20 06:53:00 EDT

Researchers have documented two humpback whales making unprecedented cross-oceanic migrations between Australia and Brazil, marking the longest lifetime swims ever recorded for the species. By analyzing unique fluke patterns from thousands of images, scientists identified these whales traveling over 14,000 kilometers between breeding grounds. These rare events support the 'Southern Ocean Exchange hypothesis,' suggesting whales may meet in Antarctic feeding grounds and take varied routes back to warmer waters. Such inter-basin movements are crucial for maintaining genetic diversity within whale populations.
