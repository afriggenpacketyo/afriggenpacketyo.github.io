Understanding the Timeline for Wage Garnishment After a Lawsuit

2026-04-13 12:35:50 EDT

Creditors can initiate wage garnishment relatively quickly after securing a legal judgment, with the process potentially beginning within one to three months from the initial lawsuit filing in swift scenarios. The timeline significantly depends on whether the debtor responds to the lawsuit; failure to respond can lead to a default judgment, accelerating the process. Once a judgment is obtained, a creditor can request a garnishment order, which is then sent to the employer to withhold a portion of wages. State laws play a crucial role, with some restricting or prohibiting garnishment while others cap the amount that can be withheld. Options to prevent or interrupt garnishment include debt management plans, debt settlement, or bankruptcy. Prompt action is vital for individuals facing potential wage garnishment.
