Businesses Find Legal Workaround for Tariffs Using "First Sale Rule"

2025-05-26 01:04:41 EDT

Businesses are minimizing the impact of tariffs by utilizing the "first sale rule," a decades-old provision in U.S. customs law. This rule allows U.S. importers to calculate customs duties based on the initial sale price of goods from the overseas producer to an intermediary, rather than the inflated price from the vendor to the retailer. The first sale rule has gained renewed attention under U.S. President Donald Trump's latest tariff regime, with companies exploring its potential cost savings. To apply the rule, businesses must demonstrate that at least two sales occurred, the sales were carried out at arm's length, the item was destined for the U.S., and documentation of the first sale price is available.
