James Webb Space Telescope Discovers New Small Moon Orbiting Uranus

2025-08-19 17:40:00 EDT

NASA has announced the discovery of a new, tiny moon orbiting the planet Uranus, identified by the James Webb Space Telescope. Currently designated S/2025 U1, the satellite is approximately six miles in diameter, making it one of the smallest of the planet's 29 known moons. Researchers believe its small size is why it eluded previous detection, even by the Voyager 2 spacecraft during its 1986 flyby. The discovery suggests that more small moons may exist within Uranus's complex system of rings and satellites, offering new insights into the ice giant's chaotic history.
