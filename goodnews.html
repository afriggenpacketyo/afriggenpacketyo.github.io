The Khankhuuluu skeleton, discovered in Mongolia in the 1970s, was initially misidentified but later recognized as a distinct species with features not seen in more iconic tyrannosaurs. This mid-grade tyrannosauroid, weighing around 1,700 pounds, was one of the larger predators of its time and lacked the bone-crushing teeth characteristic of the T. rex. The identification has led to a rewriting of the tyrannosaur family history, showing that these Asian tyrannosauroids eventually migrated to North America and evolved into apex predators.
