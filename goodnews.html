US and Switzerland Agree to Tariff Reduction Deal

2025-11-14 18:28:00 EST

The United States and Switzerland have finalized a framework trade agreement, significantly reducing tariffs previously imposed by President Trump. This deal will lower US tariffs on Swiss imports from 39% to 15%, mirroring those applied to the European Union, in exchange for Switzerland's commitment to invest $200 billion in the US by 2028. US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer stated these investments would create thousands of jobs and address deficits in sectors like pharmaceuticals. This agreement, which affects 40% of Swiss exports, coincides with Trump's broader initiative to cut tariffs on food imports like beef and coffee, aiming to alleviate domestic cost-of-living pressures.

Longest US Government Shutdown Ends After 43 Days

2025-11-14 18:19:18 EST

The longest-ever US government shutdown has concluded after 43 days, with President Trump signing a new funding bill into law. The shutdown caused 1.4 million federal employees to go without pay, suspended food aid for low-income Americans, and led to thousands of flight delays and cancellations. The new 328-page bill funds most federal agencies until January 30, guarantees back-pay for all federal workers, and reverses layoffs. While it addresses immediate funding, it notably omits a guaranteed extension of expiring health insurance subsidies, and full restoration of all government services, particularly air travel, is expected to take days or weeks.
