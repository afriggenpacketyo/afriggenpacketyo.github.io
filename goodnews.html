The Appeal of High-Stress Television in an Anxious World

2025-07-30 18:14:08 EDT

This article explores why intensely stressful television shows like "The Bear" and "The Pitt" have become so popular with audiences. These series use hyperrealistic filmmaking techniques, such as long, unbroken takes, to immerse viewers in high-pressure environments like a chaotic kitchen or a busy emergency room. Psychologists suggest that watching such scenarios can induce a form of "vicarious stress," a fight-or-flight response that can be satisfying because it carries no real-world risk. The trend may also reflect a cultural shift away from pure escapism, with viewers finding catharsis in seeing relatable characters navigate realistic, high-stakes workplace challenges.
