Understanding the Financial Risks of Claiming Social Security Benefits at Age 62

2025-08-27 12:05:01 EDT

Claiming Social Security benefits at age 62, the earliest possible age, carries substantial financial risks that can significantly impact long-term retirement security. This choice results in a permanent reduction of monthly benefits, often to about 70% of the full retirement age amount, and also limits the growth of future cost-of-living adjustments. Furthermore, individuals forgo valuable delayed retirement credits, which offer an 8% annual increase for each year benefits are postponed until age 70, potentially accumulating to a quarter-million dollars in additional lifetime benefits. The decision also has lasting consequences for a surviving spouse, as reduced benefits for the primary earner can diminish their potential survivor payments, highlighting the importance of careful planning.
