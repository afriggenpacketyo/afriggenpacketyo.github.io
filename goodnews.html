Historic 'Turkey Droving' Tradition Recalled: Walking Fowl to London for Christmas

2025-12-24 00:32:22 EST

Before modern transportation and refrigeration, thousands of turkeys were historically "droved" on foot from Norfolk and Suffolk to London for Christmas markets, a journey lasting up to three months. This practice, known as turkey droving, involved herding birds, sometimes fitted with tiny leather boots or tar-and-sand-coated feet, across fields and rough roads. The article highlights this unique tradition, where drovers foraged for food along the way and birds roosted in trees before being encouraged to continue their journey. The custom ceased with the advent of steam engines and railways in the late 1800s, transforming how Christmas turkeys reached the capital and nearly leading to the loss of heritage breeds like the Norfolk Black.
