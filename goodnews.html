Experts Explain the Science Behind Age-Related Body Odor

2025-07-05 14:28:47 EDT

The phenomenon often described as 'old people smell' is a real biological process, not just a stigma, according to health experts. As people age, changes in skin composition and the breakdown of certain fatty acids produce a compound called 2-nonenal, which has a distinct musty or greasy scent. This compound is not easily removed by standard hygiene practices due to its oily nature. Experts suggest that managing this odor involves a holistic approach, including a diet rich in antioxidants, proper hydration, and antioxidant skincare, while also noting that some medications and hormonal changes can be contributing factors.
