Scientific Study Traces First Kiss to 21 Million Years Ago

2025-11-18 19:08:59 EST

A new scientific study suggests that the mouth-on-mouth kiss evolved over 21 million years ago, likely originating with the common ancestor of humans and other great apes. Researchers defined kissing as non-aggressive oral-oral contact "with some movement of lips or mouthparts and no food transfer," observing similar behaviors in various animals including wolves, prairie dogs, polar bears, and albatrosses. This evolutionary puzzle, seen across many human societies and the animal kingdom, has no obvious survival or reproductive benefits. The study also proposes that Neanderthals likely engaged in kissing and may even have shared saliva with modern humans for hundreds of thousands of years, suggesting an ancient and widespread practice with unknown underlying reasons.
