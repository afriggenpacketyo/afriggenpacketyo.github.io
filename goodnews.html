China Launches Pilot Program to Liquidate 'Zombie Companies'

2026-04-29 01:30:08 EDT

China's State Administration for Market Regulation is initiating a pilot program in seven economic hubs to facilitate the compulsory liquidation of 'zombie companies' – unprofitable firms often sustained by government subsidies or loans. This move, enabled by changes to China's Company Law, allows regulators to petition courts for the exit of these entities if they fail to wind up voluntarily. While experts see this as a positive step for clearing smaller, dormant firms and improving market-exit mechanisms, they anticipate a limited short-term impact on the broader 'zombie company' problem, as larger, state-linked entities may continue to receive support. The program aims to enhance market efficiency and reduce the burden of non-performing businesses on the economy.
