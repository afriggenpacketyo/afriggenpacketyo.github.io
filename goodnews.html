The Story Behind Juneteenth's Name and Significance

Juneteenth, a federal holiday observed on June 19, commemorates the end of slavery in the United States and is also known as Freedom Day or Emancipation Day. The name "Juneteenth" is a portmanteau of "June" and "nineteenth," marking the date in 1865 when enslaved people in Texas were informed of their freedom under the Emancipation Proclamation. While President Abraham Lincoln had signed the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863, its implementation in Confederate territory was delayed until the end of the Civil War. Celebrations of Juneteenth vary, often including parades, parties, concerts, and educational events centered on art and cuisine.
