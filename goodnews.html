Scientists Uncover Mechanism Linking Epstein-Barr Virus to Multiple Sclerosis

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Scientists have made a significant breakthrough in understanding how the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) contributes to multiple sclerosis (MS) in certain individuals, despite nearly everyone carrying the virus. The key lies in a genetic molecule, HLA-DR15, which, when present in EBV-infected B cells, causes these cells to present a myelin protein that mimics viral structures. This "fatal similarity" tricks the immune system into attacking the myelin sheaths around nerve fibers, leading to MS. This discovery is a crucial step in understanding the disease's origins and could pave the way for targeted treatments and early childhood vaccinations to prevent symptomatic Pfeiffer's disease, thereby reducing the risk of developing MS later in life.
