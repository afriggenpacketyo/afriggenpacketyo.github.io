Understanding Bank Account Garnishment by Debt Collectors

2026-03-11 10:55:18 EDT

This article explains that debt collectors can garnish bank accounts, but only after obtaining a court judgment and within legal limits, not typically seizing an entire account. Federal law automatically protects certain funds, such as Social Security, Supplemental Security Income, Veterans Affairs benefits, or federal retirement income, by requiring banks to review the past two months of deposits. Additionally, many states offer further exemptions that cap amounts or protect wages. Borrowers are advised to act quickly by claiming exemptions, considering debt settlement, or evaluating bankruptcy if facing garnishment, as understanding these protections and acting swiftly can significantly impact how much money an individual can retain when confronted with collection actions.
