Fossil Find in China Suggests Some Pterosaurs Were Herbivores

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

A rare fossil discovery in northeastern China is challenging previous beliefs about the diet of pterosaurs, the flying reptiles of the dinosaur era. Researchers found the fossil of a Sinopterus atavismus, a type of pterosaur, with its stomach contents intact for the first time. The stomach contained phytoliths, which are microscopic mineral deposits from plants, along with small quartz crystals likely used as gastroliths to help grind the plant matter. Published in Science Bulletin, this finding provides the first direct evidence of herbivory in this group of pterosaurs, renewing a long-standing debate among paleontologists about what these ancient creatures ate.

Astronomers Observe Earliest Signs of Rocky Planet Formation Around a Young Star

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

For the first time, astronomers have directly observed the initial stages of rocky planet formation around a very young, sun-like star, offering a glimpse into how our own solar system may have begun. Using the James Webb Space Telescope and the ALMA array, researchers studied the gas disk surrounding the infant star HOPS-315, located about 1,370 light-years away. They detected silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing in a region analogous to our solar system's asteroid belt. This discovery confirms that the building blocks of Earth-like planets can form very early in a star's life.
