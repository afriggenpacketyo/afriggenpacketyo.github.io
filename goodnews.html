Yangtze River Fishing Ban Leads to Biomass Surge and Finless Porpoise Boost

2026-02-20 23:00:09 EST

China's decade-long fishing ban on the Yangtze River, initiated in 2021, has led to a significant surge in biomass and a positive impact on the endangered finless porpoise population. The ban, part of the comprehensive Yangtze River Protection Law, involved recalling over 111,000 fishing vessels and resettling 231,000 fishers with a substantial investment of over US$2.7 billion. This conservation effort aims to restore the ecological health of one of the world's most biodiverse rivers, which historically contributed over 60% of China's freshwater fisheries output. The law also introduced stricter regulations on water quality and shoreline restoration.
