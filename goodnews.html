2026-01-29 14:13:24 EST

This ferret-sized species, also known as the Humboldt marten, was rediscovered in northern California in 1996 and remains federally endangered. Utilizing non-invasive tools like hair snares and remote cameras, scientists identified 46 individual martens across a 150-square-mile area. The study found martens predominantly inhabit forested ridgetops, ravines, and riverbanks with dense canopy cover and crucial coarse woody debris, highlighting persistent knowledge gaps about these "adorable animals" and their ongoing vulnerability.
