Scientists have long been puzzled by the potato's origins, as its closest wild relative does not produce the starchy tubers we eat. The research indicates that a hybridization event between a tomato ancestor and a wild potato species combined two key genes. One gene, from the tomato, signaled the plant to form tubers, while another, from the wild potato, controlled their growth, together creating the vegetable known today.
