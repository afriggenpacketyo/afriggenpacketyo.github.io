2025-09-10 03:09:00 EDT

A new exhibition in Potsdam, Germany, is exploring the complex cultural and social history of the "Plattenbau," the prefabricated concrete tower blocks that defined much of the urban landscape in the former German Democratic Republic (GDR). Initially promoted as a socialist utopian solution to post-war housing shortages, these buildings later became symbols of social decay and rising extremism following German reunification. The exhibition, "Wohnkomplex: Art and Life in Plattenbau," uses art to delve into the collective memory associated with these structures, examining them as spaces of both community and painful transformation, and prompting a re-evaluation of their architectural heritage.
