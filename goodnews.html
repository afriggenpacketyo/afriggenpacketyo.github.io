New Research Suggests Potatoes Evolved from a Hybrid of Tomatoes and Another Plant

2025-07-31 11:00:00 EDT

New research suggests a remarkable evolutionary origin for the potato: it may have arisen from a chance hybridization event between an ancient tomato and a flowering plant from the Etuberosum group, 8 to 9 million years ago. Scientists previously struggled to reconcile the potato's mixed genetic lineage, which showed resemblances to both tomatoes and Etuberosum. The study found that neither parent plant could form tubers, the underground nutrient-storage organs. However, the unique genetic combination in their hybrid offspring enabled the development of tubers, allowing the proto-potato to colonize colder, drier environments and diversify into over 100 species, including the modern cultivated potato.
