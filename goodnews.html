2025-08-11 19:37:29 EDT

In the canals of Xochimilco, Mexico City, scientists are using environmental DNA (eDNA) to track the critically endangered axolotl salamander. With the population having plummeted from thousands per square kilometer to just a few dozen, traditional net surveys often come up empty. Researchers from Mexico’s National Autonomous University are now filtering water to find genetic traces left by the elusive creatures. This modern technique helps them monitor the axolotls' presence in both protected refuge areas and the wider, more polluted canal system, offering hope for conservation efforts.
