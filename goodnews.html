Dirty Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly, Experts Warn

2025-06-10 14:34:21 EDT

Experts warn that dirty hummingbird feeders can be harmful, even deadly, to the birds they are intended to help. If sugar water is left out too long, it spoils and attracts microbes that can cause infections, including candida, which causes tongue swelling and starvation. While not all microbes are dangerous, it is common for individual birds to be sickened by dirty feeders. The solution is regular cleaning, every two to three days in spring and fall, and daily or every other day in summer, with hot water or a vinegar and water mixture. Planting native hummingbird-friendly wildflowers is also recommended to provide natural food sources.
