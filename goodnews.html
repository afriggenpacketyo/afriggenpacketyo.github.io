Study Links Early Gut Bacteria to Reduced Hospitalization for Lung Infections

2025-06-04 20:48:13 EDT

UK scientists have discovered that the first bacteria to colonize a newborn's body may protect against dangerous infections, specifically reducing the risk of hospitalization for lung infections in young children. The study, conducted by University College London and the Sanger Institute, analyzed stool samples from over 1,000 newborns to identify early microbial species and track their health outcomes for two years. The presence of Bifidobacterium longum in early infancy was found to have a protective effect, with babies harboring this species being significantly less likely to require hospitalization for lung infections. Researchers believe that these bacteria, or compounds they produce, interact with the immune system, influencing its maturation and ability to distinguish between harmful and beneficial entities.
