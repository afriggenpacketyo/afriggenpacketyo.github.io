Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is marking a major scientific milestone: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation. This unbroken, handwritten data sequence is the longest of its kind in the UK and Ireland, providing an invaluable record of climate change. The tradition, which began on July 14, 1795, continues today with a human observer manually recording measurements, a rarity in an age of automated stations. This long-term data clearly shows changing weather patterns, including fewer frost days and shifting seasons, and has contributed to meteorological science, such as the development of the cup anemometer.
