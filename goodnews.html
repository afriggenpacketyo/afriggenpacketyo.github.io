Northern Ireland Introduces Graduated Driver Licensing to Enhance Youth Road Safety

Northern Ireland is poised to introduce graduated driver licensing (GDL) reforms, becoming the first UK region to do so, with the primary goal of significantly reducing fatal road crashes among young drivers aged 17 to 23. Commencing in October, these new regulations will mandate a six-month waiting period before taking a practical driving test and the completion of 14 training modules. Furthermore, new drivers will face an extended 24-month "R" plate restriction, alongside night-time driving and passenger limitations during their initial six months post-test. Infrastructure Minister Liz Kimmins emphasized that these changes, the most substantial in 70 years, are vital for enhancing road safety.
