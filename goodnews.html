Driving schools ordered to refund learner drivers over undisclosed booking fees

2026-04-15 07:25:05 EDT

The Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has ordered the owners of AA and BSM driving schools to refund over 80,000 learner drivers. This action follows an investigation that found the schools failed to disclose a mandatory £3 booking fee upfront during online bookings. The fee was only revealed at the checkout stage, a practice known as "drip pricing" which is illegal and can mislead consumers. Affected customers will receive an average payout of £9, totaling £760,000, and do not need to take any action to receive their refund. The CMA's enforcement action is the first under new powers granted by the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024.
