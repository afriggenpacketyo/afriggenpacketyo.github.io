Exploring the Origins of "Soccer" in the US and Canada

2026-06-13 11:33:54 EDT

This article explores the linguistic origins behind why the sport globally known as "football" is commonly referred to as "soccer" in the United States and Canada. An emeritus professor explains that the term "soccer" emerged from elite Oxford universities in the late 19th century as a colloquial, shortened form of "association football," serving to differentiate it from "rugby football." As the sport expanded worldwide, "soccer" disseminated alongside it, remaining in common usage even in Britain until the 1980s. The article further notes that American football evolved concurrently, incorporating elements from both rugby and association football, thus solidifying the continued use of "soccer" to distinguish the two distinct sports.
