Daylight Saving Time 2026 Begins Sunday, Clocks 'Spring Forward'

2026-03-08 01:18:54 EDT

Daylight saving time for 2026 commences early Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, requiring most Americans to "spring forward" and lose an hour of sleep. This annual tradition, which effectively shifts an hour of daylight from morning to evening, has been in effect consistently since the 1960s, though its start date has varied historically. While initially adopted during World Wars to conserve fuel, studies by the Transportation and Energy Departments have found minimal energy conservation benefits, and it has been associated with some negative health effects. Only Hawaii and Arizona, with the exception of the Navajo Nation, along with several U.S. territories, do not observe daylight saving time, and most phones are expected to update automatically.
