The Linguistic Journey of How the Thanksgiving Turkey Got Its Name

2025-11-26 05:00:00 EST

The name of the turkey, a centerpiece of American Thanksgiving, has a complex and confusing history rooted in mistaken identity. The bird, native to the Americas and domesticated centuries before European arrival, was brought to Europe in the 1520s. There, it was confused with the African guinea fowl, which was already known as a 'turkey' because it was imported into Europe by traders through the Turkish region. The American bird eventually inherited the name and grew in popularity, becoming a staple of English celebrations and later, a fixture of the Thanksgiving holiday after being brought back to North America by colonists.
