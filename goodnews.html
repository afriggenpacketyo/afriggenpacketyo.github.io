Genetic Study Illuminates Ancestry of Indigenous Americans

2025-05-17 02:34:00 EDT

A new genetic study has traced the migration of the first people to colonize the Americas, revealing their origins in modern-day Russia between 20,000 and 30,000 years ago. By sequencing genomes of 1,537 individuals from 139 ethnic groups, researchers found that early settlers split into isolated groups, such as Amazonian, Andean, and Patagonian communities, adapting to diverse environments. The study corroborates archaeological evidence and identifies the closest living relatives of Indigenous North Americans as West Beringian groups. This research also highlights genetic traits that affect the health of modern South Americans, offering insights into their vulnerability to infectious diseases and the importance of tailored healthcare.
