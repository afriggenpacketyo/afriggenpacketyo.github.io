Psychologists Explain Hormonal and Emotional Factors Behind Waning Sexual Desire in Long-Term Relationships

2025-10-19 00:56:00 EDT

It is common for sexual desire to decrease after the initial passionate phase of a relationship, a shift that psychologists attribute to both hormonal and emotional factors. As relationships mature, the hormone oxytocin, which promotes bonding, begins to suppress the hormones that drive initial sex drive. For women, sexual satisfaction is often linked to feeling valued and having their needs met, with a notable "orgasm gap" in heterosexual relationships contributing to lower desire. Therapists emphasize that open communication, non-sexual physical affection, and prioritizing intimacy can help couples navigate this natural evolution in their connection.
