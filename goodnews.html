Menopause Linked to Alzheimer's-Like Brain Changes in Large UK Study

2026-01-26 19:04:46 EST

A significant UK study suggests a link between menopause and brain changes resembling those seen in Alzheimer's disease. The research, involving nearly 125,000 women, found a loss of grey matter in memory and emotion-related brain regions. This finding could partly explain why women face a greater risk of dementia than men. The study, published in Psychological Medicine, also indicated that hormone replacement therapy (HRT) did not appear to prevent this grey matter loss. Researchers emphasize the need for increased awareness and support for women's mental health during menopause.

Northern Ireland to Implement Graduated Driver Licensing for Young Drivers

2026-01-26 19:01:23 EST

Northern Ireland is set to become the first UK region to introduce graduated driver licensing (GDL) for young drivers, aiming to reduce fatal road crashes among 17-23 year olds. These reforms, effective in October, include a six-month waiting period before taking a practical test, along with restrictions on night-time driving and passenger numbers. Infrastructure Minister Liz Kimmins stated the changes target the age group most susceptible to serious road injuries, accounting for 24% of fatal collisions despite holding only 8% of licenses. The scheme also removes the 45mph speed limit for restricted drivers.
