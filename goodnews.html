2025-11-18 12:30:08 EST

The U.S. penny, a fixture of American currency for 232 years, is being officially discontinued, though existing coins will remain in circulation for decades. First minted in 1793, the coin was the first to feature a president's image with Abraham Lincoln's addition in 1909 and the first to bear the motto "In God We Trust." The decision comes as the cost of producing a penny, which is now mostly zinc, has surpassed its face value, costing 3.69 cents to make one coin in 2024. Despite its retirement from production, the penny's cultural legacy will live on through numerous popular aphorisms and traditions.
