New York City Implements Ranked Choice Voting in Mayoral Primary

2025-06-23 00:04:00 EDT

New York City is utilizing ranked choice voting in its Democratic mayoral primary, allowing voters to rank up to five candidates in order of preference. If no candidate receives a majority of first-choice votes, the candidate with the fewest first-choice votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed to the voters' second choices. The process continues until one candidate has a majority. The system aims to ensure that the elected candidate has broad support and prevents candidates with fringe views from winning in a crowded field. While the computerized process is quick, the tabulation won't start until July 1 due to mail-in ballots, with official results expected on July 15. The new system is intended to be a more fair and accurate form of voting.
