2025-10-04 12:18:00 EDT

The tomb, one of the largest in the Valley of the Kings and over 3,000 years old, suffered severe deterioration. Discovered in 1799, its contents were looted, including the sarcophagus. The refurbished site features a 36-meter-long, 14-meter-deep passageway and main burial chambers for the king and Queens Tiye and Sitamun. Its exquisite wall paintings are considered among the finest from the Eighteenth Dynasty, with restoration work adhering to the highest international standards.
