North America's Oldest Pterosaur Species Discovered in Arizona

2025-07-07 15:00:00 EDT

Researchers have identified a new species of pterosaur from a 209-million-year-old fossilized jawbone, making it the oldest known flying reptile ever discovered in North America. The fossil was unearthed in Arizona's Petrified Forest National Park and analyzed by a Smithsonian-led team. The newly named species, Eotephradactylus mcintireae, was about the size of a small seagull and lived during the late Triassic period. This discovery helps fill a critical gap in the fossil record, providing new insights into the evolution of pterosaurs and the ecosystem they inhabited just before a major extinction event.
