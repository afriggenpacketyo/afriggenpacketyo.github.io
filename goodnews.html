Study Links Dim Indoor Lighting to Increased Risk of Nearsightedness

2026-02-26 13:36:13 EST

New research suggests that dim indoor lighting may contribute to the global rise in nearsightedness (myopia). The study hypothesizes that when individuals focus on close objects in dim light, their pupils constrict, significantly reducing the illumination reaching the retina. This reduced retinal illumination may signal the eye to grow longer, leading to myopia. While outdoor light provides much brighter conditions that support healthy eye development, prolonged close-up work indoors under dim lighting could be a contributing factor. Researchers noted limitations in the study, including a small subject group.
