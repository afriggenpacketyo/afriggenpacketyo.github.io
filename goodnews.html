Understanding bankruptcy waiting periods is crucial before filing for debt relief

2026-03-10 10:17:41 EDT

Navigating bankruptcy laws requires a clear understanding of waiting periods, especially for individuals who have filed previously. These timelines vary based on the type of bankruptcy filed (Chapter 7 or Chapter 13) and the intended subsequent filing, impacting eligibility for debt discharge. The waiting period typically begins from the filing date, not the discharge date. For instance, filing Chapter 7 after Chapter 7 has an eight-year wait, while Chapter 13 to Chapter 13 has a two-year wait. Beyond waiting periods, borrowers must complete credit counseling and be aware of potential scrutiny for multiple filings within a short timeframe.
