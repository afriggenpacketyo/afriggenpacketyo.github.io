Scientists Uncover Fundamental Building Blocks Behind Birdsong Diversity

Analyzing over 116,000 songs from 3,000 species using AI, the research found that combinations of three trills, three whistles, chaotic notes, and harmonies form all known birdsongs. A key finding indicates that the birds' environment significantly shapes their song composition; simpler motifs are prevalent in tropical forests to penetrate dense vegetation, while more complex songs are common in temperate regions for closer-range communication. This article highlights an evolutionary trade-off between information complexity and transmission distance.
