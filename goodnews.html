Viral Photos of Horned 'Zombie-Rabbits' in Colorado Explained by Wildlife Officials

2025-08-13 20:33:00 EDT

Recent viral photos of rabbits in Colorado with black, horn-like growths have sparked alarm online, but wildlife officials say there is a scientific explanation. The animals are suffering from Shope Papilloma Virus, a disease common in the Midwest that causes keratinous tumors to grow on their heads. The virus, which is spread by ticks and fleas, is generally harmless to the rabbits unless the growths interfere with eating, and it cannot be transmitted to humans or other pets. The phenomenon is believed to have inspired the myth of the jackalope, a fabled rabbit with antlers.
