China Develops First Lunar Timekeeping Software for Accurate Earth Synchronization

2026-01-11 23:00:11 EST

China has unveiled the world's first timekeeping software designed for the moon, addressing the challenge that clocks tick faster there due to weaker gravity. Developed by the Purple Mountain Observatory, this model accounts for both lunar gravity and motion, enabling accurate synchronization of lunar events with Earth clocks. Researchers reported the method maintains accuracy within tens of nanoseconds over a millennium. Harvard astronomer Jonathan McDowell noted that lunar timekeeping is evolving from a case-by-case approach to a critical engineering necessity for navigation systems, where even microsecond differences can rapidly impact calculations.
