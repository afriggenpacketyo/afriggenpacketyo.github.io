Do You Really Need Electrolyte Drinks to Stay Hydrated?

2025-08-04 05:00:00 EDT

Despite the multi-billion dollar market for sports drinks and electrolyte powders, medical experts and sports scientists suggest most people do not need them for routine hydration. Electrolytes like sodium and potassium are lost in sweat, but a normal, varied diet typically provides more than enough to replenish them. The body, particularly the kidneys, is highly effective at regulating electrolyte levels, storing or excreting them as needed. While these drinks can be useful for elite athletes during prolonged, intense exercise or in cases of severe medical dehydration, for the average person, drinking plain water and eating a balanced diet is sufficient to stay properly hydrated.
