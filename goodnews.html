Switching Banks After Levy or Garnishment Order Won't Erase It

2026-04-09 14:22:50 EDT

Moving money to a new bank account after a levy or garnishment order has been issued will not make the order disappear. Court orders are tied to the individual, not a specific account, meaning creditors can pursue funds at the new institution. Creditors have methods to discover new accounts, such as post-judgment discovery requests or subpoenas to employers. While moving banks might offer a temporary reprieve, it's not a long-term solution. Addressing the underlying debt through settlement negotiations, bankruptcy, or working with a debt relief professional are more effective strategies.
