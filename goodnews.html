Nobel Prize in Medicine Awarded to Three Scientists for Immune Tolerance Discovery

2025-10-06 07:17:35 EDT

The 2025 Nobel Prize in medicine has been awarded to Mary E. Brunkow, Fred Ramsdell, and Dr. Shimon Sakaguchi for their groundbreaking discoveries related to peripheral immune tolerance. Their collective work identified a specific type of T-cell, now known as regulatory T-cells (T-regs), and the controlling gene, Foxp3, which prevents the immune system from attacking the body's own tissues. This mechanism acts as a crucial security check, curbing overactive immune cells that could otherwise cause autoimmune diseases. Their research has opened a new field in immunology and is now foundational for developing new treatments for cancer and autoimmune conditions.
