Understanding the Air Quality Index

2025-06-03 11:48:00 EDT

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a tool used to measure air pollution severity and categorize health risks, using six color-coded categories from good (green) to hazardous (maroon). The EPA calculates the AQI based on five major pollutants regulated by the Clean Air Act, including ground-level ozone and particulate matter. The AQI values range from 0 to 500, with lower numbers indicating cleaner air.
