2026-03-27 14:53:37 EDT

British Olympic Gold Medalist Mary Rand Dies at 86

2026-03-27 14:49:19 EDT

Mary Rand, the trailblazing British athlete who won Olympic gold in the long jump at the 1964 Tokyo Games, has passed away at the age of 86. Rand made history as the first British woman to secure an athletics gold medal, also earning silver in the inaugural women's pentathlon and bronze in the 4x100m relay, becoming the first to win three medals at a single Olympics. She broke both British and world records with her long jump performance. Described by fellow Olympian Ann Packer as "the most gifted athlete I ever saw," Rand's illustrious career included 12 national titles across various disciplines before her retirement at 28 due to injury.
