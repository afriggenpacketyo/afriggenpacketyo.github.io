Common Blood Pressure Medication Shows Potential to Slow Growth of Aggressive Cancers

2025-11-17 14:37:31 EST

Researchers have discovered that hydralazine, a drug used to treat high blood pressure since the 1950s, may be effective in fighting fast-growing cancers like glioblastoma. The study revealed that the drug works by targeting and disabling ADO, a cellular enzyme that helps tumor cells survive in low-oxygen environments. In lab experiments, treating human glioblastoma cells with hydralazine stopped them from multiplying and forced them into a permanent dormant state. Because the drug is already FDA-approved, it could potentially be repurposed for cancer therapy more quickly than developing a new medication, though further research in living systems is required.
