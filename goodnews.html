Meteorologists Explain Why No Hurricanes Made U.S. Landfall This Season

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

For the first time in a decade, the Atlantic hurricane season is concluding without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States. Meteorologists attribute this unusual pattern to a persistent area of low pressure, or an upper-level trough, that lingered over the eastern U.S. This system repeatedly steered developing storms northward and back out to sea, a phenomenon known as "recurvature." While the season produced 13 named storms, including four major hurricanes, this atmospheric steering pattern prevented them from striking the U.S. coastline directly.
