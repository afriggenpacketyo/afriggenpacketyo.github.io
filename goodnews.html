Australia Approves Chlamydia Vaccine for Koalas

2025-09-14 20:11:23 EDT

Australia has approved a world-first single-dose vaccine to protect koalas against chlamydia, a leading cause of death and infertility for the beloved marsupials. Developed by University of the Sunshine Coast (UniSC) researchers over a decade, the vaccine aims to combat the rapid, devastating spread of the disease, which affects up to 70% of some koala populations. Chlamydia can cause blindness, starvation, and bladder damage in koalas. While antibiotics previously offered limited and often problematic treatment, this new vaccine provides three levels of protection: reducing infection, preventing disease progression, and even reversing existing symptoms. The approval for widespread production and use by the APVMA offers significant hope for the species' conservation efforts.
