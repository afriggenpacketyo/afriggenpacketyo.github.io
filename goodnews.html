New Study Suggests Gut Movement May Aid Brain Waste Clearance

2026-05-07 19:04:50 EDT

New research indicates that the coordinated contractions of our abdominal muscles, traditionally understood for their role in stability, may also significantly contribute to overall brain health by aiding in waste clearance. Neuroscientist Patrick Drew's team observed through advanced imaging that mouse brains exhibit a gentle movement milliseconds before a step, a motion directly linked to abdominal muscle contraction. This brain movement, facilitated by the vertebral venous plexus—a network of veins connecting the abdomen to the spine—is hypothesized to actively drive cerebrospinal fluid out of the brain. The study, published in Nature Neuroscience, puts forward the compelling idea that this hydraulic mechanism plays a crucial role in flushing out protein waste and other unnecessary material from the brain.
