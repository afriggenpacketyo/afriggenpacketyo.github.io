Chinese Researchers Observe Quantum Friction Effect for the First Time in Graphene

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A team of scientists from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has become the first to experimentally observe the phenomenon of quantum friction between solid surfaces. Using folded graphene, they discovered that friction did not increase with thickness and, in some cases, thicker folds slid more easily. This counterintuitive effect is attributed to how bending the material alters electron movement, restricting energy loss as heat. The breakthrough, published in Nature Communications, could pave the way for developing ultra-efficient, low-wear nanodevices and advanced quantum materials.
