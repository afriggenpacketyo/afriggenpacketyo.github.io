US-China Team Develops Novel Bubble-Popping Method for Robot Launch and Movement

2025-09-06 23:00:08 EDT

A collaborative US-China research team has developed an innovative method to launch millimetre-scale robots up to 1.5 meters into the air and enable them to swim, by harnessing the energy from rapidly collapsing vapor bubbles. This novel technique, detailed in the peer-reviewed journal Science, involves using lasers to quickly heat light-absorbing materials, causing water droplets on the surface to boil and trigger the formation of vapor bubbles. Once these bubbles grow to a certain point, they suddenly collapse, rapidly releasing energy and creating a strong force. This controlled 'cavitation' mechanism provides an efficient and precise propulsion system for small robots on wet surfaces.
