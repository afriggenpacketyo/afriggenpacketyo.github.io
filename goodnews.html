A happiness researcher, Mo Gawdat, advocates for a "90-second rule" to better manage negative emotions like anger and stress. The concept, based on neuroscience, suggests that the physiological response to an emotional trigger, involving hormones like cortisol, lasts only about 90 seconds. After this initial period, any prolonged feeling of upset is a conscious or unconscious choice to rerun the thought, thus renewing the hormonal cycle. Gawdat, who began his research after the tragic death of his son, advises using this 90-second buffer to consciously shift focus, ask practical questions about the situation, and choose a more constructive response rather than ruminating.
