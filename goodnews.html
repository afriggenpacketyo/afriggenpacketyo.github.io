German States Overhaul Burial Laws to Allow for More Personalized Funeral Options

2025-09-20 07:57:00 EDT

Two German states, Rhineland-Palatinate and Saxony-Anhalt, have modernized their burial legislation to reflect changing societal attitudes toward mourning and memorials. The new laws permit river burials in the Rhine, allow urns to be kept at home, and authorize the creation of "memorial diamonds" from ashes, practices previously not allowed in Germany. These changes address a cultural shift where cremation is now far more common than traditional burial, leading to underutilized cemeteries. While the move is supported by funeral directors and reflects a trend towards personalization, it has drawn criticism from Christian churches concerned about the dignity and permanence of these new memorial forms.

Neuroscientist Explains the Science of Limerence and How to Overcome Romantic Obsession

2025-09-20 05:00:00 EDT

Neuroscientist Tom Bellamy's new book explores "limerence," a term for the intense, often debilitating state of romantic infatuation characterized by intrusive thoughts and extreme emotional swings. Bellamy explains that while this state is a natural part of early love, it can become a form of "person addiction" when the object of affection is unavailable or provides unpredictable reinforcement, driving the brain's reward system into a state of craving. To break free, he recommends strategies such as limiting contact, reframing positive daydreams into negative "daymares" to spoil the perceived reward, and redirecting energy toward new personal goals to create a sense of purpose outside the obsession.
