French Scientists Discover New Blood Type in Guadeloupe Woman

2025-06-21 11:50:03 EDT

French scientists have identified a new blood type, "Gwada negative," in a woman from Guadeloupe, marking the 48th blood group system recognized by the International Society of Blood Transfusion. The discovery was made after researchers found an unusual antibody in the patient's blood sample during routine tests, leading to the identification of a genetic mutation through high-throughput DNA sequencing. The woman is believed to be the only known carrier of this blood type, inherited from both parents, and scientists hope to find others with the same blood group to improve care for patients with rare blood types.
