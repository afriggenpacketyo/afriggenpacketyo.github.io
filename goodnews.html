In 1961, she was a standout member of the Mercury 13 program, an initiative to test female pilots for space, where she outscored many men in rigorous evaluations but was ultimately denied entry to NASA due to her gender. Undeterred, Funk built an illustrious career as a flight instructor, the first female FAA inspector, and an NTSB air safety investigator. At 82, she finally realized her lifelong dream, flying to space aboard Jeff Bezos' Blue Origin rocket in 2021. Her unwavering determination, resilience, and groundbreaking achievements continue to inspire future explorers, solidifying her legacy.
