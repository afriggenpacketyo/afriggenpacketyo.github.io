2025-10-23 12:28:18 EDT

A new study published in *Nature* indicates that mRNA-based COVID-19 vaccines, like those from Pfizer and Moderna, may significantly improve the effectiveness of cancer immunotherapy. Researchers found that late-stage melanoma and lung cancer patients who received a COVID-19 vaccine while undergoing immune checkpoint inhibitor treatment had substantially higher three-year survival rates. The study suggests the mRNA vaccine acts as an "alarm," activating the immune system to better recognize and attack tumor cells, potentially turning previously unresponsive "cold" tumors "hot." A nationwide clinical trial is being planned to validate these promising findings.
