A three-armed Link spacecraft, developed by Katalyst Space Technologies, successfully rocketed into orbit from the Marshall Islands in the Pacific Ocean. The mission aims to intercept and capture the 1.6-ton telescope, which has tracked cosmic explosions and gamma-ray bursts since its launch in 2004, and then boost its orbit by 150 miles using its thrusters. If successful, Swift could resume operations by September, preventing its predicted demise in October and preserving its valuable scientific contributions.
