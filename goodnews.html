Euclid Telescope Discovers Oldest Quasars, Deepening Cosmic Mystery of Early Universe

2026-07-06 06:45:24 EDT

The Euclid space telescope has successfully discovered 31 new quasars, including the two oldest ever observed, originating from a time when the universe was merely 670 million years old. Quasars, powered by supermassive black holes at the heart of early galaxies, are the brightest objects in the universe and serve as crucial "lighthouses" for studying the cosmos's infancy. This significant discovery, which doubles the number of known ancient quasars, deepens a "perplexing" cosmic mystery: how these incredibly massive objects grew so rapidly in the universe's early stages. Scientists plan to use Euclid and the James Webb telescope to construct a comprehensive "quasar chronicle" of the first billion years.
