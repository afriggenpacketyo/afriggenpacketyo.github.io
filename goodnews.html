Psychologist Identifies Tone of Voice as the Primary Source of Conflict in Relationships

2025-10-05 12:05:24 EDT

According to psychologist Mark Travers, the most common cause of fights between couples is not money or chores, but rather the tone of voice used during conversations. He explains that nonverbal cues, especially tone, carry significant emotional weight and can make simple questions sound like accusations, leading to defensiveness and escalation. Research suggests that how something is said often matters more than the words themselves. To mitigate this, Travers advises individuals to become self-aware, apologize for harsh tones, and rephrase their statements, while urging partners on the receiving end to calmly request a different approach instead of mirroring the negativity.
