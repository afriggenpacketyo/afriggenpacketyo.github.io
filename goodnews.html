Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Evolutionary Path of Bird Tails

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A new fossil discovery in China, detailed in Science Advances, sheds light on the evolutionary transition of bird tails from their dinosaur ancestors. Researchers found that birds shed their dinosaur tails in a step-by-step process, leading to the development of the pygostyle, a fused skeletal structure that anchors tail feathers. The rarity of early bird and bird-like dinosaur fossils has made studying this significant morphological change challenging, making this discovery particularly valuable for understanding avian evolution.
