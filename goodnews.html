He was a co-founder of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee and a Freedom Rider, enduring beatings and arrests for his activism. Later, he worked with Martin Luther King Jr. and dedicated his life to institutionalizing nonviolence globally, teaching and leading peace initiatives. His memoir, "In Peace and Freedom," chronicles his journey and enduring legacy in the fight for justice.
