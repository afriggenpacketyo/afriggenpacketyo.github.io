Astronomers Witness Earliest Stages of Rocky Planet Formation for the First Time

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb Space Telescope and the ALMA array, astronomers have for the first time directly observed the initial stages of rocky planet formation around a young, sun-like star named HOPS-315. Located 1,370 light-years away, the infant star's surrounding gas disk showed signs of silicate minerals condensing, the fundamental building blocks of planets like Earth. This discovery provides an unprecedented glimpse into the processes that may have occurred at the dawn of our own solar system. The finding suggests that the formation of Earth-like planets could be a common process, offering new insights into the potential for similar worlds elsewhere in the universe.
