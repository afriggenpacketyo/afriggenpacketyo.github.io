Understanding the Implications When a Debt Collector Sells Your Debt

2025-11-21 13:52:01 EST

As economic conditions tighten, debt collectors frequently sell delinquent accounts, transferring ownership to another agency or debt buyer, often for a fraction of the original balance. When this occurs, the new company must notify the borrower, and previous payment arrangements may no longer apply, though the debt balance itself remains unchanged. Crucially, a debt sale does not restart the credit reporting clock, and borrowers' rights under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act remain fully intact.
