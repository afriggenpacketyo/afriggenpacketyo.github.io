NPR "Founding Mother" Susan Stamberg Dies at 87, Leaving Enduring Legacy

2025-10-16 15:27:40 EDT

Susan Stamberg, an original NPR staffer and the first U.S. woman to anchor a nightly national news program, has died at 87. A "founding mother" of NPR, she was known for her distinctive voice and wide-ranging stories, from politics to culture, and for her famous cranberry relish recipe. Stamberg broke barriers for women in broadcast journalism, insisting on office space and rejecting traditional "deep-voiced announcer" stereotypes. Her colleagues remember her as a mentor and humanitarian who believed in journalism's power to connect people, leaving an irreplaceable mark on NPR's sensibility and programming.
