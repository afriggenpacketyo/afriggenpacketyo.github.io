New York City Implements Ranked Choice Voting in Mayoral Primary

2025-06-23 00:04:00 EDT

New York City is employing ranked choice voting in its Democratic mayoral primary, a system where voters rank multiple candidates in order of preference. This system aims to ensure that even if a voter's top choice lacks sufficient support, their subsequent rankings still influence the outcome. If no candidate secures a majority of first-choice votes, the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed based on voters' second choices. The process continues until a candidate achieves a majority, and the results won't be official until mid-July due to the arrival of mail-in ballots.
