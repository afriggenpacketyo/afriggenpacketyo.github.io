Trump Administration Considers Waiving Jones Act Amid Surging Fuel Prices

This law requires that all goods transported between U.S. ports be carried on American-built, flagged, and crewed vessels. Waiving the act would permit foreign ships to move fuel domestically, aiming to ease supply bottlenecks and potentially reduce energy costs for consumers. While the decision is not yet finalized, this move signifies a proactive governmental response to mitigate economic pressures arising from ongoing geopolitical conflicts.
