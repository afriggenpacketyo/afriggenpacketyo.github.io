2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

Following the 8.8-magnitude earthquake off Russia's coast, this article explains the science of tsunamis. These powerful ocean waves are typically generated by large, shallow underwater earthquakes that cause vertical shifts in the seafloor. Tsunamis travel at high speeds in the deep ocean and slow down as they approach the coast, causing their wave height to increase dramatically. Scientists use a network of deep-ocean sensors and coastal tide gauges to issue warnings, but prediction remains challenging. For safety, officials advise moving at least 100 feet above sea level immediately upon receiving an alert.
