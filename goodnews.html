French National Assembly passes assisted-dying legislation with stringent conditions after extensive debate.

2026-07-15 13:29:26 EDT

France's National Assembly approved a bill establishing a right to assisted dying under strict conditions, following years of parliamentary debate and previous rejections by the Senate. The proposed law permits assisted dying for French adults with serious, incurable, and life-threatening illnesses causing unbearable suffering resistant to treatment. Patients must freely express their intention to a doctor, who makes a decision within 15 days, and then the patient must self-administer a lethal substance, or a medical professional can assist if they are unable. The Prime Minister will refer parts of the bill to the Constitutional Council for review before enactment, positioning France among other European nations with similar legislation.
