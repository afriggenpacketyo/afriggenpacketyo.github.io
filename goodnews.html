Understanding required minimum withdrawals from $1 million retirement accounts

2026-05-07 10:50:10 EDT

Retirees with a $1 million retirement account must adhere to Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) starting at age 73, mandated by the IRS. The withdrawal amount is calculated by dividing the account balance by a life expectancy factor specific to the retiree's age. For instance, at age 73, the RMD on a $1 million balance is approximately $37,736, increasing with age as the life expectancy factor decreases. These distributions are treated as ordinary income, potentially affecting tax brackets, Social Security benefits, and Medicare premiums. Failure to take an RMD can result in a significant penalty.
