Study Suggests Pigs Were Domesticated in China Earlier Than Previously Thought

2025-06-12 21:00:11 EDT

Research from Chinese and US archaeologists suggests that pigs were domesticated in southern China around 8,000 years ago, potentially earlier than previously believed. The study, published in PNAS, analyzed early Neolithic sites in the Yangtze Delta region and found evidence of pigs consuming humans' cooked food and waste. This challenges the conventional view that pigs were first domesticated in the Near East. The findings indicate that pig domestication occurred alongside the development of rice cultivation and sedentary lifestyles in ancient China.
