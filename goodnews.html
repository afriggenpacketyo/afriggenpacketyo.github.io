China's Chang'e-6 Lunar Samples Suggest Far Side of the Moon is Colder

2025-10-04 06:00:14 EDT

A collaborative study by scientists in China and Britain has revealed that the far side of the moon may be colder than its near side. This discovery was made by analyzing the first-ever rock samples returned from the lunar far side by China’s Chang’e-6 mission. The minerals in these samples appear to have formed in lava temperatures approximately 100 degrees Celsius lower than those from the near side. Published in Nature Geoscience, the research provides crucial new insights into the moon's thermal asymmetry and its formation, helping to explain the long-observed differences in topography and volcanic activity between the two hemispheres.

October's Harvest Moon to be the First of Three Consecutive Supermoons in 2025

2025-10-04 01:37:54 EDT

The first supermoon of 2025, known as the Harvest Moon, will be visible in the UK on the night of October 7th. This event will be the largest and brightest full moon of the year so far and will kick off a series of three consecutive supermoons. A supermoon occurs when a full moon coincides with its closest point to Earth in its elliptical orbit, making it appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than usual. This year's Harvest Moon is notable for its late appearance in October, as it is defined as the full moon closest to the autumn equinox.
