Stephen Hawking's 1971 Black Hole Area Theorem Observationally Confirmed

2025-09-11 06:00:00 EDT

A decade after the first detection of gravitational waves, scientists have now been able to observationally verify a key theory about black holes proposed by Stephen Hawking in 1971. Hawking's area theorem states that the surface area of a black hole can never decrease. By analyzing the gravitational waves from a black hole merger detected earlier this year, researchers confirmed that the surface area of the new, combined black hole was greater than the sum of the two original black holes. This achievement, made possible by significant upgrades to gravitational wave detectors, provides powerful evidence for one of Hawking's foundational ideas.
