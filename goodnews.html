Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is celebrating a major milestone: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation, making it the longest such record in the UK and Ireland. Since July 14, 1795, observers have manually recorded data like temperature and air pressure in handwritten ledgers, a tradition that continues today alongside automated systems. This invaluable long-term data set has chronicled major weather events, including the infamous 'Night of the Big Wind' in 1839, and has contributed to meteorological science, such as the development of the cup anemometer. The unbroken sequence was notably maintained by Theresa Hardcastle, a director's widow, in 1917.
