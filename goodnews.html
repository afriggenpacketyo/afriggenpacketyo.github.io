Chinese Mineral Tellurium Offers Hope for Restoring Vision

2025-06-08 00:00:18 EDT

Researchers in China have successfully used tellurium, a rare mineral, to create an artificial retina that restored sight in blind animals, even granting them the ability to see infrared light. The nanowire implants, made from tellurium, convert both visible light and infrared radiation into electrical signals, effectively replacing damaged photoreceptor cells in the retina. This breakthrough holds significant potential for treating human blindness, as tellurium's photoelectric properties eliminate the need for auxiliary equipment. The study, published in Science, demonstrates a promising avenue for restoring vision and expanding visual capabilities. The research provides a potential future direction in treating blindness with the use of photoelectric properties of the tellurium to send electrical signals converted from light signals to the brain.
