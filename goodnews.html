Federal Judge Rules Alabama's Nitrogen Gas Execution Method Unconstitutional

A federal judge has reversed an earlier opinion, ruling that Alabama's nitrogen hypoxia execution protocol constitutes cruel and unusual punishment, thus banning its use for death row inmate Jeffrey Lee. U.S. District Judge Emily Marks found that inmates executed by nitrogen gas are likely to experience severe air hunger and distress for one to three minutes before asphyxiation. Lee had proposed a firing squad as a feasible alternative, which the state had failed to justify refusing.
