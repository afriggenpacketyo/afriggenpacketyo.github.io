Explainer: Understanding the Nuances of Credit Card Payment Grace Periods

2025-07-22 11:26:03 EDT

This article clarifies the common misunderstanding of credit card grace periods, explaining that they primarily apply to new purchases, not late payments. Most credit cards offer a window of 21 to 25 days after a billing cycle ends, during which a balance paid in full incurs no interest. However, this grace period is often forfeited if a balance is carried over from the previous month. For late payments, issuers are not required to offer a grace period, and fees or penalty APRs can be applied immediately, though some may offer a brief, informal window for customers with a good history.
