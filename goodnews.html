Japan to Study Offshore 'Blue Carbon' for CO2 Capture

2025-05-19 22:21:00 EDT

The Japanese government is initiating offshore "blue carbon" research to capture carbon dioxide through cultivated sea plants and store it in the deep sea. The goal is to leverage marine vegetation like mangroves and kelp, which absorb and store more carbon than terrestrial vegetation, to help achieve net-zero emissions by 2050. The Environment Ministry will commission studies to assess the behavior of seaweed at significant depths and the environmental impact of such projects. Japan aims to capture 1 million tons of CO2 by fiscal 2035 and 2 million tons by fiscal 2040 through blue carbon initiatives.
