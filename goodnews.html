New Research Suggests Moon Formed from Earth's Close Neighbor, Theia

2025-11-20 16:08:00 EST

New research published in Science suggests that the Moon was formed from a Mars-sized protoplanet named Theia, which collided with early Earth approximately 4.5 billion years ago. Scientists at the Max Planck Institute for Solar System Research analyzed elemental isotopes, finding Earth and Moon to be nearly indistinguishable, implying Theia originated as a close neighbor or "chemical twin" to Earth. This challenges previous theories suggesting Theia formed further away. While the "giant impact hypothesis" is widely accepted, the exact origin of Theia and the mechanism of material homogenization during the collision remain areas for further scientific investigation.
