2025-07-11 06:00:00 EDT

This article addresses common concerns about the safety of microwave ovens, clarifying that they do not use the same harmful ionizing radiation as nuclear reactions. Instead, microwaves use non-ionizing electromagnetic waves, which are much less powerful and do not make food radioactive or damage human DNA. In fact, microwave cooking can be one of the best methods for preserving nutrients in food because of the shorter cooking times. The primary risks associated with microwaves are burns from hot containers and food, and the potential for chemicals to leach from unsafe plastic containers, not radiation exposure.
