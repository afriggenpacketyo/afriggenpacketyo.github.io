Uranium: Its Properties, Uses, and Environmental Impact

2025-06-23 09:12:00 EDT

Uranium, discovered in 1789, is a naturally occurring radioactive element found in trace amounts in the environment. It has a silver-white-gray finish and is denser than other heavy metals. Uranium is used in both enriched and depleted forms, with enriched uranium serving as fuel for nuclear power plants and weapons, while depleted uranium is used for radiation shielding. Exposure to high concentrations of uranium can be life-threatening, causing cancer and organ damage, and it can enter the environment through industrial runoff and mining operations. The majority of the world's uranium production comes from Kazakhstan, Canada, and Australia.
