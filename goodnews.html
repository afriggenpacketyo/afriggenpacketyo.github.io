Los Angeles Zoo Welcomes Two New Tasmanian Devils

2025-07-29 19:16:59 EDT

For the first time in five years, the Los Angeles Zoo is home to Tasmanian devils, having welcomed two males named Danny Zuko and Crush. The animals, indigenous only to the island of Tasmania, arrived from a conservation organization in Australia and are now on view in the Animals of Australia exhibit. Tasmanian devils are known for having the strongest bite relative to their body size of any mammal and for their noisy feeding frenzies. The species is classified as endangered due to threats from a contagious facial tumor disease, as well as conflict with humans and other predators.
