Nigerian State of Kano Implements Sweeping Ban on Chainsaw Usage to Combat Deforestation

2025-10-06 19:30:00 EDT

The Nigerian state of Kano has enacted a stringent law banning chainsaw usage for cutting or felling trees, aiming to protect natural resources and combat deforestation. Commissioner Dahir Muhammad Hashim announced fines starting at 250,000 Nigerian naira ($168), escalating to 500,000 naira with potential imprisonment for repeat offenses. Enforcement will involve security agencies, local authorities, and community monitors. A Chainsaw Usage Permit Framework is being introduced, requiring annual licenses and an inspection-based approval process. Each tree removed under a permit must be replaced with two to three saplings, with collected fees funding reforestation and climate resilience programs, underscoring a serious commitment to environmental preservation.
