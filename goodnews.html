The Evolution of 'Gossip' from Godly Origins to Negative Connotation

2026-07-16 05:00:00 EDT

The word "gossip" has a fascinating etymological journey, originating from the Old English "godsibb," meaning "sponsor or godparent," and later evolving to describe a close friend. Initially used for women friends gathering socially, the term began to acquire a negative, gendered connotation around the 16th century, associating it with idle chatter and rumor-spreading. Feminist scholars argue this derogatory shift reflected societal misogyny, aiming to devalue women's conversations and control their social interactions. Some modern authors now advocate for reclaiming "good gossip" as a valuable form of female connection and truth-telling, distinct from malicious rumor-mongering.
