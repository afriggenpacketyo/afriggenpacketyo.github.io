Tennessee Launches Nation's First Domestic Abuser Registry

2026-01-02 15:09:03 EST

Tennessee has implemented "Savanna's Law," creating the first statewide registry of convicted domestic abusers in the U.S., effective January 1. Named after Savanna Puckett, who was tragically shot to death by her ex-boyfriend with an extensive history of domestic violence allegations, the law requires the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation to maintain a database of individuals convicted of or pleading guilty to at least two domestic violence offenses after the effective date. The database will include offenders' names, dates of birth, photos, and conviction locations, but notably not their addresses, and is not retroactive. This pioneering initiative aims to provide critical information, which Puckett's mother believes could have saved her daughter's life by allowing her to be aware of his past.
