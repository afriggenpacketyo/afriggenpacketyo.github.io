Key Information Regarding the End of Daylight Saving Time in 2025

2025-11-02 00:48:56 EDT

Daylight saving time concludes on Sunday, November 2, 2025, at 2 a.m., as clocks "fall back" by one hour, granting most Americans an extra hour of sleep. This annual adjustment means darkness will arrive earlier in the evenings, with sunrises also occurring sooner. The practice, which began in Germany during World War I to save energy, became a legal requirement in the U.S. with the Uniform Time Act of 1966. While most of the U.S. observes this change, states like Hawaii and Arizona, along with several territories, do not. There's ongoing debate, with some politicians, including former President Trump, advocating for its elimination or permanent daylight saving.
