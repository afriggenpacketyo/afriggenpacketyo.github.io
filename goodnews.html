James Webb Telescope Directly Images a Potential Newly Discovered Exoplanet

2025-06-26 18:31:57 EDT

For the first time since its launch, NASA's James Webb Space Telescope has captured direct images of what is believed to be a previously undiscovered exoplanet. The celestial body, named TWA 7b, orbits a young star and is estimated to be similar in mass to Saturn. Scientists utilized high-contrast imaging and a coronagraph, a tool that blocks a star's bright glare, to reveal the faint object. While a small possibility remains that the object is a background galaxy, researchers state the evidence strongly suggests it is a planet. The discovery could provide new insights into planetary formation and debris disks around young stars.
