Explainer: The Urban Heat Island Effect and Why Cities Are Hotter

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

This article explains the science behind the "urban heat island effect," a phenomenon causing cities to be significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. Dark, hard surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, while waste heat from vehicles and air conditioning further elevates temperatures. Conversely, the countryside benefits from the natural cooling effects of vegetation and water. As climate change intensifies heatwaves, this effect makes growing urban populations particularly vulnerable to extreme heat.
