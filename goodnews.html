Cash Transfers Proven to Significantly Reduce Infant Mortality in Rural Kenya

2025-08-18 10:08:27 EDT

A major experiment in rural Kenya has revealed that unconditional cash transfers can dramatically reduce infant and child mortality, on par with medical interventions like vaccines. Infants born to families receiving $1,000 were nearly half as likely to die, with deaths of children under five dropping by 45%. The study found cash most effective in reducing deaths during birth and the weeks after, largely by improving access to healthcare facilities and enabling families to afford necessary care. This suggests cash can be a powerful tool in public health, especially where access to care is financially constrained.
