Daylight Saving Time to End in the UK on October 26

2025-10-11 19:32:29 EDT

Residents in the United Kingdom will set their clocks back by one hour at 2:00 AM BST on Sunday, October 26, marking the end of British Summer Time (BST) and the return to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). This annual change provides an extra hour of sleep for most but also means sunsets will occur an hour earlier. The practice of Daylight Saving Time was first adopted in Europe during World War One to conserve fuel. While debates continue about making BST permanent, concerns about dark winter mornings, particularly in Scotland, have prevented a permanent shift.
