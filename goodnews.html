Uruguay Becomes First Latin American Nation to Legalize Euthanasia

2025-10-16 02:38:00 EDT

Uruguay has made history by becoming the first country in Latin America to legalize euthanasia, following Senate approval of the "Dignified Death" bill with a 20-11 vote. The new law permits mentally sound adult citizens suffering from a terminal, irreversible disease to choose assisted death administered by a healthcare professional. It specifically excludes assisted suicide, where patients self-administer medication. This legislative milestone aligns with Uruguay's socially liberal history, having previously pioneered laws on legalized cannabis, same-sex marriage, and abortion in the predominantly Catholic region. Public support for legal euthanasia in Uruguay is over 60%, despite the Catholic Church expressing "sadness."
