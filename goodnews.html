Psychologist Adam Grant Recommends Practical Job Auditions to Identify Top Candidates

2025-11-09 10:00:02 EST

Wharton psychologist Adam Grant argues that traditional job interviews often fail to identify the best candidates because they rely on telling rather than showing skills. He suggests interviewers should instead incorporate two key practices: giving candidates a task that is a real part of the job, and offering them a "do-over" by providing feedback and observing their ability to learn and improve. Grant asserts that a candidate's growth between a first and second attempt is a much better predictor of future job performance than a single, static interview, helping employers discover hidden talent and assess true potential.
