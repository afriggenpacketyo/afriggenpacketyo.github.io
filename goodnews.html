Social Security disability benefits protected from most private debt garnishment, but exceptions exist

2026-05-21 13:44:12 EDT

Social Security disability benefits (SSDI and SSI) are largely protected from garnishment by private creditors, including credit card companies and medical debt collectors, due to federal law. Banks are also required to protect a portion of these benefits deposited into accounts. However, certain government debts, such as federal tax debt, defaulted student loans, child support, and alimony, can still lead to garnishment or offset. Mixing protected benefits with non-protected funds in a bank account can complicate matters during collection attempts. While creditors cannot garnish benefits directly, they can still pursue lawsuits, leading to judgments and potential bank levies. Options like debt settlement and bankruptcy can provide relief for those struggling with debt while on disability.
