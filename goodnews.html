Europe's Top Court Rules Beverages Labeled 'Gin' Must Contain Alcohol

2025-11-13 11:10:00 EST

The Court of Justice of the European Union has ruled that a beverage cannot be labeled as "gin" if it does not contain alcohol. The decision came after a German business association challenged a product named "Virgin Gin Alkoholfrei" (Non-alcoholic Virgin Gin), arguing it violated EU law. According to a 2019 EU regulation, gin must be produced with ethyl alcohol of agricultural origin and have a minimum alcoholic strength of 37.5%. The court found a "clear prohibition" on using the term for non-alcoholic drinks, stating that the label is misleading to consumers and constitutes unfair competition against producers who comply with the legal definition.
