2026-07-10 17:30:57 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the exciting discovery of a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, based on fossils unearthed in the country's northeastern Kalasin Province. This long-necked sauropod, estimated to have lived approximately 150 million years ago and reached lengths of up to 20 meters, belongs to the Mamenchisauridae family, a group predominantly found in China. The identification stemmed from a uniquely structured dorsal vertebra, revealing distinct air-cavity features that set it apart from other known dinosaurs. This groundbreaking find, detailed in the scientific journal Nature, marks the first such discovery in Thailand, significantly enhancing the understanding of dinosaur biodiversity in Southeast Asia.
