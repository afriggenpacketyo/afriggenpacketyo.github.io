Understanding Bankruptcy Waiting Periods Crucial for Debt Relief Filers

These timelines vary significantly based on the type of bankruptcy previously filed and the type being considered next, typically Chapter 7 or Chapter 13. For instance, an eight-year wait is common between two Chapter 7 filings, while a four-year period applies from Chapter 7 to Chapter 13. Filing too soon can limit debt discharge, as eligibility is measured from the previous filing date, not the discharge date.
