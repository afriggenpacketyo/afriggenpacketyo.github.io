An Exploration of Why American Bread Often Differs in Quality from European Counterparts

2025-10-20 08:00:00 EDT

Many travelers notice a significant difference in the quality and taste of bread in countries like France compared to the United States. This disparity is partly due to differing food production standards, exemplified by a 1993 French law mandating that traditional baguettes in boulangeries contain only four ingredients: flour, water, leavening, and salt. In contrast, mass-produced American bread often contains numerous additives and conditioners to facilitate rapid, industrial-scale baking. This focus on speed and convenience in the U.S. comes at the expense of the slow fermentation process that develops complex flavors and aromas.
