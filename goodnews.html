The fossil of a Sinopterus atavismus was found with phytoliths, microscopic plant minerals, and quartz crystals in its stomach. The presence of quartz suggests the creature may have used gastroliths, or stomach stones, to grind tough plant matter, similar to some modern birds. This finding provides direct evidence of herbivory in this species, renewing a contentious debate among paleontologists about what these ancient creatures actually ate.
