Chinese Scientists Cultivate Lab-Grown Heart Pacemaker from Stem Cells

2026-05-22 22:00:07 EDT

Scientists in Shanghai have achieved a groundbreaking medical feat by successfully cultivating the world's first laboratory-engineered sinoatrial node, which functions as the heart's inherent pacemaker, utilizing human pluripotent stem cells. This autonomously beating organoid signifies a potential revolution in both cardiac disease research and the development of new drug screening methodologies. The sinoatrial node is an indispensable structure responsible for orchestrating the heart's continuous and rhythmic contractions, thereby ensuring the efficient circulation of blood throughout the entire body. The successful engineering of this "master conductor" in a laboratory setting opens up promising new avenues for creating biological pacemakers and pioneering innovative treatments for various abnormal heart rhythms, conditions that can prove life-threatening when the natural node malfunctions.
