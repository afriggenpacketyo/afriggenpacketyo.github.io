Evaluating Annuities Versus Certificates of Deposit for Retirement Planning

2025-08-06 12:51:59 EDT

The article explores whether annuities or Certificates of Deposit (CDs) are superior for retirement planning, concluding that both serve distinct purposes. CDs offer simplicity, predictability, and FDIC-insured principal, making them ideal for short-term goals or conservative portfolio anchors, though their returns often lag inflation. Annuities, conversely, are designed for lifetime income, converting lump sums into guaranteed payments and mitigating longevity risk, especially valuable for those without pensions. However, annuities are more complex, with varying fees and limited liquidity. Many retirees find a combination of both—CDs for short-term safety and annuities for long-term income—offers the most comprehensive strategy. (not investment advice)
