Understanding the 7-Year Rule for Credit Card Debt and Its Impact on Your Finances

2025-09-30 13:48:22 EDT

The "7-year rule" for credit card debt primarily refers to the provision in the Fair Credit Reporting Act that requires most negative information, like late payments and charge-offs, to be removed from a credit report after seven years. This clock starts from the date of the first delinquency. Additionally, states have statutes of limitations, often around seven years, which limit a creditor's ability to sue for the debt. However, the debt itself does not disappear after seven years; collectors can still attempt to contact you for payment, though they lose their legal recourse to sue.
