India Restores Centuries-Old Royal Kitchen in Awadh, Maintaining Public Service

2026-03-28 19:25:03 EDT

In Uttar Pradesh, India, a centuries-old royal kitchen located within Lucknow's Chota Imambara complex is undergoing meticulous restoration while remarkably continuing its tradition of serving food to thousands. Constructed in 1837 by Muhammad Ali Shah, this kitchen historically provided meals for both the royal household and ordinary citizens, a practice that endures today during the holy months of Ramadan and Muharram. Managed by the Hussainabad Trust, its operations are funded by an endowment established by the former ruler. The Archaeological Survey of India is carefully restoring the structure using traditional lime-based mortar and 'lakhauri' bricks, ensuring the preservation of its profound historical and cultural significance.
