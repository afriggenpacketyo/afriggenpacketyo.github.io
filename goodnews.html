Global Adoption of Cloud Seeding Increases Amidst Climate Crisis and Water Scarcity

2026-02-24 01:10:37 EST

A growing number of countries, including the US, China, India, and Saudi Arabia, are increasingly utilizing cloud seeding technology to address water scarcity and climate challenges. This decades-old technique aims to enhance precipitation by introducing particles like silver iodide into clouds, potentially increasing local rainfall or snowfall by 5% to 15%. Recent breakthroughs in measurement and attribution have rekindled interest, despite past controversies over environmental risks and geopolitical tensions. Cloud seeding is seen as a cost-effective method to augment water resources compared to desalination, offering a proactive approach to managing critical water supplies in an era of environmental uncertainty.
