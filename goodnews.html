Space Telescope Captures Warped Galaxy View Through an Einstein Ring

2025-12-07 12:12:17 EST

The Space Telescope Advent Calendar presents a captivating image showcasing a warped and distorted distant spiral galaxy, illustrating the astronomical phenomenon known as an Einstein Ring. This striking visual distortion arises from gravitational lensing, where light emitted from the more distant galaxy is bent and magnified as it passes around a massive, much closer galaxy, which is part of the galaxy cluster SMACSJ0028.2-7537. First predicted by Albert Einstein in 1912, the characteristic circular shape of these distortions lends the phenomenon its name, providing a unique and profound glimpse into the universe's powerful gravitational effects on light and space.
