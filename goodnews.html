The Tradition of Drinking Milk in the Indianapolis 500 Victory Circle

2025-05-22 04:00:52 EDT

The tradition of drinking milk in the Indianapolis 500 victory circle began in 1936 when Louis Meyer drank buttermilk after winning his third Indy 500. The moment was captured by a cameraman, leading a dairy industry executive to request milk be given to every winner. From 1947 to 1954, winners were handed cold water in a silver chalice, but the milk tradition returned in 1956. Since then, 69 consecutive winners have enjoyed milk in the victory circle, continuing the iconic tradition.
