2026-01-28 05:00:00 EST

The article explores the surprising origins of American football, tracing its roots back to unregulated games played in medieval England. The term 'football' itself emerged from games played on foot with a ball, evolving from simple, often violent, village contests. While these early forms resembled modern soccer, the game gradually made its way to America through English settlers. The formalization of rules in England in the late 19th century led to distinct versions of football and rugby. American football, as we know it, began to take shape in U.S. colleges, with a significant transformation occurring in 1876 with the establishment of the first set of rules for the American game.
