Financial Article Clarifies Misconceptions About Debt Expiration After Seven Years

2025-11-10 11:41:43 EST

This article debunks the common myth that debts are automatically forgiven after seven years. While the Fair Credit Reporting Act requires most negative information, such as late payments and collections, to be removed from a credit report after seven years, the underlying debt itself does not disappear. Creditors can legally continue to pursue collection of the owed amount. The article distinguishes this from the statute of limitations, which varies by state and determines the period during which a creditor can sue for the debt. It advises that facing old debts through settlement or management plans is a more effective strategy.
