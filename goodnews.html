2025-09-22 18:20:00 EDT

This article examines the scientific basis for the administration's announcement linking Tylenol (acetaminophen) use during pregnancy to autism, concluding the evidence is muddy and insufficient. While some small, observational studies have suggested an association, they struggle to control for confounding factors, such as the underlying fever the drug was taken to treat, which is itself a known risk factor. The largest and most robust study, covering 2.5 million children in Sweden, found no causal link after comparing siblings. Experts emphasize that the rise in autism diagnoses is largely attributable to broader diagnostic criteria and increased awareness, not a single environmental cause.
