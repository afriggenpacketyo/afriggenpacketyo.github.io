Experts Question Widespread Need for Electrolyte-Enhanced Sports Drinks

2025-08-04 05:00:00 EDT

Despite the multi-billion dollar market for sports drinks and electrolyte powders, medical experts and sports scientists suggest that most people do not need them for routine hydration. Electrolytes like sodium and potassium are indeed lost through sweat, but a varied diet typically provides more than enough of these minerals to replenish what is lost during normal activity. The body, particularly the kidneys, is also highly effective at regulating its own electrolyte levels. While these drinks can be useful for elite athletes or during prolonged, intense exercise, for the average person, drinking plain water is sufficient.
