Understanding Wind Chill: Risks and Protection in Frigid Conditions

2026-01-30 05:00:00 EST

Wind chill measures how quickly the body loses heat when low temperatures combine with high winds, posing dangers like frostbite and hypothermia to millions in the central and eastern United States. The National Weather Service explains that wind removes the insulating layer of warm air around the skin, making it feel much colder and increasing the risk of tissue damage. Children, the elderly, and those with underlying medical conditions are particularly vulnerable to these extreme conditions. Protection involves dressing warmly in layers, minimizing outdoor time, and covering all exposed skin to prevent serious health risks.
