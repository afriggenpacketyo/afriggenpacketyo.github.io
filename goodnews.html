NASA Declares End of Mission for MAVEN Mars Orbiter After Loss of Contact

2026-06-04 08:27:35 EDT

NASA has declared the end of its highly successful Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution Mission (MAVEN) orbiter after losing contact with the spacecraft in December 2025. Designed for a one-year mission, MAVEN operated for an extraordinary 11 years, providing unprecedented insights into how the solar wind eroded Mars' atmosphere over billions of years. The $582 million probe also served as a crucial communications relay for NASA's Mars rovers. Efforts to reestablish contact were unsuccessful, with data suggesting the spacecraft began rotating uncontrollably.
