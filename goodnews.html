Scientists confirm that the comet, currently about 416 million miles from the sun, poses no danger to Earth, with its closest approach in October being more than 1.5 times the distance from the Earth to the sun. The object was spotted by the ATLAS telescope in Chile and is believed to be larger than the two previously detected interstellar visitors, 'Oumuamua and Borisov.
