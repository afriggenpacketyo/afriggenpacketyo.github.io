Semaglutide Shows Promising Results in Reversing Liver Disease in Clinical Trial

2025-05-03 05:00:56 EDT

A new clinical trial published in The New England Journal of Medicine reveals that semaglutide injections can reverse life-threatening cases of liver disease, specifically metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH). Participants receiving semaglutide, traditionally a treatment for type 2 diabetes, experienced a significant reduction in liver inflammation and fat accumulation compared to those receiving a placebo. Researchers also noted improvements in liver fibrosis and blood measures, along with an average weight loss of 10.5%. While some adverse effects like nausea and diarrhea were more common in the semaglutide group, the findings suggest it could be a valuable treatment option for fatty liver disease, pending regulatory approval.
