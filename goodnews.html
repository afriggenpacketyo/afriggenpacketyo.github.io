Daylight Saving Time 2026 Begins, Shifting Clocks Forward for Most Americans

2026-03-08 00:18:54 EST

Daylight saving time for 2026 commences on Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, causing most Americans to lose an hour of sleep as clocks "spring forward" ahead of the vernal equinox. This annual tradition, which shifts an hour of daylight from morning to evening, has been consistently observed since the 1960s, though its historical purpose of conserving fuel during World Wars has been questioned by later studies. Research by the Transportation and Energy Departments found minimal benefits in energy conservation, traffic safety, and crime reduction, while also associating the shift with negative health effects. Only Hawaii and Arizona, along with several U.S. territories, do not observe this time change, with phones typically updating automatically.
