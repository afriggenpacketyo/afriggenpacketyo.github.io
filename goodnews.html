Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina Achieves Full Federal Recognition After 137-Year Struggle

2025-12-20 15:57:24 EST

The Lumbee Tribe of North Carolina has achieved full federal recognition from the U.S. government, culminating a challenging 137-year struggle for acknowledgment. President Trump's signing of the Lumbee Fairness Act brought emotional relief and celebration to tribal members and Chairman John L. Lowery. This historic milestone unlocks extensive federal resources, including vital funding for housing, education, and significantly, access to health care services through the Bureau of Indian Affairs. Estimates suggest nearly $250 million in health benefits will be provided over four years. Despite opposition from the Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians regarding proper eligibility procedures, the Lumbee now stand as the 575th federally recognized tribe, marking a long-awaited correction of historical injustice and opening new avenues for self-governance and economic development.
