Scientists Uncover Mechanism Linking Epstein-Barr Virus to Multiple Sclerosis Development

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Scientists have made progress in understanding how the common Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) contributes to multiple sclerosis (MS) in some individuals, despite infecting nearly everyone. The key lies in a genetic molecule, HLA-DR15, which, when present in EBV-infected B cells, causes the immune system to mistakenly attack myelin proteins surrounding nerve fibers. This "fatal similarity" programs the body to attack itself, significantly increasing MS risk, particularly if infection occurs in adolescence. Researchers are now exploring treatments targeting this mechanism, offering hope for future interventions.
