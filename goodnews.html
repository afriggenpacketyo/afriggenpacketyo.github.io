An Explanation of Medicare's 3-Day Rule and Its Impact on Skilled Nursing Facility Coverage

2025-09-11 11:08:05 EDT

A lesser-known Medicare regulation, the "3-day rule," significantly affects coverage for care in a skilled nursing facility (SNF) after a hospital visit. The rule mandates that a beneficiary must have a medically necessary inpatient hospital stay of at least three consecutive days for Medicare Part A to cover subsequent SNF costs. Time spent in the hospital under "observation status" does not count toward this requirement, which can lead to unexpected and substantial out-of-pocket expenses for seniors. Understanding this rule, along with potential exemptions through Medicare Advantage plans, is crucial for beneficiaries to navigate their healthcare costs effectively.
