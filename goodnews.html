2025-07-02 14:17:06 EDT

The U.S. government is scaling up a program to combat the New World screwworm, a pest whose flesh-eating larvae can devastate livestock, wildlife, and even humans. The plan involves breeding billions of male screwworm flies, sterilizing them with radiation, and releasing them from airplanes over Mexico and southern Texas. These sterile males then mate with wild females, whose eggs fail to hatch, causing the population to collapse over time. This biological control method, successfully used decades ago, is being revived to create a buffer against the pest's recent migration northward from Central America.
