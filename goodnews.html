Following the White House's announcement of President Trump's diagnosis, this article explains chronic venous insufficiency (CVI). The condition occurs when veins in the legs are unable to effectively pump blood back to the heart, causing blood to pool and leading to symptoms like swelling, achiness, and varicose veins. While it is a common and often benign condition, particularly in older adults, doctors must first rule out more serious causes like heart disease or blood clots. Treatment for CVI typically involves lifestyle changes such as wearing compression stockings, elevating the legs, and exercising to strengthen leg muscles.
