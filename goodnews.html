2025-10-17 05:38:14 EDT

He is best remembered for his historic 1995 "Murayama statement," issued on the 50th anniversary of the end of World War II, in which he expressed "deep remorse" and a "heartfelt apology" for Japan's colonial rule and wartime aggression. This apology became a cornerstone of Japan's foreign relations with its Asian neighbors and set a standard for nearly two decades, though it has been challenged by more recent nationalist leaders.
