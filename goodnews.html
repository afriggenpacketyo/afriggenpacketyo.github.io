NASA Names Artemis III Crew for Next Moon Mission Rehearsal

2026-06-09 13:53:54 EDT

2026-06-09 13:45:34 EDT

NASA's Artemis III mission, slated for 2027, will not involve a lunar landing but will serve as a crucial rehearsal for docking with prototype lunar landers in Earth orbit. The actual landing is planned for the Artemis IV mission in 2028. The Artemis III crew will test rendezvous and docking procedures with landers from SpaceX and Blue Origin, validating critical systems and spacesuits before the lunar surface mission. This adjustment in mission objectives is due to delays in the development of the SpaceX Starship lander. The mission will also test an upgraded heat shield during re-entry. The broader Artemis program aims to establish a long-term human presence on the Moon, with subsequent missions planned to build a lunar base.
