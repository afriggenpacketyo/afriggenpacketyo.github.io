2025-11-22 10:27:29 EST

Cryptocurrency investors will face stricter tax reporting requirements starting with the 2025 tax year, as the IRS will mandate that brokerages issue a new Form 1099-DA. Initially, brokers must report the gross proceeds from digital asset sales, and by 2026, they will also be required to report the cost basis information. This change aims to close a loophole that has made it easier for some investors to avoid reporting capital gains or losses, which are triggered when crypto is sold or exchanged. Experts advise investors to organize their transaction records now, as accurately tracking cost basis can be complex, especially for assets transferred between different wallets and exchanges.
