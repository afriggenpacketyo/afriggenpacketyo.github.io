Study Examines the Impact of Cannabis on Alcohol Consumption

2025-11-19 00:01:00 EST

A new study published in the American Journal of Psychiatry explores whether cannabis use can lead to reduced alcohol consumption. The experiment involved participants smoking marijuana joints with varying levels of THC and then having the opportunity to drink alcohol in a controlled bar-like setting. Results indicated that those who smoked higher potency cannabis consumed 27% less alcohol, while those with lower potency consumed 19% less, compared to a placebo. Researchers caution against recommending cannabis as a substitute for alcohol treatment but acknowledge its potential therapeutic role in alcohol use disorder.
