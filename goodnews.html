This rule suggests that speed limits should be set based on the speed that 85% of drivers naturally travel at, assuming most drivers choose a safe speed. However, critics argue this creates a feedback loop where speeding leads to higher limits, compromising safety for pedestrians and cyclists, especially in urban areas.
