States Grapple with Penny Rounding as Coin Production Ends

2026-03-12 07:53:17 EDT

With the cessation of penny production, several U.S. states are implementing guidance for rounding cash purchases to the nearest nickel. The U.S. Mint ended penny production last year due to the cost exceeding its face value. This has led to shortages and challenges for cash transactions. States like Arizona, Florida, and Indiana are considering or have enacted laws allowing businesses to round cash payments, often using symmetrical rounding (rounding down for prices ending in 1, 2, 6, 7 cents and up for 3, 4, 8, 9 cents). While the Treasury Department suggests no overall effect on consumer prices, some researchers indicate that more frequent rounding up could lead to collective losses for consumers. The transition also raises questions about increased demand for nickels, which are also costly to produce.
