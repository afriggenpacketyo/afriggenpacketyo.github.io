Humpback Whales Achieve Record-Breaking Ocean Crossings Between Australia and Brazil

2026-05-19 20:41:53 EDT

Scientists have identified two humpback whales that completed separate, record-breaking journeys of over 9,300 miles (15,000 kilometers) between Australia and Brazil. These whales, identified by their unique tail markings, traveled in opposite directions, covering greater distances than any previously known humpback migration. This discovery, published in Royal Society Open Science, challenges existing assumptions about humpback whale movements and the separation of populations between mating sites.
