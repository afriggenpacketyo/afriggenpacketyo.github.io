A Guide to the Role and Jurisdiction of the International Court of Justice

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, is based in The Hague and adjudicates legal disputes between states. Comprised of 15 judges from different countries, its jurisdiction applies when nations agree to submit to its authority, either through a blanket declaration or on a case-by-case basis. The court also hears cases based on specific treaties, such as the UN Genocide Convention, which is the basis for South Africa's case against Israel and Ukraine's case against Russia. While its rulings are legally binding, the ICJ has no enforcement mechanism, relying on the cooperation of the involved parties.
