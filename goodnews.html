Uranium: Abundance, Uses, and Environmental Impact

2025-06-23 09:12:00 EDT

Uranium, a naturally occurring radioactive element, is more common than gold and found in trace amounts in rocks, soil, water, and air. It is used in both enriched and depleted forms, with enriched uranium serving as fuel for nuclear power plants and weapons, while depleted uranium is used for radiation shielding and armor-piercing projectiles. The concentration of uranium in the environment is generally low, but higher concentrations in drinking water can pose health risks, though the carcinogenic effects are unclear. Uranium can enter the environment through industrial works and mining, with Kazakhstan, Canada, and Australia being major producers.
