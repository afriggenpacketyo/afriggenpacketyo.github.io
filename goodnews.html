2025-07-05 09:30:01 EDT

Mo Gawdat, a former Google executive and happiness researcher, advocates for a "90-second rule" to better manage feelings of anger and stress. The concept, based on neuroscience, suggests that the physiological response to an emotional trigger, such as the release of stress hormones, lasts only about 90 seconds. After this period, any lingering negative feeling is a result of consciously or unconsciously re-running the thought in one's mind.
