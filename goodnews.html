UK Financial Regulator Proposes Compensation Scheme for Car Finance Mis-selling

2025-10-07 15:11:36 EDT

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) has proposed a compensation scheme for car finance mis-selling, potentially leading to payouts for 14 million agreements made between 2007 and 2024. The scandal stems from "discretionary commission arrangements" where dealers received undisclosed commissions based on customer interest rates, incentivizing higher charges. The FCA expects average payouts of £700 per mis-sold agreement, totaling an estimated £8.2 billion. While a Supreme Court ruling narrowed the scope, lenders have already set aside over £2 billion for potential redress, with the scheme expected to be operational by early next year.
