New study indicates Tylenol use during pregnancy is not linked to autism

2026-04-13 16:42:37 EDT

A comprehensive study from Denmark, involving over 1.5 million children, has found no link between acetaminophen (Tylenol) use during pregnancy and later autism diagnoses. This research contradicts previous concerns, including those amplified by the Trump administration, which had suggested a potential association. The study, published in JAMA Pediatrics, found similar rates of autism diagnoses in children exposed to Tylenol in utero and those who were not. Previous studies had indicated a marginal link that disappeared when controlling for genetic factors, and a US review in 2025 noted potential confounders. Health officials had previously considered a label change for acetaminophen, but this new evidence suggests Tylenol is a safe option for pain and fever relief during pregnancy.
