Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is celebrating a major milestone: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation. This unbroken, handwritten dataset is the longest of its kind in the UK and Ireland, providing an invaluable record of climate change. Since the first entry on July 14, 1795, observers have manually recorded temperature and air pressure, a tradition that continues today alongside modern automated systems. The records have captured major weather events, like the 'Night of the Big Wind' in 1839, and contributed to meteorological science, including the development of the cup anemometer.
