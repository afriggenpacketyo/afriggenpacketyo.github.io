Understanding the Statute of Limitations on Medical Debt in the US

2025-12-01 11:46:20 EST

Like other consumer debts, medical debt is subject to a statute of limitations, which restricts the time a collector has to sue for repayment. This time limit varies by state, typically ranging from three to six years, after which the debt becomes "time-barred," meaning legal action cannot be taken. However, the debt does not disappear and can remain on a credit report for up to seven years. Consumers should be cautious, as making a partial payment or even acknowledging the debt can reset the statute of limitations clock, restarting the period for potential legal action.
