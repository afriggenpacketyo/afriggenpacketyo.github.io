Chinese Military Study Validates Robot-Aided Telesurgery for Remote Medical Care

A Chinese military study published in The BMJ has found robot-aided telesurgery to be as reliable as conventional local surgery, demonstrating its potential to revolutionize remote medical care. Telesurgery enables surgeons to operate remotely using haptic controls and 3D visualization, with movements transmitted via ultra-low-latency networks to a robotic system on the patient's side.
