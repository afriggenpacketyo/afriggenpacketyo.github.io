New York City's Rat Population Potentially Decreasing Due to Harsh Winter and Sanitation Efforts

2026-02-28 10:00:14 EST

New York City's notorious rat population is showing signs of decline, attributed to the city's most severe winter in years which causes rats to cease mating due to hazardous conditions for pups. Rodentologist Bobby Corrigan anticipates a drop in numbers, explaining that adult rats, while cold-hardy, avoid breeding in extreme cold. This environmental factor, combined with the Department of Sanitation's "massive effort to containerize trash," has led to a 14-month streak of decreased rat sightings. Despite these positive developments, experts concede that complete eradication is unlikely, and some "rat tour" operators continue to thrive by guaranteeing sightings for curious tourists.
