Understanding the Differences Between Heat Exhaustion and Life-Threatening Heat Stroke

2025-07-01 07:40:00 EDT

As temperatures rise, it is crucial to distinguish between heat exhaustion and the more dangerous heat stroke. Heat exhaustion involves symptoms like headache, heavy sweating, and dizziness, and is treated by moving to a cool place and rehydrating. However, if not managed, it can progress to heat stroke, a life-threatening emergency where the body's temperature regulation system fails and core temperature can rise rapidly. Signs of heat stroke include confusion, seizures, and loss of consciousness, requiring immediate emergency medical attention and rapid cooling efforts.
