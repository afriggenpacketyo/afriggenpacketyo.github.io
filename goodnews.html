Tiny Zooplankton Play Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New scientific research reveals that tiny marine animals called zooplankton are "unsung heroes" in the fight against climate change. These creatures undertake a massive annual migration in the Southern Ocean, gorging on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface before sinking deep into the ocean to burn the stored fat. This process effectively locks away vast amounts of carbon, equivalent to the annual emissions of about 55 million cars, preventing it from warming the atmosphere. The findings highlight a much larger role for these organisms in regulating the global climate than previously understood.
