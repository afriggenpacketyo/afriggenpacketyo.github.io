Study Reveals "Super Movers" in Their 80s Significantly Reduce Cognitive Decline Risk

2026-07-06 05:00:00 EDT

A new study published in Neurology found that "super movers"—individuals over 80 who maintain an exceptionally fast walking pace—are about 50% less likely to experience cognitive decline than their slower peers. Researchers analyzed data from nearly 4,000 older adults, noting that these fast walkers also tended to preserve hippocampal volume, a brain region crucial for memory. The findings underscore the strong correlation between muscle health and cognitive health, as exercise releases signaling molecules that boost brain cell growth. While genetics play a role, lifestyle habits like regular exercise, healthy eating, and stress management are emphasized as key factors in promoting healthy aging and significantly reducing dementia risk.
