2025-10-17 15:43:49 EDT

Researchers determined the remains belonged to taller-than-average adult men, aged 18-50, who showed signs of early-life stress. Injuries on several skeletons, including blunt-force trauma and puncture wounds, suggest they died in battle or skirmishes, possibly related to a 260 C.E. conflict for the city. DNA analysis indicated the soldiers were not local, with origins spanning northern/Eastern Europe and the Byzantine Empire, providing insight into Roman military diversity and diet.
