2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

A new study reveals that zooplankton, tiny marine animals, play a surprisingly significant role in mitigating global warming through a massive annual migration in the Southern Ocean. These creatures gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface, grow fat, and then sink deep into the ocean to burn this fat, effectively locking away vast amounts of carbon. Researchers estimate this process sequesters as much carbon as the annual emissions of 55 million cars, a much larger amount than previously thought. This discovery highlights the crucial but often overlooked role of these organisms in regulating the planet's climate.
