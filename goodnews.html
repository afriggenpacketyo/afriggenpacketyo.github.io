Jersey Approves Landmark Assisted Dying Legislation

2026-02-26 08:46:28 EST

Jersey has approved a landmark law granting terminally ill adults the right to choose to end their own lives, making it the second British Isle to do so after the Isle of Man. Eligible individuals must have a terminal illness causing unbearable suffering, with an expected lifespan of six months, or twelve months for neurodegenerative conditions like Parkinson's and MND. The legislation, passed with 32 votes in favor, will now seek Royal Assent in the UK, with the first legal assisted deaths potentially occurring by next summer. Supporters, including Humanists UK, laud the move as a momentous vote for compassion, dignity, and choice, while opponents express deep ethical concerns about the law's potential unforeseen changes to Jersey's community and the sanctity of life.
