China's Space-Based Solar Power: The Next Frontier for Green Energy

2026-03-27 17:30:08 EDT

This opinion article explores China's advancements in space-based solar power (SBSP), positioning it as the next frontier for green energy and a potential solution to continuous power generation. SBSP involves orbital systems that collect solar energy via satellites equipped with enormous solar panels, convert it into microwaves or laser beams, and wirelessly transmit it to ground-based rectennas for electricity. The appeal lies in its continuous operation, unaffected by weather, seasons, or nightfall, overcoming significant limitations of terrestrial solar. While the concept isn't new, with early designs dating back to 1968, recent advances in robotics, wireless power transmission, and sharply falling launch costs are making SBSP economically viable, leading major space powers to increasingly recognize its potential.
