Understanding the 90-Day Preference Rule in Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Filings

2026-01-20 11:54:10 EST

The 90-day rule in Chapter 7 bankruptcy, also known as the "preference period," allows a trustee to review and potentially reverse payments of $600 or more made to creditors within 90 days before filing. This rule ensures fair treatment for all creditors, preventing debtors from favoring specific ones before liquidation. An exception exists for ordinary course payments, but large or accelerated payoffs can be clawed back, extending to one year for "insiders" like family members. (not investment advice)
