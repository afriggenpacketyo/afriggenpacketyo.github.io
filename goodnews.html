2026-03-27 21:06:54 EDT

The United Nations General Assembly overwhelmingly passed a resolution declaring the transatlantic slave trade as "the gravest crime against humanity," with 123 votes in favor, 3 against (US, Argentina, Israel), and 52 abstentions (including UK and EU states). While not legally binding, the resolution sends a powerful political message and could bolster discussions on reparations or compensation for descendants of enslaved Africans. UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres noted the wealth of many Western nations was built on "stolen lives and stolen labour," highlighting the deep and often unrecognized wounds of this exploitation. Campaigners welcome the vote as a significant step towards acknowledgment and reparatory justice, emphasizing both financial and educational components.
