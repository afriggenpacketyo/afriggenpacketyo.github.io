James Webb Telescope Discovers Salty Clouds Around the "Pink Planet" GJ504b

2026-06-18 21:49:00 EDT

Researchers using the James Webb Space Telescope have made an unexpected and significant discovery: salty clouds in the atmosphere of the "Pink Planet," formally known as GJ504b. This planetary-mass companion, located 57 light-years from Earth, is estimated to be 25 times Jupiter's mass and significantly colder than typical exoplanets at 550 degrees Fahrenheit. The findings, published in The Astronomical Journal, suggest that in environments too hot for water or ammonia clouds but too cool for silicate clouds, salt clouds can form instead. This breakthrough highlights the Webb telescope's powerful capabilities in detecting colder atmospheric conditions, which were previously "close to impossible" to observe from the ground, opening new avenues for research into diverse planetary environments.
