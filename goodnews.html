China's Yangtze River Fishing Ban Boosts Biomass and Finless Porpoise Population

2026-02-20 23:00:09 EST

This ambitious conservation initiative, backed by a substantial $2.7 billion investment, necessitated the recall of over 111,000 fishing vessels and the successful resettlement of 231,000 fishers. The ban was further strengthened by the enactment of the Yangtze River Protection Law, which also introduced more stringent regulations on water quality, sand mining, and shoreline restoration efforts. The Yangtze, recognized as one of the world's most biodiverse rivers, previously accounted for over 60% of China's freshwater fisheries output.
