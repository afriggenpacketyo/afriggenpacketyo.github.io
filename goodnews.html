Oldest Evidence of Deliberate Fire-Making by Neanderthals Discovered in UK

2025-12-10 15:52:00 EST

Archaeologists in Suffolk, UK, have uncovered what is now considered the oldest known evidence of deliberate fire-making by prehistoric humans, dating back approximately 415,000 years. The significant discovery at Barnham includes a patch of heated clay, heat-shattered handaxes, and two pieces of imported iron pyrite, suggesting Neanderthals actively created and controlled fire rather than merely utilizing natural occurrences. This finding pushes back the timeline for intentional fire-making significantly, highlighting a crucial milestone in human evolution. The controlled use of fire vastly improved diets through cooking, provided warmth for survival in colder climates, and fostered social development through communal gatherings, language, and storytelling.
