Former Japanese Prime Minister Tomiichi Murayama Dies at 101

Tomiichi Murayama, the former prime minister of Japan who issued a landmark 1995 apology for the country's World War II aggression, has died at the age of 101. As the head of the Socialist Party, Murayama led a coalition government and delivered what became known as the "Murayama statement" on the 50th anniversary of the war's end, expressing "deep remorse" for Japan's colonial rule and aggression in Asia. This apology set a precedent for subsequent governments for nearly two decades and remains a pivotal moment in Japan's post-war history. Even after retirement, Murayama was a vocal critic of nationalist politicians who sought to downplay Japan's wartime responsibilities.
