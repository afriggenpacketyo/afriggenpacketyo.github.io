Chinese Scientists Are First to Experimentally Observe Quantum Friction Effect in Graphene

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has become the first to directly observe and measure quantum friction between solid surfaces. Using folded graphene, they discovered that friction did not always increase with thickness; in some cases, thicker folds actually slid more easily. This counterintuitive effect is attributed to how bending the material changes electron movement, locking them into fixed energy levels. The breakthrough could pave the way for developing ultra-low-friction nanodevices and more efficient machines with minimal wear.
