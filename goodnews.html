Navigating Social Security Survivor Benefits After a Spouse's Death

2025-08-30 11:11:00 EDT

This article provides guidance on Social Security survivor benefits, a crucial financial resource for widowed spouses. Eligible individuals, typically aged 60 or older (or 50 with a disability), can claim benefits based on their deceased spouse's work record. It is generally not possible to collect both your own retirement benefit and a full survivor benefit simultaneously; the Social Security Administration will pay the higher of the two amounts. A common strategy is to claim one benefit first while allowing the other to grow, potentially switching to the higher-paying benefit at a later age to maximize lifetime income.
