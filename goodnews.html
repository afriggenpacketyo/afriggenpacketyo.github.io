Viral Photos of Horned "Zombie Rabbits" in Colorado Explained by Wildlife Officials

2025-08-13 20:33:00 EDT

Viral images of rabbits in Colorado with black, horn-like facial growths have sparked alarm online, but wildlife officials have a scientific explanation. The animals are infected with Shope Papilloma Virus, a disease common in the Midwest that causes keratinous tumors to grow on the rabbits' bodies. The virus is spread by ticks and fleas and is not transmissible to humans or other pets. While the growths are usually harmless to the rabbits unless they impede eating, the phenomenon is believed to have inspired the myth of the "jackalope," a fabled rabbit with antelope antlers.
