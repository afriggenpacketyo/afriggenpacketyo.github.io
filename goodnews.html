Auroras, the vibrant northern and southern lights, are caused by solar storms ejecting charged particles that collide with Earth's atmospheric atoms and molecules, making them glow. Researchers are investigating the potential for these solar energy particles to cause "big ozone depletion" in polar regions, though the damage typically heals naturally.
