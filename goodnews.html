Civil Rights Pioneer Claudette Colvin Dies at 86, Honored for Pre-Parks Bus Protest

2026-01-13 20:13:57 EST

Claudette Colvin, a pivotal but often overlooked figure in the modern civil rights movement, has died at 86. Nine months before Rosa Parks' iconic protest, a 15-year-old Colvin was arrested on March 2, 1955, for refusing to give up her seat on a segregated Montgomery bus, driven by a deep conviction for freedom. Her brave act, though less recognized, helped lay the legal and moral groundwork for the Montgomery Bus Boycott and the landmark lawsuit that outlawed racial segregation on city buses. Montgomery Mayor Steven Reed acknowledged her bravery, emphasizing that movements are built by many, including those whose courage comes early and quietly.
