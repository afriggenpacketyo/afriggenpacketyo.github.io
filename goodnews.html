New York City's Ranked-Choice Voting System Explained for Mayoral Primary

2025-06-17 18:33:14 EDT

New York City is utilizing ranked-choice voting in its mayoral primary, allowing voters to rank up to five candidates in order of preference. If a candidate receives over 50% of first-choice votes, they win outright; otherwise, the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated, and the second-choice votes from their ballots are then allocated. This process continues until two candidates remain, with the leading candidate declared the winner. The system has led to unique alliances, with some candidates cross-endorsing each other to prevent a specific candidate, like former Governor Andrew Cuomo, from winning. This is the second time New York City has used ranked-choice voting citywide.
