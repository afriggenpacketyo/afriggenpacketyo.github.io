Unusual 'smiling' fossil discovered on Holy Island by beachcomber

2026-02-19 00:07:07 EST

A woman hunting for fossils on Holy Island, Northumberland, discovered a unique fossil resembling a "smiling" face, which has been identified as a rare crinoid stem fragment. Crinoids are ancient marine animals dating back over 500 million years. The fossil's unusual shape, formed by split and curved ossicles, has captured public attention. This find is part of a tradition of finding "St. Cuthbert's beads" on the island, which are fossilized crinoid stem parts. The British Geological Survey confirmed the fossil's identity and estimated its age at approximately 350 million years.
