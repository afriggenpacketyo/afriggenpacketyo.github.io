European Union Agrees to New Targets to Reduce Food and Fast Fashion Waste

2025-09-09 08:55:00 EDT

The European Union is set to implement new binding targets aimed at significantly cutting waste from food and textiles by 2030. Member states will be required to reduce food waste from households, retailers, and restaurants by 30%, and waste from food processing by 10%. Additionally, the new rules will establish an "extended producer responsibility" scheme, making textile and footwear companies financially responsible for the collection, sorting, and recycling of their products. These measures are designed to combat the significant economic and environmental impact of the nearly 60 million tons of food and 12.6 million tons of textiles wasted annually in the bloc.
