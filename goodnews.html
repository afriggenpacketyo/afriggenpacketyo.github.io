NASA's Curiosity rover discovers new organic compounds on Mars.

2026-04-21 09:55:00 EDT

NASA's Curiosity rover has identified seven organic compounds in Martian rock, five of which are new discoveries on the planet. The findings, published in Nature Communications, also suggest the presence of a compound similar to DNA precursors. Researchers emphasize that these organic compounds, while crucial for life as we know it, could have formed through nonbiological processes. The discovery supports the theory that Mars may have been habitable billions of years ago, potentially harboring conditions suitable for life's origins. The samples analyzed date back at least 3.5 billion years, preserving these prebiotic molecules for eons.
