Thailand Grants Working Rights to Long-Term Myanmar Refugees Amid U.S. Aid Cuts

2025-09-13 15:00:00 EDT

The Thai government has lifted a decades-long ban, granting working rights to approximately 80,000 long-term Myanmar refugees residing in nine border camps. This policy shift comes in response to significant cuts in humanitarian aid, particularly from the United States under President Trump, which had previously been the largest funder of food aid. The move aims to allow refugees to achieve self-sufficiency and economic participation, with the UN Refugee Agency and other aid groups welcoming it as a potential "regional benchmark" for rights-based solutions. Despite challenges like limited skills and the ongoing civil war in Myanmar, this resolution offers a new lifeline for refugees who have spent decades confined to dilapidated camps with minimal opportunities.

2025-09-13 14:03:19 EDT

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu rejected the concept of a Palestinian state hours before the vote, while the U.S. called the resolution a "misguided and ill-timed publicity stunt."
