Britain's Oldest Prehistoric Rock Art Discovered in Welsh Cave

2026-06-01 17:01:43 EDT

Painted rock art found in Bacon Hole cave in Gower, Swansea, has been scientifically confirmed as Britain's oldest prehistoric art, dating back at least 17,100 years. The series of 10 red horizontal stripes, initially dismissed in 1928 as natural mineral seepage, was rediscovered in 2022 and accurately dated using advanced uranium-thorium methods. Archaeologists, including George Nash, believe the art may have served as a "communication system" for hunter-gatherers in a then-treeless, cold landscape, with its meaning now "way beyond our comprehension." This "rediscovery" is highly significant for understanding Wales's deep past, prompting calls for the site to be protected as a scheduled monument.
