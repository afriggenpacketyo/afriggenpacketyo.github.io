Los Angeles Approves Plan to Delay State Upzoning Law, Allowing Moderate Density

2026-03-24 17:07:30 EDT

Los Angeles City Council has adopted a strategy to delay the effects of California's SB 79, a state law overriding local zoning to allow taller, denser buildings near transit. L.A.'s plan will upzone 55 single-family and low-density areas, permitting 4-16 unit buildings up to four stories tall, a less aggressive approach than SB 79's potential nine stories. This decision, aimed at maintaining local control until 2030, was pushed by homeowners seeking to mitigate the state's upzoning. Pro-housing advocates, however, criticize the plan as the least ambitious option, questioning its effectiveness given that a similar provision has not yet resulted in new housing construction, prompting a motion to expand incentives to ensure housing actually gets built.
