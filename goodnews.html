2025-10-16 02:38:00 EDT

Uruguay's Senate has passed a "Dignified Death" bill, making it the first country in Latin America to formally legalize euthanasia. The law permits mentally sound adult citizens in the terminal stage of an irreversible disease to opt for a medically assisted death administered by a healthcare professional. The legislation, which does not permit assisted suicide, reflects strong public support in the socially liberal nation, with over 60% of Uruguayans in favor. This move continues Uruguay's history of progressive laws, following the legalization of cannabis, same-sex marriage, and abortion.
