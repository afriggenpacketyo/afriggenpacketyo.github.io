Scientists Use Environmental DNA to Search for Endangered Axolotls in Mexico City Canals

2025-08-11 19:37:29 EDT

In the canals of Xochimilco, Mexico City, scientists are using an innovative technique to search for the critically endangered axolotl. Instead of relying on nets, which often come up empty, researchers are filtering water to find traces of the salamander's environmental DNA (eDNA). The population has plummeted from an estimated 6,000 per square kilometer in 1998 to just 36 in 2014. While the search has so far found eDNA primarily within protected refuge areas, its discovery in one unprotected site offers a small sign of resilience, highlighting the urgent need for habitat restoration.
