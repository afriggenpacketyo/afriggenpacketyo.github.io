2026-06-02 19:44:13 EDT

New research indicates that engaging in 90 minutes to two hours of weight training per week can substantially decrease the risk of premature death. Data from decades-long studies suggest that consistent resistance training significantly lowers the chance of death from cardiovascular diseases, stroke, and neurological conditions, with a 27% reduction for the latter. The lowest risks of early death, up to 58%, were observed in individuals who combined high levels of both aerobic exercise and strength training. However, the article notes that exceeding two hours of strength training weekly did not yield additional health benefits, underscoring the importance of balanced physical activity for longevity.
