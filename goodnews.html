U.S. to Deploy Billions of Sterile Flies to Combat Flesh-Eating Maggot Threat

2025-07-02 13:05:39 EDT

The U.S. government is escalating its efforts to combat the New World screwworm fly, a pest whose flesh-eating larvae pose a severe threat to the nation's livestock, wildlife, and pets. The strategy involves breeding billions of male flies, sterilizing them with radiation, and releasing them from aircraft over Mexico and southern Texas. These sterile males then mate with wild females, whose eggs consequently do not hatch, causing the population to collapse over time. This sterile insect technique successfully eradicated the pest from the U.S. decades ago and is being revived to counter its recent reappearance.
