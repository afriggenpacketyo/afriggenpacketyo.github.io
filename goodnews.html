The law permits a healthcare professional to perform euthanasia for mentally competent adult patients suffering from an incurable illness that causes "unbearable suffering," without requiring a terminal diagnosis. The move reinforces Uruguay's reputation as one of the most socially liberal nations in the region, having previously legalized recreational marijuana, same-sex marriage, and abortion.
