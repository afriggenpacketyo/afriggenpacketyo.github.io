Scientists have digitally reconstructed the faces of two men from 2,500-year-old skulls excavated at an ancient burial site in Tamil Nadu, India. The project, a collaboration between Madurai Kamaraj University and Face Lab in the UK, aims to understand the appearance of early inhabitants of the Keeladi civilization. This civilization, dating back to 580 BC, suggests a complex urban society existed in southern India independently of the northern Indus Valley Civilisation. The facial reconstructions reveal features primarily of Ancient Ancestral South Indians, but also hint at global migration through traces of Middle-Eastern and Austro-Asiatic ancestries.
