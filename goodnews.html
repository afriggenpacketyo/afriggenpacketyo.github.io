Skomer Island Puffin Numbers Reach Record High Despite Global Decline

2025-05-28 01:09:10 EDT

A record number of puffins, 43,626, have been recorded on Skomer Island off the Pembrokeshire coast, marking a conservation success story amidst global population declines. The Wildlife Trust of South and West Wales attributes the thriving population to an abundance of food and the absence of predators like rats, cats, and foxes on the island. Skomer Island, managed by the WTSWW, is an internationally important seabird island located less than a mile from the Pembrokeshire coast, attracting around 25,000 visitors annually. While puffins are thriving in Pembrokeshire, they remain listed as vulnerable to extinction on the IUCN Red List due to pollution, food shortages, and climate change elsewhere.

Japan Enacts Law for CO2 Emissions Trading Scheme

2025-05-28 00:03:00 EDT

Japan's parliament has enacted a law that requires 300 to 400 major firms to participate in a carbon emissions trading system starting in April 2026. The law targets companies producing 100,000 tons of carbon dioxide or more annually across industries including steel and car production, accounting for nearly 60% of domestic greenhouse gas emissions. Companies will be allotted a maximum emission allowance, and those exceeding the threshold must purchase carbon credits from companies with a surplus. This system aims to incentivize companies to reduce emissions, but decarbonization costs may be passed on to consumers through price increases.
