The Origin and Significance of Juneteenth

2025-06-19 06:58:20 EDT

Juneteenth, a federal holiday observed annually on June 19, commemorates the end of slavery in the U.S. and is also known as Freedom Day or Emancipation Day. The name Juneteenth is a portmanteau that combines the words June and nineteenth, referencing June 19, 1865, when the last group of enslaved people in Texas were informed of their freedom under the Emancipation Proclamation. While the Emancipation Proclamation set the stage for an end to slavery, it was the 13th Amendment to the Constitution that actually abolished slavery nationwide. Celebrations often include parades, parties, concerts, educational workshops, and cultural events centered on art and cuisine, with the holiday serving as an opportunity to remember the country's foundation on centuries of slavery and celebrate independence.
