New Chinese Fossil Reveals Ancient Cross-Equator Migration Route in Pangaea

2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences have identified a new species of dicynodont, an extinct relative of mammals, from a fossil found in Gansu province, China. Named Dinanomodon guoi, this discovery marks the first bidentalian genus shared between China and South Africa, suggesting a cross-equator migration corridor existed during the supercontinent Pangaea. Dicynodonts were herbivorous, mostly toothless animals, ranging from small burrowers to large grazers. This finding provides crucial insights into the ancient distribution and evolutionary paths of these unique creatures across the prehistoric landmasses.
