Chinese Scientists Directly Observe Quantum Effect, Advancing Dark Matter Detection

2026-01-16 06:00:11 EST

Chinese scientists have achieved the first direct observation of the Migdal effect, a quantum phenomenon theorized almost 90 years ago by Soviet physicist Arkady Migdal. This breakthrough, published in Nature, involves detecting a secondary electronic recoil after a neutral particle collides with an atomic nucleus. The University of the Chinese Academy of Sciences team believes this observation offers "unprecedented insights" and could be a crucial step towards directly detecting dark matter, one of modern physics' most profound unsolved mysteries. This advancement promises to deepen humanity's understanding of the universe's fundamental composition.
