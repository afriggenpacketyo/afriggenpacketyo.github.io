Seville's Holy Week Celebrates Faith, Tradition, and Spectacle

2026-04-03 12:52:45 EDT

Seville, Spain, transforms each spring for its Holy Week (Semana Santa), a vibrant blend of faith, tradition, and spectacle. Sixty-one Catholic brotherhoods parade through the city, carrying gilded floats with statues of Christ and the Virgin Mary to the Gothic cathedral. Participants, known as nazarenos, wear distinctive pointed hoods symbolizing penitence, a tradition predating the Ku Klux Klan. While the number of self-identified Catholics has decreased, many non-religious Spaniards still find deep cultural and emotional meaning in these centuries-old processions, which are also a cherished family tradition involving unique customs like children collecting melted wax.
