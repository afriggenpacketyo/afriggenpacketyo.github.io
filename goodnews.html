Rare Sierra Nevada Red Fox Successfully GPS-Collared for Conservation Efforts

2026-02-10 21:35:42 EST

In a significant victory for conservation efforts, a critically threatened Sierra Nevada red fox, one of fewer than 50 believed to remain in the Sierra, has been successfully GPS-collared near Mammoth Lakes. This marks the first time a specimen of this elusive species has been collared in the southern Sierra Nevada, where its existence was only confirmed in 2010 after researchers previously believed it was wiped out since the 1920s. The GPS collar will provide invaluable insights into the fox's seasonal movements and daily life, which can in turn guide long-term recovery strategies for this distinct lineage. Researchers hope to better understand the ecology and conservation needs of this remote, endangered population, whose beautiful thick coat also made them attractive prey for trappers in the past.
