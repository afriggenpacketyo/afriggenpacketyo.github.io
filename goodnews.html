Japanese Superstitions Impede Sale or Rental of Stigmatized Properties

2026-07-18 03:32:00 EDT

In Japan, properties where deaths have occurred are labeled "jiko bukken," or stigmatized properties, making them exceptionally difficult to sell or rent due to deep-seated cultural superstitions. Many Japanese believe these homes carry impurity and misfortune, leading to significant price reductions, often up to 50%, or prolonged vacancies for hundreds of days. Companies like Kachimode offer unique "ghost investigations" and cleansing services, using scientific equipment to monitor for anomalies and provide reports to reassure potential tenants. This practice underscores how cultural beliefs profoundly influence Japan's real estate market, creating both challenges and niche business opportunities.

Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A team of Chinese researchers has published findings in Science Advances detailing how early birds gradually shed their dinosaur tails to evolve into the flight-adapted body plan observed today. Unlike most non-avian dinosaurs, modern birds possess a pygostyle, a short skeletal structure formed by fused tail vertebrae that anchors tail feathers and muscles, crucial for flight. This article highlights the significance of this fossil discovery in understanding one of the most profound morphological changes in terrestrial vertebrate history. The rare find provides crucial insights into the evolutionary assembly of avian anatomy, overcoming previous challenges due to scarce fossil evidence.
