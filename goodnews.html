Understanding the Income Requirements for Filing Chapter 7 Bankruptcy

2025-10-21 14:43:58 EDT

The eligibility for Chapter 7 bankruptcy, which can wipe out unsecured debts, is primarily determined by an individual's income relative to their state's median income for their household size. A "means test" compares the filer's average monthly income over the last six months to this median. If the income is below the median, they generally qualify; if it is above, they must undergo a more detailed calculation of disposable income after accounting for allowable expenses. For those who do not qualify, alternatives like Chapter 13 bankruptcy, debt settlement programs, or credit counseling offer other paths to manage overwhelming debt. (not investment advice)
