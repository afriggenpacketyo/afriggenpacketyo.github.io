New York Implements First Statewide Temporary Ban on New Hyperscale Data Centers

2026-07-14 15:54:35 EDT

New York Governor Kathy Hochul has enacted the nation's first statewide temporary moratorium on new hyperscale data centers, pausing construction for one year. The executive order aims to provide lawmakers time to create a regulatory framework that protects residents and the environment from the immense energy and water demands of these AI-driven facilities. Hyperscale data centers, consuming power equivalent to 50,000 homes and requiring millions of gallons of water, are rapidly expanding. While welcoming AI investments, Hochul emphasized the need for safeguards against rising utility costs, depleted water supplies, and noise pollution, proposing that data centers either produce their own energy or pay a premium. The move has drawn criticism from unions and industry groups, who express concerns about potential job losses and the state's competitiveness in the tech sector.
