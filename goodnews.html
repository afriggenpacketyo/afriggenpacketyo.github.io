2025-07-09 14:19:05 EDT

A notable earthquake swarm has been detected at Mount Rainier in Washington, marking the most significant seismic activity at the active volcano in over 15 years. The U.S. Geological Survey reported that hundreds of small earthquakes began early Tuesday and continued into Wednesday, though they are too small to be felt at the surface or cause any damage. Scientists state there is no immediate cause for concern, attributing the activity to the movement of water within the earth's crust above the magma chamber, rather than magma movement itself. The last comparable event occurred in 2009 and involved over 1,000 small quakes.
