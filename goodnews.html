Japan to Study Offshore "Blue Carbon" for CO2 Capture and Storage

2025-05-19 22:21:00 EDT

The Japanese government is set to initiate offshore "blue carbon" research, aiming to capture carbon dioxide through cultivated sea plants and store it in the deep sea. The goal is to accelerate decarbonization efforts, leveraging the high CO2 solubility in water and the carbon-absorbing properties of marine vegetation like mangroves and kelp. The Environment Ministry will commission the Japan Agency for Marine-Earth Science and Technology and Eneos Corp. to study the behavior of seaweed at considerable depths and assess the environmental impact. Japan aims to capture 1 million tons of CO2 by fiscal 2035 and 2 million tons by fiscal 2040 through blue carbon initiatives.
