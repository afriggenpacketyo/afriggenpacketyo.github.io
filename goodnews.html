While many cultures celebrate new beginnings on different dates according to their own calendars, the Gregorian calendar's global adoption by dominant economic forces popularized January 1st. Unraveling the Science and Mythology Behind Aurora Skies

2025-12-29 03:25:00 EST

The captivating aurora borealis and australis, vibrant green and purple lights dancing across polar skies, are a result of solar storms ejecting electrically charged particles that collide with Earth's atmospheric gases. Oxygen primarily creates green lights, while nitrogen produces purple, blue, or pink hues, with rare interactions leading to scarlet red. While typically visible near the Arctic and Antarctic Circles, a recent solar maximum has expanded their visibility to lower latitudes. Beyond their scientific explanation, these celestial displays have inspired diverse myths, from Norse legends of Valkyries to Indigenous beliefs of spirits, highlighting humanity's enduring awe.
