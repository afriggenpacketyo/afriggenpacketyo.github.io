Daylight Saving Time 2026: Details on the Upcoming Time Change and Its History

2026-03-07 06:00:13 EST

Daylight saving time for 2026 is scheduled to commence on Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, leading most Americans to "spring forward" and experience an hour less of sleep. This yearly adjustment, which effectively transfers an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening, originated in the U.S. in 1918 as a measure to conserve fuel during World War I. Despite its historical intent, government studies in later decades, including one in 2007, found only a minimal decrease in electricity consumption, while the practice has also been associated with various negative health impacts. Notably, Hawaii, most of Arizona, and several U.S. territories do not observe this controversial time change.
