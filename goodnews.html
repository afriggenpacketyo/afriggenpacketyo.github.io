2025-11-19 21:57:05 EST

NASA's Perseverance rover has made a significant discovery on Mars: a peculiar, shiny rock named "Phippsaksla" that scientists suspect is a meteorite. After four years on the Martian surface, the rover's SuperCam instrument detected high concentrations of iron and nickel within the rock, a composition characteristic of meteorites originating from ancient asteroids. Its unique placement on impact-formed bedrock outside the Jezero crater provides valuable clues for understanding meteorite interactions with the Martian environment. Researchers are actively analyzing its composition to confirm its extraterrestrial origin.
