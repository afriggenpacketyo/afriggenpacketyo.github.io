A central Berlin street, formerly known by a name with racist connotations, has been officially renamed Anton-Wilhelm-Amo-Strasse after a prolonged legal battle. This article celebrates the change, honoring Anton Wilhelm Amo, the first Africa-born scholar to earn a European doctorate in 1734. The original name, "Mohrenstrasse," derived from the 18th-century practice of bringing enslaved Africans to Germany as "court moors," was deemed to perpetuate a racist experience. Civil society groups had lobbied for decades for this change, which also marks International Day for the Remembrance of the Slave Trade and its Abolition. The renaming is seen as a crucial step in decolonizing public spaces and recognizing Black history.
