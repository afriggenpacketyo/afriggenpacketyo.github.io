2025-09-07 22:00:09 EDT

A collaborative study between Chinese and American researchers has found that Mars possesses a solid inner core, a discovery that contradicts previous models of the planet's composition. By analyzing seismic data from NASA's InSight lander, the team determined the solid core has a radius of about 600 kilometers, proportionally similar to Earth's. This finding is significant because planets are not expected to form solid inner cores without sufficient time to cool. A total lunar eclipse on Sunday night created a spectacular "Blood Moon," which was visible to stargazers across Asia and parts of Europe and Africa. This phenomenon occurs when the Earth passes directly between the sun and the moon, casting a shadow that makes the lunar surface appear a deep red. The reddish hue is caused by sunlight scattering through Earth's atmosphere, which filters out blue light and allows red light to pass through and illuminate the moon. While observers in Asia had the best view of the total eclipse, those in the Americas were unable to see the event.
