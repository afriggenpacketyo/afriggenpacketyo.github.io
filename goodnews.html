The Word 'OK' Traced Back to a 19th-Century Joke and a Presidential Campaign

2025-07-23 17:27:00 EDT

This article explores the origins of the word "OK," one of the most universally understood terms on the planet. Most scholars trace its first appearance to a Boston newspaper in 1839, where it was used as a jocular abbreviation for "oll korrect," a deliberate misspelling of "all correct." The term gained widespread popularity during President Martin Van Buren's 1840 reelection campaign, as his nickname "Old Kinderhook" conveniently aligned with the letters O.K. Its simplicity and versatility helped it spread globally, particularly through its use in telegraphy and by American soldiers in the 20th century.
