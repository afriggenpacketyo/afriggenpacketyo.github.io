Heat exhaustion, characterized by headache, fatigue, and heavy sweating, requires immediate removal from the sun, hydration, and rest. Heat stroke, a more severe condition, can lead to impaired consciousness and requires immediate medical attention, including rapid cooling of the body. Preventing these conditions involves avoiding excessive sun exposure, staying hydrated, and wearing appropriate clothing. Being aware of the symptoms and taking prompt action can mitigate the risks associated with extreme heat.
