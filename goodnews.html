Challenging Debts After Sale to Collection Agency: Your Rights Explained

2025-12-19 11:41:32 EST

Borrowers have the right to dispute a debt even after it has been sold to a collection agency, according to the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA). Collection agencies are required to send a validation notice within five days of initial contact, detailing the debt amount and the original creditor, and informing consumers of their right to dispute within 30 days. If a written dispute is sent within this timeframe, the collector must cease collection activities until the debt's validity is verified. Many agencies struggle to provide adequate documentation, especially for older debts, potentially making the debt uncollectible. If a debt is valid, exploring debt relief options like settlement or credit counseling may be beneficial.
