China Mandates Mechanical Car Door Handles to Enhance Post-Crash Rescue Safety

2026-02-03 17:59:03 EST

China has become the first nation to mandate a safety change for car door handles, requiring them to open mechanically from either side by 2027. This regulation addresses concerns that electrically powered, hidden door handles, popularized by Tesla and adopted by many automakers, can malfunction after crashes or battery failures, trapping occupants. The issue gained renewed attention after a fatal crash in a Xiaomi vehicle. The new rules also require visible and unobstructed interior mechanical releases, influencing global automakers selling in China and potentially pressuring other markets like the U.S. to follow suit for enhanced safety.
