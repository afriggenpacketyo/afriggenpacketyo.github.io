Germany Seeks Digital Sovereignty, Reducing Reliance on US Tech Due to Data Security Concerns

2026-03-29 03:53:00 EDT

Germany's public sector is actively working to reduce its dependence on US technology, driven by concerns over data sovereignty and potential US government access to sensitive data via the CLOUD Act. The German Center for Digital Sovereignty in Public Administration (ZenDiS) is developing open-source alternatives like openDesk, a flexible office suite, to mitigate risks associated with US-based providers like Microsoft and Amazon. This initiative is seen as crucial for national security, especially given instances of US sanctions impacting individuals globally and the potential for US tech companies to withhold software updates. The effort extends beyond government, with private sector interest also growing, underscoring a broader push for digital independence.
