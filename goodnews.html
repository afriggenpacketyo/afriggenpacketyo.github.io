Scientific Phenomenon of 'Urban Heat Island Effect' Explained Amid UK Heatwave

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As a heatwave affects the UK, temperatures in cities are often significantly higher than in surrounding rural areas due to a phenomenon known as the urban heat island effect. This occurs because dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, slowly releasing it as heat, which raises urban temperatures, especially at night. In contrast, rural landscapes with more vegetation and water sources provide natural cooling through evaporation. As climate change makes heatwaves more frequent and intense, the world's growing urban populations are expected to bear the brunt of this effect.
