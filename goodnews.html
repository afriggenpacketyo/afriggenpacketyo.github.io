Civil Rights Pioneer Bernard LaFayette Dies at 85

2026-03-05 20:09:22 EST

Bernard LaFayette, a revered civil rights leader instrumental in the passage of the 1965 Voting Rights Act, has died at 85. He was known for his courageous groundwork in Selma, Alabama, organizing voter registration campaigns that preceded the historic "Bloody Sunday." A co-founder of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee, LaFayette's commitment to nonviolent civil disobedience shaped numerous desegregation and voting rights initiatives across the South. His life's mission, inspired by Martin Luther King Jr., was to institutionalize and internationalize the nonviolence movement, leaving an indelible mark on American history.
