Orionid Meteor Shower Peaks Next Week, Offering Celestial Spectacle

2025-10-17 19:58:22 EDT

The annual Orionid meteor shower is set to peak around October 22, promising a spectacular display of bright, fast-moving meteors visible in the night sky until November 7. Originating from debris left by Halley's Comet as Earth passes through its orbit, these tiny particles burn up in our atmosphere, creating distinctive long streaks of light. Observers can expect around 15 meteors per hour during the peak, with larger meteors sometimes appearing as brilliant fireballs, even brighter than Venus. For optimal viewing, individuals should find a dark spot away from city lights after midnight, allow their eyes to adjust, and look towards the constellation Orion without the need for binoculars or a telescope.
