2025-10-26 09:20:34 EDT

An estimated 33 million American adults experience a phobia, an intense and often debilitating fear of something that poses little to no actual threat. These fears can range from common anxieties like flying or visiting the dentist to more unusual ones such as a fear of beards or eggs. Psychologists utilize exposure therapy, a method that involves repeatedly and safely confronting the source of the fear until the brain's response diminishes. While challenging for patients, this technique has proven effective in helping individuals manage and even conquer their deepest anxieties, demonstrating that it is possible to regain control over these powerful, misguided fears.
