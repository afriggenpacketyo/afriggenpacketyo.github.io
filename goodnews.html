Astronomers Use James Webb Telescope to Capture First Glimpse of Rocky Planet Formation

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

For the first time, astronomers have directly observed the earliest stages of rocky planet formation around a young, sun-like star named HOPS-315, located 1,370 light-years away. Using the James Webb Space Telescope and the ALMA array, researchers detected silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing in the star's surrounding disk. This discovery provides an unprecedented look into a process similar to how Earth may have formed over 4.5 billion years ago. The findings, published in Nature, suggest that the initial steps of planet formation could be a common cosmic process.
