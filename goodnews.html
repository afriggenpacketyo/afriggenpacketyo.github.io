Health Savings Accounts Can Create Significant Tax Issues for Non-Spouse Heirs

2026-05-09 09:30:01 EDT

While Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) offer triple tax advantages for medical expenses, dying with a large balance can create a "tax bomb" for non-spouse heirs. Unlike spouses, who inherit HSAs tax-free, other beneficiaries, such as children, will find the account loses its HSA status, with assets becoming taxable income in the year of death. This can push heirs into higher tax brackets, unlike inherited IRAs which allow a 10-year distribution window. Financial planners advise account holders to spend down large HSAs, donate them to charity, or distribute them among multiple heirs to mitigate this substantial tax liability.
