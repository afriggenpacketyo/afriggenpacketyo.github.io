Understanding Credit Card Grace Periods

2025-06-25 12:51:58 EDT

A credit card grace period is the time between the end of your billing cycle and your payment due date when you won't be charged interest on new purchases, but only if you pay your full statement balance by the due date. It's a temporary, interest-free loan typically lasting 21 to 25 days, allowing users to earn rewards without incurring interest charges if balances are paid in full each month. Carrying a balance from month to month typically results in losing the grace period, causing new purchases to accrue interest immediately. Cash advances rarely have a grace period, and balance transfers may or may not, depending on the card's terms. The proper use of credit cards can help people. (not investment advice)
