Archaeologists Discover 1,600-Year-Old Christian Monastic Site in Egypt

2026-03-28 10:31:04 EDT

Egyptian archaeologists have unearthed a remarkably preserved 5th-century Christian monastic site in the Qallaya area of Egypt's Beheira Governorate, offering significant insights into early Coptic monasticism. The discovery includes a guesthouse with 13 multipurpose rooms used for hospitality and teaching, alongside service facilities like a kitchen and storage areas. The site also revealed well-preserved walls, ancient paintings, a complete marble column, and numerous pottery shards, some bearing Coptic letters. An early Greek inscription, possibly a tombstone for "Abba Kir, son of Shenouda," further enhances understanding of early Coptic art and the functional progression of ascetic monastic architecture in the region.
