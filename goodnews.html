Berlin Hauptbahnhof Celebrates 20 Years as a Symbol of Reunification and Modern Transport

2026-05-26 02:57:00 EDT

Berlin Hauptbahnhof, the German capital's central train station, is celebrating its 20th anniversary as a pivotal hub and a potent symbol of German reunification. Opened in 2006, the station was strategically built on a site near the former Berlin Wall, signifying a new connection between the city's once-divided parts. Designed by architect Meinhard von Gerkan, it was envisioned as a "cathedral of transport" characterized by its extensive use of steel and glass, maximizing natural light. Despite construction challenges, including high groundwater levels and unexploded World War II bombs, the station has become one of Germany's busiest transport hubs, handling approximately 1,800 trains and over 300,000 passengers daily.
