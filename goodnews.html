NASA Selects First European Astronaut for Artemis Mission Testing Lunar Landers

2026-06-10 01:06:00 EDT

NASA has chosen Italian astronaut Luca Parmitano to pilot the Artemis III mission, marking the first European involvement in the U.S. space agency's key human spaceflight program. The mission, scheduled for 2027, will test new lunar landers in Earth's orbit, rather than on the Moon as initially planned, serving as crucial preparation for the Artemis IV mission to the lunar South Pole in 2028. Parmitano will be part of a four-member crew, alongside NASA astronauts Randy Bresnik (commander), Andre Douglas, and Frank Rubio. This selection builds on the success of the Artemis II mission, which saw humans orbit the Moon for the first time in over fifty years. Parmitano's inclusion is seen as a significant boost for Italy and Europe's role in human space exploration. His prior experience includes two missions to the International Space Station and complex spacewalks.
