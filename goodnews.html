This period varies by state, typically ranging from three to six years, after which the debt becomes "time-barred." While collectors can no longer pursue legal action once the statute expires, the debt does not disappear and may still be pursued through other means. The article explains that making a partial payment or acknowledging the debt can reset the clock on the statute of limitations, and it outlines options for managing medical debt, such as negotiating with providers or seeking debt relief.
