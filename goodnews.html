Explainer: The Role and Function of the International Court of Justice

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

This article explains the function of the International Court of Justice (ICJ), the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, located in The Hague. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, the ICJ settles legal disputes between states, unlike the International Criminal Court which tries individuals. Its jurisdiction depends on whether states have agreed to submit to its authority, either through a general declaration, specific consent, or by being party to a treaty like the Genocide Convention. While its rulings are legally binding, the ICJ has no enforcement mechanism, relying on the cooperation of the involved states.
