Understanding Required Minimum Withdrawals for a $1 Million Retirement Account

2026-05-07 10:50:10 EDT

Reaching $1 million in retirement savings triggers Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) from tax-deferred accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s, mandated by the IRS starting at age 73. The annual withdrawal amount is calculated by dividing the account balance by a life expectancy factor, which decreases with age, increasing the required percentage. These RMDs are taxed as ordinary income, potentially impacting tax brackets, Social Security benefits, and Medicare premiums. Penalties for missing RMDs can be substantial, underscoring the importance of careful planning and portfolio optimization in a volatile economic landscape. (not investment advice)
