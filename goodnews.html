Scientists Use Crossbred Coral Transplants to Restore Miami's Damaged Reefs

2025-07-04 12:54:01 EDT

In a hopeful effort to combat the effects of climate change, scientists are transplanting crossbred coral fragments onto a reef in Miami that was devastated by bleaching. The project, a collaboration between the University of Miami, the Florida Aquarium, and Tela Coral, involves crossbreeding Florida's native Elkhorn corals with a more heat-tolerant variety from Honduras. The Honduran corals thrive in waters that are significantly warmer, similar to conditions Florida's reefs are expected to face in the future. This marks the first time internationally crossbred corals have been permitted for planting in the wild, offering a potential blueprint for reef restoration across the Caribbean.
