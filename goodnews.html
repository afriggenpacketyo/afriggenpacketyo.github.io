Understanding the Physiological Impact of Heatwaves and Vulnerable Populations

2026-05-28 05:03:55 EDT

Heatwaves cause the body's blood vessels to open, lowering blood pressure and increasing heart strain, while sweating leads to fluid and salt loss, potentially causing heat exhaustion. Symptoms include dizziness, nausea, and confusion, and if severe, can escalate to life-threatening heatstroke, a medical emergency. The body strives to maintain a core temperature of 37C, but extreme heat makes this difficult, necessitating physiological responses like increased sweating. Older individuals, babies, young children, and those with underlying health conditions like heart disease or diabetes are particularly vulnerable. Experts advise staying cool, hydrated, and avoiding peak sun hours to mitigate risks.
