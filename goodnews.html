Report Finds Higher-Income Shoppers Are More Likely to Return Purchases

2025-08-29 07:00:01 EDT

A new report from the Bank of America Institute reveals that wealthier households return a higher percentage of their retail purchases compared to other income groups. In 2025, high-income shoppers returned 5.3% of their purchases, while lower-income households returned only 3.7%. The analysis suggests that affluent consumers are more likely to engage in "bracketing," where they buy multiple variations of an item with the intent to return most of them. This trend contributes to the high cost of returns for retailers, which has led many companies to tighten their return policies by shortening return windows or charging fees.
