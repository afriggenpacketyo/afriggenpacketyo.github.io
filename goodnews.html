Face Serum Advert Banned for Misleading Anti-Aging Claims

2026-04-28 19:02:15 EDT

An advert for a £49 Eucerin face serum has been banned by the Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) for misleading claims that it made users look "up to five years younger." The billboard poster asserted the product was "clinically proven" based on a study where 160 people self-reported their perceived age reduction after four weeks. The ASA raised significant concerns about the study's methodology, including the absence of a control group, undisclosed participant recruitment methods, and the fact that the serum was tested in a different climate. Furthermore, all additional evidence provided by the manufacturer, Beiersdorf, consisted of unpublished research, leading to the advert's ban.
