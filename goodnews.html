Homeowners can typically remain in their home for life, provided they use it as their primary residence and fulfill all loan obligations. These responsibilities include paying property taxes, maintaining homeowners insurance, and keeping the property in good condition. The loan becomes due upon the homeowner's death, moving out, or failing to meet these terms, at which point their heirs have several options for repayment.
