Zimbabwe Aims for Value-Added Lithium Exports with New Strategy

2026-05-24 03:00:07 EDT

Zimbabwe has initiated a new strategy to boost its position in the global lithium value chain by exporting its first consignment of lithium sulfate, an intermediate product. This follows an unexpected ban on raw mineral exports, brought forward to curb widespread smuggling and ensure the country derives maximum value from its resources. The Arcadia plant, a subsidiary of China’s Zhejiang Huayou Cobalt, now produces 50,000 metric tonnes annually of lithium sulfate, a crucial step towards further refining into battery-manufacturing staples like lithium hydroxide and carbonate. This milestone marks the first lithium salt ever produced on the continent, signaling Zimbabwe's emergence as a key player in the global market. (not investment advice)
