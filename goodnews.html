First-Ever Ranking of Antidepressant Side Effects Published by Researchers

2025-10-22 02:26:48 EDT

For the first time, researchers have ranked the physical side effects of 30 common antidepressants, revealing significant differences between the drugs. The study, from King's College London and the University of Oxford, analyzed the effects on patients within the first eight weeks of treatment, finding wide variations in weight change, heart rate, and blood pressure. Researchers emphasized that while patients should not stop their medication, the findings should empower them and their doctors to make more informed choices based on individual health profiles. The most prescribed class of antidepressants, SSRIs, generally had fewer physical side effects.
