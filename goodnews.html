China Completes 'Green Great Wall' Section to Contain Three Major Deserts

2025-07-09 00:00:08 EDT

China has completed a significant phase of its 'green great wall' project by finishing a 1,856-kilometer (1,153-mile) ecological barrier to contain three major deserts in Inner Mongolia. The green belt, which crosses the Badain Jaran, Tengger, and Ulan Buh deserts, was finalized with the placement of the last straw checkerboard. This method involves creating a grid of straw to stabilize shifting sands, which is then followed by planting drought-resistant vegetation like sacsaoul trees. The project is a key part of China's extensive efforts to combat desertification and improve its ecological environment.
