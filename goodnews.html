Chinese Study Suggests Fathers Can Pass Exercise Benefits to Offspring

2025-10-12 23:00:52 EDT

A Chinese study involving mice suggests that fathers can pass on the benefits of exercise to their children through genetic mechanisms. The research, published in the peer-reviewed journal "Cell Metabolism," found that offspring sired by exercise-trained fathers exhibited improved muscle mass, lower body fat, and stronger bones, demonstrating intrinsic exercise adaptations and enhanced metabolic parameters. Conversely, the offspring of inactive fathers performed worse in running and strength tests. The team also discovered that injecting sperm microRNAs from exercised mice into normal fertilized eggs reproduced these beneficial traits, highlighting paternal exercise as a "cost-effective route to improve offspring health."
