Australia Orders Chinese-Linked Firms to Divest from Rare Earths Miner

2026-05-18 01:17:00 EDT

Australia has mandated that six companies linked to China sell their stakes in Northern Minerals, a key developer of rare earth elements dysprosium and terbium, within two weeks. Treasurer Jim Chalmers issued the orders due to concerns over attempts by Chinese investors to gain control, citing national interest and compliance with the country's foreign investment framework. Dysprosium and terbium are critical for clean energy applications like hybrid vehicles and wind turbines, as well as specialist defense technologies, with China currently dominating around 95-98% of global processed supply. This strategic move aims to establish Australia as the first significant non-Chinese source for processed dysprosium, reflecting a broader Western effort to limit Chinese investment in critical minerals and secure independent supply chains for these vital resources.
