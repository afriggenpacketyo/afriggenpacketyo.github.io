France's parliament approves assisted dying bill

2026-07-15 15:27:00 EDT

France's parliament has passed a bill that would permit assisted dying for terminally ill adults who are experiencing constant physical suffering. The law requires patients to be able to express a free and informed choice, undergo medical examinations, and have their case reviewed by an expert panel. It applies to citizens and legal residents with a life-threatening, advanced, or terminal illness, excluding those with severe psychiatric disorders or neurodegenerative diseases. The bill, a key promise of President Emmanuel Macron, now awaits a final review by France's Constitutional Council. If approved, France will join several other European countries and some U.S. states in legalizing assisted dying.
