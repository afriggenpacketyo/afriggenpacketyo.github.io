Its mandate has expanded over decades, notably establishing the Blue Line buffer zone and implementing Security Council Resolution 1701 after the 2006 Israel-Hezbollah war, which called for disarming all armed groups near the border. Currently comprising about 7,500 peacekeepers from 47 countries, UNIFIL primarily serves an observer role, conducting patrols and liaising between Lebanese and Israeli forces, with a gradual withdrawal scheduled to conclude by the end of 2027. The mission faces criticism regarding its effectiveness in preventing hostile activities.
