2025-07-28 07:34:00 EDT

These observations were made possible by detecting the infrared light emitted when radiation from the event heats the surrounding dust. The study provides new evidence that so-called "dormant" black holes are more active than previously thought, occasionally feeding on passing stars. This new method opens up possibilities for studying a greater number of these rare events and gaining a better understanding of black holes and their galactic environments.
