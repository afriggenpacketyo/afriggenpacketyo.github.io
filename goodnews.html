The Origins and Significance of Juneteenth

2025-06-19 10:21:18 EDT

Juneteenth, a portmanteau of June and nineteenth, commemorates the end of slavery in the United States, specifically marking the day in 1865 when the last enslaved people in Texas were informed of their freedom. While President Lincoln signed the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863, its implementation in Confederate territories was delayed until the Union Army's arrival in Texas. The 13th Amendment to the Constitution later abolished slavery nationwide. Celebrations of Juneteenth vary, often including parades, parties, concerts, and educational events, serving as a joyful anniversary of independence and a reminder of the country's foundation on centuries of slavery.
