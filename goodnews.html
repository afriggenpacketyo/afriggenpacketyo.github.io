Climate Change Rewriting Polar Bear DNA in Greenland

2025-12-16 06:15:00 EST

Researchers have detected significant changes in polar bear DNA in southeast Greenland, suggesting the animals are rapidly adapting to warmer climates. This study, believed to be the first to find a statistically significant link between rising temperatures and changing DNA in a wild mammal species, revealed genes related to heat stress, aging, and metabolism behaving differently. These "jumping genes" are actively rewriting the bears' genetic code, potentially aiding their survival in a disappearing sea ice habitat and with evolving, more plant-based diets. While offering some hope for the species' future, scientists emphasize that these adaptations do not diminish the urgent need to reduce global carbon emissions and slow temperature increases to prevent widespread extinction, stressing that complacency is not an option.
