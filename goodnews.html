Kebab Company Fined £500,000 for Fraudulent Lamb Product Sales

2026-06-07 03:11:52 EDT

Kismet Kebabs Ltd, a company supplying takeaways and restaurants, has been fined £500,000 and ordered to pay £259,298 in costs for fraudulently selling "lamb" products that were primarily composed of fat, skin, and other meats. The court heard the firm engaged in "considerable dishonesty" over a prolonged period, misleading wholesalers, retailers, and consumers about the actual meat content, which often contained little actual lamb. Investigations by trading standards teams revealed significant discrepancies between declared and actual meat content, leading to the termination of a primary authority partnership with Essex council due to serious labelling and public health concerns. The company admitted to fraud by false representation.
