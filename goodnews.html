Understanding and Reducing "Phantom Energy" Drain in Homes

2025-09-03 11:04:07 EDT

"Phantom energy," or vampire energy, refers to the electricity consumed by devices when they are plugged in but not actively in use, accounting for 5% to 10% of home energy use. Appliances like internet-connected TVs can draw significantly more power in standby mode than regular ones. This wasted electricity contributes to climate change by increasing demand for emissions-releasing power sources. Experts recommend unplugging chargers and unused appliances, or disabling smart features, to reduce both utility bills and carbon emissions. Individual actions, even small ones, can collectively lead to substantial reductions in U.S. emissions and influence broader societal norms towards sustainability.
