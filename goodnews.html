Discovery in Britain Reveals Oldest Evidence of Human-Made Fire at 400,000 Years Old

2025-12-11 06:00:00 EST

Archaeologists have uncovered the earliest evidence of humans intentionally making fire at a 400,000-year-old site in eastern Britain, pushing back previous estimates by over 350,000 years. The discovery includes fragments of iron pyrite alongside fire-cracked flint handaxes, suggesting early humans, possibly Neanderthals, brought the rare pyrite to the site specifically for fire-starting. This ability to create fire, rather than just utilize natural fires, is considered one of the most pivotal discoveries in human history. It significantly advanced early human survival, enabling better food preparation, predator deterrence, settlement in colder climates, and fostering social and behavioral evolution around campfires.
