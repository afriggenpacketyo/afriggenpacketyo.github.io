2025-09-27 22:29:41 EDT

Lieutenant Colonel George E. Hardy, the last surviving Tuskegee Airman who flew combat missions in World War II, has died at the age of 100. Hardy, a member of the 332nd Fighter Group, completed 21 missions in Europe and 45 in the Korean War, followed by 70 in the Vietnam War. Remembered for his courage, resilience, and perseverance against racism, he received numerous honors, including the Distinguished Flying Cross with Valor and the Congressional Gold Medal. Beyond his military service, Hardy considered his education his greatest achievement, earning degrees in electrical and systems engineering. His passing marks the end of an era for the pioneering Black aviators.

Indian Researchers Win Ig Nobel Prize for Study on Smelly Shoes

2025-09-27 20:25:28 EDT

Initially aiming to design better shoe racks, they discovered the pervasive problem was odor, caused by the bacterium Kytococcus sedentarius. Their research, based on a survey of university students, led to the development of a prototype UVC light-equipped shoe rack designed to sterilize footwear. Experiments showed that a short burst of UVC light effectively eliminated the "rotten-cheese-like" smell, replacing it with a mild burnt-rubber scent, earning them recognition for their delightfully oddball yet inventive scientific endeavor.
