Australia Approves Vaccine to Combat Chlamydia Epidemic Threatening Koalas

2025-09-09 23:22:07 EDT

A vaccine developed by University of the Sunshine Coast scientists has received approval for rollout, offering a critical tool to save Australia's endangered koala population from a devastating chlamydia epidemic. The disease, a different strain from human chlamydia, causes painful infections, blindness, infertility, and is often fatal, contributing to up to 50% of koala deaths. Current antibiotic treatments can be deadly by destroying essential gut bacteria. The single-dose vaccine, proven to reduce symptoms and mortality by at least 65%, provides a significant hope, though habitat loss remains another major threat that also needs addressing.
