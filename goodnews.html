Understanding Pentecost: The Birthday of the Christian Church

2026-05-22 11:56:00 EDT

Pentecost, also known as Whitsun or Trinity Sunday, is a Christian holiday celebrating the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the Apostles 50 days after Easter. Its roots lie in the Jewish Feast of Weeks. The event is seen as a "miracle of understanding" where the disciples, empowered by the Holy Spirit, could communicate with diverse crowds, leading to the formation of the early Christian Church. The holiday is marked by traditions like the scattering of rose petals in some churches, symbolizing the tongues of flame, and is celebrated globally as a public holiday in many countries.
