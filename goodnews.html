Businesses are minimizing the impact of tariffs by utilizing the "first sale rule," a decades-old provision in U.S. customs law. This rule allows importers to calculate duties based on the initial sale price between a manufacturer and a vendor, rather than the inflated price from an intermediary. To apply the rule, there must be at least two sales involving an overseas producer and intermediary, carried out independently with proof the item is destined for the U.S., along with documentation of the first sale price.
