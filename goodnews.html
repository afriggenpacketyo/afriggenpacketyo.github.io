Brazil Deploys Bacteria-Infected Mosquitoes to Combat Dengue Fever

2025-07-26 09:19:53 EDT

Brazil is undertaking a massive public health initiative by breeding and releasing millions of mosquitoes infected with Wolbachia bacteria to combat dengue fever. This innovative approach works because the bacteria prevents the dengue virus from replicating inside the mosquitoes, effectively stopping its transmission to humans. A pilot program in the city of Niterói demonstrated remarkable success, reducing dengue cases by approximately 90% and also significantly lowering rates of Zika and chikungunya. The Brazilian government is now expanding this 'Wolbito' program nationwide, building new insect-rearing factories to protect millions from these debilitating diseases.
