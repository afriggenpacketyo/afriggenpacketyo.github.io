EU Court Bans 'Golden Passport' Programs

2025-05-04 00:32:00 EDT

The European Court of Justice (ECJ) has banned Malta's "golden passport" program, the last of its kind in the EU, ruling that it undermines the concept of EU citizenship. The court found that Malta's policy made nationality a "mere commercial transaction" and that the "practice does not make it possible to establish the necessary bond of solidarity and good faith between a member state and its citizens, or to ensure mutual trust between member states." While purchased passports are now ruled out, "golden visas" that offer residence permits for investments still exist.
