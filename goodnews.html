Debt Forgiveness vs. Debt Consolidation

2025-06-10 11:30:25 EDT

Debt forgiveness and debt consolidation are two options for individuals struggling with debt, but they offer different approaches and consequences. Debt consolidation combines multiple debts into one new loan or payment plan, potentially lowering the interest rate, but it does not reduce the principal balance. Debt forgiveness, or debt settlement, involves negotiating with creditors to accept less than what is owed in return for a lump-sum payment, which can significantly reduce the amount owed but often damages credit. Choosing between the two depends on individual financial circumstances and priorities, with debt consolidation being a less risky option for those who can manage monthly payments and debt forgiveness being a potential option for those facing bankruptcy.
