TSA to Impose $45 Fee for Domestic Air Travelers Without REAL ID

2025-12-02 01:38:34 EST

The Transportation Security Administration (TSA) has announced that starting in February, US air travelers aged 18 and older without a REAL ID-compliant license will be charged a $45 fee. This fee applies to passengers who do not have another acceptable form of identification, such as a passport, and need to use the TSA's alternative "Confirm.ID" verification system. The REAL ID requirement, a post-9/11 security measure, has been in effect since May, but the new fee is intended to incentivize the remaining non-compliant travelers to update their identification. Officials noted that paying the fee does not guarantee entry through security if identity cannot be verified.
