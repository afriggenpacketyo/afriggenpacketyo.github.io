China's Thalassaemia Treatment Shows Promise for Inherited Blood Disorder

2026-04-28 03:31:25 EDT

A new base-editing drug developed in China, known as CS-101, is offering hope for patients with thalassaemia, a common inherited blood disorder. China faces a significant burden with 30 million gene carriers and millions affected by severe forms of the disease, which can require lifelong transfusions and lead to premature death. The therapy utilizes the transformer Base Editor (tBE) technology. Early trials involving five Chinese patients with beta-thalassaemia have shown rapid restoration of haematopoietic function, leading to increased total haemoglobin and fetal haemoglobin levels, effectively freeing them from the need for blood transfusions.
