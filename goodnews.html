The Historical and Linguistic Journey of the Thanksgiving Turkey

2025-11-26 05:00:00 EST

The turkey, a centerpiece of American Thanksgiving, has a complex and somewhat unflattering history within the English language, where its name is associated with failure and abrupt endings. The bird's name, *meleagris gallopavo*, likely originated from a case of mistaken identity by European colonists, who confused it with the African guinea fowl, which was already known as a "turkey." Etymologists trace the term back to traders bringing the guinea fowl through the Turkish region. Despite its confusing origins, the bird became a holiday staple after being brought back to North America by English settlers.
