Christopher Nolan's 'The Odyssey' Features American Accents, Challenging Period Film Conventions

2026-07-16 08:00:00 EDT

The trailer for Christopher Nolan's new film adaptation of 'The Odyssey' has sparked debate due to its cast, including British actors, speaking with contemporary American accents. This choice challenges the long-standing cinematic convention, dubbed 'The Queen's Latin,' where period and fantasy epics often feature British accents. Dialect coaches explain that this trope originated from Hollywood coding villains as British elites and heroes as Americans in early epics, associating British accents with imperialism. Nolan's approach, inspired by Emily Wilson's stripped-down translation, aims for an "earthy narrative" with "emotional not intellectual meaning," suggesting a shift in how power and relatability are conveyed in film.

This move grants the government control over the plant's future, preventing its closure and avoiding reliance on imports for virgin steel, a product not made elsewhere in the UK. Unions have welcomed the decision, urging serious investment to transform the industry into a leading producer of green steel.
