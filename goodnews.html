2025-07-17 12:40:35 EDT

Stargazers are preparing for the annual Perseid meteor shower, one of the year's most impressive celestial events, which will be active from July 17 to August 24. The phenomenon occurs as Earth passes through the debris trail of Comet 109P/Swift-Tuttle, causing small particles to burn up in the atmosphere and create bright streaks of light. For optimal viewing, experts recommend finding a dark location away from city lights, preferably between midnight and sunrise. No special equipment is needed, but clear skies are essential. Viewers should allow their eyes to adjust to the darkness for the best chance of spotting the meteors radiating from the constellation Perseus.
