Japan to Study Offshore Carbon Capture and Storage Using Marine Plants

2025-05-19 22:21:00 EDT

The Japanese government is initiating offshore "blue carbon" research to capture carbon dioxide through cultivated sea plants and store it in the deep sea, aiming to accelerate decarbonization efforts. The Environment Ministry will commission the Japan Agency for Marine-Earth Science and Technology and Eneos Corp. to investigate the behavior of seaweed at significant depths and assess the environmental impact of such projects. This initiative seeks to mitigate climate change by leveraging the high CO2 absorption capacity of marine vegetation, especially in light of declining absorption capacity of forests due to aging trees. Japan aims to capture 1 million tons of CO2 by fiscal 2035 and 2 million tons by fiscal 2040 through blue carbon.
