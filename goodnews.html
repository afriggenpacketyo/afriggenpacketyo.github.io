British Columbia Adopts Permanent Daylight Saving Time Despite Health Concerns

2026-03-07 18:25:42 EST

British Columbia is preparing to permanently adopt daylight saving time, a decision strongly backed by over 90% of its residents who favor extended evening daylight. Premier David Eby noted that the province's geographical position already results in dark mornings during winter, making the shift less impactful for daily routines. However, this move faces significant opposition from sleep medicine and public health experts, who warn of various negative health consequences. They highlight that permanent daylight saving disrupts natural circadian rhythms, potentially leading to increased car accidents, heart attacks, strokes, and obesity, as evidenced by a failed U.S. attempt in the 1970s and recent Stanford research. Experts advocate for permanent standard time due to its proven health benefits.
