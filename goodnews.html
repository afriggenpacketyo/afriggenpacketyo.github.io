Sunscreen Explained: Understanding SPF and Star Ratings for Effective Protection

2025-08-11 04:51:35 EDT

With high UV levels expected across the UK, understanding sunscreen labels is crucial for skin protection. The Sun Protection Factor (SPF) indicates how much UVB radiation, which causes sunburn, is blocked; SPF 30 is recommended and filters about 97% of UVB rays. The star rating, from one to five, measures protection against UVA radiation, which is associated with skin aging and cancer. For optimal safety, experts advise choosing a sunscreen with both a high SPF and a high star rating. Sunscreen should be reapplied at least every two hours, as its effectiveness diminishes with sweat and water exposure.
