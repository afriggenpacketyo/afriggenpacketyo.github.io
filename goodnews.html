Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A team of Chinese researchers has published significant findings in Science Advances, illustrating how birds gradually evolved from their dinosaur ancestors by shedding their long, bony tails step by step. This fossil discovery provides crucial insights into one of the "most profound morphological changes" in terrestrial vertebrate history, detailing the evolutionary assembly of the flight-adapted bird body plan. Unlike most non-avian dinosaurs, modern birds possess a pygostyle, a short, fused skeletal structure formed by the final caudal vertebrae, which acts as a vital anchor for tail feathers and muscles essential for flight. This research addresses the previous "exceeding rarity" of early-diverging bird and birdlike dinosaur fossils, significantly enhancing our understanding of avian evolution.
