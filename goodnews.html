Cyclosporiasis: Understanding the Parasitic Illness and Prevention

2026-07-17 09:32:35 EDT

This article provides a comprehensive explanation of cyclosporiasis, a gastrointestinal illness caused by the cyclospora parasite, which typically spreads through raw produce and water contaminated with human feces. The most commonly reported symptom is watery diarrhea, often described as "explosive," alongside cramps, nausea, fatigue, and loss of appetite, with symptoms potentially lasting from several days to over a month. The illness is seasonal in the US, with past outbreaks frequently linked to contaminated raw fruits, vegetables, and herbs such as lettuce, basil, and berries. While generally not life-threatening and usually resolving on its own, it poses a greater risk to individuals with weakened immune systems. Prevention involves thoroughly washing produce, though cooking is recommended when possible, as some foods may retain the parasite.
