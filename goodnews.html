Its significance lies in being the first of its earliest type confirmed to have been minted in Edinburgh, a central part of the Scottish kingdom, challenging previous assumptions that all early David I coins originated from Carlisle. This discovery profoundly enhances experts' understanding of how and where coinage was produced in medieval Scotland, a period with scarce documentary evidence.
