Explainer: Why Urban Areas Experience Higher Temperatures Than Rural Surroundings

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

Urban areas often experience significantly higher temperatures than the surrounding countryside due to a phenomenon known as the "urban heat island" effect. This occurs because cities are dominated by hard, dark surfaces like asphalt roads and buildings, which absorb and retain the sun's energy rather than reflecting it. This stored heat is slowly released, warming the air, especially at night. The effect is compounded by a lack of vegetation and water for natural cooling, as well as waste heat from vehicles and air conditioning, making city dwellers more vulnerable during heatwaves.
