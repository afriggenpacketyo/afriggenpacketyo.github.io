2025-12-02 06:30:00 EST

This article explains the history and impact of Giving Tuesday, an international day of charitable giving held on the first Tuesday after Thanksgiving. Launched in 2012 by New York's 92nd Street Y and the United Nations Foundation, the movement was created as a positive response to the consumerism of Black Friday and Cyber Monday. It quickly went viral, growing from an estimated $10 million in online donations in its first year to $3.1 billion in the U.S. in 2023. Now an independent organization active in 80 countries, Giving Tuesday encourages not only monetary donations but also volunteering and other forms of civic engagement, promoting a broader conversation about effective philanthropy.
