US Treasury to add Trump's signature to paper currency for 250th anniversary

2026-03-26 18:41:37 EDT

The Treasury Department announced that President Donald Trump's signature will be featured on U.S. paper currency to commemorate the nation's 250th anniversary. This marks the first instance of a sitting president's signature appearing on legal tender, with the Treasurer's signature being removed for the first time since 1861. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent stated that the move recognizes the administration's "historic achievements" and commitment to economic growth. The first $100 bills with Trump's signature are slated for production in June, with other denominations to follow.
