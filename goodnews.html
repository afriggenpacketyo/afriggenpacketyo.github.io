New Research Links Abdominal Muscle Contractions to Brain Fluid Movement and Health

2026-05-07 19:04:50 EDT

New research suggests that coordinated contractions in abdominal muscles, like those during walking, play a role in brain health by propelling fluid movement within the skull. Neuroscientist Patrick Drew's team observed mice brains moving milliseconds before a step, linking this motion to the vertebral venous plexus, a network of veins connecting the abdomen to the spine. This "hydraulic system" increases pressure on the brain, moving it forward and potentially helping to flush out protein waste and other unnecessary materials. Future research will explore how the brain detects these mechanical signals and the impact of conditions like obesity on this belly-brain relationship.
