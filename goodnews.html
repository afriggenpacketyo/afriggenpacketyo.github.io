New York City Rat Population May Decline Due to Severe Winter Conditions

2026-02-28 10:00:14 EST

New York City's notorious rat population might be on the decline following one of the city's most severe winters in years, according to rodentologist Bobby Corrigan. Extreme cold causes rats to stop mating, leading to fewer offspring in the spring, though adult rats are well-equipped to survive the cold in their burrows. This potential decline comes as the city's Department of Sanitation reports a 14-month decrease in rat sightings, attributed to a new policy requiring sealed trash containers. While a complete eradication is unlikely, these combined factors suggest progress in managing the city's persistent rodent problem.
