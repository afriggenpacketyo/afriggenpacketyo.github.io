MPs Vote to Decriminalise Abortion for Women in England and Wales

2025-06-17 16:32:08 EDT

Members of Parliament in England and Wales have voted to decriminalize abortion for women, marking the biggest change to abortion laws in nearly 60 years. The amendment to the Crime and Policing Bill, put forward by Labour MP Tonia Antoniazzi, passed with a significant majority. While the law will still penalize those who assist a woman in obtaining an abortion outside the legal framework, women who terminate their pregnancies outside the rules will no longer face police investigation. The move was supported by abortion providers and various organizations, while some expressed concerns about the limited protections afforded by the law.
