Understanding Debts That Do Not Qualify for Debt Settlement Programs

2025-10-16 15:14:33 EDT

This article clarifies that not all debts are eligible for settlement programs, which typically negotiate with creditors for reduced lump-sum payoffs, emphasizing the importance of understanding exclusions. Debts generally excluded fall into two main categories: those backed by government authority and those secured by collateral, where lenders have other means of recovery. Examples include secured debts like mortgages and auto loans, federal student loans, tax debts owed to the IRS, child support, alimony, court fines, and criminal restitution. For these non-qualifying debts, alternative strategies such as Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 bankruptcy, or specialized government programs like the IRS's 'Offer in Compromise,' may provide more comprehensive solutions.
