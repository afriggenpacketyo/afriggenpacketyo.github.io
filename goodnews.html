Scientists Successfully Extract and Analyze Ancient RNA from a 39,000-Year-Old Woolly Mammoth

2025-11-14 11:41:30 EST

Researchers have extracted and analyzed RNA from the remarkably well-preserved remains of a woolly mammoth named Yuka, which died approximately 39,000 years ago in Siberia. Unlike DNA, which is a blueprint, RNA reveals which genes were active at the time of death, providing a snapshot of the animal's cellular processes. The findings, published in the journal Cell, confirmed the presence of RNA related to muscle function and stress responses, consistent with the theory that the mammoth was attacked by cave lions. This breakthrough opens new possibilities for studying extinct species and even ancient RNA-based viruses.
