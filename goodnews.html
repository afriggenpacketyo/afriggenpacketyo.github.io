The Ingenious Water-Flow Model of the Economy by Bill Phillips

2026-04-07 06:30:00 EDT

Economist Bill Phillips, renowned for the Phillips Curve, developed a unique water-flow machine in his garage in 1949 to model the British economy. This Rube Goldberg-like device, with water flowing between tubs representing economic sectors, demonstrated how government spending and taxation influenced economic flows, impressing even skeptical academics at the London School of Economics. Phillips's work aimed to understand and moderate the chaotic business cycles of booms and busts, building on Keynesian ideas of government as a balancing force. His subsequent graph, illustrating the inverse relationship between unemployment and wage growth (inflation), became a foundational tool for central bankers in managing economic stability.
