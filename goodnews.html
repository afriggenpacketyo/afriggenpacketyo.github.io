Study Suggests Diverse Exercise Regimen is Key to Boosting Health and Longevity

2026-01-20 20:17:52 EST

A 30-year study tracking 110,000 US adults suggests that engaging in a variety of physical activities each week is more effective for boosting health and living longer than focusing on a single sport. Researchers found that active individuals who diversified their exercise routines were 19% less likely to die during the study period, a greater effect than individual sports. While total exercise amount remains crucial, combining activities like resistance training and aerobic exercise offers complementary benefits.
