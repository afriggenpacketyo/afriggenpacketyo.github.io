Tiny Zooplankton Play Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny marine creatures called zooplankton are unsung heroes in the fight against climate change, locking away vast amounts of carbon in the deep ocean. These animals gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton near the surface in Antarctica, grow fat, and then sink hundreds of meters to burn that fat, sequestering the carbon. This seasonal migration process stores as much carbon as the annual emissions of about 55 million gasoline-powered cars. The discovery highlights a crucial, previously underestimated natural process for mitigating global warming, even as threats to these creatures grow.
