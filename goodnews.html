Nobel Laureate Chen-ning Yang, Renowned Physicist, Dies at 103

2025-10-18 00:23:49 EDT

Nobel Prize-winning theoretical physicist Chen-ning Yang has died in Beijing at the remarkable age of 103, leaving behind an indelible legacy in the scientific community. Widely regarded as one of the 20th century's greatest physicists, Yang's most significant contribution was the co-authorship of the Yang-Mills theory in 1954 with Robert Mills. This revolutionary set of equations provided the mathematical foundation for understanding the electromagnetic, weak, and strong fundamental forces of nature. The Yang-Mills theory became a cornerstone of the Standard Model, which unifies these forces and elucidates the behavior of all known elementary particles. His profound insights fundamentally reshaped modern physics and continue to influence scientific research globally.
