Exhibition in Potsdam Re-examines Cultural Legacy of East Germany's Plattenbau Architecture

2025-09-13 06:39:00 EDT

A new exhibition in Potsdam, Germany, is exploring the complex and divisive cultural legacy of the 'Plattenbau,' the prefabricated concrete slab apartment blocks that defined much of East German urban architecture. Initially promoted by the socialist state as a modern solution to housing shortages and a symbol of utopian community living, these buildings became associated with dystopia and social decay after German reunification. The exhibition, "Wohnkomplex: Art and Life in Plattenbau," uses artworks to explore these dualities, from the collective memory of a shared living environment to the painful transformation marked by unemployment and the rise of neo-Nazism in the 1990s. The show is part of a broader renewed interest in reassessing the architectural and social history of the GDR.
