NASA Shares Close-Up Images of Rare Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

2025-11-19 20:09:00 EST

NASA has released new close-up images of 3I/ATLAS, a rare interstellar comet making a unique flyby through our solar system. Discovered in July, this celestial object represents only the third confirmed interstellar comet to enter our cosmic neighborhood. The comet, estimated to span between 1,444 feet and 3.5 miles across, is anticipated to make its closest approach to Earth in mid-December, passing within approximately 170 million miles. Scientists hypothesize that it originated in a star system predating our own, offering a profound "window into the deep past." NASA officials have unequivocally stated that this "friendly solar system visitor" is indeed a comet, dispelling any circulating rumors of it being an alien spacecraft.
