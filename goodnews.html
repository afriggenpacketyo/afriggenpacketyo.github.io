Early Peanut Introduction Reduces Allergies in Children, Study Confirms

2025-10-20 19:39:52 EDT

A new study published in Pediatrics reveals that landmark 2015 medical advice to introduce peanut products to infants as early as four months has significantly reduced peanut allergies. Approximately 60,000 children have avoided developing peanut allergies, with rates in children aged 0-3 declining over 27% after initial guidance and over 40% after expanded recommendations in 2017. This real-world data reinforces the effectiveness of early allergen introduction, despite initial slow adoption by pediatricians. Advocates emphasize continued education and policy changes, such as including peanut products in infant food packages, to further combat rising food allergy prevalence.
