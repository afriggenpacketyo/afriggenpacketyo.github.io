Researchers Identify Cause of Rare Blood Clotting in Johnson & Johnson COVID Vaccine

2026-02-11 17:00:00 EST

Scientists believe they have identified the cause of vaccine-induced immune thrombotic thrombocytopenia (VITT), the rare but deadly blood clotting linked to adenovirus-based COVID vaccines like Johnson & Johnson's. Researchers found VITT was an immune overreaction to the adenovirus, specifically a subset of antibodies that cross-reacted with a blood clotting molecule. Crucially, this overreaction occurred in genetically predisposed individuals who also had an additional small genetic change in their immune cells. This landmark discovery could lead to safer vaccine designs, preventing future rare adverse events and bolstering public trust in essential vaccine platforms.
