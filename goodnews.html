Understanding Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) for a $250,000 retirement account

2026-05-06 10:58:18 EDT

Retirees with a $250,000 traditional IRA or 401(k) must begin taking Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) at age 73, with annual withdrawal amounts determined by the IRS life expectancy factor. For instance, at age 73, the RMD is approximately $9,434, increasing to over $12,300 by age 80. These mandatory withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income and can potentially push retirees into higher tax brackets or affect Medicare premiums. Failure to take the full RMD can result in a substantial penalty. The article also suggests investment strategies like high-yield savings accounts, gold, and dividend stocks for managing remaining retirement funds.
