Perseid Meteor Shower Peak in August to Be Obscured by Bright Moon

2025-08-11 01:11:01 EDT

The annual Perseids meteor shower, one of the most anticipated celestial events, is expected to peak on August 12-13. However, this year's spectacle will be significantly diminished by a bright waning gibbous moon, which will be 84% full. According to the American Meteor Society, the moonlight is projected to reduce visibility by at least 75%, lowering the expected hourly meteor count from around 50 to just 10-20. For better viewing, experts suggest looking for the meteors a week after the peak when the sky will be darker. The Perseids are caused by debris from the comet 109P/Swift-Tuttle interacting with Earth's atmosphere.
