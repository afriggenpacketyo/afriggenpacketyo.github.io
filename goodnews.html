Chinese Researchers Make First Direct Observation of Quantum Friction Effect

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A team of scientists in China has reported the first-ever direct experimental observation of quantum friction, a phenomenon occurring between solid surfaces at the quantum level. While studying folded graphene, the researchers discovered that friction did not always increase with thickness; in some cases, thicker folds slid more easily. This counterintuitive effect was traced to how bending the material altered electron movement, restricting energy loss as heat. The breakthrough, published in Nature Communications, could pave the way for developing ultra-low-friction materials for use in highly efficient nanodevices and machines.
