New Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Ancient Cross-Equator Migration Route in Pangaea

2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences have identified a novel species of dicynodont, *Dinanomodon guoi*, based on a fossil discovered in Gulang county, Gansu province, China. This significant finding marks the first bidentalian genus shared between China and South Africa, strongly suggesting the existence of a cross-equator migration corridor during the supercontinent Pangaea. Dicynodonts were herbivorous, mostly toothless animals considered ancient relatives of modern mammals. This discovery offers crucial new insights into the ancient distribution and migratory patterns of prehistoric life across the vast landmass of Pangaea, enhancing our understanding of evolutionary history.
