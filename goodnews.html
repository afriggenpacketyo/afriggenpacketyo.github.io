Chinese Scientists Make First Direct Observation of Quantum Friction Effect

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has become the first to experimentally observe and measure quantum friction between two solid surfaces. Using folded graphene, they discovered a counterintuitive effect where friction did not always increase with thickness; in some cases, thicker folds slid more easily. The team attributed this phenomenon to changes in electron movement within the bent material, which locked them into fixed energy levels and reduced the conversion of motion into heat. The findings could pave the way for developing ultra-efficient, low-wear nanoscale devices and machines.
