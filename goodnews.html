Key Changes to England's Special Educational Needs (SEND) System Explained

2026-02-23 07:47:38 EST

England is implementing significant reforms to its Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND) system, aiming to streamline support for children. A major change involves phasing out Education, Health, and Care plans (EHCPs) for all but the most complex needs by 2035, introducing Individual Support Plans (ISPs) as a more flexible alternative. The reforms also introduce tiered support levels: 'targeted,' 'targeted plus,' and 'specialist,' alongside national inclusion standards. While children with existing EHCPs will retain them until their educational phase concludes, new assessments will determine eligibility under the reformed system.
