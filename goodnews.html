Live Nation to Pay $280 Million and Open Ticketmaster Platform in Antitrust Settlement

2026-03-09 12:58:07 EDT

Live Nation, parent company of Ticketmaster, has agreed to a $280 million settlement with the Department of Justice and 40 states, addressing antitrust charges. The agreement mandates Live Nation to sell some amphitheaters and requires Ticketmaster to open its platform to other ticket sellers, aiming to increase competition and potentially lower prices. However, not all states agreed to the deal, with New York's Attorney General vowing to continue litigation, arguing the settlement doesn't fully resolve the monopoly issue. A federal judge also expressed displeasure at not being informed of the tentative settlement until late in the process.

HELOC Rates Decline Over 2.5 Percentage Points in 18 Months, Offering Borrowing Opportunities

2026-03-09 12:26:03 EDT

Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) interest rates have significantly declined by over two and a half percentage points in the last 18 months, reaching an average of 7.18% by early March 2026. This drop follows the Federal Reserve's interest-rate cut campaign, which began in late 2024 and is expected to continue. Homeowners currently hold a record $11.6 trillion in tappable equity, making HELOCs an attractive option for accessing funds with variable rates that adjust with market conditions. Prospective borrowers are advised to review credit scores and shop around for the best rates, while also calculating costs under various rate scenarios due to the variable nature of HELOCs. Live Nation Reaches Tentative Antitrust Settlement, Judge Expresses Displeasure

2026-03-09 12:13:14 EDT

Live Nation, the entertainment giant encompassing Ticketmaster, has reached a tentative settlement with the U.S. Justice Department in an antitrust case, a development that angered the overseeing judge. The case, sparked by issues like the Taylor Swift Eras tour ticket sale, accused Live Nation of monopolistic practices. Under the proposed deal, Live Nation will allow venues to use multiple ticket vendors and artists to hire other promoters, and will divest up to 13 concert halls, alongside a $280 million payment to 40 states. However, the judge criticized being kept in the dark about the agreement, and some states have rejected the settlement, vowing to continue legal action.
