NASA's Van Allen Probe Safely Re-enters Earth's Atmosphere After Extended Mission

2026-03-12 07:23:36 EDT

A 1,300lb NASA Van Allen Probe spacecraft successfully re-entered Earth's atmosphere over the Eastern Pacific region, nearly 14 years after its launch. Most of the probe was expected to burn up during re-entry, posing a low risk of harm to humans. The mission, originally designed for two years, ultimately lasted almost seven, gathering crucial and unprecedented data on Earth's two permanent radiation belts. These Van Allen belts play a vital role in shielding Earth from harmful cosmic radiation, solar storms, and streaming solar wind, making the probe's data invaluable for scientific understanding.
