Understanding the Origins and Evolution of Daylight Saving Time

2026-03-06 18:35:19 EST

Daylight saving time (DST), a practice often debated, has a complex history with various attributed origins. While often linked to farmers, they did not necessarily support its adoption. Figures like Benjamin Franklin, with a satirical essay, and George Hudson, an entomologist seeking more daylight, are cited as early proponents. British builder William Willet campaigned for it to save energy and promote activity. Germany first adopted DST in 1916 during World War I to conserve fuel, with the U.S. following in 1918 before repealing it and then re-establishing it nationwide during World War II and through the 1966 Uniform Time Act.
