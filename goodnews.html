These findings place the origins of large-scale mummification in the region far earlier than the previously known oldest examples, such as Chile’s Chinchorro mummies from 7,000 years ago and early Egyptian practices from 4,500 years ago. The study provides new insight into the sophisticated mortuary rituals of ancient hunter-gatherer societies in Asia.
