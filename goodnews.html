Chinese Mathematicians Solve Decades-Old Kervaire Invariant Problem

2025-05-11 06:00:13 EDT

Chinese mathematicians have resolved the Kervaire invariant problem, a 65-year-old mathematical puzzle, using major computational methods. This breakthrough provides proof that manifolds of Kervaire invariant one exist in dimension 126, ending a long-standing mystery in the mathematical community. The Kervaire invariant is a function that measures whether a smooth framed manifold can be converted into a sphere through 'surgery.' This achievement was made by Wang Guozhen, Lin Weinan, and Xu Zhouli from Fudan University and UCLA.
