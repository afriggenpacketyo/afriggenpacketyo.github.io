2025-10-01 13:45:54 EDT

Meteorologists are monitoring two Atlantic hurricanes, Humberto and Imelda, for a potential interaction known as the Fujiwhara effect. This rare phenomenon occurs when two tropical storms get close enough to orbit a common center, sometimes merging or altering each other's paths. While a full merger is unlikely, the proximity of the two storms has already had an impact, with the stronger Hurricane Humberto appearing to pull Hurricane Imelda along its track, potentially guiding it away from the U.S. mainland. The swells generated by both storms are still expected to create life-threatening surf and rip currents along the U.S. East Coast.
