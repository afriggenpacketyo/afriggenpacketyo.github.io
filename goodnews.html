The concept of "privilege of historic backwardness," as articulated by Leon Trotsky, is presented as an explanation for China's rapid economic and technological advancement. This theory suggests that less developed countries can assimilate advancements from developed nations, skipping intermediate stages and adopting ready-made solutions. This allows for a unique, combined development trajectory, as seen in China's growth and in some African economies leapfrogging to 5G and e-commerce. The article posits that China's ability to modernize rapidly after periods of disruption is a prime example of this phenomenon.
