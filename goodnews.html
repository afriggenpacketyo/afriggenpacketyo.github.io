European Union implements ban on Bisphenol A in food packaging

2026-06-19 13:09:00 EDT

The European Union has enacted a ban on Bisphenol A (BPA) in food packaging, effective July 2026. This chemical, previously restricted in baby bottles and thermal paper, is now prohibited in food wrappers, containers, and cans due to potential health risks. BPA can leach into food and is linked to various health issues, including endocrine disruption, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and certain cancers. While the ban is strict, some transitional periods exist for specific applications like can linings where alternatives are not yet available, aiming to prevent food waste. Consumers are advised to opt for glass or stainless steel alternatives and avoid heating food in plastic containers to minimize exposure.
