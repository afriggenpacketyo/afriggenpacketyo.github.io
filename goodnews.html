Prostate Cancer Focal Therapy Shows Effectiveness with Reduced Side Effects in Major Study

2026-07-16 19:36:40 EDT

A significant 10-year NHS study suggests that focal therapy for prostate cancer, using high-intensity ultrasound or cryotherapy, is as effective as surgery or radiotherapy but with fewer side effects. The study of nearly 3,500 men found excellent long-term cancer control, with only two deaths from the disease, and less than half the risk of issues like urine leakage or loss of sexual function. Researchers advocate for wider availability of this less invasive treatment, which is currently limited to 10 centers in England despite its benefits. The findings are expected to pressure health regulators to approve focal therapy as a routine treatment.
