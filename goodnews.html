UEFA to Hold Summit with Leagues on 'Microscopic' VAR Interventions

2026-03-19 08:11:02 EDT

Europe's leading football leagues have been invited by UEFA to a critical summer summit to address the inconsistent and often controversial application of Video Assistant Referee (VAR) technology. Roberto Rosetti, UEFA's head of referees, initiated the meeting with the aim of resetting VAR to its original purpose of correcting only clear and obvious errors, rather than intervening in "microscopic" incidents. VAR usage and the thresholds applied vary significantly across major European leagues, with the Premier League, despite having the lowest intervention rate, still facing considerable controversy over decisions. The ultimate goal is to establish a more universal approach to the laws of the game and VAR implementation, fostering greater consistency and reducing contentious calls.

Eli Lilly’s Retatrutide Shows Promise in Late-Stage Diabetes Trial

2026-03-19 08:06:14 EDT

Eli Lilly announced that its experimental obesity drug, retatrutide, successfully completed its first late-stage trial for Type 2 diabetes patients, demonstrating efficacy in managing blood sugar and promoting significant weight loss. The drug reduced hemoglobin A1c levels by an average of 1.7% to 2% and led to an average weight loss of 16.8% at its highest dose over 40 weeks. Retatrutide, dubbed the "triple G" drug, mimics three hunger-regulating hormones, distinguishing it from existing treatments. While not yet approved, these results position it as a potential next-generation pillar in Lilly's cardiometabolic portfolio, with further trial findings expected by year-end.
