Scientists Identify Bacteria as Cause of Decade-Long Sea Star Epidemic

2025-08-04 13:16:00 EDT

Researchers have finally identified the cause of a devastating sea star wasting disease that has killed over five billion sea stars along North America's Pacific coast since 2013. A new study reveals the culprit is a bacteria, Vibrio pectenicida, which was found in the coelomic fluid of infected animals. The epidemic has critically endangered the sunflower sea star, a keystone species whose decline has led to an explosion in sea urchin populations and the destruction of kelp forests. This discovery provides hope for developing interventions, such as selective breeding or probiotics, to help restore sea star populations.
