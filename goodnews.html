This event is the second of three consecutive supermoons and occurs when the moon's elliptical orbit brings it to its closest point to Earth, known as the perigee. At this point, the moon can appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than at its farthest point. The name 'Beaver Moon' is thought to originate from the time of year when beavers are most active preparing their dams for winter. The final supermoon of the year will occur on December 4th.
