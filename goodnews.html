China's Private Sector Seeks Policy Support Ahead of 'Two Sessions'

2026-03-01 22:00:10 EST

China's private entrepreneurs are advocating for significant government support to address critical operational issues ahead of the annual "two sessions" political meetings. The Beijing Dacheng Enterprise Research Institute has proposed 38 suggestions, focusing on protecting private sector rights and resolving challenges such as overly rigid law enforcement, financing difficulties, and hidden market access barriers. Key recommendations include establishing a closed-loop mechanism for debt collection and creating a unified national platform for monitoring and expediting debt settlement. The institute also called for dedicated government bond funds to inject liquidity into the debt chain, ensuring enterprises prioritize settling outstanding debts with both upstream and downstream parties to improve overall cash flow and clarify creditor-debtor relationships. Key Issues Identified for Upcoming Scottish and Welsh Elections

2026-03-01 19:08:14 EST

A recent BBC poll, conducted by Savanta, highlights the key issues expected to influence voters in the upcoming Scottish and Welsh parliamentary elections on May 7. The economy, particularly the cost of living, health and social care services, and immigration levels are identified as the top three concerns. While older voters and women prioritize health, immigration resonates with former Reform voters, and housing is a concern for younger demographics. Despite widespread dissatisfaction with the economy and health services, voters often attribute blame to the UK government rather than their devolved administrations, complicating the electoral landscape for the SNP and Labour.
