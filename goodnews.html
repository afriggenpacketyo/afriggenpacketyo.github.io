The etymology of 'algorithm' traces back to a 9th-century Persian mathematician

2026-06-11 05:00:00 EDT

The word 'algorithm' originates from the Latinized name of the 9th-century Persian mathematician Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi. Al-Khwarizmi's influential work introduced algorithmic methods and popularized Hindu-Arabic numerals in the West, laying the groundwork for algebra. The term evolved through Latin and French before reaching its modern form in English, sometimes conflated with 'arithmetic.' Algorithms, defined as precise sets of instructions, have been crucial in fields from navigation to space exploration, predating modern computing. Despite contemporary concerns about AI's influence, the historical roots of algorithms highlight their long-standing utility in problem-solving.
