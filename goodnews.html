Scientists Still Debate Why Some Tree Leaves Turn Red in Autumn

2025-11-05 12:03:44 EST

Despite being a common seasonal phenomenon, scientists have not reached a consensus on why the leaves of some tree species turn red in the fall. While yellow leaves are explained by the unmasking of existing pigments as green chlorophyll breaks down, red hues come from new pigments created just before the leaves drop. One leading theory, the photoprotection hypothesis, suggests the red acts as a sunscreen to help the tree recover valuable nitrogen. Another theory posits it's a co-evolutionary trait to signal to insects, like aphids, that the tree is an undesirable host for laying eggs.
