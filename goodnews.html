Chinese Mathematicians Crack Decades-Old Kervaire Invariant Problem

2025-05-11 06:00:13 EDT

Chinese mathematicians from Fudan University Shanghai Centre for Mathematical Sciences and UCLA have successfully resolved the 65-year-old Kervaire invariant problem, which concerns the existence of specific topological spaces. This breakthrough involved advanced computational methods and confirms the existence of manifolds of Kervaire invariant one in dimension 126. The Kervaire invariant, introduced by John Milnor in 1950, is a function that assesses whether a smooth framed manifold can be converted into a sphere through surgery; the problem sought to identify dimensions where this invariant is non-zero, indicating the presence of unique shapes that cannot be transformed into a sphere. The findings, outlined in a paper yet to undergo peer review, provide a definitive answer to a mathematical mystery that has puzzled experts for decades, and could be applied to further problems in the field.
