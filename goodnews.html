New Jersey Beaches Under Advisory Due to Elevated Fecal Bacteria Levels

2026-06-21 16:45:02 EDT

Several New Jersey coastal swimming areas are currently under advisories after routine water testing detected elevated levels of enterococci, a common indicator of fecal bacteria, surpassing state safety standards. The New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection has issued warnings for five locations in Ocean and Cape May counties, although no beaches have been officially closed. While enterococci are generally not considered harmful to humans themselves, their presence signals that other potentially disease-causing organisms, such as viruses or bacteria, may be present in the water. Authorities will continue intensive monitoring and additional sampling until water quality returns to acceptable levels, advising sensitive individuals to remain cognizant of air quality.
