Long-Lost Rembrandt Painting Authenticated After Decades

2026-03-02 11:54:40 EST

Rembrandt's "Vision of Zacharias in the Temple," a painting from 1633, has been rediscovered and authenticated by experts after being lost for 65 years. Excluded from the master's works in 1960 and sold to a private collector in 1961, the artwork resurfaced when its owners presented it to the Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam for examination. A meticulous two-year analysis confirmed its authenticity through paint analysis, stylistic similarities, and the age of the wooden panel, all consistent with Rembrandt's early works. This high-quality piece, depicting the biblical scene of Zacharias being told he will have a son, showcases Rembrandt at the peak of his early career, adding a significant work to his known oeuvre.

Premier League Grappling in Corner Kicks Prompts Calls for Rule Changes

2026-03-02 11:20:39 EST

Corner kicks in the Premier League are increasingly marred by excessive holding and wrestling, leading to calls for intervention from former referees and managers who believe "something needs to be done." While the Premier League set a high bar for fouls at the season's start, many perceived clear infringements go unpunished, frustrating fans who see microscopic offsides penalized instead. Proposed solutions include changing the law to allow fouls before the ball is in play or requiring attackers to start outside the goal area.
