The specimen is 15 million years older than any previous find and is the most complete pachycephalosaur skeleton ever unearthed, offering unprecedented insights into the creature's anatomy. The fossil reveals that even juvenile pachycephalosaurs had fully developed, thick skulls, suggesting they engaged in head-butting behavior from a young age. The discovery fills a critical gap in the evolutionary history of this unique group of dinosaurs and is now housed at the Mongolian Academy of Sciences.
