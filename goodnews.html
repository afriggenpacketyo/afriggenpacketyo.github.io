Google's Debug program plans to release millions of sterile mosquitoes in the US to combat disease

Google's Debug program intends to release millions of male mosquitoes infected with Wolbachia bacteria in Florida and California as a method to control disease-spreading mosquito populations. The bacteria render the male mosquitoes sterile, so when they mate with wild females, their eggs do not hatch, leading to a reduction in the mosquito population over generations. The primary target is the invasive Aedes aegypti mosquito, which transmits diseases like dengue, Zika, and chikungunya. While the EPA's approval is pending, the program aims to offer an environmentally friendly solution to public health threats posed by these mosquitoes.
