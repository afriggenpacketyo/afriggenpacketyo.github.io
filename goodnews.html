Understanding the 6-Month Rule for Reverse Mortgages

2025-09-16 12:55:29 EDT

The reverse mortgage 6-month rule dictates the timeline for heirs to address the loan after the borrower passes away or permanently leaves the home. Lenders must issue a "Due and Payable" letter, giving the estate six months to either repay the loan balance in full or sell the property to cover the debt. This rule primarily applies to federally insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgages (HECMs). While extensions are possible with HUD approval, the deadline underscores the importance of advance estate planning for borrowers who wish for their heirs to keep the family home.
