EU Council Poised to Approve Expanded Air Travel Passenger Rights

2026-07-13 02:29:00 EDT

A comprehensive package of reforms aimed at granting additional rights to air passengers is expected to receive formal approval from the Council of the European Union on Monday. These new regulations will apply to all flights departing from EU airports and arrivals operated by EU-based airlines. Key provisions include the right for children under 14, pregnant individuals, and those with reduced mobility to sit next to companions without seat reservation fees. Airlines will also be mandated to correct name spellings on tickets free of charge, provide printed boarding passes at no extra cost, and display fares inclusive of cabin baggage by default. The reforms also clarify rules for compensation and support during flight disruptions, such as meals and accommodation.

Chinese Researchers Achieve 100-Fold AI Speed Boost with Optical Chip Breakthrough

2026-07-13 02:00:12 EDT

Researchers at Peking University have developed a groundbreaking all-optical interconnect system that dramatically enhances AI distributed inference speeds by over 100 times, while utilizing only one-ninth of the conventional computational resources. This innovative approach optically links standard electronic chips, specifically field-programmable gate array (FPGA) chips, with specialized algorithms. The system incorporates custom-designed silicon photonic transceiver chips capable of converting electrical signals to optical at 400 gigabits per second. This significant advancement offers a radically different and more energy-efficient pathway to boost AI computational power, moving beyond the traditional reliance on simply adding more GPUs and expanding data centers.
