Study Suggests Azelastine Nasal Spray May Significantly Reduce COVID-19 Infection Risk

2025-09-04 13:29:54 EDT

A clinical trial conducted by researchers at Saarland University in Germany has found that an over-the-counter nasal spray may significantly reduce the risk of contracting COVID-19. The study, published in JAMA Internal Medicine, showed that participants using an azelastine nasal spray three times daily had a 2.2% infection rate, compared to 6.7% in the placebo group. Researchers noted the spray, commonly used for hay fever, was associated with a lower rate of symptomatic infections and even rhinovirus infections. The findings suggest azelastine could serve as an accessible prophylactic measure, though larger trials are needed to confirm its effectiveness against various respiratory pathogens.

Navigating the Optimal Age for Claiming Social Security Benefits

2025-09-04 13:26:06 EDT

Deciding when to claim Social Security benefits is a critical financial decision for retirees, with significant implications for their monthly income. While benefits can be claimed as early as age 62, doing so results in a permanent reduction of about 30% compared to waiting until full retirement age, which is between 66 and 67. Conversely, delaying claims past full retirement age increases benefits by approximately 8% annually until age 70, maximizing the monthly payout. The optimal choice depends on individual circumstances, including immediate financial needs, health, and other retirement income sources, with financial experts suggesting strategies like creating income bridges to delay claiming.
