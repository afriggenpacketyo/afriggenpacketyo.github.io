An Explanation of Credit Card Charge-Offs and the Seven-Year Rule for Credit Reporting

2025-10-27 13:45:06 EDT

This article explains the financial concept of a credit card charge-off, which occurs when a creditor deems a debt uncollectible, typically after 180 days of non-payment. While the issuer writes off the debt for accounting purposes, the borrower still owes the money, and the account is often sold to a collection agency. A charge-off remains on a person's credit report for seven years from the date of the first missed payment, significantly impacting their credit score during that time. The article notes that while the negative mark disappears after seven years, the debt itself may still be legally collectible depending on state laws.
