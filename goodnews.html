Firefighters like Sidney Frelow and Charles Cousins took great pride in their work, becoming role models within Denver's Five Points community. Despite facing racial hurdles, these firefighters made significant contributions, with some even losing their lives in the line of duty, such as during the St. James fire in 1895. Retired Fire Chief Rod Juniel, who had to fight to join the department in the 1970s, emphasizes the need to continue addressing equitable service within the fire department.
