Jazz Legend Sonny Rollins, "Saxophone Colossus," Dies at 95

2026-05-25 22:50:23 EDT

Sonny Rollins, the revered jazz tenor saxophonist known as the "Saxophone Colossus" and one of the last iconic figures of post-World War II jazz, has passed away at 95 from pulmonary fibrosis at his home in Woodstock, N.Y. His prolific career spanned over six decades, during which he collaborated with legends like Miles Davis, Charlie Parker, and John Coltrane, and earned two Grammys before his 2014 retirement due to respiratory illness. Rollins was celebrated for his innovative improvisations, extended solos, and unique ability to infuse new vitality into standard tunes, always approaching a solo as a story to tell. His immense contribution to music is further cemented by his archives, which he donated to the Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture in Harlem.
