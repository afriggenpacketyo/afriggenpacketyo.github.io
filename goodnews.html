Brazil Deploys "Good Mosquitoes" in Massive Effort to Combat Dengue Fever

2025-07-26 09:19:53 EDT

To combat devastating dengue fever outbreaks, Brazil is undertaking a massive public health initiative by releasing millions of specially bred mosquitoes. These "Wolbitos," developed by the nonprofit World Mosquito Program, are infected with a bacterium called Wolbachia, which prevents the dengue virus from replicating inside them, thereby stopping transmission to humans. A pilot program in the city of Niterói led to a 90% drop in dengue cases, prompting the government to build a new factory capable of producing billions of these mosquitoes to protect up to 140 million Brazilians over the next decade.
