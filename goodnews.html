Chinese Scientists Observe Quantum Friction Effect for the First Time

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has become the first to directly observe and measure quantum friction between two solid surfaces. Using folded graphene, the scientists discovered that friction did not always increase with thickness; in some cases, thicker folds exhibited less resistance. This counterintuitive effect was traced to how bending the material altered electron movement, restricting the conversion of motion into heat. The breakthrough provides the first experimental evidence of this phenomenon and could pave the way for developing ultra-efficient, low-wear nanoscale machines and devices.
