China Completes Massive Green Belt to Combat Desertification in Inner Mongolia

2025-07-09 00:00:08 EDT

China has completed a 1,856-kilometer (1,153-mile) green belt across three major deserts in its Inner Mongolia region as part of its effort to combat desertification. This "green great wall" project aims to stabilize the shifting sands of the Badain Jaran, Tengger, and Ulan Buh deserts. The method involves creating straw checkerboards to lock the sand in place, followed by planting drought-resistant vegetation like the sacsaoul tree. This ecological barrier is a key component of China's long-term strategy to prevent sandstorms and protect its northern territories from expanding deserts.
