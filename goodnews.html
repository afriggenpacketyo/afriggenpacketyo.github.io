Forecasters predict no further strengthening, attributing this to strong wind shear that is pushing the storm's heaviest weather offshore and mitigating rainfall totals, thus potentially sparing Louisiana from the worst impacts. While a tropical storm watch was in effect for New Orleans and coastal flood warnings for Mississippi and Louisiana, the immediate aftermath suggests a less severe event than anticipated. Despite predictions of a below-average hurricane season due to El Niño, experts warn that unusually warm Gulf waters still elevate the risk of more powerful storms, emphasizing the need for continued preparedness.
