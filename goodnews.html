Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A significant new Chinese fossil discovery is providing crucial insights into the evolutionary process through which birds developed their unique flight-adapted body plan. This research reveals that birds gradually shed their long, bony dinosaur tails, transitioning step-by-step to the modern pygostyle—a short, fused tail structure essential for anchoring tail feathers and muscles, which is vital for flight. The rarity of early-diverging bird and birdlike dinosaur fossils has made studying this transformation challenging, making this find particularly important. Researchers published their findings in Science Advances, highlighting profound morphological changes in terrestrial vertebrate history.
