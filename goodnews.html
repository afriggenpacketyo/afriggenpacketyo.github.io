Scientists Debate the Evolutionary Reason for Red Autumn Leaves

2025-11-05 12:03:44 EST

The reason why some tree leaves turn red in the fall remains a scientific mystery with several competing theories. While yellow leaves are explained by the unmasking of existing pigments after chlorophyll breaks down, red hues come from new pigments called anthocyanins, produced just before leaves drop. One leading hypothesis suggests these pigments act as a sunscreen, protecting the leaf's nutrient-retrieval process from excess light. Another theory posits that the red color co-evolved as a warning signal to deter insects like aphids from laying eggs. Researchers continue to explore these and other possibilities, such as anti-fungal properties.
