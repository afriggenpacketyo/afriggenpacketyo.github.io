WHO Approves First Malaria Drug Specifically Designed for Infants

2026-04-24 19:30:00 EDT

The World Health Organization (WHO) has approved the first malaria drug specifically designed for infants, marking a significant breakthrough ahead of World Malaria Day. This new combination of artemether and lumefantrine is safe for babies weighing less than 5 kilograms, addressing a critical medical care gap. Previously, infants were treated with drugs developed for older children, which exposed them to risks of dosing errors and toxicity. This approval enables UN agencies to procure and distribute the treatment in malaria-endemic areas, particularly Sub-Saharan Africa, where children under five account for 70% of malaria deaths, offering new hope.

"Mini-Volcanoes" Along California Coast Are Actually Spontaneous Combustion Events, Experts Clarify

2026-04-24 18:18:57 EDT

Fire crews in Santa Barbara responded to a hillside fire, known locally as the "Hope Ranch Volcano," but experts clarify these are not true volcanoes. James Boles, a professor emeritus at UC Santa Barbara, explains they are spontaneous combustion events where oxygen interacts with unstable compounds in cliffside rocks, generating intense heat that ignites organic materials. This phenomenon has been observed since the 1800s, and similar occurrences are reported along Ventura County's coast, dubbed the "Rincon Volcano." While visually dramatic, these "steaming mountains" are geological curiosities, demonstrating natural processes rather than underlying volcanic activity.
