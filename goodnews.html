New Australian Native Bee Species with Horns Named 'Megachile Lucifer'

2025-11-10 21:29:59 EST

Australian scientists have identified a new native bee species, aptly named Megachile Lucifer, distinguished by tiny, prominent horns found exclusively on the female's face. Discovered in the remote Bremer Ranges of Western Australia, this "light bringer" bee represents the first new addition to its taxonomic group in two decades. Researchers speculate the horns might function as a defense mechanism, aid in pollen and nectar collection, or assist in gathering nesting materials. The study strongly advocates for the formal protection of its habitat, as both this unique bee and the rare wildflower it pollinates face significant threats from habitat disturbance and climate change, especially from mining activities that frequently neglect native bee species in their environmental impact assessments.
