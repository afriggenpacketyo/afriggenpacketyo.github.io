This event has deep roots in the Jewish Feast of Weeks (Shavuot), a harvest festival also marking the revelation of the Ten Commandments. Symbolizing a "miracle of understanding" where people of different languages could communicate, Pentecost is celebrated in over 30 countries, often with the color red and traditions like scattering rose petals. Despite its theological significance, its abstract nature and lack of commercial ties make it less tangible for many compared to other Christian festivals.
