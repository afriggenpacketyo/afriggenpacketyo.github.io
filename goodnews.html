Designed to both generate energy and transmute long-lived radioactive waste into less hazardous isotopes, this advanced reactor could make nuclear power a "green, safe, stable energy source for 1,000 years." Researchers at the Chinese Academy of Sciences are completing the installation of superconducting particle accelerators, crucial for powering the system. This project represents a significant step in nuclear technology, addressing the challenge of managing highly hazardous, long-lived actinides from conventional nuclear fuel, which can remain dangerous for hundreds of thousands of years.
