Economic Analysis Explains Why Overall Price Levels Rarely Decrease

2025-11-20 06:30:00 EST

This article explains that despite political promises to lower prices, the overall price level in an economy almost never goes down, and for good reason. A sustained drop in prices, known as deflation, is typically a sign of a severe economic crisis, as it discourages consumer spending and can lead to a downward spiral. The Federal Reserve intentionally targets a modest inflation rate of around 2% to encourage steady economic activity. While prices for individual goods can fluctuate due to supply shocks, the aggregate cost of living consistently rises, a reality that often clashes with public perception and political rhetoric.
