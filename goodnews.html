Sociolinguist explains the evolution and endurance of American accents

2026-03-31 07:45:00 EDT

Sociolinguist Valerie Fridland discusses the origins and persistence of American accents in a Vox podcast interview, explaining that the modern American accent developed from the speech of early British colonists, leveling out many distinct British features. Over time, regional differences emerged due to diverse settlement patterns along the Atlantic Coast. Fridland notes that accents are deeply tied to identity and belonging, and while some accents, like the Transatlantic accent, have faded due to being perceived as artificial or elitist, others have endured. The Midwest and West developed unique blends influenced by various immigrant groups and subsequent resettlement, leading to the perception of some Western accents as "accent-less" due to earlier leveling.
