Understanding Effective Tax Rates for Pretax vs. Roth 401(k) Contributions

2025-10-07 14:52:07 EDT

When deciding between traditional and Roth 401(k) contributions for retirement savings, focusing on effective tax rates rather than just marginal rates can be crucial. Certified financial planner Cody Garrett explains that while marginal rates apply to the last dollar earned, effective rates represent taxes paid as a percentage of total income. Given the progressive U.S. federal income tax brackets, a retiree's effective tax rate might be significantly lower than their working-year marginal rate. Understanding this distinction allows individuals to strategically choose pretax contributions if they anticipate a lower effective tax rate in retirement, or Roth contributions if they expect higher rates, optimizing tax payments over their lifetime.
