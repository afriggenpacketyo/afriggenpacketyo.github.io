Understanding Required Minimum Distributions from Retirement Accounts

2026-05-18 12:35:30 EDT

Retirees must strategically manage Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) from tax-deferred accounts like 401(k)s and IRAs, typically starting at age 73. The annual RMD is calculated by dividing the account balance by an IRS-determined life expectancy factor, meaning older individuals withdraw more. For a $200,000 account, this could range from approximately $7,500 at age 73 to over $9,400 at age 79, with associated tax implications. Bipartisan Bill Proposed to Allow Direct Charitable Donations from 401(k)s

2026-05-18 11:42:33 EDT

A new bipartisan, bicameral bill, the Charity Parity Act, aims to allow retirees aged 70½ or older to make qualified charitable distributions (QCDs) directly from their 401(k)s and similar workplace retirement plans. Currently, QCDs, which offer tax benefits by excluding donations from income and counting towards Required Minimum Distributions, are only permitted from IRAs, necessitating a rollover from 401(k)s. Proponents argue the legislation would modernize rules and eliminate unnecessary steps, making charitable giving easier for a broader range of retirees. The bill has been referred to congressional committees, with its passage uncertain. (not investment advice)
