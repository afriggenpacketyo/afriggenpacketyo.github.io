Semaglutide Shows Promise in Reversing Liver Disease in Clinical Trial

2025-05-03 05:00:56 EDT

A clinical trial published in The New England Journal of Medicine revealed that semaglutide injections can reverse life-threatening cases of liver disease. Participants with metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH) experienced significant improvements after receiving semaglutide, traditionally a treatment for type 2 diabetes. The study found that 62.9% of participants had less inflammation and fat accumulation in their livers compared to 34.3% in the placebo group. 2025-05-03 04:33:49 EDT

They were eventually rescued by a helicopter after being discovered by local fishermen. Man Bitten 200 Times Creates 'Unparalleled' Snake Antivenom

2025-05-03 02:29:15 EDT

Scientists have developed an "unparalleled" antivenom using antibodies from the blood of Tim Friede, a man who deliberately injected himself with snake venom for nearly two decades. Antibodies found in Friede's blood have shown protection against fatal doses from various snake species in animal tests. This research, detailed in the journal Cell, identified two broadly neutralizing antibodies that could target neurotoxins and is a step toward a universal antivenom.
