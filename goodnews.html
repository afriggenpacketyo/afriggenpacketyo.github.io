Purchasing Physical Silver: Banks Generally Do Not Sell Bars and Coins

2026-01-28 09:21:20 EST

Many investors, seeking to diversify their portfolios amid economic uncertainty, are turning to precious metals like silver. However, most retail banks in the U.S. do not sell physical silver bars or coins directly to customers. This is due to logistical complexities, security costs, low profit margins compared to traditional banking products, and regulatory focus. While some large international or private banks might facilitate purchases for high-net-worth clients, it's typically through allocated accounts rather than direct over-the-counter sales. Investors are advised to use reputable online dealers, local coin shops, or the U.S. Mint for physical silver, or consider paper silver products like ETFs. (not investment advice)
