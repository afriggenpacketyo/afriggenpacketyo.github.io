Civil Rights Leader Bernard LaFayette, Selma Organizer, Dies at 85

2026-03-05 22:45:33 EST

Bernard LaFayette, a revered civil rights leader and instrumental organizer of the Selma voting rights campaign, has passed away at the age of 85. His courageous, behind-the-scenes work in Selma was crucial in paving the way for the landmark Voting Rights Act of 1965. As a co-founder of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee, LaFayette endured assassination attempts and physical assaults, steadfastly adhering to the principles of nonviolence he espoused. His contributions extended to desegregation efforts in Nashville and the Chicago Freedom Movement, and he was with Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. on the day of his assassination, dedicating his life to institutionalizing and internationalizing nonviolence.
