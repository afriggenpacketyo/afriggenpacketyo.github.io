ETFs trade like individual stocks with fluctuating prices, while mutual funds are priced at the end of the market day. Investors should review a fund's prospectus for investment objectives, risks, fees, asset composition, and performance history. ETFs generally have lower expense ratios than mutual funds due to lower transaction management costs, and mutual funds often have minimum investment thresholds.
