NASA Prepares for Artemis II, First Crewed Moon Mission in Over 50 Years

2026-01-17 02:50:56 EST

NASA is diligently preparing for the Artemis II mission, targeting a launch as early as the first week of February, which will be the first crewed Moon mission in over 50 years. This ambitious 10-day endeavor will send Commander Reid Wiseman, Pilot Victor Glover, and Mission Specialists Christina Koch and Jeremy Hansen further into space than any humans have previously traveled. Although Artemis II will not involve a lunar landing, its critical objective is to thoroughly test the Orion spacecraft's life-support, propulsion, power, and navigation systems, as well as manual flight capabilities in lunar orbit. This mission is designed to lay essential groundwork for the subsequent human lunar landing, planned for the Artemis III mission, projected for 2027 or later.
