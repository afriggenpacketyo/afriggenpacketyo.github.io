Understanding the '609 Letter' for Challenging Credit Report Errors

2026-02-03 14:41:35 EST

A "609 letter" is a formal request under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) used to challenge inaccurate or unverifiable items on a credit report. Borrowers send these letters to credit bureaus, demanding documentation to prove the accuracy of negative marks. If verification isn't provided within 30 days, the item must be removed. This strategy is most effective for genuine errors, not for legitimate debts. For actual unmanageable debt, alternative solutions like debt settlement or management plans are generally more appropriate. (not investment advice)
