2025-07-31 14:40:57 EDT

This rule stipulates that if the loan balance exceeds the home's value, heirs can settle the debt by paying 95% of the property's current appraised value, rather than the full amount owed. This prevents them from being "underwater" on the inheritance. The provision offers peace of mind for senior homeowners, ensuring their loved ones won't face an unmanageable financial burden if they choose to retain the property.
