Dozens of Beluga Whales to be Relocated from Canadian Park to US Aquariums

2026-07-16 20:57:00 EDT

Twenty-eight beluga whales from a closed Canadian theme park, Marineland, are slated for relocation to aquariums across the United States as part of an international emergency rescue plan. Marineland, unable to care for the whales after closing to the public in 2024, had faced the possibility of euthanizing the animals. Federal officials approved the import to ensure the whales receive necessary medical treatment and care, as no Canadian facilities can provide it. The whales will be transported to accredited aquariums including SeaWorld San Antonio and San Diego, and the Georgia Aquarium. This rescue effort addresses concerns over animal welfare and the park's capacity to maintain the animals.

Focal Therapy Shows Lower Side Effects for Prostate Cancer, Study Suggests

2026-07-16 19:36:40 EDT

A 10-year NHS study led by Imperial College London indicates that focal therapy, a less invasive treatment for prostate cancer, is as effective as surgery or radiotherapy but with a significantly lower risk of side effects. The study, which followed nearly 3,500 men, found excellent long-term cancer control with less than half the risk of side effects like urinary leakage or loss of sexual function. Researchers are advocating for wider availability of focal therapy, which uses high-intensity ultrasound or cryotherapy to target cancerous tissue. Currently, only about 1,000 men in the UK receive this treatment annually, despite an estimated 15,000 who could benefit, leading to a "postcode lottery" in access.
