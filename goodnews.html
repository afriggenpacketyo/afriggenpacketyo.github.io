Explainer: The Causes and Effects of Urban Heat Islands

2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

"Urban heat islands" are metropolitan areas that become significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas due to human activity and infrastructure. Dark surfaces like asphalt and concrete absorb and retain heat, while tall buildings trap warm air and block cooling winds, intensifying heatwaves for city dwellers. Solutions to mitigate this effect include adding more green spaces, installing "cool roofs" that reflect sunlight, and using permeable pavements that enhance water evaporation, all of which can help lower urban temperatures.
