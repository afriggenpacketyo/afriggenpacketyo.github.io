Scientists Use Environmental DNA to Search for Endangered Axolotls in Mexico City Canals

2025-08-11 19:37:29 EDT

Researchers from Mexico’s National Autonomous University are using environmental DNA (eDNA) to track the critically endangered axolotl in the canals of Xochimilco, Mexico City. With the salamander population having plummeted from 6,000 per square kilometer in 1998 to just 36 in 2014, physical sightings are rare. Scientists filter water from the canals to capture genetic particles shed by the animals, allowing them to detect the species' presence without capturing one. This innovative technique offers hope for monitoring the remaining population and guiding conservation efforts to restore the axolotl's polluted habitat.
