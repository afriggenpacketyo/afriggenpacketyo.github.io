Two U.S. States and Territories Opt Out of Daylight Saving Time Annually

2026-03-06 11:47:09 EST

While most of the U.S. prepares to "spring forward" for daylight saving time, two states, Arizona and Hawaii, along with five U.S. territories, do not observe the practice. States can exempt themselves from the Uniform Time Act of 1966, which made daylight saving a legal requirement, without federal permission. Arizona opted out in 1968 due to its hot climate, aiming to reduce energy costs and keep people indoors during peak heat. Hawaii's proximity to the equator provides consistent daylight, making the change unnecessary. The article notes widespread American dissatisfaction with time changes and ongoing efforts to end or permanently adopt daylight saving at state and federal levels.
