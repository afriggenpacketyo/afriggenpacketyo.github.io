Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A team of Chinese researchers has published a groundbreaking study in the peer-reviewed journal Science Advances, revealing new insights into how birds evolved from their dinosaur ancestors by gradually shedding their long tails. Unlike most non-avian dinosaurs, modern birds feature a pygostyle, a short skeletal structure formed by the fusion of caudal vertebrae, which serves as a vital anchor for tail feathers and muscles essential for flight. Studying this significant transformation has historically been difficult due to the scarcity of early bird and birdlike dinosaur fossils. This new discovery provides crucial evidence, helping to unravel the complex evolutionary assembly of the flight-adapted bird body plan.
