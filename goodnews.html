China's Yangtze River Fishing Ban Leads to Ecological Recovery

2026-02-20 23:00:09 EST

China's decade-long fishing ban on the Yangtze River has resulted in a significant surge in biomass and a boost for the critically endangered finless porpoise population. The comprehensive ban, implemented in 2021 and reinforced by the Yangtze River Protection Law, involved recalling over 111,000 fishing vessels and resettling 231,000 fishers with a substantial investment. The law also introduced stricter regulations on water quality, sand mining, and shoreline restoration. This article highlights the positive environmental impact of the ambitious conservation effort on one of the world's most biodiverse and economically vital waterways, which once contributed over 60% of China's freshwater fisheries output.
