Hestermann argues that this intuitive, often unintentional, pattern of reporting aligns with a right-wing populist agenda, influencing public perception of violent crime and threat. Criminologist Gina Wollinger terms this "migrantization" of crime, stressing that crime correlates with poverty and lack of prospects, not origin. The study advocates for greater editorial diversity to combat this bias.
