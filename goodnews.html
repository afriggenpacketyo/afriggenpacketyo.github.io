A stunning free-kick goal by Crystal Palace's Eberechi Eze against Chelsea was correctly disallowed by VAR due to a little-known rule infringement. The decision was made because a Crystal Palace player was standing less than one meter away from the defensive wall formed by Chelsea's players at the moment the kick was taken. According to IFAB's Law 13, introduced in 2019, all attacking players must maintain at least a one-meter distance from a wall of three or more defenders. The rule was implemented to prevent management problems and time-wasting caused by attackers positioning themselves within the wall.
