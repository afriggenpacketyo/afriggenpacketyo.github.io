Gravitational Wave Data Confirms Stephen Hawking's 1971 Black Hole Theory

By analyzing the waves from a collision of two black holes, researchers verified Hawking's area theorem, which states that the surface area of a resulting black hole can never be smaller than the sum of the areas of the original black holes. The observation, made possible by significant upgrades to the detectors, marks a major milestone in gravitational wave astronomy. Hawking, who passed away in 2018, had hoped this prediction could be tested with such data.
