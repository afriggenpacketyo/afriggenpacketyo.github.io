Simple "Thumb Test" May Help Identify Risk for Aortic Aneurysm

2025-09-27 05:16:02 EDT

A simple self-check known as the "thumb test" could offer a clue about a person's risk for an aortic aneurysm. The test involves placing the thumb across the palm; if the thumb extends beyond the edge of the palm, it can indicate joint hypermobility associated with connective tissue diseases like Marfan syndrome. These conditions can weaken blood vessel walls, increasing aneurysm risk. However, cardiologists caution that the test is not a diagnostic tool, as most people with an aneurysm will have a negative result, and a positive result does not confirm an aneurysm is present. They advise anyone with a positive test to discuss it with their doctor.
