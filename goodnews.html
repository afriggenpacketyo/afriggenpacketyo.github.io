This model involves routing online sales through a middleman company that acts as a U.S. merchant of record, purchasing the product at a wholesale price from the retailer. The middleman then pays the U.S. tariff on the lower wholesale value rather than the full retail price, which can cut the tariff cost by 30% to 60%. This "tariff arbitrage" allows brands to absorb the financial pressure from the ongoing trade war without passing the entire cost increase on to consumers.
