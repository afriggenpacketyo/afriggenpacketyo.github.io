Japan Reforms Divorce Law to Allow Joint Child Custody

2026-05-23 19:44:22 EDT

Japan has enacted a landmark revision to its Civil Code, effective April 1, 2026, allowing divorced couples to share custody of their children, a significant departure from its previous sole custody system. This change aims to enable children to maintain relationships with both parents, bringing Japan in line with other G7 nations that recognize joint custody. While offering hope for many separated parents who felt cut off from their children, critics raise serious concerns that joint custody could endanger victims of domestic violence and child abuse by forcing continued contact with abusive partners. The reform reflects evolving societal views on family and parental responsibility, amidst Japan's declining birthrate and aging population.
