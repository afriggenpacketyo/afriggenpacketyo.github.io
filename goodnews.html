Euclid Telescope Discovers Oldest Quasars, Deepening Cosmic Formation Mystery

2026-07-06 06:45:24 EDT

The European Space Agency's Euclid telescope has achieved a groundbreaking discovery, identifying 31 new quasars, including the two oldest ever observed, dating back to when the universe was merely 670 million years old. These incredibly bright objects, powered by supermassive black holes, offer unprecedented insights into the early universe and the epoch of reionization. However, their existence at such an early stage deepens a "perplexing" cosmic mystery, as scientists struggle to comprehend how these massive black holes grew so rapidly. The findings, published in Astronomy & Astrophysics, underscore Euclid's transformative role in advancing our understanding of cosmic evolution.
