Researchers initially suspected environmental pollutants but found no evidence, instead determining the tumors are genetically distinct from their hosts, suggesting they are clones that originated in one fish and have since become infectious. This discovery adds catfish to a small list of animals with transmissible cancers and offers new avenues for studying cancer metastasis.
