The Inescapable Tic: AI's Reliance on "Not X, It's Y" Phrasing

2026-07-12 07:30:00 EDT

AI-generated writing is characterized by an inescapable and frequently used rhetorical device: the "It's not X; it's Y" construction, often termed negative parallelism. This linguistic tic, prevalent across major chatbots like ChatGPT and Claude, has reportedly quadrupled in corporate communications since 2023 and appears three times more often in AI writing than in human-authored text. While effective when deployed judiciously, its excessive use makes AI output feel formulaic and repetitive. Researchers hypothesize that this pattern arises from AI models hedging between statistically likely and highly-rated word choices during text prediction, with its persistence amplified as AI increasingly trains on its own generated content. This pervasive cliché, paradoxically, also serves as a detectable marker for distinguishing AI writing from human text.
