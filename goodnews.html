Understanding the Risks: Can an Annuity Run Out of Money?

2025-05-22 14:49:21 EDT

The ability of an annuity to provide a steady income stream for life depends on the type of annuity and the financial health of the insurer. Immediate and lifetime annuities are designed to guarantee income for life, while fixed-term annuities pay out for a set number of years and then stop. Variable and indexed annuities can be tied to investment performance, and their cash value could be depleted if the underlying investments perform poorly. Choosing a lifetime income annuity, diversifying retirement income, researching the insurance company, and monitoring withdrawal habits are strategies to protect against losing annuity payments.
