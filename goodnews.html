Physiological Changes Explain Why Alcohol Tolerance Decreases with Age

2025-12-08 05:00:00 EST

Health experts explain that physiological changes are the reason people often feel the effects of alcohol more intensely as they age. The liver enzymes responsible for metabolizing alcohol become less efficient, and a natural loss of muscle mass reduces the body's water content, leading to a higher blood alcohol concentration from the same amount of drink. For women in perimenopause, alcohol can also worsen symptoms like mood swings and sleep disruption. Doctors advise that while worse hangovers are unpleasant, they serve as a natural deterrent, encouraging reduced consumption, which is beneficial for long-term health by lowering risks of cancer, liver disease, and cognitive decline.
