Crystal Palace Goal Disallowed Due to Attacking Player's Proximity to Defensive Wall

The decision was made because a Crystal Palace player was standing less than one meter away from Chelsea's defensive wall when the kick was taken. According to IFAB's Law 13, which was introduced in the 2019-20 season, all attacking players must maintain at least a one-meter distance from a wall formed by three or more defenders. The rule was implemented to prevent management problems and time-wasting, ensuring a fairer process for free-kicks.
