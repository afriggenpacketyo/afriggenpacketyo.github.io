New California Law Requires Bars and Nightclubs to Offer Drink Lids to Patrons

2025-07-02 06:00:00 EDT

A new law in California, effective this week, mandates that all establishments selling alcohol for on-site consumption must provide lids for drinks upon a customer's request. This measure, intended to help prevent drink spiking, builds on previous legislation requiring bars to post signs about the availability of drug-testing kits. Authored by Assemblymember Josh Lowenthal, the bill allows establishments to charge a reasonable price for the lids but does not require them to be provided otherwise. The Department of Alcoholic Beverage Control will enforce the law during regular inspections, focusing initially on education rather than punitive action for non-compliance.
