Chinese Scientists Develop Lab-Grown Sinoatrial Node, Potential Pacemaker Replacement

2026-05-22 22:00:07 EDT

Scientists in Shanghai have achieved a significant medical breakthrough by creating the world's first laboratory-grown sinoatrial node from stem cells, the heart's natural pacemaker. This autonomously beating organoid represents a transformative advancement for cardiac disease research and drug screening. The sinoatrial node, often called the heart's "master conductor," regulates the heart's rhythm by sending electrical signals that control chamber contractions. The successful engineering of this biological pacemaker in the lab offers a promising new avenue for treating abnormal heart rhythms and could potentially replace traditional electronic pacemakers in the future, marking a major step in regenerative medicine.
