Scotland Becomes First UK Nation to Screen Newborns for Spinal Muscular Atrophy

2026-03-22 22:55:52 EDT

Scotland has become the first nation in the UK to introduce routine newborn screening for Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA), a rare genetic condition that causes progressive muscle weakness and can severely limit life expectancy without treatment. This two-year pilot program will integrate the SMA test into the standard heel prick screening for all babies born in Scotland, aiming for early detection. Prompt diagnosis allows for immediate gene therapy, which can be life-changing by improving symptoms and potentially preventing their development. The initiative is a direct result of extensive campaigning by parents like Carrie and Tony Pearson, whose own daughter, Grayce, was diagnosed too late for optimal early interventions.
