Native American Women Revitalizing Traditional Facial Tattoos

Native American women are reclaiming their cultural heritage by reviving the tradition of facial tattoos, known as inchunwa, among southeastern Indigenous nations. Receiving inchunwa is a deeply personal and spiritual experience, reflecting a commitment to community, heritage, and setting the groundwork for being a good ancestor. The inchunwa symbolizes personal promises, honors lost siblings, and acknowledges the thousands of missing and murdered Indigenous women, as well as symbolizing the tribe's living culture.
