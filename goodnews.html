Study Suggests Gut Movement May Aid Brain Waste Clearance

2026-05-07 19:04:50 EDT

New research indicates that coordinated contractions in abdominal muscles, like those during walking, influence brain movement and may play a crucial role in flushing out waste. Neuroscientist Patrick Drew's team at Penn State observed that mouse brains move milliseconds before a step, linking this motion to abdominal muscle contractions. The connection is attributed to the vertebral venous plexus, a network of veins connecting the abdomen to the spine, creating a hydraulic system that increases pressure on the brain. Computer simulations suggest this mechanism helps move cerebrospinal fluid, potentially aiding in the clearance of protein waste and other unnecessary materials from the brain.
