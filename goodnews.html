Understanding the 7-Year Rule for Credit Card Debt on Credit Reports

2025-09-30 13:48:22 EDT

The widely discussed "7-year rule" for credit card debt primarily refers to the Fair Credit Reporting Act, which mandates that most negative information, such as late payments and charge-offs, must be removed from a credit report after seven years. This clock typically starts from the date of the first missed payment. Additionally, states have statutes of limitations, often around seven years, that prevent creditors from suing to collect old debts. However, the debt itself does not disappear and collectors may still attempt to contact the individual. Making a payment on an old debt can sometimes restart the statute of limitations, making it crucial for consumers to understand their rights.
