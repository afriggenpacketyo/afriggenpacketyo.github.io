Hanukkah: Origins, Traditions, and Modern Celebrations

2025-12-12 09:54:00 EST

Hanukkah, the Jewish Festival of Lights, commemorates the rededication of the Second Temple in Jerusalem following the Maccabean Revolt around 200 BC. Celebrated over eight days, it marks the miracle of a small jar of oil burning for eight days. The festival involves lighting a nine-branched candelabrum, the hanukkiah, each evening after sunset. While traditionally not a gift-giving holiday, it has become more commercialized, with customs including exchanging gifts and enjoying oil-fried foods like sufganiyot (jelly doughnuts) and latkes (potato pancakes). The date of Hanukkah varies annually according to the Gregorian calendar.
