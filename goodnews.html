First Drug to Aid Walking for Multiple Sclerosis Patients Approved for NHS in England

2026-07-22 19:38:58 EDT

The first drug specifically designed to help individuals with multiple sclerosis (MS) improve their walking ability is now being made available on the NHS in England. Fampridine, known as a "signal booster," works by enhancing nerve signals, thereby making movement easier for eligible patients. This medication, previously available in Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland but rejected in England due to cost-effectiveness, could benefit up to 5,000 people annually. Clinical trials have shown that approximately four in ten users experienced significant improvements in both their walking speed and endurance, offering a life-changing enhancement to their independence and overall quality of life.
