Jehovah's Witnesses Win Legal Battle for Holocaust Persecution Archive in Germany

2026-07-05 02:19:00 EDT

Germany's Federal Court of Justice has ruled in favor of the Jehovah's Witnesses in a dispute over a unique archive meticulously documenting their persecution during the Holocaust. The archive, bequeathed by survivor Annemarie Kusserow, was unlawfully sold to the Bundeswehr Military History Museum by her brother. The court ruled that in cases of a "singular, historical significant archive," the state has a duty to investigate the seller's right to ownership. This legal victory is significant for the religious community, which endured severe persecution, including imprisonment and executions, under the Nazi regime for their refusal to pledge allegiance or serve in the military, and faced continued persecution in the GDR.
