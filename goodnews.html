This breakthrough, exploring around 1,000 genes across 13 feline cancer types, suggests shared biological processes driving tumor growth. Scientists believe studying household pets, exposed to similar environmental factors as humans, could unlock new insights into cancer development, risk factors, and potential treatments for both species, making the domestic cat a key research model.
