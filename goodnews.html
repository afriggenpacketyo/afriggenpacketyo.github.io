Renowned theoretical physicist Chen-ning Yang, a Nobel Prize winner, has died in Beijing at the age of 103. He was widely regarded as one of the 20th century’s greatest physicists, often ranked alongside Albert Einstein. In 1954, Yang co-authored a set of equations with Robert Mills, resulting in the Yang–Mills theory, which proved as significant as Einstein’s theory of relativity. This theory described how the electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions operate in the subatomic world, forming the mathematical basis for the Standard Model, the cornerstone of modern physics. His profound contributions unified fundamental forces and explained elementary particle behavior.
