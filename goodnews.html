Health Guide Distinguishes Between Heat Exhaustion and Heat Stroke and Advises on Emergency Response

2025-07-01 07:40:00 EDT

As extreme heat becomes more common, it is crucial to understand the difference between heat exhaustion and the more dangerous heat stroke. Heat exhaustion presents with symptoms like headache, heavy sweating, and dizziness, and can be treated by moving to a cool place and rehydrating. However, if left untreated, it can progress to heat stroke, a life-threatening condition where the body's temperature regulation fails and sweat production stops. Heat stroke requires immediate emergency medical attention, and first aid involves cooling the person's body as quickly as possible while waiting for help to arrive.
