Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is celebrating a major milestone: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation, making it the longest such record in the UK and Ireland. Since July 14, 1795, observers have manually recorded temperature and air pressure, creating an invaluable dataset for climate science. This unbroken tradition, maintained by hand, predates modern meteorology and has captured significant weather events, from major storms to the first observations of noctilucent clouds. The institution also credits observer Theresa Hardcastle, who ensured the record's continuity after her husband's death in 1917.
