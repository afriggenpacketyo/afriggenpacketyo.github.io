Explainer on Methane's Potent but Short-Lived Role in Climate Change

2025-10-22 05:04:00 EDT

Methane (CH4) is a powerful greenhouse gas responsible for about 20-30% of global warming since the Industrial Revolution, trapping roughly 84 times more heat than carbon dioxide over a 20-year period. While it degrades in the atmosphere in about 12 years, its short-term impact is significant. Approximately 60% of methane emissions come from human activities, primarily agriculture, landfills, and the energy sector, where it leaks during the production and transport of oil and gas. Experts state that simple measures, such as detecting and fixing leaks in fossil fuel infrastructure, could slash energy-sector methane emissions by 75%, representing a critical opportunity for rapid climate action.
