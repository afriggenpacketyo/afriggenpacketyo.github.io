EU reaches agreement on new rules to strengthen vital medicines supply chains

2026-05-12 02:08:00 EDT

European Union negotiators have agreed on new rules designed to bolster the bloc's supply chains for essential medicines and reduce reliance on non-EU manufacturers. The initiative aims to combat drug shortages, which have affected the availability of critical medicines like painkillers and antibiotics. The proposed rules would prioritize medicines produced within Europe in public procurement, offer faster approvals and funding for strategic projects, and facilitate joint purchasing among member states. These measures are a response to vulnerabilities exposed during the COVID-19 pandemic and the increasing concentration of pharmaceutical production in Asia, particularly China.
