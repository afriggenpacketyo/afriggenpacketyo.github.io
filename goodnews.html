UK Clocks to Go Back on Last Sunday of October

2025-10-11 19:32:29 EDT

The clocks in the UK will revert to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) on the last Sunday of October, marking the end of British Summer Time (BST). This annual change, occurring at 02:00 BST on Sunday, October 26, will grant most people an extra hour of sleep. The shift will bring sunset forward by an hour, leading to noticeably darker evenings. Daylight Saving Time, first adopted by Germany in 1916, was introduced in the UK to maximize evening daylight during wartime. Debates persist about permanently adopting BST, with arguments for economic boosts and reduced road accidents, but concerns over dark winter mornings, especially in Scotland.
