China-US Study Reveals Mars Possesses Unexpected Solid Inner Core

2025-09-07 22:00:09 EDT

A new China-US study, leveraging seismic waves recorded by NASA’s now-retired InSight lander, has revealed that Mars possesses a solid inner core. This core, approximately 600km in radius, constitutes about 18% of Mars’ total radius, a proportion comparable to Earth’s inner core. Scientists found this discovery unexpected, as most celestial bodies in the solar system have not cooled enough to form such a solid core, which typically requires significant time for liquid metal to "freeze" under specific pressure and temperature conditions. This finding could revive the long-debated theory that Earth was not the only planet to have once developed plate tectonics, adding Mars to the exclusive list of celestial bodies with solid inner cores.

The red color results from sunlight reflecting and scattering through Earth's atmosphere, with shorter blue wavelengths dispersing more easily.
