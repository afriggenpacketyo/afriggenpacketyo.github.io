Social Security Survivor Benefits Generally Protected from Debt Garnishment

2026-05-19 11:59:29 EDT

Social Security survivor benefits generally receive federal protections, shielding them from garnishment by private creditors for common consumer debts like credit cards or medical bills, as federal law largely shields these benefits from traditional garnishment actions. However, certain government agencies, such as the IRS for unpaid federal taxes or the Department of Education for federal student loans, can still garnish up to 15% of monthly payments under specific circumstances. A critical vulnerability arises once funds are deposited into a bank account, as protections become murky beyond two months' worth of deposits, potentially requiring court action to prove the funds' origin. Beneficiaries are advised to understand their rights and proactively explore debt relief options to protect their financial stability.
