Separated Baby Sea Otter Successfully Reunited with Mother on California Coast

2025-11-14 11:58:04 EST

Scientists Successfully Extract Ancient RNA from 39,000-Year-Old Woolly Mammoth

2025-11-14 11:41:30 EST

Researchers have successfully extracted and analyzed RNA from the remarkably well-preserved remains of a 39,000-year-old woolly mammoth named Yuka, found in Siberian permafrost. Unlike DNA, which is a blueprint, RNA provides a snapshot of which genes were active at the time of death, revealing cellular processes. The analysis confirmed the presence of RNA related to muscle function and stress responses, consistent with the theory that the animal may have been attacked by predators. This breakthrough opens new possibilities for studying extinct species and even ancient RNA-based viruses.
