Exploring the Downsides of Gold Ownership Despite Recent Price Surges

2026-03-03 11:07:08 EST

Despite gold's recent price surge, offering over 100% returns in a year, investors should understand its inherent downsides. Gold does not generate income like stocks or bonds, making its value dependent on future price shifts. Physical gold incurs storage and insurance costs, while ETFs have expense ratios. Liquidity can be complex for physical gold, and in the U.S., it's taxed as a collectible at a higher long-term capital gains rate of up to 28%. Additionally, over-allocating to gold creates concentration risk, with most advisors recommending keeping it below 10% of a portfolio. (not investment advice)

Following sporadic initial eruptions, it settled into regular 20-30 foot eruptions every few hours.
