Donation After Circulatory Death (DCD) Revolutionizes Organ Transplantation in the US

2026-07-23 06:20:00 EDT

Donation after Circulatory Death (DCD) has significantly transformed organ transplantation in the U.S., now accounting for nearly half of all organ donors. This process allows organs to be recovered after circulation ceases, unlike traditional brain-death donation. Advances in technology and surgical techniques have made DCD more viable, increasing the supply of life-saving organs. However, the process creates a critical interval between the decision to withdraw life support and the declaration of death, raising ethical considerations and the need for strict safeguards to ensure donation does not influence end-of-life decisions.
