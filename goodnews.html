Atmospheric Conditions Cited as Reason for No US Hurricane Landfalls This Season

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

For the first time in a decade, the Atlantic hurricane season is concluding without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States. Meteorologists attribute this unusual outcome to a persistent weather pattern known as "recurvature," where storms veered northward and out to sea before reaching the coast. This was caused by an area of low pressure, or an upper-level trough, over the eastern U.S., which weakened the western edge of the Bermuda High pressure system. This atmospheric setup repeatedly steered the 13 named storms of the season away from the U.S. coastline.
