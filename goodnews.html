The Evolution of the Peak District: From Hidden Wonders to UK's First National Park

2026-04-18 19:36:46 EDT

The Peak District, Britain's first national park, is celebrating 75 years, with its history as a tourist attraction dating back centuries, including a famous cave controversially renamed "Devil's Arse." The advent of railways in the 19th century significantly opened the area to mass tourism, yet limited public access due to extensive private ownership sparked major protests like the 1932 Kinder Scout mass trespass. This pivotal event, alongside persistent campaigning by groups such as the Ramblers' Association, ultimately contributed to the 1949 act establishing national parks and the subsequent 2000 Countryside and Rights of Way Act. These legislative milestones transformed the region into a cherished "Natural Health Service" with expanded public access.
