Strategies to Stop Wage Garnishment This March

2026-03-06 12:27:46 EST

Facing wage garnishment can severely impact financial stability, but several options exist to halt or reduce it. Filing for bankruptcy, either Chapter 7 or Chapter 13, triggers an automatic stay that immediately stops most collection activities, including garnishment. Negotiating a settlement directly with the creditor, often for a lump-sum payment, can also lead to voluntary cessation of garnishment. Challenging the garnishment in court is possible if procedural errors or other legal grounds exist. Claiming exemptions under federal and state laws, particularly for protected income like Social Security, can reduce or eliminate garnishment. Enrolling in a debt management plan through a credit counseling agency may also lead creditors to pause collection.
