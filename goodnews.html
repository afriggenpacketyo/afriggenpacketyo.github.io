Bioengineered Food Pipe Offers Hope for Young Patients with Esophageal Defects

2026-03-20 06:41:40 EDT

A groundbreaking medical advancement, involving the implantation of a lab-grown food pipe in pigs, offers significant hope for young patients born with missing sections of their esophagus. Scientists at Great Ormond Street Hospital and University College London successfully created functional esophageal sections using donor pig tissue stripped of cells, then reseeded with new cells and matured in a bioreactor. This innovation has restored normal swallowing function in Göttingen minipigs, a breed anatomically similar to human children. Researchers aim to offer this treatment to children within the next five years, potentially revolutionizing care for conditions like esophageal atresia.
