Study Reveals AI Productivity May Lead to New Pattern of 'AI Brain Fry' Burnout

2026-03-08 13:57:54 EDT

A new study published in Harvard Business Review suggests that artificial intelligence, rather than solely easing work, may be causing a new form of burnout dubbed "AI brain fry." Researchers found that workers constantly juggling multiple AI tools reported increased decision fatigue and more errors, with one in seven experiencing mental fatigue from this cognitive load. The study highlights a "productivity paradox" where AI can reduce burnout by offloading repetitive tasks, but simultaneously create it through intensive oversight and the expansion of workers' perceived capabilities. Businesses are urged to redesign workflows and provide intentional leadership and training to mitigate this cognitive overload, as "AI brain fry" can lead to mistakes and slower decision-making.
