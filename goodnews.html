Ranked-Choice Voting in NYC Mayoral Primary Explained

2025-06-17 18:33:14 EDT

New Yorkers will use ranked-choice voting in the upcoming mayoral primary, allowing voters to rank their top five candidates. If no candidate receives over 50% of first-choice votes, the candidate with the least votes is eliminated, and the votes are redistributed based on voters' second choices. This system has led to unusual alliances, with some candidates cross-endorsing each other to prevent a particular candidate from winning. The New York City Board of Elections will provide preliminary totals for first-choice votes on election night, but full results will be reported a week later due to the complexity of tabulating ranked-choice ballots.
