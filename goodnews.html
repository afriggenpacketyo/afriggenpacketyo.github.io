Reverse Mortgages Do Not Directly Affect Social Security or Medicare Benefits

2025-08-28 14:25:15 EDT

A reverse mortgage, a financial product allowing homeowners aged 62 and older to convert home equity into cash without making traditional monthly payments, does not directly reduce or interfere with Social Security or Medicare benefits. Social Security payments are calculated based on an individual's earnings history, independent of assets, and Medicare eligibility is not determined by income or wealth. However, critical distinctions apply to need-based programs such as Supplemental Security Income (SSI) and Medicaid. Significant, unspent lump-sum withdrawals from a reverse mortgage could potentially cause recipients to exceed the strict asset limits for SSI or Medicaid, thereby affecting their eligibility for these particular programs. Consulting a financial advisor is highly recommended to structure payouts strategically and avoid unintended impacts on these vital lifelines. (not investment advice)
