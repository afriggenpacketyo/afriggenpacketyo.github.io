Ancient Horses Migrated Between Asia and North America via Land Bridge

2025-05-15 22:00:07 EDT

A new study reveals that ancient horses repeatedly migrated between North America and Eurasia during the late Pleistocene era, approximately 50,000 to 13,000 years ago. This migration occurred when lower sea levels created a land bridge connecting the two continents. The research indicates genetic exchanges between horse populations in both directions, with fossils of Dalianensis horses found in northeastern China showing mixed ancestry from Eurasian and American populations. These Dalianensis horses, which lived around 17,000 years ago, were slightly larger than modern wild horses, highlighting the dynamic movement and genetic mixing of species across continents during this period.
