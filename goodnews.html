Civic Assemblies Emerge as a Method to Enhance Public Participation in U.S. Politics

2026-05-21 09:00:11 EDT

Civic assemblies, where randomly selected residents deliberate on complex local issues, are gaining traction in the U.S. as a way to re-engage citizens in governance. Originating in Europe, these assemblies involve participants meeting for extended periods with experts to formulate policy recommendations, which are then often adopted by local officials. Examples include Los Angeles residents shaping a city charter preamble and recommending council expansion, and assemblies in Kentucky and North Carolina addressing transparency and land use. Proponents believe these processes foster direct participation, community problem-solving, and a deeper understanding of policy challenges, moving beyond traditional political divisions.
