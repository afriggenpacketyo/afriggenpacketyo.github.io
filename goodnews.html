Report Finds Limited Long-Term Benefits to Delaying Kindergarten Enrollment

2026-03-24 00:02:40 EDT

A new report indicates that "red-shirting," the practice of holding a child back from kindergarten for an additional year, provides limited long-term benefits, with any initial academic advantages typically evening out by the third grade. The article highlights that while older kindergartners might initially appear more prepared, this academic edge is not sustained. Potential disadvantages include a higher risk of dropping out of school later and losing a year of potential workforce experience. The report also notes that this practice is more prevalent among affluent families who can afford extended childcare, though it acknowledges that individual circumstances, such as developmental or behavioral delays, can still make delaying kindergarten a beneficial option for some children.

Australia and European Union Finalize Major Free Trade Agreement

2026-03-23 21:28:00 EDT

Australia and the European Union have successfully finalized a significant new free trade deal following eight years of negotiations, an agreement announced jointly by EU chief Ursula von der Leyen and Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese. The article highlights that this accord, valued at approximately A$10 billion, is set to eliminate over 99% of tariffs on EU goods exports to Australia and substantially reduce tariffs on Australian agricultural products such as wine, fruit, and most dairy. Key concessions include Australia's ability to continue using some protected geographical names like feta and gruyere, and 'prosecco' for exports for a transitional period of 10 years. This deal aims to significantly diversify trade and foster "collective resilience" in an evolving global landscape.
