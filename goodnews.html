Paleontologists Discover 500-Million-Year-Old, Three-Eyed Predator

2025-05-17 16:38:36 EDT

Paleontologists have discovered a 500-million-year-old fossil of a three-eyed predator named Mosura fentoni, nicknamed the "sea moth," in the Burgess Shale of the Canadian Rockies. This creature, part of the extinct group radiodonts, was about the length of an index finger and had a circular mouth lined with teeth. The discovery reveals that Mosura had a more complex anatomy than previously known for radiodonts, with a multi-segmented abdomen-like region lined with gills, similar to modern arthropods. The findings provide new insights into the diversity of life during the Cambrian period.
