Understanding the Longevity and Limits of Debt Collection Efforts

2025-11-14 13:11:54 EST

While debt collectors rarely give up on pursuing an unpaid debt, there are legal limits on how long they can use the courts to force payment. A debt may be sold multiple times to different agencies, but each state has a statute of limitations (typically 3-6 years) after which a lawsuit cannot be filed. However, collectors can still attempt to collect voluntarily on this "time-barred" debt, and making a payment or promising to do so can restart the legal clock. Consumers have rights under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act to stop harassment and can explore debt relief programs to resolve unmanageable balances.
