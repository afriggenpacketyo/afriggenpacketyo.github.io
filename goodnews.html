Post-Brexit Food Deal May Require UK Marmalade to Be Re-branded as 'Citrus Marmalade'

2026-04-02 19:03:38 EDT

Under the UK government's proposed EU food deal, British marmalade may need to be re-branded as "citrus marmalade" on shop shelves. This change aligns with updated EU regulations, which now broaden the legal definition of marmalade across Europe to include non-citrus spreads, departing from decades-old rules influenced by British lobbying for Seville orange marmalade. While the new name is set for Northern Ireland, its implementation in England, Wales, and Scotland is pending, with regulators considering potential consumer confusion. Manufacturers are already adapting labels, though the impact on British perception of marmalade remains uncertain.

Artemis II Crew Cleared for Crucial Translunar Injection Burn to the Moon

2026-04-02 17:56:21 EDT

The Artemis II mission management team has cleared the Orion capsule and its four-person crew, including Christina Koch as the first woman to travel around the moon, for a critical translunar injection (TLI) burn. This maneuver will propel the spacecraft out of Earth's orbit and onto a "free-return trajectory" for its 10-day journey around the moon. The crew has successfully tested Orion's systems and maneuverability. This trailblazing flight aims to test procedures for future moon landing missions, with NASA planning another Orion crew flight next year to rehearse rendezvous and docking for 2028 landings.
