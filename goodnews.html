NASA Captures Image of Intense X-Class Solar Flare

2025-06-18 12:14:50 EDT

NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory captured an image of an intense X-class solar flare, the most powerful type, erupting from the sun on Tuesday evening. Solar flares are giant explosions that release energy, light, and high-speed particles into space, potentially disrupting radio communications, electric power grids, and navigation signals. These flares are often associated with coronal mass ejections (CMEs), which are large bubbles of plasma that can also disrupt communications and cause geomagnetic storms on Earth. In May 2024, a strong CME caused the most significant geomagnetic storm in over 20 years, leading to radio blackouts and visible northern lights as far south as Florida.
