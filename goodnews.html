MIT Scientist Offers Nuanced View on AI's Impact on the Labor Market

2025-11-28 06:45:00 EST

This article presents an interview with MIT researcher Neil Thompson, who argues against the simple narrative that AI will destroy all jobs. He explains that automation's effect depends on whether it replaces high-expert or low-expert tasks within a profession. For example, when GPS automated the most expert task of taxi drivers (navigation), their wages fell, but the number of drivers increased. Conversely, when spellcheck automated the least expert task of proofreaders, their wages rose, but their numbers shrank. He suggests that while AI will cause job churn, history shows that new roles emerge.
