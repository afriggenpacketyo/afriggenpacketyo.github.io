The Reverse Mortgage 6-Month Rule and Its Implications for Heirs Explained

2025-09-16 12:55:29 EDT

A key feature of reverse mortgages, particularly federally insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgages (HECMs), is the "6-month rule." This rule dictates that after the borrower dies or permanently leaves the home, their heirs have six months to repay the loan balance, typically by selling the property or refinancing. Lenders must issue a "Due and Payable" letter within 30 days of this qualifying event, starting the clock. While extensions are possible, this timeline emphasizes the need for homeowners with reverse mortgages to engage in careful estate planning and communicate clearly with their families to avoid a stressful deadline or potential foreclosure.
