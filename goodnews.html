Understanding Home Ownership with a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

A reverse mortgage allows homeowners aged 62 and older to convert home equity into cash without monthly payments, but it raises questions about ownership. The homeowner retains the legal title and ownership of the house; the lender only places a lien on the property to secure the loan. The loan balance, including interest and fees, grows over time and becomes due when the borrower sells the home, moves out, or passes away. At that point, the borrower's heirs can choose to repay the loan to keep the home or sell the property, pay off the mortgage, and retain any remaining equity.
