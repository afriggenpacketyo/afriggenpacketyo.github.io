World's Oldest and Largest Iceberg, A23a, Nears End of Its Journey

2025-09-05 05:01:00 EDT

The world's oldest and largest iceberg, A23a, is rapidly breaking apart and melting in the warmer waters of the South Atlantic Ocean, signaling the end of its nearly 40-year journey. Calved from Antarctica's Filchner-Ronne ice shelf in 1986, A23a has repeatedly held the title of largest iceberg, having prolonged its existence by getting stuck twice in colder waters. Now, having drifted far north into waters well above freezing, it has shrunk significantly and is expected to disintegrate completely in the coming weeks. Scientists confirm this is a normal part of the iceberg lifecycle, not directly linked to climate change, and its demise will continue to provide valuable data on how such enormous icebergs impact ocean environments.
