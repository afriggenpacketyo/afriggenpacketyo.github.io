Businesses Employ 'Tariff Engineering' to Skirt Higher Duties

2025-06-18 03:56:58 EDT

Faced with increasing tariffs, businesses are employing "tariff engineering" to reclassify products and reduce duty payments. This involves altering materials, dimensions, or compositions to fit into a different harmonized system code with lower tariff rates. While not inherently illegal, this practice allows companies to strategically design products to minimize duty liability, with examples ranging from adding cubic zirconia to lapel pins to altering clothing designs. Sectors like fast-moving consumer goods and garments find tariff engineering relatively easy, while highly regulated sectors like automotive and aerospace face greater challenges due to extensive validation requirements.
