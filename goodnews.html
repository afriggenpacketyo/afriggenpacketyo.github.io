The amendment to the Crime and Policing Bill, passed by a majority of 242 votes, means women who terminate their pregnancies outside the current legal framework will no longer face criminal investigation. The law will still penalize anyone who assists a woman in getting an abortion outside the legal framework. The move was welcomed by abortion providers and women's rights campaigners, but criticized by anti-abortion groups.
