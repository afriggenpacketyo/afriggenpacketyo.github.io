New Jersey Meteorite Reveals "Alien World Chemistry" and Prebiotic Molecules

2026-07-16 10:26:00 EDT

A meteorite that impacted a New Jersey home in July 2024 has been identified as a rare CM1/2 carbonaceous chondrite, containing prebiotic molecules and other fundamental "building blocks of life." Scientists discovered that the meteorite had been exposed to "concentrated salty fluids," or brine, indicating that its parent asteroid once harbored liquid water that subsequently evaporated. The presence of these brines, along with soluble organic compounds like magnesium organic compounds and amino acids, lends strong support to the theory that such meteorites may have delivered crucial organic materials to early Earth, potentially contributing to the genesis of life. This finding is hailed as one of the most scientifically valuable meteorite recoveries.
