Assisted Dying Law Approved in Jersey, Second British Isle to Legalize Practice

2026-02-26 08:46:28 EST

Jersey has approved a landmark law granting terminally ill adults the right to choose to end their own lives, making it the second part of the British Isles, after the Isle of Man, to fully legalize assisted dying. Eligible individuals must have a terminal illness causing unbearable suffering, with an expected lifespan of six months, or 12 months for neurodegenerative conditions. The law, which passed with 32 votes in favor and 16 against, will now proceed for Royal Assent in the UK, with the first legal assisted deaths potentially occurring as early as next summer. Proponents hail it as a compassionate step, while opponents raise deep ethical concerns.
