Experts Debunk Myth That Turkey's Tryptophan Causes Post-Thanksgiving Sleepiness

2025-11-27 10:02:38 EST

The long-held belief that turkey is the cause of Thanksgiving drowsiness due to its tryptophan content is a myth, according to scientific analysis. While turkey does contain the sleep-associated amino acid, it has no more than other meats like chicken and far less than some cheeses or soy products. The real culprit for post-feast fatigue is postprandial somnolence, the body's natural response to digesting a large, high-carbohydrate, high-fat meal, which diverts blood flow from the brain to the gut.
