The Historical Connection Between St. Martin's Day and Carnival in Germany Explained

2025-11-11 00:58:00 EST

In Germany, November 11th marks two seemingly unrelated celebrations: the solemn St. Martin's Day processions with children's lanterns and the exuberant start of the Carnival season. Both customs, however, share a common medieval origin tied to the agricultural calendar and religious fasting. St. Martin's Day historically signified the end of the harvest year and was the last day for feasting before the start of Advent, which was a six-week period of fasting before Christmas. Carnival's kickoff on this day thus represents a final, joyous indulgence before a period of religious solemnity.
