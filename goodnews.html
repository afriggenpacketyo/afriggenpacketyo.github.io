Rare New Zealand Snail Filmed Laying Egg from its Neck

2025-05-08 05:24:48 EDT

For the first time, footage has captured a Powelliphanta augusta snail, a threatened species endemic to New Zealand, laying an egg from its neck. The video was taken at a facility where conservation rangers have been caring for the snails in chilled containers for nearly two decades, mimicking their natural alpine habitat. These snails are hermaphrodites and can reproduce when encased in a hard shell, simultaneously exchanging sperm with another snail, which is stored until each creates an egg. Each snail takes eight years to reach sexual maturity and lays about five eggs a year, with each egg taking over a year to hatch, highlighting the species' slow survival and reproductive habits.
