Northern Lights Expected to Illuminate UK Skies Again Following Strong Solar Storm

2026-03-21 04:57:17 EDT

The breathtaking Northern Lights, also known as the Aurora Borealis, are anticipated to grace the skies across the United Kingdom once more on Saturday evening, building on a strong geomagnetic storm that produced a visible display as far south as Norfolk on Friday. This celestial phenomenon occurs when charged particles from the Sun, released during a coronal mass ejection, interact with gases in Earth's atmosphere, creating vibrant colors. Forecasters at the Met Office Space Weather Prediction Centre expect geomagnetic activity to remain high, increasing the likelihood of seeing the lights across Scotland, Northern Ireland, and northern areas of England and Wales, with a small chance for southern regions. Clear skies in the early part of Saturday night are expected to provide optimal viewing conditions for this natural wonder.
