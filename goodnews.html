Opinion Piece Explores the Medieval Origins of the Modern Slang Phrase '6-7'

2025-11-20 10:42:38 EST

A recent cultural phenomenon among middle schoolers, the slang phrase "6-7," has historical roots dating back to the 14th century. The expression originated from a dice game called Hazard, a precursor to craps, where rolling a six and seven together was associated with risk, worry, and confusion. This meaning has persisted through centuries, appearing in the works of Chaucer and Shakespeare, eventually evolving into the phrase "at sixes and sevens." The article argues that the endurance of this phrase highlights a deep, often unrecognized connection between modern English speakers and their medieval ancestors.
