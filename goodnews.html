Understanding Home Ownership Dynamics with a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

This article clarifies a common point of confusion regarding reverse mortgages: the homeowner retains legal ownership and the title to their property. The lender does not take ownership but instead places a lien on the home to secure the loan, which accrues interest over time. Homeowners are still responsible for property taxes, insurance, and maintenance. The loan typically becomes due when the borrower sells the home, moves out, or passes away, at which point the balance must be repaid by the owner or their heirs.
