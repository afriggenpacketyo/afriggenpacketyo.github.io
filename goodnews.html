Report Compares Housing Affordability Across U.S. States

2025-11-22 09:30:01 EST

A new analysis from ConsumerAffairs reveals significant disparities in the time required to save for a 10% home down payment across different U.S. states. In Iowa, it takes an estimated 8.7 years, the shortest duration in the nation, while in California, it would take over 25 years. The study calculates these timelines by comparing median household income with median home prices, taxes, and living costs, assuming a household saves 10% of its discretionary income annually. The findings show that affordability is primarily driven by lower home prices rather than higher incomes, with all but one of the top 10 fastest-saving states having home prices below the national median.
