Chapter 13 Bankruptcy: Understanding Income and Repayment

2025-10-22 12:07:07 EDT

Chapter 13 bankruptcy does not take all of your income, but requires filers to put disposable income toward a repayment plan over three to five years. Disposable income is what remains after paying for reasonable and necessary living expenses, as determined by the bankruptcy court. The court reviews a detailed budget of monthly income and expenses to establish a realistic payment amount, ensuring filers maintain a basic standard of living. Not everyone repays the same percentage of their debt, as payments are based on affordability rather than taking all earnings. (not investment advice)
