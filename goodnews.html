New Study Suggests Gut Movement May Aid Brain Waste Clearance

2026-05-07 19:04:50 EDT

New research suggests that coordinated contractions in abdominal muscles, like those during walking, may play a crucial role in brain health by helping to flush out waste material. Neuroscientist Patrick Drew's team observed that brain movement inside the fluid-filled skull cavity occurs milliseconds before a step, propelled by abdominal muscle contractions. This motion, linked by the vertebral venous plexus, pushes blood into the spinal cord, increasing pressure on the brain and moving cerebrospinal fluid out. The study, published in Nature Neuroscience, hypothesizes that this "hydraulic system" is vital for clearing protein waste, opening new avenues for research into how physical conditions like obesity affect brain health.
