Florida's Big Cypress National Preserve Celebrates 51st Anniversary

2025-10-05 05:06:33 EDT

Big Cypress National Preserve, the first national preserve in the United States, is celebrating its 51st anniversary. Established on October 11, 1974, the preserve was created through the combined efforts of conservationists, hunters, and local Indigenous tribes to protect the area from a proposed jetport development in the Everglades. Spanning 729,000 acres, the preserve protects a vital freshwater swamp that supports the health of the Everglades and the rich marine estuaries on Florida's southwest coast. The unique designation as a preserve allows for specific activities less regulated than in a national park, born from a compromise to protect the land without adding it to the nearby Everglades National Park.
