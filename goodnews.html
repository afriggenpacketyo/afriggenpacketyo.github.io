An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Jurisdiction

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial body of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between states. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, its rulings are legally binding on UN member states, though it lacks a mechanism for direct enforcement. The court's jurisdiction applies when states agree to submit to it, or when a case is brought under a specific UN convention, such as the Genocide Convention. Recent high-profile cases involve Ukraine's suit against Russia and South Africa's case against Israel.
