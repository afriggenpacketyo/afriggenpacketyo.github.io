Antarctica's First Dinosaur Bone Identified After Decades in Storage

2026-06-29 01:10:46 EDT

A fossil initially unearthed in 1985 and stored for 40 years in a drawer at the British Antarctic Survey has been confirmed as Antarctica's first dinosaur bone. Palaeontologists identified the 10cm specimen as a tail bone from a Titanosaur, a group known for containing the largest dinosaurs. The discovery was initially recorded as a "vertebra of large reptile," with researchers at the time likely mistaking it for a marine reptile fossil. This finding provides crucial insights into the prehistoric life of Antarctica, revealing that the now-uninhabitable continent once supported a diverse ecosystem around 80 million years ago, despite the challenges of fossil excavation in the region.
