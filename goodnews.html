NASA Delays Artemis II Moon Mission Again to Address Helium Pressurization Issue.

NASA has moved its Artemis II moon rocket off the launch pad and back to the Vehicle Assembly Building to address a helium pressurization problem, further delaying the mission until at least April 1. This issue, discovered after a recent fueling test, prevents the proper repressurization of the upper stage's helium system, which is critical for pushing propellants and minimizing fire risk. This marks another setback for the mission, which had previously experienced delays due to hydrogen leaks that required suspect seals to be replaced. Engineers will now gain access to the upper stage to repair the system and replace limited-life batteries, impacting the launch window for the four-astronaut flight around the moon.
