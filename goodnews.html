2026-06-01 17:01:43 EDT

Painted rock art, consisting of 10 red horizontal stripes, found in Gower's Bacon Hole cave in Wales has been confirmed as Britain's oldest prehistoric art, dating back at least 17,100 years. Initially dismissed in 1928 as natural mineral seepage, scientific advances, including uranium-thorium dating, proved its human origin. Archaeologists believe the art could have been a "communication system" for hunter-gatherers who inhabited the fertile plateau that once connected Gower to Devon. This "significant rediscovery" sheds new light on early human activity in north-western Europe and highlights the need for the cave to be protected as a scheduled monument.
