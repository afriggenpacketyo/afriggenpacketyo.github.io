Millions of sterile male Mexican fruit flies are slated for release over parts of San Diego County following the detection of a mated female fruit fly near Spring Valley. This initiative, known as "sterile insect technique," aims to curb the population of this destructive pest by having sterile males mate with wild females, thereby halting reproduction. The Mexican fruit fly is a highly damaging pest that lays eggs in fruits like apples, grapefruits, and avocados, causing them to rot. A quarantine area in San Diego County, now encompassing 76 square miles, has been expanded, with the sterile male flies to be dispersed at a rate of 250,000 per square mile weekly.
