Exploring the Origins of 'Soccer' in the U.S. and Canada

2026-06-13 11:33:54 EDT

This article delves into why the sport known globally as "football" is called "soccer" in the U.S. and Canada. According to an emeritus professor at the University of Michigan, the term "soccer" originated in elite Oxford universities in England during the late 19th century. It was a slang shortening of "association football," distinguishing it from "rugby football." As both sports spread, "soccer" traveled to other continents, including North America, where it became prevalent alongside the rise of American football. The article notes that British newspapers continued to use "soccer" into the 1980s, contrary to popular belief about its American exclusivity.
