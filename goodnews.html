Physiological Changes Cause Increased Alcohol Sensitivity with Age

2025-12-08 05:00:00 EST

Medical experts explain that people often experience increased sensitivity to alcohol as they age due to several physiological changes in the body. The liver enzymes responsible for metabolizing alcohol become less efficient, and a natural decline in muscle mass reduces the body's water content, which would otherwise dilute alcohol in the bloodstream. These factors lead to a higher blood alcohol concentration from the same amount of drink, causing the effects to be felt more intensely and for longer. For women in perimenopause, alcohol can also exacerbate symptoms like mood swings and sleep disruption, making moderation increasingly important for overall health.
