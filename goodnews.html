NASA Satellite Set for Atmospheric Re-entry After 14 Years in Space

2026-03-10 10:35:09 EDT

A 1,300-pound NASA probe, the Van Allen Probe A, is scheduled to re-enter Earth's atmosphere approximately 14 years after its launch in August 2012. NASA estimates the re-entry will occur around 7:45 p.m. ET on Tuesday, with a 24-hour margin of uncertainty. The probe is expected to largely burn up upon atmospheric entry, though some components may survive. The risk to people on Earth is considered low, with NASA citing a 1 in 4,200 chance of harm. The Van Allen Probes were designed to study Earth's radiation belts, and their mission significantly exceeded its initial two-year duration, breaking records for spacecraft longevity in that environment. The probe's twin, Van Allen Probe B, is expected to re-enter in the 2030s.

Understanding Bankruptcy Waiting Periods is Crucial Before Filing in March

2026-03-10 10:17:41 EDT

Individuals considering bankruptcy in March must understand the strict waiting periods that govern re-filing after a previous discharge. These timelines vary significantly based on the type of bankruptcy filed (Chapter 7 or Chapter 13) and the intended new filing. For instance, filing Chapter 7 again typically requires an eight-year wait, while Chapter 7 to Chapter 13 has a four-year wait. Filing too soon can limit debt discharge eligibility, even if a new case is permitted. The waiting period is calculated from the filing date, not the discharge date. Consulting with a bankruptcy attorney is recommended to navigate these complex rules and explore alternative debt relief strategies.

Expert Reveals Five Phrases Highly Successful People Use to Stay Motivated

2026-03-10 09:30:02 EDT

According to behavioral design expert Nir Eyal, highly successful individuals employ specific phrases to maintain motivation through setbacks. These phrases reframe challenges as temporary learning opportunities rather than personal verdicts. Examples include 'I haven't figured this out yet,' which transforms a perceived limitation into a solvable problem, and 'This is information, not a verdict,' to avoid emotional reasoning. Asking 'What would you tell a friend?' fosters self-compassion, while 'I'm building the skill, not proving my talent' shifts focus to growth. Finally, 'This feeling is temporary, my goal isn't' helps individuals embrace discomfort and persevere towards their objectives.
