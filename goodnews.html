California's 'Mini Volcanoes' Identified as Spontaneous Combustion, Not Volcanic Activity

2026-04-24 18:18:57 EDT

Localized flare-ups along California's coast, often referred to as "Hope Ranch Volcano" and "Rincon Volcano," are not actual volcanoes, according to geological experts. These phenomena are attributed to spontaneous combustion, where oxygen interacts with unstable compounds like iron sulfides in the cliffside rocks during earth movements. This oxidation process generates intense heat, causing organic materials to ignite. While visually resembling volcanic activity, geologists confirm the absence of underlying volcanoes, clarifying a long-standing local misconception about these recurring fiery events.
