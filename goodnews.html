Understanding Home Ownership with a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

A reverse mortgage allows homeowners aged 62 and older to borrow against their home's equity without making monthly payments, providing cash flow for retirees on fixed incomes. Contrary to some misconceptions, the borrower retains the title and legal ownership of the home throughout the loan period; the lender simply places a lien on the property. The loan balance, including interest and fees, becomes due when the homeowner sells the house, moves out permanently, or passes away. Heirs then have the option to repay the loan to keep the home or sell it to settle the debt. (not investment advice)
