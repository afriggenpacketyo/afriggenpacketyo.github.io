2026-05-31 02:13:43 EDT

The health condition Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) has been renamed to Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome (PMOS) by the International PCOS Network, a change hoped to improve understanding, diagnosis, and treatment for over 170 million women globally. Patients like Rochelle Lewis, who experienced years of symptoms before diagnosis, believe the new name could facilitate quicker identification, better reflecting the condition's metabolic and endocrine nature beyond just ovarian issues. The hormonal disorder, which can affect fertility and increase the risk of type 2 diabetes, currently has no cure, with treatments focused on alleviating symptoms. Advocates hope the name change will also lead to more specialized referrals and community support.
