Active until his final years, Habermas consistently advocated for an open, rigorous democracy, supporting the right to asylum and a unified European Union. His groundbreaking work, "The Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere," explored the evolution of public opinion, and his theories, including "The Theory of Communicative Action," promoted language and communication as foundations for social progress.
