Space Telescope Advent Calendar Reveals Image of Galaxy Cluster Abell 209

2025-12-03 12:38:56 EST

The third image in the 2025 Space Telescope Advent Calendar features Galaxy Cluster Abell 209, located approximately 2.8 billion light-years from Earth. The stunning photograph captures over 100 distinct galaxies, which, despite appearing close together, are separated by millions of light-years. The immense combined mass of these galaxies creates a powerful gravitational field that warps spacetime, a phenomenon known as gravitational lensing. This effect visibly bends and magnifies the light from even more distant galaxies behind the cluster, causing them to appear as stretched or streaky arcs of light near the center of the image.
