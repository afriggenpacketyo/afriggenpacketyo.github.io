2026-02-14 05:37:00 EST

This article critically examines the scientific understanding of oxytocin, often dubbed the "love hormone," and the hype surrounding its ability to induce love. While early studies on prairie voles and human trust games suggested its role in bonding, later research revealed significant replication issues, with the effects often disappearing in larger studies. Neuroscientists emphasize that oxytocin is not inherently social and can amplify existing feelings, both positive and negative, including aggression and envy towards out-group members.
