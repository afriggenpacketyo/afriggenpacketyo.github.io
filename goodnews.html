Gray Wolf Spotted in Los Angeles County for the First Time in Over a Century

2026-02-10 18:43:21 EST

A three-year-old black coat female gray wolf, BEY03F, was confirmed in Los Angeles County on February 7, marking the first verified sighting in the region in over 100 years. Tracked by a GPS collar, the wolf traversed nearly the entire Sierra Nevada mountains, likely seeking a mate, representing the most southern verified record of a gray wolf in modern times. This event is a significant conservation milestone, as gray wolves were extirpated from the continental U.S. by hunters in the early 20th century and reintroduced to Yellowstone in the 1990s. While conservationists celebrate this expansion, advocates continue to seek stronger protections for the species nationwide.
