Archaeologists in England Discover Unusually Large 2,000-Year-Old Roman Shoes

2025-07-04 08:08:19 EDT

Archaeologists have unearthed a collection of unusually large, 2,000-year-old shoes at the Magna Roman Fort, part of the Hadrian's Wall World Heritage Site in northern England. Eight of the discovered leather shoes are over 30cm long, with one measuring nearly 13 inches, equivalent to a modern US size 15. This discovery is highly unusual, as such large sizes represent a much higher proportion of footwear at this site compared to other Roman settlements. Researchers are now investigating which Roman regiments were stationed there to understand who might have worn these exceptionally large shoes.

How the Continental Army Forged a Unified American Identity During the Revolution

2025-07-04 07:31:00 EDT

This historical essay argues that a cohesive American national identity was born not from shared culture, but from the shared struggle of the Continental Army during the Revolutionary War. In 1775, the colonies were disparate and competitive, but eight years of fighting together under George Washington transformed them. Soldiers from different regions endured common hardships, fostering new loyalties that transcended provincial boundaries. After the war, these veterans became central to nation-building, advocating for a strong federal government and establishing new national traditions, like celebrating Washington's birthday, to replace British ones.
