Palaeontologists Announce Discovery of New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species in Thailand

2026-07-10 17:30:57 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new plant-eating dinosaur species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This sauropod, estimated to be up to 20 meters long with an unusually long neck, is thought to have lived approximately 150 million years ago. The discovery, detailed in the scientific journal Nature, marks the first Mamenchisauridae family member found in Thailand, a group typically associated with China. Unique vertebral characteristics, including a Y-shaped bone arrangement and a distinctive air-cavity structure, differentiate this species, significantly expanding the understanding of dinosaur distribution and evolution in Southeast Asia.
