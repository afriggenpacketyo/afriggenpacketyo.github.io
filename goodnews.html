New technologies like machine perfusion have made this process more viable, but it creates a sensitive interval between the decision to withdraw life support and the actual declaration of death. This process requires careful ethical management to ensure donation decisions are decoupled from end-of-life care. The growing reliance on DCD highlights both the life-saving potential and the complex ethical considerations involved in modern organ transplantation.
