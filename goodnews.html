Adapting Infrastructure to Withstand Extreme Heat.

2025-06-06 07:06:00 EDT

Roads, railways, and bridges are increasingly vulnerable to extreme heat, leading to failures and disruptions in transportation. Rising temperatures cause asphalt to melt, rail tracks to buckle, and bridge joints to expand, necessitating adaptation strategies. Solutions include heat-reflective coatings for roads, lighter-colored pavements, and the use of more heat-resistant materials in railway construction. Bridges are being redesigned with better expansion joints and stronger bearings to withstand temperature fluctuations, ensuring the longevity and safety of critical infrastructure.
