Greenland's Size on Maps Can Be Deceiving, Especially Mercator Projection

2026-01-07 14:01:59 EST

The perceived size of Greenland on maps, particularly those using the Mercator projection, can be significantly misleading. This common projection distorts landmasses near the poles, making Greenland appear comparable in size to Africa, when in reality, Africa is about 14 times larger. While Mercator maps were useful for navigation, alternative projections like Robinson or Equal Earth offer more accurate representations of relative land sizes. The choice of map projection depends on its intended use, whether for measuring distances, directions, or areas, highlighting the importance of understanding cartographic distortions.
