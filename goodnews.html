NASA's Perseverance Rover Detects Evidence of Mini-Lightning Strikes on Mars

2025-11-26 11:00:00 EST

Scientists have solved a long-standing mystery by detecting direct evidence of lightning on Mars for the first time. The microphone on NASA's Perseverance rover accidentally recorded the distinct electrical interference and audible shockwaves of small electrical arcs, or mini-lightning, generated within Martian dust devils. These sparks, just a few centimeters long, were observed 55 times over two Martian years, typically associated with dust storms. This discovery confirms that atmospheric electrical activity occurs on the Red Planet and provides crucial data for assessing potential hazards for future robotic and human missions.
