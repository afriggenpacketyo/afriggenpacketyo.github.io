Skywatchers Anticipate Peak of Annual Geminids Meteor Shower

2025-12-13 15:14:27 EST

Skywatchers globally are eagerly anticipating the peak of the annual Geminids meteor shower, an impressive celestial event expected to illuminate the night sky with up to 120 meteors per hour. Distinct from most meteor showers, which originate from cometary debris, the Geminids are unique as they are caused by Asteroid 3200 Phaethon, a rocky metallic mass that scientists continue to investigate as a potential "dead comet" or "rock comet." Stargazers in the Northern Hemisphere will have the optimal viewing experience, with recommendations to find a dark location and allow eyes to adjust for about 30 minutes to fully appreciate the dazzling display until dawn. This yearly December phenomenon offers a remarkable opportunity for astronomical observation and appreciation.
