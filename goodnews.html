Wealthy Investors Utilize ETF Conversions to Defer Capital Gains Taxes

2025-08-21 17:06:58 EDT

A financial strategy known as a 351 conversion is gaining traction among wealthy investors as a way to defer capital gains taxes on highly appreciated assets. The process involves exchanging assets from a taxable account, such as a separately managed account (SMA), for shares in a new exchange-traded fund (ETF) before it launches. This allows the investor to avoid triggering a taxable event until they eventually sell the ETF shares. While the strategy offers significant tax advantages, it comes with strict diversification requirements and high investment minimums, often starting at $1 million. Financial advisors caution that investors must be comfortable holding the resulting ETF for the long term, as options to exit the position without incurring taxes are limited.
