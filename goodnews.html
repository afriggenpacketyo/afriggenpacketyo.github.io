The UK is experiencing significant diurnal temperature ranges, with warm spring days followed by chilly, frosty nights, a phenomenon common in early spring. High pressure systems bring clear skies, allowing for rapid daytime warming due to higher sun angles and efficient energy delivery. Conversely, the ground and lower atmosphere remain cool after winter, leading to quick heat loss overnight. This combination, along with dry air, widens the temperature gap, creating a "temperature rollercoaster" across the country.
