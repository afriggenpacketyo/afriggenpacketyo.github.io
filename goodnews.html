IRS Offers Tax Relief Programs for Those Facing Medical Crisis Debt

2026-03-27 14:27:20 EDT

The IRS provides several relief programs for taxpayers burdened by tax debt due to medical emergencies, which can severely disrupt finances through lost income and unexpected costs. Options include installment agreements for manageable payments over time, and Offers in Compromise, allowing eligible taxpayers to settle debt for less than the full amount, especially if earning potential is reduced long-term. Additionally, 'Currently Not Collectible' status can temporarily pause collection efforts during severe hardship, and penalty abatement may reduce or eliminate penalties for those unable to file or pay on time due to serious illness. Acting promptly is crucial to maximize relief options.

Austria Proposes Ban on Social Media for Children Under 14

2026-03-27 13:31:00 EDT

Austria's government has announced plans to ban social media use for children under 14, joining a growing global trend to protect young users from harmful online content. Vice Chancellor Andreas Babler stated the move addresses the risks of addiction, unrealistic beauty ideals, violence glorification, and misinformation. The proposed draft law, expected by June, will apply to platforms relying on addictive algorithms and generating profits from child users. The government also plans to introduce a new school subject to enhance media literacy among young people. Australia and France have already implemented similar age restrictions for social media access.
