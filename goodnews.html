October's Harvest Moon to Appear as First Supermoon of 2025

2025-10-07 03:55:18 EDT

Stargazers can observe the first of three consecutive supermoons of 2025 on Tuesday night with the rise of the full Harvest Moon. A supermoon occurs when a full moon coincides with its closest approach to Earth, known as the perigee, making it appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than other full moons. This year's Harvest Moon, the full moon nearest to the autumn equinox, is occurring unusually late in October. The phenomenon provides extra light around sunset for several evenings, a trait historically beneficial for farmers. Two more supermoons are expected to follow this year in November and December.
