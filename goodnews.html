NASA Targets April 1 for Artemis II Crewed Moon Mission Launch

2026-03-12 22:49:51 EDT

NASA is preparing for an April 1 launch of its Artemis II mission, aiming to send four astronauts on a nine-day journey around the Moon. This highly anticipated flight, the first piloted test of the Space Launch System rocket and Orion capsule, was previously delayed by hydrogen fuel leaks and upper-stage pressurization issues, which have now been addressed. The crew, including Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen, will fly within 4,100 miles of the Moon's surface, traveling farther from Earth than any previous human mission. Despite inherent risks, NASA managers express readiness, emphasizing this critical step toward returning astronauts to the lunar surface by 2028.
