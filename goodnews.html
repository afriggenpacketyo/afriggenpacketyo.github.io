UK Sky-Watchers Anticipate Rare Blood Moon Total Lunar Eclipse

2025-09-07 01:54:00 EDT

Sky-watchers across much of the UK are preparing for a stunning spectacle this evening as a total lunar eclipse, commonly known as a "Blood Moon," is set to turn the full Moon a deep red. This astronomical phenomenon occurs when the Earth passes directly between the Sun and Moon, blocking direct sunlight and casting a shadow, while red light is refracted through Earth's atmosphere. While the full eclipse begins at 18:30, visibility in the UK will vary based on moonrise times, with eastern parts enjoying the longest view. Dr. Edward Bloomer of the Royal Observatory Greenwich emphasizes that this event offers a unique and tangible demonstration of the solar system's mechanics.
