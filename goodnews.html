South African Scientists Use Radioactive Isotopes to Deter Rhino Poaching

2025-08-01 01:20:58 EDT

In an innovative effort to combat rhino poaching, scientists in South Africa have launched the Rhisotope Project, which involves injecting rhino horns with harmless radioactive isotopes. The initiative, a collaboration between the University of the Witwatersrand and conservationists, aims to make the horns easily detectable by radiation scanners at international borders and airports. This would help identify and intercept smuggled horns, leading to the arrest of traffickers. After successful trials confirmed the process is safe for the animals, the first group of rhinos received the injections in a wider rollout to protect the country's declining population.
