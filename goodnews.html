Tiny Zooplankton Play a Major Role in Fighting Climate Change, Research Reveals

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research has revealed the significant role of tiny marine animals called zooplankton in mitigating global warming through a massive annual migration in the Southern Ocean. These creatures gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface, grow fat, and then sink hundreds of meters into the deep ocean to burn that fat over winter. This process effectively locks away vast amounts of carbon, equivalent to the annual emissions of about 55 million cars, preventing it from re-entering the atmosphere. Scientists call these overlooked animals "unsung heroes" but warn that their populations are facing growing threats.
