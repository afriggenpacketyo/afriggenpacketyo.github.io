Research Links Moderate Weightlifting to Increased Longevity

2026-06-14 09:00:27 EDT

A new study published in the British Journal of Sports Medicine suggests that engaging in resistance training for 90 to 119 minutes per week may be associated with a longer life. The research, which followed over 147,000 U.S. adults for up to 30 years, found a 13% lower risk of all-cause death and a 19% lower risk of death from heart disease in this group. The article notes this outcome persisted even after adjusting for other factors like age and diet, with the lowest overall death risk found in those combining high aerobic activity with moderate to high resistance training. Even smaller amounts of resistance training, 30 to 59 minutes per week, were associated with a 12% decreased risk of cancer death.
