Hardy was among the first Black military pilots in U.S. history and flew 21 combat missions over Europe during World War II as a P-51 "Red Tail" fighter pilot. His distinguished military career also included flying combat missions in both the Korean and Vietnam Wars. After retiring from the Air Force, Hardy became a dedicated advocate for the legacy of the Tuskegee Airmen, who collectively received the Congressional Gold Medal in 2007 for their courage and patriotism in the face of racial discrimination.
