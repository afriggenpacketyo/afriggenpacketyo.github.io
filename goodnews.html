The Unexpected Origin of Barbie: Inspired by a German Comic Strip Character

2025-08-13 08:30:00 EDT

The iconic Barbie doll was inspired by Lilli, a sensual German comic strip character created in 1952 for the Bild tabloid, initially intended for adult male readers. Lilli, described as a "sassy secretary" but interpreted by some as a high-class call girl, became a popular plastic doll in 1955, sold in tobacco shops. Ruth Handler, co-founder of Mattel, discovered Lilli in Switzerland and subsequently launched Barbie in 1959, modeled after Lilli and named after Handler's daughter. Mattel later acquired the rights to Lilli, ceasing its production, while Barbie went on to become a global phenomenon, inspiring one of the highest-grossing films.
