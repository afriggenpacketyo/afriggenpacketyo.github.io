California to Start Ticketing Driverless Cars for Traffic Violations

2026-05-02 11:31:40 EDT

California's Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) has announced new regulations, effective July 1, that will allow police to ticket driverless cars for traffic violations. These rules stem from a 2024 law designed to implement deeper regulation on autonomous vehicle (AV) technology. Under the updated guidelines, police can now issue "notices of AV noncompliance" directly to the car's manufacturer. The regulations also mandate that AV companies respond to calls from police and other emergency officials within 30 seconds, and they will face penalties if their vehicles enter active emergency zones, addressing previous incidents where law enforcement was unable to cite driverless vehicles lacking human operators.
