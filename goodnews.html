Daylight Saving Time 2026 Begins, Shifting Clocks Forward

2026-03-08 06:00:04 EDT

Daylight Saving Time for 2026 commenced early Sunday, March 8th, at 2 a.m. local time, causing most Americans to lose an hour of sleep as clocks "sprang forward." This annual tradition, in effect since the 1960s, moves an hour of daylight from morning to evening, with sunrise and sunset occurring later. While initially adopted to conserve fuel during World Wars, studies in 1974 and 2007 found minimal energy benefits. The time shift has also been linked to negative health effects. Only Hawaii and Arizona, along with several US territories, do not observe Daylight Saving Time, which will conclude on November 1st.
