This pest's larvae feed on the soft tissues of palm trees, digging tunnels and causing severe damage that often leads to the tree's death. The infestation jeopardizes native trees, which hold significant symbolic value in various Uruguayan locations. Scientists have been tasked with developing an early detection strategy, highlighting the urgency and the need for international cooperation in addressing this environmental threat.
