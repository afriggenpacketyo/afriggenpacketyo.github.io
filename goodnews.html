The United States consistently has the highest prescription drug prices in the world, a reality shaped by a unique and complex healthcare system. Unlike countries with national health insurance that negotiate prices for their entire population, the U.S. market is fragmented among Medicare, Medicaid, and numerous private insurers, weakening its collective bargaining power. Furthermore, U.S. policy prioritizes broad access to all available drugs, limiting the ability to refuse coverage for a drug if a manufacturer's price is deemed too high. This system allows pharmaceutical companies to set prices much higher than in other developed nations.
