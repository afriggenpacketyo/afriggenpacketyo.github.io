The Evolution and Misuse of the Business Buzzword "Synergy"

2026-01-14 05:00:00 EST

The article delves into the fascinating etymology and contemporary misuse of "synergy," a term often derided as quintessential business jargon. Originating from Greek words meaning "together" and "work," its earliest applications were in religious discourse during the 17th century, discussing the collaboration of divine grace and human will. The word later found use in 19th-century medicine and mid-20th-century psychology. However, "synergy" truly permeated the lexicon of corporate America in the 1960s, notably through Igor Ansoff's "Corporate Strategy," becoming an overused buzzword in the 80s and 90s. Its frequent, often vague, deployment in business has, ironically, diluted its original meaning of combined action yielding a greater effect.

US Civil Rights Pioneer Claudette Colvin Dies at 86

2026-01-14 01:29:00 EST

Claudette Colvin, a courageous and often overlooked pioneer of the U.S. civil rights movement, has passed away at the age of 86. At just 15 years old, in March 1955, she defiantly refused to surrender her seat to a white woman on a segregated bus in Montgomery, Alabama, an act that occurred nine months before Rosa Parks' more famous protest. Colvin's arrest led her to become a key plaintiff in the landmark lawsuit that successfully challenged bus segregation laws nationwide. Despite facing ostracism from the movement for personal reasons, her significant bravery eventually gained wider recognition, culminating in the expungement of her 1955 arrest record in 2021, solidifying her legacy as a vital figure in American history.
