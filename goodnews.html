Mysterious Gelatinous Blobs in US Waterways Identified as Harmless Bryozoan Colonies

2025-08-28 16:44:35 EDT

Large, gelatinous masses resembling 'mutant brains' that have been spotted in U.S. lakes and rivers have been identified by wildlife officials as magnificent bryozoans, which are harmless. These blobs are not single organisms but colonies composed of thousands of tiny, filter-feeding invertebrates called zooids that work together. According to the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, these ancient creatures have existed for over 470 million years and play a beneficial role in freshwater ecosystems by filtering plankton and cleaning the water. Despite their strange appearance, they pose no threat and are a natural part of the aquatic environment.
