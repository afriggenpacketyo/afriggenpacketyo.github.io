2026-07-15 00:00:01 EDT

Not all debt is inherited; very few become the financial responsibility of heirs. Key exceptions include co-signed loans, joint accounts, and mortgages attached to inherited property, where the survivor may assume full responsibility. The article highlights that even if an estate is insolvent, beneficiaries are generally not personally liable for unpaid debts.
