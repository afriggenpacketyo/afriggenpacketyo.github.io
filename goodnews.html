Study Suggests Frequent Interaction with Annoying People May Accelerate Biological Aging

2026-03-13 05:57:00 EDT

A new study published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences suggests that regularly interacting with "hasslers"—people who cause stress—may accelerate biological aging. Researchers found a cumulative effect, with each additional hassler associated with an average 1.5% increase in the aging process, equivalent to almost two extra months of biological aging over a decade. This stress contributes to higher risks for cardiovascular disease and reduced immune function. Women were more likely to report having hasslers, particularly among colleagues or family, though annoying spouses showed no significant association with accelerated aging.
