Report Highlights High Public Costs of Urban Sprawl in U.S. Cities

2026-06-03 08:30:00 EDT

A new report by the Pew Charitable Trusts, World Resources Institute, and ECOnorthwest suggests that urban sprawl significantly increases public infrastructure costs for American cities. The research indicates that building homes in existing communities (infill development) requires approximately $21,000 less in upfront infrastructure expenses per home than building at the urban fringe. Additionally, infill development results in about 50% lower ongoing maintenance costs and generates 13% more property taxes per acre. This highlights a fiscal incentive for cities to prioritize denser, infill housing to improve solvency and potentially reduce property taxes, despite existing regulatory and cultural barriers.

Consistent Strength Training Linked to Reduced Risk of Early Death

2026-06-03 07:52:33 EDT

New research indicates that engaging in 90 minutes to two hours of weight training per week can significantly reduce the risk of premature death. Analyzing data from decades-long studies involving nearly 150,000 individuals, researchers found a 13% lower risk of early death from any cause and a 27% reduction for neurological diseases like dementia. The study, published in the British Journal of Sports Medicine, also highlighted that the greatest benefits were observed in those combining high levels of aerobic exercise with strength training. However, exceeding two hours of strength training weekly did not yield additional benefits, providing clear guidelines for health improvement.
