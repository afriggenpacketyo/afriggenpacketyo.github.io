Remarkable Fish Discovered Climbing African Waterfall

2026-04-05 07:00:00 EDT

Researchers have documented a species of fish, the shellear (Parakneria thysi), capable of climbing a 50-foot waterfall in the Democratic Republic of Congo. These small fish, about the size of a french fry, use their rear pelvic fins for support and their front pectoral fins, equipped with tiny hooks, to grip the rock face. They ascend by making rapid lateral undulations, pausing to rest on flat surfaces during their arduous, approximately ten-hour journey. This behavior, previously unobserved in Africa, is believed to be part of an upstream migration, highlighting the importance of protecting these unique aquatic habitats from potential disruption by human activities like dam construction. The discovery underscores the incredible adaptability and diversity of fish species.
