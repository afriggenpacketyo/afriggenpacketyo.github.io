Golden eagles are venturing back into England from southern Scotland after years of absence, raising hopes they might nest. The resurgence is due to the South of Scotland Golden Eagle Project (SSGEP), which translocated 28 juveniles and 15 sub-adult golden eagles from the Scottish Highlands. These eagles have been spotted in Northumberland, the North Pennines, the Lake District, and as far as Lancashire and Yorkshire. Golden eagles were wiped out in England and Wales by the mid-19th Century, mainly due to persecution. The SSGEP is tracking the birds' movements and hopes they will establish a population in England.
