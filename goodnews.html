Japan Significantly Increases Foreigner Visa Fees for the First Time in Decades

2026-06-21 23:45:51 EDT

Japan has enacted a fivefold increase in visa fees for foreigners, marking the first adjustment since 1978, effective July 1. Single-entry visas will rise from 3,000 yen to 15,000 yen, and multi-entry visas to 30,000 yen. Foreign Minister Toshimitsu Motegi attributed the hike to inflation and exchange rate fluctuations, anticipating no immediate impact on inbound tourism, which saw a record 42.7 million visitors last year. The revisions also include substantial increases for permanent residency and status change applications, aiming to align Japan's fees with other G7 economies.
