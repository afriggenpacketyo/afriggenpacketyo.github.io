South African Scientists Use Radioactive Isotopes in Rhino Horns to Deter Poaching

2025-08-01 01:20:58 EDT

A new anti-poaching initiative in South Africa, known as the Rhisotope Project, is underway to combat the illegal rhino horn trade by making the horns radioactive. Scientists from the University of the Witwatersrand are injecting small, harmless amounts of radioactive material into rhino horns, which can be detected by radiation sensors at international borders and airports. This innovative method aims to disrupt trafficking networks by making the horns easier to trace and riskier to smuggle. The project hopes to protect the country's large but dwindling rhino population, which is heavily targeted by poachers for the black market.
