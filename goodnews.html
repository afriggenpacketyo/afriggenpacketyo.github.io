Chinese Study Suggests Fathers Can Pass Exercise Benefits to Offspring

2025-10-12 23:00:52 EDT

A Chinese study conducted on mice suggests that fathers can pass on the benefits of exercise to their offspring, including improved muscle mass, lower body fat, and stronger bones. Researchers from Nanjing University and Nanjing Medical University found that offspring sired by exercise-trained fathers displayed "intrinsic exercise adaptations and improved metabolic parameters" compared to those from sedentary fathers. Conversely, the offspring of inactive mice performed worse in running and strength tests. The team also discovered that injecting sperm microRNAs from exercised mice into normal fertilized eggs reproduced these beneficial traits in the offspring, indicating a genetic mechanism. This research highlights paternal exercise as a "cost-effective route to improve offspring health."
