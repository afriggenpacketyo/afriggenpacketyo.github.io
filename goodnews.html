Biologists Intensify Efforts to Save Bat Populations from Devastating White-Nose Syndrome

2025-11-15 21:59:23 EST

Biologists in North Carolina are urgently working to protect bat populations, especially tricolored bats, from the devastating white-nose syndrome. This deadly fungal disease causes bats to prematurely awaken from hibernation due to severe itching, leading them to deplete vital fat reserves in a fruitless search for food and ultimately starve. The dwindling bat numbers are a significant ecological concern, as these creatures play a crucial role in pest control by eating crop-damaging insects, reducing the need for pesticides. An innovative strategy involves using bug-attracting lights near hibernating sites to help bats accumulate enough fat, offering a hopeful intervention against the disease's destructive impact.
