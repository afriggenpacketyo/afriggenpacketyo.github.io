South African Scientists Use Radioactive Isotopes to Combat Rhino Poaching

2025-08-01 01:20:58 EDT

A new anti-poaching initiative in South Africa, named the Rhisotope Project, involves injecting rhino horns with harmless radioactive isotopes. This innovative technique, developed by the University of the Witwatersrand and its partners, makes the horns detectable by radiation scanners at international borders and airports. The goal is to devalue the horns on the black market and facilitate the arrest of traffickers. After successful trials confirmed the method is safe for the animals, the project officially launched with the injection of five rhinos, aiming to protect the nation's declining population.
