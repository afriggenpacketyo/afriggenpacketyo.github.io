Reverse Mortgages Generally Do Not Affect Social Security or Medicare Benefits

2025-08-28 14:25:15 EDT

For most retirees, a reverse mortgage typically does not impact Social Security retirement benefits or Medicare eligibility, as these are based on earnings history and not assets. This article clarifies that Social Security payments remain unchanged, and Medicare coverage is unaffected regardless of home equity use. However, it warns that need-based programs like Supplemental Security Income (SSI) and Medicaid can be affected. Large lump-sum payouts from a reverse mortgage could push recipients over asset limits for SSI, while unused funds might impact Medicaid eligibility. Consulting with a financial advisor and a HUD-approved housing counselor is crucial to navigate these nuances and avoid unintended consequences. (not investment advice)
