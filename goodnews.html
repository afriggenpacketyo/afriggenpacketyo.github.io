A new study published in the journal Cell suggests that the modern potato evolved from a tomato ancestor approximately 9 million years ago through a hybridization event. Researchers analyzed hundreds of genomes from wild and cultivated potatoes to solve the long-standing puzzle of the vegetable's origin. The findings indicate that an ancient tomato provided a key gene for forming tubers, which combined with a gene from a wild, non-tuber-producing potato relative from Chile. This genetic mixing allowed the potato to develop its characteristic starch-rich tubers, demonstrating how interbreeding between species can spark the evolution of entirely new traits and organisms.
