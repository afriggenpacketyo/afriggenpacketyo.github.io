2025-10-28 15:14:54 EDT

Debt cancellation, also known as debt forgiveness, offers a way for individuals to eliminate some or all of their outstanding debt, but eligibility is highly specific. This form of relief is not granted arbitrarily and typically requires meeting strict criteria through established programs or legal processes. For instance, federal student loan forgiveness may depend on public service employment, while medical debt cancellation often requires demonstrating significant financial hardship to a hospital's charity care program. Other avenues include debt settlement for credit cards or filing for bankruptcy, which can discharge many unsecured debts but has its own set of qualifications and consequences.
