Artemis II Astronauts Successfully Depart Earth Orbit Towards the Moon

2026-04-03 00:27:18 EDT

The Artemis II mission crew, including Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen, successfully departed Earth's orbit on Thursday following a critical six-minute engine firing. This "trans-lunar injection" burn accelerated their Orion capsule to 24,500 mph, placing them on a free-return trajectory that will carry them around the far side of the moon and back to Earth without further major rocket firings. This historic journey marks the first time humans have traveled beyond low-Earth orbit since 1972, aiming to test the Orion spacecraft's capabilities and flight control protocols for upcoming moon landing missions planned for 2028. The crew expressed immense awe at the Earth's view, highlighting the power of human perseverance and collective aspirations for space exploration as they pursue this record-breaking distance from our planet.
