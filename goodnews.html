Michigan's Black Beach Sand Explained as Natural Mineral Phenomenon

2025-09-21 04:01:31 EDT

Streaks of black sand found on Michigan's beaches, particularly at Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore, are a natural occurrence and not pollution. The dark color comes from magnetite, an iron-based mineral, which is heavier than the surrounding quartz sand and gets concentrated by wave action. The National Park Service recently clarified this phenomenon to reassure beachgoers. While most black sand in the state is natural, some areas have black "stamp sands" which are remnants from historical copper mining operations.
