Businesses Employ "Tariff Engineering" to Avoid Higher Duties

2025-06-18 03:56:58 EDT

With the imposition of tariffs by the Trump administration, businesses are increasingly using "tariff engineering" to lower their duty payments. This involves altering an item's materials or dimensions to fit into a different harmonized system code with a lower tariff rate. While not illegal, this practice requires strategic design choices to create products that qualify for different tariff classifications. Companies in sectors like consumer goods and garments can relatively easily implement tariff engineering, while more heavily regulated sectors face greater challenges due to extensive validation and approval requirements.
