HELOC vs. Reverse Mortgage: Expert Advice for Seniors

2025-05-20 10:14:31 EDT

Senior homeowners seeking to tap into their home equity have two primary options: a home equity line of credit (HELOC) or a reverse mortgage. HELOCs offer a flexible credit line with lower interest rates than credit cards or personal loans, suitable for short-term needs like home improvements or debt consolidation. Reverse mortgages, available to homeowners 62 and older, allow seniors to convert home equity into cash without monthly payments, ideal for those with limited income. While HELOCs require repayment, reverse mortgages are repaid when the homeowner dies, moves, or sells the home, but they accrue interest and fees over time. Experts recommend considering individual financial situations and consulting professionals to determine the best option.
