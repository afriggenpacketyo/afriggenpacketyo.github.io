House Passes Aviation Safety Bill Following Deadly Midair Collision Near D.C.

2026-04-14 20:03:11 EDT

The House of Representatives passed the ALERT Act, an aviation safety bill, in a 396-10 vote, responding to the January 2025 midair collision that killed 67 people. The bill mandates that all aircraft, including military planes, operating near busy airports install collision-avoidance technology (ADS-B In) by 2031, with exceptions for certain military aircraft. This legislation aims to prevent future accidents by improving aircraft location data sharing and air traffic control processes. While a step forward, victims' families and some senators argue the bill doesn't go far enough, as the technology is not yet market-ready and may face implementation delays.
