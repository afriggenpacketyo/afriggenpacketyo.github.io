The Unexpected Evolution of Botox from Toxin to Medical and Cosmetic Treatment

2026-03-18 06:00:00 EDT

Originating from the deadly botulinum toxin responsible for 19th-century botulism outbreaks in Germany, Botox has undergone a remarkable transformation from a terrifying poison to a versatile medical and cosmetic treatment. Physician Justinus Kerner first speculated on its potential in small doses. Decades later, its paralytic properties were harnessed, notably by Jean Carruthers, who observed its wrinkle-smoothing effect during eye spasm treatments. This discovery, alongside her dermatologist husband Alastair, propelled Botox into mainstream use. Today, it's approved for nine medical conditions and used off-label for many more, extending its utility across diverse medical fields beyond just aesthetics.
