Employer rights and limitations regarding wage garnishment clarified

2026-06-04 11:51:40 EDT

This article clarifies what employers can and cannot do when an employee's wages are garnished. Employers are legally obligated to comply with valid garnishment orders, typically withholding a maximum of 25% of disposable earnings or a specified amount based on minimum wage, whichever is less. They may also charge a small administrative fee. Crucially, federal law prohibits employers from firing an employee solely due to a single wage garnishment. However, this protection may not extend to multiple garnishments. Employers cannot withhold more than the legal limit or continue garnishing wages after the order has ended. Understanding these rules is vital for both employers and employees to ensure compliance and protect income.
