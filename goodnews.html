Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

Ireland's Armagh Observatory is marking a major scientific milestone: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation. This unbroken sequence of handwritten data, which began on July 14, 1795, is the longest of its kind in the UK and Ireland. The tradition continues today, with a human observer manually recording temperature and other data, a practice largely replaced by automated stations elsewhere. This invaluable long-term record has chronicled significant weather events and contributed to the development of meteorology, including the invention of the four-cup anemometer by a former director.
