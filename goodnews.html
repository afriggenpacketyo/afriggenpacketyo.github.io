Understanding Credit Card Grace Periods and Their Importance

The article explains the concept of a credit card grace period, which is the time between the end of a billing cycle and the payment due date when no interest is charged on new purchases, provided the full statement balance is paid by the due date. The grace period allows cardholders to use credit cards without incurring interest charges, effectively providing a temporary, interest-free loan. However, carrying a balance from month to month typically results in the loss of the grace period on new purchases, causing interest to accrue immediately. To maximize the benefits of the grace period, the article advises paying the full statement balance by the due date each month, automating payments, tracking billing cycles, and avoiding cash advances.
