Hiker Discovers Rare 1,500-Year-Old Gold Sword Fitting in Norway

2026-05-14 08:00:56 EDT

An anonymous hiker in Norway unearthed a rare 1,500-year-old gold sword fitting from the sixth century's Migration Period, hidden beneath a storm-felled tree in the Austrått district of Sandnes. This richly decorated artifact, measuring about six centimeters wide, is the first such discovery in the Rogaland region and one of only 17 found in Northern Europe. Archaeologists believe it belonged to a chieftain, emphasizing his position and power, and was likely buried in a rock crevice as an offering to the gods during a period of crop failures and crisis. The spectacular find is slated for display at the Museum of Archaeology at the University of Stavanger, providing new insights into the elite who ruled the area during that turbulent historical period.
