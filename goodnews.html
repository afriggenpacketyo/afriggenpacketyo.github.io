Understanding Required Minimum Distributions from Retirement Accounts

2026-05-06 10:32:56 EDT

Retirees with traditional IRAs or 401(k)s must begin taking Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) at age 73, with amounts determined by account balance and IRS life expectancy factors. For a $250,000 account, RMDs can range from approximately $9,400 at age 73 to over $12,300 by age 80, increasing annually regardless of market conditions. These distributions are taxed as ordinary income, potentially pushing retirees into higher tax brackets, and failing to take them incurs a significant penalty.
