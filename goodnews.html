2025-07-08 10:18:00 EDT

A telescope in Chile has spotted the third known interstellar object to enter our solar system, a comet designated 3I/ATLAS. Currently about 670 million kilometers from the sun, the comet poses no threat to Earth and will make its closest approach in October at a distance of 240 million kilometers. Scientists are studying its trajectory and composition, noting it appears larger than the previous two interstellar visitors, 'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Such objects are considered pristine specimens from other planetary systems, offering a unique opportunity to study materials from beyond our solar system.
