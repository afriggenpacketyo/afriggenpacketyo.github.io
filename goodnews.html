Scientists discover protein that synthesizes DNA without a template, challenging biological dogma

2026-06-01 01:14:00 EDT

Researchers have identified a novel protein in bacteria, named Drt3b, that can synthesize DNA without relying on an existing genetic template. This groundbreaking discovery challenges the long-held biological principle that DNA synthesis always requires a pre-existing strand to copy from. The Drt3b protein uses its own structure as a mold to assemble DNA building blocks, a mechanism previously thought impossible for producing long, sequence-specific DNA strands. While the exact function of this newly synthesized DNA is still under investigation, the finding opens new avenues for understanding microbial defense systems and potentially for developing novel tools for custom DNA synthesis. The discovery has sparked debate about its implications for the central dogma of biology.
