Uruguay Becomes First Latin American Nation to Legalize Euthanasia

2025-10-16 02:38:00 EDT

Uruguay has made history by becoming the first country in Latin America to legalize euthanasia, following its Senate's approval of the "Dignified Death" bill with a significant majority. The new law permits mentally sound adult Uruguayan citizens who are in the terminal stage of an irreversible disease to choose euthanasia, which must be performed by a healthcare professional. It explicitly excludes assisted suicide, where a patient self-administers a lethal dose of medication. This legislative milestone reinforces Uruguay's long history of socially liberal laws in a predominantly Catholic region, where public support for legal euthanasia is robust, with over 60% in favor.
