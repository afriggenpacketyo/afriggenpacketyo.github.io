Trade with China Did Not Wreck the U.S. Economy

2025-06-05 06:00:00 EDT

A comprehensive review challenges the narrative that trade with China significantly damaged American manufacturing. The "China shock" theory, which suggests that Chinese imports caused substantial job losses, has been exaggerated, and populist narratives blaming this trade for U.S. economic decline are not supported by rigorous evidence. While some areas were negatively affected, the overall impact on manufacturing employment was modest, and job losses were often offset by gains in other sectors. The decline in U.S. manufacturing employment began decades before China's WTO entry, primarily due to technological advances and shifting consumer demand, and protectionist policies based on a misdiagnosed problem may do more harm than good.
