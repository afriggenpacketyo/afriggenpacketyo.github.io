Netherlands Agrees to Repatriate 'Java Man' Fossils to Indonesia

2025-09-26 13:38:00 EDT

The Dutch government has announced it will return over 28,000 fossils, including parts of the historic "Java Man" collection, to Indonesia. These fossils, discovered in 1891 by Dutch anthropologist Eugene Dubois during the colonial era, are among the first known remains of Homo erectus. The decision follows a recommendation from a Dutch committee which concluded the fossils were likely removed against the will of the local population. This repatriation is part of a broader effort by the Netherlands to reckon with its colonial past and return culturally significant artifacts.
