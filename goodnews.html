El Niño Expected to Influence 2026 Hurricane Seasons in Atlantic and Pacific

2026-05-15 09:55:00 EDT

The transition into an El Niño climate pattern is expected to significantly impact the 2026 hurricane seasons. In the Eastern Pacific, warmer ocean waters fueled by El Niño create ideal conditions for increased hurricane activity. Conversely, El Niño tends to suppress hurricane development in the Atlantic basin due to cooler waters and increased vertical wind shear. While the Atlantic season may statistically see fewer systems, it does not preclude the formation of storms. This contrasts with recent years under La Niña, which correlated with above-average Atlantic hurricane seasons.
