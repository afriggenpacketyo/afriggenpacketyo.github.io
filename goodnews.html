2025-07-02 13:05:39 EDT

The U.S. government is reviving a decades-old strategy to combat the New World screwworm fly, a pest whose flesh-eating larvae can devastate livestock and wildlife. The plan involves breeding billions of male flies, sterilizing them with radiation, and releasing them from aircraft over Mexico and southern Texas. These sterile males then mate with wild females, whose eggs fail to hatch, causing the population to collapse over time. This biological control method is considered more effective and environmentally friendly than pesticides and is being ramped up in response to the pest's recent reappearance in southern Mexico.
