China's Economic Leapfrog Explained by 'Privilege of Historic Backwardness'

2026-07-07 21:30:07 EDT

The article explores how China's rapid economic and technological advancement can be understood through Leon Trotsky's theory of "the privilege of historic backwardness." This concept suggests that less developed countries can assimilate innovations from advanced nations, skipping intermediate stages and achieving a complex, combined development. Examples include African economies leapfrogging to 5G and e-commerce. The theory posits that China's ability to adopt ready-made frameworks, rather than slavishly reproducing past stages, has been crucial to its post-Mao era growth, despite historical challenges like famine and the Cultural Revolution. The degree of this leapfrogging is determined by a country's economic and cultural capacities.
