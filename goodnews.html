Economic analysis suggests 'privilege of historic backwardness' explains China's rapid growth

2026-07-07 21:30:07 EDT

An opinion piece suggests that Leon Trotsky's theory of "the privilege of historic backwardness" helps explain China's rapid economic and technological advancement. The article posits that China, by not following the exact developmental path of advanced nations, has been able to "skip over intermediate steps" and assimilate modern frameworks more efficiently. This concept is illustrated by fast-growing African economies adopting 5G and e-commerce without earlier stages of development. The author argues that despite the destruction of the Mao era, modern frameworks were established that facilitated China's subsequent leapfrog into the 21st century, challenging predictions of collapse and highlighting the complex, combined nature of its development.
