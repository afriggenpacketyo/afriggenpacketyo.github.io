The discovery includes areas for flax processing and more than 80 pit houses used as workshops, revealing the sophisticated nature of Viking society. This site, located near the historical center of Aarhus, suggests a well-structured society with extensive international trade networks, challenging the simplistic view of Vikings as merely barbaric raiders and highlighting their economic prowess.
