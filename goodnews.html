An exhibition titled "Wohnkomplex: Art and Life in Plattenbau" in Potsdam is currently re-evaluating the complex cultural legacy of East Germany's distinctive prefabricated concrete slab buildings. These "Plattenbau" structures were initially conceived as a socialist utopia, providing modern housing and integrated community facilities to address severe post-World War II shortages. However, following German reunification, these residential complexes became symbols of painful societal transformation, experiencing rising unemployment and extremism in the urban clusters designed for working-class families.
