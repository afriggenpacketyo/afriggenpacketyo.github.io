Dirty Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly, Experts Warn

2025-06-10 14:34:21 EDT

Wildlife experts are warning that dirty hummingbird feeders can be harmful or even deadly to the birds. Sugar water left out too long can spoil and grow harmful microbes, leading to infections like candida, which causes tongue swelling and starvation. While not all microbes are dangerous, it's common for individual birds to get sick from dirty feeders. To prevent this, feeders should be cleaned every two to three days in spring and fall, and daily or every other day in summer. The solution is to mix one part refined white sugar with four parts water, avoid food coloring, and use purified water.
