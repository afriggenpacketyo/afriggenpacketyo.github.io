The phenomenon occurs when the Moon passes directly through the Earth's darkest shadow, or umbra, causing it to take on a distinct reddish hue. This striking visual effect is the result of sunlight being filtered and refracted through Earth's atmosphere before reaching the lunar surface. The full eclipse was visible in its entirety from numerous countries, captivating observers and photographers who captured images of the captivating display.
