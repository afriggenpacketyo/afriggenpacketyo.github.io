Daylight Saving Time to End for 2025 This Weekend

2025-11-01 06:56:45 EDT

Daylight saving time for 2025 will conclude on Sunday, November 2, at 2 a.m., at which point clocks will "fall back" one hour to 1 a.m. This change means most Americans will gain an extra hour of sleep, but it will also result in earlier sunrises and sunsets. The practice, legally established by the Uniform Time Act of 1966, is not observed in Arizona, Hawaii, and several U.S. territories. There is ongoing debate about eliminating the biannual time change, with a 2022 Senate bill to make daylight saving time permanent having stalled in the House, and President Trump previously expressing a desire to end the practice.
