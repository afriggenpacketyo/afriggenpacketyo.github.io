A new clinical trial published in The New England Journal of Medicine reveals that semaglutide injections can reverse life-threatening cases of metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH). The study, involving 800 participants across 37 countries, showed that 62.9% of those treated with semaglutide experienced less inflammation and fat accumulation in their livers, compared to 34.3% in the placebo group. Semaglutide, traditionally used for type 2 diabetes, also led to improvements in liver fibrosis and weight loss.
