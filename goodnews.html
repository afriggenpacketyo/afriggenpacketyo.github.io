An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Function

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial body of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between member states. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, the court's jurisdiction applies when states agree to submit to its authority, either through a blanket declaration or on a case-by-case basis. It also hears cases based on specific treaties, such as the Genocide Convention, which has been invoked in recent cases involving Ukraine, Myanmar, and Israel. However, the ICJ's primary limitation is its lack of an enforcement mechanism, meaning its binding rulings depend on the cooperation of the involved nations.
