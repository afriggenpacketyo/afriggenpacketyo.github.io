A leading figure of the Frankfurt School, Habermas remained active in public discourse until his final years, advocating for asylum rights and a unified European Union against rising nationalism. His seminal work, "The Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere," explored the evolution of public opinion in democratic societies. Habermas's theories promoted open debate and reasoned consensus as foundations for social progress, profoundly influencing Germany's post-war self-image and inspiring generations of thinkers and activists.
