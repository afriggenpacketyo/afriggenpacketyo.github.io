New York City to Use Ranked Choice Voting in Democratic Mayoral Primary

New York City is implementing ranked choice voting in its upcoming Democratic mayoral primary election, a system where voters rank multiple candidates in order of preference. This system aims to ensure that even if a voter's top choice doesn't win, their other rankings still contribute to the final outcome. If no candidate secures a majority of first-choice votes, the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed to the voters' second choices, continuing until a winner emerges. While this system can lead to broader support for the elected candidate, it also presents challenges in terms of transparency and predictability, potentially leading to questions about the accuracy of the results.
