Experts clarify misconceptions about inherited debt

2026-07-15 00:00:01 EDT

Experts are clarifying common misconceptions about inherited debt, emphasizing that not all debts are passed on to heirs. Generally, debts are paid from the deceased's estate, not directly by beneficiaries, unless they co-signed, were joint account holders, or live in community property states with spousal debts. Mortgages on inherited property require continued payments or sale to cover the loan. While co-signers are typically responsible, they can negotiate terms or sell assets. If an estate is insolvent, debts may go unpaid, and beneficiaries are not obligated to cover them. Seeking professional advice is crucial for navigating these complex situations.
