Study Suggests Elevated Body Temperature Alone Can Help Combat Viral Infections

2025-11-27 14:00:00 EST

New research published in the journal Science provides evidence that fever can directly combat certain viruses, independent of the broader immune response. In a study using mice, scientists created a heat-tolerant version of a human influenza virus and found that elevated ambient temperatures helped the mice fight off the normal strain but not the modified one. This suggests that the heat itself, not just the immune system activity that causes it, plays a crucial role in defense against temperature-sensitive pathogens. The findings revive ancient theories about the benefits of fever and raise questions about the common practice of immediately treating low-grade fevers with medication.

Using advanced drone mapping and microbotanical analysis of sediment, his team found that the holes were arranged in patterns mirroring Incan counting devices and contained traces of crops and materials used for packaging goods.
