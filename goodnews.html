Civil Rights Icon Bernard LaFayette, Architect of Selma Voter Registration, Dies at 85

2026-03-05 20:09:22 EST

Bernard LaFayette, a revered civil rights leader instrumental in the Selma voter registration campaign that culminated in the landmark 1965 Voting Rights Act, has passed away at the age of 85. As a co-founder of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC) and director of the Alabama voter registration campaign, LaFayette courageously laid the groundwork for change in Selma, enduring beatings as a Freedom Rider and facing an assassination attempt. He later collaborated with Martin Luther King Jr. on the Poor People's Campaign and dedicated his life to advancing nonviolence globally. His legacy is one of profound impact on social justice and equality.
