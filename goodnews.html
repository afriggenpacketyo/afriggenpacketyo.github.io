"Gus" the T. rex Skeleton Sells for Record $50.1 Million at New York Auction

2026-07-14 12:36:26 EDT

Standing 12.5 feet tall and 38 feet long, Gus is among the largest and most complete T. rex skeletons ever discovered, approximately 61% complete with an exceptionally preserved skull. The fossil, dating back 67 million years, was unearthed in South Dakota's renowned Hell Creek Formation. This historic sale underscores a growing trend of ultra-rich collectors acquiring significant paleontological finds, raising questions about public access to such discoveries.

Understanding Legal Responsibilities for Deceased Relatives' Debts

2026-07-14 12:13:51 EDT

Generally, individuals are not legally responsible for a deceased relative's debts, as these typically become claims against the deceased person's estate, which is settled using their remaining assets. However, important exceptions exist if one co-signed a loan, was a joint account holder, or inherited property secured by the debt, like a mortgage. Debt collectors are usually restricted to contacting the estate's executor and cannot imply that family members personally owe the balance.
