Understanding the 5-Year Rule for Annuities

2025-06-04 13:34:59 EDT

The 5-year rule is an IRS regulation that affects how non-spouse beneficiaries must take distributions from an inherited annuity when the original contract owner dies. It essentially requires that the entire value of the annuity be distributed, either all at once or in multiple withdrawals, within five years of the original owner's death. This rule applies to non-qualified annuities funded with after-tax dollars and comes into play when the beneficiary is not a spouse. Understanding this rule can help beneficiaries avoid costly mistakes and ensure the money is distributed in a way that aligns with their financial goals. (not investment advice)
