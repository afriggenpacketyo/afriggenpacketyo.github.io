Lower Basin States Propose Colorado River Water-Saving Plan

2026-05-03 15:32:57 EDT

California, Arizona, and Nevada have jointly proposed a voluntary water-saving plan for the Colorado River, aiming to conserve 3.2 million acre-feet of water through 2028. This initiative seeks to provide a crucial interim solution while the seven basin states remain deadlocked on long-term agreements for managing the critically dwindling resource, which supplies 40 million people across the American West. The proposal, which also envisions additional conservation measures and a dedicated pool to meet tribal trust obligations, still requires approval from state water agencies and the Arizona legislature. With Lake Mead and Lake Powell at historic lows due to consistent overdrawing and climate change, these lower basin states underscore the urgent need to stabilize water supply and prevent further decline.
