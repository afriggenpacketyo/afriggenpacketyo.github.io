Digital Tablet Tool Significantly Reduces Antibiotic Overuse in Rwandan Clinics

2026-03-05 14:19:50 EST

A new computer tablet-based tool, ePOCT+, has dramatically reduced unnecessary antibiotic prescriptions in rural Rwandan clinics, from 71% to 25% of pediatric visits. Developed by Rwandan researchers, the tool guides clinicians through diagnosis and treatment suggestions, incorporating simple diagnostic tests. This innovation addresses the high rates of antibiotic overuse in sub-Saharan Africa, which contribute to antimicrobial resistance, without compromising patient health outcomes. The Rwandan government is now considering integrating a version of ePOCT+ into its national electronic medical record system, recognizing its potential to improve care quality and save costs for the public health insurance system.
