Long-Lost Rubens Masterpiece Discovered in Paris Sells for $2.7 Million

2025-11-30 17:00:52 EST

A Peter Paul Rubens painting, "Christ on the Cross," believed lost for over 400 years, was discovered in a Paris townhouse and subsequently sold for 2.3 million euros ($2.7 million) at auction. Completed in 1613, its existence was previously known only through engravings by other artists. Auctioneer Jean-Pierre Osenat found the artwork during a routine inspection and had it authenticated by the Centrum Rubenianum in Antwerp. Scientific analysis, including microscopic examination of paint layers, confirmed its authenticity, revealing pigments consistent with Rubens' Flemish Baroque technique. The piece was last owned by 19th-century French painter William Bouguereau, passed down through his family.

Playwright Tom Stoppard Remembered for Revitalizing the Comedy of Ideas

2025-11-30 16:11:07 EST

Esteemed Czech-born British playwright Tom Stoppard has passed away at 88, leaving behind a legacy of intellectually vibrant and witty works. Known for his "comedy of ideas," Stoppard reinvigorated the genre with plays like "Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead," "Jumpers," and "Arcadia," blending absurdist humor with profound philosophical inquiry. He was celebrated for his dazzling wordplay, intricate dramatic structures, and an omnivorous intellect, earning multiple Tony Awards. While sometimes criticized for intellectual brilliance over emotional depth, his later works, such as "The Real Thing" and "Leopoldstadt," explored more personal themes. Stoppard's unique contributions to theater will continue to captivate audiences for generations.
