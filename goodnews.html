Major Study Suggests Diet, Not Lack of Exercise, Is the Primary Driver of Modern Obesity

2025-07-24 05:00:00 EDT

A comprehensive new study challenges the common belief that a sedentary lifestyle is the main cause of rising obesity rates. Researchers compared the daily calorie expenditure of people across diverse cultures, from hunter-gatherers to office workers, and found that total energy burn is surprisingly similar across groups despite vast differences in physical activity. This suggests our bodies compensate for increased activity by reducing energy spent on other background functions. The findings provide strong evidence that changes in diet, particularly the rise of ultra-processed foods, are the principal factor behind the global obesity epidemic, not a collective decline in exercise.
