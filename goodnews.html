2025-07-31 21:07:58 EDT

Researchers solved the long-standing puzzle of the potato's origin by analyzing hundreds of potato genomes. They found that a hybridization event between a non-tuber-producing Chilean species and an ancient tomato provided the crucial genetic combination for tuber formation. A specific gene from the tomato ancestor signals the plant to create tubers, while a gene from the other parent helps control their growth, demonstrating how interbreeding can spark new evolutionary traits.
