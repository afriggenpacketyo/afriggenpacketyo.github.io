Understanding and Mitigating the Urban Heat Island Effect

2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

Urban heat islands describe metropolitan areas that are considerably hotter than their rural surroundings, primarily due to heat absorption by concrete and asphalt, air trapping by tall buildings, and pollution. This phenomenon intensifies heatwaves, making densely populated urban centers vulnerable, with temperatures potentially increasing by 10 to 15 degrees Celsius. To combat this, cities are implementing various solutions, including increasing green spaces with drought-resistant trees, installing reflective 'cool roofs,' and utilizing 'cool pavements' that enhance water evaporation. Global examples from New York, Tokyo, and Singapore demonstrate effective strategies to mitigate urban temperatures and address climate change impacts.
