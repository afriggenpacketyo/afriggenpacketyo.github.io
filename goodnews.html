Understanding Your Obligation to Pay a Debt That Has Been Sold to a Collection Agency

2025-11-20 13:29:11 EST

When a creditor sells a delinquent account to a debt collector, the borrower's legal obligation to pay the debt typically remains intact. The new owner of the debt purchases the legal right to collect the outstanding balance. However, under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, consumers have the right to request written verification of the debt, and collection efforts must pause until this is provided. It is also crucial to check if the debt is within the statute of limitations for legal action in your state. If the debt is valid, options for those unable to pay include negotiation, debt relief services, or credit counseling. (not investment advice)
