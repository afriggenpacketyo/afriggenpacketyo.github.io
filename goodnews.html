Scientists Successfully Extract Ancient RNA from a 39,000-Year-Old Woolly Mammoth

2025-11-14 11:41:30 EST

Researchers have successfully extracted and analyzed RNA from the remarkably well-preserved remains of a 39,000-year-old woolly mammoth named Yuka, found in Siberian permafrost. The findings provided insights into the mammoth's muscle function and stress responses, and also corrected its sex to male, contrary to initial visual inspections. This breakthrough opens new avenues for studying extinct species and potentially even ancient RNA viruses.
