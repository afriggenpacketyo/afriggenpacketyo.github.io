NASA's Perseverance Rover Discovers Strongest Evidence Yet of Potential Ancient Martian Life

2025-09-10 22:48:54 EDT

NASA's Perseverance rover has collected rock samples from a dried river channel on Mars that contain the most compelling potential signs of ancient microbial life found to date. The sample, from the "Bright Angel" formation, contains organic carbon along with iron phosphate and sulfide compounds, which on Earth are byproducts of microorganisms consuming organic matter. Scientists stress that these features could also be formed by non-biological processes and that a definitive conclusion requires analyzing the samples in labs on Earth. The discovery marks a significant step in the ongoing mission to determine if life ever existed on Mars.

China has opened the Changtai Yangtze River Bridge, the world's longest cable-stayed bridge, connecting the cities of Changzhou and Taizhou in Jiangsu province. The 10.3-kilometer structure is a multi-purpose crossing, carrying an expressway, a local road, and an intercity railway on a single structure for the first time on the river. The bridge is a major engineering feat that will significantly reduce travel time between the two cities from over an hour to just 20 minutes.
