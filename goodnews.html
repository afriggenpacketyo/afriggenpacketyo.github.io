NASA's Van Allen Probe A Expected to Re-enter Earth's Atmosphere Today

2026-03-10 10:35:09 EDT

A 1,300-pound NASA satellite, Van Allen Probe A, is anticipated to re-enter Earth's atmosphere today, concluding its nearly 14-year journey in space. Launched in August 2012, the probe, along with its twin, was instrumental in exploring Earth's permanent radiation belts, known as the Van Allen belts. During its extended mission, which far exceeded its initial two-year projection, the probe made significant discoveries, including providing the first data on the existence of a temporary third radiation belt during intense solar activity. While most of the spacecraft is expected to disintegrate upon re-entry, some components may survive, though NASA assures the public that the risk of harm to individuals on Earth is very low, estimated at 1 in 4,200.
