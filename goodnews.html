An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Function

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial body of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between member states. Comprised of 15 judges from different countries, the court's rulings are legally binding on the parties involved, though it lacks direct enforcement power. Its jurisdiction applies when states agree to submit to it, either through a general declaration or on a case-by-case basis. The ICJ also hears cases based on specific UN treaties, such as the Genocide Convention, which has been invoked in recent cases involving Ukraine, Myanmar, and Israel.
