An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Authority

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between states. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, its rulings are legally binding for the 193 UN member states, though enforcement is a major challenge. The court's jurisdiction applies when states agree to it, either through a blanket declaration, mutual consent for a specific dispute, or by being signatories to treaties like the Genocide Convention, which designates the ICJ as the arbiter. However, the ICJ lacks an enforcement body, relying on the cooperation of the involved parties.
