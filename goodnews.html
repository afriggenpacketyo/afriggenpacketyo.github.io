New York City to Use Ranked Choice Voting in Mayoral Primary

2025-06-23 00:04:00 EDT

New York City will be using ranked choice voting in its upcoming Democratic mayoral primary election, a system where voters rank multiple candidates in order of preference. If no candidate receives a majority of first-choice votes, the candidate with the fewest first-choice votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed to the voters' second choices. This process continues until a candidate receives a majority. The system aims to ensure that the elected candidate has broad support and prevents candidates with fringe views from winning in a crowded field, but it also makes races less predictable and requires more research from voters.
