Supreme Court Invalidates Trump's Tariffs, Citing Lack of Presidential Authority Under Emergency Powers Act

2026-02-21 21:29:20 EST

The Supreme Court, in a 6-3 decision, invalidated President Trump's most sweeping tariffs, ruling that he lacked the authority to impose them under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA). The majority, a bipartisan coalition of justices, found that IEEPA's power to "regulate... importation" does not encompass the power to impose tariffs, which are distinct from other authorized actions and generate revenue. Chief Justice John Roberts' majority opinion, partially invoking the major questions doctrine, stressed that Congress grants tariff powers explicitly and with constraints. The ruling marks a significant legal check on presidential authority in trade matters.
