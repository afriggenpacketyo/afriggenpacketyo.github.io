Vermont Becomes First US State to Ban Paraquat Herbicide Citing Parkinson’s Disease Concerns

2026-05-26 16:46:54 EDT

Vermont has become the first U.S. state to ban the weedkilling pesticide paraquat, with Governor Phil Scott signing the legislation that takes effect November 1. Lawmakers cited National Institutes of Health studies linking paraquat exposure to a substantially increased risk of Parkinson’s disease, non-Hodgkin lymphoma, and childhood leukemia. While some expressed concerns about farmer competitiveness, proponents argued the scientific evidence warranted the ban for public health. Syngenta, a major producer, recently ceased making paraquat, though generic versions remain available. Advocates are now pushing for a national ban, highlighting the rising incidence of Parkinson's globally and the need for protective measures.
