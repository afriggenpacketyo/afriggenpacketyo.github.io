Occurring for four-day periods from March to August, coinciding with full and new moons, female grunion bury themselves in the sand to lay eggs while males wriggle around them. This "cryptic species" event is a cherished annual tradition for many Southern California families, with runs predicted from Tuesday to Friday this week. Conservation efforts protect grunion populations, with fishing prohibited from April through June and strict rules for catching them by hand.
