Understanding the Income Limits for Filing Chapter 7 Bankruptcy

2025-10-21 14:43:58 EDT

For individuals overwhelmed by debt, Chapter 7 bankruptcy can offer a fresh start, but eligibility is largely determined by income through a process called the "means test." To qualify, a person's monthly income over the past six months must generally be below their state's median income for a household of their size. If their income is higher, they may still qualify if, after deducting allowed expenses like housing and healthcare, their disposable income is limited. For those who don't qualify, alternatives like Chapter 13 bankruptcy, debt settlement programs, or debt management plans offer other paths to financial relief without the same long-term credit impact.
