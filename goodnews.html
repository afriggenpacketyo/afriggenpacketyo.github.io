FDA Approves Implantable Nerve Stimulator for Rheumatoid Arthritis Treatment

2025-09-22 06:00:00 EDT

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration has approved an experimental device that treats rheumatoid arthritis by stimulating the vagus nerve. The implant, made by SetPoint Medical, is for patients whose symptoms are not controlled by medication. It works by sending electrical pulses to the vagus nerve for one minute each day, which signals the brain to reduce the production of inflammatory proteins in the spleen. While not a cure for all, clinical trials showed that 35% of patients in a difficult-to-treat group experienced meaningful improvement, offering a new therapeutic option for a debilitating autoimmune disease.
