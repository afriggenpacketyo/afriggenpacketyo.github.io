Found at Barnham, near a site where Neanderthals likely lived, the discovery includes heated clay, heat-shattered handaxes, and non-local iron pyrite, strongly suggesting intentional fire ignition rather than opportunistic use of natural fires. This groundbreaking finding significantly pushes back the timeline for early human control of fire, a pivotal moment in evolution that dramatically improved diets through cooking and enabled survival in colder environments. The use of fire also fostered social cohesion, potentially aiding the development of language and storytelling.
