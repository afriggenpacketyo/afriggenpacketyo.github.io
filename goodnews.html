The Historical and Linguistic Journey of the Thanksgiving Turkey

2025-11-26 05:00:00 EST

The turkey, a centerpiece of the American Thanksgiving meal, has a complex and somewhat confusing history, particularly regarding its name. The bird, first domesticated in the Americas, was introduced to Europe in the 1520s where it was mistaken for the African guinea fowl, which was already known as a 'turkey' because it was imported through the Turkish region. The American bird eventually inherited the name and grew in popularity, later being brought back to North America by English colonists. Over time, the word 'turkey' also entered the English lexicon in various idioms, often with negative connotations, such as 'cold turkey' for quitting suddenly or as slang for a failure.
