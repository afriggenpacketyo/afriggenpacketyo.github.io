New Research Indicates Fertility Decline for Women Over 49, Even with Donor Eggs

2026-07-06 19:33:47 EDT

New research suggests that women aged 49 and older face significantly lower chances of fertility treatment success, even when utilizing young donor eggs. A study of 1,774 women found that those 49 and over had nearly double the miscarriage risk and lower pregnancy rates compared to younger women. Experts believe age-related changes in the womb lining, rather than just egg quality, may be the primary cause. While success rates remain meaningful, the findings challenge the idea that donor eggs fully "reset" the reproductive clock, urging realistic counseling for older couples.
