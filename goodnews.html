2025-09-26 12:57:13 EDT

A retirement income strategy known as the "rule of thirds" is gaining traction as an alternative to traditional methods like the 4% withdrawal rule. This approach involves converting one-third of retirement savings into a guaranteed lifetime income product, such as a fixed annuity, to cover essential expenses. The remaining two-thirds of the portfolio can then be invested for growth or kept liquid for unexpected costs, balancing financial security with flexibility. While this method can reduce stress during market volatility, retirees should consider the trade-offs, including the illiquidity of the annuity portion and the continued market risk for the invested funds.
