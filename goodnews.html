Piedmont, California to Memorialize First Black Family Driven Out 100 Years Ago

2025-06-24 12:29:49 EDT

A memorial sculpture park is planned in Piedmont, California, to honor Sidney and Irene Dearing, the first Black family to own a home in the city. In 1924, the Dearing family faced intense racism, including violence and threats, leading them to sell their home back to the city for their safety. The memorial project, initiated in 2020, aims to acknowledge and address this dark chapter in Piedmont's history. Artist Walter Hood's design will feature portals and a symbolic mailbox to represent the couple's unrealized life in the community, with the city committed to confronting its past and fostering a more inclusive future. The memorial is expected to be completed within the next 12 months.
