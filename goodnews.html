Understanding the Body's Response to Heatwaves and Identifying At-Risk Individuals

2026-05-28 05:03:55 EDT

Extreme heat can have significant physiological effects, causing blood vessels to dilate, lowering blood pressure, and increasing the heart's workload. This can lead to symptoms like dizziness, nausea, and confusion, progressing to heat exhaustion if not managed. The body's natural cooling mechanisms, such as sweating, can lead to fluid and salt loss, further exacerbating these effects. Prolonged exposure to high temperatures can result in heatstroke, a medical emergency requiring immediate attention.\n\nCertain groups are more vulnerable to the effects of heatwaves, including older adults, young children, and individuals with chronic health conditions. Medications can also impact the body's ability to regulate temperature and stay hydrated. The UK Health Security Agency recommends precautions like staying cool indoors, drinking plenty of fluids, and avoiding strenuous activity during the hottest parts of the day. In 2022, heatwaves in England were linked to a significant number of excess deaths, underscoring the serious health risks associated with extreme temperatures.
