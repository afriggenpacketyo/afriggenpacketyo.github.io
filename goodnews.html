California Coastal "Volcanoes" Are Actually Cases of Spontaneous Combustion

2026-04-24 18:18:57 EDT

Recent fires erupting from cliffsides in Santa Barbara, known locally as the "Hope Ranch Volcano," are not actual volcanoes, according to geologists. Experts explain these phenomena as spontaneous combustion, where oxygen interacts with unstable compounds like iron sulfides in moving earth layers, generating intense heat. This heat can cause organic materials to ignite, a process observed in the area since the 1800s. Firefighters sometimes refer to it as a "natural solfatara," but geologists clarify that without an underlying volcano, this term is inaccurate. Similar occurrences in Ventura County are also cases of geological shifts, clarifying common misconceptions about these dramatic coastal flare-ups.
