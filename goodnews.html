2026-05-17 10:44:00 EDT

Irish researchers have made a significant discovery, uncovering the oldest surviving English-language poem, "Caedmon's Hymn," within a 9th-century medieval manuscript of Bede's "Ecclesiastical History of the English People" in a Roman library. This find pushes back the known diffusion of the English language by three centuries, establishing it as the earliest example of written English literature. The nine-line hymn, composed in Old English by a Northumbrian agricultural worker in the 7th century, was uniquely found embedded within the main Latin text, a rare occurrence compared to earlier marginal notations. The manuscript's complex journey across continents before its rediscovery by Elisabetta Magnanti and Mark Faulkner adds a fascinating layer to this important academic and historical find.
