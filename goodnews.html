China Nears Completion of 'Most Difficult' Dali-Ruili Railway Project

2026-02-18 20:03:05 EST

China is on track to complete the world's 'most difficult' railway project, the Dali-Ruili line in Yunnan province, before 2030. This ambitious railway is the first to cut through the Hengduan Mountains, navigating extremely complex geological and topographical challenges. Spanning approximately 330km along China's southwestern frontier, the project is divided into two sections, with the first completed in 2022. The full line, which connects Dali to the border town of Ruili (a major crossing with Myanmar), is expected to be operational within five years. Its completion will significantly enhance China's connectivity with Southeast Asia and represents a major milestone in global railway engineering.
