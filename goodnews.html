Economic Analysis Explains Why Widespread Price Decreases Are Unlikely and Undesirable

2025-11-20 06:30:00 EST

This article explains that while politicians often promise to lower prices, a general decrease in the overall price level, known as deflation, is rare and typically signals a severe economic downturn. Central banks, like the Federal Reserve, intentionally target a small amount of inflation, around 2%, to encourage spending and investment. A deflationary spiral, where falling prices cause consumers to delay purchases, can grind an economy to a halt, as seen during the Great Depression. Therefore, while individual prices fluctuate, the aggregate cost of goods and services is designed to rise slowly and steadily over time. (not investment advice)
