2026-03-07 06:00:13 EST

This annual tradition, which shifts an hour of daylight from morning to evening, has occurred on the second Sunday of March since 2007. Historically, its start date has varied, with a brief experiment of year-round daylight saving during the 1970s energy crisis. While initially intended to conserve fuel, studies have found minimal energy benefits.
