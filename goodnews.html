Volunteers and Cows Collaborate to Protect Rare Butterfly Habitat in Wales

2026-07-10 05:07:01 EDT

Conservationists in Wales are successfully working to protect the rare high brown fritillary butterfly, whose numbers have drastically declined since 1970. In the Alun Valley, volunteers led by Paul Dunn have enlisted local commoners' association members to graze cows on the site for the first time in over half a century. The cattle help manage bracken, which, in excess, inhibits the growth of common dog violet, the butterfly's crucial food source. This innovative "no-fence grazing" approach, utilizing solar-paneled collars, has created an ideal habitat, contributing to a rise in butterfly species and fostering a strong community spirit among the dedicated volunteers.
