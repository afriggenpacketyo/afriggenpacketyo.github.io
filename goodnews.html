The Genesis of Football's Penalty Shootout: A Historical Retrospective

2026-03-28 02:15:22 EDT

This article recounts the historical introduction of football's first official penalty shootout, which took place on August 5, 1970, during a Watney Cup match between Manchester United and Hull City. Before this innovation, tied knockout games were decided by replays, drawing lots, or coin tosses, with the 1968 European Championship and Olympics highlighting the need for a more skill-based resolution. The concept, proposed by Israeli Football Association officials, was adopted by FIFA's lawmakers, replacing the "cruel system" of luck. The inaugural shootout saw George Best score the first penalty and Ian McKechnie become the first goalkeeper to save and miss one.
