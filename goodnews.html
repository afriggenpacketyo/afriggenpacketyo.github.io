Medieval Leather Artifacts, Including Shoes and Bags, Uncovered in Oslo

2025-09-11 11:55:04 EDT

Archaeologists in Oslo, Norway, have unearthed thousands of remarkably well-preserved medieval leather artifacts, including shoes, purses, and sword sheaths, providing an extraordinary glimpse into the city's historical fashion and daily life. Discovered in the Bjørvika port district, an area historically submerged, the oxygen-deprived clay soil facilitated the exceptional preservation of these organic materials over centuries. The findings, which include 68 children's shoes reflecting adult styles, primarily date from the 13th to 15th centuries, revealing a puzzling 400-year gap in subsequent historical layers. This significant excavation offers unique insights into the resourcefulness of medieval Osloans, particularly the upper class, and their habits of extensive repair and reuse of items.

Understanding Medicare's 3-Day Rule for Skilled Nursing Facility Coverage

2025-09-11 11:08:05 EDT

Medicare's "3-day rule" is a critical regulation requiring a medically necessary inpatient hospital stay of at least three consecutive days for Part A to cover subsequent skilled nursing facility (SNF) care. This rule significantly impacts out-of-pocket costs, as failure to meet it can leave beneficiaries responsible for the entire SNF bill, which can amount to tens of thousands of dollars. The rule becomes particularly problematic due to the increasing use of observation status, which does not count towards the three-day requirement. However, exemptions exist, such as Medicare Advantage waivers and appeal rights, making it essential for seniors to understand these nuances for their healthcare planning.
