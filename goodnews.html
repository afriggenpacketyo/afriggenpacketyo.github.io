New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, Discovered in Thailand

2026-07-11 04:23:45 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new species of long-necked, plant-eating dinosaur, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, based on fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This sauropod, estimated to have lived approximately 150 million years ago and reached lengths of up to 20 meters, represents the first discovery of its Mamenchisauridae family in Thailand. A CT scan of a recovered dorsal vertebra revealed unique characteristics, including a distinctive Y-shaped arrangement of supporting bones and a unique air-cavity structure, distinguishing it from all other known dinosaurs. This significant scientific find was published in the Nature scientific journal, marking a major contribution to the field of palaeontology.
