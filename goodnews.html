Bipartisan Bill Proposed to Allow Direct Charitable Donations from 401(k) Retirement Plans

2026-05-18 11:42:33 EDT

A new bipartisan bill, the Charity Parity Act, aims to simplify tax-free charitable giving for older Americans by allowing qualified charitable distributions (QCDs) directly from 401(k)s and similar workplace retirement plans. Currently, QCDs, available to those 70½ or older, must originate from an IRA, often requiring an extra rollover step. Proponents argue this modernization would eliminate unnecessary hurdles, offering significant tax benefits by excluding donations from income and counting towards required minimum distributions, potentially preventing Medicare premium increases for higher earners.

Understanding Creditor Limitations When Retirees Are Sued for Debt

2026-05-18 11:38:48 EDT

Retirees facing debt lawsuits often worry about losing their fixed incomes and assets, but federal law provides significant protections against creditors. Social Security benefits are largely shielded from garnishment for private debts, though federal debts like taxes or student loans are exceptions. While ERISA-governed 401(k)s and pensions also have strong federal protections, these can weaken once funds are withdrawn and commingled with other accounts. Home and other property protections vary by state, with homestead exemption laws playing a crucial role in safeguarding primary residences from unsecured creditors.
