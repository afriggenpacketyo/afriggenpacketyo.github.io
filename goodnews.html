He leveraged Venetian double-entry bookkeeping and an extensive intelligence network to amass immense wealth. Fugger became an indispensable banker to Habsburg emperors, securing lucrative access to silver and copper mines, which were in high demand across Europe and Asia. His influence extended to persuading the Pope to relax the ban on charging interest, fundamentally reshaping lending practices. Fugger's lasting legacy includes the Fuggerei, the world's first social housing complex, and an estimated wealth equivalent to hundreds of billions today.
