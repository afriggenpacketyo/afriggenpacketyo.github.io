2025-08-21 11:07:51 EDT

A recent study published in Science Advances suggests that public wolf hunting in the Western U.S. has a minimal impact on reducing livestock losses for ranchers. Researchers analyzed data from Montana, Idaho, Oregon, and Washington and found that killing a wolf saved only a small fraction of a single cow, meaning roughly 14 wolves would need to be killed to prevent one livestock death. The study also found that hunting did not decrease the number of government-led removals of problematic wolves. These findings challenge the long-held assumption that reducing wolf populations directly and effectively mitigates human-wildlife conflict for ranchers.
