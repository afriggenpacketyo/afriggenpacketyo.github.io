Researchers Explore Innovative Methods to Vaccinate Bats to Prevent Disease Spillover to Humans

2026-03-13 15:45:42 EDT

Chinese researchers are developing innovative methods to vaccinate bats against deadly pathogens like Ebola, Nipah, and rabies, aiming to prevent disease spillover to humans while simultaneously protecting bat populations. Virologist Aihua Zheng and his team propose two main strategies: utilizing genetically engineered vaccine-carrying mosquitoes to either bite or be consumed by bats, and delivering oral rabies vaccines via saline solution that bats readily drink. Both approaches successfully induced antibody production in lab-tested bats, offering protection from subsequent rabies infection. This promising research offers a humane and ecologically sound alternative to culling bats, which play critical roles in pollination and pest control, representing an exciting development in global health and wildlife conservation efforts.
