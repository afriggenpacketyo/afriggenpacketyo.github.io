While the number is somewhat arbitrary, health experts note that it coincidentally aligns with general physical activity guidelines and can be a useful motivator. Studies show health benefits, such as reduced cardiovascular risk, can begin at far lower step counts, around 2,500 to 3,000 steps a day. Experts emphasize that consistency and intensity matter more than a specific number, encouraging people to simply move more than they currently do.
