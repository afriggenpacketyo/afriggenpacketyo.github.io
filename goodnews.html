NASA Prepares for Historic Artemis II Crewed Lunar Orbit Mission

2026-03-30 05:00:11 EDT

NASA is preparing for the historic Artemis II mission, scheduled for launch on April 1, sending a four-person crew on a nine-day journey around the moon. This critical test flight aims to thoroughly evaluate the Space Launch System rocket and Orion capsule's systems, including a new reentry trajectory designed to mitigate issues encountered during Artemis I. The mission represents a significant milestone in NASA's long-term goal of establishing a sustained human presence on the moon by 2028, with the crew set to break distance records and test vital life support functions. This endeavor lays the groundwork for future deep space exploration and potential lunar habitation.

Cuba is currently facing its most severe energy crisis since the collapse of the Soviet Union, exacerbated by the cessation of oil supplies from Venezuela and prior U.S. threats of tariffs on countries assisting the island. Rare Bronze Age Shield Discovered in Scotland to Feature in New Exhibition

2026-03-30 02:58:35 EDT

A "spectacular and intricate" Bronze Age shield, initially discovered in a Beith peat bog in 1779 and residing in London for over 230 years, is returning to Scotland for a new exhibition. This rare combat guard, which shows evidence of spear and other weaponry damage, will be displayed alongside five other shields from the same period, offering a "once-in-a-lifetime" opportunity to delve into life and conflict in Bronze Age Scotland. Its unique design indicates high-status ownership, and its burial alongside other shields suggests an ancient ceremony, possibly symbolizing a pact of peace among communities. The exhibition will explore thousands of years of Scottish prehistory.
