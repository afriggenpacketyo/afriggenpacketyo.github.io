2025-10-09 20:20:07 EDT

The dolphin-sized marine reptile, dating back approximately 185 million years, is the only known example of its kind and fills a crucial gap in ichthyosaur evolution. Marks on its skull suggest it may have been killed by a bite to the head from a larger ichthyosaur, while malformed limb bones and teeth indicate serious injury or disease during its life. The discovery highlights the dangerous nature of life in the Mesozoic oceans and the ongoing significance of Dorset's Jurassic Coast for paleontological finds.
