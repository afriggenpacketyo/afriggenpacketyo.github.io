NASA's Perseverance Rover Finds Strongest Hints of Ancient Martian Life

2025-09-10 22:48:54 EDT

NASA's Mars rover Perseverance has uncovered rocks in a dry river channel on Mars that may contain the strongest hints yet of ancient microscopic life. Scientists stress that definitive conclusions require in-depth analysis of these samples back on Earth, a mission currently on hold due to cost and logistical challenges. The samples from Neretva Vallis, a former river channel, show organic carbon and minuscule specks enriched with iron phosphate and iron sulfide, compounds often produced by microorganisms on Earth. While non-biological processes could also be responsible, these findings represent the most compelling candidates in the rover's search for past Martian life.
