Ancient Skeleton Discovered Upright Near French School Playground Prompts Archaeological Inquiry

2026-03-18 11:18:14 EDT

Children at a French primary school in Dijon made a fascinating discovery: a remarkably well-preserved ancient skeleton sitting upright in a circular pit next to their playground. This finding adds to a series of similar mysterious burials in Dijon, where the enigmatic Gaul people interred their dead in a seated, west-facing position around 300-200 BC. Archaeologists are actively investigating the purpose of this atypical burial method, pondering if it represented a punishment, a mark of status, or possibly even a form of human sacrifice. Understanding State-Specific Statutes of Limitations for Credit Card Debt

2026-03-18 11:16:42 EDT

With U.S. credit card debt nearing $1.3 trillion, many borrowers face collection calls and potential lawsuits over unpaid balances. This article details the varying statutes of limitations across states, which legally limit how long creditors have to file a lawsuit to collect a debt. Arkansas, Delaware, Mississippi, and North Carolina are highlighted for having some of the shortest periods, typically three years from the last payment or account activity. While the debt itself doesn't disappear and collection attempts may continue, it becomes "time-barred," generally preventing legal enforcement. Borrowers are cautioned that acknowledging or making a partial payment on old debts could restart the statute of limitations.
