The Etymology of 'Algorithm': Tracing its Roots to a 9th-Century Persian Mathematician

2026-06-11 05:00:00 EDT

The word 'algorithm,' now often associated with artificial intelligence and its potential dangers, has a fascinating etymology tracing back to the 9th-century Persian mathematician Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi. His name, Latinized as 'algorismus,' eventually evolved into the modern term. Al-Khwarizmi was instrumental in introducing algorithmic methods and Hindu-Arabic numerals to the West, laying foundations for algebra. The concept of an algorithm as a set of well-defined instructions for accomplishing a task has been crucial in fields ranging from navigation to space exploration, predating modern computing by centuries. Despite current concerns about AI's impact, algorithms have historically been vital tools for human problem-solving.
