The Linguistic History of How the Thanksgiving Turkey Got Its Name

2025-11-26 05:00:00 EST

The name of the turkey, a staple of American Thanksgiving, has a confusing and uncertain history rooted in a case of mistaken identity from the 16th century. European explorers brought the North American bird, meleagris gallopavo, back to Europe, where it was confused with the African guinea fowl, which was already called a "turkey" because it was imported through the Turkish region. The American bird's popularity grew, and it eventually inherited the name, which it retained when English colonists brought it back to North America. The word "turkey" has also evolved into various negative colloquialisms in the English language.
