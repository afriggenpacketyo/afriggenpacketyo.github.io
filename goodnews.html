Australia Approves World-First Vaccine to Combat Chlamydia Epidemic in Koalas

2025-09-09 18:04:56 EDT

Australian regulators have approved the world's first vaccine designed to protect endangered koalas from chlamydia, a disease that has devastated their populations. Developed by scientists at the University of the Sunshine Coast over more than a decade, the single-dose vaccine has been shown to reduce mortality by at least 65%. Chlamydia can cause blindness, infertility, and death in koalas, and is responsible for up to half of all deaths in some wild colonies. This approval marks a significant breakthrough in the effort to save the iconic species, which is also threatened by habitat loss and bushfires.
