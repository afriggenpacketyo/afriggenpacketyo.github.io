Astronomers Witness Earliest Stages of Rocky Planet Formation for the First Time

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb and ALMA telescopes, astronomers have captured the first-ever direct evidence of rocky planet formation around a nascent, sun-like star named HOPS-315. Located 1,370 light-years away, the infant star's surrounding gas disk shows signs of silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing, the fundamental building blocks of terrestrial planets like Earth. This discovery provides an unprecedented look into a process that occurred in our own solar system over 4.5 billion years ago. The observation suggests that the initial steps of planet formation may be a common cosmic event, offering new insights into the potential prevalence of Earth-like worlds.
