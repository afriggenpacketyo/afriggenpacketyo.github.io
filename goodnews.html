2025-12-22 04:27:00 EST

Scientific evidence suggests that women process alcohol differently from men, leading to faster and more intense intoxication, even when consuming the same amount. Beyond physical differences, women also exhibit 'telescoping,' a faster progression from alcohol use to dependence, and hormonal fluctuations can increase alcohol's rewarding effects. Understanding these biological distinctions is crucial for addressing alcohol-related health consequences and rethinking equality in drinking habits.
