New Clinical Trial in Africa Shows Promising Results for HIV Cure

2025-08-25 06:48:00 EDT

A clinical trial in South Africa has yielded a potential cure for HIV in one young woman, a landmark development for the continent most affected by the virus. The trial, part of the FRESH program, focused on women who began antiretroviral therapy very soon after infection. The treatment strategy involved a "kick and kill" approach, using a drug to flush the virus from its reservoirs and then neutralizing it with tailored antibodies. While the virus returned in most participants after they stopped their regular medication, four women remained in remission, with one still HIV-free and off treatment more than two years later.
