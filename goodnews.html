Explainer: The Science Behind How Tsunamis Form and the Dangers They Pose

2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

Following the 8.8-magnitude earthquake off Russia's coast, this article explains the science of tsunamis. They are typically generated by strong, shallow, underwater earthquakes with vertical tectonic plate movement, which displaces a massive volume of water. In the deep ocean, the waves travel as fast as a jet but are often unnoticeably low; they slow down and grow dangerously high as they approach shallower coastal waters. Predicting their exact size and impact remains challenging, and experts stress the importance of heeding all evacuation warnings immediately, as even small-looking waves can carry immense destructive power.
