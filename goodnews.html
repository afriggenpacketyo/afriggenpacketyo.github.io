New Scan Technique Shows Promise for Earlier Endometriosis Detection

2026-04-29 20:08:59 EDT

A pilot study at Oxford University suggests a new CT scan technique, combined with a molecular tracer, shows promise for earlier detection of endometriosis. This condition, affecting one in ten women, often takes an average of nine years to diagnose, with conventional scans frequently missing early-stage disease. If confirmed in larger studies, this non-invasive method could significantly reduce diagnostic delays, offering women earlier explanations for severe symptoms and enabling better life planning. The technique correctly detected endometriosis in 16 of 19 women, including 14 of 17 cases later confirmed by surgery, marking a hopeful step towards improving patient outcomes for a painful and often debilitating condition.
