UK Government Proposes Major Reform to End Leasehold System for Flats

2026-07-07 19:13:38 EDT

The UK government is advancing a major reform to flat ownership through its Commonhold and Leasehold Reform Bill, intending to ban new leasehold flats and transition to a commonhold system. This proposed change would empower residents to collectively own and manage their buildings, providing significantly more control over shared facilities and maintenance costs, addressing frustrations with the existing "feudal" leasehold model. While advocates highlight increased autonomy and alignment with successful international property ownership structures, some critics express concerns about potential difficulties in dispute resolution and securing funds for maintenance within commonhold arrangements. The legislation also aims to cap ground rents, though the compensation for existing freeholders remains a contentious issue, likely facing legal challenges.
