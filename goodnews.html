Over the past 30 years, the UK has experienced 19 white Christmases compared to seven white Easter Sundays. However, March generally records more days of snowfall than December, with an average of 4.2 snow days versus 3.9. Consequently, when Easter falls in March, the probability of a white Easter is approximately equivalent to that of a white Christmas, although this likelihood significantly decreases in April.
