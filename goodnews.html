California, Arizona, Nevada Propose Colorado River Water-Saving Plan

2026-05-03 15:32:57 EDT

California, Arizona, and Nevada have jointly proposed voluntary water-saving measures for the Colorado River, aiming to conserve 3.2 million acre-feet of water over three years. This plan seeks to stabilize historically low reservoir levels (Lake Mead and Lake Powell) while broader negotiations among the seven basin states remain deadlocked. The proposal also includes saving an additional 700,000 acre-feet through conservation and infrastructure, plus a conservation pool for tribal trust obligations. The lower basin states emphasize this unified package as crucial for the system's stability, awaiting approval from state agencies and the federal government.
