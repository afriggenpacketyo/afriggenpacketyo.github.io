Pentecost: Understanding the Christian holiday's origins and traditions

2026-05-22 11:56:00 EDT

Pentecost, also known as Whitsun or Trinity Sunday, is a Christian holiday commemorating the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the Apostles, occurring 50 days after Easter. Its roots lie in the Jewish Feast of Weeks. The event is described as a "miracle of understanding" where the disciples, empowered by the Holy Spirit, could communicate in different languages. Celebrated across Christian faiths, Pentecost symbolizes the birth of the Christian Church. Traditions include the color red, symbolizing the Holy Spirit, and in some churches, the scattering of rose petals to represent flames of the spirit.
