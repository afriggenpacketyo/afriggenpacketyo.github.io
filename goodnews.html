NASA Launches Twin Satellites to Study 'Space Weather' Origins

2025-07-23 16:40:12 EDT

NASA has successfully launched the Tandem Reconnection and Cusp Electrodynamics Reconnaissance Satellites (TRACERS) mission aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket. The identical twin satellites will fly in formation to study how the solar wind, a stream of charged particles from the sun, interacts with Earth's magnetic field. This interaction creates "space weather," which can generate spectacular auroras but also disrupt GPS signals, power grids, and satellites. By taking precise measurements from two points simultaneously, scientists hope to better understand and eventually predict these potentially hazardous space phenomena.
