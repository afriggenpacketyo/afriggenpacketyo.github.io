First Dinosaur Bone from Antarctica Identified After Decades in Storage

2026-06-29 00:40:41 EDT

An unassuming fossil, stored for 40 years in a drawer at the British Antarctic Survey, has been definitively identified as the first dinosaur bone ever discovered in Antarctica. Unearthed in 1985, the specimen was recently studied by palaeontologists who confirmed it is a tail bone from a Titanosaur, a group encompassing the largest dinosaurs to have walked the Earth. This significant discovery, made by a collections manager who recognized its distinctive shape, offers crucial new insights into how these colossal beasts lived in a part of the world with a sparse fossil record. It helps to reveal that the now-frozen continent was once a habitable environment supporting a diverse cast of large prehistoric characters around 80 million years ago.
