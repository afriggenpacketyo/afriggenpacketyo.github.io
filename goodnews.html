An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Function

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between member states. Comprised of 15 judges from different countries, the court's rulings are legally binding, though it lacks its own enforcement mechanism and relies on the cooperation of the involved nations. The ICJ's jurisdiction applies when states agree to submit to it or when a case is brought under a specific UN convention, such as the Genocide Convention, which has been the basis for recent cases involving Ukraine, Myanmar, and Israel.
