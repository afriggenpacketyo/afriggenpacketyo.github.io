Greenland's Size Appears Distorted on Maps, Especially Mercator Projection

2026-01-07 14:01:59 EST

President Trump's renewed interest in annexing Greenland highlights the island's significant size, which is often distorted on world maps, particularly the common Mercator projection. Greenland, the world's largest island, appears comparable in size to Africa on Mercator maps, despite Africa being approximately 14 times larger. This distortion occurs because the projection makes latitudes and longitudes straight lines, exaggerating landmasses further from the equator. Alternative projections like Robinson or Equal Earth aim for more accurate representations of relative land sizes.
