Tiny Zooplankton Play Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny marine animals called zooplankton are "unsung heroes" in the fight against climate change, locking away vast amounts of carbon in the deep ocean. These creatures gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface in Antarctica, grow fat, and then sink hundreds of meters to hibernate, burning the fat and releasing the carbon deep underwater. This process, known as the seasonal vertical migration pump, stores as much carbon as the annual emissions of about 55 million cars. The discovery highlights the Southern Ocean's critical role in absorbing excess heat, though growing threats to these vital creatures are a cause for concern.
