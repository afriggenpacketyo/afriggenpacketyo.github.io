This article explains the "95% rule," a critical consumer protection feature of federally insured reverse mortgages. This rule stipulates that if heirs wish to keep the property after the borrower's death, they only need to pay the lesser of the full loan balance or 95% of the home's current appraised value. This prevents a situation where heirs would have to pay more than the home is worth, especially if the loan balance has grown significantly over time, providing them with a more manageable option to retain the family home.
