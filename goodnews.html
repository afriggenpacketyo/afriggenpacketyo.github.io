China's wholesale inflation reaches near 4-year high due to Iran war and AI boom, consumer inflation lags

2026-06-10 00:00:17 EDT

China's producer price index (PPI) surged by 3.9% year-on-year in May, marking the highest inflation rate in nearly four years and exceeding economists' expectations. This increase is largely attributed to rising raw material costs, exacerbated by the ongoing Iran war's impact on global supply chains, and a boom in artificial intelligence investment driving up demand for tech components. Factory input costs for fuel and power saw a significant jump, as did prices for non-ferrous metal materials. In contrast, consumer inflation remained below forecasts, rising by 1.2% year-on-year, indicating weak domestic demand despite rising wholesale costs. Analysts suggest that Chinese factories are facing squeezed profit margins due to their inability to pass on increased costs to consumers. The report also notes that China's export growth remained robust, particularly for AI-related goods.
