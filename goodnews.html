An unusual market condition known as an inverted yield curve currently means that short-term CDs (six months to one year) often provide higher interest rates than long-term CDs (three to five years). To maximize returns, financial experts suggest savers look primarily at online banks, which have lower overhead, and consider a "laddering" strategy, which involves purchasing multiple CDs with staggered maturity dates to balance high short-term rates with long-term savings goals.
