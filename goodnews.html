2025-05-13 15:24:02 EDT

The Social Security cost-of-living adjustment (COLA) for 2026 is projected to be the lowest annual benefit increase in five years, estimated at 2.4%. This adjustment, which may change based on inflation, would be lower than the 2.5% boost seen in 2025 and the lowest since 2021. The COLA provides an annual inflation adjustment to Social Security beneficiaries, including retirees and disabled individuals. It is calculated by comparing third-quarter inflation data from the current year to the previous year, using the Consumer Price Index for Urban Wage Earners and Clerical Workers (CPI-W). The CPI-W increased by 2.1% over the past 12 months.
