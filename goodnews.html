Analyzing Monthly Payouts for a $250,000 Annuity at Age 60

2026-05-01 11:31:04 EDT

For a 60-year-old considering a $250,000 immediate fixed annuity, monthly payouts can range from approximately $1,115 to $1,325, depending on gender and the chosen payout structure. Men typically receive higher monthly amounts due to shorter statistical life expectancies, while women's payouts are adjusted downward accordingly. A single life annuity offers the largest monthly check but ceases upon the annuitant's death, leaving nothing for heirs. In contrast, options like "life + period certain" or joint life annuities provide extended coverage to beneficiaries or spouses at a reduced monthly rate, balancing income with security. Annuities offer guaranteed income, reducing market volatility and longevity risk, making them a valuable tool for those without pensions seeking predictable retirement funds, though they come with trade-offs like illiquidity.
