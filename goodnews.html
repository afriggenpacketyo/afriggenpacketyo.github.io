Understanding the 90-Day Preference Rule in Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Filings

2026-01-20 11:54:10 EST

This article elucidates the "90-day rule" within Chapter 7 bankruptcy, formally known as the preference period, which grants a bankruptcy trustee the authority to scrutinize and potentially reverse specific payments made to creditors within 90 days prior to filing. Any payment exceeding $600 to a creditor during this timeframe may be classified as a "preferential transfer," allowing the trustee to reclaim and equitably redistribute these funds among all creditors. While routine, ordinary course payments are typically exempt, unusual large sums face scrutiny. The rule's lookback period extends to one year for "insiders" such as family members. This article stresses the rule's critical importance for borrowers to prevent complications and potential accusations of fraud during the bankruptcy process. (not investment advice)
