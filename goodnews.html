2026-03-25 16:12:31 EDT

The United Nations General Assembly has voted to recognize the enslavement of Africans during the transatlantic slave trade as "the gravest crime against humanity," a significant move spearheaded by Ghana. The resolution encourages UN member states to consider offering apologies for the slave trade and contributing to a reparations fund. It passed with 123 votes in favor, while the United States, Israel, and Argentina voted against, and 52 countries, including the United Kingdom and EU member states, abstained. Although not legally binding, this resolution holds considerable moral authority, aiming to address the persistent racial inequalities and underdevelopment that continue to affect people of African descent globally.
