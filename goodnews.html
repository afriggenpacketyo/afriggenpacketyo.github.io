Examining the Barriers That Limit Both Deceased and Living Organ Donations in the U.S.

2025-09-05 06:00:00 EDT

Despite widespread support for organ donation in the United States, a significant gap persists between the number of available organs and the over 100,000 people on transplant waiting lists. For deceased donations, the primary bottleneck is medical, as only about three in 1,000 people die in circumstances that keep organs viable for transplant. For living donations, the hurdles are both psychological and practical; potential donors face significant out-of-pocket costs for lost wages and travel, and the process of helping a stranger is a major psychological leap. Mistrust in the healthcare system and a simple lack of awareness about living donation further contribute to the shortage, which results in 13 deaths per day.
