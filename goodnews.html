Man's Self-Experimentation Leads to 'Unparalleled' Snake Antivenom

2025-05-03 02:29:15 EDT

Scientists have developed an "unparalleled" antivenom using antibodies from a man who deliberately injected himself with snake venom for nearly two decades. Tim Friede's blood contains antibodies that protect against fatal doses from a wide range of venomous snakes. The antivenom cocktail, tested on mice, showed significant protection against deadly neurotoxins. Friede's motivation was to develop better therapies for snakebite victims, leading to the discovery of broadly neutralizing antibodies. The research is a significant step towards creating a universal antivenom, potentially saving thousands of lives each year.
