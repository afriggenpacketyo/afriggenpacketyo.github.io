2025-09-07 19:13:39 EDT

Stargazers across vast regions of Asia, alongside parts of Europe and Africa, were treated to the awe-inspiring spectacle of a "Blood Moon" on Sunday night during a total lunar eclipse. This captivating celestial event unfolds when the sun, Earth, and moon achieve perfect alignment, causing the Earth's shadow to bathe the lunar surface in an eerie, deep red glow. The distinctive crimson color results from sunlight reflecting and scattering through our planet's atmosphere, where shorter blue wavelengths are more readily dispersed than the longer red ones. While Asia enjoyed the prime viewing, parts of Europe and Africa observed a partial eclipse as the moon ascended, making it a globally significant astronomical event.
