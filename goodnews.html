While the number was initially arbitrary, subsequent research has shown it is a reasonably effective benchmark for health. Studies indicate that health benefits, such as reduced cardiovascular risk, can begin at lower step counts, around 2,500 to 3,000 per day, and may plateau near 7,500 steps. Experts agree that while 10,000 is a good target, the most important thing is to be more active than you currently are.
