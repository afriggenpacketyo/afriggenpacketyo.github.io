Japanese Superstitions Create Challenges for Selling or Renting 'Stigmatized Properties'

2026-07-18 03:32:00 EDT

In Japan, deep-seated cultural superstitions significantly impact the real estate market, making "jiko bukken," or stigmatized properties where unfortunate events like suicides, fires claiming lives, or "lonely deaths" occurred, extremely difficult to sell or rent. Japanese people often perceive death as impure, leading to widespread reluctance in occupying such homes, even when they are perfectly habitable. Estate agents are legally obligated to disclose a property's history, further complicating sales and often necessitating significant rent reductions. Specialist companies now offer unique services like "ghost investigations" and Shinto priest blessings to "cleanse" these properties, attempting to alleviate fears and make them more appealing to potential tenants and buyers.

Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A team of Chinese researchers has published significant findings in the peer-reviewed journal Science Advances, detailing how birds gradually shed their dinosaur tails, evolving the flight-adapted body plan seen today. Unlike most non-avian dinosaurs, modern birds possess a pygostyle, a short skeletal structure formed by the fusion of the final few caudal or tail vertebrae, which acts as a crucial anchor for tail feathers and muscles vital for flight. This discovery sheds new light on one of the most profound morphological changes in terrestrial vertebrate history. The article highlights how this research addresses the previous difficulty in studying this transformation due to the exceeding rarity of early-diverging bird and birdlike dinosaur fossils.
