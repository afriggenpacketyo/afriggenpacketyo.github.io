Study Links Inhaler Propellants to Significant Carbon Emissions

A new study published in the Journal of the American Medical Association reveals that inhalers, particularly metered-dose inhalers, contribute significantly to greenhouse gas emissions, thereby exacerbating climate change and potentially worsening respiratory conditions. Over a decade, from 2014 to 2024, these devices generated an estimated 24.9 million metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent emissions in the U.S., a figure comparable to the annual emissions of approximately 530,000 gas-powered cars. The core issue lies with the hydrofluoroalkane propellants used in these inhalers, not the medication itself. Researchers emphasize a "tremendous opportunity" to mitigate this carbon footprint by transitioning to lower-emission alternatives, thereby safeguarding both patients' health and the planet from deteriorating air quality.
