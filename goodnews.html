The Dutch government has announced it will return over 28,000 fossils, including the historic "Java Man" remains, to Indonesia. These fossils, discovered in 1891 by Dutch anthropologist Eugene Dubois on the island of Java, were the first known evidence of Homo erectus, a key ancestor of modern humans. The decision follows a recommendation from a committee which concluded the items were likely removed against the will of the local population during the Dutch colonial era. This act of repatriation is part of a broader effort by the Netherlands to reckon with its colonial past and return culturally significant artifacts to their countries of origin.
