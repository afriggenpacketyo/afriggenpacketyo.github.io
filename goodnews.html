United Airlines Flight Attendants Ratify New Contract Including 31% Wage Increases

2026-05-12 12:58:05 EDT

United Airlines flight attendants have overwhelmingly approved a new five-year labor contract, securing average 31% raises to base pay by August. This agreement, ratified by 82% of voting members, is the first raise for the airline's 30,000 flight attendants in nearly six years. The contract also introduces boarding pay, a significant improvement as airlines traditionally started pay only after the aircraft door closed. Additional benefits include $741 million in back pay and quality-of-life enhancements, such as restrictions on red-eye flights and "sit pay" during disruptions.
