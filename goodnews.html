Deconstruction of Buildings as a Climate-Friendly Solution

2025-06-13 06:00:00 EDT

A growing movement in the construction industry promotes deconstruction, or taking apart buildings piece by piece, as a method to reuse materials, reduce landfill waste, and minimize the carbon footprint of construction. Deconstruction creates a circular economy by harvesting lumber and other materials from old buildings instead of sourcing new ones. While deconstruction can be more costly and time-consuming, some local governments are incentivizing or requiring it to meet waste reduction goals. The future of climate-conscious construction involves planning for disassembly before a building is even built, thus ensuring materials can be reused. The practice can limit the climate impact of our built environment, which accounts for nearly 40% of global greenhouse gas emissions, so it is important that the practice continues to develop and be more widely adopted.
