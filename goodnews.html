The Historical Origins of "Soccer" in the United States and Canada Explored

2026-06-13 11:33:54 EDT

The article explores why "football" is commonly called "soccer" in the U.S. and Canada, tracing the term's origins to 19th-century England. It explains that "soccer" derived from "association football" (to distinguish it from rugby football) and was a slang term coined by wealthy Oxford university students. The word traveled globally as the sport spread, becoming prevalent in countries like Australia, New Zealand, and South Africa, as well as North America. The article notes that American football, which evolved from rugby with elements of soccer, also gained popularity around the same time, leading to the continued use of "soccer" to differentiate the sports.
