Chinese Scientists Develop Gene-Edited Tomato with Popcorn Aroma

2026-02-23 02:06:38 EST

Researchers in China have successfully engineered a gene-edited tomato that surprisingly emits the distinct aroma of buttered popcorn. This scientific breakthrough was achieved by simultaneously silencing two specific genes responsible for suppressing aromatic compounds within the tomato plant. The innovative approach was inspired by the popular fragrant rice, which naturally carries a similar scent and is known to command a higher market price. This research, led by Xu Shengchun of the Biotechnology Research Institute at Xianghu Laboratory, was published in the Journal of Integrative Agriculture, marking a novel direction in agricultural biotechnology that explores creating new flavor and aroma profiles rather than solely focusing on restoring traditional ones.

Magnitude 3.5 Earthquake Strikes Off Catalina Island

2026-02-23 01:59:22 EST

A magnitude 3.5 earthquake was reported on Sunday evening, with its epicenter located 13 miles off the coast of Rancho Palos Verdes, in the vicinity of Catalina Island. The U.S. Geological Survey confirmed the seismic event, which occurred at a depth of 6.8 miles. This particular earthquake is the sole one of magnitude 3.0 or greater recorded in the nearby area over the past ten days. On average, the Greater Los Angeles region typically experiences about five earthquakes with magnitudes ranging from 3.0 to 4.0 each year, according to recent three-year data. Residents who felt the tremor were encouraged to report their observations to the USGS.

Pilgrims and visitors are forming long lines to view the saint's bones, which were ceremoniously transferred from their centuries-old stone sarcophagus to a plexiglass case in the lower church. This rare exhibition allows direct public interaction, with visitors permitted to touch the outer case, and coincides with the reinstatement of St. Francis' feast day as a public holiday.
