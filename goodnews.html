Understanding Home Equity Requirements for a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-06 15:11:59 EDT

For seniors considering a reverse mortgage to supplement retirement income, lenders typically require homeowners to have at least 50% to 60% equity in their property. This means the outstanding mortgage balance should not exceed about half of the home's appraised value. While the FHA, which backs the most common type of reverse mortgage, does not set a specific percentage, this range is the industry standard. The actual amount a senior can borrow is further determined by their age, current interest rates, and federal lending limits, not just their total equity. Homeowners who fall short of the equity requirement may need to pay down their mortgage or consider other options. (not investment advice)
