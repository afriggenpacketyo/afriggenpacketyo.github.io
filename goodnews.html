2025-11-20 14:03:26 EST

Lenders not only assess the individual borrower's financial standing but also scrutinize the entire condominium association's health. Key factors include the homeowners association's financial reserves, the percentage of owner-occupied units versus rentals, and whether the association is involved in any active litigation. Lenders may also impose stricter loan-to-value limits for condos, potentially capping borrowing at 70% to 80% of the property's value, making thorough research and comparison of lender requirements essential. Guidance on Handling Debts That Have Been Sold to Collection Agencies

2025-11-20 13:29:11 EST

When a lender sells a delinquent debt to a collection agency, the borrower's legal obligation to pay typically remains intact, transferring from the original creditor to the new owner. Under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, consumers have the right to request written validation of the debt to confirm its legitimacy and amount. It is also crucial to check the statute of limitations in your state, as collectors cannot sue for time-barred debts. If the debt is valid but unaffordable, options include negotiating a settlement, working with a credit counseling agency on a management plan, or considering professional debt relief services.
