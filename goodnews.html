Understanding Spousal Responsibility for Credit Card Debt After Death

2026-07-01 11:47:32 EDT

This article clarifies whether a surviving spouse automatically inherits credit card debt upon their partner's death. Generally, debt is paid from the deceased's estate, not automatically transferred to the spouse. However, significant exceptions exist, such as joint accounts where both spouses are equally responsible, or in community property states where certain debts incurred during marriage are considered jointly owned. Co-signed or guaranteed debts also remain the survivor's obligation. The article advises understanding legal obligations, reviewing account documents, and exploring repayment or debt relief options, especially if the estate is insolvent. (not investment advice)
