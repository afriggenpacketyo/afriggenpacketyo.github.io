2026-03-19 11:14:12 EDT

This article outlines essential wage garnishment rules, emphasizing the balance between creditor rights and consumer protections in the current economic climate. Federal law generally caps garnishment at 25% of disposable earnings or the amount exceeding 30 times the federal minimum wage, though state laws can offer further protections. A court judgment is typically required for consumer debts, but exceptions exist for federal student loans, taxes, and child support, which may also have higher garnishment limits. The article stresses that certain income types, like Social Security, are protected, and borrowers retain rights to notice and to challenge garnishment orders. (not investment advice)
