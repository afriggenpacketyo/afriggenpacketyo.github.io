As the youngest Red Tail fighter pilot, he flew 21 combat missions over Europe during World War II after joining the U.S. Army Air Forces in 1944. He went on to serve with distinction in both the Korean and Vietnam Wars, flying a combined 115 combat missions. The Tuskegee Airmen were the first Black military pilots in the U.S. armed forces, celebrated for their courage and skill in the face of systemic racism. Hardy's passing leaves only 13 documented original members of the historic group still alive.
