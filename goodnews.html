Drones Drop Live Mosquitoes in Hawaii to Save Endangered Birds

2025-06-16 07:30:00 EDT

In Hawaii, scientists are using drones to drop containers of live mosquitoes in remote forests to combat avian malaria and save endangered honeycreepers. The mosquitoes are male and contain a strain of bacteria called wolbachia, which prevents their eggs from hatching when they mate with local females. The goal is to reduce the population of malaria-carrying mosquitoes without harming the local ecosystem, as mosquitoes are not native to Hawaii. This approach, known as the incompatible insect technique (IIT), has shown promise in other parts of the world for controlling mosquito populations and stemming the spread of human diseases.
