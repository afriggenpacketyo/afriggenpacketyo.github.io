Decades-Old "85th Percentile" Rule for Setting U.S. Speed Limits Faces Reevaluation

2025-08-17 00:00:53 EDT

A long-standing principle known as the "85th percentile rule," which often dictates that speed limits should match the speed of the fastest 15% of drivers, is being reconsidered across the United States. Critics argue this method creates a dangerous feedback loop where speeding leads to higher official limits, especially in urban areas with pedestrians. In response to rising road fatalities, the Federal Highway Administration has clarified that other factors like road use and crash frequency should be considered. Some states and cities are now adopting new models or lowering residential speed limits to prioritize safety over traffic flow.

China Explores "Scissor Wing" Design for Hypersonic Drone Carrier

2025-08-16 22:00:14 EDT

Chinese researchers are developing a hypersonic drone carrier concept featuring an innovative "scissor wing" or oblique wing design, a concept once explored by NASA. The design involves a single, centrally mounted wing that rotates up to 90 degrees to optimize performance across different speeds. At low speeds, the wing is perpendicular to the fuselage for maximum lift, while at hypersonic speeds like Mach 5, it aligns parallel to the fuselage, turning the entire aircraft into a "waverider" for high efficiency. This adaptable design aims to overcome the traditional compromises between low-speed stability and high-speed drag.
