Common mistakes include waiting until the last minute in December, which can lead to rushed and poor decisions, and overlooking forgotten accounts like old 401(k)s. The penalty for failing to take the full RMD is a steep 25% of the shortfall. Experts also note that many retirees underutilize qualified charitable distributions (QCDs), a strategy that allows direct tax-free transfers from an IRA to a charity, satisfying the RMD requirement while reducing taxable income.
