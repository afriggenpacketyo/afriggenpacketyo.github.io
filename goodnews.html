The Historical Roots of Tipping in the United States Explained

2025-07-27 07:30:00 EDT

The practice of tipping in the U.S. has its origins in the post-Civil War era, where it was used as a way for employers, particularly in the restaurant and service industries, to avoid paying wages to formerly enslaved Black workers. This system was later codified with the creation of a lower "tipped minimum wage," currently $2.13 per hour at the federal level, with the expectation that customer tips make up the difference. This article explains how powerful lobbying from groups like the National Restaurant Association has preserved this system, effectively shifting the responsibility of paying wages from employers to consumers.
