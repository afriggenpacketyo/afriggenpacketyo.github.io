New AI Model Predicts Future Health Risks from Sleep Data

2026-01-11 04:42:00 EST

A new artificial intelligence model, SleepFM, can estimate a person's risk for approximately 130 diseases, including Parkinson's, dementia, and various cancers, years before symptoms appear, based on just one night's sleep data. Developed by Stanford University researchers, SleepFM was trained on nearly 600,000 hours of polysomnography data from 65,000 sleepers, correlating sleep patterns with 25 years of electronic health records. The model identifies discrepancies between brain and heart signals during sleep, which may indicate hidden physical stresses. While promising for early detection, researchers emphasize that AI identifies correlations, not causes, and requires medical interpretation, also noting data limitations from diverse populations.
