Study Finds Ants Alter Nest Architecture to Mitigate Disease Spread

2025-10-21 07:13:58 EDT

New research reveals that black garden ants actively redesign their nests to reduce the transmission of a lethal fungus, a concept termed "architectural immunity." Scientists observed that when exposed to the pathogen, ant colonies built more compartmentalized nests with fewer interconnections and more distant entrances. These structural changes, combined with social distancing behaviors like self-isolation by infected ants, create an effective defense system. The findings, published in the journal *Science*, demonstrate a sophisticated form of social immunity and suggest that principles of disease-mitigating design could offer insights for human public spaces.

2025-10-21 06:02:35 EDT

The population of the critically endangered North Atlantic right whale has increased slightly, reaching an estimated 384 individuals, according to a new report from the North Atlantic Right Whale Consortium. This marks the fourth year of slow but steady growth for the species, which had seen its numbers decline by about 25% in the previous decade due to ship strikes and fishing gear entanglement. Scientists credit conservation measures, particularly in Canada, for the positive trend.
