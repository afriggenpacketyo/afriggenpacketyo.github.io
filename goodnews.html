The Evolving Symbolism of the Statue of Liberty from Abolition to Immigration

2025-07-06 09:19:19 EDT

The Statue of Liberty was originally conceived in 1865 to celebrate the end of slavery in the United States, symbolized by the broken chains at her feet, not as a beacon for immigration. It was poet Emma Lazarus's 1883 sonnet, "The New Colossus," that reframed the statue as a "mother of exiles" for fundraising purposes. This new meaning solidified with the opening of Ellis Island in 1892 and was later cemented by President John F. Kennedy's "A Nation of Immigrants," transforming the monument into a powerful, enduring symbol of America's promise to newcomers.
