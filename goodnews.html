New Review Finds No Increased Risk of Autism or ADHD from Acetaminophen Use in Pregnancy

2026-01-16 18:36:40 EST

A comprehensive new meta-analysis, published in The Lancet Obstetrics, Gynecology & Women's Health, concludes that using acetaminophen (Tylenol) as directed during pregnancy does not increase a child's risk of autism, ADHD, or intellectual disability. This review, which included approximately 60 studies and focused on sibling-comparison designs, provides the strongest evidence to date, reassuring pregnant individuals and aligning with existing medical guidance. The findings challenge previous observational studies that suggested a possible link, attributing those associations to underlying conditions like fever or pain rather than the medication itself. Medical experts emphasize that untreated fever or pain can pose greater risks to both the pregnant person and the fetus.
