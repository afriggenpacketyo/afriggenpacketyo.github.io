Analysis Explores Why American Bread Differs in Quality from European Counterparts

2025-10-20 08:00:00 EDT

This article investigates the common perception that bread in Europe, particularly France, tastes better than mass-produced bread in the United States. The primary difference lies in production methods and regulations; a 1993 French law mandates that traditional baguettes sold in a boulangerie contain only four basic ingredients and be made fresh daily. In contrast, American industrial bread often contains over 30 ingredients, including dough conditioners and preservatives, to facilitate rapid, large-scale production. This emphasis on speed and convenience in the U.S. sacrifices the time needed for fermentation, which is crucial for developing the complex flavors and aromas found in artisanal bread.
