Experts Question Health Claims of Magnesium Lotion for Sleep

A social media trend promoting magnesium lotion as a sleep aid and remedy for various ailments is gaining popularity, but scientific evidence to support these claims is lacking. Dermatologists point out that the skin is a barrier, not a sponge, and there is little proof that magnesium can be effectively absorbed topically. Any perceived benefits are more likely attributable to the act of massage or the moisturizing properties of the lotion itself, creating a powerful placebo effect. While sleep experts agree that using the lotion is likely harmless, they do not recommend it, as supplements are unregulated and proven medical treatments exist for conditions like restless legs syndrome.
