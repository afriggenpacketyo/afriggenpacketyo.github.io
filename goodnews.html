Explainer: The Science Behind the Urban Heat Island Effect

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As a heatwave impacts the UK, this article explains the "urban heat island effect," the phenomenon causing cities to be significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. The primary cause is the prevalence of dark, hard surfaces like asphalt and concrete, which absorb and retain the sun's heat, releasing it slowly and elevating temperatures, especially overnight. In contrast, rural landscapes with more vegetation and water benefit from natural cooling through evaporation. With global temperatures rising, this effect makes growing urban populations increasingly vulnerable to the dangers of extreme heat.
