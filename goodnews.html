Explaining California's Lengthy Vote Counting Process in Recent Elections

2026-06-04 19:38:00 EDT

California's extensive vote-counting process, which often extends for days or even weeks after Election Day, is attributed to the state's rigorous commitment to ensuring every voter's voice is heard, especially given the millions of mail-in ballots cast. State law permits ballots postmarked on or before Election Day to be counted if received by the county within a week, inherently contributing to these delays. Robust election security measures, including a meticulous signature matching process involving both machine and manual verification, alongside a "ballot curing" mechanism for rejected ballots, further prolong the timeline. Experts highlight that ultra-competitive races, such as the recent gubernatorial and Los Angeles mayoral elections, exacerbate the waiting period as late-arriving mail-in ballots frequently determine the final outcomes.
