Measles Vaccination Rates Decline in Most U.S. Counties, Study Reveals

2025-06-02 19:01:11 EDT

A recent study indicates a widespread decline in childhood measles vaccination rates across the United States since before the COVID-19 pandemic, with significant variations within states. The MMR vaccination rate decreased from 93.92% in the 2017-2018 school year to 91.26% in the 2023-2024 school year across 33 states, with 78% of the 2,066 counties studied experiencing a decline. The study highlights the vulnerability of certain areas to measles outbreaks, pointing to Gaines County in West Texas, the epicenter of a recent outbreak, with a vaccination rate just over 80%. The CDC reports that 96% of U.S. measles cases are among those unvaccinated or with unknown vaccination status, underscoring the importance of herd immunity, which requires over 95% community vaccination.
