Fact Check Debunks Misleading European Sexual Assault Statistics Comparisons

2025-10-24 11:30:00 EDT

A viral chart claiming the UK is Europe's "sexual assault capital" due to high reported rates is misleading, as confirmed by a DW fact check. The article explains that significant differences in legal definitions, reporting practices, and cultural factors across countries make direct comparisons problematic. For instance, the UK's consent-based laws and counting multiple offenses per incident can inflate its figures compared to nations with narrower definitions. Criminologists recommend victim surveys, rather than police report statistics, as a more accurate method for measuring the true prevalence of sexual assault.
