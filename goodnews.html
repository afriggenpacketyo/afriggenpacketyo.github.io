2025-10-16 02:38:00 EDT

Uruguay's Senate has given final approval to a bill legalizing euthanasia, making it the first country in Latin America to formally pass such a law. The "Dignified Death" bill allows mentally sound adults with terminal, irreversible diseases to choose a medically assisted death administered by a healthcare professional. The legislation, which does not permit assisted suicide, reflects Uruguay's history of socially liberal policies and has strong public support, with over 60% of citizens in favor. The move follows decriminalization efforts in Colombia and Ecuador but sets a new legal precedent for the region.
