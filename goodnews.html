Mount Etna: Five Facts About Europe's Most Active Volcano

2025-06-03 08:21:00 EDT

Mount Etna, located on the island of Sicily, Italy, is Europe's largest and most active volcano, with over 500,000 years of eruptive history. It is a stratovolcano, characterized by steep inclines and multiple vents, and is one of the best-monitored volcanoes globally. While it has been continuously erupting for thousands of years, volcanologists can pinpoint new eruptions multiple times a year, as the volcano is in a state of persistent activity. Mount Etna poses a threat to nearby populations due to debris and dust, with past lava flows reaching the eastern seaboard of Sicily, but the volcanic soil also supports diverse flora and fauna, making it a natural laboratory for ecological studies.
