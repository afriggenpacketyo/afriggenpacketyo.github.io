UK Introduces New Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) System for Visitors

2026-02-24 19:05:49 EST

The UK is implementing a new Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) system from February 25th, requiring most visa-free travelers from 85 countries, including the US and Australia, to obtain digital permission. This initiative aims to streamline immigration and enhance security. An ETA costs £16, is valid for two years, and allows multiple six-month visits. However, the new rules pose challenges for dual nationals, who cannot get an ETA and must instead possess a British passport or a digital certificate of entitlement linked to their second nationality passport to enter the country. These documents can take weeks and incur costs, creating logistical hurdles for some travelers.
