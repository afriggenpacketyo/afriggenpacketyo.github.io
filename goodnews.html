Tapping Home Equity to Pay Off Debt: Weighing Pros and Cons

2026-05-28 17:06:23 EDT

With rising interest rates on credit cards and persistent inflation, many homeowners are considering using their home equity to consolidate debt. This strategy can offer lower interest rates compared to credit cards, potentially saving thousands of dollars and simplifying repayment with a single monthly payment. However, it carries significant risks, as it converts unsecured debt into a secured loan, potentially leading to foreclosure if payments are missed. Variable rates on home equity lines of credit (HELOCs) can also create payment uncertainty. While it can help disciplined borrowers pay off debt faster, it may also encourage further debt accumulation if spending habits remain unchanged. Careful consideration and a solid repayment plan are crucial.
