"Blood Moon" Lunar Eclipse Visible Across Asia, Europe, and Africa Astounds Stargazers

2025-09-07 19:13:39 EDT

Stargazers across Asia, parts of Europe, and Africa witnessed a captivating "blood moon" during a total lunar eclipse. This celestial phenomenon occurs when the sun, Earth, and moon align, causing Earth's shadow to cast an eerie, deep red hue on the moon's surface. The distinctive red color is a result of sunlight reflecting and scattering through Earth's atmosphere, with shorter blue wavelengths dispersing more easily. People in Asia had the best view, while those in Europe and Africa experienced a partial eclipse at moonrise. The Americas, however, missed out on this millennia-old spectacle.
