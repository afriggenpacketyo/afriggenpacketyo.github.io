Historical Context of Brown v. Board of Education and the Dismantling of 'Separate but Equal'

2025-09-21 09:29:59 EDT

The landmark 1954 Supreme Court ruling in Brown v. Board of Education, which declared state-sponsored school segregation unconstitutional, was the culmination of a decades-long legal battle led by the NAACP. The decision overturned the "separate but equal" doctrine established by the 1896 Plessy v. Ferguson case, which had legally sanctioned racial segregation across the United States. Spearheaded by attorney Thurgood Marshall, the NAACP's Legal Defense Fund systematically challenged segregation case by case, arguing it was a system designed to maintain racial hierarchy. While the ruling did not immediately end segregation, it was a monumental step toward racial equality and propelled Marshall to later become the first African American Supreme Court Justice.
