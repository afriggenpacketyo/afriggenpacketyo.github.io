The Historic Introduction of Football's Penalty Shootout in 1970

2026-03-28 02:15:22 EDT

This article recounts the historic introduction of football's first official penalty shootout on August 5, 1970, in a Watney Cup match between Manchester United and Hull City. Prior to this, tied games were decided by replays, drawing lots, or coin tosses, with the 1968 European Championship and Olympic quarter-final providing catalysts for change. The Israeli Football Association proposed the shootout system to FIFA in 1969, leading to its adoption. The article vividly describes the inaugural shootout, including George Best scoring the first penalty and Ian McKechnie becoming the first goalkeeper to save and then miss one, marking a significant shift in how football matches are decided.
