Crystal Palace Goal Disallowed Due to Attacking Player's Proximity to Defensive Wall

2025-08-17 10:06:13 EDT

A stunning free-kick by Crystal Palace's Eberechi Eze against Chelsea was correctly disallowed by VAR due to a little-known rule regarding player positioning. The goal was nullified because a Palace player was standing less than one meter away from Chelsea's defensive wall when the kick was taken. According to IFAB Law 13, when three or more defenders form a wall, all attacking players must maintain at least a one-meter distance until the ball is in play. This rule was introduced in the 2019-20 season to prevent attackers from interfering with the wall, which often caused delays and management issues for referees.
