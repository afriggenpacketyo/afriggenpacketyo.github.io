The '6-month rule' is a critical component of reverse mortgages, dictating the timeline for heirs after the borrower passes away or permanently leaves the home. Lenders must issue a 'Due and Payable' letter, giving heirs six months to either repay the loan balance in full or sell the property to satisfy the debt. This rule primarily applies to federally insured Home Equity Conversion Mortgages (HECMs). While extensions are possible, the tight timeline underscores the need for borrowers to engage in thorough estate planning and communicate clearly with their families to avoid a stressful situation or potential foreclosure on the family home.
