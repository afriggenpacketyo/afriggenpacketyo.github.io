The sterile males will mate with wild females, preventing their eggs from hatching and thus reducing the mosquito population over generations. This method is designed to avoid increasing mosquito bites, as males do not bite humans. The project aims to target the invasive Aedes aegypti species, which transmits diseases like dengue and Zika, without harming native mosquito populations.
