2026-06-10 12:45:00 EDT

This vast necropolis, containing nearly 500 skeletons, supports extensive communities of deep-sea life, including species believed to be new to science. The discovery suggests whale falls act as crucial food sources and evolutionary hotspots on the ocean floor. Researchers were astonished by the scale and depth of the find, which offers new insights into whale evolution and the interconnectedness of deep-sea ecosystems. The area's V-shaped trench likely funnels carcasses to the seafloor.
