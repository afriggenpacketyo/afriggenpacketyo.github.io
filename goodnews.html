Historical Tradition of Turkey Droving to London for Christmas Explored

2025-12-24 00:32:22 EST

This article delves into the historical practice of "turkey droving," where thousands of turkeys were walked from Norfolk and Suffolk farms to London markets for Christmas. Before refrigeration and railways, these birds, some wearing tiny leather boots or having tar-coated feet, embarked on a three-month journey covering about three miles daily. The tradition, which gained popularity among wealthy Victorian families, saw drovers foraging for food along the way. The practice ceased with the advent of steam engines in the late 1800s, transforming how Christmas turkeys reached the capital. The article also highlights the Norfolk Black breed's near extinction and rescue.
