A supermoon occurs when a full moon coincides with its closest approach to Earth, known as the perigee, making it appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than other full moons. This year's Harvest Moon is the full moon that falls closest to the autumn equinox, traditionally providing extra light for farmers. Skywatchers can look forward to two more supermoons this year, occurring on November 5th and December 4th, offering more opportunities to observe the amplified lunar spectacle.
