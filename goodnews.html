Consumers Retain Right to Dispute Debts Even After Sale to Collection Agencies

2025-12-19 11:41:32 EST

Consumers retain the legal right to dispute a debt, even if it has been sold to a collection agency, thanks to protections under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA). This right is crucial because debt sales often involve incomplete or inaccurate records, making verification essential. Upon initial contact, collection agencies must provide a validation notice, granting a 30-day window to send a written dispute. If the agency cannot validate the debt with original documentation, they are prohibited from further collection efforts or reporting to credit bureaus, effectively making the debt uncollectible. For valid but overwhelming debts, options like debt settlement or credit counseling can provide a path forward. (not investment advice)
