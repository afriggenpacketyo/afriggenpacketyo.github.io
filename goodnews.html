California Enacts Major Housing Reform to Streamline Urban Development

2025-07-01 18:37:38 EDT

California has passed significant reforms to the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) in an effort to boost housing construction. The new legislation, championed by Governor Gavin Newsom, largely waives CEQA requirements for most low- and mid-rise housing projects in urban areas zoned for multifamily use. This change aims to eliminate lengthy environmental studies and the threat of lawsuits that have historically delayed or blocked new developments. While other obstacles like interest rates and labor costs remain, the reform removes a key tool that groups have used for decades to stall housing projects, signaling a major policy shift.
