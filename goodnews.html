Fossil Discovery in China Suggests Ancient Marine Animals Practiced Social Distancing

2025-07-30 00:39:15 EDT

Chinese paleontologists have discovered that ancient, clam-like marine animals known as "lamp shells" may have practiced a form of social distancing to enhance their survival. A 436-million-year-old fossil find in Guizhou province revealed that the species Nucleospira calypta arranged themselves in a regular, checkerboard-like pattern on the sea floor. Researchers believe the creatures used hundreds of fine, bristle-like structures to maintain optimal spacing from one another. This arrangement likely improved their ability to filter feed without interference from their neighbors.
