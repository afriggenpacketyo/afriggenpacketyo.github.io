Helsinki Achieves Zero Traffic Fatalities Through Smart City Planning

2025-08-18 03:29:00 EDT

Helsinki, Finland, achieved the remarkable feat of zero traffic fatalities last year, making it one of the largest cities globally to do so and aligning with the European Union's "Vision Zero" program. This success is attributed to key measures such as reducing speed limits to 30 kmph on over half of its streets, including around schools, and implementing a comprehensive Traffic Safety Development Programme. The city utilizes crash and driving speed data, along with resident feedback, to identify dangerous areas, improve infrastructure, coordinate with police for speed enforcement, and prioritize public transport, demonstrating how data-driven planning can significantly enhance urban road safety.
