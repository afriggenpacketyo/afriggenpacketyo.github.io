Chinese Researchers Develop Infrared Contact Lens for Night Vision

2025-06-01 08:43:00 EDT

Researchers in China have created an infrared contact lens that enables humans to see in the dark by converting infrared light into visible light. The lens combines conventional soft contact lenses with nanoparticles made of gold, sodium gadolinium fluoride, ytterbium, and erbium ions. These upconversion contact lenses (UCLs) convert infrared light with wavelengths between 800 and 1,600 nanometers into visible light, but the resulting images are somewhat blurred. While not as powerful as night vision goggles, the lenses could be used in surgical procedures, cryptography, or counterfeit protection, and tests showed humans could recognize patterns and letters in the dark.
