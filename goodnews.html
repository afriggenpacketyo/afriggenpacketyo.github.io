Physiological Changes Explain Why Alcohol Tolerance Decreases with Age

2025-12-08 05:00:00 EST

Health experts explain that physiological changes are the reason why alcohol often has a stronger effect as people age. As individuals get older, liver enzymes that metabolize alcohol become less efficient, and muscle mass, which stores water that dilutes alcohol, is replaced by fatty tissue. This results in a higher blood alcohol concentration from the same amount of alcohol, with effects lasting longer. For women in perimenopause, alcohol can also exacerbate symptoms like mood swings and sleep disruption. Doctors advise that cutting back on alcohol is beneficial, as the health risks, including cancer and cognitive decline, increase with age.
