Rights to Dispute Debt Remain Even After Sale to Collection Agency

Consumers retain the right to dispute a debt even after it has been sold to a collection agency, thanks to explicit protections under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA). Upon first contact, collection agencies are legally required to send a validation notice, providing borrowers 30 days to send a written dispute and demand proof of the debt's validity. If the agency cannot provide adequate documentation, it is prohibited from continuing collection efforts or reporting the debt to credit bureaus, potentially rendering it uncollectible.
