Exploring the History and Controversies of Daylight Saving Time

2026-03-27 09:32:00 EDT

As most of Europe prepares to observe Daylight Saving Time (DST), the article delves into its history, folklore, and ongoing debates. DST, observed in about a third of countries, involves shifting clocks forward in spring and back in autumn, primarily in temperate regions to maximize evening daylight. The concept was first mooted by a New Zealander in 1895 and later campaigned for by a British builder, but it was Germany that first officially implemented it during World War I to conserve coal. Despite popular myths, farmers have historically opposed DST due to disruptions. Public opinion is shifting, with many Americans preferring its abolition or permanent implementation due to its disruptive effects.
