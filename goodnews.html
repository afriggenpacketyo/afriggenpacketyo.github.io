Physicists Apply Fluid Mechanics to Everyday Kitchen Conundrums, Optimizing Liquid Drainage

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Physicists at Brown University, Thomas Dutta and Jay Tang, delved into everyday kitchen questions, using fluid mechanics to determine optimal drainage times for various liquids. Their research, published in Physics of Fluids, employed Navier-Stokes equations and practical experiments, revealing that low-viscosity liquids like water drain in seconds and milk in about 30 seconds. More viscous liquids, such as olive oil, require over nine minutes, while cold maple syrup can take hours. The study also found that a wok needs approximately 15 minutes for 90% of its residual water to collect, demonstrating the scientific principles behind common household observations.
