Google's Debug Program Plans to Release Sterile Mosquitoes to Combat Disease

2026-06-03 09:26:00 EDT

Scientists at Google's Debug program are planning to release millions of male mosquitoes infected with Wolbachia bacteria in Florida and California to combat disease-spreading Aedes aegypti mosquitoes. These sterile males will mate with wild females, preventing their eggs from hatching and reducing the overall population without increasing human bites, as males do not bite. The invasive Aedes aegypti species spreads diseases like dengue and Zika, and experts emphasize the ethical necessity of controlling this "very bad mosquito" due to its global health threat. The project is awaiting EPA approval.
