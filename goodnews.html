Expert Advises Early Estate Planning to Prevent Financial and Medical Complications

2026-01-03 10:05:01 EST

Financial planning columnist Beth Pinsker emphasizes the importance of early estate planning, even starting at age 18, to prevent significant financial and medical complications for individuals and their families. She highlights common oversights, such as failing to activate powers of attorney at banks or neglecting wills, which can lead to substantial out-of-pocket costs and legal difficulties for caregivers. Pinsker recommends establishing virtually costless health-care proxies and powers of attorney, easily accessible documents that authorize others to make critical decisions during emergencies. These foundational steps ensure loved ones can provide assistance efficiently and avoid potentially more expensive, after-the-fact interventions, ultimately easing burdens during difficult times. Expert Cautions Against Investing in Top-Performing 2025 ETFs Due to High Risk

2026-01-03 09:15:01 EST

An investment expert advises caution when considering the top-performing ETFs of 2025, stating they often have "very little, if any" role in a long-term portfolio due to their speculative nature. Many of these funds, such as leveraged ETFs, aim to produce 200% to 300% of an index's daily return, resetting each day, and are thus intended for short-term trading, not sustained investment, making them highly volatile. The article also highlights precious metals mining funds as risky, as their performance depends on both volatile metal prices and the underlying, often indebted, businesses.
