Simple 'Thumb Test' Can Be an At-Home Indicator for Aortic Aneurysm Risk

2025-09-27 05:16:02 EDT

A simple self-check known as the "thumb test" may help indicate an increased risk for an aortic aneurysm. The test involves placing your thumb across your flat palm; if the thumb extends beyond the far edge of the palm, it can be a sign of joint laxity associated with connective tissue diseases like Marfan syndrome. These conditions can weaken blood vessel walls, increasing aneurysm risk. Cardiologists caution that the test is not a diagnostic tool, as many people with aneurysms will have a negative test, and a positive result does not confirm an aneurysm is present. However, a positive test, especially when combined with other risk factors, should prompt a conversation with a doctor about further evaluation.
