New Law Makes Tailgating at Football Matches a Criminal Offence in England and Wales

2026-03-19 20:01:16 EDT

A new law in England and Wales will criminalize "tailgating" – entering football matches without a ticket by following legitimate ticket-holders – effective before Sunday's Carabao Cup final. Offenders face up to a five-year football banning order and a £1,000 fine. This legislation stems from the severe disorder at the Euro 2020 final at Wembley in 2021, where thousands forced entry, prompting a report that called for stronger sanctions. The Unauthorised Entry to Football Matches Act also targets fraudulent ticket use, aiming to enhance safety and prevent similar chaos, providing police with new tools to enforce stadium security.
