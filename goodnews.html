If a candidate receives over 50% of first-choice votes, they win outright; otherwise, the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated, and subsequent rounds count voters' second-choice votes. This system has led to unique alliances, such as cross-endorsements between progressive candidates Zohran Mamdani and Brad Lander, aimed at preventing former Governor Andrew Cuomo from winning.
