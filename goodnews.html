Understanding Protections and Exceptions for Social Security Disability Benefits from Debt Garnishment

2026-05-21 14:02:25 EDT

Social Security disability benefits are generally protected from garnishment by private creditors like credit card companies, thanks to federal law. Banks are also required to shield up to two months' worth of directly deposited benefits from garnishment orders. However, these protections do not extend to certain government debts, including federal tax debt, defaulted federal student loans, child support, alimony, or restitution orders. Mixing protected disability income with other funds in a bank account can also complicate matters, potentially leading to temporary freezes. Debt relief options like settlement or bankruptcy are available for those struggling.
