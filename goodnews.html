2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

This article explains the function of the International Court of Justice (ICJ), the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, located in The Hague. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, the court adjudicates disputes between states, distinct from the International Criminal Court which tries individuals. Its jurisdiction depends on states' consent, either through a blanket declaration or specific agreements, such as the UN Genocide Convention, which underpins cases like Ukraine v. Russia and South Africa v. Israel. However, the ICJ lacks an enforcement mechanism, relying on the cooperation of the involved parties for its rulings to be effective.
