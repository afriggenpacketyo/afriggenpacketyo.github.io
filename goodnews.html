2025-08-04 13:16:00 EDT

Researchers have finally identified the cause of a mysterious wasting disease that has killed over five billion sea stars along North America's Pacific coast since 2013. The culprit is a bacterium, Vibrio pectenicida, which was found in the bodily fluid of infected sea stars, causing them to develop lesions and disintegrate. The epidemic has devastated more than 20 species, with the sunflower sea star population declining by about 90%, leading to its classification as critically endangered. This discovery is crucial for developing conservation strategies, as the loss of these predators has allowed sea urchin populations to explode, destroying vital kelp forests.
