2025-09-07 04:56:28 EDT

A total lunar eclipse is set to occur, creating a "Blood Moon" spectacle visible to sky-watchers in many parts of the United Kingdom. The Moon will pass through Earth's shadow, taking on a reddish hue due to a phenomenon known as Rayleigh scattering. While the full eclipse lasts for over an hour, visibility in the UK will be limited to the period after moonrise, with eastern regions getting the best view. Astronomers advise finding a clear horizon to observe the event, which is the last total lunar eclipse visible from the UK until August 2026.
