Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Continuous Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is marking a major scientific milestone: 230 years of continuous, unbroken daily weather observation. This handwritten data sequence, which began on July 14, 1795, is the longest of its kind in the UK and Ireland. Unlike modern automated stations, the observatory maintains the tradition of a human observer manually recording temperature and air pressure. This extensive historical record provides invaluable insights into long-term climate patterns and has documented major weather events, contributing significantly to the science of meteorology over more than two centuries.
