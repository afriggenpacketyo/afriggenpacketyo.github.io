The UK government has announced it will ban the use of non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) to silence victims of workplace sexual misconduct, harassment, or discrimination. An amendment to the Employment Rights Bill will void confidentiality clauses that seek to prevent workers from speaking out about such allegations. The move follows years of campaigning by activists, including Harvey Weinstein's former assistant Zelda Perkins, who argued NDAs have been abused to cover up criminality and protect powerful individuals. Second Nationwide Test of UK's Emergency Alert System Scheduled for September

2025-07-07 17:30:45 EDT

The UK government has announced it will conduct a second nationwide test of its emergency alert system for mobile phones on September 7. The system, designed to warn the public of imminent dangers like extreme weather or terror attacks, will cause compatible phones to vibrate and sound a siren for 10 seconds. A previous national test in 2023 experienced technical issues, with some users on the Three network and others not receiving the alert at all. The upcoming test aims to ensure these problems have been resolved and to familiarize the public with the system, which has been used successfully in several regional emergencies.
