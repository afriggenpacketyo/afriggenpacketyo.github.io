England to Implement Newborn Screening for Spinal Muscular Atrophy

2026-07-15 20:20:52 EDT

All newborn babies in England will now be tested for Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA) as part of a significant study, marking a major step forward in early detection. This announcement is a profound victory for former Little Mix singer Jesy Nelson, who has tirelessly campaigned for the screening since her twin daughters were diagnosed with the severe genetic condition. SMA causes progressive muscle weakness, impacting essential functions like movement, breathing, and swallowing, and can be fatal in its most severe forms before the age of two. Pioneering gene therapies exist that can correct the genetic defect, but they must be administered before symptoms manifest to prevent irreversible damage, underscoring the critical importance of this new universal newborn testing.
