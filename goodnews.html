Aviation Pioneer Wally Funk, Oldest Woman in Space, Dies at 87

2026-07-09 12:45:22 EDT

Wally Funk, a pioneering North Texas aviator and the oldest woman to travel to space, has died peacefully at 87, surrounded by loved ones. Celebrated for over seven decades in aviation, Funk was a significant barrier-breaker, notably as one of the elite women in the 1961 Mercury 13 program, where she successfully completed rigorous testing and outscored many male counterparts. Despite being denied astronaut status at the time, she blazed her own trail, becoming the first female flight instructor at Fort Sill, first female FAA inspector, and first female NTSB air safety investigator. Her lifelong dream of space travel was finally realized in 2021 at age 82 with Blue Origin, making her the oldest woman in space and a Guinness World Record holder.
