Study Reveals Pigeons Use Magnetic Sensors in Livers for Navigation

2026-05-29 07:49:00 EDT

A new study by researchers from the University of Bonn and the Max Planck Institute of Animal Behavior suggests that pigeons' ability to navigate using magnetic fields, known as magnetoreception, is partly facilitated by superparamagnetic macrophages in their livers. These immune cells, which accumulate iron, appear to transmit magnetic information to the brain. When these macrophages were depleted, pigeons lost their magnetic orientation capabilities under overcast conditions, though their visual navigation remained intact. This discovery offers a previously unknown mechanism for magnetic perception in animals, building on millennia of human observation of pigeons' homing skills.
