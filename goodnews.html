Boston Fed Study: Credit Card APRs Significantly Impact Consumer Spending

2026-03-31 08:51:03 EDT

A new study by the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston indicates that credit card Annual Percentage Rates (APRs) have a substantial "economically meaningful" impact on consumer spending. The research found that a 1 percentage point increase in APR leads to an approximate 9% drop in credit card spending the following month, particularly among financially constrained consumers. This suggests that cardholders are responsive to borrowing costs and adjust their spending habits accordingly, even when carrying a balance. The findings challenge the notion that consumers are unaware of interest rates and highlight a potential 'K-shaped' economic trend where lower- and middle-income households cut back on spending.
