The luxury steamer, carrying 53 passengers and crew along with cargo, encountered an uncontrollable leak and storm in 1872, leading to its sinking and the loss of eight lives. Ehorn, 80, used a clue from another wreck hunter to locate the vessel about 20 miles offshore Wisconsin. The ship's hull remains intact with oak interiors preserved, though its upper cabins are gone. This marks Ehorn's 15th discovery, highlighting the ongoing urgency to find wrecks before invasive mussels destroy them.
