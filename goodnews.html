California Kelp Forest Revived Through Large-Scale Culling of Sea Urchins

2025-08-31 12:00:34 EDT

One of the world's largest and most successful kelp restoration projects has revived a vital marine ecosystem off the coast of Southern California. Over the past 13 years, scientists and divers from The Bay Foundation have smashed nearly 6 million voracious purple sea urchins across 80 acres of ocean floor. The urchin population had exploded due to an imbalance in the ecosystem, creating "barrens" where kelp could not grow. By culling the urchins, the fast-growing giant kelp has been able to return, restoring critical habitat for over 800 marine species.
