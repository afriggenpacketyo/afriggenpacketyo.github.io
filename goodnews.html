Understanding the Duration and Prevention of Bank Levies

2026-03-16 13:05:37 EDT

This article explains bank levies, a serious debt collection tactic where creditors obtain a court judgment to freeze or seize funds directly from a bank account. A levy is typically a one-time action, but the underlying legal judgment can remain valid for 10 years or more, allowing for multiple levies if debt remains unpaid. Federal and state tax levies operate differently, often continuously. The article outlines strategies to stop or prevent levies, including challenging them in court, negotiating with creditors, using debt settlement programs, or filing for bankruptcy.
