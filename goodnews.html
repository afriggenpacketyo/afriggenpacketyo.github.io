Stephen Hawking's 1971 Black Hole Area Theorem Confirmed by Gravitational Wave Data

2025-09-11 06:00:00 EDT

A decade after the first detection of gravitational waves, scientists have used the advanced capabilities of the LIGO detectors to observationally confirm a key theory proposed by Stephen Hawking in 1971. The theory, known as the black hole area theorem, posits that the surface area of a black hole can never decrease. By analyzing the gravitational waves from a black hole merger detected earlier this year, researchers were able to calculate the surface areas of the initial black holes and the final, larger one, proving the total area increased as Hawking predicted. The confirmation is a major milestone for physics and a testament to Hawking's enduring legacy.
