Phoenix Firefighters Employ Ice Bags for Rapid Heatstroke Treatment

2025-07-29 20:08:07 EDT

Phoenix firefighters are adopting a remarkably effective, low-tech solution to combat deadly heatstroke: cold water immersion using bags of ice. This emergency protocol involves placing patients suffering from core temperatures of 104 degrees or higher into a bag filled with ice water, rapidly cooling them on the way to the hospital. The method, originally military-developed, cools patients three to five times faster than traditional techniques, significantly preventing brain damage and organ failure. This innovative approach has already led to a decrease in heat-related deaths in Phoenix, setting a national example as heatwaves intensify due to climate change.

Additionally, its high tyramine content may trigger migraine headaches in prone individuals and can dangerously interact with certain depression medications. In rare cases, it may also cause severe allergic reactions for people with grass or ragweed allergies.
