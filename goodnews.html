The Economic Principle Behind Cheaper Computers and Why It May Be Ending

2025-12-04 05:00:00 EST

Despite widespread inflation, the price of computers and other electronics has generally decreased over time, a phenomenon largely explained by Moore's Law. Coined by Intel co-founder Gordon Moore, the law observed that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles approximately every two years, making computing power exponentially cheaper and more powerful. This has allowed devices like laptops to offer more features for less money compared to just five years ago. However, experts warn that Moore's Law is reaching its physical limits, meaning the era of predictably cheaper and more powerful electronics may be coming to an end without new technological breakthroughs.
