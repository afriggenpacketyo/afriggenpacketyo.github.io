Chinese Scientists Make Breakthrough in Observing Quantum Friction

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team in China has become the first to directly observe and measure quantum friction between two solid surfaces, using folded layers of graphene. In a surprising discovery, the scientists found that friction did not always increase with thickness; some thicker folds actually slid more easily. This counterintuitive effect was traced to how the material's bending altered electron movement, locking them into fixed energy levels and reducing the conversion of motion into heat. The findings could pave the way for creating ultra-efficient nanodevices with minimal wear.
