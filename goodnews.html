Researchers Discover Ninth-Century Manuscript with Early Western Musical Notations

2025-10-28 13:14:31 EDT

Researchers in Pennsylvania have identified what may be some of the earliest written musical notes in Western history on a ninth-century Latin sacramentary manuscript. The notations, which consist of characters and dots appearing above the word "alleluia," provide instructions for the rising and falling of a singer's pitch. Previously held in a private collection and overlooked, the discovery is significant as it represents a foundational moment in the development of modern music notation. While earlier forms of written music exist from ancient civilizations, this document is among the first known examples from the Western tradition, potentially predating other famous manuscripts from Laon, France, and St. Gallen, Switzerland.

Analysis: Debt Settlement Offers More Control Than a Credit Card Charge-Off

2025-10-28 13:03:43 EDT

When dealing with overwhelming credit card debt, a charge-off is generally more damaging to a person's credit than a debt settlement. A charge-off occurs when a creditor gives up on collecting a debt after about six months of nonpayment, leaving a severe negative mark on a credit report for seven years. In contrast, debt settlement is a proactive process where the borrower negotiates to pay a reduced portion of the debt, which, while still harmful to credit, demonstrates responsibility. The article suggests that settlement provides a clearer path to financial recovery, as it resolves the debt and allows for credit rebuilding sooner than an unresolved charge-off.
