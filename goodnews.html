2026-01-14 01:29:00 EST

Claudette Colvin, a significant yet often historically overshadowed US civil rights pioneer, has passed away at the age of 86. At just 15, she courageously refused to yield her seat to a white woman on a segregated bus in Montgomery, Alabama, in March 1955, an act that predated Rosa Parks' more widely recognized defiance by several months. Colvin's brave protest led to her brief arrest and later, as one of four Black female plaintiffs, a successful lawsuit challenging bus segregation laws, which had far-reaching implications for public transportation across the US. Despite being ostracized for personal reasons, she received belated recognition, with her 1955 arrest record finally expunged in 2021.
