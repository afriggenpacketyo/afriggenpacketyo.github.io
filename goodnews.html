Japan to Study Offshore Carbon Capture Using Sea Plants

2025-05-20 03:01:00 EDT

The Japanese government is initiating offshore research to capture carbon dioxide using cultivated sea plants and store it in the deep sea, aiming to accelerate decarbonization efforts. The project involves the Japan Agency for Marine-Earth Science and Technology, Eneos Corp., and others. Coastal plants like mangroves and kelp absorb more carbon than terrestrial vegetation. The study will assess seaweed behavior at considerable depths and the environmental impact of such projects. Japan aims to capture 1 million tons of CO2 by fiscal 2035 and 2 million tons by fiscal 2040 through blue carbon initiatives, compared to 34 tons absorbed by coastal plants in fiscal 2023.

Grigorovich was praised for revitalizing male dance, creating roles demanding exceptional strength and artistry.
