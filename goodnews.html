2026-02-20 23:00:09 EST

China's ten-year fishing ban on the Yangtze River, implemented in 2021, has yielded positive ecological outcomes, including a significant surge in biomass and a boost for the endangered finless porpoise population. The comprehensive ban, reinforced by the Yangtze River Protection Law, involved the recall of over 111,000 fishing vessels and the resettlement of 231,000 fishers, backed by an investment exceeding $2.7 billion. The Yangtze, one of the world's most biodiverse rivers, previously contributed over 60% of China's freshwater fisheries output. This conservation effort also introduced stricter regulations on water quality, sand mining, and shoreline restoration, aiming to protect the vital waterway's ecological health.
