Slovenia Legalizes Medically-Assisted Suicide for Terminally Ill Adults

2025-07-19 04:11:25 EDT

Slovenia's parliament has passed a law legalizing medically-assisted suicide, making it the first Eastern European nation to do so. The legislation, which followed a supportive public referendum, applies to mentally competent, terminally-ill adults facing unbearable suffering with no hope of recovery. To qualify, patients must have exhausted all treatment options and provide repeated, voluntary consent, excluding those with solely mental illnesses. The law is seen as a landmark for end-of-life rights in the region, though specific procedures and oversight mechanisms are still being finalized before implementation.
