Tiny Zooplankton in Antarctica Play Major Role in Storing Atmospheric Carbon

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny zooplankton in the Southern Ocean play a surprisingly large role in combating global warming by locking away vast amounts of carbon. These creatures gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface, grow fat, and then migrate deep into the ocean for winter, where they burn this fat and release the carbon. This process, known as the seasonal vertical migration pump, prevents the carbon from re-entering the atmosphere for centuries. The study quantifies this effect as equivalent to the annual emissions of 55 million gasoline-powered cars, highlighting the overlooked importance of these small organisms.
