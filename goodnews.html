Chinese Scientists Unlock Gene Secret for Drought-Tolerant Rice

The researchers identified a gene called HMGB1, which obstructs the development of deeper, thicker roots crucial for drought resistance. By removing this gene, irrigated rice developed longer roots and greater drought tolerance, similar to upland rice, allowing it to thrive in water-scarce environments. This breakthrough could enhance food security in regions facing water scarcity.
