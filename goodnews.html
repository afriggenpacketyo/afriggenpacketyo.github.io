2026-04-13 16:42:37 EDT

This contradicts warnings issued by the Trump administration and the FDA in September 2025, which led to a reported decrease in pregnant individuals using the pain reliever. The Danish study observed similar rates of autism diagnoses in children exposed to Tylenol in utero and those not. Previous US reviews had noted potential relationships but couldn't rule out confounding factors, such as pregnant autistic individuals experiencing higher pain levels. Tylenol remains a recommended safe option for pain and fever relief during pregnancy.
