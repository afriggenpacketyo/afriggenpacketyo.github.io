California, Arizona, Nevada Agree to Significant Colorado River Water Cuts

2026-07-23 17:57:15 EDT

California and two other lower basin states, Arizona and Nevada, have avoided the most drastic water cutbacks the Trump administration could have imposed on the Colorado River, agreeing instead to reduce their water usage by at least 3.2 million acre-feet through 2028. Under this plan, California will cut its water use by approximately 12%, Arizona by 31%, and Nevada by 28%. This agreement, largely accepted by the federal government, aims to address the deepening crisis caused by the river's dramatic shrinkage over the past 27 years, intensified by climate change, with major reservoirs like Lake Mead and Lake Powell at record lows. While offering temporary relief, federal officials indicate that much larger cutbacks could still be necessary in future years to prevent critical reservoir levels.
