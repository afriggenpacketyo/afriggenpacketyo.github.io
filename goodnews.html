2025-08-28 07:04:01 EDT

Scientists have successfully deciphered the genome of *Yersinia pestis*, the bacterium causing plague, from a mass grave in ancient Jerash, Jordan. This discovery provides definitive proof that this microbe was responsible for the Justinian Plague, which devastated the Eastern Roman Empire from 541 AD to 750 AD, killing tens of millions. The findings confirm the pathogen's presence near the pandemic's epicenter, resolving a long-standing mystery. A companion study indicates that later plague pandemics did not stem from a single ancestral strain, but rather from recurring animal reservoirs, highlighting the cyclical nature of pandemics driven by human factors.
