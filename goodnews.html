Skomer Island Puffin Population Thrives Amidst Global Decline

2025-05-28 01:09:10 EDT

Skomer Island, off the coast of Pembrokeshire, Wales, has recorded a record high of 43,626 puffins, a stark contrast to the rapid decline in global puffin populations. The Wildlife Trust of South and West Wales attributes this success to the island's isolation, which protects the birds from predators like rats, cats, and foxes, as well as human impact. Skomer also hosts 350,000 breeding pairs of manx shearwaters and thousands of guillemots and razorbills, monitored annually by the WTSWW. Globally, puffins are listed as vulnerable to extinction due to pollution, food shortages, and climate change, highlighting the importance of Skomer's conservation success.
