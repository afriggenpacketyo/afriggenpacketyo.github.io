Fossil Discovery in China Suggests Some Flying Reptiles Were Herbivores

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

A significant fossil discovery in northeastern China is challenging previous beliefs about the diet of ancient flying reptiles. Researchers found the stomach contents of a Sinopterus atavismus, a type of pterosaur, which contained phytoliths—microscopic mineral deposits from plants. The fossil also included small quartz crystals, likely ingested as gastroliths to help grind down tough plant material, a behavior seen in many modern birds and lizards. This finding, published in Science Bulletin, provides the first direct evidence of herbivory in this group of pterosaurs, renewing scientific debate on the topic.
