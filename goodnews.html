Unusual Atmospheric Patterns Steered All Atlantic Hurricanes Away from U.S. Landfall This Year

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

For the first time in a decade, the Atlantic hurricane season is concluding without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States. Meteorologists attribute this unusual outcome to a persistent atmospheric pattern, where a dip in the jet stream created an area of low pressure over the eastern U.S. This phenomenon repeatedly weakened the Bermuda High pressure system that typically steers storms westward, causing this year's 13 named storms to "recurve" northward and back out to sea. While the season was active, this protective steering current prevented any major hurricane impacts on the U.S. mainland.
