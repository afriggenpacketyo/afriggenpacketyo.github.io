Scientists Document Rock-Climbing Fish in Democratic Republic of Congo

2026-04-05 07:00:00 EDT

Researchers have formally documented thousands of tiny shellear fish (Parakneria thysi) climbing a 50-foot waterfall in the Democratic Republic of Congo, a behavior previously unrecorded in Africa. These fish, about the size of a french fry, use their rear pelvic fins for support and pectoral fins with tiny hooks to grip the rock, wriggling vertically in a 'power burst' movement. The arduous ascent takes nearly ten hours, with fish resting frequently. Scientists believe this vertical migration is crucial for connecting upstream and downstream populations, highlighting the importance of protecting their habitat from water diversion. This discovery underscores the remarkable adaptability of fish species.

The Historical Reasons Why Easter Lacks Secular Prominence Compared to Christmas

2026-04-05 06:00:00 EDT

An article explores why Easter, despite its theological importance, never achieved the same widespread secular cultural prominence as Christmas in North America and Europe. Historically, Puritans objected to both holidays as times of excessive revelry, associating them with 'misrule' and anti-Catholic sentiment. While Christmas was reinvented in the 19th century as a bourgeois, family-friendly holiday centered on childhood, Easter lacked a similar cultural 'redemption' movement. Its core theological message of death and resurrection proved harder to secularize than the birth of Jesus. Consequently, Christmas became a federal holiday, while Easter remained primarily a religious observance.
