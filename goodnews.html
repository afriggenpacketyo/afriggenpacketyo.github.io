Discovery in Britain Reveals Oldest Evidence of Humans Making Fire 400,000 Years Ago

2025-12-11 06:00:00 EST

A new study published in Nature presents the earliest evidence of humans actively making fire, dating back 400,000 years, found at a site in eastern Britain. Archaeologists discovered fragments of iron pyrite alongside fire-cracked flint handaxes in what appears to be a hearth, with the rarity of pyrite in the area suggesting it was intentionally brought for fire-starting purposes. This groundbreaking discovery pushes back the known timeline for human fire-making by over 350,000 years, highlighting its profound impact on early human evolution. The ability to create fire enabled protection from predators, improved nutrition, and fostered crucial social and behavioral development around campfires, shaping early belief systems and language.
