Los Angeles County Sees First Decade-Long Decline in Deaths Among Unhoused Population

2026-03-10 17:08:23 EDT

Los Angeles County reported a significant and unprecedented decrease in deaths among its unhoused population in 2024, marking the first decline in a decade. Over 2,200 unhoused individuals died, 300 fewer than in 2023, with the mortality rate dropping by 10%. This positive shift is largely attributed to a 21% reduction in drug overdose fatalities, driven by enhanced prevention and harm reduction efforts. Despite this progress, the county still faces a humanitarian crisis with an average of six unhoused deaths daily, and officials express concern over rising traffic deaths and suicides, alongside impending budget challenges for homeless services.
