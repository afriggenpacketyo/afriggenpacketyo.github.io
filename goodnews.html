Understanding the Saffir-Simpson Scale for Hurricane Wind Speeds and Damage Potential

2025-08-18 08:30:01 EDT

The Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale is the standard for rating hurricanes, classifying them into five categories based on sustained wind speeds to estimate potential property damage. A Category 1 storm has winds of 74-95 mph, while a Category 5, the most severe, features winds of 157 mph or higher, capable of causing catastrophic destruction. Storms rated as Category 3 or higher are designated as 'major' hurricanes due to their potential for significant loss of life and damage. The article uses Hurricane Erin, the first hurricane of the 2025 season, as a recent example to contextualize the scale's importance in communicating storm severity.
