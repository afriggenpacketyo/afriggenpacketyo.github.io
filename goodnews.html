Fossil Discovery in China Suggests Some Pterosaurs Were Herbivores

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

A rare fossil discovery in northeastern China provides the first direct evidence that some pterosaurs, the flying reptiles of the dinosaur era, may have been plant-eaters. Researchers found phytoliths, which are microscopic plant minerals, inside the stomach of a Sinopterus atavismus fossil. The presence of these plant remains, along with small quartz crystals likely used as gastroliths to grind tough food, challenges previous assumptions about their diets. This finding renews a long-standing debate among paleontologists about the dietary habits of these ancient creatures that once dominated the skies.

This discovery provides a glimpse into the processes that likely formed Earth and other rocky planets in our own solar system over 4.5 billion years ago. The findings suggest that the building blocks for Earth-like planets may be common during the earliest phases of star and planet development.
