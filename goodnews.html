Explainer: Why a General Decrease in Prices is Unlikely and Economically Undesirable

2025-11-20 06:30:00 EST

While politicians often promise to lower prices, a general decrease in the overall price level, known as deflation, rarely happens and is typically a sign of a severe economic downturn. Economists explain that a modest, stable rate of inflation (around 2%) is by design, as it encourages spending and investment. Deflation can lead to a dangerous spiral where consumers delay purchases in anticipation of even lower prices, causing economic activity to halt, as seen during the Great Depression. Therefore, the goal of economic policy is not to make everything cheaper, but to ensure wages rise in line with gradual inflation. (not investment advice)
