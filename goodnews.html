Scientists Uncover Eight Fundamental Sound Motifs Underlying Birdsong Diversity

2026-07-23 19:55:29 EDT

Scientists in France have revealed the underlying simplicity of birdsong's "spectacular diversity," identifying eight fundamental sound types, or "motifs," that compose the world's birdsongs. By analyzing over 116,000 songs from more than 3,000 species with AI, researchers found that these motifs — three types of trill, three types of whistle, chaotic notes, and harmonies — are combined in various ways. A key finding is that the birds' environment shapes their song composition; tropical forest birds use simpler motifs to penetrate dense soundscapes, while temperate region birds use more complex compositions for closer-range communication, reflecting an evolutionary trade-off between information density and transmission distance.
