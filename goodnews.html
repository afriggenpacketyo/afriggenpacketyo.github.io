2026-02-11 05:00:00 EST

The article explores the 150-year history of the "hat trick," a term referring to three consecutive achievements in a sport, most famously scoring three goals in hockey. Originating in cricket, where players were historically awarded a hat for dismissing three batters with successive balls, the tradition evolved into fans throwing hats onto the ice in hockey. Canadian hat shops like Sammy Taft and Henri Henri initially capitalized on this by gifting hats, cementing the connection between the feat and headwear. Today, tossed hats are often given to the scoring player or displayed, symbolizing a unique and cherished unwritten rule in the game.
