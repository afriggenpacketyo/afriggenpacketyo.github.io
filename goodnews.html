2025-08-10 18:38:00 EDT

A 2022 study conducted on mice has suggested a surprising and plausible link between nose-picking and an increased risk for Alzheimer's disease. Researchers found that damaging the nasal tissue can create a direct pathway for certain bacteria, like Chlamydia pneumoniae, to travel up the olfactory nerve to the brain. In response to this bacterial presence, the mouse brains produced more amyloid-beta protein, the same protein that forms plaques found in the brains of humans with Alzheimer's. While the findings are yet to be confirmed in humans, they offer a new avenue for understanding the environmental triggers of this complex neurodegenerative disease.
