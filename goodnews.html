US Military to Exhume and Identify Pearl Harbor's Unknown USS Arizona Casualties

2026-03-06 07:33:26 EST

The U.S. military is embarking on a significant and long-awaited effort to exhume and identify the remains of 88 sailors and Marines from the USS Arizona, who were killed during the devastating 1941 Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor and subsequently buried as unknowns. This poignant initiative, scheduled to commence in November or December, aims to leverage advanced DNA technology to finally attach names to these servicemen, providing long-sought closure for their families. The USS Arizona alone accounted for nearly half of the 1,177 servicemen who perished in the aerial assault. After years of initial reluctance, the Defense POW/MIA Accounting Agency is proceeding, following extensive efforts by an organization that helped collect DNA samples from over 600 family members.

Indonesia to Implement Social Media Ban for Users Under 16

2026-03-06 06:19:00 EST

Indonesia is set to implement a new regulation that will ban users under the age of 16 from accessing major social media platforms, including popular sites like YouTube, TikTok, Facebook, and Instagram. Communication Minister Meutya Hafid justified the measure by declaring a "digital emergency," emphasizing the growing threats children face from exposure to pornography, cyberbullying, online fraud, and, critically, addiction to digital platforms. The restrictions will be phased in gradually, with existing accounts belonging to users under 16 slated for deactivation. While the government has yet to detail the precise methods for age verification, this proactive step aligns Indonesia with similar measures recently enacted in countries such as France, Australia, and several US states, reflecting a growing global concern over the detrimental impact of digital platforms on minors.
