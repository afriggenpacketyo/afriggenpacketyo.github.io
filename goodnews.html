2025-07-07 15:00:04 EDT

Paleontologists have identified a new species of pterosaur from a fragile jawbone fossil discovered in Arizona's Petrified Forest National Park, making it the oldest known flying reptile in North America. The creature, named Eotephradactylus mcintireae, lived approximately 209 million years ago and was about the size of a small seagull. The finding, detailed in a Smithsonian-led paper, helps fill a critical gap in the fossil record from the late Triassic period. The discovery was made by a long-time museum volunteer and provides new insights into the early evolution of pterosaurs before their eventual extinction.
