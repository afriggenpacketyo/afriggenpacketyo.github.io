Understanding IRS Reporting Requirements for Selling Silver

2026-02-03 12:42:20 EST

Investors selling silver need to understand IRS reporting requirements, as the agency doesn't automatically track every transaction, though certain sales trigger mandatory reporting. Dealers are mandated to file Form 1099-B for sales of 1,000 ounces or more of silver bars, or $1,000 face value of pre-1965 U.S. coins. However, individuals are legally obligated to report all silver sales on their tax returns, regardless of whether a 1099-B is issued, as silver is treated as a collectible and subject to a maximum capital gains tax rate of 28%. The IRS is increasingly sophisticated at identifying unreported income through data matching and monitoring large deposits. Despite these complexities and less favorable tax treatment, silver remains a valuable investment for portfolio diversification and as a tangible hedge against economic uncertainty.
