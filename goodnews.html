Explaining "Corn Sweat" and Its Impact on Midwest Heat Waves

2025-07-23 16:50:00 EDT

The term "corn sweat" refers to the process of evapotranspiration, where vast fields of corn release significant moisture into the atmosphere during hot, sunny weather. This phenomenon dramatically increases humidity, making the air feel more oppressive and elevating the heat index to dangerous levels, as seen in recent Midwest heat waves. While the process itself has a slight cooling effect, the intense humidity it creates can offset this, trapping heat and preventing nighttime cooling. This agricultural reality, driven by monoculture farming, exacerbates the human impact of extreme heat events that are becoming more common with climate change.
