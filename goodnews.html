Understanding Wage Garnishment for the Self-Employed: Protections and Risks

2026-05-07 12:34:33 EDT

With rising household debt, self-employed individuals need to clearly understand how creditors can collect on unpaid balances, as traditional wage garnishment from an employer does not apply. While direct wage garnishment is typically not possible, creditors can pursue other aggressive methods once a court judgment is obtained. These include bank account levies, which can freeze and seize funds, and liens placed on property. Additionally, "accounts receivable garnishment" can intercept payments owed to the self-employed by clients, often catching individuals by surprise. State laws vary on protections, but proactive options like direct negotiation, repayment plans, challenging judgments, or bankruptcy can help mitigate these collection efforts.
