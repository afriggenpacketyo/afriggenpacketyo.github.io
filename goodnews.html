Two Chinese Mathematicians Win Fields Medal for First Time

Hong Wang and Yu Deng, two Chinese academics, have become the first mathematicians from China to win the prestigious Fields Medal, widely considered the Nobel Prize equivalent in mathematics. They share the award with John Pardon from the US and Jacon Tsimerman from Canada, each receiving 15,000 Canadian dollars. Wang and Deng were honored for solving century-old mathematical problems. Wang, along with Josh Zahl, solved a problem posed by Soichi Kakeya in 1917 regarding the smallest area swept by a rotating pencil in three-dimensional space. Deng, a professor at the University of Chicago, addressed Hilbert's Sixth Problem from 1900, concerning the calculation of a system's behavior based on individual particle movements, specifically by analyzing gas behavior. Their achievement has generated significant national pride in China, with related hashtags trending on social media platforms.
