Despite Paragraph 175 criminalizing male homosexual acts, Magnus Hirschfeld's Scientific-Humanitarian Committee and Institute for Sexual Science pioneered progressive ideas about human diversity. The city's tolerant atmosphere fostered a vibrant queer nightlife and artistic scene, with venues like Eldorado and cafes serving as crucial meeting points. This era of openness was brutally curtailed by the Nazis in 1933, but its legacy laid the foundation for future LGBTQ+ rights movements.
