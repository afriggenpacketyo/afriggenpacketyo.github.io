Fact Check: Cloud Seeding Did Not Cause Rain in Iran

2026-04-28 05:53:00 EDT

Claims circulating on social media that Iran bombed a cloud seeding facility in the UAE, causing rain and snow in Iran, have been debunked by fact-checkers. Scientists explain that cloud seeding technology requires existing clouds prone to precipitation and operates on a small scale, incapable of altering regional weather patterns. Videos showing rain and snow in Iran are consistent with weather forecasts and natural precipitation events, with snow in Mashhad in April being plausible due to its elevation. The UAE's rain enhancement program is a research initiative, and there is no evidence of it being attacked or of it having the capacity to influence weather across borders.
