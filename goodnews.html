Explainer: The Causes and Solutions for Urban Heat Islands

2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

Urban heat islands are metropolitan areas that become significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas, a phenomenon that intensifies heatwaves for city dwellers. This effect is caused by heat-absorbing materials like concrete and asphalt, a lack of cooling vegetation, and heat trapped by tall buildings and pollution. The temperature difference can be as much as 15 degrees Celsius. Potential solutions include greening cities with more trees and parks, installing "cool roofs" that reflect sunlight, and using permeable pavements that enhance water evaporation to cool surfaces.
