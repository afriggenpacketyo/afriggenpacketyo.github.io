Science-Backed Strategies Offered to Combat Widespread Smartphone Addiction

2025-07-21 12:49:00 EDT

With individuals spending an average of 4 hours and 37 minutes daily on their phones, smartphone addiction has become a prevalent global issue, impacting mental and physical health. Studies link problematic smartphone use to increased depression, anxiety, sleep disturbances, and physical pain. This behavioral addiction shares symptoms with gambling or video-game addiction, often serving as an escapist tool from stress. Scientists recommend a multi-faceted approach to quitting, including leaving phones out of bedrooms, reducing notifications, setting screens to black-and-white, and using self-control apps. The more methods applied simultaneously, the higher the long-term success rate, with some clinical trials showing short-term effectiveness in reducing problematic use. Understanding the root cause of addiction and seeking professional help, such as cognitive behavioral therapy, are also crucial steps.

Royal Navy Warship HMS Nottingham Wreckage Discovered 109 Years After WW1 Sinking

2025-07-21 11:30:33 EDT

The wreckage of HMS Nottingham, a Royal Navy warship sunk during World War One, has been discovered in the North Sea, 60 miles off the coast of Scotland, 109 years after its demise. A team of ten divers from ProjectXplore identified the 492ft ship at a depth of 82 meters, confirming its identity through name stamps, dimensions, and equipment. The vessel was torpedoed three times by a German U-boat on August 19, 1916, resulting in the deaths of 38 crew members. The captain and 377 other crew were rescued. After a century of failed attempts, ProjectXplore's extensive research and sonar surveys led to this significant historical find, documenting the site this month.
