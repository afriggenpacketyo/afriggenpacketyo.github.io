Astronomers Witness Earliest Stages of Rocky Planet Formation for the First Time

2025-07-20 04:11:00 EDT

Using the James Webb Space Telescope and the ALMA array, astronomers have captured the earliest known evidence of rocky planet formation around a young, sun-like star named HOPS-315. Researchers detected silicate minerals and silicon monoxide gas condensing within the star's gas disk, the fundamental building blocks of planets like Earth. This discovery provides a direct glimpse into the processes that likely occurred at the dawn of our own solar system. The findings, published in Nature, suggest that the initial steps of planet formation may be a common cosmic process, offering new insights into the potential prevalence of Earth-like worlds.
