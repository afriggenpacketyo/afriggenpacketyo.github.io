Financial Experts Warn Overlooking Beneficiary Designations is a Costly IRA Mistake

2025-11-09 07:30:01 EST

Forgetting to name or update a beneficiary for an Individual Retirement Account (IRA) is one of the biggest and most common mistakes investors make, according to financial planning experts. Since beneficiary designations on retirement accounts override instructions in a will, an outdated choice can lead to assets unintentionally going to an ex-spouse. If no beneficiary is named, the IRA typically defaults to the owner's estate, forcing the assets through a costly and time-consuming probate process. The funds are then subject to compressed tax brackets and must be withdrawn within five years, limiting tax planning opportunities for heirs. (not investment advice)
