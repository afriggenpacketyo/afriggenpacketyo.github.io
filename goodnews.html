2025-05-28 14:00:00 EDT

A new study by researchers at the Tokyo University of Agriculture suggests that cats use their sense of smell to differentiate between their owners and strangers. The study found that cats spent twice as long smelling samples from strangers compared to those from their owners, and favored their right nostrils when sniffing stranger scents. Additionally, a cat's personality traits, quantified using the Feline Five test, influenced how they interacted with the odors, with more "neurotic" male cats repeatedly returning to the smell tubes. While the study suggests smell is a tool for recognizing humans, it does not conclude it is the most important mechanism.
