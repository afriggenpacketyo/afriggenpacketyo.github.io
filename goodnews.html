Retail companies are increasingly using a supply chain strategy known as "tariff hacking" or B2B2C (business-to-business-to-consumer) to reduce the impact of trade war tariffs on consumer prices. The model involves routing online orders through a third-party middleman company, which acts as the official merchant of record. This intermediary purchases the product from the retailer at a wholesale price and pays the U.S. tariff on that lower value, rather than the final retail price. This creative arbitrage can significantly cut tariff costs, allowing brands to avoid passing the full expense on to customers, though some experts question the long-term viability and scalability of the practice.
