New Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Ancient Cross-Equator Migration Corridor

2026-01-04 23:00:10 EST

Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences have identified a new species of dicynodont, Dinanomodon guoi, based on a fossil discovered in Gansu province, China. This significant finding marks the first bidentalian genus shared between China and South Africa, suggesting the existence of a cross-equator migration corridor during the supercontinent Pangaea. Dicynodonts, ancient relatives of mammals, were herbivorous, mostly toothless animals ranging in size from small burrowers to large grazers. This discovery provides crucial insights into the ancient biogeography and evolutionary history of these extinct therapsids, enhancing our understanding of prehistoric life and continental connections.
