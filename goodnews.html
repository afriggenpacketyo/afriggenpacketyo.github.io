Breakthrough Experimental Vaccine Targets Severe Intestinal Illness

2026-07-06 19:57:07 EDT

Scientists have achieved a significant milestone in developing an experimental vaccine against enterotoxigenic Escherichia coli (ETEC), a leading cause of severe diarrheal illnesses globally. This bacterium disproportionately affects millions of children in low- and middle-income countries annually, and currently, no broadly effective vaccine exists. The new technology, developed by researchers at the University of Bergen and NORCE, targets a specific toxin produced by ETEC, which has long been a major obstacle to vaccine development. Now licensed to Valneva, the approach shows promising results in eliciting strong antibody responses that appear to offer protection against the infection, though further laboratory studies, clinical trials, and regulatory review are still required before public availability.

New Research Indicates Fertility Success Declines for Women Over 49, Even with Young Donor Eggs

2026-07-06 19:33:47 EDT

New research suggests that women aged 49 and over face a lower chance of successful fertility treatment, even when using young donor eggs, challenging the long-held idea that donor eggs fully "reset" the reproductive clock. A study of 1,774 women found that those 49 and older had double the risk of miscarriage and significantly lower pregnancy rates compared to women aged 35-40. Researchers believe age-related changes in the womb lining, or endometrium, may be the primary cause, potentially opening avenues for future treatable interventions. Experts emphasize that while success rates decline, they remain meaningful, and patients should receive realistic counseling about the effects of reproductive aging, particularly beyond 49 years.
