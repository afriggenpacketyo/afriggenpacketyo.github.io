Understanding Methane's Potent Impact on Climate Change and Mitigation Efforts

2025-10-22 05:04:00 EDT

Methane (CH4), a colorless and odorless gas, is a powerful greenhouse gas, trapping 84 times more heat than carbon dioxide over a 20-year period, contributing 20-30% of global warming since the Industrial Revolution. While it dissipates faster than CO2, 60% of its emissions stem from human activities like agriculture, landfills, and the energy sector. Leaks, flaring, and venting in oil and gas operations are major contributors. Solutions include repairing leaks, with the EU implementing regulations for companies to measure, report, and reduce methane emissions. The UN Environment Programme also uses satellites to detect leaks, with increasing industry and government response, though more action is needed to meet global reduction targets.
