Scientists Develop Genetic Modification to Block Malaria Transmission in Mosquitoes

2025-07-23 13:02:33 EDT

Researchers have discovered a method to make mosquitoes highly resistant to spreading malaria by making a single, precise change to their genome using CRISPR gene-editing technology. The study, published in Nature, found that altering one amino acid in the FREP1 gene largely prevents the malaria parasite from completing its journey to the mosquito's salivary glands. Scientists believe this genetic tweak could be spread through a wild mosquito population using a gene drive, which forces the trait to be inherited by nearly all offspring. While the approach is still in early stages, it represents a promising new tool in the global fight against malaria.
