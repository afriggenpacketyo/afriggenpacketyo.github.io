Understanding Income Limits for Bankruptcy Filings

2026-01-06 10:46:53 EST

There is no single maximum income that automatically disqualifies individuals from filing for bankruptcy. Eligibility for Chapter 7 bankruptcy is determined by the means test, which compares household income to the state median and considers essential expenses. Chapter 13 bankruptcy, which restructures debt into a repayment plan, does not have an income ceiling and often requires a steady income. Factors like household size and cost of living also influence eligibility, meaning higher earners may still qualify for Chapter 7 if their disposable income is low after necessary expenses. Alternatives like debt settlement and management programs also exist.
