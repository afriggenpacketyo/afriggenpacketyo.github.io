Supreme Court Grapples with Gun Rights for Marijuana Users, Challenging 1968 Gun Control Act

2026-03-02 18:47:13 EST

The Supreme Court on Monday heard arguments concerning the Second Amendment rights of marijuana users, a case that could significantly impact the 1968 Gun Control Act. Despite marijuana's legality in many states, it remains federally illegal, making it a crime for users to own guns. The Justice Department argued the federal law is analogous to historical bans on "habitual drunkards," but justices questioned this comparison, particularly regarding the severity of disarming individuals for moderate marijuana use. The case highlights the difficulty of applying the Court's 2022 Bruen decision, which requires gun laws to be analogous to historical precedents, to modern contexts. A decision is expected by summer.

Canada's British Columbia to Adopt Year-Round Daylight Saving Time

2026-03-02 18:36:36 EST

British Columbia (BC) is set to eliminate seasonal clock changes, transitioning to year-round daylight saving time after the upcoming March 8, 2026, one-hour clock change. Premier David Eby announced the decision, citing constituent feedback and the goal of reducing disruptions for families and businesses while providing more evening light in winter. A 2019 public engagement report showed 93% support for the change due to health and wellness concerns. This move will align Pacific time with Alberta's Mountain time during winter and with U.S. West Coast states during summer, as other regions, including much of Asia and Africa, have also abolished the practice. A small eastern region of BC will still observe mountain time changes.
