Understanding Wage Garnishment Implications When Changing Jobs

2026-05-27 11:47:35 EDT

Amid rising household debt and elevated inflation, more Americans face wage garnishments. This article explains that a wage garnishment typically ceases with employment termination, as the former employer can no longer withhold funds. However, the underlying debt and court judgment persist, allowing creditors to seek a new garnishment order with a new employer, often quickly. Federal and state laws limit the percentage of disposable earnings that can be garnished, with specific rules for debts like federal student loans or child support. Exploring debt relief options like bankruptcy or debt settlement can provide a more permanent solution to end garnishment.

Navigating Required Minimum Distributions from a $400,000 Retirement Account

2026-05-27 11:08:29 EDT

Americans nearing retirement face challenges from elevated inflation and market volatility, making understanding Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) crucial for those with tax-deferred accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s. The IRS mandates withdrawals, typically starting at age 73, calculated by dividing the account balance by a life expectancy factor. For a $400,000 account, RMDs range from approximately $15,094 at age 73 to $19,802 at age 80, increasing annually. These withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income and can impact tax brackets and Medicare premiums, with a 25% penalty for missed distributions. (not investment advice)
