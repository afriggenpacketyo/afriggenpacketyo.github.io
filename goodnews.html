Scientists Win Nobel Prize for Discoveries in Immune System Regulation

2025-10-06 06:28:37 EDT

The 2025 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine has been awarded to Shimon Sakaguchi, Mary Brunkow, and Fred Ramsdell for their groundbreaking discoveries regarding immune system tolerance. Their research identified regulatory T-cells, which act as 'security guards' to prevent the immune system from attacking the body's own tissues. This fundamental work has opened new avenues for developing treatments for autoimmune diseases like multiple sclerosis and rheumatoid arthritis, as well as new strategies for cancer therapies and organ transplantation. The laureates' discoveries are considered decisive for understanding how our immune system functions properly.
