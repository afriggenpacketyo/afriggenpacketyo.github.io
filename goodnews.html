Polycystic Ovary Syndrome Renamed to Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome

2026-05-12 16:56:28 EDT

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS), a common hormonal imbalance affecting millions of women, has been officially renamed Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome (PMOS) following a global consensus published in The Lancet. This change aims to better reflect the condition's impact on hormones, metabolism, and ovary function, rather than solely focusing on ovarian cysts which are not a defining feature. Experts hope the new name will improve diagnosis, reduce stigma, and encourage more comprehensive care for the estimated 5-6 million women in the U.S. affected by PMOS. The new terminology does not alter the existing diagnostic criteria.
