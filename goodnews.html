Navigating Unpaid Medical Debt After a Loved One's Death

This article clarifies that unpaid medical debt typically becomes a claim against a deceased person's estate, rather than automatically transferring to surviving family members. The estate's executor is responsible for settling valid debts, with unsecured medical bills usually ranking lower in priority. However, exceptions exist where family members may be held liable, such as if they co-signed bills, reside in a community property state, or are subject to filial responsibility laws. The article advises proactively reducing medical debt during one's lifetime through negotiation, charity care, or debt relief strategies to prevent financial burdens on heirs.
