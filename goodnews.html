Chinese Scientists Make First Direct Observation of Quantum Friction Effect

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has reported the first-ever direct experimental observation of quantum friction between two solid surfaces. The scientists discovered that when studying folded layers of graphene, friction did not always increase with thickness and, in some cases, thicker folds slid more easily. This counterintuitive effect was traced to how the material's bending altered electron movement, locking them into fixed energy levels and reducing the conversion of motion into heat. The findings could have significant implications for designing highly efficient, low-wear nanodevices and advanced quantum materials.
