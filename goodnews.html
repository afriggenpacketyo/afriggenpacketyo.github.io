The "Great Stink" of 1858: How London Solved its Sewage Crisis

2026-07-10 09:22:00 EDT

This article recounts London's "Great Stink" of 1858, when a severe heatwave exacerbated the stench from the River Thames, which had become an open sewer due to an overwhelmed and outdated system. The crisis, which saw outbreaks of dysentery, typhoid, and cholera, forced Parliament to act after lawmakers themselves fled the putrid conditions. Engineer Joseph Bazalgette then designed and built a revolutionary 1,800-kilometer underground sewer system, completed by 1875. This monumental infrastructure project not only eradicated cholera but also served London's growing population for over 150 years, with the new Thames Tideway Tunnel finally relieving pressure in 2025.
