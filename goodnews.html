Understanding Assets Targeted by Creditors After a Debt Lawsuit

After winning a debt lawsuit, creditors typically target various assets to satisfy the judgment, including funds in bank accounts through levies, future earnings via wage garnishment, and holdings in non-retirement investment accounts. They may also place liens on real estate equity, though many states offer homestead exemptions to protect a portion of home value. Valuable personal property, such as vehicles or collectibles, can also be pursued, but essential items are often protected by exemption laws. The article emphasizes that federal and state laws limit creditors' powers and advises struggling borrowers to explore debt relief options early to avoid garnishment or asset seizures.
