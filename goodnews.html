Benin Reshapes Cotton Industry with Chinese Assistance, Boosting Local Processing

2026-06-21 02:00:05 EDT

Benin, recognized as Africa's largest cotton producer, is actively reshaping its cotton industry infrastructure with significant support from China. A pivotal development is the construction of a new 184km road, which effectively connects the crucial northern cotton-growing belt to the Glo-Djigbé Industrial Zone (GDIZ) located near Cotonou, the country's economic capital. The GDIZ currently processes a substantial fifth of the national cotton harvest into finished apparel for global brands, marking a strategic move to reduce raw cotton exports. This infrastructure investment is designed to alleviate transport bottlenecks, enhance local value addition, and position Benin as a more integrated and competitive participant in the international textile supply chain, fostering economic growth.
