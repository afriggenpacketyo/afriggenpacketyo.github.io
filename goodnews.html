Helsinki's Smart City Planning Achieves Near-Zero Traffic Fatalities

2025-08-18 03:29:00 EDT

Helsinki, Finland, has achieved remarkable success in reducing traffic fatalities, recording zero deaths in the last year, a feat shared with Oslo in 2019 but notable for a city of its size. This success is largely attributed to the reduction of speed limits to 30 kmph on over half of its streets and around schools, alongside a comprehensive Traffic Safety Development Programme. The city utilizes crash and speed data, combined with resident feedback, to identify and improve dangerous road sections, reconstructing infrastructure like bike paths and street lighting. Helsinki's approach aligns with the EU's "Vision Zero" program, aiming for no traffic fatalities by 2050 through a combination of infrastructure improvements, increased public transport use, and effective speed enforcement.
