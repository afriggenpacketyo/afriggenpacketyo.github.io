Chinese Scientists Develop Lab-Grown Heart Pacemaker from Stem Cells

2026-05-22 22:00:07 EDT

Scientists in Shanghai have achieved a significant breakthrough by creating the world's first laboratory-grown sinoatrial node, the heart's natural pacemaker, using human pluripotent stem cells. This 3D organoid is capable of autonomous beating, offering a new avenue for controlling abnormal heart rhythms. Researchers believe this development could revolutionize cardiac disease research, drug screening, and potentially lead to biological pacemakers that replace electronic devices. The sinoatrial node acts as the heart's "master conductor," regulating electrical signals for efficient blood pumping, and its failure can be life-threatening.
