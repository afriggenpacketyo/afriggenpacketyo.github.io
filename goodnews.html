Comparing the Financial Consequences of Credit Card Charge-Offs and Debt Settlements

2025-10-28 13:03:43 EDT

This article explores the difference between two negative credit events: a credit card charge-off and a debt settlement. A charge-off, which occurs after about six months of nonpayment, is generally more damaging as it signifies a complete default and remains on a credit report for seven years. In contrast, a debt settlement, while still harmful to a credit score, demonstrates a proactive effort to resolve the debt by negotiating a partial repayment. This gives the borrower more control over the outcome and can provide a clearer path to financial recovery compared to the prolonged negative impact of an unpaid charge-off.
