Finland Pioneers World's First Permanent Underground Nuclear Waste Repository

2025-08-30 06:10:00 EDT

Finland is set to become the first country to implement a permanent solution for high-level nuclear waste by burying it deep underground. The Onkalo project, a repository constructed over two decades, will store spent fuel rods from the nation's five nuclear power plants. Using a multi-barrier system, the waste is encapsulated in copper canisters, surrounded by clay, and placed within stable bedrock nearly 1,500 feet below the surface. Hailed as a game-changer by the International Atomic Energy Agency, this facility provides a long-term management model that could be adopted by other nations grappling with the challenge of nuclear waste.
