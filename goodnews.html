Mysterious "Golden Orb" from Alaskan Ocean Depths Identified as Giant Sea Anemone Base

2026-04-23 10:41:29 EDT

Scientists have successfully solved the mystery of the "golden orb" discovered two miles deep in the Gulf of Alaska in 2023, definitively identifying it as the base of a giant sea anemone, specifically Relicanthus daphneae. Initially baffling researchers, the object was meticulously analyzed and found to be a fibrous material densely covered in spirocysts, specialized stinging cells unique to cnidarians. Through a combination of morphological study and advanced whole-genome sequencing, researchers were able to precisely match it to a specimen previously collected in 2021. The golden portion is typically concealed beneath the anemone, which can grow impressively up to three feet across with tentacles extending six feet. This exciting discovery underscores the persistent mysteries of deep-sea exploration and the invaluable power of modern scientific techniques in expanding our understanding of marine life.
