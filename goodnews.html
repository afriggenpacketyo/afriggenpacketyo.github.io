Daylight Saving Time 2026 Begins, Shifting Clocks Forward for Most Americans

2026-03-08 06:00:04 EDT

Daylight Saving Time for 2026 officially began early Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, requiring most Americans to adjust their clocks forward by one hour and effectively losing an hour of sleep. This annual practice, which shifts an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening, has been consistently observed since the 1960s, though it originated in 1918 as a fuel conservation measure during World War I. Despite its historical intent, studies, including one by the Department of Energy, have indicated minimal benefits in terms of energy conservation and have linked the time shift to negative health impacts. Only Hawaii and Arizona, excluding the Navajo Nation, along with several U.S. territories, opt out of observing Daylight Saving Time. The country will revert to standard time on the first Sunday of November.
