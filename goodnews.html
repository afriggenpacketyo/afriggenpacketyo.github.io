Virginia governor signs National Popular Vote bill, moving closer to popular vote presidential elections

2026-04-14 13:12:44 EDT

Virginia Governor Abigail Spanberger has signed legislation enacting the National Popular Vote Interstate Compact, bringing the U.S. closer to a system where the presidential election winner is determined by the national popular vote. With Virginia's addition, the compact now represents 222 electoral votes, inching closer to the 270 needed for activation. This compact would allocate states' electoral votes to the candidate who wins the national popular vote, regardless of individual state outcomes. Supporters argue this ensures every vote counts equally, while opponents anticipate legal challenges regarding its constitutionality and the requirement for congressional assent.
