Uruguay Becomes First Latin American Nation to Legalize Euthanasia

2025-10-16 02:38:00 EDT

Uruguay's Senate has passed a bill legalizing euthanasia, making it the first country in Latin America to formally do so. The "Dignified Death" bill allows mentally sound adult citizens with terminal, irreversible diseases to choose a medically assisted death. The legislation, which does not permit assisted suicide, received broad support in both houses of parliament and is backed by over 60% of the Uruguayan public, according to recent polls. This landmark law continues Uruguay's history of socially liberal policies in a predominantly Catholic region, following its earlier legalization of cannabis, same-sex marriage, and abortion.
