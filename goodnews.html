Pentecost: Understanding the Christian Holiday's Origins and Traditions

2026-05-23 08:14:00 EDT

Pentecost, celebrated 50 days after Easter, marks the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the Apostles and is considered the birthday of the Christian Church. While rooted in the Jewish Feast of Weeks, it signifies a 'miracle of understanding' where the Apostles could communicate in different languages. The holiday is observed globally as a public holiday in many countries, often symbolized by the color red and traditions like scattering rose petals from church ceilings. These practices aim to make the abstract concept of the Holy Spirit more tangible, though its intangible nature makes it less universally understood than other Christian holidays.

Teen's Rare Genetic Disorder Diagnosed After 18-Year Diagnostic Odyssey

2026-05-23 08:00:00 EDT

Her persistent symptoms, including seizures, balance issues, and developmental delays, baffled doctors for years. A visit to the Cleveland Clinic's Undiagnosed Disease Clinic, utilizing whole genome sequencing, identified the rare genetic mutation. This diagnosis has provided a clear path for treatment and support, allowing Lucia to advocate for herself and reconnect with her former self.
