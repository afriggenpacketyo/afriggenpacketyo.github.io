Understanding Home Ownership with a Reverse Mortgage: Debunking Common Misconceptions

2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

This article clarifies the often-misunderstood concept of home ownership when a reverse mortgage is in place, emphasizing that borrowers unequivocally retain legal title to their property. Contrary to popular belief, the lender does not assume ownership; instead, the loan is secured by a lien on the home, similar to a conventional mortgage. Homeowners remain responsible for property taxes, insurance, and maintenance obligations. Although the loan balance accrues interest and fees, reducing home equity over time, the deed remains in the borrower's name. Upon the borrower's death or permanent departure, heirs have the option to repay the loan, sell the property, or allow the lender to recover the debt, with federal insurance protecting against shortfalls.
