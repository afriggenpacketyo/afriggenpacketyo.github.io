2025-08-14 06:16:52 EDT

While viral photos have led to nicknames like "zombie rabbits," experts from Colorado Parks and Wildlife state there is no reason for public panic, as the virus cannot spread to humans or other animal species. The growths, which are wart-like tumors, typically do not harm the rabbits unless they obstruct their vision or ability to eat, and their immune systems often fight off the virus over time. New Research Suggests 7,000 Daily Steps is an Optimal Target for Health Benefits

While 10,000 steps per day is a widely cited fitness goal, new research suggests a more achievable target of around 7,000 steps may be optimal for significant health benefits. The study also linked this target to a reduced risk of cancer, type 2 diabetes, and dementia. Researchers noted that while exceeding 7,000 steps isn't harmful, the additional health benefits show diminishing returns, making 7,000 a realistic and effective goal for many people.
