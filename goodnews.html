Below-Average Atlantic Hurricane Season Forecasted Amid Strengthening El Niño

2026-05-21 13:08:07 EDT

Forecasters predict a less active Atlantic hurricane season for 2026, with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) forecasting a 55% chance of below-average activity. This prediction is largely attributed to a developing El Niño phenomenon, which is expected to increase vertical wind shear and disrupt storm formation. While Atlantic sea surface temperatures are warmer than normal, potentially supporting more storms, the El Niño's influence is anticipated to be dominant. NOAA forecasts between eight and 14 named storms, with three to six becoming hurricanes, including one to three major hurricanes. Despite the forecast, officials caution that even a single storm can cause significant devastation.
