Understanding Required Minimum Distributions for a $1 Million Retirement Account

2026-05-07 10:50:10 EDT

This article explains Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) for tax-deferred retirement accounts, a crucial aspect for those with significant savings like $1 million. The IRS mandates these annual withdrawals starting at age 73, calculated by dividing the account balance by a life expectancy factor. For example, a 73-year-old with $1 million must withdraw approximately $37,736. The article highlights that RMDs increase with age and can impact tax brackets, Social Security benefits, and Medicare premiums. Failing to take RMDs incurs a penalty of up to 25% of the unwithdrawn amount, emphasizing the need for careful financial planning (not investment advice).
