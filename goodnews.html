France Enacts New Consent-Based Law Redefining Sexual Assault

2025-10-29 15:48:00 EDT

France has passed a landmark consent-based law, redefining sexual assault as any sexual act occurring without explicit consent, a move aligning it with several other European countries. The new legislation mandates that consent must be "freely given, informed, specific, prior and revocable," and explicitly states it cannot be presumed from silence or inaction. This significant legal reform aims to shift societal norms from a culture of sexual assault to one of consent. The impetus for the bill was partly driven by recent high-profile cases, including the serial sexual assault of Gisele Pelicot, which brought national attention to the need for clearer consent definitions.
