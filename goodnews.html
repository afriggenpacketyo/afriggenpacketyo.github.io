Chinese Scientists Observe Quantum Friction Effect for the First Time in Graphene

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team from China's Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics has become the first to directly observe and measure quantum friction between solid surfaces. Using folded graphene, the scientists discovered that friction did not always increase with thickness; in some cases, thicker layers slid more easily. This counterintuitive effect was traced to how bending the material altered electron movement, locking them into fixed energy levels and reducing the conversion of motion into heat. The breakthrough, published in Nature Communications, could pave the way for developing ultra-efficient, low-wear nanoscale devices.
