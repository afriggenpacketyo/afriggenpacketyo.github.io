Chinese Study Suggests Paternal Exercise Can Genetically Transmit Health Benefits to Offspring

2025-10-12 23:00:52 EDT

A Chinese study on mice, published in Cell Metabolism, indicates that fathers can pass on the benefits of exercise to their offspring through genetic mechanisms. Researchers found that pups sired by exercise-trained fathers exhibited improved muscle mass, lower body fat, and stronger bones, while the offspring of inactive mice performed worse in physical tests. The team, from Nanjing University and Nanjing Medical University, also discovered that injecting sperm microRNAs from exercised mice into normal fertilized eggs reproduced these beneficial traits. This suggests that paternal exercise offers a cost-effective route to enhance offspring health and intrinsic exercise adaptations.

New Research Uses Thermal Cameras to Objectively Measure Stress Levels

2025-10-12 19:28:34 EDT

Psychologists at the University of Sussex are utilizing thermal cameras to objectively measure stress levels by observing changes in facial blood flow. Their research shows that a drop in nose temperature, visible as a blue area on thermal images, correlates with stress, as the nervous system redirects blood to vital organs in a 'fight or flight' response. This non-invasive technique, demonstrated in a controlled stress test involving impromptu speeches and mental arithmetic, could be a 'game changer' for monitoring stress in humans and animals.
