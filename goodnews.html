Asking a Key Question Can Help Identify Trustworthy Financial Advisors

2025-09-24 08:58:09 EDT

Identifying a trustworthy financial advisor can be simplified by asking one crucial question: "Are you a fiduciary?" A fiduciary has a legal obligation to act in a client's best interest, a standard not all financial professionals adhere to. Many operate under a "suitability" standard, where recommendations only need to be appropriate, not necessarily optimal. Failing to work with a fiduciary can lead to higher fees and misaligned advice, potentially costing clients tens of thousands of dollars over time. Consumers can verify an advisor's fiduciary status through resources like the SEC's Investment Adviser Public Disclosure site and by seeking fee-only advisors.
