US Health Officials Investigate Widespread Parasitic Outbreak Causing Diarrhea

2026-07-15 18:24:48 EDT

A significant outbreak of cyclosporiasis, a parasitic infection causing severe diarrhea, has affected nearly 7,000 people across 34 U.S. states. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) is investigating the source, with initial suspicions pointing towards contaminated food or water, and potentially raw produce. While no deaths have been reported, over 140 individuals have required hospitalization. The CDC anticipates case counts will continue to rise as data is collected and analyzed.
