An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Jurisdiction

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial body of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between member states. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, the court's jurisdiction applies when states agree to submit to it or when a case is brought under a specific UN convention, such as the Genocide Convention. Recent prominent cases include Ukraine's lawsuit against Russia and South Africa's case against Israel. However, the ICJ's primary limitation is its lack of an enforcement mechanism, meaning its binding rulings ultimately depend on the cooperation of the involved parties.
