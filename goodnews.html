2026-07-24 08:00:14 EDT

AI researcher Daniel Meissler's "work versus gym" distinction offers a simple framework for deciding when to use AI assistance. If a task is "work"—where the outcome matters more than the process, like moving heavy objects—AI can be highly beneficial. However, if a task is like the "gym"—where the process of doing it builds essential skills, such as writing a policy memo to develop critical thinking—then relying on AI can hinder personal growth. The article argues that while AI excels at predictable, accurate "work" writing, it can prevent students and creatives from developing crucial skills, prompting a re-evaluation of how society values human effort and art.
