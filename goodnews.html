How the Continental Army Forged a Unified American Identity During the Revolutionary War

2025-07-04 07:31:00 EDT

This historical analysis argues that the Continental Army, under George Washington's command, was instrumental in creating the foundations of a unified American national identity. Before the war, the thirteen colonies were disparate and competitive, lacking a shared culture. The army fostered unity by establishing national symbols like a new flag, creating shared traditions such as celebrating Washington's birthday instead of the King's, and forging bonds among soldiers from different regions through common hardship and sacrifice. After the war, many veterans became influential Federalists, advocating for a strong central government, their political views shaped by the challenges they faced due to a weak, decentralized Congress.
