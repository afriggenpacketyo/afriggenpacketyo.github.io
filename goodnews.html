House Passes Aviation Safety Bill Following Deadly Midair Collision

2026-04-14 20:03:11 EDT

The US House of Representatives approved the ALERT Act, an aviation safety bill, in a bipartisan 396-10 vote, months after a prior version failed. This legislation was prompted by a January 2025 midair collision near Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport that killed 67 people. The act mandates that all aircraft, including most military planes, install safety instruments to receive location data from other aircraft, aiming to prevent future accidents. However, victims' families and some senators argue the bill does not go far enough, as the collision prevention technologies it relies on are not yet widely available or market-ready. The NTSB also noted the bill initially fell short of its recommendations.

Exercising According to Body Clock May Maximize Health Benefits, Study Suggests

2026-04-14 19:54:08 EDT

New research suggests that aligning exercise routines with one's natural internal body clock, or chronotype, can significantly enhance health benefits. The study, published in the journal Open Heart, found that early risers benefit most from morning workouts, while night owls see greater gains from evening sessions. Volunteers who synchronized their exercise experienced improved sleep, lower blood pressure, and healthier blood sugar levels. Researchers explain that the body clock influences sleep-wake patterns, hormone levels, and energy throughout the day, impacting exercise performance and adherence. This highlights that a "one-size-fits-all" approach to exercise timing may be suboptimal, especially for those at higher risk of heart disease.
