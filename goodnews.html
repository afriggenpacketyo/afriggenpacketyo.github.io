Chinese Drug VV116 Shows Promise Against Deadly Nipah Virus

2026-01-27 21:07:03 EST

Researchers from the Wuhan Institute of Virology found that VV116 allowed two-thirds of infected hamsters to survive a lethal dose and significantly reduced the amount of the virus present in the lungs, spleen, and brain. This finding marks the first demonstration of VV116's therapeutic potential against Nipah. The institute believes the drug could serve as a preventive measure for high-risk groups and a readily available treatment option for current and future outbreaks.

Understanding Intrusive Thoughts and Their Link to OCD

2026-01-27 20:18:01 EST

The article explores "intrusive thoughts," unsettling scenarios that most people experience occasionally, such as wondering "What if I just started shouting?" For some, these thoughts become overwhelming obsessions leading to compulsive behaviors, indicative of Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD). Dr. Nina Higson-Sweeney, a psychology researcher, explains that obsessions are unwanted thoughts, feelings, or sensations, while compulsions are repeated, ritualized acts done to neutralize anxiety. OCD is increasingly prevalent among young adults, with symptoms more than tripling in a decade in England. The article differentiates between common, dismissible intrusive thoughts and those that become all-consuming and require professional help.
