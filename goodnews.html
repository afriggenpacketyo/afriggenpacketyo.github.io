The History and Evolution of the Annual White House Turkey Pardon

2025-11-25 06:31:43 EST

The annual presidential turkey pardon has evolved from a simple gift into a formal White House tradition, though its origins are often misunderstood. While people have sent turkeys to presidents since the 19th century for consumption, the modern ceremony was shaped by the National Turkey Federation starting in 1947. President John F. Kennedy was the first to spare a turkey in 1963, an act the media labeled a "pardon." The term was first used officially by President Ronald Reagan in 1987, and President George H.W. Bush formalized the annual pardoning event in 1989.
