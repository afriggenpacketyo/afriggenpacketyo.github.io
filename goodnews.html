Explaining Cyclosporiasis: Symptoms, Transmission, and Prevention of the Parasitic Illness

2026-07-17 09:32:35 EDT

Cyclosporiasis is a gastrointestinal illness caused by the cyclospora parasite, typically spreading through raw produce and water contaminated with human feces. Seasonal in the U.S., outbreaks usually occur from May to August, often linked to items like lettuce, berries, and herbs. Symptoms include watery, "explosive" diarrhea, cramps, nausea, and fatigue, lasting days to over a month, though it's rarely life-threatening. Prevention involves thoroughly washing fresh produce, though some foods may retain the parasite. Health officials recommend purchasing whole heads of lettuce and cooking vegetables when possible to mitigate infection risks.
