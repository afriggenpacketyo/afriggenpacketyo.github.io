2025-11-05 04:34:58 EST

The largest and brightest full moon of 2025, known as the Beaver Moon, is set to rise this week. This celestial event is classified as a "supermoon" because the full moon's timing coincides with its perigee, its closest point to Earth in its elliptical orbit. As a result, the moon can appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than at its farthest point. The name "Beaver Moon" is thought to originate from the time of year when beavers are actively preparing their dams for winter. This will be the second of three supermoons occurring this year.
