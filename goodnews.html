Scientific Study Finds Correlation Between UAP Sightings and Mid-Century Nuclear Tests

2025-10-28 10:00:01 EDT

A new study published in Scientific Reports has identified a statistical link between unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP) sightings and above-ground nuclear testing in the mid-20th century. Researchers analyzed archival astronomical plates from the Palomar Observatory Sky Survey (1949-1958) and cross-referenced mysterious transient light sources with dates of nuclear tests and a database of eyewitness UAP reports. They found that these transient lights appeared 45% more often within a day of a nuclear test, and that UAP reports also rose slightly during these periods. The findings suggest that many of these phenomena are real observations rather than camera defects, providing empirical data that supports a potential connection between UAPs and nuclear activity.
