New research indicates that zooplankton, tiny marine animals, play a far more significant role in mitigating global warming than previously understood. According to the study, these creatures gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the ocean's surface in Antarctica before sinking deep into the ocean to hibernate. This process, known as the seasonal vertical migration pump, effectively locks away vast amounts of carbon from the atmosphere, equivalent to the annual emissions of about 55 million cars. This discovery forces a reassessment of the Southern Ocean's carbon storage capacity and highlights the importance of these overlooked "unsung heroes" of the marine ecosystem.
