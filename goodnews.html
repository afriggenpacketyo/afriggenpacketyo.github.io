German Researchers Discover Potent Antibody That Neutralizes Most HIV Variants

2025-11-28 04:54:00 EST

Researchers at the University Hospital of Cologne in Germany have identified a highly effective antibody, named 04_A06, that could represent a significant advancement in HIV treatment and prevention. The antibody was found to neutralize 98% of the nearly 340 HIV variants it was tested against in laboratory experiments. It works by blocking the site where the virus binds to and infects human cells, effectively intercepting it before it can multiply. While still in early stages, scientists are hopeful that this discovery could lead to a powerful passive immunization, administered via injection every six months, offering a new weapon against the global epidemic.
