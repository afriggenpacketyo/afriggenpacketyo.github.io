Ancient Roman Temple Dedicated to Mysterious Mithras Cult Discovered in Germany

2026-03-12 10:00:57 EDT

Archaeologists in Regensburg, Germany, have unearthed the oldest-known Roman temple dedicated to Mithras in Bavaria, dating back to the first or second centuries A.D. This rare discovery was made during preparations for a construction project and offers new insights into the secretive cult of Mithraism, which flourished across the Roman Empire alongside early Christianity. The site contained drinking vessels, tableware, and animal bones from rituals, confirming its use as a Mithraeum. A silver votive plaque reading "DEO INVICTO," a title for Mithras, further solidified the identification of the ancient sanctuary.
