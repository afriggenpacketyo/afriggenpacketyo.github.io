Temu Halts China Shipments to U.S. Due to Tariff Exemption End

2025-05-05 07:38:54 EDT

Temu, the China-based low-cost retailer, has stopped shipping goods from China to the U.S. following the expiration of the de minimis tariff exemption. This exemption allowed low-value parcels to enter the U.S. without tariffs, but its end means Temu's China-sourced products now face high tariffs. Temu is transitioning to a local fulfillment model, with sales in the U.S. now handled by locally based sellers and orders fulfilled from domestic warehouses. The company is also actively recruiting U.S. sellers to join the platform, aiming to help them reach more customers and grow their businesses. U.S. consumers had complained about tariff surcharges that sometimes doubled the value of the merchandise.

A new study in the American Journal of Psychiatry reveals that more American adults with mental health needs are engaging in talk therapy, while the use of psychiatric medications without therapy has decreased. Between 2018 and 2021, the percentage of adults receiving psychotherapy rose from 6.5% to 8.5%, marking a shift from previous trends where medication use was increasing. This change may reflect greater awareness and accessibility of talk therapy, partly due to the expansion of telehealth during the COVID-19 pandemic. The study also found that the types of providers offering talk therapy have expanded to include licensed counselors and social workers.
