Social Security Benefits Can Be Garnished After Age 70 Under Specific Circumstances

2026-05-28 12:19:33 EDT

Social Security benefits can be subject to garnishment even after recipients turn 70, primarily for federal debts such as back taxes, defaulted student loans, and child support. The Treasury Offset Program allows the federal government to withhold benefits to satisfy these obligations. While private creditors generally cannot directly garnish Social Security payments, they can levy bank accounts where benefits are deposited, though federal law protects a rolling two-month equivalent of deposits. Individuals facing debt risks are advised to explore options like IRS payment plans, student loan rehabilitation, or debt relief strategies to protect their retirement income.
