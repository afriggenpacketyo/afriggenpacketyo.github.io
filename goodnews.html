The rule stipulates that if a homeowner with a reverse mortgage passes away or moves out, their heirs can settle the debt by paying either the full loan balance or 95% of the home's current appraised value, whichever is less. This prevents a situation where the heirs would have to pay more than the property is worth, especially if the loan balance has grown larger than the home's value. This FHA-insured provision offers peace of mind and a predictable path for estate planning. (not investment advice)
