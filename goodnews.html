High Seas Treaty Achieves Ratification Threshold, Set to Become Law

2025-09-19 22:12:08 EDT

The High Seas Treaty, a crucial global agreement designed to protect the world's oceans and reverse damage to marine life, has reached its critical 60th ratification with Morocco's endorsement, ensuring it will become international law in January. This landmark deal, which took two decades to negotiate, will pave the way for establishing international marine protected areas. Environmentalists are celebrating this as a "monumental achievement" and proof that countries can collaborate effectively for environmental protection. The treaty's goal is to safeguard 30% of the world's national and international waters by 2030, a vital step given that currently only 1% of the high seas are protected from overexploitation and pollution.
