Economic Analysis Explains Why Widespread Price Decreases Are Unlikely and Undesirable

2025-11-20 06:30:00 EST

Despite political promises to lower consumer prices, widespread price reduction, or deflation, rarely occurs and is typically a sign of a severe economic downturn. Economists explain that central banks, like the Federal Reserve, intentionally target a small, steady rate of inflation (around 2%) to encourage spending and investment. A deflationary spiral, where falling prices cause consumers to delay purchases, can grind an economy to a halt, as seen during the Great Depression. While prices for individual goods can fluctuate due to supply shocks, the overall price level consistently rises by design to maintain economic stability and growth. (not investment advice)
