2026-07-11 21:38:50 EDT

New Jersey fire departments are actively removing and destroying aqueous film-forming foam (AFFF), which contains cancer-linked "forever chemicals" known as PFAS. Studies have connected AFFF exposure to elevated cancer rates among firefighters, prompting states like New Jersey to collect and process over 150,000 gallons of the hazardous material. The destruction is handled by companies like Revive Environmental, which uses supercritical water reactors to render the chemicals harmless. This initiative highlights the critical need to protect first responders, as many, including veteran firefighter "Boots" Gancarz, have already faced cancer diagnoses after decades of exposure.
