Chinese Study Suggests Fathers Can Pass Exercise Benefits to Offspring

2025-10-12 23:00:52 EDT

A Chinese study, published in Cell Metabolism, indicates that fathers can epigenetically transmit the benefits of exercise to their children. Research on mice showed that offspring of exercised fathers displayed improved muscle mass, lower body fat, and stronger bones, while those with inactive fathers performed worse. The team from Nanjing University and Nanjing Medical University also found that injecting sperm microRNAs from exercised mice into fertilized eggs reproduced these beneficial traits. This groundbreaking discovery suggests a "cost-effective route to improve offspring health" through paternal exercise.

Thermal Cameras Reveal Stress Levels Through Facial Temperature Changes

2025-10-12 19:28:34 EDT

Psychologists at the University of Sussex are using thermal cameras to study stress, discovering that a drop in nose temperature can objectively measure stress levels. During an experimental stress test, participants' noses dipped by 3-6 degrees as blood flow shifted away from the nose towards eyes and ears, a physical reaction to perceived danger. This non-invasive technique, which can also monitor recovery, is hailed as a "game changer" for stress research. It holds potential for managing harmful stress levels and monitoring stress in non-communicative individuals, including great apes in sanctuaries.
