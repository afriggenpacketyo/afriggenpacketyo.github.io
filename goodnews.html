Connecticut Nonprofit Works to Restore Long Island Sound Ecosystem with Used Oyster Shells

2025-07-21 19:58:09 EDT

A nonprofit organization in Connecticut is helping to restore the marine ecosystem of the Long Island Sound by returning used oyster shells to the water. The group, Collective Oyster Recycling and Restoration, collects discarded shells from over 50 local restaurants. These shells provide a critical surface for new baby oysters to grow, which in turn helps filter water, prevent erosion, and create habitats for other species. After a year-long curing process to eliminate contaminants, the shells are deposited back into the sound, helping to replenish oyster beds that have been overfished for generations.
