2025-05-27 08:27:10 EDT

Businesses are finding a workaround to minimize the impact of tariffs by using a decades-old piece of legislation known as the "first sale rule." This rule allows U.S. importers to use the price of the first sale in a number of transactions to calculate customs duties, potentially saving significant costs. To apply the rule, there must be at least two sales, the sales must be at arm's length, there must be proof the item was destined for the U.S., and there must be documentation of the first sale price. While the rule has been around since 1988, it has gained renewed attention under the Trump administration's tariff regime.
