This forensic technique, which compares an old photograph of an individual to a recovered skull, proved successful after more modern methods like familial DNA matching and dental records yielded no conclusive results. Investigators from the Defense POW/MIA Accounting Agency meticulously aligned a photo of Duquesne with the skull, finding compelling evidence in the unique shadow cast by a prominent upper canine tooth. This case highlights the enduring value of refining older forensic methods to provide answers and bring closure to long-unresolved military identifications.
