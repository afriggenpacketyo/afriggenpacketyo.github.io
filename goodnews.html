Breakthrough Gene Therapy Injection Shows Promise in Reversing Congenital Hearing Loss

2025-07-06 23:10:30 EDT

A new gene therapy has successfully reversed a form of congenital deafness, with a clinical trial showing significant hearing improvement in all ten participants within weeks of a single injection. The treatment delivers a healthy copy of the OTOF gene, which is crucial for transmitting sound signals to the brain, directly into the inner ear. Researchers from Sweden’s Karolinska Institutet reported that the therapy was safe and most effective in younger participants, with one seven-year-old girl regaining nearly all of her hearing. The success of this trial opens the door for developing similar therapies for other genetic causes of deafness.
