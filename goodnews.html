Understanding the Science and Folklore Behind Aurora Displays

2025-12-22 06:55:00 EST

The mesmerizing aurora displays, or Northern and Southern Lights, are caused by charged particles from solar storms interacting with Earth's atmosphere. The different colors, such as green from oxygen and purple or pink from nitrogen, depend on the atmospheric elements involved. While typically visible near the poles, recent solar maximum activity has allowed auroras to be seen at lower latitudes. Researchers are also investigating potential impacts of auroras on the ozone layer in polar regions. Beyond the science, these celestial phenomena have inspired rich folklore and mythology across various cultures for millennia, often interpreted as omens or divine signs.
