Research Links Regular Weight Training to Reduced Mortality Risk

2026-06-03 07:52:33 EDT

New research indicates that engaging in 90 minutes to two hours of weight training weekly significantly lowers the risk of premature death from various causes, including heart disease, stroke, and neurological diseases like dementia. A comprehensive study published in the British Journal of Sports Medicine, spanning 30 years and involving nearly 150,000 participants, found a 13% reduction in overall premature death risk, and an even greater 27% reduction for neurological disease-related deaths. This growing body of evidence strongly underscores the profound long-term health advantages of consistent resistance training, offering a promising avenue to enhance longevity and potentially ease pressure on overstretched health services.
