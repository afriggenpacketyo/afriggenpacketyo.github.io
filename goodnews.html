Japanese Regions Benefit Economically from Anime and Manga Tourism

A study by the Meiji Yasuda Research Institute indicates that foreign tourism to anime and manga-related locations in Japan has significantly boosted local economies. In 2024, approximately 3 million foreign tourists visited these sites, contributing to an estimated 54.3 billion yen in related goods purchases, a 53% increase from 2016. The growing popularity of Japanese entertainment content could also help reduce Japan's digital trade deficit. Optimism that this trend will continue to boost local economies is tempered by the need for sustainable tourism management.

'Dragon Man' Skull Identified as Denisovan

2025-06-20 22:16:12 EDT

A mysterious human skull discovered in the 1930s, known as "dragon man," has been identified as a Denisovan after previous attempts to extract DNA failed. Researchers successfully extracted DNA from dental calculus, calcified dental plaque, and compared it to previous samples. The findings indicate that the "dragon man" is the first intact Denisovan specimen discovered to date. Denisovans are known to have coexisted with modern-day humans and are closely related to Neanderthals, expanding our understanding of human evolution. The skull was originally hidden from the Japanese army by a Chinese laborer and later donated to a museum.
