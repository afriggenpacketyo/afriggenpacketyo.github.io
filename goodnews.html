Massive Kelp Forest Restoration Project Succeeds Off California Coast

2025-08-31 12:00:34 EDT

A significant kelp forest off the coast of Southern California has been successfully restored through a 13-year effort led by The Bay Foundation. The project involved divers manually smashing nearly 5.8 million invasive purple sea urchins, which had created barren underwater landscapes by devouring the kelp. This large-scale culling has cleared over 80 acres, allowing the vital "sequoias of the sea" to regrow rapidly, in some cases within months. The restored ecosystem now supports hundreds of marine species, improves water quality, and serves as a potential model for similar conservation efforts worldwide.
