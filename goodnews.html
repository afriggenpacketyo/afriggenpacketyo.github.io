2026-05-30 11:40:17 EDT

For the Muckleshoot tribe in the Pacific Northwest, salmon are integral to their culture, history, and economy, embodying their identity as the "Salmon People." Traditional fishing practices, like net casting, are passed down through generations, connecting members to their heritage and spiritual beliefs, such as the legend of Salmon Boy. The tribe's fight for fishing rights culminated in the 1974 Boldt Decision, granting them half the harvestable salmon. Today, Muckleshoot fishers account for 2% of wild salmon intake, with their catch feeding people globally. The tribe actively invests in habitat protection and water cleanliness to combat environmental challenges, reversing 1990s declines and supporting a thriving fishing community of 500 fishers and over 200 boats.
