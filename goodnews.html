NASA Discovers Third Known Interstellar Comet Passing Through Solar System

2025-07-03 17:56:06 EDT

NASA has identified a new interstellar comet, named 3I/ATLAS, making it the third known object from another star system to visit our solar system. Discovered using a telescope in Chile, the comet is currently 420 million miles from the sun and traveling at 37 miles per second, posing no threat to Earth. Astronomers are actively monitoring the object to determine its size and composition, with preliminary observations suggesting it may be several miles across and larger than the previous two interstellar visitors, Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The comet is expected to make its closest approach to the sun in late October, offering a rare opportunity for scientific observation.
