Research Reveals Why Type 1 Diabetes is More Aggressive in Young Children

2025-11-12 21:48:00 EST

Scientists have uncovered the reason behind the more severe and aggressive nature of Type 1 diabetes in young children compared to older individuals. The study, published in Science Advances, reveals that the pancreas in children, particularly those under seven, is still developing, making its insulin-producing beta cells more vulnerable to immune system attacks. In younger children, these cells exist in smaller clusters, leading to their rapid destruction before they can mature into more durable larger groups. This discovery offers hope for new drugs, like the licensed immunotherapy teplizumab, which could potentially delay the disease's onset by allowing the pancreas time to mature.
