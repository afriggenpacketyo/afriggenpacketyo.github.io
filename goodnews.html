Japan to Explore "Blue Carbon" for CO2 Capture and Storage Offshore

2025-05-20 03:01:00 EDT

The Japanese government is set to initiate "blue carbon" research, focusing on capturing carbon dioxide through cultivated sea plants and storing it in the deep sea, aiming to accelerate decarbonization efforts. Coastal and ocean plants like mangroves and kelp are known to absorb and store more carbon than terrestrial vegetation, making them a key component of this initiative. The Environment Ministry will collaborate with the Japan Agency for Marine-Earth Science and Technology and Eneos Corp. to study seaweed behavior at considerable depths and assess the environmental impact of such projects. Japan aims to capture 1 million tons of CO2 by fiscal 2035 and 2 million tons by fiscal 2040 through blue carbon, significantly increasing its carbon capture capabilities.
