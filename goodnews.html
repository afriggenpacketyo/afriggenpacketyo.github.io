It evolved to describe close friends, particularly women gathering socially, before acquiring its negative, gendered connotation in the 16th century, referring to women spreading rumors. Feminist scholars argue this transformation reflects societal shifts and misogyny, aiming to denigrate and control women by devaluing their conversations. Some advocate for reclaiming "good gossip" as a form of truth-telling and community building among women.
