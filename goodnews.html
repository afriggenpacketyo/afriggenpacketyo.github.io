Clarifying the Misconception That Debts Are Forgiven After Seven Years

2025-11-10 11:41:43 EST

This article debunks the common myth that debts are automatically forgiven or erased after seven years. It clarifies that while the Fair Credit Reporting Act requires most negative information, such as late payments and collections, to be removed from a credit report after seven years, the underlying debt itself does not disappear. Creditors can still legally attempt to collect the amount owed. The piece distinguishes this credit reporting timeline from state-specific statutes of limitations, which determine how long a creditor has to sue a borrower over an unpaid debt, emphasizing the importance of proactively managing old balances.
