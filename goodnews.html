Understanding the Key Differences Between ETFs and Mutual Funds for Investors

2025-09-22 07:00:01 EDT

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) and mutual funds, while both offering diversified portfolios, have distinct characteristics crucial for investors. ETFs trade like stocks on an exchange, providing real-time pricing, whereas mutual fund transactions are processed at day's end. ETFs generally offer significant tax efficiency due to their 'in-kind' transaction mechanism, which minimizes capital gains distributions. Additionally, ETFs typically have lower expense ratios, making them a cheaper option for many investors, though mutual funds may offer access to a broader universe of funds and are more common in 401(k) plans.
