Zambia is working to revive Sona, an ancient writing system from the Chokwe, Luchazi, and Luvale people, which was nearly erased by colonialism. The writing system consists of geometric patterns used on sand, cloth, and bodies, conveying mathematical principles, cosmic references, and instructions on community life. Originally taught and maintained by women, Sona is being rediscovered through research and the knowledge of surviving community elders. The Women's History Museum of Zambia is leading the effort to document and archive women's histories and indigenous knowledge, aiming to piece together a fragmented cultural heritage disrupted by colonial influences.
