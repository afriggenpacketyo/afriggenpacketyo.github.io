NASA Deactivates Instrument on Voyager 1 to Extend Historic Mission

2026-04-19 01:40:28 EDT

NASA has deactivated one of Voyager 1's science instruments, the Low-energy Charged Particles experiment, to conserve power and extend the mission of the most distant human-made object. Launched in 1977 for a projected five-year journey, the probe has remarkably operated for nearly 49 years, reaching interstellar space in 2012. The article explains that the spacecraft's radioisotope thermoelectric generator is slowly diminishing its power output, making conservation critical to its longevity. Engineers hope this shutdown, along with a future "Big Bang" power conservation plan, will keep at least one instrument operational into the 2030s, allowing the venerable probe to continue exploring the space between the stars.
