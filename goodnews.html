Supreme Court Invalidates Trump's Sweeping Tariffs, Citing Lack of Authority

2026-02-21 21:29:20 EST

The Supreme Court, in a significant 6-3 ruling, invalidated President Trump's most sweeping tariffs, finding he lacked the authority to impose them under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA). The majority, comprising three liberal and three conservative justices including Chief Justice John Roberts and Justices Neil Gorsuch and Amy Coney Barrett, applied the "major questions doctrine." This doctrine asserts that broad assertions of executive power on issues of political or economic significance require clear congressional authorization, which they found absent in IEEPA's text. Liberal justices Sonia Sotomayor, Elena Kagan, and Ketanji Brown Jackson concurred based on ordinary statutory interpretation, emphasizing that IEEPA does not grant the president tariff-imposing power, a distinction previously upheld by all presidents before Trump.
