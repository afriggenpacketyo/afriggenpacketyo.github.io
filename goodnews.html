Man Bitten Over 200 Times by Snakes Develops 'Unparalleled' Antivenom

2025-05-03 02:29:15 EDT

Scientists have developed an "unparalleled" antivenom using antibodies from the blood of Tim Friede, a US man who deliberately injected himself with snake venom for nearly two decades. The antibodies in Friede's blood have shown to protect against fatal doses from a wide range of venomous snake species in animal tests. The current antivenom therapies have to match the specific species of venomous snake anyone has been bitten by. Friede's 18-year mission could be a significant step in finding a universal antivenom against all snakebites - which kill up to 140,000 people a year and leave three times as many needing amputations or facing permanent disability.
