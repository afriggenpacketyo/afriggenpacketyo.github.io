"Super Generation" Monarch Butterflies Undertake Epic Migration to Mexico

2026-07-19 19:01:25 EDT

Tens of millions of monarch butterflies undertake a remarkable annual migration from Canada and the northern U.S. to remote mountains in Michoacan, Mexico, a journey spanning thousands of miles. A special "super generation" of monarchs, born in late summer, can live up to nine months—nine times longer than most—by suppressing a reproductive hormone, allowing them to complete this epic journey. Despite a 70% population decline over 30 years due to habitat loss, conservation efforts, including planting milkweed, are showing signs of helping the population rebound. Their arrival in Mexico coincides with the Day of the Dead, where locals believe they represent returning spirits.
