According to NASA, the event will peak on the nights of August 12-13, offering a display of 50 to 100 meteors per hour. The shower is caused by Earth passing through the debris trail of Comet Swift-Tuttle, creating bright streaks and occasional fireballs in the night sky. For the best viewing experience, observers in the Northern Hemisphere should look to the sky in the early morning hours before dawn.
