Terminally Ill Young Ants Emit 'Kill Me' Scent to Prevent Colony Epidemics

2026-01-07 06:00:00 EST

Scientists have discovered that terminally ill young ants, or pupae, altruistically emit a "kill me" scent to signal adult worker ants to disinfect and kill them, preventing deadly epidemics within the colony. This phenomenon, termed "destructive disinfection," is a complex disease defense strategy in social insect colonies, which are highly susceptible to rapid infection spread. The research, published in Nature Communications, found that pupae only send this costly signal when worker ants are present and capable of disinfection. This self-sacrificing behavior helps protect the colony's overall genetic survival, as most worker ants are sterile.
