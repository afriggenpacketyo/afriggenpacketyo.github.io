Blood Pressure Readings May Predict Dementia Risk Years in Advance

2026-03-26 15:56:53 EDT

New research suggests that simple measurements taken during routine blood pressure checks could identify dementia risk years before symptoms manifest. Studies presented at the American College of Cardiology’s Annual Scientific Session indicate that monitoring vascular aging and stiffness, through metrics like the pulse pressure-heart rate index and estimated pulse wave velocity, offers meaningful insight into future cognitive health. This reinforces the idea that effective blood pressure management, a known contributor to heart disease and a significant risk factor for dementia, is crucial for preserving long-term brain health. Researchers emphasize the need for clinicians to tailor risk assessments and treatment strategies to individuals, while further studies are needed to confirm these findings and establish causation.
