Gene Therapy Restores Sight in Six-Year-Old Girl with Rare Eye Condition

2026-04-23 03:53:39 EDT

A six-year-old girl named Saffie Sandford has had her sight significantly restored through a groundbreaking gene therapy treatment for Leber's Congenital Amaurosis (LCA), a rare inherited condition. This life-changing Luxturna therapy, administered at Great Ormond Street Hospital, involves injecting a healthy copy of a gene directly into each eye. Saffie's mother described the results as miraculous, noting her daughter's improved vision in low light conditions, which was previously a major challenge. While not a complete cure, the treatment has strengthened visual pathways, particularly in younger children, offering hope for improved vision and quality of life for those affected by similar genetic eye diseases. The success highlights the growing potential of gene therapy in treating previously untreatable conditions.
