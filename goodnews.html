Breakthrough Gene Therapy Jab Shows Promise in Reversing Congenital Hearing Loss

2025-07-06 03:49:52 EDT

A new gene therapy has shown remarkable success in reversing a form of congenital deafness, with a single injection improving hearing in all 10 participants of a clinical trial. The therapy targets a genetic mutation in the OTOF gene, which prevents the transmission of sound signals to the brain. Researchers delivered a functional copy of the gene into the inner ear, with some patients, particularly children, experiencing rapid and significant hearing recovery within months. One seven-year-old participant regained nearly full hearing. The successful and safe trial represents a major step forward for treating genetic deafness, with researchers now aiming to target other common genes responsible for hearing loss.
