New Dinosaur Species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, Discovered in Thailand

2026-07-10 17:30:57 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new plant-eating dinosaur species, Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils found in Kalasin Province. This long-necked sauropod, estimated to be 20 meters long, lived approximately 150 million years ago during the Late Jurassic period. The discovery, primarily based on a distinctive dorsal vertebra, confirms its membership in the Mamenchisauridae family, known for exceptionally long necks. This marks the first finding of its kind in Thailand, adding to the region's rich paleontological record and offering unique insights into dinosaur evolution with its unique air-cavity structure.
