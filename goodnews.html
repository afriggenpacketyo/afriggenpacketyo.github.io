Understanding the Seven-Year Rule for Debts on Credit Reports

2025-11-10 11:41:43 EST

This article clarifies the common misconception that debts are automatically forgiven after seven years. While the Fair Credit Reporting Act requires most negative information, such as late payments and collections, to be removed from credit reports after seven years, the underlying debt itself does not disappear. Creditors can still legally attempt to collect the amount owed, although state-specific statutes of limitations may prevent them from successfully suing for it. The article advises consumers to address old debts directly through strategies like settlement or consolidation rather than waiting for them to age off a credit report.
