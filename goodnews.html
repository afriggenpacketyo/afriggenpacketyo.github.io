Understanding Disease Spread: Endemic, Epidemic, and Pandemic Definitions Clarified

2026-05-11 06:31:00 EDT

The recent hantavirus outbreak highlights the importance of understanding epidemiological terms: endemic, epidemic, and pandemic. These terms describe how a disease spreads, not its severity. An endemic disease occurs regularly in a region, with constant case numbers, like malaria. An epidemic signifies an unusual increase in cases in a specific region for a limited time, often due to pathogen mutation or new introduction. A pandemic denotes worldwide spread across countries and continents, typically caused by new pathogens for which few people have immunity. The article clarifies these distinctions, noting that even harmless-percentage diseases can cause many serious illnesses during a pandemic.
