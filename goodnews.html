Carlo Petrini, Founder of the Global Slow Food Movement, Dies at 76

2026-05-22 06:22:21 EDT

Carlo Petrini, the visionary founder of the global Slow Food movement, has died at 76 in his Italian hometown. Petrini established the grassroots organization in 1986 in response to the opening of Italy's first McDonald's, advocating passionately for sustainable food production and the preservation of traditional cooking methods. The movement rapidly expanded globally, connecting diverse communities, farmers, food artisans, and activists in over 160 countries with its core philosophy of "good, clean, and fair food for all." Petrini's influential work earned him friendships with figures like King Charles III and Pope Francis, and his passing leaves a "huge void" not only in the world of food and wine science, but also in society as a whole, as recognized by Italian President Sergio Mattarella.
