Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A significant Chinese fossil discovery has offered new insights into the evolutionary process of birds shedding their dinosaur tails, revealing a step-by-step transformation. Researchers, whose findings were published in the peer-reviewed journal Science Advances, highlighted that the evolution of the flight-adapted bird body plan involved profound morphological changes. Unlike most non-avian dinosaurs, modern birds possess a pygostyle, a skeletal structure formed by the fusion of the final caudal vertebrae, which serves as an anchor for tail feathers and muscles. This rare fossil find provides crucial evidence for understanding how this aerodynamically favorable structure developed.
