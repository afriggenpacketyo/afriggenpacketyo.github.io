Social Security Benefits Can Be Garnished After Age 70 Under Specific Circumstances

2026-05-28 12:19:33 EDT

Turning 70 does not automatically exempt Social Security benefits from garnishment, as federal creditors can still seize payments through the Treasury Offset Program for debts like federal taxes, student loans, child support, and benefit overpayments. While private creditors generally cannot directly garnish benefits, they can levy bank accounts where benefits are deposited, though federal rules require banks to protect a two-month equivalent of Social Security deposits. The article advises individuals facing debt to proactively explore options such as IRS installment agreements, federal student loan rehabilitation programs, or debt relief strategies like debt settlement or bankruptcy to protect their retirement income. Doing nothing can lead to significant financial strain and potential loss of access to essential funds.
