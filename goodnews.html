NASA Declares End of MAVEN Mission After Losing Contact with Mars Orbiter

2026-06-04 08:27:35 EDT

NASA has declared the end of its highly successful Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution (MAVEN) mission, after losing contact with the Mars orbiter in December 2025. The $582 million probe, designed to study the Martian atmosphere's erosion by solar wind, operated for over a decade beyond its initial one-year mission, providing significant insights into Mars's past climate. Despite extensive efforts to reestablish communication, data fragments indicated the spacecraft was rotating uncontrollably, leading to battery drainage. MAVEN's legacy includes groundbreaking discoveries about atmospheric escape and relaying signals for Mars rovers.
