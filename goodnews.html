Authorized Users Generally Not Liable for Unpaid Credit Card Debt

2026-07-20 11:01:05 EDT

Authorized users on a credit card account are typically not held legally responsible for any unpaid debt, even if they were the ones who made purchases. The primary cardholder is the individual who applied for the card, signed the agreement, and thus bears the contractual obligation to repay the balance. While debt collectors might contact authorized users, they generally do not possess the legal standing to directly sue them for payment. In contrast, joint account holders share full legal responsibility for the debt. Should a primary cardholder face difficulties with repayment, proactive measures such as debt settlement or consolidation can prevent further escalation, with authorized users potentially assisting by encouraging early resolution or opting to be removed from the account.
