Private sector job growth exceeds expectations in April, ADP reports

2026-05-06 08:15:12 EDT

The U.S. private sector added 109,000 jobs in April, surpassing economists' expectations and indicating a robust labor market, according to ADP. This figure represents an increase from March's revised 61,000 jobs. Wage growth for job stayers increased by 4.4% annually. Job creation was concentrated in education and health services, trade, transportation, and utilities. Despite the positive headline number, the report suggests a low-hire, low-fire environment, with companies hesitant to lay off workers but also cautious about expanding hiring significantly. This economic stability offers little incentive for the Federal Reserve to lower interest rates amidst persistent inflation.

Nvidia and Corning partner on advanced optical fiber for AI infrastructure

2026-05-06 08:14:56 EDT

Nvidia, a leader in AI chip technology, has announced a significant partnership with glass manufacturer Corning to establish three advanced manufacturing facilities in North Carolina and Texas. These facilities will be dedicated to producing optical technologies crucial for Nvidia's AI infrastructure, aiming to replace copper cables with optical fibers for faster data transfer and reduced energy consumption. This collaboration is expected to create at least 3,000 jobs and increase Corning's U.S. optical manufacturing capacity tenfold. The deal underscores the escalating demand for high-performance computing in the AI sector and highlights Nvidia's strategic investments in its supply chain to support future growth.
