North America Anticipates Dazzling Aurora Borealis Display This Weekend

2026-05-16 14:07:59 EDT

Stargazers across the northern United States and much of Canada are eagerly anticipating a spectacular and relatively rare display of the Northern Lights, or aurora borealis, expected on Saturday and Sunday nights. This dazzling natural phenomenon, characterized by brilliant splashes of green, purple, and pink light streaking across the night sky, results from high-energy space particles violently colliding with atoms of gas in Earth's atmosphere, particularly near its magnetic poles. The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) predicts that the geomagnetic storm causing this aurora will be at its strongest on Saturday night, offering the best viewing opportunities just after sunset or before sunrise. Catching these lights is considered a special treat for those who do not typically reside in polar regions, where auroras are more commonly observed, usually during March and November.
