Understanding the Two-Year Rule for Filing a Second Chapter 13 Bankruptcy

2025-10-10 11:34:07 EDT

A key provision in U.S. bankruptcy law, often called the "two-year rule," governs how soon a person can receive a second debt discharge after a previous Chapter 13 case. The rule states that a new Chapter 13 filing is only eligible for a discharge if it occurs at least two years after the filing date of the prior case, not the discharge date. Misunderstanding this timing can lead to filing a new case that successfully restructures secured debts but fails to eliminate unsecured debts like credit card balances. This distinction is crucial for borrowers considering a repeat filing to achieve comprehensive debt relief.
