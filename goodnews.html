The Peak District's Evolution from "Devil's Arse" to UK's First National Park

2026-04-18 19:36:46 EDT

The article traces the Peak District's journey to becoming the UK's first national park 75 years ago, highlighting its long history as a tourist destination. Early attractions included caves with colorful names like the "Devil's Arse," later sanitized by Victorians. The arrival of railways in the 19th century opened the area to mass tourism, though much land remained privately owned. A pivotal moment was the 1932 Kinder Scout mass trespass, a protest for roaming rights that contributed to the 1949 National Parks and Access to the Countryside Act. This legislation, and later acts, gradually expanded public access and conservation efforts, making the Peak District a cherished natural health service.
