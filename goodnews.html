Financial Experts Compare the Credit Impacts of Debt Settlement and Charge-Offs

2025-10-28 13:03:43 EDT

When consumers fall behind on credit card payments, they may face either a "charge-off" or choose "debt settlement," both of which negatively impact credit scores. A charge-off, which occurs after about six months of nonpayment, is generally considered more damaging as the creditor declares the debt a loss and it remains on a credit report for seven years. In contrast, debt settlement is a proactive negotiation to pay less than the full amount owed, which, while still harmful to credit, demonstrates an effort to resolve the debt and offers more control.
