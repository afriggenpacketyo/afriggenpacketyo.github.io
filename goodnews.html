Collectible Toy Craze Highlights the History of East Germany's Monchhichi Doll

2025-07-29 03:29:00 EDT

The recent viral popularity of collectible Labubu toys, fueled by celebrity collectors, has revived interest in their perceived ancestor, the Monchhichi doll. Originally created in Japan in 1974, the monkey-like doll became a global sensation in the 1980s. While popular in West Germany, it was unavailable in the communist East, which led to the creation of a local version called the Tiemi. Produced in the toy-making town of Sonneberg, the Tiemi became a beloved item throughout the Eastern Bloc but ceased production after German reunification, making original models sought-after collector's items today.
