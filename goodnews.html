Medical Experts Explain Why Alcohol Affects People More with Age

2025-12-08 05:00:00 EST

As people age, their ability to tolerate alcohol often decreases due to key physiological changes, according to health experts. The liver enzymes responsible for breaking down alcohol become less efficient, and the body's composition shifts from muscle to fatty tissue. Since muscle stores water that dilutes alcohol, having less muscle mass results in a higher blood alcohol concentration that persists for longer. For women in perimenopause, alcohol can also worsen symptoms like mood swings and sleep disruption. Doctors advise that cutting back on drinking is beneficial, as alcohol-related health risks increase with age.
