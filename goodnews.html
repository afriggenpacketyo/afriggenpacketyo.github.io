Connecticut Non-Profit Restores Marine Ecosystem by Recycling Used Oyster Shells

2025-07-21 19:58:09 EDT

A non-profit organization in Connecticut is working to restore the health of the Long Island Sound by returning used oyster shells to the water. The initiative, called Collective Oyster Recycling and Restoration, collects discarded shells from over 50 local restaurants. These shells provide a critical habitat for new oysters to grow, which in turn filter the water and support hundreds of other marine species. After a year-long curing process to eliminate contaminants, the shells are deposited back into the sound, helping to rebuild vital and historically overfished oyster beds.
