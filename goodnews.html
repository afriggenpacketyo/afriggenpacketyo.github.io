2025-09-16 02:26:33 EDT

The UK government is set to introduce the long-awaited 'Hillsborough Law,' which will establish a legal 'duty of candour' requiring public officials to be truthful during investigations into major disasters. The legislation, officially the Public Office (Accountability) Bill, aims to prevent cover-ups and will impose criminal sanctions for breaches. It was championed by families of the 97 victims of the 1989 Hillsborough football disaster, who faced decades of institutional obstruction.
