UN General Assembly Designates African Enslavement as "Gravest Crime Against Humanity"

2026-03-25 16:12:31 EDT

The United Nations General Assembly has passed a resolution recognizing the enslavement of Africans during the transatlantic slave trade as "the gravest crime against humanity." Proposed by Ghana, the resolution urges UN member states to consider offering apologies and contributing to a reparations fund. It was adopted with 123 votes in favor, three against (the United States, Israel, and Argentina), and 52 abstentions, including the United Kingdom and European Union member states. While General Assembly resolutions are not legally binding, this move carries significant global moral weight, aiming to acknowledge historical injustices and address lingering racial inequalities and underdevelopment affecting people of African descent worldwide.
