The rare rock, designated NWA 16788, represents about 6.5% of all known Martian material on our planet. It was likely ejected from Mars by an asteroid impact and traveled 140 million miles through space before landing in the Sahara Desert, where it was discovered in 2023. Such meteorites are exceptionally rare, providing scientists with invaluable data to help unlock the secrets of the red planet's geology and history.
