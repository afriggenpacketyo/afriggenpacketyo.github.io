Study Suggests Pigs Were Domesticated in China During Neolithic Period

2025-06-12 21:00:11 EDT

Research indicates that pigs were domesticated in southern China around 8,000 years ago, challenging the conventional view that pigs were first domesticated in the Near East. Archaeologists from Chinese and US institutes analyzed two early Neolithic sites in the Yangtze Delta region, finding evidence that pigs were consuming humans' cooked foods and waste. The findings suggest that the domestication of Sus scrofa, the wild boar, occurred alongside the development of rice cultivation and sedentary lifestyles in ancient society. This study provides new insights into the history of animal domestication and early human societies in China.

NHS to Offer World-First 'Trojan Horse' Cancer Therapy

2025-06-12 20:05:15 EDT

The NHS in England will be the first to offer a "Trojan horse" therapy, which delivers toxic drugs inside cancer cells, to treat the blood cancer myeloma. The drug, belantamab mafodotin, has been shown to halt the cancer for nearly three times longer than current therapies and reduces side effects. The therapy works by binding a lethal chemotherapy drug to an antibody that targets plasma cells, releasing its toxic payload once inside. Clinical trials showed the therapy halted the cancer for three years, compared to 13 months with current treatments, and around 1,500 patients a year could benefit from the new drug.
