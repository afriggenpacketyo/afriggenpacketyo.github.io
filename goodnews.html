Explainer on How Credit Card Payment Grace Periods Function

2025-07-22 11:26:03 EDT

This article clarifies the common misunderstanding surrounding credit card grace periods. Most cards offer a grace period on new purchases, which is the time between the end of a billing cycle and the payment due date, during which no interest is charged if the balance is paid in full. However, this protection is typically lost if a balance is carried over from the previous month. For late payments, there is no official, required grace period; fees and penalty APRs can be applied immediately after the due date, though some issuers may offer a brief, informal window.
