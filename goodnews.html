Genetic Engineering of Mice Explored to Combat Lyme Disease

2026-05-24 19:00:15 EDT

Scientists on Nantucket are pioneering a genetic engineering project, "Mice Against Ticks," to combat Lyme disease by making wild mice immune to the bacteria. This novel approach, led by MIT's Kevin Esvelt and Tufts University's Sam Telford, aims to interrupt the infection cycle by adding an antibody gene to mouse embryos, ensuring heritable immunity without harming the mice or the broader ecosystem. The goal is to dramatically reduce infected ticks and alleviate the suffering caused by Lyme disease, which affects 15% of Nantucket residents. The project emphasizes careful community buy-in and initial field trials on a private island to assess ecological impacts before any potential large-scale experiments.
