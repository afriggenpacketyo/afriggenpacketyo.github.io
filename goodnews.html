Understanding the Impact of Perimenopause on Mental Health

2025-10-14 06:31:07 EDT

Perimenopause, the transitional stage before menopause, can significantly affect mental health due to fluctuating hormone levels, particularly estrogen. These hormonal shifts can lead to symptoms like anxiety, depression, irritability, and brain fog, which are often mistaken for stress or burnout. The decline in estrogen can disrupt brain functions related to mood and cognition, while also indirectly causing issues like insomnia through hot flashes and night sweats. Experts recommend lifestyle adjustments such as diet changes, exercise, and symptom tracking, as well as medical options like hormone therapy, to help manage these challenging mental health symptoms.
