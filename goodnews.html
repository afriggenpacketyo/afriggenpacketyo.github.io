2026-06-08 05:00:00 EDT

The device delivers targeted vibrations to the spine and hips, mimicking some effects of exercise to stimulate bone-building cells. Clinical trials suggest it significantly reduces bone strength loss in the spine over 12 months compared to a placebo. While experts note it should be an adjunct to, not a replacement for, established bone-health practices like exercise and nutrition, early results are encouraging for post-menopausal women.
