Lessons from the Euro's Internationalization for China's Yuan Ambitions

2026-04-26 18:00:14 EDT

Daniel Gros, director of the Institute for European Policymaking, discusses the lessons China can learn from the euro's experience as it seeks to internationalize the yuan. Gros emphasizes that the US dollar's dominance stems from its deep financial markets and network effects, making it difficult for other currencies to compete. For a currency to become international, it requires an open capital account, which entails relinquishing control over its value. He notes that the euro's internationalization has been slower than initially anticipated due to these factors, suggesting similar challenges for the yuan.
