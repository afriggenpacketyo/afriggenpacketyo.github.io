Bernard LaFayette, Key Selma Voting Rights Organizer, Dies at 85

2026-03-06 19:17:58 EST

Bernard LaFayette, a pivotal figure in the American Civil Rights Movement and a crucial organizer for the Selma voting rights campaign, has passed away at the age of 85 from a heart attack. His courageous groundwork in Selma, Alabama, two years before "Bloody Sunday," was instrumental in setting the stage for the demonstrations that ultimately led to the passage of the Voting Rights Act of 1965. As a co-founder of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee, LaFayette championed nonviolent resistance, enduring assassination attempts and beatings. His lifelong dedication to social justice, deeply inspired by a childhood experience, leaves an indelible legacy on American history and civil rights advocacy.
