Understanding Intrusive Thoughts: When Normal Experiences Become Obsessive Compulsive Disorder

2026-01-27 20:18:01 EST

This article explores the phenomenon of intrusive thoughts, which are unsettling scenarios many people experience occasionally, such as wondering "What if I just started shouting?" For some individuals, however, these thoughts become overwhelming obsessions leading to compulsive behaviors, often indicating Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD). Psychology researcher Dr. Nina Higson-Sweeney explains that obsessions are unwanted thoughts, while compulsions are repeated, ritualized acts done to alleviate the anxiety caused by these obsessions. With 1-4% of the population living with OCD and a tripling of symptoms among young adults in England, recognizing when these thoughts become all-consuming and lead to compulsions is crucial for seeking appropriate support and managing this distressing mental health condition effectively.
