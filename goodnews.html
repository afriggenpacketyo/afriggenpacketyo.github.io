Lower Basin States Propose Voluntary Water-Saving Measures for Colorado River

2026-05-03 15:32:57 EDT

California, Arizona, and Nevada have proposed voluntary water-saving measures for the Colorado River over the next three years, aiming to buy time amidst stalled negotiations among the seven basin states. The plan seeks to save 3.2 million acre-feet of water through voluntary cutbacks by 2028, with an additional 700,000 acre-feet through conservation and infrastructure improvements. This initiative also includes creating a conservation pool to ensure federal trust obligations to Arizona tribes are met. The proposal, which requires approval from state water agencies and the Arizona legislature, represents a unified effort by the Lower Basin states to stabilize the critically low levels of Lake Mead and Lake Powell, which supply 40 million people.
