Scientists and Startups Explore Phytomining to Harvest Metals Using Plants

2025-09-23 05:47:00 EDT

A novel and environmentally friendly technique known as phytomining is being developed to harvest valuable metals from the ground using plants. Certain 'hyperaccumulating' species can absorb high concentrations of metals like nickel, zinc, and even gold from the soil, storing them in their leaves and stems. After harvesting and incinerating the plants, a metal-rich 'bio-ore' is left behind, which can be processed into valuable resources like nickel sulfate for batteries. This method offers a climate-friendly alternative to conventional mining by cleaning contaminated land and operating with near-zero carbon emissions.
