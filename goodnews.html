2025-11-25 12:35:38 EST

The first known instance of a president sparing a turkey dates back to Abraham Lincoln in 1863, who saved his son's pet turkey, Jack, from becoming Christmas dinner. Lincoln is also credited with establishing Thanksgiving as a national holiday during the Civil War. Over the decades, the practice of presidents receiving and sometimes sparing gift turkeys evolved into the formal, lighthearted ceremony known today.
