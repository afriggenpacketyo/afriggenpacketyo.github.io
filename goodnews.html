2025-06-08 00:00:18 EDT

Chinese researchers have developed an artificial retina using the rare mineral tellurium, successfully restoring sight and granting "super vision" to blind animals. The device, composed of tellurium nanowire implants, enabled genetically blind mice and a monkey to see visible and infrared light. Tellurium's excellent photoelectric properties allow it to convert light into electrical energy, serving as a replacement for damaged photoreceptor cells in the retina. This advancement presents a promising avenue for treating human blindness, offering the potential to restore sight and enhance visual capabilities. The study highlights China's growing role in scientific innovation and the potential of rare minerals in medical applications.
