Key Pitfalls to Avoid When Considering a Reverse Mortgage This Fall

2025-09-30 15:30:30 EDT

Reverse mortgages, while offering older homeowners a way to access home equity without monthly payments, present several critical pitfalls that prospective borrowers should diligently avoid this fall. A common misstep is neglecting the true costs, which encompass substantial upfront fees like origination and mortgage insurance, alongside continuous obligations for property taxes and homeowners insurance. Many borrowers also overlook the significant impact on their heirs, who will be responsible for repaying the loan or refinancing to retain the property. Furthermore, taking out more money than genuinely needed can rapidly deplete home equity due to accruing interest, and failing to assess how proceeds might affect eligibility for means-tested benefits like Medicaid can lead to unforeseen financial complications.
