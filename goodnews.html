Understanding Haboobs: Causes, Dangers, and Safety Measures for Dust Storms

2025-08-26 15:00:47 EDT

A massive dust storm, known as a haboob, recently impacted the Phoenix area, disrupting travel and causing power outages. This article explains that a haboob is a type of dust storm specifically caused by outflowing winds from an incoming thunderstorm over dry, dusty regions. When strong downdrafts hit the earth's surface, they push outward, kicking up vast amounts of dust ahead of the storm, significantly reducing visibility. The National Weather Service advises drivers caught in a haboob to pull off the pavement, turn off all lights, and set the emergency brake to avoid hazardous conditions. These phenomena are common in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in the U.S. Southwest.
