Wealthy Retirees May Face an Unexpected Social Security Tax Increase

2025-09-14 09:11:08 EDT

A "Social Security tax trap" can catch affluent retirees by surprise, significantly increasing their tax burden due to required minimum distributions (RMDs) from tax-deferred retirement accounts like traditional IRAs. These mandatory withdrawals, which begin at age 73, increase a retiree's combined income, potentially pushing them over the threshold where Social Security benefits become taxable. This can cause a sudden jump from paying no tax on benefits to paying tax on up to 85% of them, and may also trigger higher Medicare premiums. One strategy to mitigate this is to gradually convert traditional IRA funds into a Roth IRA. (not investment advice)
