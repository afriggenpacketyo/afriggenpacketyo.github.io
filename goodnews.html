Hurricane Katrina's Legacy: Emphasizing Pet Inclusion in Disaster Preparedness

2025-08-26 06:00:00 EDT

Hurricane Katrina in 2005 highlighted the tragic consequences of not including pets in disaster evacuation plans, as tens of thousands of animals were left behind and many people refused to evacuate without their companions. This led to the landmark 2006 Pet Evacuation & Transportation Safety (PETS) Act, incentivizing states and agencies to incorporate pets into their disaster planning. Emergency responders learned a crucial lesson: efforts to save pets also save humans, as nearly half of those who didn't evacuate stayed due to their animals. The messaging has since shifted, urging pet owners to always take their animals with them during evacuations, though challenges in rescue efforts still persist.
