Cardiologist Explains How Extreme Summer Heat Can Harm Heart Health

2025-07-12 05:00:40 EDT

A cardiologist warns that extreme summer heat significantly strains the cardiovascular system, increasing the risk of cardiac events, especially for individuals with pre-existing conditions. As the body works to cool down, blood vessels widen, forcing the heart to pump faster and harder, which can lead to dehydration or thickened blood. The article highlights that even minor increases in body temperature can elevate heart rates and raise the risk of heart attacks and arrhythmias. To mitigate risks, it is advised to stay hydrated, avoid strenuous activity during peak heat, and seek cool, shaded environments.

Ancient Tombs of Western Xia Dynasty in China Added to UNESCO World Heritage List

2025-07-12 05:00:08 EDT

A vast tomb site from the Western Xia dynasty (1038–1227) in China has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage site. Located west of Yinchuan, the 40-square-kilometer area includes nine imperial mausoleums and over 270 subordinate tombs belonging to the Tangut people. The site also features extensive ancient flood control systems and a large architectural complex used for worship and administration. The World Heritage Committee recognized the site as a significant testament to the cultural fusion and interaction among diverse traditions along the ancient Silk Road.
