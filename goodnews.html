Humpback Whales Achieve Record-Breaking Lifetime Swims Between Continents

2026-05-20 06:53:00 EDT

Two humpback whales have made unprecedented lifetime journeys, sighted in breeding grounds off both Australia and Brazil, marking the first scientific observation of such vast inter-oceanic travel. Identified through unique tail fluke images, one whale traveled approximately 14,200 kilometers, while the other covered 15,100 kilometers over 22 years. These findings support the "Southern Ocean Exchange hypothesis," suggesting whales can meet in Antarctic feeding grounds and occasionally take different routes to distant breeding areas. Researchers believe these rare crossings, observed in a tiny fraction of the studied population, could maintain genetic diversity and may become more frequent due to climate-driven changes in the Southern Ocean.
