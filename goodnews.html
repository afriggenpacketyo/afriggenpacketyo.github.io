Understanding the Dangers of Heat Exhaustion and Heat Stroke

2025-07-01 07:40:00 EDT

This article explains the critical differences between heat exhaustion and the more dangerous heat stroke, offering guidance on how to recognize and respond to each. Heat exhaustion, an earlier stage of overheating, presents with symptoms like headache, heavy sweating, and dizziness, and can be treated by moving to a cool place and rehydrating. If left untreated, it can progress to heat stroke, a life-threatening emergency where the body's temperature regulation system fails, sweat production stops, and body temperature can rise rapidly. For heat stroke, immediate emergency medical attention is crucial, along with rapid cooling measures.
