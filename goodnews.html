New Drug to Aid Walking for Multiple Sclerosis Patients Now Available on NHS in England

2026-07-22 19:38:58 EDT

The first drug specifically designed to help people with multiple sclerosis (MS) walk is now available on the NHS in England, offering a significant advancement for patients. Fampridine, known as a "signal booster," works by enhancing nerve signals, thereby making movement and independent living easier for eligible individuals. Up to 5,000 people in England could benefit annually from this treatment, which has previously been available in Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland. Clinical trials demonstrated that approximately four in ten users experienced improved walking speed and endurance, offering a life-changing opportunity for increased mobility and independence for many living with MS.
