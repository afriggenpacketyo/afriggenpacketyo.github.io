The Evolution of "Broadcasting" from Agriculture to Mass Media

2025-10-15 05:00:00 EDT

The term "broadcasting," commonly associated with radio and television, originated in agriculture, describing the scattering of seeds over a wide area, a practice still used today. Its adoption into media began in the early 1920s with radio, notably Frank Conrad's broadcast of presidential election results, which "electrified the country." The Communications Act of 1934 solidified its new definition for public dissemination of radio content. Over time, broadcasting evolved to include television and adapted to new technologies, becoming a crucial source of news, entertainment, and cultural influence, despite modern digital platforms not technically being "broadcasters."
