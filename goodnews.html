Chinese Scientists Create Lab-Grown Heart Pacemaker from Stem Cells

2026-05-22 22:00:07 EDT

Scientists in Shanghai have achieved a significant medical breakthrough by using human pluripotent stem cells to create the world's first laboratory-grown sinoatrial node, which functions as the heart's natural pacemaker. This autonomously beating organoid represents a transformative development for cardiac disease research and drug screening. The sinoatrial node is crucial for regulating the heart's continuous and rhythmic contractions, and its failure can lead to life-threatening conditions. This engineered biological pacemaker offers a novel approach to controlling abnormal heart rhythms, potentially paving the way for new therapeutic interventions and improved treatments in cardiology.
