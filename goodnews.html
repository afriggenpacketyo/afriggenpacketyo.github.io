Rethinking AI's Impact on Jobs: Beyond Simple Replacement

2026-06-11 12:00:00 EDT

This article challenges the common fear that AI will make numerous careers obsolete, arguing that "Can AI do my job?" is the wrong question. Using the example of radiologists, whose numbers and salaries increased despite AI advancements, the piece proposes three key questions to assess a job's vulnerability: whether it's a "weak bundle" or "strong bundle" of tasks, how demand changes if the output becomes cheaper (Jevons paradox), and if AI acts as the expert or enhances human expertise. It concludes that AI often transforms jobs by automating routine tasks, allowing humans to focus on more complex, "messy" responsibilities, but warns that long-term predictions remain difficult.
