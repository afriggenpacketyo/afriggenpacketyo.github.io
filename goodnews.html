Scientists Discover Potato Evolved from Ancient Tomato Hybrid

2025-07-31 21:07:58 EDT

Researchers have solved a long-standing botanical mystery, discovering that the modern potato evolved from an ancestor of the tomato around 9 million years ago. A study published in the journal Cell explains that the potato was created through a hybridization event between a tomato ancestor and a non-tuber-producing wild potato species. The tomato parent provided a crucial gene that signals the plant to form starchy tubers, while the other parent contributed a gene that helps control their growth. This finding demonstrates how interbreeding between species can spark the evolution of entirely new traits and organisms.
