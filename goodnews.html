The Historical and Linguistic Journey of How the Turkey Got Its Name

2025-11-26 05:00:00 EST

The name of the turkey, a central feature of Thanksgiving meals, has a confusing and uncertain history rooted in a case of mistaken identity. When the North American bird (Meleagris gallopavo) was first brought to Europe in the 16th century, it was confused with the African guinea fowl, which was already known as a "turkey" because it was imported through the Turkish region. Over time, the American bird became more popular but retained the misnomer, which has since evolved into various negative English idioms like "cold turkey."
