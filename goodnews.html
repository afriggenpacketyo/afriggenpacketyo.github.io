Experts Explain Interest Rate Differences Between Home Equity Loans and HELOCs

2025-05-19 10:52:05 EDT

Experts explain that home equity loans and HELOCs have different interest rates due to the fixed versus variable nature of the rates, the flexibility of draw periods, and secondary market requirements. Home equity loans typically have higher, fixed rates because lenders assume the risk of future rate increases, while HELOCs offer lower, variable rates that adjust with economic conditions. The flexible draw periods of HELOCs allow lenders to manage capital more dynamically, often resulting in lower initial rates. Home equity loans are frequently sold to outside investors, driving higher fixed-rate pricing, while HELOCs are kept on banks' balance sheets, resulting in lower starting rates.
