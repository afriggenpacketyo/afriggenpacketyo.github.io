Explainer: The Science Behind the Urban Heat Island Effect

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As a heatwave affects the UK, this article explains why cities often experience significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas, a phenomenon known as the urban heat island effect. The primary cause is the prevalence of dark, hard surfaces like roads and buildings, which absorb and retain the sun's energy, releasing it slowly as heat. In contrast, rural landscapes with more vegetation and water benefit from natural cooling through evaporation. This effect makes growing urban populations particularly vulnerable to the impacts of climate change and more intense heatwaves.
