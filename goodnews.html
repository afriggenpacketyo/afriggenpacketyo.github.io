Taiwan Proposes Tighter Conscription Rules to Address Draft Dodging

2025-12-15 23:00:10 EST

Taiwan's defense ministry has unveiled a draft amendment to its physical classification standards for conscription, aiming to close loopholes and enhance fairness in the draft system. The proposed changes would significantly narrow the criteria for exemption, with only individuals with a BMI above 45 or a height of 144cm or below qualifying. Previously, exemptions were granted for men with a BMI above 35 or extreme height measurements. The revisions also include provisions for alternative service for transgender and intersex individuals, a move that has faced backlash from rights groups. Officials stated the aim is to ensure a more equitable distribution of military service obligations. The proposed legislation will require legislative approval. These measures reflect Taiwan's ongoing efforts to strengthen its defense preparedness amidst regional security concerns.
