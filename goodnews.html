San Diego County to Release Sterile Male Mexican Fruit Flies to Combat Infestation

2026-06-29 20:30:43 EDT

Millions of sterile male Mexican fruit flies are set to be released across parts of San Diego County to combat a growing infestation, following the detection of a mated female fly. This "sterile insect technique" involves dispersing male flies to mate with wild females, thereby halting reproduction and dramatically reducing the pest population. Mexican fruit flies are highly destructive, capable of destroying or damaging a wide array of fruits, nuts, and vegetables, and their presence has already led to expanded quarantine zones in the county. This proactive measure aims to protect California's agriculture from one of the world's most harmful pests, which typically enters the state via infested produce.
