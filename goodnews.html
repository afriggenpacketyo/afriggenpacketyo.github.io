Another widely accepted explanation links it to the idiom "for the love of the game," implying that a player's participation is for passion, regardless of their score. Despite modern alternatives like "bagel" or simply "zero," "love" persists in tennis, reflecting the sport's strong and enduring respect for its traditions and unique scoring system.
