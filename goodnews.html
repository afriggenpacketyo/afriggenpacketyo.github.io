Fact Check: European Sexual Assault Statistics Misleading for Cross-Country Comparison

2025-10-25 14:58:00 EDT

A fact-check article debunks a viral chart claiming the UK is the "sexual assault capital of Europe," explaining that comparing sexual assault statistics across countries is misleading. Data from sources like the World Population Review, derived from UNODC, explicitly warns against such comparisons due to major differences in legal definitions, reporting practices, and cultural factors. For example, the UK's 2003 overhaul of sexual offenses law to be consent-based and its method of counting multiple offenses per perpetrator inflate its figures compared to nations with narrower definitions. Criminologists advocate for victim surveys as a more accurate measure, as police statistics only reflect reported incidents.
