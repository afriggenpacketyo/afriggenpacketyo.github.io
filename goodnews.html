Wiseman's unique style eschewed narration, interviews, and traditional film scores, instead capturing raw, unscripted life within institutions like prisons and hospitals. He believed documentaries should be as emotionally rich as fiction films, providing viewers with information to form their own conclusions. Despite his meticulous editing process, Wiseman maintained that his films were his personal interpretation of reality, not "The Truth." His legacy includes 44 films, many shown on PBS, and a lasting influence on film schools.
