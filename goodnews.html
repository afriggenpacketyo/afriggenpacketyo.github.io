Lower Basin States Propose Voluntary Water-Saving Plan for Colorado River

2026-05-03 15:32:57 EDT

California, Arizona, and Nevada have jointly proposed a three-year plan for voluntary water-saving measures from the Colorado River, aiming to conserve 3.2 million acre-feet of water by 2028. This initiative seeks to stabilize historically low water levels in Lake Mead and Lake Powell, which supply 40 million people in the American West, while broader negotiations among all seven basin states remain stalled. The plan also includes an additional 700,000 acre-feet through conservation and infrastructure improvements, alongside a conservation pool for tribal trust obligations. The proposal requires approval from state water agencies and the Arizona legislature, and cooperation from the federal government, amidst growing pressure from record heat and significantly reduced snowpack in the upper basin.
