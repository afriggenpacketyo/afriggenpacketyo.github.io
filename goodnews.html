2025-05-26 01:04:41 EDT

Businesses are minimizing the impact of tariffs by utilizing a decades-old piece of legislation known as the "first sale rule." This rule allows U.S. importers to calculate customs duties based on the price of the first sale in a series of transactions, potentially reducing duty costs by stripping out the middleman's profit. While the rule has been around since 1988, it has gained renewed attention amid recent tariff regimes. To apply the rule, businesses must have at least two sales involving an overseas producer and an intermediary, sales must be at arm's length, there must be proof the item was destined for the U.S., and documentation of the first sale price is required.
