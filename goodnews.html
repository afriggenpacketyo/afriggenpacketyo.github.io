Paleontologists Uncover 500-Million-Year-Old Three-Eyed Predator

2025-05-17 16:38:36 EDT

Paleontologists have discovered the fossilized remains of a 500-million-year-old, three-eyed predator named Mosura fentoni, also known as the "sea moth." The creature, which roamed the seas during the Cambrian period, was about the length of an index finger and had a circular mouth lined with teeth. Researchers believe the Mosura used its body flaps to swim, similar to modern stingrays, sharing the waters with fellow radiodont and early apex predator, the iconic Anomalocaris canadensis. The discovery provides new insights into the diversity of life on Earth prior to the Cambrian explosion.
