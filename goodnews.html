Tiny Zooplankton Play Major Role in Carbon Sequestration, New Research Finds

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research reveals that tiny marine animals called zooplankton are unsung heroes in the fight against global warming due to their epic annual migration in the Southern Ocean. These creatures gorge on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface, grow fat, and then sink hundreds of meters into the deep ocean to burn that fat, effectively locking away vast amounts of carbon. This process, known as the seasonal vertical migration pump, stores as much carbon as the annual emissions of approximately 55 million cars. The findings highlight a crucial, previously underestimated natural mechanism for mitigating climate change.
