The Surprising Century-Long History of the Word 'Dude'

2025-07-30 05:00:00 EDT

The word "dude," often associated with 1980s surfer culture, actually originated in New York City a century earlier. Linguists trace its first use to the 1880s as a term for young, fashion-obsessed men who emulated a flashy, Anglophile style, likely as a reference to "Yankee Doodle Dandy." The term later evolved to describe East Coast tourists visiting "dude ranches" out West. In the mid-20th century, it was adopted by African American and Mexican American communities as a marker of solidarity before being popularized by California subcultures and Hollywood, eventually becoming a widely used, gender-neutral term of familiarity.
