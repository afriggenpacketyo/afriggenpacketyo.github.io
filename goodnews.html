China Develops New Caesium Extraction Technology to Reduce Import Reliance

This innovative extraction technology has the potential to markedly increase China's domestic supply and bolster its overall resource independence.

Google's Debug Program Plans to Release Millions of Sterile Mosquitoes in US

2026-06-02 09:43:00 EDT

Google's Debug program intends to release millions of male mosquitoes, genetically modified with Wolbachia bacteria, in Florida and California as an innovative strategy to combat disease-carrying Aedes aegypti mosquitoes. These sterile male mosquitoes, which do not bite humans, will mate with wild females, preventing their eggs from hatching and consequently reducing the overall mosquito population over generations. This targeted, environmentally friendly approach focuses on an invasive species responsible for transmitting illnesses like dengue, yellow fever, and Zika virus, without adversely impacting native ecosystems since Aedes aegypti is not a primary food source for local wildlife. The project, currently awaiting EPA approval, aims to significantly diminish the presence of these harmful mosquitoes and curb disease transmission.
