Arthur Konnerth, a renowned neuroscientist, is departing Germany to take on a full-time position in China, marking a significant move in the global scientific community. Konnerth is celebrated for his groundbreaking contributions to modern neuroscience, including pioneering the in vitro brain slice patch-clamp recording method. This technique, which he helped transform into a standard method in 1989, allows scientists to measure electrical currents in individual brain cells connected within tissue slices. Additionally, his team introduced an imaging method in 2003 that enabled the observation of entire brain cell networks in action, fundamentally advancing the study of how the brain controls behavior.
