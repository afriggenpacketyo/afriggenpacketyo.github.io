Brazil Deploys 'Good Mosquitoes' in Massive Effort to Combat Dengue Fever

2025-07-26 09:19:53 EDT

Brazil is scaling up a public health initiative that releases millions of mosquitoes specially engineered to combat the spread of dengue and other viruses. These "Wolbitos," bred by the nonprofit World Mosquito Program, are infected with a bacteria called Wolbachia, which prevents the dengue virus from replicating inside them. When these mosquitoes mate with the wild population, they pass on the bacteria, effectively neutralizing their ability to transmit the disease. A pilot program in the city of Niterói resulted in a 90% drop in dengue cases, prompting the Brazilian government to build a massive new factory to produce billions more.
