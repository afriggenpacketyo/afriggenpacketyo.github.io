Ancient Whale Graveyard Discovered in Indian Ocean's Deepest Reaches

2026-06-11 21:00:15 EDT

Researchers have discovered the Earth's deepest and most extensive known accumulation of whale fossils in the Indian Ocean, with some remains dating back 5.3 million years. This ancient graveyard, detailed in a Nature paper, reveals unique whale-fall ecosystems and offers new insights into cetacean evolution. A whale fall is a rare natural phenomenon where a carcass sinks to the ocean floor, supporting deep-sea scavengers and ecosystems for extended periods. This discovery reshapes understanding of the limits and biogeography of these deep-sea environments, establishing certain deep-sea floors as vital fossil archives for tracing marine life history.
