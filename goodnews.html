Explainer Details Causes and Solutions for Urban Heat Islands

2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

Urban areas, known as "urban heat islands," experience significantly higher temperatures than their rural surroundings due to heat-absorbing materials like concrete and asphalt, and human activities. This effect, which can raise temperatures by up to 15 degrees Celsius, intensifies heatwaves for the more than half of the world's population living in cities. The phenomenon is caused by a lack of cooling vegetation, the trapping of heat in "urban canyons" between tall buildings, and pollution. Solutions to mitigate this effect include increasing green spaces, installing reflective "cool roofs" and pavements, and implementing better urban planning.
