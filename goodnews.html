NASA's Van Allen Probe Safely Re-enters Earth's Atmosphere After Extended Mission

2026-03-12 07:23:36 EDT

NASA's Van Allen Probe spacecraft, weighing over 1,300 pounds (600kg), successfully re-entered Earth's atmosphere over the Eastern Pacific region, nearly 14 years after its launch. Most of the probe was expected to burn up during re-entry, with NASA assessing a very low one-in-4,200 chance of harm from any surviving components. Originally designed for a two-year mission, the probe operated for almost seven years, collecting unprecedented data on Earth's two permanent radiation belts. These Van Allen belts play a crucial role in shielding Earth from harmful cosmic radiation, solar storms, and streaming solar wind, thereby protecting both human spaceflight and critical technology.
