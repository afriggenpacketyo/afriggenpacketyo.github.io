The Historical Practice of Walking Turkeys to London for Christmas

2025-12-24 00:32:22 EST

Long before modern refrigeration and railways, Christmas turkeys were herded on foot from Norfolk and Suffolk farms on a three-month journey to London markets, a practice known as turkey droving. Thousands of birds, including the nearly lost Norfolk Black breed, would cover about three miles daily, some even fitted with tiny leather boots or protected with hot tar and sand on their feet. Drovers would forage for berries, acorns, and corn along the way to feed the birds. This unique tradition, driven by demand from wealthy Victorian families, eventually ceased with the advent of steam engines and improved transportation methods in the late 1800s.
