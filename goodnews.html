2025-08-01 01:20:58 EDT

In an innovative effort to combat rhino poaching, a South African university has launched the Rhisotope Project, which involves injecting rhino horns with harmless radioactive isotopes. The goal is to make the horns easily detectable by radiation scanners at borders and airports, thereby disrupting trafficking networks and leading to arrests. Following successful trials, five rhinos were treated in the project's official launch. Conservationists hope this method will provide a new layer of protection for the world's dwindling rhino population, which has been devastated by the illegal horn trade.
