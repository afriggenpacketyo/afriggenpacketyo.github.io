The Word 'OK' Traces its Origins to a 19th-Century Joke and a Presidential Campaign

2025-07-23 17:27:00 EDT

The ubiquitous word "OK" is a uniquely American invention that originated as a joke in the 1830s. Scholars trace its first appearance to a Boston newspaper in 1839 as a playful, misspelled abbreviation of "all correct" written as "oll korrect." The term gained widespread popularity during the 1840 presidential campaign of Martin Van Buren, whose nickname "Old Kinderhook" conveniently matched the initials. His supporters formed "O.K. Clubs," cementing the term in the American lexicon. Its simplicity and versatility allowed it to spread globally, becoming one of the most recognized words on the planet.
