The Zidanku Silk Manuscripts, dating back to the Warring States Period, were illegally excavated in 1942 and later acquired by an American collector. Their repatriation follows diplomatic efforts by the National Cultural Heritage Administration of China and the Smithsonian's new ethical returns policy. Volume I, a larger and more complete work, remains privately owned, and efforts are underway to secure its return.
