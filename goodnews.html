Four DBT Skills to Manage Intense Emotional Distress

2026-07-20 05:00:00 EDT

This article introduces four dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) skills, known as TIP, for quickly reducing intense emotional distress. Developed by psychologist Marsha Linehan, DBT techniques are now widely recognized for managing difficult emotions. The TIP acronym stands for temperature, intense exercise, paced breathing, and paired muscle relaxation. These skills, such as plunging one's face into cold water or performing quick bursts of exercise, aim to lower emotional intensity from extreme to moderate levels, providing clarity to address underlying issues. The article emphasizes that intense moments of distress are temporary and will eventually pass.

2026-07-20 02:00:10 EDT
