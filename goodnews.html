Researchers Identify Malnutrition-Related Diabetes as a Distinct Condition Requiring Unique Treatment

2025-09-30 07:00:09 EDT

Researchers are advocating for the formal recognition of malnutrition-related diabetes mellitus (MRDM) as a distinct condition, now classified as 'type 5' diabetes. This form of the disease develops in chronically undernourished individuals with a low BMI, whose pancreas underproduces insulin but who do not exhibit the insulin resistance characteristic of type 2 diabetes. While not yet officially recognized by the American Diabetes Association or WHO, proponents argue its unique biological mechanisms warrant a separate classification. Treatment for type 5 diabetes focuses on nutritional rehabilitation and carefully managed insulin or oral medications to avoid dangerously low blood sugar levels.
