Cardiologists are advising individuals with high blood pressure to avoid taking licorice root supplements. The herb contains a substance called glycyrrhizin, which can dangerously elevate blood pressure by causing the body to retain sodium and lose potassium. This imbalance puts additional strain on the cardiovascular system and can lead to serious complications, including arrhythmias. The FDA has previously warned that consuming even two ounces of black licorice daily for two weeks can cause significant cardiovascular issues. Instead of supplements, doctors recommend proven lifestyle changes like the DASH or Mediterranean diets.
