AI Chatbots Exhibit Distinctive "Negative Parallelism" Writing Style

2026-07-12 07:30:00 EDT

AI chatbots frequently exhibit a distinctive writing tic known as "negative parallelism," where sentences state what something isn't before defining what it is, exemplified by phrases like "It's not X; it's Y." This construction has reportedly quadrupled in corporate communications since 2023 and appears three times more often in AI writing than in human-generated text, according to researchers. While effective when used judiciously, its pervasive use by models such as ChatGPT can make AI-generated content feel formulaic. Experts speculate its prevalence derives from initial human training data and the chatbots' text-prediction mechanisms. The difficulty in eradicating this ingrained pattern is exacerbated as AI models increasingly train on AI-generated text, potentially leading to "model collapse."
