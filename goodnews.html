Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Weather Data Collection

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory is marking a major milestone, celebrating 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation, the longest such record in the UK and Ireland. Since July 14, 1795, observers have manually recorded temperature and air pressure, creating an invaluable climate dataset. This tradition continues today, with a human observer still logging data by hand, providing a unique contrast to modern automated stations. The records have captured significant meteorological events, from major storms to the first observations of noctilucent clouds, and have contributed to the development of meteorology, including the invention of the four-cup anemometer.
