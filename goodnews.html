Nobel Laureate Physicist Chen-ning Yang Dies at 103

2025-10-18 00:23:49 EDT

Renowned Nobel Prize-winning theoretical physicist Chen-ning Yang has passed away in Beijing at the age of 103. Often ranked alongside Albert Einstein for his profound contributions, Yang was celebrated for his groundbreaking work that significantly advanced the field of physics. In 1954, he co-authored the seminal Yang-Mills theory with Robert Mills, a set of equations that proved as crucial to physics as Einstein's theory of relativity. This theory provided the mathematical framework for what later became known as the Standard Model, which unifies three of nature's four fundamental forces—electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions—and explains the behavior of all known elementary particles. His passing marks the end of an exceptionally long and impactful life dedicated to expanding scientific understanding.
