Micro-Robots Show Promise in Treating Sinus Infections Without Antibiotics

2025-07-07 13:00:58 EDT

Scientists have developed microscopic robots capable of treating sinus infections by directly targeting and eliminating bacteria within the nasal cavity. These dust-sized micro-robots, made of magnetic particles and copper atoms, are guided by magnets to the infection site. Once in position, a fiber optic light heats the particles, enabling them to break through mucus and destroy the bacteria, offering a precise, drug-free alternative to traditional antibiotics. While animal trials in pigs and rabbits have been successful and shown no tissue damage, further research is needed to ensure the technology's safety and efficacy for human use, which could help combat antibiotic resistance.
