Ancient Tombs from Western Xia Dynasty in China Added to UNESCO World Heritage List

2025-07-12 05:00:08 EDT

A vast complex of ancient tombs from China's Western Xia dynasty has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage site. Located near the city of Yinchuan, the 40-square-kilometer site includes nine imperial mausoleums and over 270 subordinate tombs dating back to a period between 1038 and 1227. The World Heritage Committee recognized the site as a significant testament to the cultural fusion and interaction between diverse traditions along the historic Silk Road. The complex also features extensive flood control structures and a large architectural site used for worship and management.
