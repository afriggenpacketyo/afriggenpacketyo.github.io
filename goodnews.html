A reverse mortgage allows homeowners to convert home equity into cash, but concerns exist about outliving the loan. The loan does not expire or become due as long as the borrower lives in the home and meets obligations like paying taxes and insurance. However, it is possible to outlive the home's equity, as the loan balance grows with interest and can eventually exceed the property's value, leaving little for heirs.
