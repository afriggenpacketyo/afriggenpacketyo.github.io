The Origin Story of the Word Radar

2025-05-14 16:00:21 EDT

Following radar outages at some of the country's busiest airports, the nation's aviation infrastructure is again under scrutiny. The word radar is a near acronym that stands for RAdio Detection And Ranging and was coined by the U.S. Navy in either 1940 or 1941. Radar works by emitting pulses of radio waves that bounce off of an object, and by timing how long it takes for the signal to go out and come back, one can calculate how far away something is. The application dates back to the late 19th century, and the British military unleashed the true potential of radar technology under pressure from the Germans in the 1930s.
