Coastal Rail Service Between San Diego and Orange Counties to Resume After Landslide Repairs

2025-05-29 21:20:44 EDT

Coastal rail service connecting Orange and San Diego counties is set to resume on June 7 after nearly six weeks of repairs to reinforce tracks vulnerable to landslides and coastal erosion. The California Coastal Commission approved emergency construction, involving the placement of 5,900 tons of boulders and 240,000 cubic yards of sand to stabilize the rail line. The Orange County Transportation Authority (OCTA) is also constructing a 1,400-foot-long catchment beside the rail, with the total project cost estimated at $300 million. The OCTA acknowledged the recurring interruptions to passenger rail service due to eroding bluffs in San Clemente.
