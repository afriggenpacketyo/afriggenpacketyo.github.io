Largest Known Piece of Mars on Earth to Be Auctioned for up to $4 Million

2025-07-10 21:11:51 EDT

Sotheby's is auctioning NWA 16788, the largest known piece of Mars on Earth, with an estimated value of up to $4 million. The meteorite, weighing just over 54 pounds, represents about 6.5% of all known Martian material on our planet. It is believed to have been dislodged from Mars by an asteroid strike and traveled 140 million miles before landing in the Sahara Desert, where it was discovered in 2023. Martian meteorites are exceedingly rare, comprising less than 1% of all meteorites found on Earth. The auction provides a unique opportunity for a private collector to own a significant piece of the red planet.
