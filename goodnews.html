René Cárdenas, Pioneering Spanish-Language Dodgers Announcer, Dies at 96

2026-05-11 16:49:45 EDT

René Cárdenas, a groundbreaking figure in sports broadcasting, has died at 96. He was the first radio announcer to broadcast Major League Baseball games in Spanish for a domestic audience, beginning with the Dodgers in 1958. Cárdenas also helped establish Spanish-language broadcasts for the Houston Astros and Texas Rangers, calling games for 38 seasons. His innovative style and professionalism paved the way for future Latino broadcasters, including Jaime Jarrín, who recognized Cárdenas's pivotal role. Born in Nicaragua, Cárdenas was instrumental in bringing baseball to a wider Spanish-speaking audience in the United States, leaving an enduring legacy.
