Chinese Exporters Use Letters of Credit to Navigate Trade War

2025-06-13 02:00:17 EDT

Following the imposition of reciprocal tariffs on imports from China by the United States, Beijing has encouraged the increased use of letters of credit (L/Cs) to help Chinese exporters shift their focus to the domestic market. Domestic L/Cs, issued by major Chinese banks, serve as a guarantee to the seller that the agreed amount will be paid by the buyer in time, enhancing payment security and credit. Unlike international L/Cs, which can only be used in transactions involving physical goods, domestic L/Cs can also be used in the trade in services. The payment period for domestic L/Cs is generally one year.
