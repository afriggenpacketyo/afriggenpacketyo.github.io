Antivenom Developed From Man Bitten Over 200 Times by Snakes

2025-05-03 02:29:15 EDT

Scientists have developed an "unparalleled" antivenom using antibodies found in the blood of Tim Friede, a US man who deliberately injected himself with snake venom for nearly two decades. The antibodies protect against fatal doses from a wide range of venomous snake species in animal tests, offering a potential solution to the challenge of matching antivenoms to specific snake species. Friede's 18-year mission, documented on YouTube, aims to create a universal antivenom to combat snakebites, which kill up to 140,000 people annually. The research identified two broadly neutralising antibodies that could target two classes of neurotoxin, and a cocktail of these antibodies protected mice from fatal doses of venom from 13 of 19 deadly snake species.
