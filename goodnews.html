Chinese Researchers Announce Cheaper, In-Body CAR-T Cancer Therapy

2025-07-19 02:00:12 EDT

Scientists in China have developed a new method to produce CAR-T cell therapies directly within the human body, potentially making the advanced treatment for blood cancers and other diseases more accessible and affordable. This innovative gene therapy approach avoids the costly and complex process of engineering cells in a lab, which can currently exceed $139,200 per treatment. Researchers from a Wuhan-based hospital successfully used this in-vivo technique on four patients with multiple myeloma, a common form of blood cancer. The breakthrough, published in The Lancet, could significantly reduce costs and simplify the delivery of this promising immunotherapy worldwide.
