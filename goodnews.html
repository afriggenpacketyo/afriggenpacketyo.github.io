The Importance of Cleaning Hummingbird Feeders to Protect Bird Health

2025-06-10 07:30:00 EDT

Hummingbird feeders can be a valuable supplementary food source for these tiny birds, especially during migration, but dirty feeders can be deadly. Spoilage of sugar water leads to microbial growth, causing infections like candida, which swells the birds' tongues and hinders feeding. Wildlife rehabbers frequently rescue hummingbirds with feeder-related infections, emphasizing the need for regular cleaning and nectar replacement. To maintain a healthy feeder, use a 1:4 sugar-to-water ratio, avoid additives, and clean the feeder every one to three days, scrubbing with vinegar or hydrogen peroxide if mold is present. Planting native wildflowers is another way to support local hummingbird populations.
