Donation After Circulatory Death (DCD) Transforms US Organ Transplantation

2026-07-23 06:20:00 EDT

Organ donation after circulatory death (DCD) has become a significant breakthrough in American transplantation, now accounting for nearly half of all deceased organ donors. DCD allows patients who are dying but not brain-dead to donate organs after life support is withdrawn and circulation ceases. This method has dramatically increased the supply of organs, saving thousands of lives and shortening waiting lists. However, its growth also raises ethical questions about the delicate interval between the decision to withdraw life support and the declaration of death, particularly concerning new techniques like normothermic regional perfusion (NRP).
