Antarctica's First Dinosaur Bone Discovered After 40 Years in a Drawer

2026-06-29 01:10:46 EDT

An unassuming fossil, which spent 40 years overlooked in a drawer at the British Antarctic Survey, has been identified as the first dinosaur bone ever discovered on the continent. The specimen, a tail bone from a Titanosaur, a group renowned for including the largest dinosaurs to walk the Earth, was initially unearthed in 1985 but its significance was not recognized at the time. This groundbreaking discovery sheds new light on prehistoric life in Antarctica, an area with a historically sparse fossil record. It demonstrates that the now-frozen continent once harbored a vibrant and diverse ecosystem of massive creatures approximately 80 million years ago, highlighting the potential for further discoveries within existing collections.
