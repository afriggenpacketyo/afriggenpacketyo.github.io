Scientific Explainer Details the Formation and Dangers of Tsunamis

2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

Following the major earthquake off Russia's coast, this article explains the science behind tsunamis. Generated primarily by strong, shallow, underwater earthquakes that cause vertical shifts in the seafloor, tsunamis can travel across deep oceans at speeds comparable to a jet airplane. As they approach shallower coastal waters, their speed decreases but their wave height and power increase dramatically, making them incredibly destructive. While forecasting remains challenging, scientists use tide gauges and deep-ocean pressure sensors to predict their arrival and potential impact, emphasizing the importance of heeding evacuation warnings immediately.
