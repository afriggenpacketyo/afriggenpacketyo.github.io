Chinese Researchers Develop World's First Free Flight Control Software for Bamboo Drones

2026-04-04 02:00:10 EDT

A Chinese research team has developed and released the world's first free flight control software specifically designed for bamboo drones, addressing the unique engineering challenges posed by such materials. Unlike conventional airframes, bamboo structures introduce low-frequency vibrations, typically in the 8-20 hertz range, which standard flight controllers struggle to manage. The team's innovation involved redesigning control algorithms and tuning an extended Kalman filter, leveraging bamboo's natural vibration-damping characteristics. This significantly reduced control latency from 15-20 milliseconds to as low as 8-10 milliseconds, thereby improving responsiveness while maintaining stability and paving the way for the industrialization of bamboo-based unmanned aerial vehicles.
