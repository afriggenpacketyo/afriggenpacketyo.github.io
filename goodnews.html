2026-05-29 12:16:54 EDT

Federal law largely shields Social Security benefits from garnishment by commercial creditors, even if a debt is personally assumed through co-signing or joint accounts. While exceptions exist for federal obligations like taxes or student loans, private debts usually cannot seize benefits.
