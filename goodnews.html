The Historical and Economic Origins of Tipping Culture in the United States

2025-07-27 07:30:00 EDT

The practice of tipping in the United States has deep and controversial roots, originating not as a reward for good service but as a system to avoid paying fair wages. This article explains that the custom became widespread after the Civil War, allowing employers to hire newly freed Black workers without providing them a real wage, instead suggesting customers pay a small gratuity. This history led to the creation of the subminimum wage for tipped workers, which under federal law is just $2.13 an hour, a figure that has not changed since 1991. Consequently, customer tips are not just a bonus but are essential for service workers to reach the standard federal minimum wage.
