German Traditions of St. Martin's Day and Carnival Share Historical Roots

2025-11-10 10:51:00 EST

In Germany, November 11 marks both the solemn St. Martin's Day, celebrated with children's lantern parades, and the boisterous start of the Carnival season. The two seemingly contradictory events are historically linked, originating from the end of the medieval farming year and the period before a traditional six-week Advent fast. St. Martin's Day was a time for a final feast before the fasting began, evolving into the modern Carnival's exuberant celebration. The date's customs thus reflect a blend of charity, remembrance, and a final burst of revelry before a more contemplative season.
