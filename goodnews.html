China's central bank adjusts policies to support yuan and cross-border financing

2026-02-27 05:36:47 EST

In response to the strengthening of China's currency, the People's Bank of China has implemented two policy adjustments to reduce foreign exchange hedging costs and bolster cross-border yuan financing. The central bank has refined regulations for domestic banks involved in cross-border yuan interbank financing, explicitly supporting their role in providing stable yuan liquidity to offshore markets. These reforms aim to enhance the rule-based nature and transparency of managing such businesses, ensuring a smoother offshore yuan market liquidity supply. Additionally, the central bank is cutting the foreign exchange risk reserve ratio for forward forex sales from 20% to zero, effective Monday, to lower hedging costs for businesses and potentially ease demand for the yuan, which could lead to a slight weakening of the currency.
