Dirty Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly

2025-06-10 14:34:21 EDT

This article discusses the importance of maintaining clean hummingbird feeders to prevent harm or death to the birds. While hummingbird feeders can provide a supplementary food source, especially when natural wildflowers are scarce, dirty feeders can harbor harmful microbes that cause infections in hummingbirds. These infections, such as candida, can lead to swollen tongues and difficulty feeding, potentially causing starvation. The solution involves regularly cleaning feeders every two to three days with hot water or a vinegar and water solution, and refilling them with a mixture of one part refined white sugar to four parts water, avoiding red food coloring and distilled water.
