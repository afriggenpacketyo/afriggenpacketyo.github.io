Understanding Effective Tax Rates Can Inform Roth vs. Pretax 401(k) Decisions

2025-10-07 09:39:05 EDT

Financial planners suggest that when choosing between pretax and Roth 401(k) contributions, savers should consider their effective tax rate rather than just their marginal tax rate. Your marginal rate is the tax on your last dollar earned, while the effective rate is the average rate paid on your total income, which is often much lower. An individual might be in a high marginal bracket during their working years but have a significantly lower effective tax rate in retirement when their income is drawn from savings. This calculation could make pretax contributions more advantageous than commonly believed, as future withdrawals might be taxed at a lower overall rate than the initial savings.
