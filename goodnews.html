Understanding 401(k) Vesting: Employer-Matched Funds May Not Be Yours Immediately

2025-11-28 11:17:50 EST

Many employees receiving a 401(k) match from their employer may not have immediate ownership of those funds due to vesting schedules. These schedules require an employee to work for a specific period, sometimes up to six years, before they gain full rights to the matching contributions. While some companies offer immediate vesting, the majority use either "cliff" vesting (100% ownership after a set time) or "graded" vesting (ownership increases incrementally over several years). This practice is used to encourage employee retention but can result in workers forfeiting thousands of dollars in retirement savings if they are laid off or leave their job prematurely.
