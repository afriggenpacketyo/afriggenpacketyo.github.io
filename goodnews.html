This legal practice involves altering an item's materials or dimensions to fit into a different product classification code. For example, some manufacturers are bundling steel and aluminum elements into final products to qualify for lower duties under Section 232. Companies must balance redesigning products with avoiding fraudulent misrepresentation of product classifications, as illustrated by Ford's case of misclassifying cargo vans.
