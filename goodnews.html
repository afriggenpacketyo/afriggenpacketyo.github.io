Nepal Offers Free Mountain Climbing Permits as Everest Fees Rise

2025-08-11 21:55:25 EDT

Nepal is offering free climbing permits for 97 of its Himalayan mountains for the next two years, a strategic move to boost tourism in its remote Karnali and Sudurpaschim provinces. This initiative aims to promote "unexplored tourism products and destinations," contrasting with the first Mount Everest permit fee increase in nearly a decade, which raises peak season costs to $15,000. While mountaineering is a significant revenue source for Nepal, questions remain regarding the country's plans to improve infrastructure and connectivity to these less-developed areas, and how local communities will cope with a potential influx of climbers.
