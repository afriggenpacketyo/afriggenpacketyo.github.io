Understanding the Nuances of Repeated Bankruptcy Filings and Alternative Debt Relief Options

2026-02-23 11:07:09 EST

There is no strict legal limit to the number of times an individual can file for bankruptcy, but mandatory waiting periods between filings vary significantly depending on the specific chapter chosen. For instance, Chapter 7 filings require an eight-year waiting period before another discharge can be granted, while Chapter 13 requires two to four years, depending on the nature of the previous filing. Filing before these periods expire means a discharge of debts will not be received, which largely defeats the primary purpose for most filers. Experts strongly advise exploring alternatives like debt settlement or credit counseling to mitigate severe long-term credit damage. (not investment advice)
