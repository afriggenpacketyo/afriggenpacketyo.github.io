Sociologist Challenges Misconceptions in German Migrant Crime Statistics

2026-04-19 05:21:00 EDT

Ahead of the 2025 German crime statistics release, sociologist Susann Prätor challenges the common misinterpretation of migrant crime rates, cautioning against comparing "apples to oranges." She emphasizes that young men are globally more prone to criminal activity, and non-German populations in Germany are, on average, significantly younger. Prätor also points out that studies show people perceived as foreign are nearly three times more likely to be reported to the police, which can inflate official statistics. She advocates for comprehensive studies on unreported crime to reveal underlying socioeconomic factors like domestic violence, lower education, and peer groups, providing a more accurate and nuanced understanding of the issue.
