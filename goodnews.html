Social Security Administration Processes Millions of Retroactive Payments Under Fairness Act

2025-06-04 16:37:31 EDT

The Social Security Administration (SSA) has processed 2.5 million retroactive payments for public pension holders who were previously restricted from receiving full benefits due to the Windfall Elimination Provision (WEP) and Government Pension Offset (GPO). The Social Security Fairness Act, signed into law in January 2025, eliminated these provisions, allowing the SSA to adjust benefits for 3.2 million people. Beneficiaries should have received a one-time retroactive payment by the end of March 2025, with increased monthly benefits starting in April. The SSA expects to update all beneficiary records by November 2025, and those who haven't received payments should verify their information with the agency. (not investment advice)
