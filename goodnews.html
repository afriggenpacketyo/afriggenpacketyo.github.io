Sir Andreas Whittam Smith, Co-founder of The Independent Newspaper, Dies at 88

2025-12-01 00:48:34 EST

Sir Andreas Whittam Smith, the visionary co-founder of The Independent newspaper, has died at the age of 88. He launched the publication in 1986 with colleagues, pioneering a non-partisan editorial stance that transformed British journalism. Described as a "radical and a pioneer," Sir Andreas previously worked at other prominent British papers before establishing The Independent, which quickly became known for its independent voice. Beyond journalism, he held significant roles, including First Estates Church Commissioner, overseeing the Church of England's investment portfolio, and President of the British Board of Film Classification. He was knighted in 2015 for his public service.

2025-11-30 20:47:00 EST

Ludwig A. Minelli, the pioneering founder of the Swiss right-to-die organization Dignitas, died at 92 through assisted death. Minelli, a former journalist and lawyer, founded Dignitas in 1998 and was instrumental in advocating for the decriminalization of assisted suicide, notably in Germany and through successful appeals to the European Court of Human Rights. His work championed individual autonomy in end-of-life decisions, allowing people from around the world to access Dignitas's services. Assisted death is now legal in several countries and U.S. states, reflecting a growing acceptance of the principles Minelli tirelessly defended.
