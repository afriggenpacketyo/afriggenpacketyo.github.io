French Fencer Cleared of Doping After 'Contaminated Kiss' Defense Succeeds

2025-07-08 08:08:36 EDT

The Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS) has cleared French Olympic fencer Ysaora Thibus of a doping violation, accepting her defense that she was contaminated by kissing her partner. Thibus tested positive for the anabolic substance ostarine, but judges found it was scientifically plausible that the substance was transferred via saliva from her then-partner, who was unknowingly using a product containing it. The World Anti-Doping Agency had sought a four-year ban, but the court's decision allows Thibus, a 2022 world champion, to continue her career without suspension.

TSA Begins Phasing Out Shoe Removal Requirement at Some US Airports

2025-07-08 07:17:00 EDT

The Transportation Security Administration (TSA) is no longer requiring passengers to remove their shoes during standard security screening at a number of U.S. airports. The change, which is being implemented in phases, has been observed at airports including Baltimore, Fort Lauderdale, and Philadelphia. This relaxes a rule that has been in place nationwide since 2006, following the attempted "shoe bomber" incident in 2001. The move, which does not affect the existing policy for TSA PreCheck members, is part of an effort to enhance the passenger experience while maintaining security.
