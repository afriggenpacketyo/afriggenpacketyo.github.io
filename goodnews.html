Cardiologist Explains Risks of Extreme Summer Heat on Heart Health

2025-07-12 05:00:40 EDT

A cardiologist from the Miami Cardiac & Vascular Institute explains that extreme summer heat forces the heart to work harder to maintain stable blood pressure. As the body heats up, blood vessels widen to cool down, increasing the heart's oxygen demand and strain. Studies show that even minor temperature increases can significantly raise heart rates and the risk of cardiovascular events like heart attacks, especially for those with pre-existing conditions. The article advises staying hydrated, avoiding midday heat, and watching for warning signs such as chest tightness, shortness of breath, or dizziness to prevent serious cardiac stress.

Ancient Western Xia Dynasty Tombs in China Added to UNESCO World Heritage List

2025-07-12 05:00:08 EDT

A vast tomb site in northwest China, dating back to the Western Xia dynasty (1038–1227), has been officially recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site. Located near the city of Yinchuan, the 40-square-kilometer area includes nine imperial mausoleums and over 270 subordinate tombs belonging to the Tangut people. The site also features ancient flood control structures and a large architectural complex used for worship and management. According to the World Heritage Committee, this designation acknowledges the site as a significant testament to the cultural fusion and interaction between diverse traditions during that historical period.
