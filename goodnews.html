Understanding Medicare's 3-Day Rule for Skilled Nursing Facility Coverage

2025-09-11 11:08:05 EDT

Medicare's "3-day rule" is a crucial regulation requiring a minimum three-consecutive-day inpatient hospital stay for Part A to cover subsequent skilled nursing facility (SNF) care. This rule significantly impacts out-of-pocket costs, as failing to meet it can leave beneficiaries responsible for tens of thousands of dollars in SNF expenses. The article clarifies that observation status or emergency room time does not count towards the three days, a common pitfall. While restrictive, exemptions exist for Medicare Advantage plans and Accountable Care Organizations, making it vital for seniors to understand these nuances and potential appeal rights to ensure coverage.
