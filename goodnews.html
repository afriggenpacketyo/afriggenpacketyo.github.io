2026-07-14 09:33:40 EDT

While the Fed's policy rate influences borrowing costs, fixed-rate mortgages primarily track the 10-year Treasury yield, which responds to market expectations about inflation and Fed policy over the long term. When investors anticipate cooling inflation, the 10-year yield can drop, subsequently lowering mortgage rates. Additionally, actions by entities like Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac to purchase mortgage-backed securities can also reduce rates.
