NASA Launches Robotic Mission to Rescue Falling Swift Telescope

2026-07-03 06:28:17 EDT

NASA has launched the LINK spacecraft, a three-armed robot, on an ambitious and high-risk mission to rescue the Swift observatory, a crucial telescope detecting the universe's most powerful explosions, from crashing to Earth. Swift is gradually falling due to increased solar activity expanding Earth's atmosphere, which drags on the observatory and lowers its altitude. The LINK craft, developed by Katalyst Space Technologies, will attempt to slowly approach, grab, and gently haul Swift back to a stable 373-mile orbit over the next two to three months. This unprecedented rescue operation underscores Swift's immense scientific value, as it provides unique means to study high-energy cosmic phenomena, making its preservation a top priority for the science community.

Its discovery, 250 years later, offers unique insights into the early distribution and profound importance of the Declaration for American revolutionaries. Conservation efforts have since been undertaken to repair and stabilize this precious artifact, which serves as a tangible link to the nascent stages of American independence.
