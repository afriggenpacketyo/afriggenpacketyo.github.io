2025-07-06 03:49:52 EDT

A new clinical trial has shown that a single gene therapy injection can significantly reverse hearing loss in both children and adults with a specific genetic form of deafness. The therapy delivers a healthy copy of the OTOF gene to the inner ear, which is responsible for producing a protein essential for transmitting sound signals to the brain. Researchers from Sweden’s Karolinska Institutet reported that all 10 participants in the small-scale trial showed considerable hearing improvement within six months, with some younger patients regaining almost full hearing. The treatment was found to be safe and well-tolerated, offering hope for future therapies targeting other genetic causes of deafness.
