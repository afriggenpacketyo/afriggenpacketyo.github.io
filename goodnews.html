Trump Administration Confident in Securing Investors for Alaska LNG Project Despite High Costs

2025-06-02 17:46:05 EDT

The Trump administration remains confident in attracting investors for the Alaska LNG project, a massive liquified natural gas initiative, despite its $40 billion price tag. Energy Secretary Chris Wright stated that countries seeking to reduce their trade deficit with the U.S. could invest in American energy. The project involves constructing an 800-mile pipeline from the North Slope to the Cook Inlet, where the gas would be cooled and shipped to Asian allies. While energy analysts are skeptical due to the project's lack of clear commercial logic, the administration plans to build the project in stages, initially serving domestic demand. Southern California Water Agencies Settle 15-Year Legal Battle Over Water Costs

2025-06-02 17:26:47 EDT

The San Diego County Water Authority and the Metropolitan Water District of Southern California have settled a 15-year legal battle over water costs. The agreement establishes the price the Water Authority will pay for delivering water supplies, resolving a long-standing dispute. The settlement is expected to foster greater collaboration among the agencies, improving their finances and enabling more efficient water distribution. The Water Authority will now pay a fixed price to MWD for delivering supplies, starting at $671 per acre-foot in 2026, with annual adjustments for inflation. The lawmakers emphasized California's importance as the nation's largest agricultural state, with farms receiving significant cash receipts. The administration will keep eight of the nine offices open, except for one in Mt. Shasta, which is still under discussion.
