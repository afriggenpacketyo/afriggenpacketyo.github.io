Ancient Stone Age Settlement Discovered Off Denmark Coast, Submerged by Rising Seas Millennia Ago

2025-08-26 09:31:11 EDT

An international archaeological project has unearthed a Stone Age settlement off Denmark's coast, which was submerged by rising sea levels approximately 8,500 years ago following the last ice age. Divers have recovered significant artifacts, including animal bones, stone tools, and a seal tooth, providing a unique "time capsule" of coastal hunter-gatherer life. Researchers are employing dendrochronology to precisely date preserved submerged trees, enabling them to map the rapid historical changes in sea levels that forced ancient populations inland. This discovery offers crucial insights into how past societies adapted to shifting coastlines, providing valuable context for today's challenges with climate change-driven sea level rise.
