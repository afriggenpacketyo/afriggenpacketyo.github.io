Creditors Can Garnish Wages Quickly After Lawsuit Judgment, Depending on Response

2026-04-13 12:35:50 EDT

Creditors can initiate wage garnishment relatively quickly after obtaining a legal judgment, potentially within one to three months if a borrower does not respond to a lawsuit. The process involves filing the lawsuit, serving notice, a response window for the debtor, obtaining a judgment, and then requesting a garnishment order sent to the employer. Failure to respond significantly speeds up the timeline, leading to a default judgment. State laws vary regarding wage garnishment, with some states having restrictions. Proactive steps like debt management plans, settlement, or bankruptcy can help prevent or interrupt garnishment.
