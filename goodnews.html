Study Indicates Pigs May Have Been Domesticated in China as Early as Neolithic Period

2025-06-12 21:00:11 EDT

Research suggests that pigs were already domesticated in southern China around 8,000 years ago, according to archaeologists from Chinese and US institutes. The study analyzed two early Neolithic sites in the Yangtze Delta region, finding that pigs were consuming humans' cooked foods and waste. These findings suggest that the domestication process of wild boars occurred alongside the development of rice cultivation and sedentary lifestyles in ancient society in southern China. This challenges the conventional view that pigs were first domesticated in the Near East.
