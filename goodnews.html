Explainer on the Two-Year Rule for Filing Chapter 13 Bankruptcy

2025-10-10 11:34:07 EDT

This article clarifies a critical timing requirement known as the "two-year rule" for individuals considering filing for Chapter 13 bankruptcy for a second time. Under U.S. Bankruptcy Code, a person who received a discharge in a prior Chapter 13 case cannot receive another discharge in a new Chapter 13 case unless the new case is filed at least two years after the original filing date of the first case. This distinction is crucial, as the clock starts from the initial filing date, not the discharge date. Misunderstanding this rule can result in proceeding with a second bankruptcy without the benefit of having unsecured debts wiped out.
