2025-10-02 07:00:00 EDT

While incidence rates for certain cancers have nearly doubled since 1990, the study found that rates of metastatic cases and overall death rates have remained relatively flat. This indicates that doctors may be identifying more cancers that would not have become life-threatening, a phenomenon known as overdiagnosis. However, the findings are nuanced, as mortality has genuinely increased for some cancers like colorectal, highlighting the complexity of interpreting these public health trends.
