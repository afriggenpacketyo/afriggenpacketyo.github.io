2026-05-09 07:20:44 EDT

Studies indicate that simple, brief interactions with strangers can profoundly boost happiness and a sense of belonging, with lasting positive impacts on individuals. Psychologist Gillian Sandstrom's research demonstrates that people tend to report greater happiness on days when they engage in more "weak tie" interactions, such as exchanging smiles with a vendor or having a short chat with a barista. University students assigned to engage with strangers reported increased comfort, reduced isolation, and a notable improvement in their mood, reinforcing the idea that intentional acts of kindness, even through casual conversations, benefit both the giver and receiver.
