Key Differences Between ETFs and Mutual Funds for Investors

2025-09-22 07:00:01 EDT

This article outlines the key differences between Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) and mutual funds, crucial for investors making financial choices. ETFs trade like stocks throughout the day, offering real-time pricing, while mutual fund transactions are priced at day's end. A significant advantage of ETFs is their greater tax efficiency, as they rarely distribute capital gains to investors in taxable accounts, unlike many mutual funds. ETFs also tend to have lower expense ratios, making them generally cheaper to own. However, mutual funds offer a larger universe of options and are more commonly available in 401(k) plans.
