The Genesis of Football's Penalty Shootout: A Historical Retrospective

2026-03-28 02:15:22 EDT

This article delves into the fascinating history of football's penalty shootout, tracing its official introduction on August 5, 1970, at Boothferry Park in Hull. Before this innovation, tied matches were resolved by replays, drawing lots, or coin tosses, as seen in the 1968 European Championship and Olympic quarter-finals. The concept, developed by Israeli Football Association officials Yosef Dagan and Michael Almog, aimed to replace these arbitrary methods with a skill-based decider. The first professional shootout saw George Best score and Denis Law miss, while Hull City's goalkeeper Ian McKechnie became the first to save and then miss a penalty, an experience that stayed with him for life.
