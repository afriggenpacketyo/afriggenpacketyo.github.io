The French Open's Unlikely Namesake: Aviation Pioneer Roland Garros

Garros was born in 1885 and became a celebrity through daredevil aviation feats before enlisting in WWI, where he revolutionized aerial combat by developing a method to fire a machine gun through propeller blades. His legacy of persistence, captured in his motto "Victory belongs to the most persevering," now aptly applies to the Grand Slam tournament.
