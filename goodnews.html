Many residents, unwilling to leave their beloved pets behind, risked their lives by staying, contributing to the hurricane's high death toll. In direct response to this crisis, the federal Pet Evacuation & Transportation Safety (PETS) Act was enacted in 2006, providing incentives for states and agencies to integrate pets into their disaster planning. While considerable advancements have been achieved in animal emergency response, the article stresses the paramount importance of individuals being personally prepared to evacuate with their pets and having sufficient emergency provisions, as local resources cannot possibly accommodate all animal needs.
