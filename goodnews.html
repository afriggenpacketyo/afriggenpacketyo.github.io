2025-06-05 00:16:00 EDT

Chile and Google have signed an agreement to install the first-ever submarine fiber optic cable connecting South America to Asia and Oceania by 2027, known as the "Humboldt Project." The 14,800-kilometer cable will run from Valparaiso, Chile, to Sydney, Australia, via French Polynesia, offering a faster alternative to existing connections through the U.S. The project aims to improve connectivity for Chile, Argentina, Paraguay, and Brazil, with Google investing between $300 million and $550 million, and the Chilean government contributing $25 million.
