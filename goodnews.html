Historical Practice of Turkey Droving to London for Christmas Revealed

2025-12-24 00:32:22 EST

This article uncovers the historical practice of "turkey droving," where thousands of turkeys were walked from farms in Norfolk and Suffolk to London for Christmas before modern transport existed. The birds, some wearing tiny leather boots or having their feet coated in tar and sand, embarked on a three-month journey, covering about three miles daily. Drovers would forage for food along the route, with the turkeys roosting in trees overnight. This unique tradition, which faded with the arrival of railways in the late 1800s, highlights a fascinating aspect of historical food supply and the crucial role of families like the Peeles in preserving breeds such as the Norfolk Black.
