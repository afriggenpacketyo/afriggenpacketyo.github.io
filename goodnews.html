2025-08-23 01:37:29 EDT

Hawaii's Kilauea volcano has erupted again, spewing an arc of lava 100 feet into the air across a section of its summit crater floor, marking its 31st such display since December. This latest eruption is contained entirely within the Halemaumau Crater, posing no immediate threat to nearby homes, and offers spectacular views for lucky residents and visitors at Hawaii Volcanoes National Park. Scientists explain that magma is continuously feeding a lower chamber, building pressure until it forces out older, degassed magma, akin to a shaken champagne bottle. This pattern of repeated lava fountains is the fourth in 200 years for Kilauea, one of the world's most active volcanoes, and while the current eruption's end or future changes remain uncertain, it continues to be a powerful natural spectacle.
