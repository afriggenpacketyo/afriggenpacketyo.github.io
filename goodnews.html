Research Reveals Why Type 1 Diabetes Is More Aggressive in Young Children

2025-11-12 21:48:00 EST

Scientists have made a significant discovery, revealing why type 1 diabetes is more severe and aggressive when it develops in young children, particularly those under the age of seven. Research published in the journal Science Advances demonstrates that the pancreas, specifically its insulin-producing beta cells, is still actively developing in early childhood, rendering it far more vulnerable to destructive immune system attacks. In contrast, in older individuals, beta cells mature into larger, more durable clusters, allowing for some continued insulin production which lessens disease severity. This crucial finding offers renewed hope for new immunotherapy drugs, such as teplizumab, which could potentially delay the disease by providing the pancreas time to mature, thereby improving long-term outcomes for children diagnosed at a young age.
