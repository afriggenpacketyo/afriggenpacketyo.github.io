First Malaria Treatment Specifically Formulated for Newborns Receives Approval

2025-07-08 01:15:08 EDT

The first malaria treatment specifically designed for babies and very young children weighing under 4.5kg (10lbs) has been approved for use and is expected to be rolled out in African countries soon. Previously, infants were treated with adjusted doses of drugs meant for older children, creating a risk of overdose and a significant 'treatment gap'. Developed by Novartis in a not-for-profit partnership, the new medicine, Coartem Baby, provides a safe and effective dose for the most vulnerable group. This is considered a major public health breakthrough, as nearly three-quarters of malaria deaths occur in children under five.
