China Develops World's First Zero-Emission Coal Fuel Cell

2026-04-26 06:00:15 EDT

China has unveiled a groundbreaking direct coal fuel cell technology capable of producing electricity with zero emissions, fundamentally challenging traditional perceptions of coal as a heavily polluting fuel. This innovative system eliminates combustion entirely, instead using electrochemical oxidation of pulverized coal to generate electricity directly, bypassing intermediate steam cycles. Crucially, the high-purity carbon dioxide generated by the reaction is captured in situ and catalytically converted into valuable chemical feedstocks or mineralized into compounds like sodium bicarbonate. This development represents a significant advancement in clean energy technology, offering a potential pathway to utilize coal resources more sustainably and reduce carbon footprints globally.

In 1977, he became the first Indian photographer invited to join the prestigious Magnum Photos, nominated by the legendary French photographer Henri Cartier-Bresson. Rai's profound work left an indelible mark on Indian photojournalism, documenting history with a distinctive and impactful visual style that resonated globally.
