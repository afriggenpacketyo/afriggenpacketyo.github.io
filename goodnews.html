Inheriting a Health Savings Account Can Pose Significant Tax Burdens for Non-Spouse Heirs

2026-05-09 09:30:01 EDT

While accumulating a substantial balance in a Health Savings Account (HSA) is generally considered a prudent financial move for future medical expenses, the inheritance of such an account can present significant tax challenges for non-spouse heirs. Unlike surviving spouses who inherit HSAs without immediate tax implications, beneficiaries such as children or friends will find the account stripped of its tax-advantaged status, with the entire balance becoming taxable income in the year of the account holder's death. Financial planners caution that this "tax bomb" could potentially propel heirs into the highest marginal tax bracket. Mitigation strategies include spending down the HSA, donating it to charity, or distributing the inheritance among several individuals, and using it to cover the deceased's unpaid medical expenses within 12 months.
