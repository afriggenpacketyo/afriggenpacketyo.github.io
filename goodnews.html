New Research Proposes Structural Flaws Sank Shackleton's Endurance, Not Just Ice Pressure

2025-10-06 20:51:35 EDT

A new study challenges the long-held belief that Sir Ernest Shackleton's legendary ship, Endurance, sank solely due to overwhelming ice pressure during its 1914 Antarctic expedition. Aalto University professor Jukka Tuhkuri argues the vessel's structural weaknesses, particularly in its engine room's lack of cross-beams and limited hull bracing, made it vulnerable to the compressive forces of Antarctic ice, for which it was not adequately designed. Tuhkuri's research, which consulted expedition diaries and structural analysis, suggests the ship's reputation for indestructibility was unwarranted, as it was built for Arctic rather than more severe Antarctic conditions. This finding offers a fresh perspective on a famous historical maritime disaster, coming over three years after the remarkably preserved remains of Endurance were discovered in the Weddell Sea in 2022.
