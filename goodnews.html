Chinese Scientists Develop Crocodile-Scale Inspired Armor Technology

2026-05-11 02:00:08 EDT

Chinese scientists at Ningbo University have developed innovative armor technology inspired by the unique asymmetric, overlapping scales of crocodile skin. Their research, published in the peer-reviewed Chinese journal Acta Armamentarii, confirms through experimental verification that this bio-inspired structure can effectively cause projectile deflection. Instead of conventional hexagonal ceramic tiles used in composite armor, the team engineered a mosaic of diamond-shaped alumina ceramic units. These units are arranged at 45-degree angles and bonded with epoxy resin onto an aluminum alloy backing plate, leveraging the inherent hardness, high compressive strength, and low density of ceramic materials to create a more effective protective barrier against projectiles.
