Study Reveals Physical Decline in Strength and Fitness Commences Around Age 35

2026-01-19 14:59:45 EST

A new Swedish study, tracking individuals for nearly half a century, indicates that measurable declines in physical fitness and strength typically begin around age 35 and continue gradually through midlife. Researchers observed these declines across various measures, including aerobic fitness and muscle strength, using standardized tests on a cohort born in 1958. While sustained physical activity helps maintain higher overall fitness levels, it doesn't entirely prevent age-related deterioration. The study, published in the Journal of Cachexia, Sarcopenia and Muscle, also highlighted that increasing activity levels later in life can still improve physical capacity by 5% to 10%, demonstrating the body's responsiveness to exercise beyond peak years and offering hope for maintaining health.
