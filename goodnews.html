2026-04-19 05:21:00 EDT

A sociologist is questioning the interpretation of German crime statistics, particularly the disproportionate representation of non-citizens among suspects. She also points to studies suggesting that individuals perceived as foreign are more likely to be reported to the police, regardless of actual crime rates. Prätor advocates for studies on unreported crime and a more nuanced approach to understanding the complex factors contributing to crime statistics, rather than solely focusing on nationality. The article highlights how demographic differences, such as a younger male population among certain migrant groups, can influence these statistics.

NASA Deactivates Voyager 1 Instrument to Extend Mission Life

2026-04-19 01:40:28 EDT

NASA has deactivated one of the remaining science instruments on the Voyager 1 spacecraft, the most distant human-made object, to conserve its dwindling power supply. Launched in 1977 for a five-year mission, Voyager 1 has operated for nearly 50 years, crossing into interstellar space. The spacecraft's power, generated by a radioisotope thermoelectric generator, is declining, necessitating such measures to extend its operational life. The Low-energy Charged Particles experiment (LECP) was turned off on April 17th, following a joint decision by mission engineers to prioritize scientific capabilities. This action is expected to buy the mission approximately another year of operation, with further power conservation plans being developed.
