Explainer: The Role and Jurisdiction of the International Court of Justice

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based at the Peace Palace in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial organ of the United Nations. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, its primary function is to settle legal disputes submitted to it by states. The court's jurisdiction applies when states have agreed to it, either through a blanket declaration, mutual consent for a specific case, or by being signatories to treaties like the Genocide Convention that designate the ICJ as the arbiter. However, the ICJ has no enforcement mechanism, meaning its binding rulings ultimately depend on the cooperation of the involved parties.
