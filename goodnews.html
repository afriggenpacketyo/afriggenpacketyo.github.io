This article explains the timeline for wage garnishment, a process that can lead to a portion of a paycheck being withheld after a creditor secures a court judgment. The process typically involves filing a complaint, serving legal notice, potentially a default judgment if no response, and then a writ of garnishment to the employer. While the full timeline can vary, inaction from borrowers can lead to garnishment in as little as a few months. The article emphasizes that federal law caps garnishment at 25% of disposable earnings and highlights debt relief options like settlement or bankruptcy, which can halt or prevent garnishment, stressing the importance of early intervention.
