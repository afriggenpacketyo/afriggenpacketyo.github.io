New Guidelines Recommend Universal Lipoprotein(a) Test for Heart Disease Prevention

2026-03-13 16:56:19 EDT

New guidelines from the American Heart Association and American College of Cardiology recommend a one-time lipoprotein(a) test for all adults to assess genetic risk for heart disease. This simple blood test, which measures a stable, genetically determined marker, aims to provide a more complete picture of cardiovascular risk beyond traditional LDL levels. The guidelines also advocate for wider use of coronary calcium scoring and the PREVENT risk assessment tool, alongside earlier and more intensive treatment with cholesterol-lowering statins. This significant shift towards a more proactive and comprehensive preventive approach is expected to lead to a substantial reduction in heart attacks, strokes, and overall cardiovascular disease.

Researchers Develop Innovative Methods to Vaccinate Bats Against Deadly Viruses

Both approaches successfully induced antibody production and protected bats from subsequent infection in lab settings.
