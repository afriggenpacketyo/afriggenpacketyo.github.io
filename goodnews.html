Fossil Discovery in China Suggests Ancient Marine Animals Practiced Social Distancing

2025-07-30 00:39:15 EDT

Chinese paleontologists have discovered 436-million-year-old fossils of brachiopods, ancient clam-like invertebrates, that appear to have practiced a form of social distancing. The fossils, found in Guizhou province, were arranged in a highly regular, checkerboard-like pattern on the former sea floor. Researchers believe the extinct species, Nucleospira calypta, used hundreds of thin, bristle-like structures to sense their neighbors and maintain optimal spacing, likely to improve their efficiency in filter feeding and enhance survival.
