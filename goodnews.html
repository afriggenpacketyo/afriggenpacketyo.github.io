Nobel Laureate Chemist Rudolph Marcus Dies at 102, Honored for Electron Transfer Theory

2026-07-17 14:29:30 EDT

Rudolph Marcus, a Caltech professor and Nobel laureate in chemistry, has died peacefully at his home at the age of 102. Marcus was awarded the Nobel Prize in 1992 for his groundbreaking "Marcus Theory," which mathematically describes how electrons jump between molecules without breaking chemical bonds. His work, initially controversial but later confirmed by experiments, significantly advanced understanding in areas like photosynthesis, cellular respiration, and battery technology. Marcus, who continued research until his death, was celebrated for his fundamental contributions to science and his humble approach, famously stating that the focus should always be on the work itself.
