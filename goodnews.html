The History and Significance Behind the Name Juneteenth

2025-06-18 11:59:05 EDT

Juneteenth, now a federally recognized holiday, commemorates the end of slavery in the U.S. and is celebrated annually on June 19. The name Juneteenth combines the words June and nineteenth, originating from June 19, 1865, when enslaved people in Texas were informed of their freedom under the Emancipation Proclamation. Although the Emancipation Proclamation was issued earlier, its enforcement in Confederate states, including Texas, was delayed until the Union Army arrived. The holiday symbolizes freedom, unity, and resilience, with celebrations including parades, parties, and educational events.
