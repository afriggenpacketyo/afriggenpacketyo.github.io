October's Harvest Moon to be First Supermoon of 2025

2025-10-07 03:55:18 EDT

The first of three consecutive supermoons in 2025 will be visible on Tuesday night, as the full Harvest Moon reaches its closest point to Earth. This phenomenon occurs because the Moon's elliptical orbit brings it nearer to our planet at certain times, making it appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than other full moons. This year's Harvest Moon, the full moon closest to the autumn equinox, occurs unusually late in October. Skygazers will have two more opportunities to see a supermoon this year, in November and December.
