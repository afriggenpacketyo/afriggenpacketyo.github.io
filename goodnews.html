2026-03-11 18:20:47 EDT

A 1,300-pound NASA satellite, Van Allen Probe A, re-entered Earth's atmosphere over the eastern Pacific Ocean, nearly 14 years after its launch. Most of the probe was expected to burn up upon re-entry, with a low 1 in 4,200 chance of harming anyone on Earth. The probe, along with its twin, was designed to explore Earth's radiation belts, known as the Van Allen belts, which shield the planet from cosmic radiation. Its mission, originally planned for two years, lasted nearly seven, making significant discoveries, including evidence of a third radiation belt during intense solar activity.
