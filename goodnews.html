Scientists Identify Genetic Switches for Regenerating a Whole Plant from a Single Cell

2025-10-12 02:18:59 EDT

A collaborative team of scientists from China and the Netherlands has discovered the mechanism that allows a single plant cell to regenerate into an entirely new plant. The research, published in the journal Cell, identifies two specific genetic switches that can be flipped to revert a differentiated somatic cell back to an embryonic state. This process, known as somatic embryogenesis, was previously thought to be exclusive to fertilized eggs. The breakthrough offers profound insights into one of biology's most fundamental processes and could have significant implications for agriculture, conservation, and plant biotechnology by enabling more efficient plant propagation.
