An ice core, potentially over 1.5 million years old, has arrived at the British Antarctic Survey in the UK, where it will be melted to study Earth's ancient climate. Scientists hope the analysis will provide crucial data on the Mid-Pleistocene Transition, a mysterious period when Earth's glacial cycles changed dramatically. Explainer: What to Know About Chronic Venous Insufficiency, Trump's Recent Diagnosis

2025-07-17 18:31:24 EDT

This article provides a medical explanation of chronic venous insufficiency (CVI), the condition President Trump was recently diagnosed with. CVI is a common circulatory issue, affecting up to 40% of the U.S. population, where damaged valves in leg veins hinder blood return to the heart, causing blood to pool and leading to symptoms like swelling and varicose veins. According to vascular surgeons, the condition is generally not serious and is often managed with non-surgical treatments like compression stockings and lifestyle changes. In more advanced cases, skin discoloration or ulcers can occur, but for most patients, the symptoms are mild and treatable.
