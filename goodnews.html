NASA's Van Allen Probe A Successfully Re-enters Earth's Atmosphere

2026-03-11 17:26:39 EDT

NASA's 1,300-pound Van Allen Probe A, a satellite launched in August 2012, successfully re-entered Earth's atmosphere after a nearly 14-year mission. Originally designed for a two-year operational period, the probe and its twin, Van Allen Probe B, significantly exceeded expectations by functioning for almost seven years. Their mission was to explore Earth's radiation belts, leading to groundbreaking discoveries such as the identification of a temporary third radiation belt during periods of intense solar activity. While most of the probe was anticipated to incinerate upon re-entry, NASA had estimated a minimal 1 in 4,200 chance of any components reaching the ground and causing harm.
