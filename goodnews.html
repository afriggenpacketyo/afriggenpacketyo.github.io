Spain Finalizes Amnesty Plan for Undocumented Migrants, Offering Temporary Residence Permits

2026-04-14 15:59:00 EDT

Spain's left-leaning government has finalized an amnesty plan, enacted via royal decree, allowing hundreds of thousands of undocumented migrants to apply for one-year temporary residence permits. To qualify, individuals must prove arrival before January 1st of this year, five months' residence, and no criminal record. This policy, which bypasses parliamentary approval, contrasts with other European nations' efforts to curb irregular migration. Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez advocates the plan as an act of "normalization, justice, and necessity," highlighting migration's importance for economic growth and enabling taxed employment. Critics from the center-right Popular Party call the move unsustainable, while a union for immigration officers demands more resources to handle the expected influx of applications.
