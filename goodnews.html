Understanding Bank Levies: Creditors' Power to Seize Funds and Debtor Protections

2026-05-21 11:29:19 EDT

A bank levy allows a creditor to freeze and seize funds from a bank account to satisfy unpaid debt, a process that can be alarming given rising inflation and household debt. While a creditor can theoretically take all funds up to the judgment amount, federal and state laws offer crucial protections. Federal law shields certain funds like Social Security and veterans' benefits, requiring banks to protect two months' worth of these payments. State laws may also exempt minimum balances or recently deposited wages. Debt settlement or bankruptcy are options to halt collection efforts, protect assets, and potentially resolve debt before a levy occurs. (not investment advice)
