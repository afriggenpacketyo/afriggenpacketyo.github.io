Explainer on Options Available After Failing the Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Means Test

2025-11-03 13:10:25 EST

This article explains the consequences and alternatives for individuals who fail the means test required for Chapter 7 bankruptcy. Failing the test indicates one's income is too high to qualify for debt liquidation, but it is not the end of the road for debt relief. The primary option is to convert the case to a Chapter 13 bankruptcy, which involves a three- to five-year repayment plan. Other alternatives to bankruptcy include debt settlement, where creditors agree to accept less than the full amount owed, or a debt management plan through a credit counseling agency to lower interest rates.
