Understanding Bank Account Garnishment: Protections for Debtors

2026-03-11 10:55:18 EDT

This article explains that debt collectors cannot automatically garnish an entire bank account; they must first obtain a court judgment against the borrower. Once a judgment is secured, a writ of garnishment can freeze funds up to the debt amount, often without prior notice from the bank. However, federal law automatically protects certain funds, such as Social Security and Veterans Affairs benefits, from garnishment. Many states also offer additional exemptions, and debtors can claim these protections, consider debt settlement, or explore bankruptcy options. Understanding these legal mechanisms and acting quickly is crucial for individuals facing potential account seizure (not investment advice).
