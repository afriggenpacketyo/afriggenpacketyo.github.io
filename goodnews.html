Born in Ontario in 1934 during the Great Depression, the five identical sisters made history as the first known quintuplets to survive past infancy, quickly becoming a global sensation. Their upbringing was marked by controversy, as the Ontario government removed them from their parents and publicly displayed them in a compound dubbed "Quintland," turning them into a major tourist attraction.
