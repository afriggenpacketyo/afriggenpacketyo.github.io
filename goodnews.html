Banks Generally Do Not Sell Physical Silver to Everyday Investors

2026-01-28 09:21:20 EST

This article clarifies that most retail banks in the U.S. do not sell physical silver bars or coins directly to everyday investors, despite a recent resurgence in precious metals as a popular hedge against inflation and economic uncertainty. The reasons for this include the costly logistics of storing and insuring physical silver, the relatively low profit margins compared to traditional banking services, and banks' focus on core financial regulations. While high-net-worth clients might access precious metals through some large international banks via allocated accounts, physical silver is rarely available for direct purchase at branches. For those seeking physical silver, the article recommends exploring online dealers, local coin shops, or the U.S. Mint, while noting that paper silver products like ETFs offer exposure without physical ownership.
