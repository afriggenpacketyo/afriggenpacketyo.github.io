California Avoids Summer Power Alerts for Second Consecutive Year, Crediting Battery Storage Growth

2025-10-17 06:00:00 EDT

California has successfully navigated another summer without issuing a single "Flex Alert" for energy conservation, a significant shift attributed to the massive expansion of its battery energy storage capacity. State officials and energy experts say the investment in batteries, which has grown over 3,000% since 2020, has been a "game changer" for grid reliability. These batteries store solar power generated during the day and discharge it in the late afternoon and evening when demand peaks and solar generation drops off. This has not only stabilized the grid and prevented rolling blackouts but also enabled a dramatic decrease in the use of fossil-fuel plants.

Former Japanese Prime Minister Tomiichi Murayama, Noted for 1995 War Apology, Dies at 101

2025-10-17 05:38:14 EDT

Tomiichi Murayama, the former Prime Minister of Japan who issued a landmark 1995 apology for the country's World War II aggression, has passed away at the age of 101. As head of the Socialist Party, Murayama led a coalition government from 1994 to 1996. His most enduring legacy is the "Murayama statement," which expressed "deep remorse" and a "heartfelt apology" for the suffering Japan caused, particularly to other Asian nations. This statement became a cornerstone of Japan's post-war diplomacy for nearly two decades, though it has been downplayed by more recent nationalist leaders. Murayama remained a vocal critic of attempts to whitewash Japan's wartime history.
