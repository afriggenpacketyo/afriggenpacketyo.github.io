2025-06-12 21:00:11 EDT

Research by archaeologists from Chinese and US institutes suggests that pigs were already domesticated in southern China around 8,000 years ago. The team analyzed two early Neolithic sites in the Yangtze Delta region, finding that pigs were eating humans' cooked foods and waste. The findings suggest the domestication process of Sus scrofa occurred alongside the development of rice cultivation and sedentary lifestyles. This challenges the conventional view that pigs were first domesticated in the Near East, adding a new perspective to the history of animal domestication.
