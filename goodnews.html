'Zombie Rabbits' with Horn-like Growths Go Viral, Identified as Shope Papilloma Virus

2025-08-13 20:33:00 EDT

Viral photos circulating online depict rabbits in Colorado exhibiting black, horn-like growths, leading to widespread speculation about "zombie-rabbits." Colorado Parks and Wildlife has confirmed the authenticity of these images, identifying the cause as the Shope Papilloma Virus, a relatively harmless disease. This virus, primarily found in the American Midwest, spreads through fleas, ticks, or direct rabbit-to-rabbit contact, though it cannot transmit to humans or pets. The papillomas typically recede as the rabbits' immune systems combat the infection, unless they obstruct crucial functions like eating or sight. Historically, this condition is thought to have inspired the mythical "jackalope" and has even contributed to significant scientific understanding of how viruses can be linked to cancer.
