Spanish Court Applies Gender Violence Law in Animal Abuse Case for First Time

2025-09-25 06:23:36 EDT

In a landmark ruling, a Spanish court has convicted a man for killing a puppy by applying a law designed to combat gender-based violence. The court found that the 18-year-old man intentionally threw the puppy he co-owned with his partner off a cliff as a means to inflict psychological harm on her, classifying it as an act of "vicarious violence." This legal concept typically refers to harming a child to hurt the mother. The ruling is the first time this principle has been extended to animal abuse in Spain, recognizing the animal's death as an instrument of psychological abuse against the woman and therefore warranting stronger punitive measures.
