Study Suggests Vitamin D May Slow Down Biological Aging

2025-05-26 19:54:06 EDT

A new study from Mass General Brigham and the Medical College of Georgia indicates that daily vitamin D supplements could potentially slow biological aging. The research, which tracked over 1,000 U.S. adults over four years, found that vitamin D3 may protect against the shortening of telomeres, which are protective caps on DNA that erode with age. Participants taking vitamin D3 experienced significantly less telomere shortening compared to those taking a placebo, suggesting a potential way to protect the body at the cellular level. While further research is needed to confirm these findings and determine the impact on different populations, the study offers a promising strategy for countering the aging process.
