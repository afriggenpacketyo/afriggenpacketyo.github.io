New Research Explores Cannabis's Role in Reducing Alcohol Consumption

2025-11-19 00:01:00 EST

Scientists are actively researching whether cannabis can help individuals decrease their alcohol consumption, a practice colloquially known as "California sober." A significant study published in the American Journal of Psychiatry revealed that participants who smoked higher-potency cannabis drank 27% less alcohol, and those with lower-potency strains drank 19% less, compared to a placebo group. While these findings offer some of the strongest data yet, researchers emphasize that it is too early to recommend cannabis as a direct treatment for alcohol use disorder. The study, conducted in a simulated bar environment, indicates a potential therapeutic role for cannabinoids, though further research is needed to understand long-term effects and individual variations.
