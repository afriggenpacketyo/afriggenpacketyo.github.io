Exploring Ultimogeniture: How Youngest Sons Inherit in Pakistan's Chitral Region

2025-08-27 07:52:44 EDT

This article delves into "chiro bash," or ultimogeniture, an unusual inheritance practice in Pakistan's mountainous Chitral district where the youngest son inherits the family home. This custom ensures parents are cared for in old age and promotes family continuity, contrasting with the Western practice of primogeniture. While it can cause friction among siblings, it also encourages older children to seek independence. However, the practice is facing pressure from formal Shariah-based inheritance laws and cultural shifts as more young adults leave for urban education and work, making its long-term survival uncertain.
