Hummingbird Feeders Can Be Deadly if Not Cleaned Properly

2025-06-10 14:34:21 EDT

Hummingbird feeders, while intended to help the birds, can be deadly if not cleaned regularly. Sugar water left out too long can spoil, attracting microbes that cause infections, including candida. Infected birds can develop swollen tongues, making it difficult to eat and leading to starvation. It is recommended to clean feeders every two to three days in spring and fall, and daily or every other day in the summer. The solution is simple: mix one part refined white sugar with four parts drinking water, and avoid other sugars or red food coloring.
