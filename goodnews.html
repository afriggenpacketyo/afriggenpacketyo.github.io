Psychologist Explains How Embracing Boredom Can Enhance Life's Meaning and Reduce Anxiety

2026-03-22 10:15:25 EDT

Psychologist Arthur C. Brooks argues that eliminating boredom through constant device use inadvertently diminishes life's meaning and increases anxiety and depression. He explains that boredom activates the brain's "Default Mode Network," crucial for mind-wandering, abstract thinking, and contemplating life's deeper questions. By constantly seeking distraction, this network remains inactive, leading to a sense of emptiness. Brooks suggests reintroducing silent spaces into daily life—like phone-free walks or meals—to reactivate this cognitive function, ultimately fostering calmness, peace, and a richer sense of purpose.
