Scientists Discover New Massive Dinosaur Species in Thailand, Named Nagatitan

2026-05-15 12:57:59 EDT

Scientists in Thailand have identified a new massive dinosaur species, Nagatitan chaiyaphumensis, from skeletal remains, estimating it weighed 27 tons and stretched 88 feet. This long-necked herbivore, which roamed Thailand 100-120 million years ago, is the largest ever found in Southeast Asia. Dubbed "the last titan" due to its discovery in one of the youngest rock formations containing dinosaurs in Thailand, it was dwarfed only by its contemporary predator, a relative of Carcharodontosaurus. A life-size reconstruction of Nagatitan is now on display at Bangkok's Thainosaur Museum.
