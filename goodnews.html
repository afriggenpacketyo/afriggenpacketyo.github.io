Nearly 3,000-Year-Old Human Remains Discovered in El Salvador

2026-07-22 09:30:42 EDT

Archaeologists in El Salvador have exhumed human remains estimated to be nearly 3,000 years old, dating back to around 850 BC, from a burial site in Antiguo Cuscatlan, a suburb of San Salvador. The discovery included a small Usulutan-style ceramic vessel shaped like a sea turtle and remnants of cassava branches, all preserved beneath layers of volcanic ash. The remains, found face down, will undergo radiocarbon dating and DNA analysis to determine their precise age and the person's sex. This significant find could become one of Mesoamerica's oldest pre-Hispanic burials, providing crucial new information about the ancient populations that inhabited this region over two millennia ago.

India Pursues Ambitious Goal to Become a Global Semiconductor Manufacturing Hub

2026-07-22 09:17:00 EDT

India is making its biggest push yet to become a major player in the global semiconductor industry, aiming to reduce global reliance on a few East Asian manufacturers. The government has approved a $13.2 billion "Semicon 2.0" program, building on a 2021 initiative, to develop a comprehensive chip ecosystem from design to advanced manufacturing and packaging. While India already possesses strong capabilities in chip design, large-scale fabrication remains a significant challenge, requiring massive investment, uninterrupted electricity, vast quantities of ultra-pure water, and an extensive supplier network. Experts suggest focusing initially on mature 28-nanometer chips before advancing to more sophisticated technologies, with the goal of a $100 billion domestic market by 2030.
