2025-11-28 06:00:00 EST

Olive ridley sea turtles, once feared to be on the verge of collapse in India, are now showing a significant population rebound thanks to decades of conservation efforts. Twenty years ago, conservationists counted fewer than 100,000 nests, but last season that number soared to approximately one million. This recovery is attributed to protective measures like seasonal fishing bans, protected coastal zones, and community-led initiatives such as the Velas Turtle Festival, which protects eggs from predators and promotes eco-tourism. Despite this success, the turtles still face threats from illegal fishing nets, plastic pollution, and potential pressure to loosen protections as their numbers grow.

German Researchers Discover Potent, Broadly Neutralizing Antibody Against HIV

2025-11-28 04:54:00 EST

Researchers at the University Hospital of Cologne in Germany have identified a highly effective antibody that can neutralize a wide range of HIV variants, raising hopes for new prevention and treatment strategies. The antibody, named 04_A06, was discovered in the blood of HIV-infected individuals who had developed a strong natural immune response. In laboratory experiments, it successfully neutralized 98% of the nearly 340 HIV variants tested by blocking the virus from entering and infecting human cells. This discovery could lead to a passive immunization administered every six months, offering a powerful alternative to daily prophylactic pills.
