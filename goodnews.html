EU Implements Comprehensive Ban on BPA in Food Packaging to Safeguard Public Health

2026-06-19 13:09:00 EDT

The European Union is banning Bisphenol A (BPA) and other bisphenols from food packaging starting July 2026, extending previous restrictions on baby bottles. BPA, an industrial chemical used in plastics and can linings, is linked to immune system alterations, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, obesity, and hormonal disruption. Despite most humans having BPA traces, this new EU regulation is the world's strictest, though some transition periods and exceptions exist for specific products. Consumers are advised to choose fresh foods, glass containers, and bisphenol-free products for greater safety.

This "hyperparasite" preys on the well-known "zombie fungus" (Ophiocordyceps), which typically manipulates and kills insects. Instead of infecting insects, Pleurocordyceps infiltrates and feeds directly on the Ophiocordyceps tissue within the host ant. This discovery highlights the immense biodiversity and intricate ecological interactions within Borneo's rainforests, offering potential for new antimicrobial drugs and biocontrol agents against agricultural pests, while reassuring that "zombie fungus" poses no threat to humans.
