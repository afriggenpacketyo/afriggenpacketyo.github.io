Bright Moon Expected to Obscure Viewing of Annual Perseids Meteor Shower Peak

2025-08-08 14:00:53 EDT

The annual Perseids meteor shower, one of the most anticipated celestial events, is set to peak on August 12-13, but viewing conditions are expected to be poor. A bright, 84% full waning gibbous moon will coincide with the peak, significantly reducing visibility. The American Meteor Society predicts that the moonlight will wash out most of the fainter meteors, cutting the number of visible meteors from a potential 50 per hour to just 10-20. The Perseids are caused by debris from comet 109P/Swift-Tuttle burning up in Earth's atmosphere, and they will remain active, though less intensely, until late August.
