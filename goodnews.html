Original Medicare Coverage Gaps Explained

2025-09-10 11:02:53 EDT

Original Medicare, covering hospital and medical services, leaves gaps that can lead to unexpected out-of-pocket costs for retirees, and these gaps include regular dental care, vision and hearing care, prescription drugs, long-term care, care while overseas, and certain preventive treatments. Nearly 90% of Medicare beneficiaries supplement their coverage to address these gaps, with options including Medicare Advantage (Part C) and Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plans. Medicare Advantage plans combine Part A and Part B coverage, often including additional benefits, while Medicare Supplement plans cover out-of-pocket costs like copayments and deductibles. Seniors are advised to carefully evaluate their healthcare needs and explore supplemental coverage options to ensure comprehensive protection.
