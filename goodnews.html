Research Finds Wild Chimpanzees Regularly Consume Alcohol from Fermented Fruit

2025-09-17 14:00:09 EDT

A new study has found that wild chimpanzees in Uganda and Côte d'Ivoire consume the equivalent of nearly two human alcoholic beverages per day by eating naturally fermented fruit. Researchers measured the alcohol content in the figs and plum-like fruits that make up the chimps' diet, finding it to be around 0.3% by weight. While this low concentration means the chimps are not getting drunk, they are exposed to a steady, low dose of ethanol. The findings lend support to the "drunken monkey" hypothesis, which suggests human attraction to alcohol may have evolutionary roots in the fruit-eating habits of our primate ancestors.
