2026-07-10 17:30:57 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, from fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This long-necked sauropod, estimated to have lived approximately 150 million years ago, measured up to 20 meters (66 feet) in length. A CT scan of a recovered dorsal vertebra revealed unique characteristics, including a Y-shaped arrangement of supporting bones and a distinctive air-cavity structure, distinguishing it as the first Mamenchisauridae family member found in Thailand. This significant discovery was published in the Nature scientific journal.
