North America's Oldest Pterosaur Fossil Discovered in Arizona

2025-07-07 15:00:00 EDT

Researchers have identified the oldest known pterosaur fossil in North America from a fragile jawbone discovered in Arizona's Petrified Forest National Park. The flying reptile, named Eotephradactylus mcintireae, was about the size of a small seagull and lived approximately 209 million years ago during the late Triassic period. The discovery, detailed in a Smithsonian-led paper, helps fill a significant gap in the fossil record and provides new insights into the evolution of these early flying animals. The fossil was unearthed from a rich bone bed that has yielded thousands of other important Triassic fossils.
