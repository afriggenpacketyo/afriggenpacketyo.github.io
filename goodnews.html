Unveiling the Origin of the Political Phrase 'Not Fit for Purpose'

2026-04-04 01:01:25 EDT

This article uncovers the true origin of the pervasive British political phrase "not fit for purpose," tracing it back to Sir David Normington, who was the permanent secretary of the Home Office in 2006. He initially coined the term in a private memo to Home Secretary John Reid, specifically to describe technological and process shortcomings within the Immigration and Nationality Directorate, rather than the entire department or its personnel. Reid subsequently popularized the phrase publicly, leading to its widespread adoption as a shorthand for governmental inefficiency. Its enduring legacy includes the creation of the Ministry of Justice and its continued use by politicians across the spectrum.
