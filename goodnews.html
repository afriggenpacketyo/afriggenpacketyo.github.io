Rare Blue Micromoon to Appear This Weekend, Not Returning Until 2028

2026-05-28 10:24:57 EDT

A rare blue micromoon is set to grace the night sky this weekend, offering a celestial spectacle not to be seen again until December 2028. A blue moon occurs when there are two full moons within a single calendar month, and a micromoon happens when the full moon is at its farthest point from Earth, appearing slightly smaller and dimmer. While the blue moon will not visually appear blue, its rarity makes it a significant event for skywatchers. The phenomenon will be particularly interesting in the Southern Hemisphere, where the moon will briefly obscure the bright star Antares.
