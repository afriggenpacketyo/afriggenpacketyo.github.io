Understanding Wage Garnishment for Old Debts Beyond Seven Years

2026-03-23 11:49:28 EDT

This article clarifies the complex issue of whether debt collectors can garnish wages for debts older than seven years, addressing a common misconception often linked to credit report timelines. While most negative marks typically disappear from credit reports after approximately seven years, the underlying debt itself does not automatically vanish. Wage garnishment remains a real threat if a creditor successfully obtained a court judgment before the state's statute of limitations expired, as these judgments can extend collection power for a decade or more and are often renewable. The article strongly advises proactive debt management strategies, such as debt settlement or credit counseling, and consulting an attorney if faced with legal action, to effectively avoid unexpected collection actions on older accounts.
