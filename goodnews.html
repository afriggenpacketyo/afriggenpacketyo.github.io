A long-standing traffic engineering guideline known as the "85th percentile rule" is facing increased scrutiny amid rising road fatalities. The rule suggests speed limits should be set based on the speed that 85% of drivers naturally travel, effectively letting drivers dictate the limit. Critics argue this creates a dangerous feedback loop where speeding leads to higher limits, especially in urban areas with pedestrians. In response, the Federal Highway Administration has clarified that the guideline is not a strict rule, encouraging states and cities to also consider factors like pedestrian safety and crash frequency.
