Explainer on Why American Bread Often Tastes Inferior to European Varieties

2025-10-20 08:00:00 EDT

An article explores why bread in countries like France is often perceived as higher quality than mass-produced American bread. In France, a 1993 law mandates that traditional baguettes sold in a "boulangerie" contain only four basic ingredients: flour, water, leavening, and salt, emphasizing time and natural fermentation. In contrast, American industrial bread often contains over 30 ingredients, including conditioners and preservatives, prioritizing speed, consistency, and a long shelf life over the development of complex flavors and textures.
