2025-10-06 05:00:00 EDT

Researchers point to a combination of genetic factors, such as females having two X chromosomes, and behavioral differences. Risky mating behaviors and physical investments made by males to reproduce come at a survival cost. In contrast, the sex that invests more in caregiving for offspring tends to live longer, an evolutionary pressure to ensure the young survive. The findings suggest that while biological factors play a role, human males may be able to narrow the gap by adopting less risky behaviors.
