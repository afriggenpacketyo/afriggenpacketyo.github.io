This event happens at the precise moment when Earth's northern half is tilted most directly towards the Sun, causing the longest period of daylight and the shortest night. The solstice date can vary slightly between June 20th and 22nd due to the Earth's orbit taking nearly six hours longer than a calendar year, a difference accounted for by leap years. This tilt is also responsible for Earth's distinct seasons.
