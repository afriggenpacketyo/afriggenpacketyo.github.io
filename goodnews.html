Taylor Swift's New Album Highlights How Vinyl Records Avoid US Tariffs

2025-10-03 14:29:07 EDT

The release of Taylor Swift's latest album, "The Life of a Showgirl," underscores a unique exception in U.S. trade policy that protects physical music from import tariffs. A Cold War-era provision known as the Berman Amendment classifies items like vinyl records and CDs as "informational materials," exempting them from regulations such as the recent rollback of the "de minimis" threshold for low-value imports. While this exemption benefits consumers and record labels, some U.S.-based vinyl manufacturers argue that it puts them at a competitive disadvantage.
