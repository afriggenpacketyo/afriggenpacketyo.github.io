Scientists Use Environmental DNA to Search for Endangered Axolotls in Mexico City

2025-08-11 19:37:29 EDT

In Mexico City's Xochimilco borough, scientists are filtering canal water to find DNA traces of the critically endangered axolotl. Once plentiful, the unique salamander is now nearly impossible to find in the wild due to habitat degradation and pollution. By analyzing environmental DNA (eDNA) left behind by the creatures, researchers can detect their presence even when physical searches yield no results. So far, the technique has confirmed axolotls exist in protected refuge areas, offering a sliver of hope and guiding conservation efforts to restore the species' fragile habitat.
