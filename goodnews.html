Atmospheric Conditions Prevented Any Hurricanes from Making U.S. Landfall This Season

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

For the first time in a decade, the Atlantic hurricane season is concluding without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States. Meteorologists attribute this unusual phenomenon to a persistent weather pattern known as "recurvature," where storms veered northward and out to sea before reaching the coast. This was caused by an area of low pressure, or an upper-level trough, over the eastern U.S., which weakened the western part of the Bermuda High pressure system. This atmospheric setup effectively acted as a shield, steering multiple potentially threatening storms away from the U.S. coastline throughout the season.

The History of the White House Thanksgiving Turkey Pardon Tradition

2025-11-25 10:29:59 EST

The annual White House tradition of pardoning a Thanksgiving turkey officially dates back to President George H.W. Bush in 1989, but its origins are much older. The first informal turkey pardon is believed to have been granted by President Abraham Lincoln in 1863 at the request of his son, Tad. Lincoln also established Thanksgiving as a national holiday during the Civil War, following a campaign by writer Sarah Josepha Hale. Over the decades, the practice evolved from presidents simply receiving gift turkeys to the formal, lighthearted ceremony known today, where two turkeys are spared and sent to live out their days on a farm.
