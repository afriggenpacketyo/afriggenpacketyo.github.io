Historical Roots of "Soccer" Term in US and Canada Explored

2026-06-13 11:33:54 EDT

This article delves into the historical reasons behind the prevalent use of the term "soccer" in the US and Canada, rather than "football," tracing its origins back to 19th-century Oxford University. Emeritus professor Szymanski explains that "soccer" emerged as a slang term, a shortened form of "association football," used to differentiate it from "rugby football." This word, coined by wealthy university students, subsequently traveled globally as the sport itself gained international popularity. While British newspapers continued to employ "soccer" well into the 1980s, its widespread adoption in North America became particularly prominent due to the concurrent rise of American football, which shares significant historical connections with rugby.
