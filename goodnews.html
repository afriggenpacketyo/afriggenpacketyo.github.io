Five Phrases to Avoid When Communicating With Children

2025-06-22 13:03:57 EDT

According to a study of over 200 parent-child relationships, parents who avoid defiance in their children do not use threats, bribes, or harsh consequences, and instead use language that encourages cooperation. Phrases like "Because I said so," "If you don't listen, you'll lose [X privilege]," "Stop crying. You're fine," "How many times do I have to tell you?," and "You know better than that" can trigger a child's fight-or-flight response. Instead, parents should explain their reasoning, give children agency, validate emotions, invite problem-solving, and encourage self-reflection. This approach helps children feel respected, emotionally safe, and involved, which reduces power struggles and fosters emotional resilience.
