Debt Collectors Re-Aging Accounts Illegally?

2025-06-12 12:30:32 EDT

Debt collectors may be illegally re-aging old debts, a practice where they reset the clock on expired debts to make them appear newer on credit reports. This allows them to pursue payment more aggressively, even taking legal action that would otherwise be barred by statutes of limitations. Consumers can identify re-aging by checking credit reports for debts that reappear after seven years or have suspiciously recent delinquency dates. If re-aging is suspected, individuals should file a dispute with credit bureaus and report the debt collector to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) to protect their credit history.
