Chinese Scientists Develop Hair-Thin Fiber Integrated Circuit for Electronic Textiles

2026-01-23 17:00:11 EST

Chinese scientists have achieved a significant advancement in fiber-based electronics by developing a novel electronic device called a fiber integrated circuit (FIC). This breakthrough allows fibers, as thin as a human hair, to compute like traditional chips or display information like transistors. The innovation involves building functional circuits on an elastic substrate, then rolling it into a thin fiber, resembling a scroll. This technology boasts a transistor density of 100,000 per centimeter, meeting industry standards for very large-scale integration while remaining fully flexible. This development paves the way for machine-washable cloth to function like computers or televisions, revolutionizing the field of electronic textiles and their practical applications.
