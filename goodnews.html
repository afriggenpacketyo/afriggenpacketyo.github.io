Atmospheric Conditions Prevented Any Hurricanes from Making U.S. Landfall This Season

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

For the first time in a decade, the 2025 Atlantic hurricane season concluded without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States. Meteorologists attribute this unusual phenomenon to a persistent weather pattern known as "recurvature," where storms veered northward and out to sea before reaching the coast. This pattern was primarily caused by an area of low pressure, or an upper-level trough, that lingered over the eastern U.S. This trough weakened the western edge of the Bermuda High pressure system, which typically steers hurricanes westward toward the U.S. Instead, the steering currents consistently deflected the 13 named storms of the season away from the mainland, a significant deviation from recent years of destructive landfalls.

Exploring the Origins of the White House Thanksgiving Turkey Pardon Tradition

2025-11-25 10:29:59 EST

The annual White House tradition of pardoning a Thanksgiving turkey, while officially started by President George H.W. Bush in 1989, has roots stretching back to President Abraham Lincoln. In 1863, Lincoln informally spared a turkey at the request of his son, Tad, establishing a precedent for presidential clemency towards the festive bird. The practice of presidents receiving presentation turkeys became more regular in the 20th century, but it was President Ronald Reagan who first began sending the birds to farms to live out their days. This article traces the evolution of the lighthearted ceremony from a historical anecdote into a widely anticipated and formalized White House event, which continues with President Trump pardoning two turkeys named Waddle and Gobble.
