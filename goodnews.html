Three Scientists Awarded Nobel Prize in Medicine for Immunology Breakthroughs

2025-10-06 09:37:43 EDT

Mary E. Brunkow, Fred Ramsdell, and Dr. Shimon Sakaguchi have jointly won the 2025 Nobel Prize in medicine for their foundational discoveries related to peripheral immune tolerance. Their research identified a specific type of T cell, known as regulatory T cells (T-regs), and the gene Foxp3 that controls their development. These cells act as crucial guards that prevent the immune system from attacking the body's own tissues, a process that can lead to autoimmune diseases. Their work has opened a new field of immunology and is paving the way for potential new treatments for various conditions.
