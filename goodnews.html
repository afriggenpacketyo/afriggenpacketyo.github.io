2025-11-05 12:03:44 EST

While the yellow and orange colors of autumn leaves are well understood, scientists have not reached a consensus on why some trees produce vibrant red pigments just before their leaves fall. One leading theory, the photoprotection hypothesis, suggests the red pigments act as a sunscreen, protecting the leaf as it recovers valuable nitrogen. Another prominent hypothesis proposes that the red color co-evolved as a warning signal to insects like aphids, deterring them from laying eggs. Researchers continue to study this natural phenomenon, acknowledging that the purpose of this costly energy expenditure by trees remains one of autumn's biggest mysteries.
