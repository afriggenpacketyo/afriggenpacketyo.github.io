China Develops First Lunar Timekeeping Software for Synchronizing Earth and Moon Clocks

2026-01-11 23:00:11 EST

Chinese researchers have unveiled the world's inaugural timekeeping software tailored for the Moon, addressing the scientific challenge posed by the Moon's faster-ticking clocks due to its weaker gravitational field. A team at the Purple Mountain Observatory developed a sophisticated model that integrates both the Moon's gravitational effects and its orbital motion, enabling precise synchronization of lunar events with Earth's timekeeping systems. This innovative method demonstrates remarkable accuracy, reportedly remaining within a few tens of nanoseconds over a span of 1,000 years. Experts emphasize that such precise lunar timekeeping is becoming an essential engineering requirement for advanced navigation, where even minute discrepancies can have substantial impacts.
