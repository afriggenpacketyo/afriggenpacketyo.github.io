Scientists Solve Mystery of Orange Cats with Help from Cat Owners

2025-05-22 05:00:04 EDT

New research has identified the genetic mutation that gives orange cats their distinctive color, solving a decades-old mystery. Two studies, published in Current Biology, pinpointed a small piece of missing DNA near the ARHGAP36 gene responsible for the orange mutation. The research involved collecting DNA samples from hundreds of cat owners, who eagerly participated in the study. The findings reveal that the mutation activates the ARHGAP36 gene, which is not normally active in pigment cells, and intersects with the pathway responsible for red hair in humans, but in a different way.
