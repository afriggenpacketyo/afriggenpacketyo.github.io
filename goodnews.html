Spectacular aurora borealis displays visible across the UK

2026-01-20 04:08:57 EST

The skies across the United Kingdom were illuminated by the Northern Lights, also known as the Aurora Borealis, in vibrant shades of pink and green. This natural phenomenon occurs when solar eruptions send particles into Earth's atmosphere, interacting with gases to create colorful displays. While typically seen near the Arctic Circle, high solar activity allowed for visibility across much of the UK, and even as far south as northern Italy. The Met Office indicated that the activity, caused by a severe geomagnetic storm, would likely decline but might still be visible in Scotland and Northern Ireland. The interaction of solar particles with oxygen and nitrogen atoms in the atmosphere produces the observed colors, with oxygen typically glowing green and nitrogen appearing red, blue, and pink.
