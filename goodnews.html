Americans exposed to hantavirus on a cruise ship were largely transported to the University of Nebraska Medical Center (UNMC) in Omaha due to its specialized National Quarantine Unit. This federally funded facility, the only one of its kind, is designed for monitoring asymptomatic individuals in individual negative air pressure rooms, resembling a hotel rather than a hospital. Its adjacent Biocontainment Unit handles symptomatic patients requiring hospital-based care. UNMC officials emphasized the center's expertise and robust safety protocols, making it an ideal location for managing such public health threats. Two other passengers were sent to Emory University in Atlanta to preserve space, demonstrating a coordinated national response.
