Kokichi Akuzawa, a 102-year-old man from Japan, has officially become the oldest person to climb Mount Fuji, according to Guinness World Records. He reached the 12,388-foot summit on August 5, surpassing the previous record he set himself at the age of 96. The remarkable feat was accomplished over three days, just months after Akuzawa was hospitalized for heart failure. Despite the health challenges, he trained rigorously for the ascent. The centenarian mountaineer said he couldn't have done it without the support of others and was pleased to have made it to the top.
