Total Lunar Eclipse to Create "Blood Moon" Visible Across Parts of the UK

2025-09-07 04:56:28 EDT

A total lunar eclipse is set to create a spectacular "Blood Moon" visible to sky-watchers in many parts of the United Kingdom and across the globe. The phenomenon occurs when the Earth passes directly between the Sun and Moon, casting a shadow that gives the lunar surface a distinct reddish hue. This coloring is caused by Rayleigh scattering, the same effect that produces red sunsets. While visibility in the UK will vary by location due to moonrise times, the event offers a chance to witness celestial mechanics in action, with the next similar eclipse not visible in the region until 2026.
