Strategies to Reduce Exposure to Widespread "Forever Chemicals" (PFAS)

2026-06-02 12:06:38 EDT

"Forever chemicals," or PFAS, are pervasive in drinking water, food, and various consumer products, and have been linked to negative health outcomes including certain cancers. These chemicals are highly durable and don't easily break down in the environment or human body. While complete elimination is impossible, individuals can reduce exposure by checking water quality and installing certified filters, being mindful of PFAS in food (especially seafood), and identifying them in household and personal care products. Prioritizing the replacement of frequently used items and supporting manufacturers phasing out PFAS can help decrease body levels over time.
