Understanding Home Ownership When Utilizing a Reverse Mortgage

A reverse mortgage allows homeowners aged 62 and older to convert home equity into cash without monthly loan payments, but it raises questions about ownership. Homeowners who take out a reverse mortgage retain the legal title and ownership of their house; the lender does not take possession but places a lien on the property to secure the loan. The loan balance, including interest and fees, becomes due when the borrower sells the home, moves out, or dies, at which point the heirs can choose to repay the loan to keep the house or sell it to settle the debt.
