Study Shows Early Exposure to Beneficial Bacteria Can Reduce Lung Infections in Children

2025-06-04 20:48:13 EDT

UK scientists have discovered that early exposure to beneficial bacteria, specifically Bifidobacterium longum, in newborns can significantly reduce the risk of lung infections. The study, conducted by University College London and the Sanger Institute, analyzed stool samples from over 1,000 newborns and tracked their health for two years. Results indicated that babies with B. longum were less likely to be hospitalized for lung infections, suggesting these bacteria play a crucial role in immune system development. Researchers believe the bacteria interact with the immune system, helping it differentiate between harmful and harmless entities, and this protective effect was more pronounced in babies born via vaginal delivery.
