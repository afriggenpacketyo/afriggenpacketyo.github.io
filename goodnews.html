Assessing the Necessity of Medicare Supplemental Coverage for Retirees

2025-10-03 14:24:43 EDT

While not mandatory, Medicare supplemental (Medigap) coverage is often crucial for retirees to manage unpredictable and potentially steep out-of-pocket medical costs not covered by Original Medicare. Original Medicare covers only about 80% of costs and has no annual out-of-pocket maximum, leaving beneficiaries vulnerable to substantial expenses, especially as health needs increase with age. Medigap policies help cover deductibles and coinsurance, offering financial predictability and flexibility to choose any Medicare-accepting provider nationwide, contrasting with the network restrictions often found in Medicare Advantage plans. This coverage provides a vital financial buffer for most retirees. (not investment advice)
