Public officials are issuing urgent warnings regarding the concerning spread of the invasive hammerhead worm, an Asian species now detected across various U.S. states, including Maine, Texas, and California. These toxic worms pose a significant ecological threat by preying primarily on native earthworm populations, which can lead to declines in biodiversity and vital ecosystem services. Furthermore, their toxic exterior can cause irritation to humans and pets upon contact. Authorities, like the Maine Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, instruct the public not to squish or cut these worms, as this causes them to multiply. Instead, they advise freezing them for 48 hours or drowning them in soapy water, and reporting sightings to citizen science platforms like iNaturalist.
