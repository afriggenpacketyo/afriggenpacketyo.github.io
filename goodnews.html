2025-09-09 13:49:44 EDT

NASA is utilizing the James Webb Space Telescope to study TRAPPIST-1 e, an Earth-sized exoplanet located 40 light-years away that orbits within its star's habitable zone. This positioning makes it theoretically possible for liquid water to exist on its surface, a key ingredient for life. Scientists are using the telescope's spectrograph to analyze starlight passing through the planet's potential atmosphere, searching for chemical signatures that would confirm its existence and composition. While researchers believe any primary hydrogen-helium atmosphere has been stripped away by stellar radiation, they are hopeful that the planet may have developed a heavier, secondary atmosphere, similar to Earth's.

High Income Does Not Disqualify Individuals from Medicare Eligibility, But It Does Increase Premiums

2025-09-09 12:50:31 EDT

While high earnings do not make an individual ineligible for Medicare, they can significantly increase the monthly premiums for certain parts of the program. Eligibility for Medicare is primarily based on age (65 or older) or disability status, not income. However, higher earners are subject to an Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount (IRMAA), which adds a surcharge to their Part B (medical insurance) and Part D (prescription drug) premiums. These surcharges are calculated based on modified adjusted gross income from two years prior, meaning recent retirees may face higher costs initially. Financial planning strategies, such as managing withdrawals and considering Roth conversions, can help mitigate these additional expenses. (not investment advice)
