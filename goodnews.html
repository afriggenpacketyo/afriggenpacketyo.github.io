Magnitude 6.2 Earthquake Strikes Western Japan, No Tsunami Threat

2026-01-05 22:51:15 EST

A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck western Japan late Tuesday morning local time, centered in Shimane and Tottori Prefectures, followed by a magnitude 5.1 aftershock. The Japan Meteorological Agency confirmed there is no tsunami risk but issued warnings for potential aftershocks over the next week and increased risks of landslides and falling rocks in affected areas. Fortunately, no injuries have been reported, and regional electric power companies confirmed no abnormalities. While bullet train services were temporarily disrupted and visible shaking was captured on cameras, the immediate impact appears limited, indicating a significant but relatively contained seismic event for the highly prepared nation.
