Total Lunar Eclipse to Create 'Blood Moon' Spectacle Visible in the UK

2025-09-07 01:54:00 EDT

Sky-watchers in the United Kingdom are set for a celestial treat as a total lunar eclipse will turn the full moon a deep red color, an event commonly known as a 'Blood Moon'. The phenomenon occurs when the Earth passes directly between the Sun and the Moon, casting its shadow on the lunar surface. The reddish hue is caused by sunlight refracting through Earth's atmosphere, a process called Rayleigh scattering. While the full eclipse lasts for over an hour, visibility in the UK will be limited to the period after moonrise, with eastern parts of the country getting the best and longest view.
