Training Program Aims to Eliminate 'Elderspeak' in Care Settings

2025-05-03 07:34:28 EDT

A new training program called CHAT (Changing Talk) is designed to reduce the use of 'elderspeak,' a communication style resembling baby talk, when interacting with older adults. Clarissa Shaw defines elderspeak as communication that assumes frailty, incompetence, and dependence, often involving terms of endearment or plural pronouns. Studies show that elderspeak can lead to resistance to care, and CHAT has demonstrated success in reducing both elderspeak and resistant behaviors in nursing homes. The program is now being adapted for online use to reach more facilities and promote respectful communication with older individuals.
