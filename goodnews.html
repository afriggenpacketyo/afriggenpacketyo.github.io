Understanding Key Differences Between ETFs and Mutual Funds for Investors

2025-09-22 07:00:01 EDT

This article outlines the crucial distinctions between Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) and mutual funds, both popular investment vehicles for diversification. A primary difference lies in trading: ETFs trade like stocks throughout the day, while mutual fund transactions are priced at day's end. More significantly for long-term investors, ETFs offer greater tax efficiency due to their 'in-kind' transaction mechanism, which typically avoids capital gains distributions common in mutual funds. Additionally, ETFs generally have lower expense ratios, making them a cheaper option, though mutual funds still offer a broader universe of funds and are more commonly found in 401(k) plans.
