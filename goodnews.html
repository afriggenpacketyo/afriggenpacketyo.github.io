Existing Home Sales Rebound Sharply in May to Highest Level Since December

2026-06-09 10:14:32 EDT

Sales of previously owned homes experienced a significant rebound in May, rising 3.2% from April to a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 4.17 million units, exceeding economists' expectations. This marks the strongest pace since December, with sales also up 3.2% year-over-year. The increase is attributed to improved affordability, as mortgage rates slightly decreased in April, coupled with income gains outpacing home price growth in many areas. Home inventory saw a modest increase of 3.3% month-over-month, providing a 4.5 months' supply at the current sales pace. Despite rising inventory, median home prices reached a record high for May at $429,300, a 1.3% increase from the previous year. First-time homebuyers represented 35% of sales, up from the previous year. Homes remained on the market for an average of 29 days, indicating a relatively active market. Sales of higher-priced homes saw a notable increase, while sales of lower-priced homes declined.
