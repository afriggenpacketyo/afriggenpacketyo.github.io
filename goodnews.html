California Condor Pair Believed to Be Tending First Egg in Northern California in Over a Century

2026-03-05 13:03:45 EST

A pair of California condors, Ney-gem’ ‘Ne-chweenkah’ and ‘Hlow Hoo-let’, are believed to be tending to the first condor egg laid in Northern California in over a century, deep within Redwood National Park. This potential nesting marks a significant milestone for the critically endangered species, which vanished from the region due to lead poisoning and hunting. The condors were captive-bred and released in 2022 as part of the Yurok Tribe's reintroduction efforts, which view the condor as culturally vital. While the egg's presence is unconfirmed due to the remote nest, conservationists are cautiously optimistic about this signal of recovery.
