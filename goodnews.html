New WHO Study Identifies Modifiable Risk Factors for Cancer Prevention

2026-02-04 09:23:00 EST

A new World Health Organization (WHO) study, published in Nature Medicine, reveals that 7.1 million new cancer cases in 2022 were linked to modifiable risk factors (MRFs) like tobacco, alcohol, and infections, representing 37.8% of all new cases. The study, involving 36 cancer types across 185 countries, is the first to include infectious agents such as hepatitis B and HPV in MRF analysis. It highlights that HPV-caused cancers account for the highest share of preventable cancers in women globally, despite vaccine availability, underscoring issues like vaccine hesitancy and access to healthcare. Researchers emphasize the urgent need for targeted prevention strategies to address the growing cancer burden.
