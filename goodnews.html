Experts Clarify the Meaning and Misuse of the Term 'Gaslighting'

2026-03-25 05:00:00 EDT

The term "gaslighting," Merriam-Webster's 2022 Word of the Year, has become ubiquitous but is often misused, according to therapists and sociologists. Originally inspired by a 1944 film, it describes a deliberate psychological manipulation to make someone doubt their own reality, going beyond simple lying. Experts emphasize that gaslighting is "crazy-making" and aims to undermine a person's ability to trust themselves as a witness to the world. While often associated with malice, it can also be a defense mechanism used to wield power in a situation. Clarifying its precise meaning is crucial, as overuse risks trivializing a deeply serious and "soul-destroying" form of abuse, hindering victims from identifying and addressing the behavior.
