The Truth Behind Thanksgiving Drowsiness: It's Not the Turkey

2025-11-27 10:02:38 EST

The common belief that turkey induces sleepiness due to its tryptophan content is a persistent myth. In reality, turkey contains no more of the amino acid than other poultry, and not enough to cause drowsiness on its own. The actual cause of post-Thanksgiving fatigue, known as postprandial somnolence, is the consumption of a large, high-carbohydrate meal. Digesting heavy foods like stuffing, potatoes, and pie requires significant energy, diverting blood flow from the brain to the gut and leading to that familiar urge for a nap.
