New Research Explains Why Type 1 Diabetes Is More Aggressive in Young Children

2025-11-12 21:48:00 EST

Scientists have discovered that type 1 diabetes is more severe in young children because the pancreas is still developing, making it more vulnerable to the autoimmune attack that causes the disease. A study published in Science Advances shows that insulin-producing beta cells in children under seven exist in small, fragile clusters. The immune system destroys these cells before they can mature into the larger, more durable islets found in adults. This finding provides hope that new immunotherapy drugs, like teplizumab, could delay the disease's onset, allowing the pancreas time to mature and reducing the long-term severity for young patients.
