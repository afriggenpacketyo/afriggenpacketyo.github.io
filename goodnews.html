Explaining the Meteorological and Astronomical Definitions of Winter

2025-11-23 05:15:38 EST

As colder weather arrives, the official start of winter depends on which calendar you follow: meteorological or astronomical. Meteorologists define winter as the three-month period from December 1st to the last day of February, a fixed schedule that simplifies year-over-year climate data comparisons. In contrast, astronomical winter begins on the winter solstice, typically December 21st, which is the shortest day of the year, and lasts until the spring equinox in March. The meteorological definition aligns with the UK's coldest months, as historical data shows that the country's record-low temperatures have consistently occurred within the December to February timeframe.
