Mysterious Gelatinous Blobs in US Waterways Identified as Harmless Bryozoan Colonies

2025-08-28 16:44:35 EDT

Mysterious, brain-like gelatinous blobs recently spotted in U.S. waterways have been identified by wildlife officials as magnificent bryozoans, which are harmless colonies of tiny aquatic invertebrates. The U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service explained that while they may look strange, these organisms are actually beneficial to their freshwater ecosystems. Each blob consists of thousands of individual creatures called zooids that work together to filter-feed on plankton, thereby cleaning the water. These ancient organisms have existed for hundreds of millions of years and are a natural, though seldom seen, part of many slow-moving rivers and lakes across the country.
