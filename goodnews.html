England Announces Major Overhaul of Special Educational Needs System

2026-02-23 12:51:20 EST

England's government has announced a significant overhaul of its special educational needs and disabilities (SEND) system, aiming to make mainstream schools more inclusive. From 2035, only children with the most complex needs will be eligible for Education, Health and Care Plans (EHCPs), though existing plans will be maintained until at least 2029 before reassessments begin. The reforms include a substantial £4 billion investment over three years, with funds directed to schools and enhanced access to specialist experts. A new system of Individual Support Plans (ISPs) will categorize support into "targeted," "targeted plus," or "specialist" tiers, aiming to reduce parental struggles for adequate assistance and improve outcomes for children with SEND.
