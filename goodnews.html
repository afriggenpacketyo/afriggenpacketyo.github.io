Large Joro Spiders Expand Presence Across the U.S., Posing No Danger to Humans

2026-04-09 14:45:33 EDT

The Joro spider, an invasive species known for its large size and colorful appearance, has been observed expanding its presence across the United States, including recent sightings in Southern California. Despite their intimidating size, these spiders are not considered dangerous to humans or pets, and their venom is not medically significant. Experts note that Joro spiders play a role in natural pest control by consuming mosquitoes and other insects. The spiders disperse through a process called 'ballooning,' where spiderlings use silk to travel on the wind. While their prevalence is increasing, they pose no threat and are a fascinating example of adaptation.
