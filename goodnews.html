2025-09-07 19:13:39 EDT

Stargazers across Asia, significant portions of Europe, and Africa were treated to the breathtaking spectacle of a "blood moon" during a total lunar eclipse. This captivating celestial event occurs when the sun, Earth, and moon precisely align, causing Earth's shadow to cast an ethereal, deep red hue upon the moon's surface. The phenomenon is attributed to sunlight being refracted and scattered through Earth's atmosphere, with shorter blue wavelengths dispersing more readily than longer red ones. While people in Asia enjoyed the most complete view of the total eclipse, Europeans and Africans experienced a partial eclipse, continuing to inspire awe as it has for millennia.
