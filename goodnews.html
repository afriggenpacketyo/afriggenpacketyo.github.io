Canada Conditionally Approves Transfer of Marineland Beluga Whales to U.S.

2026-01-26 20:25:22 EST

Canada has granted conditional approval for Marineland to export its 30 beluga whales and four dolphins to U.S. institutions, effectively saving them from euthanasia. Fisheries Minister Joanne Thompson confirmed the decision after Marineland, a shuttered theme park, cited financial difficulties and threatened to euthanize the animals if permits weren't issued. The park is in discussions with four U.S. aquariums, including Shedd and Georgia Aquariums, for the relocation. This move aligns with a 2019 law banning whale and dolphin captivity, though Marineland's animals were grandfathered in, and follows previous denials for transfer to a Chinese aquarium.

Northern Ireland Introduces Graduated Driver Licensing to Enhance Road Safety for Young Drivers

2026-01-26 19:01:23 EST

Northern Ireland is set to become the first UK area to implement graduated driver licensing, aiming to reduce fatal road crashes involving young people. These significant reforms, effective in October, target 17-23-year-olds and include a six-month waiting period before a practical test, along with restrictions on night-time driving and passenger numbers for new drivers. Infrastructure Minister Liz Kimmins stated the changes address the disproportionate involvement of this age group in serious collisions. Police and officials welcome the scheme, which mirrors laws in other countries, emphasizing a slower, safer journey for new drivers to gain experience.
