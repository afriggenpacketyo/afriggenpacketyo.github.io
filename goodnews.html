The Safety Reasons Behind Dimming Cabin Lights for Takeoff and Landing

2025-06-29 07:00:00 EDT

Airlines dim cabin lights during takeoff and landing primarily as a critical safety measure, not to conserve energy. According to a pilot and a flight attendant, this procedure allows passengers' and crew's eyes to pre-adjust to darkness. This is crucial in the event of an emergency evacuation, particularly if there's a power failure, as it significantly reduces the time needed for eyes to adapt to low-light conditions. Dimming the lights also makes it easier to see emergency floor path lighting and to spot potential hazards outside the aircraft, such as fire or debris, during the most critical phases of a flight.
