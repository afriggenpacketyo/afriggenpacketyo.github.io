EU Negotiators Reach Agreement on New Rules for Essential Medicines

2026-05-12 02:08:00 EDT

European Union negotiators have agreed on new rules designed to strengthen the bloc's supply chains for essential medicines and reduce reliance on non-EU manufacturers. This initiative aims to address past drug shortages of critical medications like painkillers and antibiotics. The proposed rules will prioritize medicines manufactured within Europe in public procurement processes and offer faster approvals and funding for "strategic projects." The EU also plans to facilitate joint purchasing of important medicines, particularly for rare diseases. These measures come in response to identified bottlenecks in the supply of active ingredients and the concentration of production in a few countries, which became evident during global health crises. The agreement still requires approval from member states and the European Parliament.
