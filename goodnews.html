2025-11-10 10:51:00 EST

In Germany, November 11 marks both the solemn St. Martin's Day, where children parade with lanterns, and the exuberant start of the Carnival season. These seemingly contradictory celebrations share a common origin tied to the end of the agricultural year and the beginning of a pre-Christmas fasting period in the Middle Ages. St. Martin's Day honors a charitable saint, while Carnival provided a final day of feasting and revelry before the austerity of Advent. The two traditions thus represent complementary aspects of historical life: piety and celebration.
