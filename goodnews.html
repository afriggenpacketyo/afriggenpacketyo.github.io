Explainer: The Role and Powers of the Federal Communications Commission (FCC)

2025-09-19 20:34:12 EDT

Recent threats of regulatory action against ABC have raised questions about the powers of the Federal Communications Commission (FCC). Created in 1934, the FCC is an independent agency that regulates interstate and international communications by radio, television, wire, satellite, and cable. While it holds significant authority over local broadcast stations, including the power to grant and, in theory, revoke licenses, it has limited jurisdiction over cable channels or online content. The FCC is led by five commissioners appointed by the president, with no more than three from the same political party, and is legally barred from engaging in censorship.
