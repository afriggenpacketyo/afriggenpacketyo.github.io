Understanding the 50+1 Rule in German Football

2025-06-18 06:44:00 EDT

The 50+1 rule in German football ensures that club members retain control over professional football clubs, preventing external investors from taking complete control. The rule, in place since 1999, stipulates that the parent club must hold at least 50 percent plus one vote in its spun-off professional football company. This rule aims to protect fan culture and ensure a level of equality, but critics argue it puts German clubs at a financial disadvantage compared to other countries. Exceptions exist for investors with 20 years of investment, and some clubs, like RB Leipzig, are criticized for circumventing the rule.
