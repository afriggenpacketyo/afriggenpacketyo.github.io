Leonard Jacoby, Legal Advertising Pioneer, Dies at 83

2026-01-17 06:00:00 EST

Leonard Jacoby, co-founder of Jacoby & Meyers, a law firm known for pioneering legal advertising, has died at 83. Jacoby revolutionized the legal industry by making legal services accessible to the middle class through innovative advertising on billboards and television, a practice once deemed unethical by the State Bar. His firm fought a years-long battle, culminating in Supreme Court rulings that protected lawyers' right to advertise under the First Amendment. Jacoby believed that despite potential excesses, opening up the legal system to everyday people was a greater good. His legacy transformed how law firms operate and reach clients nationwide.

Preparations include rolling out the Space Launch System (SLS) rocket and Orion Space Capsule to the launch pad, followed by a wet dress rehearsal.
