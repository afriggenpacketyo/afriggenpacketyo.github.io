Researchers from the University of Bonn and Max Planck Institute discovered superparamagnetic macrophages, immune cells that accumulate iron, are present in the liver near nerve cells, postulating this is how they transmit magnetic field data to the brain. This groundbreaking discovery reveals a previously unknown mechanism for magnetoreception in animals, expanding scientific understanding of how birds achieve their remarkable navigational feats over vast distances. The findings highlight the unexpected complexity and sophistication of animal sensory systems.
