Great Apes Use Kissing and Cuddling to Build Trust Before Stressful Events

2026-07-21 19:48:38 EDT

In experiments mimicking natural feeding conflicts, chimpanzees were observed even placing fingers in one another's mouths, a surprising finding given their aggressive reputation. This "rallying cry" behavior, fostering a collective "we" mentality, is believed to have evolutionary roots dating back at least seven million years, predating human language.
