South Africa's Constitutional Court Rules Men Can Legally Adopt Their Wives' Surnames

2025-09-11 19:31:00 EDT

South Africa's highest court has overturned an apartheid-era law, ruling that husbands now have the right to take their wives' family names upon marriage. The Constitutional Court found that the existing legislation discriminated on the basis of gender and was a "colonial import" that infringed upon the right to equality guaranteed in the country's post-apartheid constitution. The case was brought by two couples who challenged the Department of Home Affairs after being denied the ability to change the husband's name. The government has been given two years to amend the Births and Deaths Registration Act to reflect the landmark decision, which has sparked widespread debate on social media.
