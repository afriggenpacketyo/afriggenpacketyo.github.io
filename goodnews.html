China Develops Jam-Proof Satellite Network Using Coded Light Signals

2026-03-29 22:01:05 EDT

Tsinghua University in China has developed a new satellite network utilizing coded light signals, dubbed "lighthouses in space," to provide jam-proof and highly precise positioning. This system offers an alternative to traditional radio wave-based systems like GPS and BeiDou. Led by Professor Xing Fei, the project places powerful light sources on satellites that send narrow, straight-line coded signals to Earth. Receivers on the ground detect this light to calculate position, making it significantly harder to interfere with than radio signals and offering greater accuracy than star-based navigation. This innovation aims to guide everything from vehicles to spacecraft, addressing vulnerabilities in existing navigation technologies.
