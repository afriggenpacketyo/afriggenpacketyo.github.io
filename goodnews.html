Understanding the Annual Migration Journey of Geese

2026-03-09 05:00:00 EDT

Geese embark on thousands-of-miles-long northbound migrations from late February to May, following the "green wave" of warmer days, melting snow, and growing vegetation. Avian ecologists explain that geese migrate in search of food and to avoid competition in their wintering areas, bulking up on corn, beans, and wheat for endurance. These journeys often involve multiple generations and "loose family associates" flying in V-formations for aerodynamic efficiency, with lead pilots rotating responsibilities. Upon arrival, they find breeding partners and establish nesting spots, with goslings hatching in early summer before their first southward flight.
