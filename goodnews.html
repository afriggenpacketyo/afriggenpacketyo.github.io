Northern Ireland to Offer Free Sign Language Classes for Deaf Children

2026-04-28 03:44:01 EDT

Northern Ireland is set to become the first part of the UK to provide free sign language classes to all deaf children and their families under a new Sign Language Bill. The legislation grants official and equal recognition to British Sign Language (BSL) and Irish Sign Language (ISL), and mandates the Department for Communities to offer classes. Advocates have long campaigned for this change, emphasizing the importance of language, culture, and equality for the deaf community. Public bodies will also be required to ensure their services are accessible to deaf individuals.

This development offers hope for millions globally affected by thalassaemia, a condition that can lead to severe health complications and premature death.
