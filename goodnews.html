Understanding Jewish Funeral Rites and Mourning Traditions

2025-10-15 10:07:00 EDT

Jewish funeral practices prioritize dignity and quick burial, with a strong focus on eternal memory among the living rather than an afterlife. Bodies undergo a ritual bath (Taharah) and are buried in simple wooden caskets or shrouds, without embalming or public viewing. Key rituals include K'riah, where close family rip garments as an expression of pain, and the communal recitation of the Mourner's Kaddish, a prayer exalting God. The mourning period often extends to seven days of shiva, followed by Kaddish recitation for specific durations, and the custom of leaving pebbles on gravestones.
