Study suggests optimism may be linked to a reduced risk of dementia

2026-04-13 13:14:55 EDT

New research published in the Journal of the American Geriatrics Society indicates a potential correlation between a person's optimism and a lower risk of developing dementia. The study followed over 9,000 cognitively healthy adults for up to 14 years and found that individuals reporting higher levels of optimism had approximately a 15% lower risk of dementia. This association remained significant even after accounting for various demographic and health-related factors. While the study highlights a possible link, researchers emphasize that it is observational and cannot prove causation, suggesting further clinical trials are needed to explore whether optimism can be boosted to impact long-term brain health.
