Paleontologists Discover 500-Million-Year-Old, Three-Eyed Predator

2025-05-17 16:38:36 EDT

Paleontologists have discovered the fossilized remains of Mosura fentoni, a 500-million-year-old, three-eyed predator with a pencil sharpener-like mouth, in the Canadian Rockies. The creature, nicknamed the 'sea moth,' provides new insights into animal life during the Cambrian period. The Mosura possessed jointed claws with long spines, body flaps for swimming, and a segmented body similar to modern arthropods. This discovery reveals that radiodonts, the extinct group to which Mosura belonged, were more complex than previously thought.
