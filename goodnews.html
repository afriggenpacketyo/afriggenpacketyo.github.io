Claudette Colvin, Civil Rights Pioneer Who Preceded Rosa Parks, Dies at 86

2026-01-21 08:28:01 EST

Claudette Colvin, a pivotal yet largely unrecognized figure in the U.S. civil rights movement, has died at 86. Nine months before Rosa Parks' iconic protest, a 15-year-old Colvin was arrested in Montgomery, Alabama, for refusing to give up her bus seat to a white passenger in March 1955. She later became one of four plaintiffs in Browder v. Gayle, the federal lawsuit that successfully challenged Montgomery's bus segregation laws, which the Supreme Court upheld. Despite her crucial role, Colvin's story was overshadowed, partly due to societal perceptions and her young age and pregnancy at the time. Her courageous actions, though belatedly recognized, were instrumental in the fight against segregation.
