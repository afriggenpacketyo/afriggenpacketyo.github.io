Largest Piece of Mars on Earth Expected to Fetch Up to $4 Million at Auction

2025-07-10 21:11:51 EDT

A 54-pound meteorite, confirmed to be the largest piece of Mars on Earth, is set to be auctioned by Sotheby's with an estimated value of up to $4 million. The rock, designated NWA 16788, was discovered in Niger's Sahara Desert in 2023 after a 140-million-mile journey through space, likely dislodged from Mars by an asteroid impact. Martian meteorites are exceptionally rare, constituting only about 0.6% of all meteorites found on our planet. This particular specimen represents a significant portion of all known Martian material on Earth, offering valuable data for scientific research.

Museum officials described the find as incredibly rare, likening the odds to hitting a hole-in-one from the moon.
