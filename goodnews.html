Upcoming Total Lunar Eclipse to Be Visible Across Europe, Africa, and Asia

2025-09-05 08:22:00 EDT

A total lunar eclipse, also known as a Blood Moon, will be visible on September 7, 2025, for observers in Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia. During this astronomical event, the Earth passes directly between the sun and the moon, casting a shadow that gives the moon a distinct reddish or orange hue. This color results from sunlight filtering through the Earth's atmosphere, where shorter-wavelength colors are scattered. Unlike a solar eclipse, a lunar eclipse is safe to view with the naked eye and can last for a longer duration, offering a spectacular celestial sight for those in the visibility path.
