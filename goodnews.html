2025-09-20 17:35:01 EDT

A large-scale Danish study from Aarhus University Hospital challenges long-held beliefs about body mass index (BMI) and health risks. Researchers found that underweight individuals were nearly three times more likely to die during the study period than those with a BMI at the high end of the normal range. The findings suggest that being overweight or moderately obese did not correlate with higher death rates compared to those in the upper-normal BMI category. This supports the concept of being "fat but fit," indicating that factors like body fat distribution and overall fitness are more critical than BMI alone.
