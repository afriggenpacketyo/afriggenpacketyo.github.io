Understanding Federal Limits on Wage Garnishment for Unpaid Credit Card Debt

2025-10-17 11:20:16 EDT

For individuals struggling with delinquent credit card debt, wage garnishment represents a severe consequence where a creditor can legally take a portion of one's earnings. Federal law limits this amount to the lesser of either 25% of disposable income or the amount by which weekly earnings exceed 30 times the federal minimum wage. However, state laws can offer greater protections, with some prohibiting wage garnishment for unsecured debts entirely. This article outlines these rules and explores proactive strategies to avoid garnishment, such as negotiating settlements with creditors, working with debt relief companies, or, as a last resort, considering bankruptcy.
