Chile and Google Partner to Install Undersea Cable Connecting South America, Asia, and Oceania

2025-06-05 00:16:00 EDT

Chile and Google have signed an agreement to install the first-ever submarine fiber optic cable connecting South America, Asia, and Oceania by 2027. The "Humboldt Project," a 14,800-kilometer cable from Valparaiso, Chile, to Sydney, Australia, via French Polynesia, aims to provide a faster alternative to existing connections via the US. The project is expected to benefit other South American countries like Argentina, Paraguay, and Brazil. Google is investing between $300 million and $550 million, while the Chilean government will contribute $25 million to the initiative which will also reduce signal latency, improving telemedicine capabilities.
