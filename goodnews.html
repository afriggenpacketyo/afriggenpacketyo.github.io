Slovenia's parliament has passed a law legalizing medically-assisted suicide, making it the first Eastern European nation to do so. The legislation, approved with a 50-34 vote, applies to terminally-ill adults facing unbearable suffering with no hope of improvement, who are mentally competent and have exhausted other treatments. This decision follows a consultative referendum where 55% of voters supported the right to end-of-life autonomy. While hailed as a victory for compassion and dignity, the law's implementation is pending the finalization of detailed procedures and oversight mechanisms, and it faces opposition from groups vowing to seek a new referendum.
