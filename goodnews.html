UN Charter's Article 2(4): Examining the Cornerstone of International Law on Use of Force

2026-03-04 03:35:00 EST

Article 2(4) of the UN Charter, a cornerstone of modern international law, prohibits states from threatening or using force against another's territorial integrity or political independence, aiming to resolve disputes peacefully. However, its interpretation remains contested, particularly regarding "pre-emptive" strikes, humanitarian intervention, and regime change, which are not explicitly defined as exceptions. The article discusses historical applications, including Iraq's invasion of Kuwait, NATO's intervention in Kosovo (deemed "illegal but legitimate"), the US-led invasion of Iraq, and Russia's invasion of Ukraine. It highlights grey zones concerning cyber operations and the scope of self-defense under Article 51, alongside challenges posed by the Security Council's veto power.
