Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaur Ancestors

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

Chinese researchers have published findings in the journal Science Advances detailing how birds gradually shed their dinosaur tails to evolve the flight-adapted body plan seen today. The study focuses on the transformation from the longer caudal vertebrae of non-avian dinosaurs to the pygostyle, a short, fused skeletal structure in modern birds that anchors tail feathers and muscles crucial for aerodynamic flight. This discovery is significant given the extreme rarity of early-diverging bird and birdlike dinosaur fossils, providing new insights into one of the most profound morphological changes in terrestrial vertebrate history.
