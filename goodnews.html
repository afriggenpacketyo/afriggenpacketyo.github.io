Fossil Find in China Suggests Some Pterosaurs May Have Been Herbivores

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

A remarkable fossil of a pterosaur discovered in northeastern China is challenging long-held beliefs about the diet of these ancient flying reptiles. The stomach contents of the Sinopterus atavismus fossil contained microscopic plant mineral deposits, known as phytoliths, and quartz crystals, which were likely ingested to help grind tough plant matter. This is the first direct evidence confirming herbivory in this type of pterosaur, suggesting that not all of these creatures were fish-eaters or carnivores. The finding, published in Science Bulletin, sheds new light on the diverse ecosystems of the dinosaur era.
