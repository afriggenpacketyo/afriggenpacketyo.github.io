Understanding Key Differences Between ETFs and Mutual Funds for Investors

2025-09-22 07:00:01 EDT

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) and mutual funds, while both offering diversified investment baskets, have key differences that impact investors. ETFs trade like stocks on an exchange, providing real-time pricing, whereas mutual fund transactions are processed at day-end prices. A significant advantage of ETFs is their tax efficiency, particularly in taxable brokerage accounts, due to "in-kind" transactions that often avoid capital gains distributions common in mutual funds. Additionally, ETFs generally have lower expense ratios, making them cheaper to own. However, mutual funds offer a larger universe of options and are more commonly available in 401(k) plans.
