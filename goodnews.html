The Unlikely Origin of 'OK,' a Word Traced to a 19th-Century Political Campaign

2025-07-23 17:27:00 EDT

The word "OK," one of the most universally understood terms on the planet, has its roots in a playful 19th-century American trend of jocular misspellings. Scholars trace its first appearance to a Boston newspaper in 1839 as an abbreviation for "oll korrect." The term was then widely popularized during President Martin Van Buren's 1840 reelection campaign, as his nickname was "Old Kinderhook." His supporters formed "O.K. Clubs," cementing the phrase in the American lexicon, from where it spread globally through technology and culture.
