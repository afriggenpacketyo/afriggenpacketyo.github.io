The Linguistic Journey of 'Kid': From Young Goat to Child

2026-02-04 05:00:00 EST

The word "kid" has a fascinating linguistic history, transitioning from exclusively referring to a young goat to becoming a common synonym for a child. This evolution began about a thousand years ago when Vikings introduced the Old Norse term to the English language during their settlement in England. By the 17th century, "kid" was used interchangeably for young goats and humans, likely due to the shared traits of being sprightly and curious. The word's usage expanded in the 1800s to include skillful young individuals and even criminals, giving rise to terms like "kidnap" and the playful verb "to kid."
