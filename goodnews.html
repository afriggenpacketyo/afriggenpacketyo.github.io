Indian Researchers Win Ig Nobel Prize for Studying Smelly Shoes

2025-09-27 20:25:28 EDT

Two Indian researchers, Vikash Kumar and Sarthak Mittal, have been awarded an Ig Nobel Prize for their study on how foul-smelling shoes impact the experience of using a shoe rack. Their research, stemming from observations in university hostels, found that the pervasive odor, caused by the bacterium Kytococcus sedentarius, often led people to keep footwear outdoors. The team conducted surveys and experiments, discovering that a brief exposure to ultraviolet C (UVC) light could effectively kill the microbes and eliminate the smell without damaging the shoes. This delightfully unconventional scientific endeavor led to a prototype for a UVC-equipped shoe rack, aiming to enhance user experience and solve a universal domestic problem.

Collagen Supplements: Do They Really Reduce Signs of Skin Ageing?

2025-09-27 18:37:43 EDT

The efficacy of collagen supplements in reducing signs of skin aging remains a subject of skepticism among dermatologists, despite their widespread popularity. Collagen, the body's most abundant protein, naturally declines with age, leading many to seek supplements in various forms. While some users report positive changes in skin, hair, and nails, consultant dermatologists like Emma Wedgeworth question whether ingested collagen peptides effectively reach the skin. Industry-funded studies often suggest benefits, but research without such conflicts of interest frequently shows no significant effect on skin hydration, elasticity, or wrinkles. Experts advise that consistent use of sunblock, a healthy diet, and quitting smoking are far more impactful strategies for maintaining youthful skin than collagen supplements.
