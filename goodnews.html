Nobel Laureate Chen-ning Yang, Pioneering Theoretical Physicist, Dies at 103

2025-10-18 00:23:49 EDT

Chen-ning Yang, a Nobel Prize-winning theoretical physicist, has passed away in Beijing at the remarkable age of 103 due to sickness, as reported by Xinhua. Yang was widely regarded as one of the 20th century's most influential physicists, often mentioned in the same breath as Albert Einstein. His groundbreaking work in 1954, co-authored with Robert Mills, introduced the Yang-Mills theory, a set of equations that proved fundamental to understanding three of nature's four basic forces: electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions. This theory established the mathematical cornerstone for the Standard Model, which unifies these forces and explains elementary particle behavior.
