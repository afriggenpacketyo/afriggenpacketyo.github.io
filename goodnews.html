2025-11-28 23:00:15 EST

In contrast, attendants buried alongside deceased elites in private tombs were overwhelmingly female. This evidence suggests the existence of a highly stratified, patrilineal society with distinct gender roles in sacrificial practices, pushing back the known timeline for female sacrifice in the region by thousands of years. The findings provide new insights into the social structure of this early state-level society.
