Experimental Gene Therapy Shows Lasting Hearing Restoration for Rare Deafness

2026-04-22 11:00:04 EDT

An experimental gene therapy has demonstrated safe and highly effective results in restoring hearing for individuals born with a rare form of genetic deafness (DFNB9). The study, published in Nature, involved 42 participants and used a modified adenovirus to deliver a healthy gene into the inner ear. Researchers observed significant hearing improvements within weeks, with benefits lasting over two years for some patients. This breakthrough offers a potential first-ever treatment for genetic hearing loss, spurring hope for similar approaches for other forms of deafness and potentially age-related or noise-induced hearing loss.
