James Webb Telescope Discovers Salty Clouds Around the "Pink Planet."

2026-06-18 21:49:00 EDT

Researchers using the James Webb Space Telescope have made a surprising discovery: clouds made of salt in the atmosphere of the "Pink Planet," formally known as GJ504b. This planetary-mass companion, located 57 light-years from Earth, is cooler than typical exoplanets, making its atmosphere challenging to study until now. The telescope's infrared imaging revealed chemical fingerprints, which only made sense when salt clouds were included in atmospheric models. This finding suggests a "middle ground" in planetary atmospheres, where salt clouds form in environments too hot for water/ammonia but too cool for silicate clouds, significantly expanding our understanding of exoplanet conditions and atmospheric diversity.
