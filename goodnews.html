Britain's Oldest Prehistoric Rock Art Confirmed in Welsh Cave After Rediscovery

2026-06-01 17:01:43 EDT

A series of 10 red horizontal stripes found in Gower's Bacon Hole cave in Wales has been confirmed as Britain's earliest prehistoric rock art, dating back at least 17,100 years. Initially dismissed as a natural phenomenon in 1928, the art was rediscovered in 2022 and scientifically dated using uranium-thorium methods, confirming human agency. Archaeologist George Nash suggests the markings could have been a "communication system" or "tally marks" from hunter-gatherers in a now-submerged fertile plateau. The discovery is hailed as significant for understanding the deep past of Wales and is prompting calls for the cave, currently under National Trust Cymru custodianship, to be designated a scheduled monument.
