Chinese Scientists Engineer E. coli Bacteria for Targeted Breast Cancer Therapy in Mice

2026-03-18 02:00:25 EDT

Chinese scientists have developed a novel cancer therapy using engineered E. coli bacteria to combat breast tumors from within, demonstrating success in a mouse study. The modified E. coli Nissle 1917 strain colonizes tumors, produces an existing anti-cancer drug directly on-site, and delivers it, significantly reducing the toxic side effects associated with traditional chemotherapy. Published in PLOS Biology, this research from Shandong University paves the way for highly targeted cancer treatments.
