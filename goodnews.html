2025-08-23 18:42:59 EDT

Researchers at Madurai Kamaraj University in India, in collaboration with the UK's Face Lab, have successfully digitally reconstructed the faces of two 2,500-year-old male skulls unearthed from an ancient burial site near Keeladi. These detailed reconstructions primarily exhibit features of Ancient Ancestral South Indians, a population group believed to be the subcontinent's first inhabitants, alongside subtle traces of Middle-East Eurasian and Austro-Asiatic ancestries. The findings provide compelling evidence for an independent urban civilization in southern India dating back to 580 BC, significantly enriching the historical understanding of the Indian subcontinent and challenging long-held narratives that have predominantly focused on northern urbanization. This scientific breakthrough offers profound insights into ancient migration patterns and the region's rich cultural heritage.
