2026-01-01 19:48:12 EST

Experts, including Professor Graeme Close, a leading sports nutrition researcher, advise that most people do not need electrolyte drinks for everyday exercise like gym workouts or 5k runs. The body naturally maintains stable electrolyte levels, and a regular, well-balanced diet provides sufficient minerals like sodium, potassium, and magnesium, making expensive products largely unnecessary. Electrolytes are only beneficial for long, intense periods of exercise, especially in hot conditions or for "salty sweaters," to replenish significant losses. For typical activity, plain water suffices, and there is little evidence that electrolyte products offer performance gains, despite marketing claims.
