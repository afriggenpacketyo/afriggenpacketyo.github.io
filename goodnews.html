The IRS has released new, higher contribution limits for health savings accounts (HSAs) for 2027, offering enhanced triple-tax benefits for eligible investors. The self-only plan limit will increase to $4,500, up from $4,400, while the family coverage limit will rise to $9,000, from $8,750. HSAs provide tax-deductible contributions, tax-free growth, and tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses, but require an eligible high-deductible health insurance plan.
