200-year-old shipwreck discovered by park rangers on remote Canadian island

2026-04-20 06:00:36 EDT

Park rangers on Sable Island, Canada, have unearthed a shipwreck believed to be over 200 years old, potentially the Swift, which sank in 1812. The discovery was made after initial findings of naval artifacts led to further excavation. Sable Island, known as the 'graveyard of the Atlantic,' has a history of numerous shipwrecks. The challenging excavation process, involving stabilization of sandy terrain, was a collaborative effort. The recovered shipwreck section, made of Bermudan cedar, is unusually complete and well-preserved, offering a rare correlation to a documented historical wreck event.
