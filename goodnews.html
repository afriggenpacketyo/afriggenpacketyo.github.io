Understanding the Impact of Medical Debt on Credit Scores and Available Solutions

2025-06-06 14:21:44 EDT

Unpaid medical debt can negatively impact credit scores, but recent changes in credit reporting rules and available relief programs are easing the burden for many patients. Paid medical collections no longer appear on credit reports, and there is a one-year waiting period before medical bills sent to collections are added to credit reports. Smaller debts under $500 are excluded from credit reports altogether. Strategies for managing medical debt include reviewing and negotiating bills, applying for financial assistance, considering debt relief programs, and monitoring credit reports. Taking proactive steps can help individuals avoid collections and limit potential damage to their credit score.
