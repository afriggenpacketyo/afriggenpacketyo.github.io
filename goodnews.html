Viral images of rabbits in Colorado with black, horn-like facial growths have sparked alarm online, but wildlife officials have clarified the cause. The animals are infected with Shope Papilloma Virus, a disease common in the Midwest that is spread by ticks, fleas, and rabbit-to-rabbit contact. The growths, or papillomas, are generally harmless to the rabbits unless they obstruct their ability to eat, and the virus does not spread to humans or other pets. The disease is not new and is believed to have inspired the myth of the "jackalope," a rabbit with antlers.
