Turkey Considers Regulations to Curb Hotel Buffet Food Waste

2025-08-23 03:08:00 EDT

Turkey is actively considering new regulations to tackle the substantial issue of food waste, particularly prevalent in hotel all-you-can-eat buffets and traditional large breakfasts. The country's Presidential Council for Agriculture and Food Policy is developing proposals, driven by data indicating Turkey discards 8.7 million tons of food annually, with an average of 102 kilograms wasted per person. Experts criticize the current system for leading to significant waste of food, resources, and energy. Suggested reforms include enabling guests to customize breakfast selections and implementing flexible pricing to align portions with actual consumption, though some tourism stakeholders remain wary, citing potential conflicts of interest and the economic importance of popular all-inclusive offerings.
