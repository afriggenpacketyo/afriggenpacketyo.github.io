Northern Lights Dazzle UK Skies Following Increased Solar Activity

2026-03-24 04:20:33 EDT

The Northern Lights, or aurora borealis, were once again visible across parts of the UK, including as far south as England, on Monday night, following an increase in solar activity. These spectacular displays occur when charged particles from the Sun collide with gases high in Earth's atmosphere, releasing energy as vibrant light. While the solar eruptions sending energy towards Earth are now starting to decline, the Met Office Space Weather Prediction Centre suggests a low chance of the aurora reappearing in northern Scotland. Each unique display is shaped by the Sun, Earth's magnetic field, and the specific gases present in our planet's atmosphere.
