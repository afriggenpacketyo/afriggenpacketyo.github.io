2026-01-19 23:13:18 EST

This phenomenon, typically visible near the Arctic Circle, occurred due to high solar activity, specifically a Coronal Mass Ejection (CME) from the Sun. The charged particles interacted with Earth's atmosphere, causing oxygen atoms to glow green and nitrogen atoms to emit purple, blue, and pink. The Met Office reported "severe geomagnetic storms" leading to widespread auroras visible as far south as northern Italy. While activity is expected to decline, some visibility may persist in Scotland and Northern Ireland.
