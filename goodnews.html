Early Bacteria Exposure May Reduce Hospitalization Risk for Infants

2025-06-04 20:48:13 EDT

UK scientists have found that the first bacteria infants encounter after birth can protect them from dangerous infections, potentially halving the risk of hospitalization for lung infections. Researchers at University College London and the Sanger Institute analyzed stool samples from 1,082 newborns in the first week of life and tracked their hospital data for two years. The presence of Bifidobacterium longum, a common early inhabitant of the human body, showed a protective effect, with only 4% of babies with this species needing hospitalization for lung infections. The study suggests that early microbial encounters play a crucial role in immune system development.
