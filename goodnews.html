New Research Reveals Sophisticated Culinary Tastes of Ancient Europeans

2026-03-14 08:00:00 EDT

New research published in the scientific journal PLOS ONE suggests that our prehistoric ancestors, living between 5,000 and 8,000 years ago in northern Europe, possessed a surprisingly sophisticated palate. Analysis of "foodcrusts" on ancient potshards revealed traces of diverse ingredients, including freshwater fish like carp, viburnum berries, amaranth, beets, and an edible weed known as oak-leaved goosefoot. This indicates that early humans were remarkably selective in what they cooked, working out complex "recipes" rather than simply consuming raw or minimally processed foods. The findings challenge long-held assumptions about prehistoric diets, suggesting a more varied and cosmopolitan culinary culture existed centuries before modern gastronomy.
