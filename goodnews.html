Caterpillars and Maggots Prized as Sustainable Protein Source in the Democratic Republic of Congo

2025-09-20 06:00:00 EDT

In the Democratic Republic of Congo, edible insects like caterpillars (Mbinzo) and palm weevil larvae (Mpose) are considered both a delicacy and a dietary staple. These insects are rich in protein and vitamins, offering a more affordable and sustainable nutritional alternative to meat in a country where two-thirds of the population lives on less than $2 a day. While traditionally harvested from forests, local NGOs like Farms for Orphans are now commercially raising the larvae to supply orphanages and markets. This practice addresses food scarcity and promotes a resilient local food system.

Neuroscientist Explains the Psychology of Limerence and How to Overcome Romantic Obsession

2025-09-20 05:00:00 EDT

Neuroscientist Tom Bellamy's new book explores 'limerence,' a term for intense romantic infatuation characterized by obsessive thoughts and emotional highs and lows. While this state can be a normal part of early love, it can become a form of 'person addiction' when a healthy bond is not possible, often due to uncertainty or incompatibility. Bellamy suggests strategies to break the cycle, such as limiting contact with the person and actively reframing positive daydreams into negative 'daymares.' He also emphasizes finding new goals and sources of personal fulfillment to redirect the brain's reward system and move past the obsession.
