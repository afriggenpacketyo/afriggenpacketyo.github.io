Researchers Discover Oldest Surviving English Poem in Medieval Manuscript

2026-05-17 10:44:00 EDT

Irish researchers have made a significant historical discovery, identifying "Caedmon's Hymn" as the oldest surviving English-language poem within a 9th-century medieval manuscript located in a Roman library. This finding dramatically pushes back the understanding of English language diffusion by three centuries, connecting scholars to the earliest stages of written English literature. Composed in Old English by a Northumbrian agricultural worker in the 7th century, the nine-line hymn was found within the main body of a Latin text, making it a unique and earlier attestation than previously known marginal or appended copies. The manuscript's complex journey across continents and centuries, eventually leading to its rediscovery, marks a remarkable academic achievement.
