What Retirees Should Know About Health Savings Accounts as They Approach Age 65

2025-11-20 14:26:58 EST

Financial experts are advising individuals with Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) to be aware of key rules as they near the Medicare eligibility age of 65. A critical point is that contributions to an HSA must stop once enrolled in Medicare, with a recommendation to cease contributions six months prior to avoid penalties related to retroactive coverage. While new funds cannot be added, existing HSA balances can be used tax-free for qualified medical expenses, including Medicare Part B and D premiums. After 65, funds can also be withdrawn for non-medical reasons without the 20% penalty, though they will be subject to ordinary income tax. (not investment advice)
