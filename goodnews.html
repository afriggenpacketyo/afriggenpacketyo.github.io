California Expands Mobile Driver's License Program, Increasing Digital ID Access

2026-07-22 23:13:44 EDT

California is significantly expanding its mobile driver’s license pilot program, enabling up to 21 million residents to securely add their driver’s license or state identification card to their smartphones. Governor Gavin Newsom signed Senate Bill 169, which elevates the enrollment cap from 15% to 60% of licensed drivers, as part of a broader initiative to modernize state agencies like the DMV. Although digital licenses are currently accepted at select Transportation Security Administration checkpoints and participating businesses, drivers are still legally required to carry their physical cards while operating a vehicle. The program aims to enhance convenience and streamline interactions, with major cities like San Jose and Los Angeles already showing high enrollment rates.

China Activates Record-Breaking Particle Accelerator for Advanced Atomic Research

2026-07-22 21:00:07 EDT

China has successfully activated a record-breaking particle accelerator, known as the High Intensity Heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF), located in Huizhou, Guangdong province. This substantial 2.6 billion yuan facility is now capable of generating the world's most intense beams of charged atoms, boasting a power output up to eight times greater than the previous global record holder in Darmstadt, Germany. HIAF uniquely integrates a 100-meter-long linear accelerator, a 570-meter-circumference circular accelerator, and multiple storage rings into a single, comprehensive complex, providing scientists with an unprecedented research tool. This significant advancement is expected to enable researchers to create previously unknown atomic nuclei and propel research in crucial fields like cancer treatment and advanced spacecraft materials.
