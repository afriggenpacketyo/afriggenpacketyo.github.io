Explainer on the Upcoming Total Lunar Eclipse and Blood Moon

2025-09-05 08:22:00 EDT

A total lunar eclipse, also known as a "blood moon," will be visible on September 7, 2025, to observers in Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia. This astronomical event occurs when the Earth passes directly between the sun and the moon, casting a shadow that envelops the moon. The moon appears reddish-orange because some sunlight filters through Earth's atmosphere, with longer red wavelengths reaching the lunar surface. Unlike a solar eclipse, a lunar eclipse is safe to view with the naked eye and can last for a longer duration.
