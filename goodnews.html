Scientists Uncover the Complex Mechanics Behind Horse Whinnies

2026-02-25 06:00:00 EST

Researchers have discovered that horse whinnies are produced by a complex interplay of two distinct vocal mechanisms: vocal fold vibration for the low frequency and a whistle-like sound generated above the larynx for the high frequency. This dual-frequency vocalization, previously thought to be rare in mammals, allows horses to convey nuanced emotional information. Experiments involving cameras, excised larynges, and CT scans confirmed the anatomical basis for this unique sound production. The study suggests that the high-frequency component indicates the pleasantness or unpleasantness of an emotion, while the low-frequency component signifies its intensity. This finding deepens our understanding of animal communication and vocal evolution.
