Meteorologists Monitor Potential Interaction Between Hurricanes Humberto and Imelda

2025-10-01 13:45:54 EDT

Hurricanes Humberto and Imelda are swirling in close proximity in the Atlantic, raising the possibility of a rare meteorological event known as the Fujiwhara effect. This phenomenon occurs when two tropical storms get close enough to orbit a common center, potentially merging or altering each other's paths. While a full merger is unlikely, experts note that Humberto's presence has already influenced Imelda's track, possibly guiding it away from the U.S. mainland. The Fujiwhara effect's outcome depends on the storms' relative strengths; often, the stronger storm absorbs the weaker one, though sometimes they simply "dance" around each other before separating. Swells from both storms are creating dangerous surf conditions along the U.S. East Coast.
