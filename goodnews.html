Explaining California's Extended Vote Counting Process in Recent Elections

2026-06-04 19:38:16 EDT

California's election process frequently extends for days or weeks to finalize vote counts, a characteristic attributed to its widespread mail-in voting system and stringent security protocols. Ballots postmarked by Election Day are legally counted if received within a week, and a meticulous signature-matching process, which includes physical verification for machine-rejected ballots, significantly contributes to these delays. The state also permits "ballot curing," allowing voters to rectify issues with their submissions. This election cycle's exceptionally competitive gubernatorial and Los Angeles mayoral races are further prolonging the wait, as late-arriving mail-in ballots are anticipated to contain the deciding votes.

Global Mangrove Forests Show Unexpected Recovery After Decades of Decline

2026-06-04 19:11:15 EDT

Coastal mangrove forests, critical ecosystems for protecting communities from storms and sequestering vast amounts of carbon dioxide, are experiencing an unexpected global comeback after decades of rapid destruction. A new study reveals that since 2010, the world has seen a net gain in mangroves, primarily driven by stronger legal protections, increased public awareness sparked by disasters like the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami, and the forests' remarkable natural regeneration capacity once human interference ceases. While this positive trend has stabilized levels in Indonesia and led to growth in Myanmar, it is not uniform globally, with West and Central Africa still facing significant destruction, particularly from oil pollution.
