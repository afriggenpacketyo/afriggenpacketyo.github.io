Understanding the '609 Letter' for Challenging Credit Report Errors

2026-02-03 14:41:35 EST

A '609 letter' is a formal request to credit bureaus for verification of specific items on a credit report, referencing Section 609 of the Fair Credit Reporting Act. This strategy is used to challenge inaccurate or unverifiable negative marks, such as accounts not belonging to the individual or incorrect payment histories. If bureaus cannot provide adequate documentation within 30 days, the item must be removed. However, it is not a tool to avoid legitimate debts, as credit bureaus can typically verify accurate information. Its effectiveness varies, making it most suitable for genuine errors rather than widespread debt relief. (not investment advice)
