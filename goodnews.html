China and South Korea Resolve Steel Trade Dispute with Price-Undertaking Deal

2026-02-28 03:00:09 EST

China and South Korea have successfully resolved a trade dispute concerning hot-rolled steel coils through a "price-undertaking" deal, a mechanism previously used to ease tensions with the European Union over electric vehicles. This agreement requires Chinese exporters to raise their export prices, thereby avoiding punitive anti-dumping duties and safeguarding market access for Chinese goods. Economists like John Gong view such deals as effective solutions for China, allowing the price premium to remain with exporting companies rather than being collected as duties by importing nations. The Chinese Ministry of Commerce welcomed the "soft landing," anticipating enhanced stability in bilateral steel trade. (not investment advice)
