Wealthy Investors Utilize ETF Conversions to Defer Capital Gains Taxes

2025-08-21 17:06:58 EDT

A financial strategy known as a 351 conversion is gaining traction among wealthy investors as a way to defer capital gains taxes on highly appreciated assets. The process involves exchanging a diversified portfolio of stocks for shares in a new exchange-traded fund (ETF) before it launches, effectively postponing the tax bill until the ETF shares are sold. While this method can be highly effective, it comes with strict diversification requirements and high investment minimums, often starting at $1 million. Financial advisors note that while the strategy can seem "like magic," investors must be comfortable holding the resulting ETF for the long term. (not investment advice)

Federal Government Considers Easing Restrictions on Psilocybin from 'Magic Mushrooms'

2025-08-21 16:26:44 EDT

The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services is officially reviewing a petition to reclassify psilocybin, the psychoactive compound found in "magic mushrooms." The Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) has requested the review, which could move psilocybin from a highly restrictive Schedule I narcotic to a less restrictive Schedule II classification. This change would acknowledge its potential medical value and could pave the way for its therapeutic use in treating conditions like depression and anxiety. The move follows a years-long legal effort by medical professionals seeking to use the substance for end-of-life care.
