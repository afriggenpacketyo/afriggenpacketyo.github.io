The Aviation Pioneer Behind the French Open's Name

2026-05-26 14:08:00 EDT

The French Open tennis tournament is named after Roland Garros, an early 20th-century aviation pioneer and World War I fighter pilot, not a tennis player. Garros set altitude and distance records, including the first flight across the Mediterranean Sea, and revolutionized aerial combat by pioneering forward-firing gun mechanisms. He was considered a national hero in France. In 1928, a decade after his death in action, the new Paris tennis stadium was named in his honor, a decision made by his former business school classmate and president of the Stade Français rugby club. His legacy of perseverance is seen as a fitting parallel to tennis champions.
