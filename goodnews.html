Launched in 2012, the probe exceeded its original two-year mission, operating for nearly seven years to gather data on Earth's radiation belts. Most of the spacecraft was expected to burn up during re-entry, with a low one in 4,200 chance of harm from any surviving components. The mission studied the Van Allen belts, which shield Earth from cosmic radiation and solar storms. The probes' extended operation provided unprecedented insights into these critical protective layers of our planet.
