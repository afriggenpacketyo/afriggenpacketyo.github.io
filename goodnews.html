2025-07-24 05:00:00 EDT

Researchers compared the daily calorie expenditure of people from diverse cultures, including active hunter-gatherers and sedentary office workers, and found that total energy burn was remarkably similar across all groups when adjusted for body size. This suggests that our bodies compensate for activity levels to maintain a stable energy output. The findings provide strong evidence that the dramatic shift in our food supply, particularly the rise of ultra-processed foods, is the primary driver of the obesity epidemic.
