Astronomers Observe Comet 41P Reversing Its Spin Direction, a First for Science

2026-03-27 10:38:09 EDT

Astronomers have, for the first time, observed a comet, 41P/Tuttle-Giacobini-Kresák, reversing its spin direction, a unique event discovered through archival data. During its 2017 solar passage, the comet's rotation significantly slowed, and later analysis confirmed it began spinning in the opposite direction. Scientists explain this phenomenon by the "jets of gas" emitted as solar heat melts frozen ice on the comet's small nucleus, effectively acting as thrusters that counter its original motion. This unprecedented observation, likened to "pushing a merry-go-round," suggests the comet's surface is evolving rapidly, potentially leading to its eventual instability, fragmentation, or disintegration over a much shorter timescale than typical cometary evolution.
