2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

The key ingredients include a powerful underwater earthquake, typically magnitude 6.5 or higher, that causes a vertical shift in the seafloor. The depth of the quake and its proximity to the surface are also critical factors. While forecasting remains challenging, modern deep-ocean sensors are improving prediction accuracy. Scientists note that tsunamis travel at jet-plane speeds in the deep ocean but slow down and grow in height as they approach shallower coastal waters, which is when they become most dangerous.
