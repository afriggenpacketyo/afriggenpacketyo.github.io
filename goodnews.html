Understanding the Legal Limits on Wage Garnishment for Credit Card Debt

2025-10-17 11:20:16 EDT

For individuals with delinquent credit card debt, wage garnishment is a serious consequence where a creditor, after winning a court judgment, can legally take a portion of one's paycheck. Federal law limits this garnishment to the lesser of 25% of disposable income or the amount by which weekly earnings exceed 30 times the federal minimum wage. However, many states offer stricter protections, with some, like Texas and Pennsylvania, largely prohibiting wage garnishment for unsecured debts. To avoid this outcome, consumers can negotiate directly with creditors, work with debt relief companies, enter a debt management plan, or consider bankruptcy as a final resort.
