The Surprising Medieval English Origins of Modern Football

2026-01-28 05:00:00 EST

The origins of the word 'football' and the modern game, played in events like the Super Bowl, can be traced back to medieval England. The term 'football' initially referred to any game played on foot with a ball, often involving kicking. These early versions were unregulated, violent street games with large numbers of players, more akin to modern soccer. The formalization of distinct rules for football and rugby in England in the late 19th century eventually led to the development of American football. The name 'football' persisted for the American version despite the minimal use of feet, as its creators felt they were still playing a form of football.
