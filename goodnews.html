NASA Deactivates Voyager 1 Instrument to Prolong Historic Interstellar Mission

2026-04-19 01:40:28 EDT

NASA has deactivated one of Voyager 1's science instruments, the Low-energy Charged Particles (LECP) experiment, to conserve power and extend the life of the historic probe. Launched in 1977, Voyager 1 is the most distant human-made object, operating for nearly 49 years on diminishing plutonium-generated electricity. This strategic shutdown, following a similar move for Voyager 2, aims to buy another year for the mission, which continues to send data from interstellar space 15 billion miles from Earth. Engineers are also planning a "Big Bang" power conservation strategy, hoping to keep at least one instrument operational into the 2030s.
