Tailgating at Football Matches Becomes Criminal Offence in England and Wales

2026-03-19 20:01:16 EDT

Entering a football match in England and Wales without a ticket, known as 'tailgating,' will become a criminal offense under new laws effective before the Carabao Cup final. Offenders face up to a five-year football banning order and a £1,000 fine. This legislation directly addresses the severe disorder at the Euro 2020 final at Wembley in 2021, where thousands illegally forced entry, an event a report warned could have led to deaths. The new act also criminalizes using forged tickets or posing as staff. Policing minister Sarah Jones emphasized providing police with tools to prevent future chaos and ensure fan safety.
