2025-10-22 02:26:48 EDT

A groundbreaking study by King's College London and the University of Oxford has, for the first time, ranked the physical side effects of various antidepressants, highlighting significant differences between medications. Analyzing 151 studies across 30 drugs and over 58,500 patients, the research revealed impacts such as weight changes of up to 2kg and heart rate variations of 21 beats per minute within the first eight weeks of treatment. This information is crucial for doctors to personalize antidepressant prescriptions, matching drugs to individual patient needs and health conditions. The findings, published in The Lancet, aim to empower patients in shared decision-making and improve treatment adherence, with researchers developing a free online tool to aid selection.
