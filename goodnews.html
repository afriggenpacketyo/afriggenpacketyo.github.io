UK Watchdog Calls for Mandatory Vet Price Publication Amid Rising Costs

2025-10-15 03:38:54 EDT

The UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has proposed that veterinary practices be mandated to publish their price lists, empowering pet owners with greater transparency to compare costs before treatment. This recommendation stems from an investigation revealing that vet prices have surged by 63% over seven years, nearly double the inflation rate, often without owners receiving upfront estimates for potentially thousands of pounds in treatments. The CMA's provisional findings suggest reforms such as requiring vets to disclose if they are part of a large group, capping prescription fees, and prohibiting bonuses for specific treatments. These measures aim to tackle a significant lack of consumer awareness and address the escalating costs within the £6.3 billion veterinary sector, with a final decision expected next year.
