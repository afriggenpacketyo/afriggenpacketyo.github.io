Explainer: How Long Homeowners Can Remain in Their Homes with a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-13 13:06:01 EDT

A reverse mortgage allows homeowners aged 62 and older to convert home equity into cash and remain in their home for as long as they meet specific obligations. There is no predetermined time limit, but the loan becomes due if the borrower moves out, sells the property, passes away, or fails to pay property taxes and homeowners insurance. For married couples, a surviving co-borrower can continue living in the home without the loan becoming due. Once a maturity event occurs, the borrower's heirs typically have six months, with possible extensions, to repay the loan, often by selling the property.
