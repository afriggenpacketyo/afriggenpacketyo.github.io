New Research Links Electric Vehicle Characteristics to Increased Motion Sickness

2025-06-29 22:00:13 EDT

As electric vehicles (EVs) become more common, studies are revealing they are more likely to induce motion sickness than traditional combustion engine cars. Researchers attribute this to the unique acceleration and deceleration patterns of EVs, particularly the regenerative braking systems. This feature, which converts kinetic energy back into electrical energy to charge the battery, can cause low-frequency deceleration that provokes queasiness in passengers. According to Chinese researchers, this issue affects not only passenger comfort but also their health, as it can cause feelings of tension and fidgetiness during travel.
