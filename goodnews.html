Colorado Pioneers Solution to Economic Challenges in Electric Vehicle Battery Recycling

2026-07-13 05:00:00 EDT

The electric vehicle (EV) battery recycling industry is grappling with significant economic hurdles, as many salvage yards find it unprofitable or even costly to dispose of old batteries, especially those made with cheaper materials like iron phosphate. Recyclers frequently impose "gate fees" to accept these less valuable batteries, transforming them into a financial liability rather than a valuable asset for scrapyard operators. In response, Colorado has introduced a groundbreaking "producer responsibility" law, mandating that EV manufacturers cover the costs associated with recycling unwanted batteries. This innovative legislation aims to mitigate hazardous waste, facilitate the recovery of critical minerals, and ensure responsible end-of-life management for EV batteries, setting a potential precedent for other states.

Airlines will also be mandated to correct minor name misspellings on tickets free of charge and provide printed boarding passes to checked-in passengers at no additional cost.
