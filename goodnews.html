2025-11-27 12:01:32 EST

A U.S. archaeologist may have solved the longstanding mystery of the "Band of Holes," a series of over 5,200 pits in Peru's Pisco Valley. Using drone mapping and microbotanical analysis, researchers concluded the holes were a sophisticated accounting and storage system for agricultural goods, created during the pre-Inca period and adapted by the Inca civilization. The organized, segmented layout of the holes mirrors the structure of khipus, the knotted-string devices used by the Inca for record-keeping. Fossilized seeds of crops and plant materials used for packaging found in the pits further support the theory, debunking more fanciful explanations.
