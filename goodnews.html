Built in 1961 to prevent East Germans from fleeing to the West, the wall stood for 28 years before its fall in 1989. Post-collapse, pieces were eagerly acquired by individuals, governments, and institutions, particularly in the U.S., where they often serve as memorials or pleas for unification. While some segments are preserved in their original gray, many are now painted, reflecting diverse interpretations and evolving into pop culture artifacts, though their symbolic impact is gradually receding.
