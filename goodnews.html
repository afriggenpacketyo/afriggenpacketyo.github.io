2026-05-21 11:29:19 EDT

This article explains that while a bank levy allows creditors to freeze and seize funds from a bank account to satisfy unpaid debt, it doesn't automatically mean they can take everything. The amount seized depends on state laws and federal protections, which shield certain funds like Social Security, Supplemental Security Income, or veterans' benefits, requiring banks to protect two months' worth of these payments. Creditors typically need a court judgment, with exceptions for federal student loan servicers and the IRS. The article advises that borrowers facing a levy should explore debt relief options like settlement or bankruptcy to protect assets and potentially eliminate debt, as acting quickly is crucial to interrupt the collection process.
