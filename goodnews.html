Scientists Uncover Why Type 1 Diabetes is More Severe in Young Children

2025-11-12 21:48:00 EST

Scientists have identified why type 1 diabetes manifests more aggressively in young children, particularly those under seven years old. Research, published in Science Advances, reveals that the pancreas's beta cells, responsible for insulin production, are still developing in early childhood, making them highly vulnerable to immune system attacks. In younger children, these cells exist in small, easily destroyed clusters, whereas in older individuals, they mature into more durable islets. This discovery offers hope for new immunotherapy drugs like teplizumab, which could delay the disease's onset by allowing the pancreas more time to mature, potentially altering the prognosis for children diagnosed with type 1 diabetes.
