Ancient Mahogany Canoe Discovered in Florida Following Hurricane Ian

2025-09-28 07:00:11 EDT

An ancient 9-foot wooden canoe, unearthed during the cleanup from Hurricane Ian in 2022, has been carefully preserved by Florida state archaeologists. The discovery is notable as it is the first of its kind found in the state made of mahogany, suggesting it may have originated outside of Florida, possibly in the Caribbean. While radiocarbon dating has so far been inconclusive, tool marks indicate it was crafted with iron tools, placing it in the historical period after European contact. This find adds to Florida's extensive collection of over 450 recorded ancient canoes, reinforcing the region's historical significance as a hub for water-based transportation.
