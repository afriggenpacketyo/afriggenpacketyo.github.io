The Evolution of 'Gossip': From Sacred Origins to Sinful Reputation

2026-07-16 05:00:00 EDT

The word "gossip" has a surprising etymological history, evolving from the Old English "godsibb," meaning "sponsor or godparent," to its current, often negative connotation. Initially, it referred to a close friend or neighbor, especially women attending a birth, before acquiring a derogatory, gendered meaning around the 16th century, linked to women spreading rumors. Feminist scholars argue this transformation reflects broader societal shifts and the devaluation of women's social interactions and knowledge in an increasingly misogynistic era. Some advocates now seek to reclaim "good gossip" as a valuable form of female communication and connection.
