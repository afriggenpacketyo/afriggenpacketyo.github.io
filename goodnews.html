Understanding Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) for Retirement Accounts

2026-05-07 10:50:10 EDT

Reaching $1 million in retirement savings triggers mandatory annual withdrawals known as Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) from traditional IRAs and 401(k)s, typically starting at age 73. The withdrawal amount is calculated based on the account balance and the IRS's life expectancy tables, increasing with age. These distributions are taxed as ordinary income, potentially raising tax brackets, affecting Social Security benefits, and increasing Medicare premiums. Missing an RMD can result in a penalty of up to 25% of the amount that should have been withdrawn, emphasizing the importance of adherence to these rules.
