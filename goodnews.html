Scientists Transplant Crossbred Corals to Help Florida Reefs Resist Climate Change

2025-07-04 12:54:01 EDT

In an effort to save Florida's reefs from rising ocean temperatures, scientists are transplanting a new generation of crossbred corals onto a reef near Miami. The project, a collaboration between the University of Miami and other institutions, involves breeding native Florida Elkhorn corals with corals from a warmer reef in Honduras. The goal is to create more 'thermally tolerant' offspring that can better survive bleaching events, which have devastated Florida's coral populations. This is the first time internationally crossbred corals have been permitted for planting in the wild, offering a potential blueprint for reef restoration across the Caribbean.
