Understanding Deadlines for Debt Collection from Estates

2026-06-26 12:51:34 EDT

When a loved one passes away, settling their financial affairs involves a probate process where creditors can pursue outstanding debts from the deceased's estate. Debt collectors face strict, state-specific deadlines, known as creditor claim periods, which dictate how long they have to file a claim; these windows can range from a few months to over a year. Executors must diligently adhere to these deadlines, as failure to do so can result in creditors losing their right to collect from the estate's assets. While secured debts typically remain tied to their collateral, surviving relatives are generally not liable for the deceased's debts if the estate proves insolvent.
