UK Government to Introduce 'Hillsborough Law' to Enforce Public Official Accountability

2025-09-15 17:30:22 EDT

The UK government is set to introduce the long-awaited "Hillsborough Law," officially named the Public Office (Accountability) Bill, which will legally require public officials to be truthful during investigations into major disasters. This landmark legislation, championed by the families of the 97 victims of the 1989 Hillsborough stadium disaster, establishes a "duty of candour" and threatens criminal sanctions for non-compliance. Prime Minister Keir Starmer stated the law aims to end the "culture of cover-ups" seen not only in the Hillsborough case but also in other national scandals like the Grenfell Tower fire and the Post Office Horizon crisis.

Explaining the Decades-Long Fight for Justice Leading to the Hillsborough Law

2025-09-15 17:30:17 EDT

The introduction of the Hillsborough Law in the UK Parliament marks a pivotal moment in a 36-year struggle for justice by the families of the 97 football fans who died in the 1989 stadium disaster. This new legislation was born from the injustices exposed during the protracted legal battles, where public authorities were not legally obligated to cooperate transparently, leading to cover-ups and defensive narratives. The law establishes a legal "duty of candour" for public officials and aims to create parity in legal funding for victims of state-related disasters, ensuring others do not face the same institutional obstacles.
