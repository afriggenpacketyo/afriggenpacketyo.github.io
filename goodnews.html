Research Reveals Tiny Zooplankton Play a Major Role in Storing Ocean Carbon

2025-07-04 19:10:52 EDT

New research highlights the crucial role of tiny, often overlooked sea creatures called zooplankton in mitigating global warming. These animals engage in an epic annual migration in the Southern Ocean, gorging on carbon-rich phytoplankton at the surface before sinking deep into the ocean to burn their fat reserves. This process effectively locks away vast amounts of carbon, equivalent to the annual emissions of about 55 million cars, preventing it from entering the atmosphere. Scientists describe these creatures as "unsung heroes," but warn that their populations are facing growing threats just as their significant contribution to the planet's health is being fully understood.
