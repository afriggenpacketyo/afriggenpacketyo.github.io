Scientists Crossbreed Honduran and Floridian Coral to Restore Reefs

2025-07-15 20:58:18 EDT

Marine scientists are undertaking a novel conservation effort by crossbreeding tough Elkhorn coral from Honduras with Florida's struggling native populations to restore the state's damaged reefs. This initiative, which created a hybrid nicknamed the "Flonduran," is the first time an international cross of corals has been permitted for planting on wild reefs. Over 95% of Florida's Elkhorn coral has been lost due to warming oceans and disease, threatening coastal protection from storm surges. The hope is that the hardier hybrid corals will survive future marine heatwaves and help rebuild the vital reef ecosystem.
