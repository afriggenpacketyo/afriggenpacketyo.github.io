Moore's Law Continues to Drive Down Computer Prices Amidst Widespread Inflation

2025-12-04 05:00:00 EST

Despite persistent inflation across most sectors, the cost of computers and other electronics has steadily decreased, a trend largely attributed to Moore's Law. This principle, observed by Intel co-founder Gordon Moore, posits that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles approximately every two years, making computing power exponentially cheaper and more powerful over time. For example, an entry-level MacBook Pro is now $200 cheaper than its comparable model five years ago, despite having superior features. However, experts at MIT suggest this decades-long trend may be nearing its physical limits, as transistors become so small that heat and power consumption pose significant challenges, signaling a potential end to predictable price drops.
