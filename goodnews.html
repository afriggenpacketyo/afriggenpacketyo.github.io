Unlike spouses, who inherit HSAs tax-free, other beneficiaries face the entire account balance becoming taxable income in the year of death, potentially pushing them into the highest marginal tax bracket, currently 37%. Financial planners strongly advise account holders to consider spending down large HSAs if no spouse is designated, donating them to charity, or strategically spreading the inheritance among multiple individuals to dilute the severe tax hit.
