First Dinosaur Bone from Antarctica Identified After Decades in Storage

2026-06-29 01:10:46 EDT

A fossil that lay forgotten in a drawer for 40 years has been identified as the first dinosaur bone ever found in Antarctica. Unearthed in 1985, the specimen was initially misidentified and stored in the British Antarctic Survey's collection, believed to be from a marine reptile. Palaeontologists have now confirmed it is a tail bone from a Titanosaur, a group containing the largest dinosaurs to ever walk the Earth. This significant discovery sheds new light on how these ancient creatures lived in a region now considered uninhabitable, greatly contributing to understanding prehistoric ecosystems at the bottom of the world.
