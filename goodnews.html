Daily Peanut Consumption May Improve Brain Blood Flow and Memory in Older Adults, Study Finds

2025-12-03 10:07:13 EST

A new study from Maastricht University suggests that eating a daily serving of peanuts could enhance cognitive function in older adults. Researchers found that participants aged 60 to 75 who consumed 60 grams of unsalted, skin-roasted peanuts daily for 16 weeks showed a 3.6% increase in overall cerebral blood flow. This improvement was particularly noted in brain regions responsible for memory and language, and participants also demonstrated a 5.8% improvement in verbal memory tests. The study's authors attribute these benefits to nutrients like L-arginine, unsaturated fats, and polyphenols found in peanuts.
