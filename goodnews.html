Deep-Sea Whale Graveyard in Indian Ocean Reveals 5 Million Years of Whale Evolution

2026-06-11 21:00:15 EDT

Researchers have discovered the Earth's deepest and most extensive known accumulation of whale fossils and carcasses in the Indian Ocean, dating back about 5.3 million years. Detailed in the peer-reviewed journal Nature, this "whale fall" phenomenon creates unique deep-sea ecosystems as carcasses drift and then sink to the ocean floor, where they are consumed by scavengers. The findings, a collaboration between Chinese, Italian, and New Zealand researchers, significantly reshape the understanding of whale-fall ecosystems and provide a rich fossil archive for tracing cetacean evolution over geological time.
