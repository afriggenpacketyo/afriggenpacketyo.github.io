TSMC Reports Significant Profit Increase Driven by Robust AI Chip Demand

2026-04-16 08:23:25 EDT

UK Financial Regulator Eases Short Selling Rules for Hedge Funds

2026-04-16 08:18:22 EDT

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) has relaxed rules for hedge funds by easing restrictions on short selling and reducing administrative burdens. This overhaul aims to streamline regulations for investors who bet against company values, aligning with the UK's broader push to cut red tape. The new framework, effective July 13, will publish aggregated data on net short positions rather than identifying individual short sellers, mitigating reputational and strategic risks for hedge funds. This anonymized approach is expected to allow hedge funds to operate more freely without fear of market backlash or short squeezes, while also easing compliance costs for the industry, particularly for smaller firms.

Northern Ireland Minister Proposes £100 Payment for Low-Income Households to Offset Heating Oil Costs

2026-04-16 08:10:37 EDT

Communities Minister Gordon Lyons is proposing a £100 payment for low-income households in Northern Ireland to help with rising heating oil bills. This proposal, aimed at individuals with an annual income of approximately £30,000 or less, would benefit around 340,000 households. The plan is part of a broader discussion among Stormont ministers to address the pressures faced by farmers, businesses, and households due to escalating fuel costs. The UK government has pledged £17 million for mitigation, and Lyons seeks to match this amount from the executive to provide more substantial support. The scheme, if approved, would take several months to implement.
