The Cultural Divide: Why Easter Lags Behind Christmas as a Secular Holiday

2026-04-05 06:00:00 EDT

Historically, both holidays faced Puritan objections due to their association with revelry and perceived paganism. However, Christmas was notably reinvented in the 19th century through popular literature, transforming into a bourgeois, child-centered family holiday. Easter, lacking such a cultural "PR machine," maintained its theological focus. Its central message of death and resurrection is inherently more challenging to secularize than the birth narrative of Christmas, contributing to its distinct, less commercialized cultural footprint today.
