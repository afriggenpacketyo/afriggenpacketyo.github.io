Underground fiber-optic cables offer potential for city-wide sensing and privacy-preserving data collection

2026-04-16 06:00:00 EDT

Thousands of miles of fiber-optic cables beneath Los Angeles and other cities hold untapped potential for "opportunistic sensing," transforming existing infrastructure into a city-scale sensor network. By analyzing vibrations transmitted through the cables, cities can gain insights into traffic patterns, infrastructure health, earthquake risks, and environmental conditions without intrusive surveillance. This technology offers a privacy-preserving method of data collection, as it tracks physical disturbances rather than identifying individuals. While requiring cooperation from telecom providers and political will, repurposing fiber-optic networks could provide valuable urban data at a modest cost, enabling better governance and risk mitigation.
