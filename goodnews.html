Income Limits for Bankruptcy Filings Are More Complex Than Commonly Believed

Contrary to popular belief, there is no universal maximum income that automatically disqualifies someone from filing for bankruptcy. Income evaluation varies significantly between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy filings. Chapter 7 requires passing a 'means test' comparing household income to state median, but also considers allowable expenses, allowing higher earners to qualify if disposable income is low. Chapter 13, designed for debt restructuring over three to five years, has no income ceiling and often requires a steady income. Courts consider factors like household size and cost-of-living, meaning income affects the type of bankruptcy, not necessarily the eligibility itself. (not investment advice)
