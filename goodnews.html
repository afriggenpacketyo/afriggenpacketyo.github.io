The History and Significance of Juneteenth

2025-06-19 09:47:00 EDT

Juneteenth, now a federal holiday in the U.S., commemorates the end of slavery, specifically marking June 19, 1865, when the last enslaved people in Texas were informed of their freedom under the Emancipation Proclamation. Despite the Emancipation Proclamation being signed in 1863, its enforcement in Confederate states, including Texas, was delayed until the arrival of Union soldiers. The name Juneteenth combines June and nineteenth, symbolizing the date when freedom finally reached those enslaved in Texas. Celebrations often include parades, parties, concerts, and educational workshops, reflecting the joy of emancipation and the remembrance of the country's history of slavery.
