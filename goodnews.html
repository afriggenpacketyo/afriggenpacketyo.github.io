2026-05-01 03:56:32 EDT

Two rare first-edition volumes of John James Audubon's "Birds of America," one of the world's most valuable books, are now on public display in Glasgow. Owned by the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Glasgow for nearly two centuries, these meticulously hand-coloured volumes feature life-size images of over 200 North American bird species. The exhibition offers a rare opportunity to view this ornithological masterpiece, with each massive page requiring two people to turn. The article also acknowledges the historical context, noting the contribution of enslaved and indigenous people to the book's creation, and its origins in Audubon's 1826 promotional tour in the UK.
