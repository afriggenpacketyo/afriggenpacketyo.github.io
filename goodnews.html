Study Reveals Human Influence Diverged Animal Size Evolution in Middle Ages

2025-09-01 13:05:00 EDT

Research from the University of Montpellier indicates that human activities caused a significant divergence in the body size evolution of wild and domesticated animals starting in the Middle Ages. By analyzing over 225,000 animal bones from the past 8,000 years in France, scientists found that wild species like foxes and deer began to shrink. This was attributed to habitat fragmentation and intensified hunting. Concurrently, domesticated species such as sheep, cattle, and chickens grew larger due to increasingly systematic selective breeding and controlled management, showcasing the profound and lasting impact of human civilization on the natural world.
