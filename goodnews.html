Exploring the Historical Origins of Why "Football" is Called "Soccer" in the U.S.

2026-06-13 11:33:54 EDT

This article delves into the historical reasons why the sport known globally as "football" is commonly referred to as "soccer" in the United States and Canada. The term "soccer" originated in England during the late 19th century, derived from "association football" by Oxford university students who would shorten words and add "-er." As American football developed from rugby, it became necessary to distinguish the two sports. Despite "soccer" being widely used in Britain until the 1980s, the term has since become a point of cultural difference, with Americans sometimes apologizing for its use, assuming British sensitivity.
