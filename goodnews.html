Explaining the Reverse Mortgage '95% Rule' and Its Importance for Heirs

2025-07-31 14:40:57 EDT

A key consumer protection for federally-backed reverse mortgages, known as the "95% rule," offers a significant benefit to the heirs of a homeowner. This rule stipulates that if heirs wish to keep the property after the borrower passes away or moves out, they only have to repay either the full loan balance or 95% of the home's current appraised value, whichever amount is less. This ensures that family members will not be forced to pay more than the home is worth, even if the loan balance has grown larger than the property's value over time.
