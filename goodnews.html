A Guide to the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale Categories

2025-08-18 08:30:01 EDT

As Hurricane Erin churns in the Atlantic, understanding the system used to classify such storms is crucial for public awareness and safety. Hurricanes are rated on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale, which consists of five categories based on sustained wind speeds, providing an estimate of potential property damage. A Category 1 storm features winds of 74-95 mph, while a Category 5, the most severe, has winds of 157 mph or higher, capable of causing catastrophic destruction. Storms rated as Category 3 or higher are designated "major hurricanes" due to their potential for significant loss of life and damage.
