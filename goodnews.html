Scientists Discover Earliest-Known Dog Remains, Revealing Deeper Ancient Human-Canine Bond

2026-03-25 15:13:00 EDT

Scientists have identified the earliest-known dog remains, a female puppy dating back 15,800 years, found in present-day Turkey, predating previous discoveries by nearly 5,000 years. DNA analysis suggests the pup resembled a small wolf, offering new insights into the domestication of dogs, believed to have diverged from gray wolves at least 24,000 years ago. Evidence at the Pinarbasi rock shelter site includes human and dog burials, alongside indications that ancient hunter-gatherers fed fish to their canine companions.
