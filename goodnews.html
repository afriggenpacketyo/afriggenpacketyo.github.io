Swiss Voters Decisively Reject Proposals to Cut Public Broadcasting Fees

2026-03-08 14:00:00 EDT

Swiss voters overwhelmingly rejected a proposition to significantly cut mandatory annual household fees for public broadcasting, with 62% voting against the measure in a national referendum. The proposal, largely backed by right-wing parties, aimed to reduce fees from 335 to 200 Swiss francs and encourage partial privatization by selling airtime for sports and entertainment. Opponents, including the Swiss Broadcasting Corporation (SRG) and over a thousand university professors, argued that strong public broadcast services are crucial for fostering democracy and social cohesion, particularly in combating disinformation. The SRG, which broadcasts in four national languages, had already announced cost-cutting measures and job reductions independent of the referendum's outcome, aiming to save 17% of its budget by 2029.
