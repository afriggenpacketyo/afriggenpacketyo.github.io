Daylight Saving Time 2026 Begins, Shifting Clocks Forward an Hour

2026-03-07 23:00:15 EST

Daylight saving time for 2026 is set to begin on Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, requiring most Americans to "spring forward" and consequently lose an hour of sleep. This annual practice, which has been observed on the second Sunday of March since 2007, effectively shifts an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening. Clocks will eventually "fall back" to standard time on the first Sunday of November. While initially adopted during World Wars to conserve fuel, studies have indicated that daylight saving offers minimal energy conservation benefits and has been linked to various negative health impacts. Notably, Hawaii, Arizona (with the exception of the Navajo Nation), and several U.S. territories do not observe this time change.
