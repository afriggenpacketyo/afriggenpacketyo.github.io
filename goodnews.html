British Columbia to Permanently Adopt Year-Round Daylight Saving Time

2026-03-02 18:36:36 EST

Canada's British Columbia is set to eliminate seasonal clock changes, transitioning to year-round daylight saving time after March 8, 2026. Premier David Eby announced this decision, citing strong public support from constituents who wish to reduce disruptions and benefit from an extra hour of evening light during winter months. A 2019 public engagement report in the province revealed that 93% of participants supported year-round daylight saving time, primarily due to health and wellness concerns. This move aligns British Columbia with many countries globally that have abolished the practice, and the province expresses hope that neighboring U.S. states will soon follow suit.
