This article explores the necessity of electrolyte drinks for hydration, concluding that most people do not need them. While electrolytes like salt and potassium are indeed lost in sweat, medical experts and sports scientists assert that regular water intake and a balanced diet, including fruits and vegetables, are generally sufficient for most individuals. The human body is adept at regulating its electrolyte levels, and the typical Western diet often provides more than enough sodium. Electrolyte supplements are primarily beneficial for elite athletes engaging in prolonged, heavy sweating or individuals experiencing rapid fluid loss due to severe illness. For the general public, plain water remains the most effective hydration choice.
