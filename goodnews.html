Chinese Scientists First to Observe Quantum Friction Effect in Graphene

2025-07-24 22:00:15 EDT

A research team in China has become the first to directly observe and measure quantum friction between solid surfaces. The scientists at the Lanzhou Institute of Chemical Physics were studying folded layers of graphene when they discovered that friction did not always increase with thickness; in some cases, thicker folds slid more easily. This counterintuitive effect was traced to how bending the material altered electron movement, locking them into fixed energy levels and reducing the conversion of motion into heat. The findings, published in Nature Communications, could aid in designing ultra-efficient, low-wear nanoscale devices and machines.
