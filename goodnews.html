Chinese Scientists Make First Direct Observation of Migdal Effect, Key to Dark Matter Detection

2026-01-16 06:00:11 EST

Chinese scientists have achieved the first direct observation of the Migdal effect, a quantum phenomenon proposed almost 90 years ago that could be crucial for detecting dark matter. Soviet physicist Arkady Migdal theorized in 1939 that a neutral particle colliding with an atomic nucleus would cause a secondary electronic recoil, generating a detectable signal. For 87 years, this remained purely theoretical until a team led by researchers from the University of the Chinese Academy of Sciences successfully observed it using cutting-edge equipment. This breakthrough, published in Nature, offers "unprecedented insights" into the universe's invisible "glue" and significantly advances dark matter detection research.
