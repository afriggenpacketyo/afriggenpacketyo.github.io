Colorado Pioneers Law to Tackle EV Battery Recycling Economic Challenges

2026-07-13 05:00:00 EDT

The electric vehicle (EV) battery recycling industry faces significant economic hurdles, as salvage yards often struggle to profit from or even responsibly dispose of old batteries, particularly those with cheaper iron phosphate chemistries. These batteries frequently require a "gate fee" for recycling due to their lower mineral value, turning them into a costly liability rather than a valuable asset. Colorado has pioneered a solution by enacting a "producer responsibility" law, which mandates EV manufacturers to cover the cost of recycling unwanted batteries. This legislation aims to prevent environmental hazards, foster a circular economy, and reduce reliance on foreign critical minerals by ensuring proper end-of-life management for these essential components.

2026-07-13 02:29:00 EDT

The Council of the European Union has formally approved a new package of reforms granting additional rights to air passengers, set to take effect in mid-2027. These rules will apply to all departures from EU airports and arrivals by EU-based airlines. Key provisions include the right for children under 14 and passengers with reduced mobility to sit next to companions free of charge, free corrections for misspelled names, and printed boarding passes without extra cost. The reforms also clarify compensation rules for flight disruptions, mandating information within 96 hours and payment or refusal within 30 days, alongside entitlements for refreshments and accommodation during delays.
