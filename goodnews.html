Six Planets Align for Rare 'Planetary Parade' Visible in the Night Sky

2026-02-27 01:54:50 EST

A captivating "planetary parade" will grace the night sky, featuring six planets—Mercury, Venus, Uranus, Jupiter, Mars, and Neptune—all visible in the same part of the sky shortly after sunset. For UK viewers, Sunday offers the prime opportunity to witness this rare celestial event. Dr. Becky Smethurst explains that while the planets aren't perfectly aligned in their orbits, they appear in a straight line from our perspective because they orbit in the same flat plane. Jupiter will be the easiest to locate, shining brightly, while Mercury and Venus will be low on the western horizon. Optimal viewing requires a clear horizon, allowing eyes to adjust to darkness, and using red light on mobile devices.
