Explainer: The Science Behind How Tsunamis Form and What to Do in an Alert

2025-07-30 20:03:06 EDT

Triggered by events like the recent 8.8-magnitude earthquake off Russia, tsunamis are powerful series of ocean waves with specific geological origins. They are typically generated by large, shallow underwater earthquakes that cause vertical movement of the seafloor, displacing massive volumes of water. While difficult to precisely forecast, scientists use tide gauges and deep-ocean sensors to track these waves, which can travel at jet-plane speeds in deep water before slowing and growing in height near the coast. Experts advise that upon receiving a warning, people should immediately move to higher ground.
