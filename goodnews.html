U.S. to Airdrop Billions of Sterilized Flies to Combat Flesh-Eating Screwworm Pest

2025-07-02 14:17:06 EDT

The U.S. government is scaling up a program to combat the New World screwworm, a flesh-eating parasite that poses a significant threat to livestock, wildlife, and pets. The plan involves breeding billions of male screwworm flies, sterilizing them with radiation, and releasing them from airplanes over Mexico and southern Texas. These sterile males then mate with wild females, whose eggs subsequently fail to hatch, causing the pest population to collapse over time. This sterile insect technique has been successfully used to eradicate the pest in the past and is considered more effective and environmentally friendly than widespread pesticide use.
