Archaeologists Discover 8,500-Year-Old Stone Age Settlement Submerged Off Denmark's Coast

2025-08-26 09:31:11 EDT

Archaeologists have discovered a well-preserved Stone Age settlement submerged beneath the Bay of Aarhus in northern Denmark, dating back over 8,500 years. This finding is part of a six-year, $15.5 million international project to map lost Mesolithic settlements in the Baltic and North Seas. Divers recovered animal bones, stone tools, arrowheads, and worked wood, preserved in an oxygen-free environment. Researchers are using dendrochronology to precisely date submerged tree stumps, shedding light on how ancient societies adapted to rapid sea-level rise, a phenomenon relevant to today's climate change challenges.
