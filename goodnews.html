Archaeologists Unearth Unusually Large 2,000-Year-Old Roman Shoes in Northern England

2025-07-04 08:08:19 EDT

Archaeologists have discovered a collection of 'unusually large' 2,000-year-old shoes at the Magna Roman Fort, part of the Hadrian's Wall World Heritage Site. Eight of the excavated shoes are over 30cm long, with one reaching nearly 13 inches, equivalent to a modern U.S. size 15. The finds are puzzling researchers, as such large sizes are rare at other Roman sites but appear to be common at Magna. The shoes were preserved in a low-oxygen defensive ditch, and archaeologists are now investigating which Roman regiments were stationed at the fort to understand who might have worn them.
