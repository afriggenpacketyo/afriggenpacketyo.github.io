Meteorologists Explain Why No Hurricanes Made U.S. Landfall in 2025

2025-11-25 11:17:37 EST

For the first time in a decade, the Atlantic hurricane season is concluding without a single hurricane making landfall in the continental United States. Meteorologists attribute this unusual outcome to a persistent atmospheric pattern known as "recurvature." A recurring dip in the jet stream over the eastern U.S. created an area of low pressure that consistently steered developing storms northward and back out to sea before they could reach the coast. This pattern weakened the western edge of the Bermuda High, the primary force that typically pushes hurricanes toward Florida and the Gulf of Mexico, resulting in a quiet season for the U.S. mainland.
