The Sign Language Bill (Northern Ireland) 2025 mandates the Department for Communities to offer these classes to deaf individuals under 25 and their close connections. Campaigners have welcomed the legislation, viewing it as a significant step towards equality and respect for the deaf community's language and culture. The bill also grants official and equal recognition to British Sign Language (BSL) and Irish Sign Language (ISL) in Northern Ireland. Public bodies will be legally required to ensure their services are accessible to the deaf community. This development is anticipated to cost approximately £3 million annually and aims to improve communication access and opportunities for thousands of sign language users in the region.
