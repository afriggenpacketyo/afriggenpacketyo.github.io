A new study by the National Bureau of Economic Research indicates that China's university boom has inadvertently expanded U.S. postgraduate education and local economies, particularly by creating more university spots for American students. The research challenges claims that Chinese students "crowd out" their American peers, finding instead a "crowding in" effect. For every 100 Chinese master's students, U.S. universities added approximately one new STEM master's program, and each additional Chinese student was associated with an increase of about 0.26 American master's students, primarily in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics fields. This suggests a positive, rather than competitive, dynamic in higher education.
