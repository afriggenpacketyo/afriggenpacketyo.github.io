France moves to symbolically repeal slavery legislation

2026-05-28 12:02:00 EDT

French lawmakers have voted to formally repeal slavery-era laws that defined enslaved people as "movable property," a symbolic move given that slavery was outlawed over 170 years ago. The legislation targets the "Code noir," a series of royal edicts from the late 17th and early 18th centuries that governed slavery in French colonies. While superseded by abolition, these texts were never formally abrogated. The bill, which passed unanimously in the lower house, also requires the government to report on the lasting effects of slavery and its teaching in schools. The move comes as France continues to confront its colonial legacy and the historical impact of the slave trade.
