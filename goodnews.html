Germany Replaces Unpopular 'Riester' Pension System with New State-Backed Model

2026-03-27 14:36:00 EDT

Germany's parliament has voted to replace the underperforming 'Riester' pension system, introduced in 2002, with a new state-backed savings model. The previous system was criticized for its low returns, complexity, and high costs, particularly for lower-income earners. The reform aims to simplify and reduce the cost of private retirement savings, making them more accessible and potentially profitable. Finance Minister Lars Klingbeil hailed the reform as a 'real milestone' that strengthens the third pillar of pensions and expands eligibility to self-employed workers. State subsidies are also set to be simplified and increased, especially for families, with plans for a 'starter pension' for children and young people.
