Researchers have successfully sequenced the first whole ancient Egyptian genome, revealing details about the life and ancestry of a potter who lived between 4,500 and 4,800 years ago. The man's well-preserved remains, found buried in a ceramic pot, allowed for the oldest DNA analysis from Egypt to date. The study determined he had brown hair and eyes, lived into his 60s, and had a physically demanding life consistent with pottery work.
