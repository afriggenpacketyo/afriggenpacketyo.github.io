Colorado Rabbits Sprout 'Horns' Due to Common Virus, Scientists Reassure Public

2025-08-14 06:16:52 EDT

Rabbits with grotesque, horn-like growths spotted in Fort Collins, Colorado, are not a cause for panic, according to wildlife officials. The animals are infected with the Shope papillomavirus, a relatively common and mostly harmless virus that causes wart-like tumors on their faces. The virus, which is not transmissible to humans or other species, is believed to have inspired the myth of the jackalope. While the growths can interfere with a rabbit's ability to eat if they become too large, most wild rabbits' immune systems can fight off the virus, causing the growths to disappear over time.
