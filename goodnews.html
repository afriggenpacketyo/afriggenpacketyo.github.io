Understanding the 90-Day Preference Rule in Chapter 7 Bankruptcy

2026-01-20 11:54:10 EST

The "90-day rule" in Chapter 7 bankruptcy refers to a preference period where a trustee can review and potentially reverse payments of $600 or more made to creditors within 90 days before filing. This rule ensures fair treatment among all creditors, meaning payments to a favored creditor could be clawed back and redistributed. The period extends to one year for "insiders" like family members. Borrowers must understand this to avoid complications, as strategically paying off debts before filing can backfire. Consulting a debt expert is crucial to navigate these rules and explore all available debt relief options. (not investment advice)
