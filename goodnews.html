Balendra Shah's Party Secures Landslide Victory in Nepal's General Election

2026-03-13 00:10:33 EDT

The Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP), led by rapper and former Kathmandu mayor Balendra Shah, achieved a clear majority in Nepal's general election, winning 182 out of 275 parliamentary seats. This victory marks the first time in decades that a single party has secured an outright majority in the country's two-system format. The snap election was held six months after deadly youth protests in September 2025 toppled the previous government. Shah, now the RSP's prime ministerial candidate, would become Nepal's youngest prime minister if confirmed, having defeated former Prime Minister Sharma KP Oli. The election's main issues included better governance, fighting corruption, and reducing unemployment, reflecting public frustrations.

China Achieves Mass Production of Advanced T1200 Carbon Fiber, Outpacing Rivals

2026-03-13 00:00:12 EDT

China's state-owned China National Building Material Group (CNBM) has announced the mass production of T1200-grade carbon fiber, claiming it to be the world's first factory to achieve this at a hundred-tonne level annually. This ultra-strength fiber, with a tensile strength ten times greater than ordinary steel, is less than one-tenth the diameter of a human hair. This development positions China ahead of established market leaders like Japan's Toray Industries, which had previously developed T1200-grade carbon fiber but had not revealed production capacity. CNBM chairman Zhou Yuxian hailed carbon fiber as "black gold," crucial for industrial and supply chain security and serving economic and social development.

NASA is preparing for a possible April 1 launch of its Artemis II moon mission, which will send four astronauts on a nine-day trip around the moon. The mission, a crucial test flight, has faced delays due to hydrogen fuel leaks and issues with the rocket's upper stage propellant pressurization system, both of which have now been fixed. Commander Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Canadian astronaut Jeremy Hansen will comprise the crew, aiming to travel farther from Earth than any other humans. This flight is a pivotal step in NASA's Artemis program, established during the Trump administration, which aims to return astronauts to the lunar surface by 2028, despite past budget shortfalls and the COVID pandemic.
