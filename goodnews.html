DNA Study Sheds Light on Ancestry of Indigenous Americans

2025-05-17 02:34:00 EDT

A new genetic study has traced the migration of the first people to colonize the Americas back to modern-day Russia, approximately 20,000 to 30,000 years ago. The study, published in Science, suggests that the languages and traditions of Indigenous groups in the Americas can be traced back to these early settlers. Researchers sequenced the genomes of 1,537 individuals from 139 ethnic groups in northern Eurasia and the Americas, revealing genetic codes that changed in people from different geographical regions and various Indigenous groups. This allowed them to study patterns of population history, migration, and adaptation over thousands of years and understand the origins of South American communities.
