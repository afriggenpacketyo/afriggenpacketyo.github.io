The law permits an adult citizen who is mentally competent and suffering from an irreversible, terminal disease to choose a medically assisted death administered by a healthcare professional. The legislation, which previously passed the lower house with a large majority, reflects strong public support, with over 60% of Uruguayans in favor. This move continues Uruguay's history of progressive social policies in a predominantly Catholic region, following earlier legalizations of cannabis, same-sex marriage, and abortion.
