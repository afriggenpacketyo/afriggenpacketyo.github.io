Businesses Use 'First Sale Rule' to Bypass Tariffs Legally

2025-05-26 01:04:41 EDT

Businesses are minimizing the impact of tariffs by utilizing the "first sale rule," a decades-old provision in U.S. customs law. This rule allows importers to calculate customs duties based on the initial sale price from the factory to a vendor, rather than the inflated price from an intermediary. Companies must meet specific criteria, including having at least two sales involving independent parties and proving the item was destined for the U.S. While the rule has been around since 1988, it has gained renewed attention amid increasing tariffs, offering potential cost savings for businesses willing to navigate the additional complexities.
