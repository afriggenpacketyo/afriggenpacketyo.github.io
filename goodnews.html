Scientists Unearth New Fish-Eating Spinosaurus Species with Distinctive Horn

2026-02-22 20:10:39 EST

Paleontologists in Niger have discovered *Spinosaurus mirabilis*, a new species of large, fish-eating dinosaur from the Cretaceous period, marking the first new Spinosaurus discovery in over a century. This impressive species, comparable in length to a school bus, inhabited marshy inland areas about 95 million years ago and likely hunted in shallow waters, similar to modern herons. Its most notable feature is a foot-long curved horn on its skull, which scientists speculate may have been brightly colored and used for attracting or communicating with other members of its species. The findings contribute significantly to the ongoing scientific understanding of how aquatic these massive predators truly were and their ancient environment.
