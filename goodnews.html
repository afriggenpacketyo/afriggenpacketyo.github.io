EU Court Mandates Member States Recognize Transgender Citizens' Lived Gender on IDs

2026-03-12 12:40:00 EDT

The European Court of Justice (ECJ) has ruled that European Union member states must issue identification documents that reflect transgender citizens' "lived gender" rather than their gender assigned at birth. This landmark decision, stemming from a case referred by Bulgaria's Supreme Court, asserts that documents not aligning with an individual's lived gender identity can cause "considerable inconveniences" in daily life and during travel within the bloc. The ECJ emphasized that EU law supersedes national legislation in this regard, a ruling that will impact other EU nations, such as Hungary and Slovakia, which have previously refused such changes, promoting greater legal recognition and ease of movement for transgender individuals.
