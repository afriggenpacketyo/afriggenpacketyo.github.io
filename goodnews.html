Edible Fruit Coating Extends Shelf Life, Reduces Waste

2025-06-20 01:00:09 EDT

Chinese scientists have developed a low-cost, edible, and washable protein-based coating that can more than double the shelf life of fruits by reducing moisture loss and microbial growth. Tested on 17 varieties of whole and cut fruits, including strawberries, tomatoes, kiwis, and mangos, the coating effectively delayed rot by almost a week or more. The new method is as effective as existing preservation techniques but reduces carbon dioxide emissions and the potential for toxicity, offering a promising solution to global food waste. The research team emphasized the coating's universal applicability for post-harvest and fresh-cut fruits due to its edible properties, easy washability, and low cost, costing just 9 US cents per kilogram of fruit.
