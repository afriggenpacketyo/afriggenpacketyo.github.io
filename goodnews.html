Germany Seeks Digital Sovereignty, Reducing Reliance on U.S. Tech

2026-03-29 03:53:00 EDT

Germany's public sector is actively working to decrease its reliance on U.S. technology companies, driven by concerns over data access under the U.S. CLOUD Act and potential political pressure. The German Center for Digital Sovereignty in Public Administration (ZenDiS) is developing open-source alternatives, such as openDesk, a flexible office suite, to secure sensitive government data and ensure operational independence. The initiative, founded in 2022, has already sold numerous licenses and is exploring private sector distribution, highlighting a strategic shift to mitigate risks like potential software update cessation or political blackmail from foreign tech providers.
