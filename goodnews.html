Claudette Colvin, a pivotal figure in the civil rights movement, has died at 86. At age 15, nine months before Rosa Parks, Colvin was arrested in Montgomery, Alabama, in 1955 for refusing to give up her seat on a segregated bus. Her courageous act, driven by a desire for freedom, helped lay the groundwork for the Montgomery Bus Boycott and the broader civil rights movement. Although her bravery was often overlooked, Colvin was a plaintiff in the landmark lawsuit that outlawed racial segregation on city buses, and her record was expunged in 2021.
