Woman with rare autoimmune disorder to run Boston Marathon after regaining mobility

2026-04-20 07:00:01 EDT

Marion Jones, diagnosed with the rare autoimmune disorder Neuromyelitis Optica (NMO) in 2020, is set to run the Boston Marathon after experiencing partial paralysis and significant mobility loss. NMO primarily affects the optic nerves and spinal cord, leading to severe attacks that can cause permanent vision loss or paralysis. Jones, an avid runner before her diagnosis, underwent intensive rehabilitation and has since completed six marathons. Her participation in the Boston Marathon will earn her the Abbott World Marathon Majors Six Star Finisher Medal. She is also raising money for the hospital that treated her, viewing the event as a "full-circle moment" and a "celebratory victory lap" for herself and the rare disease community.
