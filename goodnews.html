The Peak District's Evolution from Private Land to UK's First National Park

2026-04-18 19:36:46 EDT

Celebrating 75 years as the UK's first national park, the Peak District boasts a rich history of attracting visitors, from early gentry to mass tourism spurred by 19th-century railways. A pivotal moment in its transformation was the 1932 Kinder Scout mass trespass, a protest against private land ownership that significantly advanced the fight for public access rights. Subsequent legislation, including the 1949 National Parks and Access to the Countryside Act and the 2000 Countryside and Rights of Way Act, gradually opened up the area, which now welcomes approximately 13 million visitors annually.
