Understanding Key Differences Between ETFs and Mutual Funds for Investors

2025-09-22 07:00:01 EDT

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) and mutual funds, while both offering diversified asset baskets, present distinct characteristics crucial for investors. ETFs trade like stocks on an exchange, providing real-time pricing, whereas mutual funds transact once daily at the market close. ETFs generally offer greater tax efficiency due to their "in-kind" transaction mechanism, which often avoids capital gains distributions, and typically feature lower expense ratios. However, mutual funds provide a broader selection of investment strategies and are more commonly integrated into 401(k) plans. (not investment advice)
