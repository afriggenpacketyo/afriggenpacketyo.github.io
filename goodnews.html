For investors looking to add gold to their portfolios, exchange-traded funds (ETFs) offer a convenient method, but the tax implications can be complex. ETFs that hold physical gold are treated as collectibles by the IRS, meaning long-term gains are taxed at a maximum rate of 28%, which is higher than standard capital gains rates. Alternatively, ETFs that invest in gold futures contracts are subject to a "60/40 rule," where 60% of gains are taxed at the long-term rate and 40% at the ordinary income rate. Experts advise that gold can be volatile and typically recommend limiting it to a small portion of a diversified portfolio. (not investment advice)
