Explaining Chronic Venous Insufficiency, the Vein Condition Diagnosed in President Trump

2025-07-17 17:16:04 EDT

Following the White House's announcement of President Trump's diagnosis, this article explains chronic venous insufficiency (CVI). The condition occurs when veins in the legs struggle to pump blood back to the heart, causing blood to pool and leading to symptoms like swelling, achiness, and varicose veins. CVI is common in older adults, and risk factors include age, blood clots, and being overweight. While the condition itself is often benign, doctors must first rule out more serious causes of leg swelling, such as heart or kidney disease, typically using ultrasound exams to confirm the diagnosis.
