WHO Approves First Malaria Drug Specifically for Infants

2026-04-24 19:30:00 EDT

The World Health Organization (WHO) has approved the first malaria drug specifically designed for infants weighing less than 5 kilograms, ahead of World Malaria Day. This new formulation of artemether and lumefantrine addresses a critical gap in care, as previous treatments were developed for older children, posing risks of dosing errors and toxicity for newborns. The approval allows countries to authorize the drug and enables UN agencies to distribute it in malaria-endemic areas. Infants and children under five remain the most vulnerable to malaria, which significantly impacts pregnancy outcomes. This development marks a crucial step in combating the disease, especially in Sub-Saharan Africa.
