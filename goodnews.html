While a debt generally remains owed, a legal obligation to a debt collector depends on the debt's validity, age, and the collector's right to enforce it. Borrowers have the right to request debt validation, and the debt may be "time-barred" if it exceeds the state's statute of limitations, preventing lawsuits. Options beyond full payment include negotiating a lower settlement, working with debt relief companies, or considering bankruptcy for overwhelming balances.
