Women face financial risks in retirement due to longer life expectancies and long-term care needs

2026-06-07 09:30:01 EDT

Women are statistically more likely to outlive men, creating a financial risk of needing long-term care services later in life, often after their spouses have passed. Approximately 26% of women will require long-term care for over five years, compared to 17.5% of men, with women's average care duration also being longer. Experts advise proactive planning, as Medicare generally does not cover long-term care expenses. Options include relying on unpaid caregivers, Medicaid, self-insuring, or purchasing long-term care insurance, which is typically more expensive for women. Hybrid policies combining long-term care with life insurance are also becoming popular, offering benefits regardless of care needs.
