Palaeontologists Uncover New Long-Necked Dinosaur Species in Thailand

2026-07-10 17:30:57 EDT

Palaeontologists in Thailand have announced the discovery of a new species of plant-eating dinosaur, named Uragasaurus kalasinensis, based on fossils unearthed in Kalasin Province. This long-necked sauropod, estimated to have lived approximately 150 million years ago and reached up to 20 meters in length, represents the first finding of the Mamenchisauridae family in Thailand. A distinctive dorsal vertebra, analyzed through CT scans, revealed a unique Y-shaped bone arrangement and air-cavity structure, confirming its status as a novel species. This discovery significantly contributes to understanding the geographical distribution of these dinosaurs and the Late Jurassic period's biodiversity in Southeast Asia.
