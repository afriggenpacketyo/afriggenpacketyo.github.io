Natural 'White Hydrogen' Emerges as Potential Game-Changer for Clean Energy

2026-03-13 07:33:00 EDT

Researchers are exploring "white hydrogen," a naturally occurring form of hydrogen that flows from the ground, as a revolutionary clean energy source. Unlike manufactured hydrogen, which heavily relies on fossil fuels, white hydrogen forms naturally in the Earth's crust through geological processes. An estimated 5.6 trillion tons are believed to exist, with even a small percentage capable of covering global demand for centuries. While only one village in Mali currently extracts it, pioneers like Jürgen Grötsch in Bavaria aim for commercial extraction by 2030, facing legal and investment hurdles due to its nascent status as a recognized natural resource.
