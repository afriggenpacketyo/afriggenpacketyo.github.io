UK Experiences Significant Diurnal Temperature Swings in Early Spring

2026-03-19 22:30:56 EDT

The UK is currently experiencing a "temperature rollercoaster" with warm spring days succeeded by significantly chilly nights, a phenomenon attributed to high pressure systems. Clear skies, while facilitating daytime warmth, also permit rapid heat loss overnight, resulting in a large diurnal temperature range. Some regions have observed temperature differences of approximately 21C between day and night, leading to frosty mornings after mild afternoons. This pattern is common in early spring due to lengthening days allowing efficient solar energy absorption, coupled with a still-cold ground and lower atmosphere from winter, creating sharp contrasts and dry air that heats and cools quickly.
