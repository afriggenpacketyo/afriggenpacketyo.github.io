Mortgage Rates Hit Monthly Low, Boosting Refinance Activity

2026-02-18 07:45:42 EST

Mortgage interest rates have fallen to their lowest point in a month, leading to an increase in refinancing applications. The average rate for a 30-year fixed-rate mortgage decreased to 6.17%, contributing to a 2.8% rise in overall mortgage demand. While lower rates did not significantly incentivize new home purchases, the surge in refinances was substantial, with applications up 7% for the week and an impressive 132% compared to the same week last year. This trend is attributed to lower Treasury yields, influenced by weaker retail and home sales data, which outweighed positive job market reports. Despite the uptick in refinancing, purchase applications saw a 3% decline, suggesting ongoing caution among potential homebuyers due to economic uncertainties and limited housing supply.
