High Income Does Not Disqualify Individuals from Medicare Eligibility

2025-09-09 12:50:31 EDT

While eligibility for Medicare is based on age or disability status rather than income, high earners may face increased costs for their coverage. A mechanism known as the Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount (IRMAA) can lead to higher premiums for Medicare Part B (medical insurance) and Part D (prescription drugs). For instance, individuals with a modified adjusted gross income above $106,000 in 2025 will pay more than the standard premium. This article explains that while you cannot make too much money to qualify, strategic financial planning can help manage these income-based surcharges in retirement.
