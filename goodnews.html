Investing in gold through Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) provides an accessible entry point for portfolio diversification, but investors must be aware of its distinct tax treatment. Gold futures ETFs, conversely, fall under the 60/40 rule, where 60% of gains are long-term and 40% are ordinary income. While gold can offer a store of value during market turbulence, financial experts typically recommend limiting its allocation to no more than 5% of a portfolio due to its inherent volatility and historical underperformance compared to other asset classes.
