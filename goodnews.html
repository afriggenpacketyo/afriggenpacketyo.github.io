Antidepressants Can Exacerbate Heat Sensitivity, Experts Warn

2026-07-11 13:18:00 EDT

Individuals taking antidepressants may experience heightened sensitivity to summer heat, leading to symptoms like dizziness, nausea, and dehydration. Certain antidepressants, including SSRIs and tricyclic antidepressants, can interfere with the body's natural temperature regulation and sweating mechanisms. This can make it more challenging for the body to stay cool and hydrated, increasing the risk of heat-related illnesses. Medical professionals advise those on these medications to be mindful of fluid intake and to limit direct sun exposure. It is crucial to consult a doctor before altering medication dosages, as sudden cessation can cause withdrawal symptoms.
