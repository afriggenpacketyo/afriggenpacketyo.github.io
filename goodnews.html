Inherited debt generally does not impact Social Security benefits, but personal debt poses risks

2026-05-29 12:16:54 EDT

Inherited debt, such as credit card balances or medical bills, typically does not directly affect Social Security benefits, as debts are paid from the deceased's estate. Federal law provides strong protection against garnishment of Social Security benefits by commercial creditors. However, individuals may be responsible for debts if they co-signed loans, held joint accounts, or served as an executor who mishandled assets. The primary risk to Social Security income comes from personal debt, which creditors may pursue through legal means, potentially impacting credit and causing stress, though direct seizure of benefits is difficult.
