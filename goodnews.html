2025-11-05 04:34:58 EST

The largest and brightest full moon of 2025, known as the Beaver supermoon, is set to rise this week. A supermoon occurs when a full moon coincides with its perigee, the point in its elliptical orbit where it is closest to Earth, making it appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than usual. This event is the second of three supermoons scheduled for the year. The name "Beaver Moon" is a traditional nickname for November's full moon, reflecting the time of year when beavers are actively preparing their dams for winter. The final supermoon of the year will occur on December 4th.
