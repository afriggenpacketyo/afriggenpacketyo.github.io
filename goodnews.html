2025-07-19 06:00:00 EDT

The market for high-protein snacks is booming, but nutrition experts caution that marketing claims can be misleading. Many products labeled as "high-protein" also contain significant amounts of sugar and processed ingredients, undermining their health benefits. To assess a snack's true protein value, experts suggest a simple calculation: multiply the grams of protein by four and divide by the total calories to see the percentage of calories from protein, with 30% or more being a good benchmark. They also recommend prioritizing whole-food snacks like Greek yogurt, nuts, and cottage cheese over highly processed alternatives.
