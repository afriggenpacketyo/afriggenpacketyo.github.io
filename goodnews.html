A Guide to Age Considerations When Purchasing Annuities for Retirement

2025-08-18 15:24:37 EDT

This article explores whether age limits affect the ability to purchase annuities, a popular retirement income tool. While there are no federal age caps, most insurance companies impose their own limits, typically between 80 and 85 years old, primarily to manage risk related to life expectancy. The best age to buy an annuity depends on individual financial strategy rather than a specific number. Purchasing in one's late 50s or 60s allows for growth in deferred annuities, while buying an immediate annuity closer to retirement age can supplement Social Security, with older buyers often receiving higher monthly payouts.
