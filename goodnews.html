The city's plan involves locally upzoning 55 single-family and low-density areas to allow for 4-16 unit buildings up to four stories tall, thereby maintaining local control over development until 2030. This approach, favored by homeowners seeking to mitigate SB 79's impact, is considered less ambitious by pro-housing advocates. Council members also moved to expand incentives to ensure that these newly legalized housing types are actually built.
