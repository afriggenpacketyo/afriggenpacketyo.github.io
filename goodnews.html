A supermoon occurs when a full moon coincides with its closest point to Earth in its elliptical orbit, making it appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter. This year's Harvest Moon is notably late, as it is the full moon closest to the autumn equinox, traditionally providing extra light for farmers.
