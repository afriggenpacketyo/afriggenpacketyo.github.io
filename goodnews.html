Tokyo to Waive Water Fees to Combat Summer Heat

In an effort to encourage residents to use air conditioning without financial burden during increasingly hot summers, Tokyo will waive basic water utility charges for households for four months. Tokyo Governor Yuriko Koike stated that the goal is to protect residents' lives and daily living amid declining real wages and predictions of an extremely hot summer. Approximately 8.2 million households are expected to benefit, saving about 5,000 yen on water bills over the four months, though metered usage will still be charged.
