NASA deactivates an instrument on Voyager 1 to extend its interstellar mission as power dwindles.

2026-04-19 01:40:28 EDT

NASA has made the critical decision to deactivate the Low-energy Charged Particles (LECP) experiment on Voyager 1, the most distant human-made object, as a measure to conserve dwindling power and extend its historic interstellar mission. Launched in 1977 for what was initially a five-year journey, Voyager 1 has remarkably operated for nearly 49 years, crossing into interstellar space in 2012. This strategic shutdown, prompted by an unexpected drop in power levels, is expected to grant the probe approximately another year of operational life. Engineers at JPL are also developing a more comprehensive "Big Bang" power conservation plan, with the ambitious goal of keeping at least one instrument functioning on both Voyager spacecraft into the 2030s, ensuring their continued unprecedented exploration beyond our solar system.
