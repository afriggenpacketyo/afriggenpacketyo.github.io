Armagh Observatory Celebrates 230 Years of Unbroken Daily Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is marking a major milestone: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation. This unbroken sequence of handwritten data, which began on July 14, 1795, is the longest of its kind in the UK and Ireland. The tradition continues today, with a human observer manually recording temperature and pressure, a practice largely replaced by automated stations elsewhere. This invaluable historical record has documented significant weather events and contributed to the science of meteorology, including the development of the cup anemometer for measuring wind speed.
