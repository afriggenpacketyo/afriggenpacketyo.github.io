Benin Enhances Cotton Industry Infrastructure with Chinese Assistance

2026-06-21 02:00:05 EDT

Benin, Africa's largest cotton producer, is making significant strides in reshaping its cotton industry with support from China. The country is developing the Glo-Djigbé Industrial Zone (GDIZ) to process cotton domestically, aiming to end raw exports. A new 184km road connecting northern cotton regions to the coast and processing plants is a key development. This infrastructure improvement is crucial for the GDIZ, which already processes a fifth of the national cotton harvest into finished clothing. The Finance Minister views this project as a logical investment given the strategic location of Banikoara, a major cotton-producing area.
