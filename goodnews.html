He partnered with his denim supplier, Levi Strauss, to patent the design in 1873, creating a durable garment for miners and cowboys. Over the 20th century, denim transitioned from workwear to casual attire, popularized by dude ranch vacations, post-WWII leisure culture, and Hollywood, eventually becoming a symbol of rebellion and a mainstay of global fashion for all social classes.
