Explaining the Meteorological and Astronomical Definitions of Winter

2025-11-23 05:15:38 EST

The official start of winter can be defined in two different ways, leading to some confusion. Meteorologists use a fixed calendar where winter in the Northern Hemisphere always begins on December 1 and ends on the last day of February, grouping the three coldest months for consistent climate data comparison. In contrast, the astronomical definition of winter is based on the Earth's position relative to the sun, starting on the winter solstice around December 21, which is the shortest day of the year. This astronomical season then continues until the vernal equinox in March.
