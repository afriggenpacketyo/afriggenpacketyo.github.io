NASA's Perseverance Rover Discovers Potential Meteorite on Mars After Four Years

2025-11-19 21:57:05 EST

NASA's Perseverance rover has identified a peculiar, shiny rock nicknamed "Phippsaksla" on the Martian surface, which scientists believe could be a meteorite originating from an ancient asteroid. Analysis by the rover's SuperCam, which uses a laser to determine chemical makeup, revealed unusually high levels of iron and nickel. These elements are typically found together only in meteorites formed deep within ancient asteroids, not in native Martian rocks. This marks a significant discovery for Perseverance, as earlier missions like Curiosity and Opportunity had found similar metallic fragments. The rock's location atop impact-formed bedrock outside Jezero crater could offer crucial insights into how meteorites interact with the Martian environment over time, furthering understanding of cosmic visitors to the red planet.
