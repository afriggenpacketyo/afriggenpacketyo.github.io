Exploring the Origins and Growing Popularity of the Term 'Neurodivergent'

The term "neurodivergent" has gained widespread use since 2020 to describe minds that process information differently from what is considered typical. Coined in the late 1990s by activist Kassiane Asasumasu, it was intended as a broad umbrella term for solidarity among people with various neurological differences, including but not limited to autism, ADHD, depression, or conditions from brain injuries. The article traces its journey from niche online forums to mainstream conversation, noting how the pandemic's focus on mental health and workplace accommodations accelerated its adoption. While its popularity has grown, there is an ongoing effort to ensure it remains inclusive of all types of neurological variation.
