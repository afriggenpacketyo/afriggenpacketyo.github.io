2025-08-17 00:00:53 EDT

A long-standing principle known as the "85th percentile rule" is being reconsidered by some states and federal agencies for setting speed limits on U.S. roads. This rule suggests that the speed limit should be set at the speed at or below which 85% of drivers travel, effectively letting the fastest drivers determine the legal speed. Critics argue this creates a dangerous feedback loop where speed limits continually increase, especially in urban areas with pedestrians and cyclists. In response to rising traffic fatalities, officials are now encouraging a more context-sensitive approach that considers road usage and safety for all users.
