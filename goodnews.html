The Word "OK" Traces Its Origins to a 19th-Century Joke and a Presidential Campaign

2025-07-23 17:27:00 EDT

The ubiquitous word "OK" is an American invention that began as a joke in the 1830s before being popularized by a presidential campaign. Linguists trace its first appearance to a Boston newspaper in 1839 as a playful abbreviation for "oll korrect," a deliberate misspelling of "all correct." The term gained widespread use during President Martin Van Buren's 1840 reelection campaign, as his nickname "Old Kinderhook" aligned perfectly with the "O.K." abbreviation. Now considered one of the most widely understood words globally, its journey highlights how slang can evolve into a universal term.
