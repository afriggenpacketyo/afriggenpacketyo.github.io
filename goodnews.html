Swiss Voters Reject Proposal to Significantly Reduce Public Broadcaster's Licence Fee

2026-03-08 16:47:29 EDT

Swiss voters have rejected a right-wing initiative to drastically cut the annual licence fee for the national broadcaster, the Swiss Broadcasting Corporation (SBC), in a Sunday referendum. The proposal, backed by the Swiss People's Party, aimed to reduce the fee from 335 to 200 francs, but it gained only 38% support. The government and all other parliamentary parties had opposed the cut, emphasizing the fee's crucial importance for ensuring proper representation of Switzerland's four national languages and maintaining comprehensive foreign news and sports coverage, ensuring media diversity. Voters also backed enshrining cash availability in the constitution.
