Health Savings Accounts Can Create Tax Burdens for Non-Spouse Heirs

2026-05-09 09:30:01 EDT

While Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) offer significant tax advantages for covering medical expenses in old age, dying with a substantial HSA balance can create a considerable tax burden for non-spouse heirs. Unlike surviving spouses, who inherit HSAs tax-free, other beneficiaries face immediate taxation on the assets as ordinary income in the year of death, potentially pushing them into the highest marginal tax bracket. Financial planners emphasize this often-overlooked issue and suggest strategies to mitigate the tax hit, such as actively spending down the HSA during one's lifetime, donating the funds to charity, or spreading the inheritance among multiple individuals.
