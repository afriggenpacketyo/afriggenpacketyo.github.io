2025-09-28 14:55:24 EDT

This occurs when two tropical cyclones orbit a common center, sometimes merging or deflecting each other's paths. While experts consider a direct interaction unlikely in this case, as one storm is significantly stronger, the possibility highlights the complex dynamics of an active hurricane season. The two systems are being closely watched for their individual impacts on the Bahamas and the southeastern U.S. coastline.
