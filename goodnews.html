The Concept of 'Groupthink' as a Cause for Bad Decisions is Largely a Myth, Experts Say

2025-11-02 07:26:01 EST

The popular concept of "groupthink," frequently cited to explain major policy failures, is based on a psychological theory that lacks substantial scientific backing, according to many academics. The term, coined by psychologist Irving Janis in the 1970s, suggests that highly cohesive groups suppress dissent to maintain harmony, leading to flawed decision-making. However, decades of research have failed to consistently validate this hypothesis. Experts now argue that bad decisions are more often the result of factors like internal power dynamics, political pressure, and deliberate compliance rather than an unconscious psychological drive for consensus.
