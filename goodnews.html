Understanding Reverse Mortgage Impact on Social Security and Medicare Benefits

2025-08-28 14:29:08 EDT

For retirees considering a reverse mortgage, this article clarifies that such loans generally do not directly affect Social Security retirement benefits or Medicare eligibility. Social Security payments are based on earnings, and Medicare is not means-tested, meaning income and assets don't determine qualification. However, the article emphasizes that need-based programs like Supplemental Security Income (SSI) and Medicaid can be impacted. Large lump-sum withdrawals from a reverse mortgage could push recipients over SSI's asset limits, while unused proceeds might affect Medicaid eligibility for long-term care. The article advises consulting a financial advisor and a HUD-approved housing counselor to navigate these nuances (not investment advice).
