Study Links Gut Movement to Brain Health, Suggests Role in Waste Clearance

2026-05-07 19:04:50 EDT

New research suggests that coordinated contractions in abdominal muscles, like those during walking, influence brain health by propelling its movement within the fluid-filled skull cavity. Neuroscientist Patrick Drew's team observed that mouse brains moved milliseconds before a step, directly linked to abdominal muscle contraction rather than breathing or cardiac activity. This connection occurs via the vertebral venous plexus, a network of veins connecting the abdomen to the spine. Simulations indicate this "hydraulic system" drives cerebrospinal fluid out of the brain, leading to the hypothesis that it plays a crucial role in flushing out protein waste and other unnecessary materials.
