Neuroscientist Explains Memory Is Not a Record of the Past but a Tool for the Future

2025-10-19 06:30:00 EDT

According to neuroscientist Dr. Charan Ranganath, human memory is fundamentally misunderstood as a passive storage system for past events. Instead, he argues that memory is an active resource our brains use to make sense of the present, plan for the future, and construct a personal narrative. Forgetting is as crucial as remembering, as it helps filter out irrelevant details, while emotion prioritizes what gets stored. This perspective reframes memory's purpose from perfect recall to shaping our identity and decisions, explaining why our recollection of an event, rather than the experience itself, often determines our long-term satisfaction.
