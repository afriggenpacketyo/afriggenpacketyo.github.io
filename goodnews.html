NASA Deactivates Instrument on Voyager 1 to Extend Mission Life

2026-04-19 01:40:28 EDT

NASA has powered down a science instrument on the Voyager 1 spacecraft, the most distant human-made object, to conserve its dwindling power supply. Launched nearly 50 years ago for a five-year mission, Voyager 1 is now over 15 billion miles from Earth, operating on a radioisotope thermoelectric generator, which diminishes by about 4 watts annually. This strategic shutdown of the Low-energy Charged Particles experiment is part of a broader, carefully planned conservation effort. Engineers are developing a "Big Bang" plan to swap components, hoping to extend the mission by roughly another year and keep at least one instrument operational into the 2030s, continuing its unprecedented interstellar observations from beyond the heliopause.
