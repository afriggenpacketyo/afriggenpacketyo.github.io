2025-11-25 06:31:43 EST

Originating from the late 19th century when turkeys were sent to presidents as dinner, the modern ceremony was formalized by President George H.W. Bush in 1989. The practice evolved from a 1947 protest by the poultry industry against "Poultryless Thursdays" and was inadvertently named a "pardon" by the press after President John F.
