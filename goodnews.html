Navigating Options After Failing the Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Means Test

2025-11-03 13:10:25 EST

Failing the means test for Chapter 7 bankruptcy, which typically occurs if income is too high, does not eliminate all debt relief options. Individuals can often convert their case to a Chapter 13 bankruptcy, which involves a three-to-five-year repayment plan rather than immediate debt liquidation. Alternatively, one might challenge the means test results by documenting a recent drop in income or including additional allowable expenses. Other alternatives to bankruptcy include debt settlement programs, debt management plans offered by credit counseling agencies, or debt consolidation loans for those with a decent credit score.
