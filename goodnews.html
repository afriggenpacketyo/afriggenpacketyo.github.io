Armagh Observatory Commemorates 230 Years of Continuous Weather Recording

2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is celebrating a remarkable milestone: 230 years of uninterrupted, handwritten weather observations. This tradition makes it the longest continuous weather data sequence in the UK and Ireland, providing an invaluable record of climate history since July 14, 1795. Despite the prevalence of automated stations, a human observer continues to manually record daily readings, a practice maintained through generations. The records have chronicled major weather events, like the 'Night of the Big Wind' in 1839, and have contributed to the science of meteorology, including the development of the cup anemometer.
