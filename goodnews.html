Venter, often described as a scientific "maverick," was celebrated for his groundbreaking work and became the first person to sequence his own individual genome. His research team also made significant strides in synthetic biology, creating a bacterial cell controlled by lab-synthesized DNA, leaving an indelible mark on the scientific community.
