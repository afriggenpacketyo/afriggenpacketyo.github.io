Birthright Citizenship Is a New World Ideal, Rooted in Inclusion and Equality

2025-06-10 08:57:43 EDT

Birthright citizenship, or jus solis, is a foundational ideal of the Americas, granting citizenship to children born within a country's territory regardless of their parents' nationality. This principle, rooted in the rejection of blood-based systems, was first enshrined in Mexico's constitution in 1814 and spread throughout the region as a revolutionary act of inclusion. While the United States eventually adopted birthright citizenship with the Fourteenth Amendment, debates continue over its interpretation, particularly concerning undocumented migrants. The article highlights the historical and philosophical significance of jus solis as a commitment to equality and integration.
