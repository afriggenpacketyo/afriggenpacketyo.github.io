NHS to Offer World-First 'Trojan Horse' Therapy for Blood Cancer

2025-06-12 20:05:15 EDT

The NHS in England will be the first to offer a "Trojan horse" therapy for the blood cancer myeloma, a treatment that delivers toxic drugs directly inside cancer cells. This advanced chemotherapy, known as belantamab mafodotin, has been shown to halt myeloma for nearly three times longer than current therapies, reducing side effects. Paul Silvester, one of the first patients to receive the treatment, reports that it has been "life-changing," allowing him to enjoy a normal life and plan future adventures. The therapy works by binding a lethal chemotherapy drug to an antibody that targets and is absorbed by cancerous plasma cells, releasing the drug to kill the cancer. This therapy has a very positive outlook, with the chance to improve the lives of many people.
