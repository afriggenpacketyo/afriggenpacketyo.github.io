Study Suggests Optimism May Lower Dementia Risk

2026-04-13 13:14:55 EDT

New research published in the Journal of the American Geriatrics Society indicates a potential link between a person's optimism and a reduced risk of developing dementia. The study, which followed over 9,000 cognitively healthy adults for up to 14 years, found that individuals with higher levels of optimism had approximately a 15% lower risk of dementia. These findings remained consistent even after adjusting for various demographic and health-related factors. Researchers suggest that psychological and emotional resources, such as hopefulness about the future, may play a role in maintaining brain health as individuals age. While the study highlights an association, further research, including clinical trials, is needed to confirm a direct causal relationship and explore potential interventions to boost optimism for brain health benefits.
