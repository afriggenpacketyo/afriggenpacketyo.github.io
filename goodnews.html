The Economist's 2026 US Senate election forecast methodology explained

2026-04-21 13:33:14 EDT

The Economist's model for predicting the 2026 U.S. Senate elections combines national and race-level polling data with historical electoral results and nominee information. The methodology involves simulating 10,001 hypothetical scenarios for the national popular vote for the House of Representatives, introducing variations in party performance. Additional uncertainty is applied to how Senate race outcomes might differ from House results. For each simulated national environment, the model estimates a plausible range of outcomes for individual Senate races. Finally, a random outcome is selected from these ranges for each race within each simulation to generate the final forecast. This approach aims to provide a probabilistic estimate of each party's chances of winning individual races and controlling the Senate.
