Businesses Circumvent Tariffs Using the 'First Sale Rule'

2025-05-27 08:45:47 EDT

Businesses are utilizing a decades-old customs law called the "first sale rule" to minimize the impact of tariffs. This rule allows U.S. importers to calculate customs duties based on the initial sale price between a foreign manufacturer and a vendor, rather than the inflated price from the vendor to the U.S. retailer. The first sale rule has gained renewed attention under the Trump administration's tariff regime, with companies exploring its potential cost savings. To apply the rule, there must be at least two sales, carried out at arm's length by independent parties, and proof that the item was destined for the U.S., with documentation of the first sale price.
