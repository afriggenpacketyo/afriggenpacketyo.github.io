Scotland Initiates Newborn Screening for Spinal Muscular Atrophy

2026-03-22 22:55:52 EDT

Scotland has become the first nation in the UK to implement routine newborn screening for Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA), a rare and severe genetic condition causing progressive muscle weakness that can limit life expectancy without treatment. This two-year pilot program will incorporate the SMA test into the standard heel prick screening given to all babies born in Scotland, enabling early identification and access to life-changing treatments. The initiative, championed by campaigners including singer Jesy Nelson whose twins were diagnosed with SMA, offers immense hope for future generations, allowing affected infants to potentially grow up without the lifelong healthcare needs associated with the disease.
