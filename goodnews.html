NASA Launches TRACERS Satellites to Study Solar Wind and Space Weather

2025-07-24 10:10:49 EDT

NASA has launched twin TRACERS satellites aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket to investigate how electrically-charged solar wind interacts with Earth's magnetic field, a phenomenon that creates "space weather." This research is crucial for understanding and predicting impacts on critical systems like satellites, GPS, communication signals, and power grids. The identical spacecraft will fly in tandem to precisely measure rapid changes indicating how solar wind "couples" with Earth's magnetic field, which drives auroral displays but also causes disruptions like unplanned electrical currents. The mission aims to enhance safety for ground-based assets and astronauts by improving space weather forecasting.
