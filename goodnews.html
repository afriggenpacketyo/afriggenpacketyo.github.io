Scientists Document Rock-Climbing Fish in Democratic Republic of Congo

2026-04-05 07:00:00 EDT

Researchers have formally documented a unique phenomenon in the Democratic Republic of Congo: shellear fish that can climb a 50-foot waterfall, a behavior previously unrecorded in Africa. Thousands of these tiny fish, roughly the size of a fat french fry, use specialized pelvic fins for support and front pectoral fins with tiny hooks to grip the rock, wriggling vertically in a 'power burst' movement. The arduous ascent, which can take up to ten hours, is believed to be part of an upstream migration. This remarkable discovery highlights the extreme adaptability of fish and underscores the critical importance of protecting their habitats, as water diversion could severely threaten these migratory species.
