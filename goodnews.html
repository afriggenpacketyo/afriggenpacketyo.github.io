2025-07-13 19:56:04 EDT

The Armagh Observatory in Northern Ireland is celebrating a remarkable scientific achievement: 230 years of continuous, daily weather observation. This unbroken tradition of handwritten data, which began on July 14, 1795, constitutes the longest such sequence in the UK and Ireland. The records provide an invaluable resource for climate science, documenting major weather events like the infamous 'Night of the Big Wind' in 1839 and contributing to meteorological advancements like the cup anemometer. The observatory continues its tradition of manual data collection, providing a unique human element in an era of automated weather stations.
