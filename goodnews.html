Photo Exhibit Highlights Courage of Women Facing Tropical Disease in Madagascar

2025-06-21 09:34:12 EDT

A photo exhibit titled 'Sahy Rano' by photographer Miora Rajaonary showcases the courage of eight women in Madagascar who are raising awareness about female genital schistosomiasis (FGS), a neglected tropical disease affecting millions in Africa. The waterborne infection, spread by parasitic worms, leads to infertility, ectopic pregnancy, and genital ulcers, often causing shame and suspicion due to STD-like symptoms. The women in the exhibit wear traditional Malagasy face masks to emphasize their bravery in speaking about the disease, while Rajaonary uses painted backdrops and laundry ropes to symbolize the water's presence in their lives. Razanaparana, one of the women featured, contracted FGS while working in rice fields and faced abandonment by her husband due to the symptoms, highlighting the severe social impact of the disease. The exhibit aims to break taboos and promote public awareness about FGS and its impact on women's lives.
