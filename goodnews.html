2025-10-06 06:28:37 EDT

The 2025 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine has been awarded to Shimon Sakaguchi of Japan and U.S. researchers Mary Brunkow and Fred Ramsdell. Their collective work led to the discovery of regulatory T-cells, a crucial component of the immune system. These cells act as "security guards," preventing the immune system from attacking the body's own tissues, a process known as peripheral immune tolerance. Their discoveries have been fundamental to understanding and developing new treatments for autoimmune diseases, where this process fails, and for cancer, where these cells can hinder the body's ability to fight tumors.
