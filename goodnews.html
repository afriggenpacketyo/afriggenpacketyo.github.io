2026-01-26 06:00:16 EST

Researchers have discovered cave art in Indonesia, specifically on the island of Sulawesi, dating back approximately 68,000 years. This finding represents one of the earliest known examples of human creativity and symbolic thinking, pushing back the timeline of such cognitive abilities. The art consists of handprints created by blowing pigment over hands pressed against cave walls, with some fingerprints intentionally modified. The discovery is significant as it provides the oldest archaeological evidence of Homo sapiens in the Wallacea region, potentially predating previously held beliefs about the development of early human cognition.
