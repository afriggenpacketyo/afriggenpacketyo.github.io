The study, published in Science, details how nanowire implants made from tellurium were used in a biocompatible device to restore vision in mice and a monkey. Tellurium, a byproduct of copper refining, possesses excellent photoelectric properties, converting visible and infrared light into electrical energy. This advancement holds significant promise for treating human blindness, offering a potential solution for replacing damaged photoreceptor cells in the retina.
