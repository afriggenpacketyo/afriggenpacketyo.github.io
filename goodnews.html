Experts Question Efficacy and Regulation of Widespread Dietary Supplement Use in America

2025-08-19 07:30:00 EDT

A significant portion of Americans, approximately 60%, use dietary supplements, yet the health benefits and safety of many products remain scientifically unproven. According to health experts, the industry is loosely regulated under the 1994 Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act, which allows manufacturers to make vague health claims without undergoing rigorous clinical trials required for pharmaceuticals. While some supplements like creatine can offer modest benefits when combined with specific activities, many others, such as collagen, lack convincing evidence of efficacy. Health professionals generally advise obtaining nutrients from a balanced diet unless a specific deficiency is diagnosed by a doctor.
