Berlin's Pioneering Queer History in the 1920s Explored

2026-04-11 02:34:00 EDT

Berlin emerged as a global hub for queer life and activism in the 1920s, during Germany's Weimar Republic, fostering a vibrant scene for LGBTQ+ nightlife, research, and community building. This era saw the rise of figures like physician Magnus Hirschfeld, who founded the Scientific-Humanitarian Committee and the Institute for Sexual Science, pioneering research on sexual orientation and gender identity. Despite Paragraph 175 criminalizing sexual acts between men, the city offered relative freedom for artists and the queer community, with venues like Eldorado. This period of tolerance was brutally ended by the Nazis' rise to power in 1933, leading to the destruction of Hirschfeld's institute and severe persecution.
