Scientists Discover Nearly 100 New Ancient Animal Species in Chinese Quarry

2026-01-28 15:21:40 EST

Scientists have unearthed nearly a hundred new animal species in a quarry in China, providing an extraordinary glimpse into life immediately following a mass extinction event 513 million years ago. This treasure trove of over 50,000 fossils, dubbed the Huayuan biota, includes ancient relatives of worms, sponges, jellyfish, and arthropods, many with preserved soft tissues. The discovery challenges previous understandings of the Sinsk event, suggesting some species were more resilient and widespread than thought. This significant find offers crucial insights into evolution's "big bang" and how life recovered after a cataclysmic period.
