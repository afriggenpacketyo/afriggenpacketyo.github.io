Understanding Your Obligation to Pay Debts Sold to Collection Agencies

2025-11-20 13:29:11 EST

This article explains that when a delinquent debt is sold by an original creditor to a collection agency, the borrower's legal obligation to pay typically remains intact. The new owner of the debt purchases the legal right to collect the outstanding balance. Under the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), consumers have the right to request written validation of the debt to confirm its legitimacy and ensure it is within the statute of limitations. If a borrower cannot afford to pay, options such as negotiating a settlement, working with a credit counseling agency, or seeking debt relief services may be available.
