UK and Indian Researchers Reconstruct Faces of 2,500-Year-Old Skulls from Ancient South Indian Civilization

2025-08-23 18:42:59 EDT

In a significant archaeological breakthrough, researchers from Madurai Kamaraj University in India, collaborating with Face Lab at Liverpool John Moores University in the UK, have digitally reconstructed the faces of two 2,500-year-old male skulls. These skulls were excavated from Kondagai, a burial site near Keeladi, which is now recognized as an ancient urban civilization dating back to 580BC in southern India. The facial reconstructions reveal features primarily of Ancient Ancestral South Indians, with intriguing traces of Middle-East Eurasian and Austro-Asiatic ancestries, suggesting extensive ancient migration and population mixing. This discovery challenges established narratives of Indian urbanization, highlighting an independent ancient civilization in the south and sparking broader discussions on India's diverse heritage.
