Obituary: Claudette Colvin, Unsung Pioneer of the Civil Rights Movement

2026-01-21 08:28:01 EST

Claudette Colvin, a pivotal but often unsung figure in the U.S. civil rights movement, has died at 86. At 15, nine months before Rosa Parks' iconic protest, Colvin refused to give up her bus seat to a white passenger in Montgomery, Alabama, leading to her arrest. She later became one of four plaintiffs in Browder v Gayle, the federal lawsuit that successfully challenged bus segregation. Despite her crucial role, her teenage pregnancy and perceived image led to Rosa Parks becoming the public face of the movement. Colvin eventually received more recognition, including the expungement of her juvenile record in 2021.
