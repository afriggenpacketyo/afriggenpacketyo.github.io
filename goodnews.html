Understanding Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus, the Brain Disorder Affecting Billy Joel

2025-05-23 17:51:08 EDT

Billy Joel canceled his upcoming concerts due to normal pressure hydrocephalus (NPH), a brain disorder affecting his hearing, vision, and balance. NPH involves excess cerebrospinal fluid accumulation in the brain, causing difficulty walking, bladder control issues, and memory problems. The condition is diagnosed through imaging and spinal taps, and treatment typically involves a surgery called ventricular shunting. Early diagnosis and treatment can improve symptoms, but the prognosis depends on the disease's stage and other health conditions.
