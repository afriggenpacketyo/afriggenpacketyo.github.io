One of the world's largest kelp restoration projects has successfully revived a vital marine ecosystem off the coast of Southern California by culling millions of purple sea urchins. The proliferation of urchins, caused by an imbalance from overfishing and disease affecting their natural predators, had created vast underwater 'barrens'. The successful restoration of this 'forgotten forest' provides a model for other regions and demonstrates the resilience of these critical ecosystems.
