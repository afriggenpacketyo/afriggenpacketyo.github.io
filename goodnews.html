Scientists Uncover How Epstein-Barr Virus and Genetics Trigger Multiple Sclerosis

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Scientists have made a breakthrough in understanding how the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV), carried by almost everyone, triggers multiple sclerosis (MS) in a small percentage of the population. The research reveals that in individuals with the HLA-DR15 molecule, EBV-infected B cells can mimic a protein found in nerve myelin sheaths, leading the immune system to mistakenly attack its own nerve fibers. This "fatal similarity" programs the body to attack itself, making HLA-DR15 the most significant genetic risk factor for MS. The findings offer new avenues for early intervention and targeted treatments, though a direct EBV vaccine for MS is not yet feasible.
