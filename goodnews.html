UK Government Proposes Major Housing Reform to Replace Leasehold with Commonhold System

2026-07-07 19:13:38 EDT

The UK government plans to fundamentally change flat ownership by banning new leasehold flats and replacing them with commonhold, a system where residents collectively own and manage their buildings. This reform aims to address issues like rapidly rising service charges and ground rents, which have caused significant stress for millions of leaseholders. While commonhold offers greater autonomy and is prevalent globally, its implementation faces challenges, including resistance from freeholders and concerns about homeowner disputes and mortgage lender reluctance. The transition for existing leasehold buildings, particularly regarding freeholder compensation, is expected to be complex and potentially lead to legal challenges.
