HELOCs Now Cheaper Than Home Equity Loans After Latest Fed Rate Cut

2025-11-07 15:06:22 EST

Following the Federal Reserve's fifth recent interest rate cut in October 2025, Home Equity Lines of Credit (HELOCs) have emerged as a more affordable borrowing option compared to home equity loans for a $50,000 sum. The average HELOC rate is currently 7.82%, while fixed-rate home equity loans average 8.15%-8.20% for 15-year terms, resulting in approximately $10 higher monthly payments. However, this immediate advantage comes with a crucial caveat: HELOCs feature variable interest rates that can fluctuate monthly with market conditions, unlike the stable, fixed rates of home equity loans. Borrowers are strongly advised to carefully weigh these current savings against the inherent volatility of HELOCs and consult with a financial advisor to determine the most suitable long-term borrowing strategy.
