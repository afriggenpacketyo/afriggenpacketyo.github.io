New York City is using ranked choice voting in its Democratic mayoral primary, a system where voters rank multiple candidates in order of preference. If no candidate secures a majority of first-choice votes, the candidate with the fewest first-choice votes is eliminated, and their votes are redistributed to the voters' second choices.
