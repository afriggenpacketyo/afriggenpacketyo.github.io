Unexploded Mortar Shell from 1746 Battle Unearthed at Culloden Battlefield

2026-04-24 06:00:55 EDT

Archaeologists at Culloden Battlefield in Scotland have discovered an unexploded mortar shell dating back to the bloodiest battle in the country's history, fought on April 16, 1746. The 5.5-inch shell, weighing nearly 18 pounds, is believed to have been fired by government artillery and is the first intact piece of government ordnance found at the site. Officials theorize the shell did not detonate because it landed on wet ground, extinguishing its fuse. This discovery provides fresh evidence for the deployment and actions of forces during the brief but brutal battle, which ended the last serious bid to restore the Stuart monarchy on British soil. The shell has been safely cleaned and preserved for further study.
