Study Identifies Key Conditions for Life-Saving Cash Transfer Programs

2026-02-28 08:38:25 EST

Cash transfer programs, increasingly popular globally, have shown dramatic health benefits in low- and middle-income countries but only modest gains in the US. Research identifies four crucial conditions for success: transfers must be large enough to significantly alter daily realities, remove specific health barriers, reach a substantial portion of the population, and be integrated into existing social infrastructure. US pilots often lack these elements, struggling to match high living costs or address chronic diseases with short-term boosts. The federal SNAP program, however, closely aligns with these conditions, demonstrating how effective cash assistance can be when adequately funded and integrated, offering a roadmap for broader impact.

Ancient Mass Grave Reveals Insights into 7th-Century Plague in Jordan

2026-02-28 07:32:21 EST

A study of a 7th-century mass grave in Jerash, modern-day Jordan, has provided new insights into the devastating Plague of Justinian. Genetic analysis of victims' teeth revealed they succumbed quickly to a single, highly contagious strain of Yersinia Pestis, the same bacteria as the Black Death. Researchers discovered the 230 victims, buried without ceremony in abandoned workshops, were a diverse group with ancestral ties to central Africa, eastern Europe, and Anatolia, suggesting they were mobile economic migrants or displaced persons. This finding highlights the diversity of ancient populations and how pandemics disproportionately affected vulnerable newcomers, underscoring the universal experience of such crises.
