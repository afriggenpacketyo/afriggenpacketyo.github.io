New Eye Implant and Special Glasses Restore Reading Ability for Some Legally Blind Patients

2025-10-20 16:57:17 EDT

A new clinical study published in the New England Journal of Medicine reports that a tiny eye implant combined with special glasses has helped some legally blind patients with geographic atrophy regain the ability to read. The treatment, called PRIMA, involves implanting a microchip under the retina and using glasses with a camera to project near-infrared light to it. Of 32 patients, 26 showed significant visual acuity improvement. One 70-year-old patient described it as "out of this world" to read again. While the technology is promising for the leading cause of blindness in older people, it requires concentration and is not yet widely available.
