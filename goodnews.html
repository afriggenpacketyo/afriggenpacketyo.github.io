While presidents have received turkeys as gifts since the 19th century, these were originally intended for the dinner table. The modern ceremony evolved from a confluence of events, including President Kennedy casually sparing a bird in 1963 and President Reagan first using the word "pardon" as a joke to deflect a political question. The formal tradition was officially established by President George H.W. Bush in 1989, transforming a simple gift into a national photo opportunity orchestrated by the National Turkey Federation.
