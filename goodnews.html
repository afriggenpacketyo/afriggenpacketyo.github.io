British Columbia to Permanently Adopt Year-Round Daylight Saving Time

2026-03-02 18:36:36 EST

Canada's British Columbia is set to eliminate seasonal clock changes, transitioning to year-round daylight saving time after March 8, 2026, making the upcoming change its last. Premier David Eby announced this decision, citing strong constituent feedback and health and wellness concerns, aiming to reduce disruptions for families and businesses while providing an extra hour of evening light during winter months. A 2019 public engagement report found 93% support for the change. While a small eastern region will still observe mountain time changes, the province will align with Alberta during winter and with U.S. West Coast states, which are also considering similar changes, during summer.
