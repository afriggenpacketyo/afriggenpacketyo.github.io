Ninth-Century Manuscript with Early Western Musical Notations Discovered in Pennsylvania

2025-10-28 13:14:31 EDT

Researchers have discovered what may be one of the earliest examples of written musical notation in Western history on a ninth-century manuscript leaf. The document, a Latin sacramentary likely created in Germany, contains small marks and dots above the word "alleluia" that represent instructions for the rising and falling of pitch for a singer. Previously held by a private collector, the notations were identified by historian Nathan Raab, who recognized their significance. This discovery places the manuscript among the most important early witnesses to the development of modern music, alongside famed texts like the Laon Gradual.

Expert Explains Why Debt Settlement is Generally a Better Option Than a Credit Card Charge-Off

2025-10-28 13:03:43 EDT

When dealing with overwhelming credit card debt, taking proactive steps through debt settlement is typically less damaging to one's financial health than allowing an account to be charged off by the creditor. A charge-off, which occurs after about six months of nonpayment, is a severe negative mark that remains on a credit report for seven years, signaling a complete default. In contrast, debt settlement involves negotiating to pay a reduced portion of the debt, and while it also harms one's credit score, it demonstrates responsibility and provides a clear resolution, often allowing for a faster path to financial recovery.
