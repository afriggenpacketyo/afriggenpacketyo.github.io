Scientists Unravel the Science Behind the Aurora's Dazzling Colors

2025-12-29 03:25:00 EST

The captivating aurora borealis and australis, known for their vibrant green and purple lights, are caused by solar storms ejecting electrically charged particles towards Earth's magnetic poles. These particles collide with atmospheric atoms and molecules, heating them to glow in different colors, with oxygen typically producing green and nitrogen creating purple, blue, or pink hues. While usually visible near the Arctic and Antarctic circles, a recent solar maximum has expanded their visibility to lower latitudes. Research is also exploring the potential impact of these solar energy particles on the ozone layer, with some polar regions experiencing significant, though temporary, ozone depletion.
