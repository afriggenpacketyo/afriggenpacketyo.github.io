Birthright Citizenship as a New World Ideal

2025-06-10 08:57:43 EDT

The Atlantic explores birthright citizenship, or jus solis, as a New World ideal rooted in the Americas, where nearly every country grants citizenship to children born within its territory, regardless of their parents' nationality. This contrasts with jus sanguinis, which assigns citizenship based on parental nationality, often tied to bloodline or race. The article traces the history of jus solis from its origins in Mexico's 1814 constitution to its adoption throughout Spanish America, emphasizing its connection to race and slavery politics. The piece also examines challenges to birthright citizenship in the United States and the Dominican Republic, highlighting the potential for chaos and statelessness if the U.S. were to limit or abolish it.
