Geminids Meteor Shower Peaks Tonight, Offering Spectacular Skygazing

2025-12-13 15:14:27 EST

Skywatchers are invited to look up Saturday evening to witness the peak of the annual Geminids meteor shower, renowned for its dazzling display of up to 120 meteors per hour. Visible globally, with the best views in the Northern Hemisphere, this unique shower originates from Asteroid 3200 Phaethon, an unusual "rock comet" that scientists are still studying. Unlike most meteor showers caused by comet debris, the Geminids' source is a rocky metallic mass, adding to its scientific intrigue. NASA advises that lying flat on one's back for about 30 minutes in the dark will allow eyes to adjust, providing the best opportunity to fully appreciate the celestial spectacle lasting until dawn.
