Supreme Court Hears Arguments on Legality of Late-Arriving Mail Ballots

2026-03-23 09:54:44 EDT

The U.S. Supreme Court is hearing a significant election dispute concerning whether federal law prohibits states from counting mail ballots postmarked by Election Day but received afterward. The case, Watson v. RNC, challenges Mississippi's law, which allows a five-day grace period, and could impact similar measures in 13 other states. Republicans argue that federal statutes from the 1800s require ballots to be received by Election Day, while Mississippi officials contend that states have the authority to set election rules and that an "election" concludes when voters cast their ballots. A ruling, expected months before the November midterm elections, could significantly alter voting procedures nationwide, particularly for military and overseas voters who rely on mail ballots.
