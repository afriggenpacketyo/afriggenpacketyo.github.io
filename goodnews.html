Daylight Saving Time Begins Sunday, Clocks Spring Forward

2026-03-08 00:18:54 EST

Daylight saving time for 2026 commences early Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, causing most Americans to lose an hour of sleep as clocks "spring forward." This annual shift effectively moves an hour of daylight from morning to evening, a practice first adopted in the U.S. in 1918 to conserve fuel during World War I. While its energy-saving benefits have been found to be minimal, the tradition continues, with only Hawaii and Arizona, along with several U.S. territories, not observing the change.
