Understanding Garnishment Protections for Social Security Disability Benefits

2026-05-21 14:02:25 EDT

Social Security disability benefits, encompassing both SSDI and SSI, are largely shielded from garnishment by private creditors such as credit card companies, personal loan lenders, and medical debt collectors, thanks to robust federal protections. However, these safeguards are not absolute, as certain government-owed obligations, including federal tax debt, defaulted federal student loans, child support, alimony, and specific federal restitution orders, can still lead to benefit garnishment or offset. Potential complications can also arise if protected disability income is co-mingled with non-protected funds in a bank account. For individuals grappling with debt while relying on disability income, viable relief options include debt settlement negotiations, pursuing bankruptcy, or entering a debt management plan to restructure payments.
