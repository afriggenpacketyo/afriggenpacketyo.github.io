Research Questions Efficacy of Antidepressants for Chronic Pain Relief

2025-08-27 06:31:00 EDT

A new review by University of Southampton researchers, in collaboration with Cochrane, casts doubt on the widespread efficacy of antidepressants for chronic pain. Analyzing 176 trials involving 30,000 patients and 27 different antidepressants, the study found reliable evidence for only one drug, duloxetine, and noted that amitriptyline, a commonly prescribed antidepressant for pain, lacked sufficient reliable trial data. While antidepressants can help with mood issues often linked to chronic pain, the research suggests their direct pain-relieving effects are less clear, prompting experts to advise focusing on activities that bring joy to manage the condition. Study Challenges Brain Reorganization Theory in Phantom Limb Perception

2025-08-27 06:00:00 EDT

A new study in Nature Neuroscience challenges the long-held belief that the brain dramatically reorganizes after limb amputation. Researchers used MRI scans before and after amputation in three individuals, including Emily Wheldon, who experiences vivid phantom limb sensations and pain. The findings revealed that the brain's somatosensory cortex retained the original map of the missing hand, showing no evidence of the expected reorganization where neighboring areas, like the lips, would expand. This article suggests phantom limb pain may stem from the brain still expecting signals from the lost limb, offering new insights for pain treatment and promising implications for brain-computer interfaces used to control prosthetic limbs.
