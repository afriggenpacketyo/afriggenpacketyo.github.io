The Lower Basin states of California, Arizona, and Nevada have jointly put forth a comprehensive, voluntary water-saving plan for the Colorado River, spanning the next three years. This proposal aims to conserve a substantial 3.2 million acre-feet of water through voluntary cutbacks, supplemented by an additional 700,000 acre-feet from conservation efforts and infrastructure enhancements. A key component of the plan involves creating a dedicated conservation pool to ensure federal trust obligations to Arizona's tribal communities are met. This initiative seeks to bridge the gap while the seven basin states remain at an impasse over how to manage the critically low levels of Lake Mead and Lake Powell, which are threatened by climate change and excessive usage.
