2025-05-11 06:00:13 EDT

Mathematicians from Fudan University and UCLA have solved the 65-year-old Kervaire invariant problem, a longstanding puzzle in the mathematical community. This breakthrough was achieved through major computational methods. The Kervaire invariant is a function that measures whether a smooth framed manifold can be converted into a sphere through surgery. The solution proves that manifolds of Kervaire invariant one exist in dimension 126.
