Jazz Legend Sonny Rollins, Trailblazing Saxophonist, Dies at 95

2026-05-25 22:50:23 EDT

Sonny Rollins, the iconic and pioneering tenor saxophonist, passed away at 95 in Woodstock, New York, after a career spanning over six decades. Revered for his bold tone, constant experimentation, and improvisational genius, Rollins was one of the last living greats of the bebop era and a profound influence on jazz. Despite immense success, he was known for his restless pursuit of musical perfection, taking sabbaticals and embracing new styles, including his famous practice sessions on the Williamsburg Bridge. Rollins, a recipient of multiple Grammy Awards and a Kennedy Center Honoree, leaves behind a monumental legacy as one of jazz history's most vital artists.
