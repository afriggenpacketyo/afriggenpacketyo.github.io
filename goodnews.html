Donation After Circulatory Death Revolutionizes Organ Transplantation in the US

2026-07-23 06:20:00 EDT

Organ donation in the U.S. has been transformed by the rise of donation after circulatory death (DCD), now accounting for nearly half of all deceased organ donors. DCD allows patients who are dying but not brain-dead to donate organs after life support is withdrawn and circulation ceases. This breakthrough, driven by new technologies like organ care systems and normothermic regional perfusion (NRP), has significantly increased the supply of transplantable organs, saving thousands of lives. However, DCD raises complex ethical questions about the precise moment of death and the sequence of care, prompting calls for standardized protocols to maintain public trust.
