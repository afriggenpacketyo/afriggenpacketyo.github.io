Expert Explains Why Disputing a Debt on Your Credit Report Does Not Hurt Your Score

2025-11-20 12:07:12 EST

This article clarifies that disputing a debt on your credit report does not, in itself, negatively impact your credit score. When a consumer files a dispute with a credit bureau, the item is marked as "disputed" during the investigation, a notation that does not lower the score. However, if the investigation concludes that the debt is accurate and valid, the original negative information, such as late payments or a collection status, will remain and continue to affect the score. The dispute process is a tool for correcting errors, not for erasing legitimate debt.
