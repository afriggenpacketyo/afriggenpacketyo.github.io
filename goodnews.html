2025-11-28 06:45:00 EST

Amid rising layoffs attributed to artificial intelligence, an MIT researcher offers a more complex perspective on how automation affects jobs. The article interviews Neil Thompson from MIT's CSAIL, who argues that the impact of AI depends on whether it automates high-expert or low-expert tasks within a profession. Using examples like taxi drivers and proofreaders, he explains that automating the most expert tasks can lower wages but increase employment, while automating the least expert tasks can raise wages for a smaller, more specialized workforce. He suggests that while AI will undoubtedly change many jobs, historical precedent shows that new tasks and roles emerge over time.
