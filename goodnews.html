The History and Evolution of the Presidential Turkey Pardon Tradition

2025-11-25 06:31:43 EST

The annual presidential turkey pardon has evolved from a simple gift of a Thanksgiving meal into a highly publicized White House ceremony. The tradition of sending turkeys to the president dates back to the 19th century, but the modern event was formalized by President George H.W. Bush in 1989. The practice is largely driven by the National Turkey Federation, which began presenting a turkey to President Truman in 1947. While President Kennedy was the first to spare a turkey in 1963, the term "pardon" was first used in this context by President Reagan in 1987 as a joke deflecting from the Iran-Contra scandal.
