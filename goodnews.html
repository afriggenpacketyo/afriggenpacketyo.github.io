Berlin Street Renamed After Black Enlightenment Philosopher Following Legal Battle

2025-08-23 11:12:00 EDT

After a prolonged legal battle, a prominent Berlin street, previously bearing a name with deeply racist connotations, has been officially renamed Anton-Wilhelm-Amo-Strasse. This change honors Anton Wilhelm Amo, the first Africa-born scholar to earn a European doctorate in 1734, who also studied the rights of Black people in Europe. Civil society groups had campaigned for decades to replace the original name, "Mohrenstrasse," which stemmed from the derogatory term "Moor" and its association with enslaved Africans during the 18th century. Despite resistance and lawsuits from residents, the Berlin-Brandenburg Higher Administrative Court upheld the renaming, marking a significant act of decolonization and a step towards acknowledging Black history and resilience in Germany.
