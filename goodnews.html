Strategies for Coping with Longer Winter Nights and Seasonal Affective Disorder

2025-10-24 19:09:44 EDT

As clocks go back and winter nights lengthen, particularly in northern Scotland where daylight can dwindle to under six hours, many people experience symptoms of Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD). SAD is a recurring seasonal depression, often triggered by reduced daylight, causing low mood, irritability, and a loss of energy and motivation. Experts recommend adopting "light routines," utilizing therapeutic lamps, and redesigning interior spaces to be cozy and comforting. Initiatives like Orkney's "Wintering Well" boxes, offering lamps and activity guides from libraries, aim to help communities proactively manage the mental health impacts of darker months by encouraging outdoor life and new routines.
