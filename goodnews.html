The Historic Tradition of Walking Turkeys to London for Christmas

2025-12-24 00:32:22 EST

Long before modern transportation, turkeys were historically "droved" on foot from Norfolk and Suffolk farms to London markets for Christmas, a practice that lasted for three months. This unique tradition, known as turkey droving, involved thousands of birds, some reportedly wearing tiny leather boots or having their feet coated in hot tar and sand, walking about three miles daily. The Norfolk Black breed was central to this tradition and was notably saved from extinction by the Peele family. The droves ultimately ended with the advent of steam engines and railways in the late 1800s, transitioning to train and later lorry transportation for the birds.
