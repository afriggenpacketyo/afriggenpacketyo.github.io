Countries Globally Increase Use of Cloud Seeding to Address Water Scarcity

2026-02-24 01:10:37 EST

An increasing number of nations globally, including prominent players like the US, China, France, and Saudi Arabia, are actively adopting cloud seeding, a long-standing weather modification technique. This resurgence is driven by the urgent need to augment water supplies in response to escalating global demand and the impacts of the climate crisis. The process, which involves introducing particles such as silver iodide into clouds, is estimated to increase local precipitation by 5% to 15%. Recent advancements in accurately measuring its effectiveness have revitalized interest, positioning it as a viable and more economical alternative to desalination. Despite ongoing debates concerning environmental implications and geopolitical "rain stealing" accusations, cloud seeding is emerging as a critical resource for strained water systems.
