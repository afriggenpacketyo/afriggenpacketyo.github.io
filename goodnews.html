Meteorite that Crashed Through Georgia Roof Predates Earth by 20 Million Years

2025-08-09 13:13:29 EDT

A small meteorite that tore through a homeowner's roof in McDonough, Georgia, has been analyzed and found to be approximately 4.56 billion years old, making it about 20 million years older than Earth itself. A University of Georgia geologist examined fragments of the space rock, which was part of a fireball seen across the state in June. The meteorite is believed to have originated from the breakup of a larger asteroid in the belt between Mars and Jupiter about 470 million years ago. This is the 27th meteorite ever recovered in Georgia.
