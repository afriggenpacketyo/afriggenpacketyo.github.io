Understanding the IRS Offer in Compromise Program

2025-06-20 12:35:51 EDT

The Offer in Compromise (OIC) program allows eligible taxpayers to settle their tax debt with the IRS for a lower amount than what is owed. The IRS evaluates eligibility based on a taxpayer's ability to pay, income, expenses, and asset equity, and applicants must have filed all required tax returns and made necessary estimated payments. The IRS calculates a reasonable collection potential, and the offer must equal or exceed this amount, with realistic and honest financial reporting being crucial for acceptance. Applying for an OIC requires substantial paperwork, including detailed financial statements and documentation, and the review process can take six to 24 months.
