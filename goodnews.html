Physicists Calculate Optimal Draining Times for Everyday Liquids

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Two physicists at Brown University, Thomas Dutta and Jay Tang, investigated the optimal waiting time required to drain liquids from various containers, addressing common kitchen frustrations. Their research, published in Physics of Fluids, utilized Navier-Stokes equations and practical experiments, revealing that water drains in seconds, milk in about 30 seconds, olive oil in over nine minutes, and cold maple syrup can take several hours. The study, which was honored with a German award for "ludic science," demonstrates how everyday observations can inspire rigorous scientific inquiry. Their findings suggest that greater patience is often needed to extract the maximum amount of more viscous liquids.
