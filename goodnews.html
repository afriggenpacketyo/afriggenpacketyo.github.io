Mutual Funds vs. ETFs: Understanding the Differences and Their Roles in a Portfolio

2025-06-12 10:46:07 EDT

Exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and mutual funds are common investment vehicles for diversified portfolios, with both actively and passively managed options. ETFs trade like individual stocks with fluctuating prices during trading hours, while mutual funds are purchased and sold directly at the end-of-day market price. ETFs typically have lower expense ratios than mutual funds due to simpler transaction management. Investors should consult a fund's prospectus to understand its objectives, risks, fees, and performance history.
