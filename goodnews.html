Geographic Distortions on Common Maps Misrepresent Greenland's True Size

2026-01-07 14:01:59 EST

President Trump's renewed interest in acquiring Greenland has drawn attention to how its size is often misrepresented on common world maps. The Mercator projection, a widely used map type, significantly distorts the actual sizes of landmasses further from the equator, making Greenland appear roughly equivalent to Africa in size, despite Africa being 14 times larger. Geographic experts explain that these distortions are inherent in depicting a spherical globe on a two-dimensional surface. The article emphasizes that map projections are chosen based on their specific purpose, and many alternatives offer more accurate representations of relative land size.
