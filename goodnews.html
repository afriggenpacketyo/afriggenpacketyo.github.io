Understanding Required Minimum Distributions from Retirement Accounts

2026-05-05 14:08:58 EDT

Retirees with tax-deferred accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s must understand Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs), which typically begin at age 73. The amount, calculated based on account balance and IRS life expectancy factors, increases over time, potentially impacting tax brackets and Medicare premiums. For a $500,000 account, annual withdrawals can range from approximately $18,868 at age 73 to $24,752 at age 80. Failing to take RMDs incurs a penalty of up to 25% of the missed amount.
