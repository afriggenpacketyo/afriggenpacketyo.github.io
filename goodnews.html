Claudette Colvin, a foundational figure in the early civil rights movement, has passed away at the age of 86. Nine months prior to Rosa Parks' renowned act, a 15-year-old Colvin was arrested in Montgomery, Alabama, in March 1955 for her refusal to yield her bus seat to a white passenger. Her courageous act of defiance, driven by a deep conviction for freedom, played a crucial role in initiating the Montgomery Bus Boycott and the broader civil rights movement. Although her bravery was frequently overshadowed, Colvin served as one of the four plaintiffs in the landmark lawsuit that ultimately outlawed racial segregation on Montgomery's buses, leaving behind an inspiring and enduring legacy.
