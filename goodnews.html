The project, a collaboration between the University of Miami, the Florida Aquarium, and Tela Coral, involves crossbreeding resilient Elkhorn corals from the warmer waters of Honduras with local Florida corals. The goal is to create a more thermally tolerant coral population capable of surviving rising ocean temperatures. This marks the first time international crossbreeding of corals has been permitted for restoration on wild reefs, offering a potential blueprint for reef conservation across the Caribbean.
