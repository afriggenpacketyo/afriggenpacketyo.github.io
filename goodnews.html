2025-09-07 19:13:39 EDT

Stargazers across Asia, Europe, and Africa witnessed a spectacular "Blood Moon" during a total lunar eclipse on Sunday night. This phenomenon occurs when the Earth aligns between the sun and moon, casting a shadow that makes the moon appear an eerie deep red. The red hue results from sunlight scattering through Earth's atmosphere, with shorter blue wavelengths dispersing more easily, a process similar to how sunsets appear red. This celestial event, which has captivated humanity for millennia, provided a stunning visual display for observers in various parts of the world, reminding us of the wonders of the cosmos.
