Retailers Employ 'Tariff Hacking' Strategy to Mitigate Trade War Costs

2025-09-19 10:38:17 EDT

In response to increased tariffs from the ongoing trade war, retailers are adopting a B2B2C (business-to-business-to-consumer) model to reduce costs for consumers. This strategy involves routing online sales through a third-party "merchant of record," which purchases the goods at a wholesale price and pays the U.S. tariff on that lower value, rather than the full retail price. Companies using this method report significant savings, allowing them to avoid passing the full tariff costs onto customers and maintain competitive pricing. However, some logistics experts warn this "tariff arbitrage" may not be a scalable long-term solution and could attract future regulatory scrutiny.
