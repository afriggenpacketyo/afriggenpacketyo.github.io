Study Reveals Potato Evolved from an Ancient Tomato Ancestor Through Hybridization

2025-07-31 21:07:58 EDT

A new study published in the journal Cell has uncovered that the modern potato evolved from an ancient tomato ancestor approximately 9 million years ago. Researchers solved the long-standing puzzle of the potato's origin by analyzing hundreds of potato genomes. They found that a hybridization event between a wild, non-tuber-producing potato species and an ancient tomato species provided the crucial genetic combination necessary for tuber formation. This discovery demonstrates how interbreeding between different species can lead to the evolution of entirely new traits and the emergence of new species, providing a fascinating insight into plant evolution.
