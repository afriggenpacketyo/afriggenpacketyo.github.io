An Explainer on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale Categories

2025-08-18 08:30:01 EDT

The Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale classifies hurricanes into five categories based on their sustained wind speeds, providing an estimate of potential property damage. A Category 1 storm features winds of 74-95 mph causing "some damage," while a Category 5 storm has winds of 157 mph or higher and is capable of causing "catastrophic damage." Storms rated as Category 3 or higher are designated as "major" hurricanes due to their potential for significant loss of life. This article breaks down the characteristics of each category, citing historical examples like Hurricane Katrina (Category 3 at landfall) and Hurricane Andrew (Category 5) to illustrate their destructive power.
