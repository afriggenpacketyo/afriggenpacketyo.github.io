Comparing the Credit Score Impact of a Charge-Off Versus Debt Settlement

2025-10-28 13:03:43 EDT

When dealing with unpaid credit card debt, a charge-off is generally more damaging to a person's credit score than a debt settlement. A charge-off occurs when a creditor gives up on collecting a debt after about six months of nonpayment, marking it as a significant default that remains on a credit report for seven years. In contrast, a debt settlement is a proactive negotiation to pay a reduced portion of the debt, which, while still negative, shows a responsible effort to resolve the obligation. This key difference in control over the outcome can significantly affect the timeline for financial recovery. (not investment advice)
