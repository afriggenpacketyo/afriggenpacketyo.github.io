Grunion Fish Begin Annual Spawning Runs on California Beaches

2026-07-14 18:11:20 EDT

The unique natural phenomenon of grunion fish spawning is underway on California beaches, occurring for four-day periods from March to August around new and full moons. During these "grunion runs," small silver fish ride waves ashore, with females burying themselves in the sand to lay eggs while males wrap around them to fertilize. This mysterious night-time ritual is a summer tradition for many, with sightings reported from La Jolla to Ventura, and the second and third nights of the run typically offer the best viewing opportunities. Conservation efforts are in place, prohibiting fishing during peak spawning months and limiting catches to protect this cryptic species.
