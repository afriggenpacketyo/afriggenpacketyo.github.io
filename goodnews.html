Behavioral economist and Nobel laureate Richard Thaler explains the concept of "The Winner's Curse," a phenomenon where the winner of a competitive auction often ends up overpaying for an item. The theory, originally observed in oil companies bidding for drilling rights, suggests that the winner is frequently the person who most overestimates an item's value. This curse has been observed in various markets, from book publishing deals to corporate acquisitions and professional sports drafts. To avoid it, Thaler advises bidders to consider if they would still be happy winning the auction, knowing that everyone else valued the item less.
