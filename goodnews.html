Australia Expands Youth Social Media Ban to Include YouTube

2025-07-29 21:24:49 EDT

The Australian government has reversed an earlier decision and will now include YouTube in its landmark social media ban for children under the age of 16. The policy, set to begin in December, will prevent teenagers from holding YouTube accounts, which are necessary for uploading content and interacting. This change followed a recommendation from the eSafety Commissioner, who highlighted that YouTube was the most cited platform where children encountered harmful content. The government affirmed its commitment to protecting children from 'predatory algorithms,' despite YouTube's argument that it is a video-sharing platform, not social media, and potential legal threats.
