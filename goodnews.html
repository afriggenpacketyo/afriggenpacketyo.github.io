A key consumer protection for federally backed reverse mortgages, known as the "95% rule," can significantly impact a family's ability to retain a home. This rule stipulates that if heirs wish to keep the property after the borrower passes away or moves out, they only need to pay the lesser of the full loan balance or 95% of the home's current appraised value. This is crucial because accrued interest can cause the loan balance to exceed the home's worth. The rule ensures heirs are not forced to pay more than the property's value, providing peace of mind and predictability in estate planning. (not investment advice)
