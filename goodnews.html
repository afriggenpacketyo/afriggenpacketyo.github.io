Clarifying Terms: Endemic, Epidemic, and Pandemic in Disease Spread

2026-05-11 06:31:00 EDT

The article clarifies the distinct epidemiological meanings of endemic, epidemic, and pandemic, terms frequently misused but crucial for understanding disease spread. Endemic describes a disease regularly occurring in a region with constant case numbers, like malaria, not necessarily harmless. An epidemic signifies an unusual increase in cases within a specific region and time, often due to pathogen mutation or new introduction. A pandemic denotes a disease spreading across countries and continents, requiring international cooperation for control, with the danger depending on the specific virus and individual health.
