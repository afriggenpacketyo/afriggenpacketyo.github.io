Companies Use 'Tariff Engineering' to Avoid Higher Duties

2025-06-18 23:15:04 EDT

Faced with rising tariffs, businesses are increasingly using "tariff engineering" to lower duty costs by altering product materials, dimensions, or compositions. This legal practice involves reclassifying products under different "harmonized system codes" to qualify for lower tariff rates. Examples include manufacturers bundling steel and aluminum elements to qualify for lower duties, and clothing companies modifying designs to fit into different categories. The Supreme Court has previously ruled such practices legal, as long as goods are honestly presented to customs officers.
