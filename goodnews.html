Total Lunar Eclipse Creates 'Blood Moon' Visible Across Asia, Europe, and Africa

2025-09-07 19:13:39 EDT

A total lunar eclipse on Sunday created a spectacular "Blood Moon," captivating stargazers across large parts of the Eastern Hemisphere. The phenomenon, where the Earth passes directly between the sun and moon, casts a shadow that gives the lunar surface a distinct reddish hue. The full eclipse was best viewed in Asia, with partial views available in eastern Africa and western Australia. The red color results from sunlight scattering through Earth's atmosphere, where shorter blue wavelengths are dispersed, allowing longer red wavelengths to reach the moon and reflect back to observers on the ground.
