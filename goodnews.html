Red Wine Headache Mystery: Researchers Investigate Potential Causes

2025-05-09 07:01:16 EDT

Researchers are investigating why red wine is more likely to cause headaches compared to white wine, focusing on a flavanol called quercetin. Quercetin, an antioxidant found in higher concentrations in red wines, can interfere with alcohol metabolism, leading to an accumulation of acetaldehyde, a toxin that causes headaches and nausea. The study also notes that individuals of East Asian descent may be more susceptible due to an enzyme variant that hinders acetaldehyde elimination. The amount of quercetin in wine can vary based on grape-growing methods and winemaking processes.
