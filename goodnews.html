Understanding What Constitutes Harassment by Debt Collectors Under Federal Law

The Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) clearly defines specific actions that unequivocally qualify as harassment by debt collectors, empowering consumers with crucial knowledge to protect themselves. Prohibited behaviors under the FDCPA include repeated or excessive phone calls made with intent to annoy, contacting individuals at inappropriate times (before 8 a.m. or after 9 p.m., or at work if forbidden), using threats of violence or illegal actions, and employing obscene or abusive language. Additionally, publicly exposing a person's debt or continuing contact after a formal written cease-communication request are strictly unlawful. Documenting all interactions and filing complaints with regulatory bodies like the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) are crucial first steps if harassment occurs. (not investment advice)
