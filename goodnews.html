Letters of Credit Aid Chinese Exporters Amid Trade War

2025-06-13 02:00:17 EDT

In response to reciprocal tariffs imposed by the United States on imports from China, Beijing has taken steps to assist Chinese exporters in shifting their focus to the domestic market. One strategy involves encouraging the increased use of letters of credit (L/Cs) to ensure payment security and enhance credit for firms transitioning to domestic sales. Domestic L/Cs, issued by major Chinese banks, function similarly to international L/Cs, guaranteeing payment for goods and services within a one-year period. Unlike international L/Cs, domestic L/Cs can be used for both physical goods and services transactions, providing flexibility for Chinese businesses adapting to the trade war.
