The plan involves breeding billions of male flies, sterilizing them with radiation, and releasing them from aircraft over Mexico and southern Texas. These sterile males then mate with wild females, whose eggs subsequently fail to hatch, causing the population to collapse over time. This sterile insect technique was used to eradicate the pest from the U.S. decades ago and is being reinstated after the fly reappeared in southern Mexico, posing a significant threat to the American beef industry.
