Scientists Use Environmental DNA to Track Endangered Axolotl Salamanders in Mexico City

2025-08-11 19:37:29 EDT

Researchers from Mexico’s National Autonomous University are using environmental DNA (eDNA) to search for the critically endangered axolotl salamander in Xochimilco's canals. This method involves filtering water for genetic particles, which is more effective than traditional netting as the population has drastically declined from 6,000 per square kilometer in 1998 to just 36 in 2014. While traces of DNA were found primarily in protected refuge areas, their presence offers a slight hope for the species' resilience. Scientists emphasize that conserving the axolotl's habitat is crucial for its survival and brings broader ecological benefits to the region.
