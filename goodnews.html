Fossil Find in China Suggests Ancient Flying Pterosaur Had a Herbivorous Diet

2025-07-20 06:00:14 EDT

A rare fossil discovery in northeastern China is challenging previous assumptions about the diet of pterosaurs, the flying reptiles that lived alongside dinosaurs. A team of paleontologists found the stomach contents of a Sinopterus atavismus, a type of pterosaur, preserved for the first time. The stomach contained phytoliths, which are microscopic mineral deposits from plants, as well as small quartz crystals likely used as gastroliths to help grind tough vegetation. This evidence strongly suggests that this species was herbivorous, rewriting the menu for these ancient creatures and providing new insights into the ecosystems they inhabited millions of years ago.
