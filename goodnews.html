NASA's Van Allen Probe A Successfully Re-enters Earth's Atmosphere After Seven-Year Mission

2026-03-11 18:20:47 EDT

A 1,300-pound NASA Van Allen Probe A successfully re-entered Earth's atmosphere over the eastern Pacific Ocean, nearly 14 years after its launch in August 2012. The probe, along with its twin, was designed to explore Earth's permanent radiation belts, known as the Van Allen belts, and exceeded its two-year mission by almost five years. It made significant discoveries, including the existence of a third radiation belt. Most of the spacecraft was expected to burn up during re-entry, with NASA assessing a low 1 in 4,200 chance of harm to anyone on Earth.
