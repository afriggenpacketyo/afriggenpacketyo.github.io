Six-Year-Old Girl's Sight Restored Through Pioneering Gene Therapy

2026-04-22 15:31:35 EDT

A six-year-old girl, Saffie Sandford, diagnosed with the rare inherited condition Leber's Congenital Amaurosis (LCA), has experienced a life-changing restoration of her sight in the dark thanks to pioneering Luxturna gene therapy. The treatment, administered at Great Ormond Street Hospital, involved injecting a healthy copy of the gene directly into each eye. Her mother described the outcome as if "someone waved a magic wand," preventing the potential blindness by age 30 that Saffie faced. Scientists emphasize that while not a complete cure, the therapy significantly improves sight and strengthens visual pathways, especially when delivered during critical periods of brain development in young children.
