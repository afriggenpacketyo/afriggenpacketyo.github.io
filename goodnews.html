Exploring the American Origins of the Globally Understood Word "OK"

2025-07-23 17:27:00 EDT

The word "OK" is one of the most universally recognized terms, originating as a joke in 19th-century America. Scholars trace its first appearance to an 1839 Boston newspaper, where it was used as a playful, misspelled abbreviation for "oll korrect" (all correct). The term gained widespread popularity during President Martin Van Buren's 1840 reelection campaign, as his nickname "Old Kinderhook" aligned with the "O.K." initials. Its simplicity and versatility helped it spread globally, especially with the rise of telegraphy and its use by American soldiers abroad.

Explainer: How "Corn Sweat" Contributes to Dangerous Humidity During Midwest Heat Waves

2025-07-23 16:50:00 EDT

"Corn sweat" is the informal term for the moisture released by vast fields of corn through a process called evapotranspiration, which significantly increases humidity during hot, sunny weather. While a natural process for plants to cool themselves, the immense scale of corn cultivation in the Midwest can make heat waves feel more oppressive and dangerous, raising the heat index. This heightened humidity can offset the natural cooling effect of transpiration and prevent temperatures from dropping at night, increasing the risk of heat-related illnesses for vulnerable populations.
