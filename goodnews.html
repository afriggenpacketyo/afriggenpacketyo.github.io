Analysis Explores the Evolving and Politicized Use of the Term 'Ideology'

The word "ideology" has become a common tool in modern political discourse, often used pejoratively to dismiss an opponent's beliefs as extreme or baseless. Originally coined during the French Enlightenment as a neutral term for the scientific study of ideas, its meaning has shifted dramatically over time. Napoleon first weaponized it to attack his political rivals, and Karl Marx later used it to describe a "false consciousness" that served the ruling class. Today, political scientists note that both conservatives and liberals use the term as a form of name-calling to critique worldviews that challenge their own.
