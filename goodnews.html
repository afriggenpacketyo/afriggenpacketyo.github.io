Economist Bill Phillips Modeled the Economy Using Flowing Water

2026-04-07 06:30:00 EDT

Bill Phillips, the renowned economist behind the Phillips Curve, adopted a remarkably creative approach to economic study by constructing a physical model of the British economy in 1949. His elaborate Rube Goldberg-like machine utilized flowing water between various tubs and chambers to represent economic flows, such as government spending and taxation. This innovative demonstration at the London School of Economics initially met with skepticism but ultimately proved Phillips' profound understanding, leading to a faculty position. His later work with 100 years of UK data revealed the inverse relationship between unemployment and inflation, forming the basis of the influential Phillips Curve.
