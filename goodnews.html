Physics Study Reveals Optimal Waiting Times for Draining Liquids in Kitchens

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Two physicists from Brown University have calculated the optimal waiting times required for liquids like milk, olive oil, and water to drain from kitchen containers and cookware. Their research, published in Physics of Fluids, uses fluid mechanics equations to determine when approximately 90% of a liquid has drained. The study found that water drains quickly, while thicker liquids like olive oil and maple syrup require significantly longer waiting periods. For instance, it takes about 15 minutes for most residual water to collect at the bottom of a wok after washing, suggesting a need for greater patience in everyday kitchen tasks.
