Cyclosporiasis: Understanding the Parasitic Illness Linked to Contaminated Produce and Water

2026-07-09 06:00:03 EDT

Cyclosporiasis is a gastrointestinal illness caused by the cyclospora parasite, typically transmitted through raw produce and water contaminated with human feces. In the U.S., it's a seasonal concern, with outbreaks frequently occurring during summer months, often linked to items like berries, leafy greens, and herbs. Symptoms include watery and sometimes explosive diarrhea, cramps, and fatigue, which can persist for days to over a month. While usually not life-threatening and often self-resolving, thorough washing of produce is recommended, though it may not entirely eliminate infection risk.
