Benin Transforms Cotton Industry with Infrastructure Development and Chinese Collaboration

2026-06-21 02:00:05 EDT

Benin, Africa’s largest cotton producer, is actively reshaping its cotton industry by developing crucial infrastructure to support local processing rather than raw exports. A new 184km road connects the northern cotton-growing regions to the Glo-Djigbé Industrial Zone (GDIZ) near Cotonou, where a fifth of the national harvest is now processed into finished clothing for global brands. This initiative is part of the government's strategy to enhance value addition within the country, supported by China's assistance in these developmental projects. The Finance Minister views this road project as a logical investment given Banikoara's significant contribution to Benin's total cotton production.
