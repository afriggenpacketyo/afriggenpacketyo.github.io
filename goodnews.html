Stillaguamish Tribe Restores Wetlands to Revive Salmon Populations and Mitigate Floods

2026-05-03 05:00:00 EDT

The Stillaguamish Tribe in Washington is actively buying farmland and removing levees to restore thousands of acres of tidal wetlands, aiming to revive endangered Chinook salmon populations. This initiative, exemplified by the zis a ba 2 marsh, reconnects the Stillaguamish River to its floodplain for the first time in over a century, providing crucial salmon nurseries. While restoring natural habitats, the project also creates new, taller levees to protect nearby communities from increasing flood risks, demonstrating a balanced approach to ecological and human well-being. The article highlights the tribe's long-term commitment to restoring a vital resource central to their way of life.
