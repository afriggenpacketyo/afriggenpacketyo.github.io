2025-07-01 09:00:00 EDT

A new biography examines the career of Claire McCardell, a revolutionary American fashion designer from the mid-20th century whose influence remains widespread yet often uncredited. McCardell championed comfortable, practical clothing for women, introducing innovations like pockets, side fasteners, ballet flats, and denim for womenswear. She rejected the restrictive European styles of the era, focusing instead on an "American Look" that prioritized freedom of movement and ease of wear for active women. Though a household name in her time, her legacy is less prominent than contemporaries like Christian Dior, yet her design philosophy is embedded in modern everyday apparel.
