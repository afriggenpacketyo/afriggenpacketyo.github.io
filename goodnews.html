Lyrid Meteor Shower Peaks This Week, Offering Bright Celestial Display

2026-04-15 04:28:55 EDT

The Lyrid meteor shower, recognized as the oldest recorded shower, is set to brilliantly illuminate the skies this week, reaching its peak on April 22. This annual celestial event is caused by Earth passing through the dusty debris left behind by Comet Thatcher centuries ago, offering observers a chance to witness 10 to 15 meteors per hour, with occasional spectacular surges delivering up to 100. Known for their vibrant colors and exceptional brightness, the Lyrids sometimes produce exceptionally bright fireballs that can even outshine Venus. First recorded by Chinese astronomers in 687 BCE, this enduring shower provides a captivating display of cosmic beauty each year.
