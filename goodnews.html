Scientists Discover Nearly 100 New Ancient Animal Species in China After Mass Extinction Event

2026-01-28 15:21:40 EST

Scientists have made an extraordinary discovery in a small quarry in China, unearthing almost a hundred new animal species that survived a mass extinction event half a billion years ago. This treasure trove of over 50,000 fossil specimens, including ancient worms, sponges, jellyfish, and various arthropods, offers a rare and invaluable glimpse into life immediately following the Sinsk event, which ended the Cambrian explosion. Many fossils notably preserve soft parts like gills, guts, and eyes. Dubbed the Huayuan biota, this finding provides crucial insights into how life recovered and spread across the world after such a cataclysmic evolutionary bottleneck.
