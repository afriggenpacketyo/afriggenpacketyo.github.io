Understanding Required Minimum Distributions from a $100,000 Retirement Account

2026-05-11 12:05:08 EDT

This article explains Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) from retirement accounts, which federal law mandates retirees begin withdrawing at age 73. The annual RMD is calculated by dividing the account balance by an IRS-provided life expectancy factor, meaning the withdrawal amount increases with age. For a $100,000 account, annual RMDs range from $3,774 at age 73 to $5,650 at age 83, with these distributions being subject to taxes.
