Identifying and Managing Excessive Credit Card Debt

2025-09-30 11:48:23 EDT

Excessive credit card debt is not defined by a specific dollar amount but rather by its impact on an individual's financial health. Key indicators include a debt-to-income ratio for credit cards exceeding 10%, a credit utilization ratio above 30%, and the inability to pay more than the minimum monthly payment. If debt is causing significant stress or preventing other financial goals, it is considered excessive. To manage this, strategies like the debt snowball or avalanche methods, balance transfers to 0% APR cards, and debt consolidation loans can be effective in regaining control over finances.
