Real ID Enforcement Deadline Approaches for Domestic Air Travel

2025-05-06 17:43:06 EDT

Starting May 7, travelers will need a Real ID or another approved form of identification, such as a passport, to board domestic flights, as the U.S. government enforces the Real ID Act after years of postponed deadlines. The Real ID Act sets minimum standards for state-issued documentation to enhance security, requiring individuals to provide a Social Security number, verify their address, and prove their identity and lawful status. While a Real ID is sufficient for domestic flights, it will not allow travelers to cross international borders, and those without compliant identification may still be allowed to fly after additional identity verification.
