China to activate world's first accelerator-driven nuclear reactor for energy and waste treatment

2026-03-05 22:59:33 EST

China is preparing to activate the world's first megawatt-level accelerator-driven subcritical system (ADS) nuclear reactor in Guangdong province next year. Designed by the Chinese Academy of Sciences, this advanced reactor promises to generate energy while also transmuting long-lived radioactive waste into shorter-lived and less hazardous isotopes. Researchers hail it as an "internationally recognised ideal approach" for nuclear fuel breeding and waste treatment, potentially providing a "green, safe, stable energy source for 1,000 years." This development marks a significant step in nuclear technology, as no commercial ADS systems currently operate globally, with China's project leading experimental efforts to install its superconducting particle accelerators this year.

Civil rights leader Bernard LaFayette, Selma voting rights organizer, dies at 85

2026-03-05 22:45:33 EST

Bernard LaFayette, a pivotal civil rights leader and key organizer of the Selma voting rights campaign, has died at 85 from a heart attack. LaFayette's quiet groundwork in Selma, detailed in his memoir "In Peace and Freedom," was instrumental in setting the stage for the Voting Rights Act of 1965, two years before "Bloody Sunday." A co-founder of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee, he faced numerous dangers, including an assassination attempt, but remained committed to nonviolence. LaFayette dedicated his life to institutionalizing and internationalizing the nonviolence movement, a mission he adopted after Martin Luther King Jr.'s assassination, leaving a profound legacy in America and abroad.
