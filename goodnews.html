Understanding Home Ownership When Utilizing a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

A reverse mortgage allows homeowners aged 62 and older to convert home equity into cash without monthly payments, raising questions about who retains ownership of the property. Homeowners who take out a reverse mortgage remain the legal owners and keep the title to their house; the lender only places a lien on the property to secure the loan. The loan, plus interest, becomes due when the borrower sells the home, moves out, or passes away, at which point the heirs can choose to repay the loan to keep the home or sell it.
