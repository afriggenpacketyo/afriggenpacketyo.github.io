NASA's Perseverance Rover Finds Strongest Hints of Ancient Life on Mars

2025-09-10 22:48:54 EDT

NASA's Mars rover Perseverance has uncovered rocks in a dry river channel that may contain the strongest hints yet of ancient microscopic life. Scientists emphasize that in-depth analysis on Earth is needed before conclusive findings, but the 25th sample gathered from the Neretva Vallis mudstones showed organic carbon and minuscule specks enriched with iron phosphate and iron sulfide. On Earth, these compounds are byproducts of microorganisms consuming organic matter. While non-biological processes could also be responsible, researchers consider this the most compelling candidate in the rover's search for long-ago life, despite delays in the sample return mission.
