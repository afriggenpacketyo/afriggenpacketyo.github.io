2025-05-21 07:21:42 EDT

The Supreme Court has affirmed the legal right to wild camping on Dartmoor, rejecting an appeal by landowners seeking to restrict public access. The ruling centers on the interpretation of the 1985 Dartmoor Commons Act, which grants the public the right to open-air recreation. Judges stated that this right naturally includes camping. The decision is expected to reignite the debate over public access to land in England. Dartmoor National Park covers a 368-square-mile area and is a rare exception to the general lack of wild camping rights on private land in England.
