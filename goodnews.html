Army Spouse Julie Moore Remembered for Changing Military Protocol

2025-05-25 12:27:15 EDT

Julie Moore, an Army spouse, is remembered for her efforts to change military protocol regarding casualty notifications, ensuring that families receive personal support rather than telegrams. During the Vietnam War, Moore took it upon herself to comfort Army wives, working with the local Western Union office to provide support when telegrams arrived. Her advocacy led to the Army implementing a system where service members personally notify families and offer assistance. Moore's legacy endures, with service members now providing comfort and support to grieving families, and a base being renamed in her honor.
