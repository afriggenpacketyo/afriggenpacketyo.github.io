Understanding Debt Collection on Charged-Off Accounts

2025-11-04 14:33:16 EST

Many borrowers mistakenly believe a "charged-off" account means their debt is forgiven, but creditors can still actively collect on it. A charge-off, typically occurring after 180 days of nonpayment, is an internal accounting adjustment for tax purposes and does not cancel the borrower's legal obligation. Original creditors may continue collection efforts directly or sell the debt to third-party agencies, which can then sue within the state's statute of limitations, potentially leading to severe consequences like wage garnishment or property liens. Options for borrowers include negotiating a settlement, requesting a "pay-for-delete" agreement to remove the mark from credit reports, or exploring debt management programs and professional debt relief.
