Experts Question the Necessity of Electrolyte Drinks for General Hydration

2025-08-04 05:00:00 EDT

The market for sports drinks and electrolyte powders has expanded beyond athletes into a multi-billion dollar wellness industry, but medical experts question their necessity for the average person. While these products do replenish minerals like sodium and potassium lost through sweat, most people get sufficient electrolytes from a balanced diet. The body, particularly the kidneys, is highly effective at regulating electrolyte levels, excreting any excess. Experts suggest that for most individuals, drinking plain water and eating a varied diet is adequate for hydration, and these costly supplements are only truly beneficial for those engaged in prolonged, intense sweating or suffering from severe dehydration due to illness.
