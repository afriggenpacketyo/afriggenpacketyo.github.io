2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

Chinese researchers have published findings in Science Advances detailing how birds shed their dinosaur tails step by step, revealing the evolutionary assembly of the flight-adapted bird body plan. Modern birds possess a pygostyle, a short skeletal structure formed by fused tail vertebrae, which serves as an anchor for tail feathers and muscles, crucial for aerodynamics. Studying this transformation has historically been challenging due to the scarcity of early-diverging bird and bird-like dinosaur fossils. This significant discovery sheds new light on a profound morphological change in terrestrial vertebrate history.
