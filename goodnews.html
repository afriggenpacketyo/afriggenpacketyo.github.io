Understanding and Mitigating the Urban Heat Island Effect in Cities

2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

Urban heat islands are metropolitan areas significantly hotter than their rural surroundings, primarily due to heat absorption by concrete and asphalt, lack of vegetation, and trapped air from tall buildings. This phenomenon intensifies heatwaves, posing risks to urban populations and exacerbating climate change through increased energy demands for cooling. Solutions include greening cities with trees and parks, implementing "cool roofs" and pavements that reflect sunlight, and adopting practices like water spraying to lower temperatures and promote environmental stability.
