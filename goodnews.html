The Etymology of 'Algorithm' Traces Back to a 9th-Century Persian Mathematician

2026-06-11 05:00:00 EDT

The word 'algorithm' originates from the Latinized name of the 9th-century Persian mathematician Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi. His influential work introduced algorithmic methods, popularized Hindu-Arabic numerals in the West, and laid the groundwork for algebra. The term evolved through French and English, becoming 'algorism' before its current form. Algorithms, essentially well-defined sets of instructions, have been crucial for complex tasks in mathematics, navigation, computing, and even space exploration, underscoring their historical significance beyond their modern association with AI.
