Orionid Meteor Shower Peaks Next Week, Offering Celestial Spectacle

2025-10-17 19:58:22 EDT

The annual Orionid meteor shower is set to peak around October 22, offering a spectacular celestial display for skygazers across the globe. Visible between now and November 7, these bright, fast-moving meteors originate from debris left by Halley's Comet, burning up in Earth's atmosphere at speeds of about 41 miles per second and leaving distinctive light trails. For optimal viewing conditions, observers should find a dark spot away from city lights between midnight and dawn, looking towards the constellation Orion, where up to 15 meteors per hour might be visible to the naked eye without the need for binoculars or telescopes.
