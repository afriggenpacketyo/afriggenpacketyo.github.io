CDs and Money Market Accounts: Interest Earnings Compared

2026-04-22 12:15:12 EDT

When considering where to deposit $18,000, both Certificates of Deposit (CDs) and money market accounts offer competitive interest rates. For shorter terms (3 months), the interest earnings are identical. However, for longer terms (6 months to 1 year), CDs generally yield slightly more than money market accounts, assuming rates remain constant. CDs offer guaranteed returns due to their fixed rates, while money market accounts have variable rates that could increase. The primary trade-off is access to funds, as CDs typically lock up money for the term, while money market accounts offer greater liquidity.
