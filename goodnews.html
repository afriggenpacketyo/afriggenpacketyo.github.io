Physicists Unravel the Science Behind Getting the Last Drop from Bottles

2026-03-21 12:48:00 EDT

Physicists at Brown University have applied the principles of fluid mechanics to answer an everyday kitchen question: how long it takes to extract the last drop from bottles. Inspired by common frustrations, such as emptying milk cartons or efficiently draining a cast-iron wok, researchers Thomas Dutta and Jay Tang used Navier-Stokes equations and conducted experiments to calculate the optimal drainage times for various liquids. Their findings revealed significant differences: low-viscosity liquids like milk drain in about 30 seconds, thicker olive oil takes over nine minutes, and cold maple syrup can take several hours. This "ludic science" approach, which probes quirky, real-world questions, highlights how scientific curiosity can yield practical and surprising insights into our daily lives.
