Fact Check: Examining the Claim That New Bill Eliminates Social Security Taxes

2025-07-08 18:01:41 EDT

A new bill praised by the Trump administration does not eliminate federal income taxes on Social Security benefits, contrary to some claims. Instead, the legislation introduces a new, temporary tax deduction of up to $6,000 for seniors over 65 who fall below certain income thresholds. Policy experts note this deduction applies to a senior's total income, not just their benefits, and will not help low-income seniors who already pay no federal income tax. The measure primarily benefits higher-income seniors and is set to expire in 2028, making the claim of eliminating the tax misleading.
