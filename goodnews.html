Berlin Hauptbahnhof Celebrates 20 Years as a Symbol of German Reunification

2026-05-26 02:57:00 EDT

Berlin Hauptbahnhof, Germany's central train station, marks its 20th anniversary as a symbol of German reunification and a testament to modern architectural achievement. Opened on May 26, 2006, the station was built on a site that was once part of the Berlin Wall's no-man's land, physically and symbolically reconnecting the divided city. Designed as a "cathedral of transport," its multi-level structure and use of steel and glass were intended to embody the spirit of a reunified Germany. Despite some modifications to the original design due to cost-saving measures, the station has become one of Berlin's most iconic landmarks, handling approximately 1,800 trains and over 300,000 passengers daily.
