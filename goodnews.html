European Union Court Bans 'Golden Passport' Programs

2025-05-04 00:32:00 EDT

The European Court of Justice (ECJ) has outlawed Malta's "golden passport" program, the last of its kind in the EU, which offered citizenship to foreign investors. The court ruled that such programs reduce nationality to a "mere commercial transaction" and undermine the solidarity and mutual trust between member states. While Malta's Prime Minister defended the scheme, saying it had raised millions for charitable causes, he said the government would rework the system to align with the court's decision. Although purchased passports are now prohibited, "golden visas," which offer residence permits for investments, remain a point of concern, with Transparency International advocating for increased due diligence and background checks.
