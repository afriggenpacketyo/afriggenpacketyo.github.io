The planet, slightly larger than Saturn but with half its mass, orbits its star in just over three days, making it the smallest star known to host a gas giant. Scientists are puzzled by how such a small star could form and host such a large planet, as it contradicts the core accretion theory, which suggests low-mass stars lack sufficient gas and dust to build planetary cores.
