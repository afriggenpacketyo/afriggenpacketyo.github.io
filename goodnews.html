New Research Links Abdominal Muscle Contractions to Brain Fluid Movement and Health

2026-05-07 19:04:50 EDT

New research suggests that coordinated contractions in abdominal muscles, like those during walking, play a crucial role in propelling brain movement and potentially flushing waste. Neuroscientist Patrick Drew's team observed mice brains moving milliseconds before a step, linking this to abdominal muscle tension via the vertebral venous plexus. This "hydraulic system" increases pressure on the brain, driving cerebrospinal fluid out. Published in Nature Neuroscience, the study hypothesizes this mechanism helps clear protein waste and other unnecessary material from the brain. Future research will explore how physical conditions, such as obesity, affect this belly-brain connection.
