2025-12-01 11:46:20 EST

Unpaid medical bills are subject to a statute of limitations, which varies by state and typically ranges from three to six years, limiting a creditor's ability to sue for collection. However, the debt itself does not disappear after this period, and collectors may still attempt to contact individuals for payment. It is crucial for consumers to know their state's laws, as making a partial payment or acknowledging the debt can reset the statute's clock. This article explains that while the legal window to sue may close, medical debt can remain on a credit report for seven years, underscoring the importance of addressing such financial burdens proactively.
