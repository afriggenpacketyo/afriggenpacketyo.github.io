These vibrant displays, caused by electrically charged particles from solar storms interacting with Earth's atmosphere, glow green from oxygen and purple/blue from nitrogen, with rare red hues from high-altitude oxygen. Researchers at Norway's Kjell Henriksen Observatory are investigating the potential for these solar energy particles to cause significant, though temporary, ozone depletion in polar regions. China Nears Completion of World's Largest 'Super-Cold Air Battery' in Gobi Desert

China is nearing completion of the world's largest "super-cold air battery" in the Gobi Desert, a groundbreaking energy storage facility designed to stabilize renewable energy fluctuations. This innovative system works by compressing air and cooling it to -196 degrees Celsius, storing it as liquid. When electricity is needed, the liquid air is released, expanding more than 750 times to drive turbines and generate power. The facility is projected to deliver up to 600,000 kilowatt-hours per discharge cycle, running for 10 hours straight, and generate approximately 180 million kWh annually, enough for 30,000 homes. This project represents a significant step towards integrating renewable energy sources.
