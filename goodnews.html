Newly Identified Flying Reptile Species Discovered in Arizona

2025-07-07 15:00:45 EDT

Scientists have identified a new species of pterosaur, a flying reptile from the time of the dinosaurs, based on a 209-million-year-old jawbone found in Arizona's Petrified Forest National Park. The creature, named Eotephradactylus mcintireae, is now believed to be the earliest pterosaur ever discovered in North America. Advanced scanning of the fossil revealed unique details, and analysis of its teeth suggests the seagull-sized reptile fed on hard-shelled prey like primitive, armored fish. The discovery was made in a rich fossil bed that has preserved a snapshot of a diverse ecosystem from the late Triassic period, offering new insights into this transitional era of evolution.
