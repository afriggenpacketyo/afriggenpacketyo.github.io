Oldest Neanderthal Campfire Evidence Discovered in UK, Dating Back 415,000 Years

2025-12-10 15:52:00 EST

Archaeologists have unearthed the oldest known evidence of deliberate fire-making by prehistoric humans in Suffolk, UK, dating back approximately 415,000 years. The discovery at Barnham, near a former Neanderthal dwelling site, includes heated clay, shattered handaxes, and imported iron pyrite, suggesting intentional fire creation rather than opportunistic use of natural fires. This finding significantly pushes back the timeline for deliberate fire-making, previously estimated at 50,000 years ago. Controlled fire was a pivotal evolutionary step, enabling improved diets through cooking, survival in colder climates, and fostering social evolution through communal gatherings, storytelling, and language development.
