Scientists Uncover How Epstein-Barr Virus Triggers Multiple Sclerosis

2026-02-07 15:32:00 EST

Scientists have made a breakthrough in understanding how the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) triggers multiple sclerosis (MS) in a small percentage of infected individuals. The research reveals that when B cells infected with EBV also possess the HLA-DR15 molecule, the virus manipulates the cell to present a myelin protein. This protein closely resembles viral structures, causing the immune system to mistakenly attack the body's own nerve sheaths. This discovery highlights HLA-DR15 as a crucial genetic risk factor and opens new avenues for preventing and treating MS, particularly if EBV infection occurs during adolescence.
