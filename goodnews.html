Dinosaur Fossils Discovered at National Monument, Marking First Excavation in a Century

2026-01-16 10:20:02 EST

New dinosaur fossils have been discovered at Dinosaur National Monument, leading to the first excavation at the Utah site in more than a century. The significant find occurred in September 2025 during parking lot construction near the Quarry Exhibit Hall, where workers exposed a portion of dinosaur-bearing sandstone. Paleontologists swiftly intervened, pausing construction to assess and excavate the remains, which are believed to belong to a large, long-necked dinosaur, most likely a Diplodocus. Approximately 3,000 pounds of fossils and rock were carefully removed and are now undergoing cleaning and study at the Utah Field House of Natural History State Park Museum. This discovery provides valuable insights into prehistoric life and follows other recent paleontological finds in the region.
