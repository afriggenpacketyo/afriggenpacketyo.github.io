Scientists Use Drones to Drop Mosquitoes in Hawaii to Save Endangered Birds

2025-06-16 07:30:00 EDT

In Hawaii, scientists are using drones to release male mosquitoes infected with Wolbachia bacteria into forests to combat avian malaria, a disease devastating native honeycreeper bird species. The male mosquitoes, which don't bite, mate with local females, resulting in eggs that don't hatch, thereby reducing the mosquito population. This innovative approach aims to protect endangered bird species threatened by climate change, which is allowing mosquitoes to spread to higher elevations. The effort is led by a coalition of organizations known as Birds, Not Mosquitoes, and the technique has been successful in controlling mosquito-borne diseases in humans elsewhere.
