Harvard Law School Discovers Original Magna Carta in its Collection

2025-05-15 02:23:48 EDT

Two British academics have identified a manuscript in Harvard Law School's library as an original 1300 version of the Magna Carta, a foundational document for modern liberties. The manuscript, purchased for a mere $27.50 after World War II, was previously believed to be a copy. This discovery makes it one of only seven originals from that date still in existence. The find was made by Professor David Carpenter while researching digital images and confirmed through spectral imaging, underscoring the document's historical significance and its potential value, estimated to be in the millions.
