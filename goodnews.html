Despite widespread inflation, the price of computers and other electronics has generally decreased over time, a trend largely attributed to Moore's Law. This principle, observed by Intel co-founder Gordon Moore, predicted that the number of transistors on a microchip would double approximately every two years, making computing power exponentially cheaper and more powerful. This consistent technological advancement has allowed consumers to get more for their money, from laptops to smartphones. However, experts now believe Moore's Law is reaching its physical limits, suggesting that the era of predictable, rapid price drops for electronics may be coming to an end without new technological breakthroughs.
