UK Parliament Approves Hillsborough Law to Ensure Public Accountability

2026-07-14 16:39:18 EDT

The UK Parliament has approved the Hillsborough Law, formally known as the Public Office (Accountability) Bill, which mandates public authorities and officials to be truthful and cooperative in official investigations and inquiries. This landmark legislation, championed by Sir Keir Starmer and Andy Burnham, aims to prevent future cover-ups similar to the 1989 Hillsborough disaster, where police were found to have spread false narratives. Starmer emphasized the bill's profound significance for working-class individuals and victims of state failings, promising protection for future families. The bill now proceeds to the House of Lords for final scrutiny, marking a momentous step towards justice and accountability.

Bipartisan Senate Bill Aims to Address Social Security Insolvency

2026-07-14 16:11:24 EDT

A bipartisan group of senators has introduced the "Promise Act," legislation designed to shore up Social Security's finances and prevent future benefit cuts for the 70 million Americans who rely on the program. The bill, co-sponsored by Senators Dick Durbin, Bill Cassidy, and others, does not itself propose specific tax hikes, benefit reductions, or changes to eligibility. Instead, it directs the bipartisan Social Security Advisory Board to draft a comprehensive bill, informed by public input, to ensure the program's trust funds remain solvent for at least the next 50 years. This initiative responds to recent projections that the trust fund could become insolvent by 2032, triggering a 22% benefit cut without Congressional action.
