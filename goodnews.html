By analyzing seismic data from NASA's InSight lander, researchers found a solid core approximately 600km in radius, which is proportionally similar to Earth's. This discovery is significant because it was believed Mars had not cooled sufficiently to form a solid core, suggesting planetary cooling processes may be faster than previously thought. 2025-09-07 19:13:39 EDT

A total lunar eclipse on Sunday night created a spectacular "Blood Moon," visible to observers across Asia, as well as parts of Europe and Africa. The phenomenon occurs when the Earth aligns perfectly between the sun and the moon, casting a shadow that gives the moon a deep reddish hue. This color is the result of sunlight scattering through Earth's atmosphere, where shorter blue wavelengths are filtered out, allowing longer red wavelengths to reach the moon. The event, which has captivated civilizations for millennia, was best viewed in Asia, including India and China.
