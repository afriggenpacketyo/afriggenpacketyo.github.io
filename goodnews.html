Yellowstone's Old Faithful Inn Recognized as a Landmark of National Park Architecture

2025-09-01 19:51:17 EDT

The Old Faithful Inn, located in Yellowstone National Park, stands as a historic and architectural treasure, considered a benchmark for national park design. Constructed in 1904 from approximately 10,000 trees, including uniquely twisted logs sought out as "freaks of the forest," it was once advertised as the world's largest log cabin. The inn has hosted presidents and millions of travelers, surviving natural disasters and the passage of time. Its distinctive style, known as "parkitecture," heavily influenced the design of other famous lodges in national parks like Yosemite and Glacier, cementing its legacy in American history.
