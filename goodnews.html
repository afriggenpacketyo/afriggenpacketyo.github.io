Alzheimer's Association launches '6-Step Challenge' to promote brain health through movement

2026-05-12 10:28:05 EDT

The Alzheimer's Association has introduced a "6-Step Challenge" to guide individuals in adopting brain-healthy habits, addressing a widespread lack of knowledge about maintaining cognitive function as people age. The initiative emphasizes the importance of lifestyle factors such as nutrition, sleep, social connection, and particularly movement. Increased physical activity is linked to improved blood flow, better brain function, enhanced mood, and a reduced risk of dementia. The challenge encourages incorporating simple, actionable steps into daily routines, aiming for 30-45 minutes of physical activity several times a week. This program aims to empower individuals to proactively manage their brain health.
