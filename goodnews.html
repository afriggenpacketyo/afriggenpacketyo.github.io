Understanding Jewish Funeral and Mourning Rituals

2025-10-15 10:07:00 EDT

Jewish mourning rituals emphasize eternal memory among the living, with little focus on an afterlife. The deceased are buried quickly and simply, without embalming, in plain wooden caskets or shrouds, reflecting humility. A ritual bath, Taharah, precedes the burial, and public viewings are generally avoided. Close family members perform K'riah, a ritual tearing of clothing, to express grief. The seven-day period of Shiva follows, during which mourners refrain from work and grooming, receiving support from friends. The Mourner's Kaddish, a communal prayer focusing on faith, is recited for varying periods, and gravestones are typically added later.
