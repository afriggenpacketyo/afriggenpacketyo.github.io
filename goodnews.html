Retrospective on the Nuremberg Trials and Their Legacy in International Law

2025-11-20 02:58:00 EST

On November 20, 1945, the Major War Crimes Trial began in Nuremberg, holding 24 high-ranking Nazi officials personally accountable for crimes against humanity, a landmark moment in international law. The trials established the principle of individual responsibility for state actions, overcoming the defense that perpetrators were merely following orders. Despite overwhelming evidence, none of the defendants pleaded guilty, but the proceedings resulted in 12 death sentences and numerous prison terms. The Nuremberg Trials are now considered a crucial precursor to modern institutions like the International Criminal Court, though challenges in enforcing international law persist today.
