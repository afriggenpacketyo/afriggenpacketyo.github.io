Los Angeles City Council Moves to Curb "Renovictions"

2025-07-07 22:29:33 EDT

The Los Angeles City Council is progressing with legislation aimed at permanently protecting renters from "renovictions," a practice where landlords evict tenants for renovations only to charge significantly higher rents to new occupants. The council recently passed an amendment that restricts landlords from using substantial remodels as a basis for eviction unless a government agency mandates it to address housing code violations. This initiative builds on a temporary protection and seeks to establish a clear pathway for legitimate renovations while safeguarding tenant rights. Future proposals will outline requirements for landlord-provided relocation assistance, including comparable temporary housing and conditions for tenants to return to their original units with state-mandated rent increase limits.
