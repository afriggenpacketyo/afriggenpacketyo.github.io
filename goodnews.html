Explainer: Why Cities Experience Higher Temperatures During Heatwaves

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

The "urban heat island" effect is the key reason why cities often feel significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas, especially during heatwaves. This phenomenon occurs because dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's heat, which is then slowly released, raising air temperatures. The lack of vegetation and water, combined with waste heat from vehicles and air conditioning, exacerbates the effect, making urban populations more vulnerable to the impacts of climate change and extreme heat.
