Shingles Vaccine Linked to Lower Risk of Heart Disease

The study, which examined over 1 million people aged 50 or older, found a 23% lower risk of such events in those who received the vaccine. The protective effect was particularly pronounced in men, people under 60, and those with unhealthy lifestyles. The researchers propose that preventing shingles through vaccination may reduce blood vessel damage, inflammation, and clot formation, which can contribute to heart disease.
