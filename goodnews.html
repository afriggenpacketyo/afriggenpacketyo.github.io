Understanding the Timeline for Wage Garnishment After a Lawsuit

2026-04-13 12:35:50 EDT

The process of wage garnishment after a creditor files a lawsuit can vary significantly depending on borrower response and state laws. Typically, after a lawsuit is filed and the debtor is served, they have a limited window (20-30 days) to respond. Failure to respond can lead to a default judgment, speeding up the process. Once a judgment is obtained, the creditor can request a garnishment order, which is then sent to the employer. In swift scenarios without borrower response, garnishment could begin within one to three months. Various options exist to prevent or interrupt garnishment, including debt management, settlement, or bankruptcy.
