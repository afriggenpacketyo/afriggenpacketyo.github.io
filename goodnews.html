Exploring the Confusing Etymological History of the Thanksgiving Turkey

2025-11-26 05:00:00 EST

The word "turkey" has a complex and somewhat mysterious origin, rooted in a case of mistaken identity from the 16th century. The bird native to the Americas, meleagris gallopavo, was named after the African guinea fowl, which Europeans already called a turkey because it was imported through the Turkish region. As the American bird's popularity grew, it inherited the name, which has since become embedded in the English lexicon through phrases like "talking turkey" and "cold turkey." Despite its culinary importance, especially during Thanksgiving, the bird's name is a historical curiosity reflecting centuries of global trade and cultural confusion.
