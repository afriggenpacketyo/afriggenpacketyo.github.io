Lionel Jospin, Former French Prime Minister, Dies at 88

2026-03-23 11:35:00 EDT

Lionel Jospin, who served as France's Prime Minister from 1997 to 2002, has died at the age of 88. A prominent figure in the center-left Socialist party, Jospin was known for introducing the 35-hour work week and civil partnerships, embodying a vision of social progress and republican values. His political career included an ill-fated presidential bid in 2002, where he unexpectedly failed to reach the second round, but earned admiration for urging his supporters to back rival Jacques Chirac against far-right candidate Jean-Marie Le Pen. Leading French politicians, including President Macron, paid tribute to his integrity and lasting impact on French politics.
