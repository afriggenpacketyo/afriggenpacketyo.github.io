A financial strategy known as a 351 conversion is gaining popularity among wealthy investors as a way to defer capital gains taxes on highly appreciated stocks. The process involves contributing a diversified portfolio of assets into a new exchange-traded fund (ETF) before its launch in exchange for shares of that ETF. This allows the investor to avoid triggering a taxable event until they sell the new ETF shares. While the strategy can be highly effective, it has strict diversification requirements and can limit an investor's future flexibility, as their capital is then tied up in the specific ETF.
