The report described the violence as "shocking in its scale and brutality," encompassing widespread atrocities that may constitute war crimes and crimes against humanity. The RSF offensive, which successfully captured the Sudanese army's last remaining stronghold in Darfur, involved mass killings, summary executions, sexual assault, abductions for ransom, and torture. This article highlights that many of these attacks were ethnically motivated, reflecting a pattern of RSF conduct seen throughout the ongoing war, which has already created the world's largest humanitarian crisis. The UN High Commissioner for Human Rights has called for immediate accountability, cautioning that persistent impunity only perpetuates cycles of violence.
