Understanding Bank Levy Durations and How to Combat Them

2026-03-16 13:05:37 EDT

A bank levy is a legal action where a creditor obtains a court order to freeze or seize funds from a debtor's bank account. Typically, the bank holds funds for 14-21 days before releasing them, during which time the debtor may challenge the levy. However, the underlying court judgment can remain valid for 10 years or more, allowing for multiple levies if the debt is not resolved. Federal and state tax levies operate differently and can be more continuous. Strategies to stop a bank levy include challenging it in court, negotiating with the creditor, using debt settlement programs, or filing for bankruptcy.
