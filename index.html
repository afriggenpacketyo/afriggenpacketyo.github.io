This breakthrough involved advanced computational methods and confirms the existence of manifolds of Kervaire invariant one in dimension 126. The Kervaire invariant, introduced by John Milnor in 1950, is a function that assesses whether a smooth framed manifold can be converted into a sphere through surgery; the problem sought to identify dimensions where this invariant is non-zero, indicating the presence of unique shapes that cannot be transformed into a sphere. The findings, outlined in a paper yet to undergo peer review, provide a definitive answer to a mathematical mystery that has puzzled experts for decades, and could be applied to further problems in the field.
