Chinese Fossil Discovery Reveals Gradual Evolution of Bird Tails from Dinosaurs

2026-07-18 00:00:07 EDT

A new Chinese fossil discovery provides crucial insights into the evolutionary transition of birds from their dinosaur ancestors, specifically detailing how they gradually shed their long tails. Published in the peer-reviewed journal Science Advances, the research elucidates the "evolutionary assembly of the flight-adapted bird body plan," highlighting profound morphological changes that occurred over time. Unlike most non-avian dinosaurs, modern birds possess a pygostyle, a distinct skeletal structure formed by the fusion of the final few caudal vertebrae, which serves as a vital anchor for tail feathers and muscles, crucial for aerodynamic flight. This finding helps to explain the step-by-step transformation to this more aerodynamically favorable structure, addressing the previous "exceeding rarity" of early-diverging bird and birdlike dinosaur fossils.
