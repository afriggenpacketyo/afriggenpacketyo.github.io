For over 60 years, trials had to independently prove a drug's efficacy, ignoring prior research. Bayesian methods allow formal incorporation of existing knowledge from earlier studies, related drugs, or real-world evidence, which is crucial for rare diseases with limited patient populations, or for testing drugs in children after adult trials.
