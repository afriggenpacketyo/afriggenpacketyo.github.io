Authorized Users Generally Not Liable for Unpaid Credit Card Debt

2026-07-20 11:01:05 EDT

An authorized user on a credit card is typically not legally responsible for the primary cardholder's unpaid debt. Unlike joint account holders, authorized users do not sign the account agreement and have no contractual obligation to the issuer. While debt collectors may attempt to contact authorized users, they generally lack legal grounds to sue them directly. It is crucial to distinguish between authorized users and joint account holders, as the latter share equal legal responsibility. Early intervention and exploring debt relief options are recommended if the primary cardholder struggles with payments.
