The article explores whether annuities or Certificates of Deposit (CDs) are superior for retirement planning, concluding that both serve distinct purposes. CDs offer simplicity, predictability, and FDIC-insured principal, making them ideal for short-term goals or conservative portfolio anchors, though their returns often lag inflation. Annuities, conversely, are designed for lifetime income, converting lump sums into guaranteed payments and mitigating longevity risk, especially valuable for those without pensions.
