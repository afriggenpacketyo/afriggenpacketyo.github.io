Due to the Antideficiency Act, agencies cannot spend money without congressional approval, leading to the furlough of non-essential employees. Essential services, such as national security, law enforcement, and air traffic control, continue to operate, but employees in these roles work without immediate pay. While programs like Social Security and Medicare have permanent funding, customer service and administrative functions can be severely delayed, impacting the public more directly the longer the shutdown lasts.
