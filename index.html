Understanding the "609 Letter" for Disputing Credit Report Errors

2026-02-03 14:41:35 EST

A "609 letter" is a formal request to credit bureaus, based on Section 609 of the Fair Credit Reporting Act, asking for verification and documentation of specific items on a credit report. This strategy is primarily used to challenge inaccurate, unverifiable, or erroneous information, such as accounts never opened or incorrect payment histories. If credit bureaus cannot provide adequate documentation within 30 days, they are required to remove the item. However, it's crucial to understand that a 609 letter is not a means to remove legitimate debts owed, and its effectiveness varies, making it best suited for genuine reporting errors rather than avoiding financial obligations.
