Economic Analysis Explains Why Overall Price Levels Rarely Decrease

2025-11-20 06:30:00 EST

Despite political promises to lower prices, the overall price level in an economy rarely falls, and when it does, it typically signals a severe economic crisis like a recession or depression. This economic explainer clarifies that while prices of individual goods can fluctuate, a general decrease, known as deflation, is undesirable because it can lead to a "deflationary spiral" where consumers delay spending, causing economic activity to grind to a halt. Central banks, like the U.S. Federal Reserve, intentionally target a small, steady rate of inflation (around 2%) to encourage spending and investment. Therefore, promises to make everything cheaper are generally not feasible or beneficial for a healthy economy.
