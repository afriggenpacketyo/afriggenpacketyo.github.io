Attorney General Pam Bondi Announces Arrest of Key Participant in 2012 Benghazi Attack

2026-02-06 12:15:53 EST

U.S. Attorney General Pam Bondi announced the arrest of Zubayar al-Bakoush, identified as a "key participant" in the 2012 Benghazi terrorist attack that resulted in the deaths of four U.S. government officials, including Ambassador J. Christopher Stevens. Al-Bakoush was successfully taken into U.S. custody through a "foreign transfer of custody" and now faces an eight-count indictment, encompassing serious charges such as murder and terrorism. FBI Director Kash Patel emphasized the necessity of protecting the investigation's integrity, limiting further operational details. This arrest represents a significant development in a highly politically charged event that has spurred years of intense scrutiny and investigations into the Obama administration's preparedness and response, notably drawing criticism from Republicans towards then-Secretary of State Hillary Clinton.
