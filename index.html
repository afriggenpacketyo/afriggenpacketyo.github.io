Explainer: The Causes and Dangers of Urban Heat Islands

2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

Urban heat islands are metropolitan areas that become significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas, a phenomenon that intensifies heatwaves for city dwellers. This effect is caused by the prevalence of heat-absorbing materials like concrete and asphalt, a lack of cooling vegetation, and heat trapped by tall buildings. These conditions can raise temperatures by up to 15 degrees Celsius, posing health risks and increasing energy demand for air conditioning. Solutions to mitigate this effect include adding more green spaces, installing reflective "cool roofs" and pavements, and designing cities to improve airflow.
