Marco Rubio's speech at the Munich Security Conference, initially interpreted by European leaders as a conciliatory gesture, is now being critically re-evaluated as an offer of friendship presented strictly on "white, Christian, Maga terms." While Rubio emphasized intertwined destinies and a shared heritage between the U.S. and Europe, his underlying message resonated with Trumpian themes such as concerns over mass migration, civilizational erasure, and the perceived decline of Christian culture. Critics, including German Chancellor Friedrich Merz and French President Emmanuel Macron, underscored the significant divergence in values, with Europe rejecting protectionism and reaffirming commitments to human dignity. Rubio's subsequent visits to pro-Putin, anti-Brussels leaders further intensified fears of U.S. attempts to sow disunity among allies, signaling a potential transatlantic "divorce settlement."

Stephen Colbert Escalates Dispute with CBS Over FCC Equal-Time Rule and Interview Ban

2026-02-18 10:22:41 EST

Late-night host Stephen Colbert has significantly escalated his public dispute with CBS over the network's handling of his interview with Democratic U.S. Senate candidate James Talarico. Colbert initially claimed CBS prohibited the broadcast due to the FCC's equal-time rule, which would necessitate offering equal airtime to other primary candidates, and further instructed him not to discuss the matter on air. CBS subsequently denied a direct prohibition, stating it merely advised on the implications of the rule. Colbert, however, vehemently rejected CBS's denial, asserting that network lawyers had approved his initial script and accusing management of kowtowing to FCC Chairman Brendan Carr, who seeks to eliminate talk show exemptions. The host ultimately posted the interview on YouTube, which falls outside FCC jurisdiction.
