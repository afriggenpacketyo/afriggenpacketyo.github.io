Police Apologize and Agree to Settlement in Case of First Child Killed in Troubles

2025-12-11 09:33:17 EST

Police have issued an apology and agreed to a significant compensation settlement to the family of Patrick Rooney, the first child killed during the Troubles in Northern Ireland in 1969. Rooney, aged nine, was shot by Royal Ulster Constabulary (RUC) officers during rioting. A police ombudsman report in 2021 found that investigative failings prevented officers from being held accountable. The settlement, announced in the High Court, acknowledges the "hurt and suffering" caused to the family. The case highlights the long-standing quest for justice and accountability for victims of the conflict.
