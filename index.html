Former Army Employee Charged with Leaking Classified Information About Elite Military Unit

2026-04-08 22:38:05 EDT

A former Army employee, Courtney Williams, has been arrested and charged with allegedly leaking classified information about Delta Force, a U.S. Army Special Military Unit (SMU), to a reporter. Prosecutors allege Williams divulged sensitive details between 2022 and 2024, including information that was subsequently published in an article and a book. Williams, who previously held a top-secret security clearance, had her access suspended in 2016 after an internal investigation. The FBI affidavit details extensive communication between Williams and the reporter, including the transmission of documents and photographs. The charges carry a potential sentence of up to 10 years in prison, raising concerns about national security and the handling of classified information.

Iran War and Tariffs Threaten India's Garment Export Goals, Industry Leaders Report

2026-04-08 22:27:31 EDT

India's textile industry is facing significant challenges from both U.S. tariffs and the ongoing Iran war, jeopardizing its ambitious goal of exporting $100 billion worth of textiles annually by 2030. Industry leaders report that U.S. tariffs, initially eased, were followed by the Iran war, which has driven up raw material and freight costs, impacting demand. Migrant workers are reportedly leaving textile hubs due to difficulties securing essential fuels. Despite trade agreements, growth has slowed, and companies are hesitant to pass on increased costs due to fears of further demand reduction. The fragile ceasefire has cooled oil prices, but they remain elevated, continuing to pressure costs and demand for India's vital garment sector.
