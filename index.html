A civil case brought by three IRA bomb victims against former Sinn Féin leader Gerry Adams has commenced at the High Court in London. The claimants are seeking a ruling that Adams is personally liable for decisions related to car bombings in London and Manchester in 1973 and 1996, seeking nominal damages for "vindicatory purposes." Adams has consistently denied any direct or indirect involvement with the IRA. The case, expected to last seven days, will present "a jigsaw of evidence" from witnesses including former IRA members and retired police officers. The claimants aim to demonstrate Adams's alleged leadership role and culpability in the attacks.
