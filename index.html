Israeli and Lebanese ambassadors held rare direct talks in Washington, mediated by U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio, marking a significant step towards de-escalating regional hostilities. While tempering expectations for immediate breakthroughs, U.S. officials expressed hope that these discussions could create a framework for lasting peace, particularly concerning Hezbollah's influence. President Donald Trump emphasized the need for Hezbollah's removal, even as potential new peace talks with Iran are being considered. These diplomatic efforts occur against a backdrop of a fragile ceasefire with Iran and ongoing tensions in the Middle East, highlighting the complex geopolitical landscape.
