This figure represents an increase from March's revised 61,000 jobs. Wage growth for job stayers increased by 4.4% annually. Job creation was concentrated in education and health services, trade, transportation, and utilities. Despite the positive headline number, the report suggests a low-hire, low-fire environment, with companies hesitant to lay off workers but also cautious about expanding hiring significantly. This economic stability offers little incentive for the Federal Reserve to lower interest rates amidst persistent inflation.
