New Research Links Parkinson's Diverse Symptoms to a Complex Brain Network

2026-02-10 06:00:00 EST

New research published in Nature suggests that Parkinson's disease, beyond its characteristic tremors and movement difficulties, disrupts communication within a complex brain network known as the somato-cognitive action network (SCAN). This disruption may explain the disease's diverse "stranger symptoms," including issues with sleep, smell, digestion, and even thinking. Scientists found that Parkinson's patients exhibit abnormally strong connections within the SCAN network, which paradoxically creates a "traffic jam" that impedes normal signal transmission. Importantly, treatments like deep brain stimulation were shown to immediately reduce this connectivity, allowing brain activity to flow more normally. This discovery reinforces the understanding of Parkinson's as a network disorder, opening avenues for targeted therapeutic interventions.
