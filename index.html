2026-05-04 16:03:39 EDT

Berkshire Hathaway's Class B shares experienced a decline, despite CEO Greg Abel's positive reception at the annual meeting and an impressive 18% jump in first-quarter operating earnings. Abel, Warren Buffett's handpicked successor, garnered praise for demonstrating a deep understanding of Berkshire's sprawling businesses and outlining clear plans for operational excellence, reassuring many in the investment community about the conglomerate's future direction. He also articulated a measured stance on artificial intelligence, stating Berkshire would not pursue "AI for the sake of AI," and detailed ongoing efforts to enhance railway and insurance operations. The company's robust Q1 earnings were predominantly driven by its strong insurance underwriting performance, and it reported a substantial cash hoard nearing $400 billion. (not investment advice)
