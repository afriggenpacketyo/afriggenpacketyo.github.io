Van life community finds freedom and affordability on public lands

2026-05-05 07:00:27 EDT

A growing community of individuals is embracing "van life," choosing to live nomadically on public lands for affordability and freedom, particularly during winter months in the American Southwest. For as little as $180 for a seven-month permit in areas like Quartzsite, Arizona, people can legally camp and establish temporary communities. This lifestyle serves as a crucial alternative housing solution for those priced out of traditional markets, with many vehicle residents identified as "unsheltered" by federal housing policy. The article highlights stories of individuals like chef Derek Hansler and cancer survivor Stephanie Scruggs, who have found community and a sense of purpose on the road. The Bureau of Land Management (BLM) lands provide a lifeline, offering a space for innovation and mutual support within this expanding population.
