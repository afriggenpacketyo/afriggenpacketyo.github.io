Additionally, the plans aim to extend legal rights to all children with SEND through the introduction of new school-led Individual Support Plans (ISPs), with the dual objectives of providing earlier intervention and managing rapidly escalating costs. The article highlights concerns from SEND campaign groups, who worry that these changes may inadvertently lead to a watering down of existing legal rights for children with the most complex needs.
