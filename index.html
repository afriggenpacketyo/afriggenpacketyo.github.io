2026-07-02 12:50:13 EDT

A California judge has ruled that surcharges levied by home insurers statewide to cover the costs of Los Angeles County’s 2025 wildfires are legal. The decision rejected a petition from Consumer Watchdog, an advocacy group, which had argued the charges violated Proposition 103 and constituted an industry bailout. Insurers began applying these fees after the state's "insurer of last resort," the California FAIR Plan, required a $1 billion bailout following $4 billion in claims from the devastating fires. The ruling allows insurers to continue passing on a portion of these assessment costs to policyholders, impacting homeowners financially. (not investment advice)

Effective AC Settings to Reduce Electric Bills This Summer Identified by Experts

2026-07-02 12:49:00 EDT

As energy prices rise and heatwaves intensify, many are seeking ways to lower electric bills, with AC costs being a major contributor to high summer utility expenses. Experts highlight several thermostat settings that can significantly reduce consumption without requiring new HVAC systems or major investments. Key strategies include adjusting the baseline temperature, implementing automated setback schedules when the home is unoccupied or residents are asleep, and setting the fan to "auto" rather than "on" to prevent continuous operation. Addressing humidity issues also allows for slightly higher, yet still comfortable, temperature settings, offering practical solutions to manage energy costs effectively.
