A federal appeals court ruling striking down a nearly 160-year-old ban on home distilling could lead to a Supreme Court case challenging Congress's authority to regulate intrastate economic activity. This case, McNutt v. US Department of Justice, may prompt the Court to reconsider New Deal-era decisions that established broad federal regulatory powers. Conservative legal scholars and justices have long sought to limit federal power, and this case presents an opportunity to revisit the commerce clause. While the Justice Department's defense strategy in the lower court was questioned, the potential for the Supreme Court to significantly alter federal regulatory power is substantial, impacting various laws from minimum wage to anti-discrimination statutes.

Book Links 'Alien's' Weyland-Yutani Corp to Modern Labor Market Inequality

2026-04-14 06:30:00 EDT

A new book, 'The Wage Standard,' draws parallels between the exploitative practices of the fictional Weyland-Yutani Corporation in the movie 'Alien' and the pervasive "monopsony power" in today's labor market. The book argues that unchecked corporate power, similar to that of Weyland-Yutani, allows employers to underpay and mistreat workers even when multiple employers exist. Economist Arindrajit Dube contends that the erosion of worker protections like minimum wages and unions since the 1980s has exacerbated income inequality. The book suggests that institutions like unions and government regulations are crucial counterweights to employer power, essential for ensuring fair wages and worker treatment.
