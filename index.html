The previously strong political relationship between the UK and the U.S. has become strained, particularly concerning the Iran War and the use of UK military bases. While the UK government has carefully voiced policy differences with the U.S. on various issues, disagreements over the Iran War have led to direct criticism from President Trump towards Prime Minister Starmer. Trump has publicly mocked Starmer, and former UK officials describe the U.S. as behaving more like a "predator" than an ally. The UK government aims to distance itself from the economic shocks caused by the war, such as rising petrol and diesel prices, to appeal to voters ahead of upcoming elections. Despite the strains, both countries acknowledge the importance of their bilateral relationship.
