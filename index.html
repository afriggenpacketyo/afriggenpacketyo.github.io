President Donald Trump confirmed that China is actively in the process of buying soybeans, with brokerage AgResource Co reporting seven to ten cargoes already bought from the U.S. for various shipping dates. Soybeans have been a critical flashpoint in U.S.-China trade tensions, with China previously curtailing purchases, which severely impacted American farmers. This renewed buying offers substantial hope for U.S. growers who have been struggling with inflation and high input costs, fulfilling part of a deal where Beijing agreed to purchase at least 12 million tons this season.
