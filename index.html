Study Explores Potential of Weight Loss Drugs in Cancer Prevention

A new observational study suggests that GLP-1 agonists, commonly used as weight loss drugs, may modestly reduce the risk of obesity-related cancers, particularly colorectal cancer. Researchers compared GLP-1 drugs to DDP-4 inhibitors, another type of diabetes medication without the same weight loss effect, in over 170,000 adults with obesity and diabetes. The study found that patients taking GLP-1 receptor agonists had a 7% lower risk of developing an obesity-related cancer and an 8% lower risk of death from any cause. While the results were not statistically significant for men, women treated with GLP-1s had an 8% lower risk of obesity-related cancer and a 20% lower risk of all-cause mortality.
