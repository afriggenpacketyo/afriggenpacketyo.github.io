The State Committee for Monitoring Shark Incidents (CEMIT) confirmed the boy did not survive his injuries, which included a severe bite to his right thigh leading to cardiorespiratory arrest. The article notes that dozens of beaches along Pernambuco's coast have shark warnings, with Chifre beach specifically displaying four such signs, yet the boy was in the water. Following this incident, CEMIT plans to increase shark monitoring and reinstate a suspended program to track the creatures with microchips. Over 80 shark attacks have been recorded in Pernambuco since 1992, with 26 being fatal, highlighting the persistent danger in the region.
