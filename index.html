2026-07-15 06:00:52 EDT

Two prominent US advocacy groups have filed a lawsuit against the Trump administration, alleging that its sanctions targeting Palestinian rights organizations, International Criminal Court (ICC) officials, and a UN expert unlawfully infringe upon Americans' First Amendment rights. The lawsuit argues that the sweeping 2025 sanctions package has created a "profound" chilling effect on Palestine-related advocacy, compelling American citizens to sever professional relationships and abandon constitutionally protected work. The plaintiff groups, which actively collaborate with sanctioned entities on documenting alleged war crimes, contend that continuing their work could expose their American employees to criminal prosecution and civil penalties. They further argue that the sanctions exceed presidential authority under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act and are ultimately ineffective, warning of potential future abuses against other disfavored viewpoints.
