2025-04-05 14:30:28 EDT

Economists are questioning the formula behind President Trump's "reciprocal" tariff plan, arguing that it is based on flawed assumptions and results in inflated tariff rates. The formula, which divides a country's trade deficit with the U.S. by its exports and then by two, assumes an elasticity rate lower than it should be in practice. If elasticity is adjusted, tariff rates would be significantly lower, with most countries at the baseline rate. Another report also found that trade-weighted average tariff rates used to justify the tariffs are higher than they are in reality.
