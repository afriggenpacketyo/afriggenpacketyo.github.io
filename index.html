The casting of Kenyan Mexican actor Lupita Nyong’o as Helen of Troy in Christopher Nolan’s adaptation of 'The Odyssey' has ignited controversy, revealing a 'myopic understanding of identity.' Critics, including Elon Musk, condemned the choice as disrespectful to Greek heritage, despite Helen's mythical origin from a swan-god. The article argues this selective indignation, ignoring the lack of Mediterranean ancestry for other cast members like Matt Damon as Odysseus, reflects a pathology in American audiences that prioritizes superficial racial representation over artistic interpretation. It suggests that such debates undermine art's ability to transcend divisions and explore universal human conditions, urging a return to a broader appreciation of the Western canon.
