2025-05-21 01:40:03 EDT

Habeas corpus, a fundamental principle enshrined in the Constitution, safeguards individuals from unlawful detention by ensuring they are not held without valid legal grounds. If someone believes a person is being illegally detained, they can request a court order demanding the detainee be brought to court and the detaining party prove their authority. The executive branch cannot suspend habeas corpus; only Congress can do so in cases of rebellion or invasion. The Trump administration considered suspending habeas corpus for detained migrants, arguing that illegal immigration constitutes an invasion, a claim rejected by federal judges.
