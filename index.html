Explaining the Urban Heat Island Effect as Cities Face Rising Temperatures

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As heatwaves become more intense, urban areas often experience significantly higher temperatures than the surrounding countryside due to a phenomenon known as the urban heat island effect. Dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's heat, while a lack of vegetation and water sources reduces natural cooling through evaporation. This can make city centers several degrees warmer, especially at night, exacerbating the health risks of extreme heat like dehydration and heat stroke. With global populations increasingly concentrated in cities, this effect poses a growing challenge.
