Explanation of a U.S. Government Shutdown and Its Consequences

2025-10-23 10:51:54 EDT

A government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass the necessary appropriations bills to fund federal agencies before the fiscal year begins on October 1. Without this authorization, agencies must cease all non-essential functions, leading to the furlough of federal employees and the disruption of many government services. Essential services, such as national security and public safety, continue to operate, but employees in these roles work without immediate pay. Entitlement programs like Social Security and Medicare are funded separately and continue to make payments, though administrative services can be delayed, impacting the public in various ways.
