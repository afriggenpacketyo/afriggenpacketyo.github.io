Mexico first enshrined jus solis in its constitution in 1814, aiming for universal citizenship regardless of race, a concept tied to the abolition of slavery. The United States eventually adopted birthright citizenship with the Fourteenth Amendment in 1868, but its interpretation has been debated, particularly concerning undocumented migrants. While most Latin American countries uphold birthright citizenship, challenges remain, exemplified by the Dominican Republic's stripping of citizenship from individuals of Haitian descent.
