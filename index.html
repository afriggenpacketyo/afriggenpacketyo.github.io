Hired at 85, she retired at 100 from the Rosie the Riveter WWII Home Front National Historical Park. Her remarkable life also included opening one of Berkeley's first Black-owned music stores and a career as a field representative for California legislators. This article celebrates the extraordinary life and enduring legacy of a trailblazing figure who profoundly impacted historical understanding.
