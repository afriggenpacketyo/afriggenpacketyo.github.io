A Hanukkah celebration in Pacific Palisades, Los Angeles, was imbued with both joy and profound grief, occurring just after the deadly Bondi Beach terror attack and nearly a year after local wildfires. Rabbi Zushe Cunin, executive director of Chabad Palisades, acknowledged the "mixed emotions," mourning the Bondi victims while emphasizing community resilience. Despite heightened security, attendees gathered for the menorah lighting, expressing solidarity and a determination not to be deterred by antisemitism. Property developer Rick Caruso and many residents highlighted the event's significance as a symbol of hope and reconnection for a community still recovering from its own traumatic losses.
