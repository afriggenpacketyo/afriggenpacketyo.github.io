2025-12-03 20:12:52 EST

Federal agents have initiated "Operation Catahoula Crunch" in New Orleans, transforming the city into the latest focal point of the Trump administration's extensive immigration crackdown. Masked agents, under the leadership of Border Patrol Chief Gregory Bovino, are reportedly aiming for 5,000 arrests, targeting individuals released after committing serious crimes, including home invasion, armed robbery, grand theft auto, and sexual assault. However, critics, including Mayor-elect Helena Moreno, voice profound concerns about potential racial profiling and highlight that previous federal operations primarily detained individuals without criminal records. In response, local community organizers are actively educating residents about their rights, while some businesses have temporarily closed in protest and out of fear, despite Louisiana's Republican governor welcoming the federal deployment to combat crime.

D-Day Veteran Charles Shay, Who Saved Lives on Omaha Beach, Dies at 101

2025-12-03 20:07:00 EST

Charles Shay, a highly decorated Native American D-Day veteran and U.S. Army medic, has passed away at the age of 101 at his home in Normandy, France. At just 19 years old, Shay landed on Omaha Beach on June 6, 1944, where he heroically and repeatedly plunged into the sea to carry critically wounded soldiers to safety, saving them from drowning and earning him the Silver Star and France's Legion of Honor. After reenlisting and serving in the Korean War, Shay moved to France in 2018 to be close to his fallen comrades, expressing a profound desire to ensure their sacrifices are never forgotten. In his later years, he became a powerful advocate for peace, often sharing his testimony and expressing deep sadness at the resurgence of war in Europe.
