2025-06-18 23:15:04 EDT

Businesses are increasingly using "tariff engineering" to lower their duty liabilities amid rising tariffs. This legal practice involves altering a product's materials, dimensions, or composition to fit into a different harmonized system code with lower tariff rates. For example, some manufacturers bundle steel and aluminum elements into their products to qualify for lower duties, while others remove these elements when tariffs change. Companies such as Columbia Sportswear and Converse have also implemented strategic product tweaks to save on tariff costs; Columbia adds zippered pockets to shirts, and Converse adds fuzzy-felt fabric to sneaker soles.
