Understanding the Consequences of a Creditor Writing Off Unpaid Debt

2025-11-07 13:08:50 EST

This article explains that a "debt write-off" by a creditor does not mean the borrower's obligation is forgiven or erased. Instead, it signifies that the original lender has declared the debt uncollectible and is removing it from their books, often by selling it to a collection agency. This action severely damages the borrower's credit score for up to seven years and initiates a new phase of collection efforts from the new debt owner. The collection agency can still pursue the full balance, add fees, and potentially sue the borrower to recover the money.
