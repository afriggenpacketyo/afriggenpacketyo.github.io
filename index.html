Explaining the Urban Heat Island Effect as Cities Face Rising Temperatures

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As heatwaves become more common, urban areas often experience significantly higher temperatures than the surrounding countryside due to the "urban heat island" effect. This phenomenon occurs because dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's heat, while cities have less cooling vegetation and water. This retained heat is slowly released, making cities several degrees warmer, especially at night, and exacerbating the health risks of extreme heat. With global populations increasingly concentrated in urban centers, this effect makes city dwellers particularly vulnerable to the impacts of climate change and rising global temperatures.
