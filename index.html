2025-11-07 07:04:00 EST

A recent Deutschlandtrend survey conducted by infratest-dimap indicates that half of all Germans now report feeling unsafe in public spaces, including streets and public transportation, a sentiment that has doubled over the past eight years. This escalating sense of insecurity is closely tied to ongoing national debates surrounding migration and the economy, both of which respondents identify as the most urgent political problems facing Germany. While primary fears revolve around theft and verbal assaults, there is also a marked decline in the perception of national stability across foreign policy, economic standing, and social cohesion. Despite a more positive outlook on personal living conditions, public dissatisfaction with the federal government's overall performance remains notably high.
