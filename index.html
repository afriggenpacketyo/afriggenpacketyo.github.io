Yevgeni Gershman, a man with alleged ties to Israeli organized crime, has agreed to plead guilty to four federal counts related to running high-stakes illegal poker games at a luxury Encino mansion owned by former NBA All-Star Gilbert Arenas. Gershman, who has prior criminal convictions in Israel for conspiracy to commit murder and narcotics trafficking, faces a 24-month federal prison sentence. Prosecutors allege he was a central figure in the gambling operation, which also involved hiring women to provide services, and immigration fraud through a sham marriage. Arenas and other co-defendants have pleaded not guilty. This article highlights a significant case involving organized crime, illegal gambling, and immigration fraud in Los Angeles.
