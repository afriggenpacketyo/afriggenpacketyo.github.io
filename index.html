Proposal for Permanent Daylight Saving Time Faces Debate in Congress and California

2026-07-14 06:00:00 EDT

A proposal to make daylight saving time permanent nationwide is under consideration in Congress, sparking debate over its potential effects on health, business, and agriculture. The Sunshine Protection Act, supported by some lawmakers and President Trump, aims to end the twice-yearly clock changes, leading to darker mornings but later sunsets. Opponents, including medical experts and some agricultural interests, argue that permanent standard time is better for health and circadian rhythms, citing risks of sleep disruption and increased accidents. While California voters previously approved a measure to allow year-round daylight saving time, federal authorization is required. The debate highlights differing views on the benefits and drawbacks of altering timekeeping practices.

2026-07-14 06:00:00 EDT
