Understanding the transmission and symptoms of the Ebola virus

2026-05-28 05:14:00 EDT

A new Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo is concerning due to its prolonged undetected spread. The virus requires direct contact with bodily fluids to enter the body through mucous membranes or breaks in the skin. Once inside, it targets the immune system, leading to a rapid increase in viral load. Early symptoms mimic common illnesses, making diagnosis difficult until the patient is highly infectious. Even after death, the virus remains viable in bodily fluids, posing a risk during funeral practices. Containment is challenging due to population mobility and ongoing conflict, necessitating early recognition, proper infection control, and community understanding.

Understanding the health impacts of heatwaves and identifying at-risk individuals

2026-05-28 05:03:55 EDT

Extreme heat poses significant risks to the human body, causing blood vessels to dilate, lowering blood pressure, and increasing the heart's workload. This can lead to symptoms like dizziness, nausea, and confusion, potentially progressing to heat exhaustion. The body's core temperature regulation is challenged, with increased sweating leading to fluid and salt loss. Vulnerable groups, including the elderly, young children, and those with underlying health conditions, are at higher risk. During heatwaves, it's crucial to stay hydrated, seek cool environments, and avoid strenuous activity during the hottest parts of the day. Heatstroke is a medical emergency requiring immediate attention.
