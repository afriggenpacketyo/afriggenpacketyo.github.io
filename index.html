This initiative follows a year-long BBC investigation that exposed drug dealing, child sexual exploitation, money laundering, and immigration offenses linked to businesses selling illegal cigarettes and vapes. The National Crime Agency (NCA) will lead the three-year effort, which includes increased funding for trading standards and proposals to strengthen law enforcement powers, such as extending closure orders for criminal businesses. The government aims to disrupt criminal operations, seize laundered cash, and imprison those involved, marking a significant step in tackling organized crime on Britain's high streets.
