NHS to Offer At-Home MS Treatment

2025-03-11 21:24:52 EDT

The UK's National Health Service (NHS) will provide a take-home tablet medication, cladribine, to thousands of multiple sclerosis (MS) patients in England. This will reduce the need for hospital visits for injections or infusions. NICE (the drug advisory body) approved the expansion of access, making the NHS the first in Europe to offer this at-home treatment. The treatment involves two courses of about 20 tablets, 12 months apart. While the cost per tablet is high, the NHS negotiates discounts. Rollout is expected to begin in April in England, with Wales and Northern Ireland likely to follow.

Wildfire Prevention Efforts Insufficient, Says Scientist

2025-03-11 21:12:29 EDT

A fire scientist at the Missoula Fire Sciences Laboratory argues that current wildfire prevention methods are inadequate. He believes that more prescribed burns and allowing some smaller fires to burn are necessary to reduce fuel buildup and prevent larger, more destructive fires. He criticizes the tendency to aggressively fight all fires, arguing that this approach exacerbates the problem in the long run. The scientist uses the recent devastating wildfires in Los Angeles as an example of the consequences of insufficient prevention.
