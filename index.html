Volker Türk, the UN's human rights chief, has urged for urgent international regulation of autonomous weapons systems, particularly drones, citing the increasing risk of war crimes. He highlighted the widespread use of drones in conflicts in Ukraine, Sudan, and the Middle East, noting their role in causing mass casualties and terrorizing civilians. Türk warned against the development of lethal autonomous weapons, stating that 'this reality is now upon us.' The UN is calling for a treaty similar to those governing chemical weapons to regulate drone use, emphasizing the need for human control and accountability to protect civilian lives.
