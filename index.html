Understanding the Distinctions Between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 Bankruptcy

2026-02-24 11:51:47 EST

Amid rising credit card debt and delinquency rates, many Americans are considering bankruptcy, with Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 offering distinct paths to debt relief. Chapter 7, known as "liquidation bankruptcy," typically discharges most unsecured debt in three to six months, though it involves a means test and potential asset liquidation. Chapter 13, conversely, restructures debt into a three-to-five-year repayment plan, suitable for those with assets to protect or higher incomes. Both options trigger an automatic stay on collection actions, providing immediate relief, but the choice depends on individual financial situations, income stability, and asset protection goals.
