Understanding the Removal of Written-Off Debt from Credit Reports

2025-11-18 13:01:56 EST

This article explains the implications of having a debt "written off" or "charged off" by a lender and the conditions under which it can be removed from a credit report. A charge-off, which is an accounting action for the creditor, remains a legal obligation for the borrower and negatively impacts their credit score for up to seven years. Removal is possible if the seven-year reporting period expires, the information is proven to be inaccurate, or in rare cases, through a goodwill adjustment or a "pay-for-delete" agreement with a debt collector.
