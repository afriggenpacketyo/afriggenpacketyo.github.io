The decision comes after revelations that Epstein, a convicted sex offender, met at least two victims at the camp and had donated significantly to the institution. The lodge, built with Epstein's contributions, has become associated with negative connotations that the school states are "not reflective of who we are." While internal reviews found no reports of misconduct involving Epstein at Interlochen, the school is cooperating with an external investigation into historical allegations and is committed to upholding its values.
