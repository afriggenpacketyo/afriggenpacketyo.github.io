This pattern was primarily caused by an area of low pressure, or an upper-level trough, that lingered over the eastern U.S. This trough weakened the western edge of the Bermuda High pressure system, which typically steers hurricanes westward toward the U.S. Instead, the steering currents consistently deflected the 13 named storms of the season away from the mainland, a significant deviation from recent years of destructive landfalls.
