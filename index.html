Three men indicted for allegedly robbing crypto owner of $6.5 million at gunpoint

2026-05-12 10:27:29 EDT

Three Tennessee men have been indicted in federal court on charges of robbery and kidnapping for allegedly forcing a California cryptocurrency owner to transfer $6.5 million at gunpoint. Prosecutors allege the men posed as delivery drivers and used zip ties and duct tape to restrain victims before accessing their crypto accounts. The indictment details a violent robbery spree targeting multiple victims in the Bay Area and Los Angeles. The accused individuals face potential life imprisonment if convicted. U.S. Attorney Craig H. Missakian described the scheme as "brazen, violent, and dangerous," emphasizing the perpetrators' intent to steal vast sums of cryptocurrency.

Canvas parent company reaches deal with hackers to delete stolen data

2026-05-12 10:23:00 EDT

Instructure, the parent company of the educational platform Canvas, has reached an agreement with hackers to delete data stolen during a recent cyberattack. The breach caused significant disruption for students, particularly during finals week. While Instructure did not disclose details of the agreement, including whether a payment was involved, they confirmed receiving digital confirmation of data destruction. The hacking group ShinyHunters had claimed responsibility and threatened to leak data from nearly 9,000 schools. Instructure CEO Steve Daly apologized for communication failures during the incident and stated the company is conducting forensic analysis and enhancing security measures to prevent future breaches.
