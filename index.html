Inherited Debt Generally Does Not Impact Social Security Benefits

2026-05-29 12:16:54 EDT

Inherited debt, such as credit card balances or medical bills, typically does not directly affect an individual's Social Security benefits. Debts are generally paid from the deceased person's estate, and if the estate is insufficient, the debts often go unpaid. While co-signing loans or holding joint accounts can create personal liability, Social Security benefits are largely protected from garnishment by commercial creditors under federal law. The government can offset benefits for certain federal debts like unpaid taxes or defaulted student loans, but inherited personal debts do not fall into these categories. The primary risk to benefits comes from an individual's own outstanding debts.
