2026-04-13 12:10:56 EDT

A coalition of nearly 1,000 Hollywood artists and creators, including prominent figures like Ben Stiller and Joaquin Phoenix, have voiced strong opposition to Paramount's proposed acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery. They argue that the merger would further consolidate the media landscape, reduce competition, and lead to fewer production jobs and audience choices. The letter highlights a significant decline in Hollywood jobs since 2022 and points to previous consolidations that resulted in widespread cutbacks. Former Nigerian Oil Minister Denies Bribery Allegations in UK Court

2026-04-13 12:05:59 EDT

Diezani Alison-Madueke, a former Nigerian oil minister, has denied all allegations of asking for or taking bribes during her tenure. Appearing in Southwark Crown Court, she stated she "tried to push back on corruption" in Nigeria. The prosecution alleges that businessmen funded lavish spending sprees for her, including significant expenditures at luxury retailers and on home renovations in the UK. Alison-Madueke claims that the costs incurred were later repaid by the state-owned Nigerian National Petroleum Company (NNPC) and that a service company was established to manage logistics due to issues with the NNPC's financial structure. She asserted that she did not abuse her office and that the money spent had nothing to do with her.

Sid Krofft, Co-Creator of Iconic Children's Shows, Dies at 96

2026-04-13 12:05:13 EDT

Sid Krofft, the puppeteer and co-creator of beloved 1970s children's television shows such as "H.R. Pufnstuf" and "Land of the Lost," has passed away at the age of 96. Krofft, along with his brother Marty, produced a string of fantastical and colorful series that defined Saturday morning television for a generation. His career began as a young puppeteer, eventually leading to the creation of Sid & Marty Krofft Pictures. Known for his creative vision and dedication to his craft, Krofft remained active in public appearances until his final months. His legacy includes a unique contribution to children's entertainment, characterized by imaginative characters and whimsical storytelling.

Super Typhoon Sinlaku Threatens Remote U.S. Islands with Destructive Winds and Flooding

2026-04-13 12:03:10 EDT

Super Typhoon Sinlaku, the strongest storm globally this year, is on a direct path toward the Northern Mariana Islands, with landfall expected on Tuesday. The National Weather Service warns of destructive winds, heavy rainfall, and flooding. Guam is also under a tropical storm warning, with potential for damaging winds. President Trump has approved emergency disaster declarations for Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands, authorizing additional aid. Sinlaku is projected to hit the islands as a Category 4 or 5 typhoon, equivalent to a major hurricane. Residents and military personnel are urged to prepare for severe weather conditions. The Joint Typhoon Warning Center is monitoring the storm's progression.
