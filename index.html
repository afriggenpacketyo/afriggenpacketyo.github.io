Understanding the 90-Day Preference Rule in Chapter 7 Bankruptcy

2026-01-20 11:54:10 EST

The "90-day rule," or preference period, in Chapter 7 bankruptcy allows a trustee to review and potentially reverse payments of $600 or more made to creditors within 90 days before filing. This rule ensures fair treatment of all creditors, meaning payments made to specific creditors, especially large or unusual sums, can be recovered and redistributed proportionally among all creditors in the bankruptcy estate. For "insiders" like family members or business partners, this preference period extends to one year. Understanding this rule is crucial for borrowers, as attempts to strategically pay down certain debts before filing can backfire, leading to complications and increased legal costs. Consulting a debt expert is advisable to navigate these timing rules (not investment advice).
