The New York Times has filed a motion to quash subpoenas issued by the Justice Department to journalists who reported on security concerns regarding the new Air Force One. The newspaper argues that the subpoenas are in bad faith, violate constitutional rights, and aim to punish the Times for its coverage. The subpoenas, which require journalists to testify before a federal grand jury, are seen by press freedom advocates as an effort to intimidate news organizations and compel reporters to reveal their sources. The Justice Department contends that reporters are not the targets, but rather those who leak classified information, emphasizing its role in protecting national secrets.

UK Weather: Weekend Cooldown Expected After Prolonged Heatwave

2026-07-15 20:31:59 EDT

The UK is anticipating a cooldown this weekend, marking the end of a prolonged heatwave for some regions. Fresher northerly winds will cause temperatures to drop significantly in Scotland, Northern Ireland, and northern England. However, temperatures will remain warmer than average across much of the UK, particularly in southern England, where heatwave conditions have persisted for 12 consecutive days. The forecast indicates continued dry and sunny weather into early next week, with no significant rainfall expected. This prolonged dry spell has led to hosepipe bans for over eight million households in England.

US Mint to Strike Commemorative Dollar Coin Featuring President Trump

2026-07-15 20:25:12 EDT

The U.S. Mint will begin striking a commemorative $1 coin featuring President Donald Trump to celebrate the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent announced the initiative, stating the coin symbolizes American values and patriotism. While not made of real gold, it will have a gold-like finish and be available in the fall. This marks the first time a living president will be featured on U.S. currency in a century, permitted under the Circulating Collectible Coin Redesign Act of 2020. However, questions have been raised about the legality of the coin's production, as two members of the Citizens Coinage Advisory Committee stated they never reviewed the design as required by law.

Newborns in England to Be Screened for Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA)

2026-07-15 20:20:52 EDT

All newborn babies in England will undergo screening for the genetic disease spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) as part of a major study. This initiative is a significant victory for families affected by SMA, including former Little Mix singer Jesy Nelson, whose twin daughters were diagnosed with the condition. Early detection and treatment of SMA are crucial, as gene therapies can significantly improve outcomes if administered before symptoms emerge. The screening aims to identify the condition early, allowing for timely intervention that can transform babies' lives and prevent irreversible damage. Nelson described the announcement as a triumph for every family impacted by SMA.
