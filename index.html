Singapore-Based Scientist Warns of Potential Hack of China's Quantum Satellite

2025-05-31 18:00:08 EDT

A scientist based in Singapore has raised concerns about the security of China's quantum satellite, Micius, stating that it could be vulnerable to hacking. The vulnerability stems from slight time delays between the satellite's onboard lasers, which could be exploited by eavesdroppers. The scientist, Alexander Miller, analyzed communication data between a ground station and Micius, finding that the distribution of quantum keys was insecure due to these time delays. Quantum communication, which relies on quantum physics-based cryptography, is theoretically unhackable, but realistic devices are susceptible to side-channel attacks that exploit flaws in implementation, according to Miller. The finding raises questions about the security of China's expanding quantum communication network.
