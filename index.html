LA Fire Survivors Face Financial Crisis as Temporary Housing Funds Deplete

2026-05-07 13:00:00 EDT

Nearly 40% of Los Angeles County fire survivors are facing a significant financial crisis as temporary housing insurance funds dwindle or dry up, according to a new survey. This impending shortage of coverage leaves many displaced residents concerned about their ability to return home. The report highlights widening gaps in recovery based on income and race, with lower-income households and people of color disproportionately affected. Many survivors have depleted savings, taken on debt, and are cutting back on essentials. Rebuilding costs are estimated to far exceed insurance payouts, raising questions about financial feasibility for many. The Department of Angels' report aims to guide recovery efforts and ensure assistance reaches all families.
