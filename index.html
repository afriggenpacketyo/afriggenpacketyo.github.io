Explainer: How the Urban Heat Island Effect Intensifies City Temperatures During Heatwaves

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As a heatwave impacts the UK, this article explains the "urban heat island effect," a phenomenon that causes cities to be significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. The effect is primarily caused by dark surfaces like asphalt and buildings, which absorb and retain the sun's heat, releasing it slowly and elevating temperatures, especially overnight. This process is compounded by waste heat from vehicles and air conditioning, and a lack of cooling vegetation and water sources found in the countryside. As climate change makes heatwaves more frequent and intense, this effect puts growing urban populations at greater risk from extreme heat and related health issues.
