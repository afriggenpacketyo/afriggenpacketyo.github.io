2026-05-12 06:00:19 EDT

Jiangsu Hengrui Pharmaceutical, China's largest drug company by market capitalization, has finalized a substantial global collaboration and licensing agreement with U.S. pharmaceutical giant Bristol Myers Squibb (BMS), a deal potentially valued at up to $15.2 billion. The comprehensive agreement covers 13 preclinical programs, specifically including four oncology and hematology assets originating from Hengrui, four immunology assets from BMS, and five novel programs slated for joint development utilizing Hengrui’s discovery platforms. Hengrui is set to receive an upfront payment of $600 million, with the overall deal value escalating to $15.2 billion if all specified development, regulatory, and commercial milestones are successfully achieved and co-discovery options are exercised. This landmark partnership signifies a major cross-border collaboration in advanced pharmaceutical innovation. (not investment advice)
