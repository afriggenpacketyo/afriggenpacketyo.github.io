Governors of two Russian regions bordering Ukraine have reported sustained power outages affecting residents due to Ukrainian attacks on energy infrastructure. Belgorod and Kursk regions are experiencing rolling blackouts, forcing hundreds to rely on "heating points." Both regions have been frequent targets of Ukrainian drone, missile, and artillery attacks. This follows a pattern of both countries targeting energy infrastructure, with Ukraine defending its retaliatory strikes as legitimate attacks on Russia's revenue sources that fund the war. Ukrainian officials report successfully intercepting a large number of Russian drones, while also sustaining Russian attacks on energy facilities, leading to partial power outages in some areas.

Shanghai Boosts Chip Fund Significantly to Advance China's Tech Self-Sufficiency Drive

2026-02-10 05:30:12 EST

The Shanghai Integrated Circuit Industry Investment Fund has significantly expanded its capital, increasing one of its funds by over eleven-fold to 6 billion yuan (US$794 million). This move aims to inject more funding into the city's chip firms as part of China's broader strategy to achieve technological self-reliance. The fund, which has already invested in over 20 local chip companies, including major foundries like SMIC, is a key initiative by local governments to bolster the domestic semiconductor industry. This expansion reflects China's commitment to overcoming external dependencies and strengthening its position in critical technology sectors amidst global tech competition.
