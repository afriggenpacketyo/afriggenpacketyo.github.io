The U.S. International Development Finance Corporation (DFC), established during the first Trump administration, is intensifying its efforts to counter China's Belt and Road Initiative. The DFC focuses on private-led projects in over 100 countries, primarily in Central and South America, Africa, Eastern Europe, and Asia-Pacific. With its portfolio cap increased to $205 billion, the agency has expanded scope for financing. This strategy aims to provide an alternative development model to China's state-led approach. The DFC's reauthorization by Congress with a dual mandate underscores a renewed commitment to promoting U.S. economic influence and development in key regions globally.
