A report to Congress by the National Taxpayer Advocate reveals that victims of tax-related identity theft are experiencing "unconscionable" delays at the IRS, with over 500,000 cases awaiting resolution and an average resolution time of nearly two years. This lengthy process causes significant financial hardship for taxpayers, potentially impacting their ability to cover basic living expenses. The report attributes these delays, in part, to steep staffing reductions at the IRS. The number of identity theft victims and wait times have increased since 2023, highlighting a growing problem that the agency is struggling to address effectively.
