Owner of Swiss ski bar detained for 90 days following deadly New Year's Eve fire

2026-01-12 07:56:13 EST

A judge in Switzerland has ordered Jacques Moretti, co-owner of the Le Constellation ski resort bar, to be held in detention for 90 days following a deadly fire on New Year's Eve that killed 40 people, many of them under 20. Prosecutors consider Moretti, a French national, a flight risk. He and his wife, Jessica, are suspected of manslaughter and arson by negligence. The fire is believed to have been started by sparklers used with champagne bottles, igniting sound-insulating foam on the ceiling. Authorities confirmed the bar had not undergone safety checks for five years, a significant lapse given annual inspection requirements. The couple's detention is provisional and can be renewed, with bail to be set later. The tragedy has deeply affected the victims' families.
