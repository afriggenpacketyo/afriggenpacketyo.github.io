Iran's Hormuz Strait toll demands clash with international maritime law governing natural straits

2026-05-28 11:35:00 EDT

Iran's imposition of tolls for passage through the Strait of Hormuz, described as 'war reparations' and payment for 'navigational services,' contradicts international maritime law. Unlike man-made waterways like the Suez and Panama Canals, which are governed by specific conventions allowing for maintenance and operational fees, natural straits used for international shipping are protected by the UN Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS). This convention grants transit passage rights without delay and prohibits coastal states from charging full transit tolls, limiting them to service charges. Major global shipping players are refusing to pay, while the US and China oppose the levy, viewing it as a 'maritime protection racket.'
