Businesses Circumvent Tariffs Using 'First Sale Rule'

2025-05-26 01:04:41 EDT

Businesses are using a legal workaround known as the "first sale rule" to minimize the impact of tariffs, which has been around since 1988 but is gaining renewed attention. This rule allows U.S. importers to calculate customs duties based on the initial sale price from the overseas producer to an intermediary, rather than the inflated price from the vendor to the U.S. retailer. To apply the rule, there must be at least two sales involving independent parties, proof that the item was destined for the U.S., and documentation of the first sale price. While it requires trust and transparency between parties, the potential cost savings make it worthwhile, particularly for higher-value consumer goods and luxury products.
