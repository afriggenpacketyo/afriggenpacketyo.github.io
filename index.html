The Story Behind the Name of Juneteenth

2025-06-19 10:21:00 EDT

Juneteenth, a federal holiday observed on June 19th, commemorates the end of slavery in the United States. The name "Juneteenth" is a portmanteau combining "June" and "nineteenth," referencing the date in 1865 when enslaved people in Texas were informed of their freedom under the Emancipation Proclamation. Although President Lincoln signed the Proclamation in 1863, it wasn't until Union soldiers arrived in Galveston, Texas, that the news reached the westernmost Confederate state. Juneteenth is celebrated with parades, parties, concerts, and educational events, memorializing the anniversary of independence and reflecting on the country's history.
