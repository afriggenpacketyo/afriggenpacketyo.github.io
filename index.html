A New York Times investigation, delving into thousands of records, concluded that Jeffrey Epstein, a college dropout, amassed his vast fortune primarily through "scams, theft and lies," rather than financial genius. Starting at Bear Stearns, Epstein leveraged connections but later allegedly engaged in a $460-million Ponzi scheme with Steven Hoffenberg, though never charged. His significant wealth largely stemmed from misappropriating "several hundred million dollars" from retail mogul Les Wexner, selling properties and a private jet to himself at discounted prices. Even after becoming a registered sex offender, Epstein maintained financial relationships with major banks like JPMorgan and Deutsche Bank, who later paid settlements to his victims, underscoring his manipulative practices and the systemic failures that enabled his illicit activities.
