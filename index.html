Although China has temporarily eased automotive chip restrictions for a 12-month period, deep concerns remain regarding Europe's significant asymmetric trade deficit and its critical dependency on China in strategic sectors like clean technology. Experts contend that China leverages these dependencies to project power and gain economic advantages, as evidenced by demands for confidential business information from German car companies in exchange for vital magnet imports. This precarious situation is intensifying calls within the EU to move beyond its current "de-risking" approach and consider deploying stronger anti-coercion instruments to rebalance the relationship.
