The article explores the historical reasons behind the use of "soccer" in the U.S. and Canada to refer to the sport known globally as football. According to a University of Michigan professor, the term "soccer" originated in elite Oxford universities in the 1880s as a slang abbreviation for "association football," differentiating it from "rugby football." This linguistic trend of shortening words and adding "-er" spread, and "soccer" was adopted in various English-speaking countries, including Australia and South Africa. The term gained traction in the U.S. as American football, which evolved from rugby, also grew in popularity during the same period.
