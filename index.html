India's Forgotten Brush with Presidential Rule Under Indira Gandhi

2025-06-08 18:02:57 EDT

During the Emergency in the mid-1970s, Indira Gandhi's government considered shifting India from a parliamentary democracy to a presidential system. Inspired by Charles de Gaulle's France, this proposal aimed to centralize executive power and sideline the judiciary. Although the plan never fully materialized, it influenced the Forty-second Amendment Act of 1976, which expanded Parliament's powers and limited judicial review. After Gandhi's defeat in 1977, subsequent amendments rolled back these authoritarian provisions, but the idea resurfaced in 1982, underscoring the enduring tension between centralized power and democratic checks and balances in India.
