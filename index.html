Scientists found that global warming, primarily driven by the burning of fossil fuels, boosted Ragasa's winds by 7% and rainfall by 12% at landfall, intensifying its destructive power. This article emphasizes that as the planet continues to warm, tropical cyclones are expected to become increasingly destructive, posing a growing threat to coastal regions.
