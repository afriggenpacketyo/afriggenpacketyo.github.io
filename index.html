This constitutional and legal requirement halts all non-essential government functions, forcing many federal employees to be furloughed without pay. Essential services, such as national security, law enforcement, and air traffic control, continue to operate, but employees in these roles also do not get paid until funding is restored. Programs with permanent funding, like Social Security and Medicare, continue to issue payments, although their administrative services may be slowed. The last shutdown in 2018-2019 lasted 34 days and caused significant economic disruption.
