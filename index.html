2025-08-28 14:51:29 EDT

European leaders expressed outrage after Russia's deadliest attack on Kyiv since July killed at least 21 people, including four children, and extensively damaged the EU's delegation office and the British Council. EU Commission President Ursula von der Leyen reported two missiles hitting within 50 meters of the diplomatic mission within 20 seconds. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky condemned the "terror and barbarism," reiterating calls for new, tough sanctions on Russia, while UK Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer accused Putin of "sabotaging hopes of peace." The attacks, which followed a US-led diplomatic push for peace, are seen by European officials as a deliberate escalation, demonstrating Russia's disinterest in negotiations and prompting calls for a unified response from the international community.

Franco-German Leaders Address Disputes Over Future European Fighter Jet Project

2025-08-28 14:46:00 EDT

French President Emmanuel Macron and German Chancellor Friedrich Merz are set to discuss the Future Combat Air System (FCAS), Europe's most expensive defense project, amidst a persistent dispute between French and German manufacturers. The project, aiming to combine a new European fighter jet with drones for reduced U.S. dependence, faces disagreements primarily over the plane's manufacturing, with French manufacturer Dassault seeking greater decision-making power and Airbus Germany insisting on its production share. The leaders' unexpected attendance at the Franco-German Defense and Security Council meeting in Toulon aims to provide a clear political signal for continued collaboration. However, time is running out for a definitive solution for the next phase, which involves constructing the first prototype, as France and Germany grapple with financial and industrial priorities.
