The article explains that these events are broadly classified into four types—falls, topples, slides, and flows—based on the movement of materials like rock, debris, or earth. Landslides are typically triggered when the downward force on a slope overcomes its resistance, often due to factors like excessive rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic activity, or human interference. While natural disasters like these can be devastating, the risk can be mitigated. Prevention strategies include improving drainage systems, reinforcing slopes with retaining walls, and avoiding construction in high-risk areas near steep slopes or erosion valleys.
