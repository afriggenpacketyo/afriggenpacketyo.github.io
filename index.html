A Chinese commercial satellite, Hukeda-2, has successfully completed a pioneering refuelling test in low Earth orbit, utilizing an innovative flexible "octopus tentacle" robotic arm. This significant technological advancement, led by the Tsinghua Shenzhen International Graduate School, marks a crucial step towards extending spacecraft lifespans and developing advanced in-orbit servicing capabilities. The robotic arm's unique design allows it to curl, twist, and precisely wrap around objects to connect with target ports, a complex maneuver likened to "threading a needle in space" while hurtling around Earth at high speeds. This article highlights China's impressive progress in space technology and its potential for future space missions, including satellite maintenance and orbital debris removal.
