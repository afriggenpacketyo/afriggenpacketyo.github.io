German Study Reveals Institutional Racism Within Public Agencies

A comprehensive three-year study by the Research Institute for Social Cohesion (FGZ) in Leipzig has found evidence of institutional racism embedded within German public agencies, including job centers, immigration offices, police, customs, courts, and social work departments. The research indicates that racist discrimination manifests in discretionary decision-making and organizational practices across these institutions, varying in intensity. While a survey of 13,000 employees did not reveal uniformly higher levels of ethnically discriminatory attitudes compared to the general population, the study highlights language barriers as a key structural risk and points to inconsistencies in support for applicants with limited German skills. Recommendations include establishing independent complaint offices, expanding anti-racism training, and actively recruiting individuals from affected groups.
