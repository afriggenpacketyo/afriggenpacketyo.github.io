Explaining the Formal Criteria for Declaring a Famine Amid Gaza's Hunger Crisis

The Integrated Food Security Phase Classification (IPC) requires three strict criteria to be met: at least 20% of households facing an extreme lack of food, 30% of children suffering acute malnutrition, and a death rate of at least two adults per 10,000 people per day from non-trauma causes. While Gaza is currently in phase four ("emergency"), collecting reliable data, especially on mortality in a conflict zone, is challenging. Experts warn that people are already dying from hunger-related causes long before the technical threshold for a famine declaration is met.
