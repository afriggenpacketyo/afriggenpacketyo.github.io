Creditors May Continue Charging Interest on Debts After Borrower's Death

Most debts remain governed by their original agreements until resolved, with these additional charges typically becoming obligations of the deceased's estate. During probate, the executor uses estate assets to pay valid claims, including accrued interest, before distributing inheritances. Families should avoid making voluntary payments from personal funds and consider consulting an estate attorney to understand legal responsibilities and potential settlement options.
