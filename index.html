Europol has issued a stark warning that criminal gangs are recruiting children on an "industrial scale" through online platforms for "violence as a service" crimes across Western Europe, a disturbing trend now observed moving into the United States. These vulnerable youths, some as young as 13, are systematically lured via gaming sites and encrypted messaging apps to commit heinous acts, including contract killings, bombings, kidnappings, and torture, often for minimal payment or a deceptive sense of belonging. Europol's dedicated Grimm Task Force has already made nearly 300 arrests and identified thousands of online accounts linked to these crimes. This digital-age twist on criminal recruitment exploits children's inexperience, rendering them both perpetrators and tragic victims.
