Understanding the 180-day rule in bankruptcy and its implications for asset distribution

The 180-day rule in bankruptcy is a critical provision that determines whether certain assets acquired shortly after filing must be included in the bankruptcy estate. This rule commonly applies to inheritances, life insurance proceeds, and property settlements from divorce or separation. If these assets are received within 180 days of filing, they may be used to repay creditors, particularly in Chapter 7 bankruptcies. In Chapter 13, such assets can increase the repayment amount required in the plan. The rule does not typically cover regular wages earned post-filing or bonuses tied to future work. Understanding this rule is crucial for timing bankruptcy filings to protect assets.
