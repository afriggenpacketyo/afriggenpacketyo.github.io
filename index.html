Congressman Proposes Bill to Halt Gasoline Exports Amid Iran War Price Spikes

2026-04-14 12:00:43 EDT

Amidst soaring gasoline prices driven by the Iran war, Congressman Ro Khanna is introducing legislation to ban U.S. gasoline exports during periods of price spikes. The bill proposes halting exports when national average gas prices exceed $3.12 per gallon, aiming to lower domestic costs for consumers. Khanna criticizes 'big oil companies' for profiting from the conflict and advocates for a new energy policy focused on American consumers and renewable energy. While the proposal faces political challenges, it highlights the domestic economic impact of the Iran war and the push for energy security through diversified sources.
