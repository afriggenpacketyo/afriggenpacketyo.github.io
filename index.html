These scarves served practical purposes like protection and injury treatment, but also held symbolic meaning. The French army, impressed by the Croats, adopted this neckwear, with King Louis XIV popularizing it in French fashion, leading to its spread across Europe. Many European languages still retain an etymological link to "Croats" in their word for necktie, illustrating its enduring cultural and historical impact.
