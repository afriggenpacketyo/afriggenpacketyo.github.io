2025-12-23 11:46:53 EST

ServiceNow is poised to acquire cybersecurity startup Armis in a substantial $7.75 billion cash transaction, a strategic move that CEO Bill McDermott asserts will significantly augment its cybersecurity capabilities within the burgeoning AI landscape and more than triple its market opportunity for security and risk solutions. This acquisition is a key component of ServiceNow's aggressive 2025 deal-making spree, which has also seen the company acquire AI agent platform Moveworks and identity security platform Veza. McDermott envisions this consolidation establishing ServiceNow as the "only AI control tower" capable of driving workflow, action, and business outcomes across diverse environments, underscoring the vital importance of safeguarding enterprises against increasingly sophisticated AI-powered threats. Armis, which was valued at $6.1 billion in November, has demonstrated robust financial health with over $340 million in annual recurring revenue and 50% year-over-year growth.
