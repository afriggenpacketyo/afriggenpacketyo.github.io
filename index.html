France's hard-left France Unbowed (LFI) party evacuated its Paris headquarters due to a bomb threat, following accusations of responsibility for the fatal beating of far-right activist Quentin Deranque. Deranque, 23, died from a severe brain injury after being attacked by at least six people following a far-right protest in Lyon. Eleven suspects have been detained for questioning, including an assistant to an LFI member of parliament, who has since been fired. This incident has significantly escalated political tensions between France's far-right and far-left factions ahead of upcoming municipal elections and the looming 2027 presidential race, prompting President Emmanuel Macron to call for calm.
