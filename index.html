El Niño typically fosters warmer ocean waters in the Eastern Pacific, creating favorable conditions for increased hurricane activity and development by reducing vertical wind shear. Conversely, El Niño tends to create cooler waters and more wind shear in the Atlantic basin, statistically leading to a less active hurricane season there. This shift follows a period of La Niña, which was associated with above-average Atlantic hurricane seasons in recent years.
