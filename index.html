Understanding the Transition of HELOCs After 10 Years

2025-04-30 15:29:41 EDT

Home equity lines of credit (HELOCs) are structured with a draw period and a repayment period, with significant changes occurring after 10 years. During the draw period, borrowers can access funds up to their approved credit limit, typically making interest-only payments. After 10 years, the HELOC transitions to the repayment period, ending access to funds and increasing monthly payments to include both principal and interest. Strategies to prepare for this transition include making principal payments early, calculating future payments, considering refinancing options, building an emergency fund, and paying down high-rate debt.
