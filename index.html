The Louvre Museum in Paris is significantly tightening its security following an October heist where thieves, posing as workers, stole nine pieces of jewelry from the Apollo Gallery. Maintenance crews were seen installing metal bars on the infamous second-floor window used for the break-in, which exposed severe security lapses, including a lack of cameras in 35% of the Denon Wing. While all four suspected thieves have been arrested and charged, only one crown has been recovered. The museum plans further upgrades, including new anti-intrusion systems by early December and over 100 new cameras by the end of 2026, aiming to prevent future high-profile thefts at the world's most-visited museum.
