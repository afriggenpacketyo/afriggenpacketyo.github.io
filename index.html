2026-05-10 10:48:33 EDT

Streaming companies, led by Netflix, are increasingly valuing viewers who watch the most, shifting from a subscription-only model to one combining subscriptions with advertising. Netflix's recent price hike for its standard ad-free plan to around $20, while offering a $9 ad-supported tier, explicitly highlights this strategic pivot. Ad-supported subscribers can generate significant revenue, potentially exceeding ad-free users, because ads are sold based on viewership engagement. This industry-wide shift is driven by consumer resistance to continually rising subscription costs and the observation that most new subscriber growth now originates from ad-supported tiers. The ultimate goal is for streaming platforms to achieve indifference between the two subscriber types, with advertising emerging as a primary driver of future revenue growth.
