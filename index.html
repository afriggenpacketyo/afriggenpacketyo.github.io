Trump Administration Finalizes Federal Student Loan Caps for Graduate and Professional Degrees

2026-04-30 14:37:12 EDT

The Department of Education has finalized new federal student loan limits for graduate and professional degrees, effective July 1, 2026. The regulations introduce annual and aggregate lifetime borrowing caps, aiming to prevent excessive student debt. Graduate students can borrow up to $20,500 annually and $100,000 in total, while those in professional programs like medicine and law have limits of $50,000 annually and $200,000 total. The graduate PLUS loan program, which previously allowed borrowing up to the full cost of attendance, will be eliminated. While concerns were raised about the definition of professional degrees, the administration stated the rule aims to ensure access to higher education without overwhelming debt, while also encouraging institutions to reduce costs.

2026-04-30 14:33:00 EDT

International reports consistently describe Serbia's media landscape as deteriorating, with a shrinking space for independent journalism and a rise in government propaganda. The ruling Serbian Progressive Party (SNS) has systematically gained control over media outlets through financial incentives for cooperation and isolation for dissent. Pro-government media receive substantial public funding through project co-financing and state advertising, while independent outlets face market pressure and avoid advertising to maintain relations with authorities. This environment blurs the line between journalism and propaganda, with critical voices often demonized. The media's pervasive pro-government stance is evident in constant presidential presence and synchronized messaging across various platforms, leading Serbia to drop significantly in press freedom rankings.
