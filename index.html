2026-02-25 01:18:38 EST

Scotland has initiated a £36 million pilot project to establish 16 new walk-in GP clinics, with the First Minister pledging to deliver over one million additional appointments annually. These clinics are designed to provide on-the-day care for non-emergency medical issues, aiming to alleviate the persistent pressure on traditional GP practices and mitigate the morning "8am rush" for appointments. However, a BBC Verify analysis suggests that the ambitious one-million-appointment target requires careful context and faces significant challenges concerning the speed of implementation and adequate staffing. Critics also question whether this investment fundamentally addresses the long-standing issues within primary care, such as chronic GP recruitment and retention problems, rather than merely offering a supplementary service.
