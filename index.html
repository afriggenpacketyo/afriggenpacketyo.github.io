2026-02-10 08:00:00 EST

Senator Bernie Sanders is set to officially launch a campaign to place a controversial 5% one-time tax on California billionaires' assets on the November ballot. The proposal, backed by the Service Employees International Union-United Healthcare Workers West, aims to backfill federal healthcare funding cuts enacted by the Trump administration and prevent hospital closures. Sanders, a popular figure among California liberals, argues the tax is common sense for preserving access to life-saving medical care. While supporters highlight its necessity for vulnerable residents, opponents warn it could stifle innovation and prompt an exodus of wealthy entrepreneurs from the state.
