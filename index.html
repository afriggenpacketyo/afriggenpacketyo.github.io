Israel's Knesset has passed a law enabling the death penalty for Palestinians convicted in military courts of deadly "acts of terrorism." The legislation, pushed by far-right factions, mandates execution within 90 days of conviction, with a potential postponement. While theoretically applicable to Israeli citizens, its practical application is limited to cases intended to "negate the existence of the state of Israel." The law has drawn strong criticism from the UK, France, Germany, and Italy for potentially undermining democratic principles, and condemnation from the Palestinian Authority and Hamas, who view it as legitimizing extrajudicial killing and threatening Palestinian prisoners.
