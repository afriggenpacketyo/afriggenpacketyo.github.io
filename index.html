Several Republican-led states are expanding their cooperation with federal immigration authorities by using public health agencies to identify and report Medicaid recipients whose legal status is in question. States like North Carolina, Indiana, Louisiana, Montana, and Wyoming are enacting laws that go beyond federal requirements, mandating that health agencies flag individuals for U.S. Department of Homeland Security. This trend raises concerns among health policy researchers and immigration attorneys, who fear it deters immigrants, including U.S. citizens in mixed-status families, from seeking essential healthcare due to fear of deportation. Critics argue these policies can inadvertently harm citizen children by denying families access to necessary medical care and erode trust in healthcare systems.

2026-05-14 05:00:00 EDT

His comments come at a sensitive time for the Trump administration as it faces scrutiny over its handling of justice-related matters.
