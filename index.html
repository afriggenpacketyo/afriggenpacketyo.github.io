Understanding Current Wage Garnishment Rules for Borrowers

2026-03-19 11:14:12 EDT

Wage garnishment, a legally sanctioned method for creditors to seize a portion of a borrower's paycheck, is a critical concern as national debt levels reach unprecedented highs. Federal law limits garnishment to the lesser of 25% of disposable earnings or the amount by which weekly income exceeds 30 times the federal minimum wage, but specific exceptions exist for federal student loans, unpaid taxes, and child support, often allowing for higher percentages. State laws can further modify these rules, and certain income streams like Social Security benefits are protected. Borrowers are entitled to notice and the right to challenge garnishment, and employers are prohibited from retaliating for a single order, highlighting the necessity of understanding these complex regulations to protect one's financial foundation.
