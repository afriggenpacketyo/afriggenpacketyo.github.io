As a heatwave affects the UK, cities like London and Cambridge are experiencing significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to the urban heat island effect. This phenomenon occurs because dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, releasing it slowly and elevating temperatures, especially at night. The lack of vegetation and water sources in cities further exacerbates the heat, while waste heat from human activities also contributes. With global temperatures rising, this effect makes growing urban populations increasingly vulnerable to the dangerous impacts of extreme heat and associated air pollution.
