The rising cost of oil, driven by the conflict in the Middle East, has raised concerns about its impact on household bills and pump prices. Miliband indicated that direct support or an extension of the fuel duty freeze could be considered if the conflict persists. He also reiterated the government's commitment to clean energy, arguing that new North Sea exploration licenses would not lower bills and that the focus should be on homegrown power. The government is planning to expedite the construction of new nuclear power stations.
