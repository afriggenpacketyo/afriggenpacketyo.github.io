The Etymological Journey of 'Cravat' from 17th-Century Battlefields to Modern Fashion

2026-01-21 05:00:00 EST

The word "cravat," the luxurious predecessor to the modern necktie, traces its origins to the battlefields of 17th-century Europe, specifically to Croatian mercenaries during the Thirty Years' War. These soldiers wore distinctive red scarves, known as "cravates," for practical purposes like protection and treating injuries, but also as symbols of trust and love from their wives. The French army adopted this fashion, leading to its spread across Europe, notably by King Louis XIV. The necktie, patented in New York in the early 20th century, evolved from the cravat, becoming a symbol of professionalism and personal expression, with its name still linked to "Croats" in many European languages.
