Explainer: The Causes and Solutions for Urban Heat Islands

2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

Urban heat islands are metropolitan areas that become significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas, a phenomenon that intensifies heatwaves for city dwellers. This effect is caused by dark surfaces like asphalt and concrete absorbing heat, tall buildings trapping warm air, and a lack of cooling vegetation. The temperature difference can be as much as 15 degrees Celsius, posing health risks. Solutions to mitigate this effect include greening cities with more trees and parks, installing "cool roofs" that reflect sunlight, and using permeable pavements that enhance water evaporation. Cities like New York, Tokyo, and Singapore are implementing these strategies.
