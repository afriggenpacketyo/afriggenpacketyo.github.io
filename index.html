Explainer: What a Government Shutdown Means and How It Works

2025-10-20 09:20:13 EDT

A government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass the necessary appropriations bills to fund federal agencies before the fiscal year begins on October 1. Without this authorization, agencies must cease all non-essential functions, as mandated by the Antideficiency Act. During a shutdown, essential employees, such as active-duty military and air traffic controllers, must continue to work but do not get paid until funding is restored. Non-essential workers are furloughed, though a 2019 law now guarantees them back pay. While entitlement programs like Social Security continue, other services can be disrupted, and past shutdowns have cost taxpayers billions of dollars.
