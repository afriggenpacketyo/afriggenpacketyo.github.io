Thirty-eight teams across Spain's top two football divisions will wear retro shirts this weekend to celebrate their club's cultural identity and history. The kits, inspired by iconic past designs, pay homage to each club's traditions and fan base. This initiative, a collaboration between La Liga and fashion, aims to connect with the growing interest in retro football merchandise. While Barcelona, Rayo Vallecano, Getafe, and Real Madrid are not participating in shirt design, they are still involved in the campaign. Referees and broadcast graphics will also adopt a vintage style, enhancing the nostalgic atmosphere of the weekend's matches.

Scottish crime boss Steven Lyons deported from Bali to Amsterdam

2026-04-08 05:44:48 EDT

Steven Lyons, a prominent Scottish gangland figure and Interpol fugitive, has been deported from Bali to Amsterdam after his arrest on March 28. Lyons was apprehended at Denpasar International Airport on arrival from Singapore, ten days after his wife was arrested in Dubai. Indonesian immigration officials described him as a "mafia boss" and confirmed his flight to Jakarta, followed by his onward journey to Amsterdam. Spanish authorities were involved in his escort, as he is wanted in Spain. Lyons is suspected of leading a transnational criminal organization involved in drug trafficking and money laundering, with alleged operations in multiple countries. His deportation is seen as a commitment to national security and preventing Indonesia from becoming a haven for international criminals.
