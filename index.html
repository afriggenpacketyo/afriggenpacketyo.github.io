Explainer: The Mechanics and Consequences of a U.S. Government Shutdown

2025-10-31 11:55:45 EDT

A U.S. government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass appropriations bills to fund federal agencies before the fiscal year begins on October 1. This legal requirement, rooted in the Antideficiency Act, forces all non-essential government functions to cease until funding is restored. During a shutdown, essential employees like active-duty military and air traffic controllers must work without immediate pay, while non-essential workers are furloughed. While entitlement programs like Social Security continue, other services can be severely disrupted, and the economic costs from lost productivity and back pay can amount to billions of dollars.
