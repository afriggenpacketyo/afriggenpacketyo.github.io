The UK's government-backed shared ownership housing scheme, intended to aid homeownership, is criticized as "wildly unaffordable" and leaving buyers exposed to escalating costs. A National Audit Office report highlights that many participants do not fully grasp the long-term financial risks, particularly concerning increasing service charges. Buyers, often owning a small share, are responsible for 100% of these charges, which can surge dramatically, making homes unaffordable or unsellable. The article details instances of service charges consuming over half of incomes and hindering "staircasing" to full ownership, prompting calls for better regulation and transparency to ensure the scheme genuinely provides affordable housing.
