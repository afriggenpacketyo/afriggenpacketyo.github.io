U.S. trade representative Jamieson Greer criticized Canada for "doubling down on globalization" and pointed out several persistent trade irritants, including provincial liquor policies and dairy quotas, which President Trump had previously labeled as "extraordinary tariffs." Despite these disagreements, both nations recognize the necessity of continued dialogue, although reaching a deal by the upcoming July 1st deadline appears improbable.
