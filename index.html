Three men have pleaded guilty to violent disorder charges stemming from protests in Southampton that followed the murder of Henry Nowak. Connor Bishop, Reece Robinson, and Noah Etherington admitted to the charges during court hearings. Their arrests followed the release of police bodycam footage related to Nowak's stabbing. The three men have been remanded in custody and are scheduled for sentencing at Southampton Crown Court. Another man pleaded guilty to violent disorder and possessing an offensive weapon, while a second man pleaded not guilty to assaulting a police officer. This article reports on the legal proceedings related to the protests.
