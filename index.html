Revolutionary Immune Reset Therapy Puts Lupus into Remission in Early UK Trials

2026-06-11 19:31:13 EDT

An experimental CAR-T cell therapy, designed to reset a malfunctioning immune system, has successfully put the autoimmune disease lupus into remission in early UK trials, offering significant hope for patients. One of the first patients, Katie Tinkler, diagnosed 30 years ago, reports feeling "never this good," now able to ski and off all lupus medication, with her organs having recovered. The therapy involves genetically modifying a patient's own T cells to target and destroy rogue B cells, which are responsible for attacking the body in autoimmune diseases, subsequently allowing new, healthy B cells to grow. Five out of six initial patients remain in remission after more than 18 months, suggesting this approach could potentially treat other similar autoimmune disorders like multiple sclerosis and rheumatoid arthritis.
