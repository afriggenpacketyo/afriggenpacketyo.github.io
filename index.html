Modern dentistry extensively utilizes plastic, from toothbrushes and pastes to fillings, aligners, and retainers, raising growing concerns about microplastic exposure. Orthodontist Adith Venugopal highlights that prescribing plastic devices from a medical standpoint, knowing they leach millions of particles annually, is troubling given links between plastic compounds and health issues. While the American Dental Association monitors research, it states no clinical evidence yet shows significant harm from these particles. The article notes that preliminary studies are investigating microplastic release from aligners and night guards, prompting some clinicians, like Venugopal, to opt for metal alternatives when medically appropriate. This discussion emphasizes the ethical considerations of plastic use in healthcare and the need for more research into its long-term effects on human health.
