The Director of Public Prosecutions stated the case failed because the deputy national security adviser, Matt Collins, was unwilling to classify China as an active national security threat, as required after a new court ruling. However, Collins testified he believed his evidence, detailing China's espionage and cyber threats, was sufficient and was surprised by the case's abandonment, asserting he could not use terms not aligned with government policy. This article highlights a significant political and legal row over accountability and the government's nuanced stance on China.
