Cities are experiencing peak temperatures during the current UK heatwave due to the urban heat island effect, which can make built-up areas several degrees warmer than surrounding rural locations. This phenomenon occurs because dark, hard surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, releasing it slowly as heat. The lack of vegetation and water, which provide natural cooling in the countryside, exacerbates this warming.
