Anduril CEO Brian Schimpf indicated the defense tech company is not in a hurry to go public, cautioning against IPOs "in the middle of a hype cycle" to secure favorable long-term investor returns. Despite the defense tech sector experiencing a significant boom, driven by President Donald Trump's military reindustrialization ambitions and record spending, the broader tech IPO market remains subdued. This is evident from SpaceX's post-debut share decline and the delayed public offerings of major AI firms like OpenAI and Anthropic. Schimpf acknowledged that the current "crazy high valuations" in the private market might not be rational, underscoring concerns about potential overvaluation. Anduril, which saw its valuation double to $61 billion in May, prioritizes strategic growth over a premature market debut (not investment advice).
