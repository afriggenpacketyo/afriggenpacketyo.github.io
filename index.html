While President Lincoln signed the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863, its implementation in Confederate territories was delayed until the Union Army's arrival in Texas. The 13th Amendment to the Constitution later abolished slavery nationwide. Celebrations of Juneteenth vary, often including parades, parties, concerts, and educational events, serving as a joyful anniversary of independence and a reminder of the country's foundation on centuries of slavery.
