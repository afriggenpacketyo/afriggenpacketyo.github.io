Despite past strains, Presidents Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva and Donald Trump exchanged public compliments following their meeting at the White House. Trump described the meeting as 'very good' and Lula as 'dynamic,' while Lula expressed satisfaction. However, the absence of a joint press conference suggests unresolved disagreements, particularly on issues like trade and crime. Lula acknowledged significant differences in trade discussions, proposing a working group to resolve disputes. The meeting occurred amidst ongoing U.S.-Brazil tensions regarding organized crime and potential interference in Brazil's elections. Experts suggest the lack of a joint statement indicates lingering issues, though the cordial reception signals a normalization of bilateral relations.
