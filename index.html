Operating from a dedicated $13 million factory, students earn course credit and gain hands-on experience, directly addressing a projected national shortage of over 500,000 skilled construction workers. The first homes, destined for residents affected by a 2013 flood and offered at subsidized prices, represent a significant achievement for students like senior Sean McCormick, who plans to pursue a trade career. This innovative initiative effectively combines practical education with tangible community benefits, fostering both individual opportunity and societal welfare.
