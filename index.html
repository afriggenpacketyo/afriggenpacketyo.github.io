Infrared Contact Lens Provides Night Vision

2025-06-01 08:43:00 EDT

Chinese researchers have developed an infrared contact lens that allows humans to see in the dark by converting infrared light into visible light. The lens combines conventional soft contact lenses with nanoparticles made of gold, sodium gadolinium fluoride, ytterbium, and erbium ions. These nanoparticles convert infrared light with wavelengths between 800 and 1,600 nanometers into visible light, though the resulting images are somewhat blurred. While not as powerful as night vision goggles, these lenses could be used in surgical procedures, encryption, or rescue operations, and they function even better with closed eyes. The ability to see in the dark could also be useful in counterfeit protection by revealing invisible features or inks on documents.
