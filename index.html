Understanding Hantavirus: Origins, Transmission, and Symptoms Amid Cruise Ship Outbreak

Hantaviruses are a family of viruses typically spread through contact with rodent urine and droppings, but the Andes strain, confirmed in some cases, can rarely spread human-to-human through close contact. Symptoms can range from flu-like to severe respiratory distress (HPS) or kidney issues (HFRS), with HPS having a 20-40% mortality rate. There is no specific treatment or widely available vaccine, though early medical support improves survival.
