2026-03-06 18:51:12 EST

Mount Pleasant FA, a Jamaican soccer team, faces a significant disadvantage for its CONCACAF Champions Cup match against the Galaxy, with up to 10 players denied U.S. visas by the State Department. Six Haitian players on the roster are directly impacted by the Trump administration's travel bans, which also affect citizens from other countries. This situation sparks concerns about the administration's adherence to exceptions for athletes participating in major sporting events, particularly with the upcoming World Cup involving qualifiers from several restricted nations. While CONCACAF is appealing the decision, the team may be forced to field youth academy players, severely compromising their ability to compete effectively in this crucial international club tournament.
