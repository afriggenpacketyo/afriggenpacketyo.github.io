Rats are trained for this work due to their keen sense of smell, low weight, and intelligence. During his five-year career, Magawa cleared approximately 1.5 million square feet, significantly contributing to demining efforts. The statue honors his life-saving work and the ongoing partnership between humans and animals in clearing dangerous remnants of conflict.
