Unraveling the Science and Mythology Behind Aurora Skies

The captivating aurora borealis and australis, vibrant green and purple lights dancing across polar skies, are a result of solar storms ejecting electrically charged particles that collide with Earth's atmospheric gases. While typically visible near the Arctic and Antarctic Circles, a recent solar maximum has expanded their visibility to lower latitudes. Beyond their scientific explanation, these celestial displays have inspired diverse myths, from Norse legends of Valkyries to Indigenous beliefs of spirits, highlighting humanity's enduring awe.
