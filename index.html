A bank levy by a debt collector is a serious legal action allowing creditors to seize funds directly from a debtor's bank account without prior notice. Once a court judgment is obtained, a creditor can request a writ of execution, compelling the bank to freeze and transfer funds to satisfy the debt, potentially draining the account entirely. While federal and some state laws offer exemptions for certain funds, such as Social Security benefits, understanding these protections is crucial. Acting quickly to contact an attorney, negotiate a settlement, or consider bankruptcy is essential to address a bank levy and protect remaining assets.
