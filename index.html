2025-08-27 10:33:56 EDT

India is bracing for a substantial export shock as President Donald Trump's 50% tariffs on a range of Indian products officially took effect, threatening its largest export market. The initial 25% tariff was compounded by an additional 25% due to India's purchases of Russian oil, impacting an estimated $48.2 billion worth of exports. Officials warn these duties could render shipments to the US commercially unviable, potentially leading to job losses and slower economic growth in labor-intensive sectors like textiles, gems and jewelry, and leather. Despite ongoing trade negotiations, India has resisted opening its agriculture and dairy sectors to cheaper American imports, with Prime Minister Modi vowing to protect farmers and small businesses, while the government explores local consumption boosts and new export markets.
