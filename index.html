2026-05-04 08:50:34 EDT

Global economies may be "sleepwalking" into a significant recession due to investors underestimating the impact of the oil price shock stemming from the Iran war, according to market intelligence analyst Amrita Sen. Despite the S&P 500 reaching new highs, Sen argues that the surge in oil prices, exceeding 50% since the conflict began, is being dismissed by investors, particularly regarding its effects on Asian economies. She believes current oil prices should be higher and equity markets weaker, warning of a "massive energy crisis." Sen expects $80-90 a barrel to become the new floor, with rising prices impacting LNG, chemicals, and fertilizers, leading to increased food costs. She anticipates Q2 results will be significantly weaker than Q1.
