Labour MP Kim Leadbeater, the proponent of the bill seeking to legalize assisted dying in England and Wales, has voiced concerns that some members of the House of Lords are deliberately attempting to 'frustrate' its progression into law. The legislation, which successfully passed its initial stage in the House of Commons, is now encountering over 1,000 proposed amendments in the Lords, resulting in significantly slow progress during its scrutiny phase. Leadbeater fears that peers are employing procedural tactics to delay the bill, despite the Commons having already voted in favor of legalizing assisted dying. Opponents, however, maintain that the legislation necessitates substantial redrafting to adequately protect vulnerable individuals, stressing the critical importance of meticulous scrutiny for such a sensitive and ethically complex piece of lawmaking.
