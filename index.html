Emily Saliers, one half of the folk duo Indigo Girls, has revealed she has been diagnosed with two incurable movement disorders that will permanently alter her singing voice. She disclosed in an Instagram video that she has cervical dystonia with torticollis, causing involuntary neck muscle contractions, and an essential tremor affecting her jaw, throat, and diaphragm. These conditions result in an uncontrollable vibrato and make it difficult to hold straight tones, a hallmark of the duo's harmonies. Saliers is undergoing various therapies and working with a vocal coach, acknowledging that her voice "will not be what it was," a difficult reality for her. The band plans to adapt their performances using additional singers and digital tools.
