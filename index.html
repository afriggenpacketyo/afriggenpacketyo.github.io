2025-08-27 09:00:02 EDT

Wealthy Americans are increasingly traveling to Europe to purchase luxury goods, such as high-end watches and fashion items, to avoid steep U.S. tariffs imposed by the Trump administration. Tariffs, including a 39% levy on Swiss goods and 15% on most EU goods, can add thousands of dollars to the cost of luxury items if bought in the U.S. Travelers can potentially save money through favorable exchange rates and by claiming value-added tax (VAT) refunds in Europe, which can be substantial. While customs regulations require declaring overseas purchases, the practicalities and potential VAT savings make these "tariff-busting shopping sprees" an attractive option for affluent consumers.
