SNP leader John Swinney has firmly ruled out negotiations with Reform UK as his party seeks to form a government after winning the Holyrood election, falling seven seats short of a majority. Swinney, who plans talks with all other parties except Reform, called their leader Malcolm Offord's criticism "arrogant, petty and deeply undemocratic." The SNP leader emphasized the need for Scotland to unite and become "Farage-proofed" by securing independence before 2029, citing Reform's gains in England and Wales as a catastrophic threat. Despite the SNP's comfortable win, their constituency and regional vote shares were down, and the combined pro-independence vote was just over 40%, indicating challenges to their independence ambitions.
