One significant pitfall is the "no-closing-cost" mortgage, which often comes with a higher interest rate that can prove more expensive over the loan's life. Buyers are urged to compare total loan costs rather than just upfront fees. Another trap involves "discount points," where lenders quote a lower rate contingent on paying upfront fees that may not be recouped if rates drop and refinancing becomes necessary. Finally, for new construction, "builder buydowns" can offer temporary rate reductions but may inflate the home's overall price. Buyers should carefully examine all terms and ask lenders for the total loan cost to make informed decisions.
