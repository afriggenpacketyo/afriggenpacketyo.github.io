What Happens Next After Pope Francis' Death?

2025-04-21 10:46:00 EDT

Following the death of Pope Francis, the Roman Catholic Church initiates a series of rituals and traditions, including a conclave to elect the next pontiff. The Cardinal Camerlengo formally pronounces the pope dead, breaks the Fisherman's ring, and seals the papal apartment. A nine-day mourning period called Novemdiales follows, with the funeral taking place four to six days after the death. Cardinals from around the world gather in Rome and begin meeting in "General Congregations," a period where papal candidates give speeches. Between 15 and 20 days after the pope's death, the Cardinal Electors of the papal conclave isolate themselves from the world and begin the centuries-old process of choosing the next pope.
