Senior Judge Cynthia M. Rufe's ruling requires the Department of the Interior and National Park Service to restore the site to its original state and safeguard all historical items. The city of Philadelphia filed a lawsuit arguing that prior agreements with NPS necessitated communication and compromise before any changes. The judge's memo cited George Orwell's "1984," asserting that the government "does not" have the power to "dissemble and disassemble historical truths." While the Interior Department has appealed, stating they were updating exhibits for accuracy, the ruling is hailed as a victory for preserving historical context and preventing the whitewashing of American history.
