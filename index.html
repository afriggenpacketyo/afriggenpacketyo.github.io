2025-12-22 08:00:08 EST

Chinese cybersecurity researchers at the GEEKCon competition in Shanghai have successfully demonstrated a critical vulnerability in humanoid robots, illustrating how a single verbal command could potentially allow malicious actors or spies to gain full control. The researchers, from the DARKNAVY cybersecurity group, managed to compromise one internet-connected robot and subsequently use it to infect other robots, ultimately manipulating them to execute malicious instructions. This alarming finding highlights a significant national security risk, as such disruptive behavior could rapidly spread across a "robot army" and lead to widespread chaos, underscoring the urgent necessity for enhanced security protocols in advanced autonomous systems.
