The Chamber's annual report revealed that a "former board representative" had signed a $300,000 contract with a Washington-based public relations firm, with funds reportedly collected and disbursed outside official AMCHAM accounts and without board approval. Castillo, who also chairs "Republican Overseas Myanmar," was arrested as the country's military-backed government grapples with civil war and maintains a tight lid on information regarding such detentions. The U.S. State Department acknowledged awareness of the reports but cited privacy considerations for declining further comment.
