This warning follows a breakdown in talks between Health Secretary Wes Streeting and pharma firms over drug pricing deals. Novartis's UK boss stated the UK is "largely uninvestable" for major new investments, citing a 23.5% rebate rate for pharma companies, significantly higher than in other European countries. The company has already been unable to launch several medicines for public reimbursement, raising concerns about future deprioritization of the UK market and its declining competitiveness, which is impacting patient access to vital drugs.
