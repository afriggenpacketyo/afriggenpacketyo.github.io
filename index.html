2026-04-15 06:01:06 EDT

China's research vessel 'Haiyang Dizhi 2' has successfully tested an advanced device capable of cutting submarine cables and other underwater structures at depths of 3,500 meters. This deep-sea mission marks a significant step towards the engineering application of deep-sea equipment. The successful sea trial bridges the gap between development and practical deployment, suggesting the technology is poised for actual use. The device, an electro-hydrostatic actuator (EHA), integrates key hydraulic and electric components into a compact unit, eliminating the need for external piping. This development signals China's advancing capabilities in deep-sea exploration and engineering.

Properly Fitting Workwear Enhances Safety and Attracts Women to Trades

2026-04-15 06:01:00 EDT

The importance of properly fitting workwear for women is highlighted, as ill-fitting clothing can compromise safety and lead to workplace accidents. Historically, women in male-dominated industries often wore ill-fitting men's designs, impacting comfort and functionality. Companies specializing in women's workwear are now developing gender-specific designs that offer better fit, freedom of movement, and protection, without sacrificing safety standards. This focus on comfort and fit is not only improving employee well-being but also attracting more women to trades and strengthening companies' images. The trend indicates a growing recognition of gender equity in the workplace and its impact on safety and recruitment.
