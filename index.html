The Violent and Sarcastic History of 'Sarcasm'

Over time, its meaning evolved to denote a cutting remark, often delivered with a smile. Ancient grammarians like Tryphon described it as 'showing one's teeth while smiling,' and Latin author Quintilian defined it as irony used to wound with kind words. While today sarcasm is understood as saying the opposite of what one means to insult, its distinction from irony lies in its often negative and hurtful intent. Psycholinguists note its utility in indirect negativity in cultures like the U.S., though its overuse can be mistaken for genuine humor.
