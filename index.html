2025-11-03 17:45:04 EST

The UK government intends to criminalize online pornography that depicts strangulation or suffocation, a key initiative within its broader strategy to combat violence against women and girls. This legislative action stems from a review that found the portrayal of choking to be "rife" on mainstream porn sites, contributing to the normalization of such acts among younger audiences. Amendments to the Crime and Policing Bill will make both the possession and publication of this material illegal, additionally mandating online platforms to proactively identify and remove it. While advocates commend this step, some campaigners express reservations about its potential effectiveness, citing a history of existing laws against extreme pornography that have not been adequately enforced.
