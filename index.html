Explainer: How and Why a U.S. Government Shutdown Occurs

2025-10-10 10:33:12 EDT

A U.S. government shutdown happens when Congress fails to pass the necessary appropriations bills to fund federal agencies before the fiscal year begins on October 1. Without authorized funding, agencies must cease all non-essential operations, as mandated by the Antideficiency Act. During a shutdown, essential employees, such as military personnel and air traffic controllers, must continue to work without immediate pay, while non-essential workers are furloughed. While programs with permanent funding like Social Security continue, many government services can be disrupted or delayed, and the economic impact can amount to billions of dollars in lost productivity and back pay.
