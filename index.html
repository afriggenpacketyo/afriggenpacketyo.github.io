The group's campaign, promoting proposals such as limiting victim payouts for pain and suffering and capping attorney fees, has drawn strong condemnation from survivor networks and advocacy organizations. Critics argue these measures would unfairly burden survivors, policyholders, and taxpayers, rather than holding utilities accountable for igniting catastrophic fires, including last year's Eaton fire, which Southern California Edison's transmission line is the likely cause. This situation highlights the utilities' soaring profits despite their alleged roles in these devastating blazes.
