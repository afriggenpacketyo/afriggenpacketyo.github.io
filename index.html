2026-03-10 07:23:05 EDT

Iran has defended its strikes on Gulf neighbors, asserting that U.S. military assets located in these territories were "legitimate" targets in its defense against U.S. and Israeli actions. Despite issuing apologies for some strikes, Iran maintains that its actions are defensive under Article 51 of the UN Charter. However, Gulf states have expressed that these attacks have created a "huge trust gap" that could persist for decades. While Iran claims to have only targeted military infrastructure, evidence suggests attacks have also impacted civilian infrastructure and energy facilities. Analysts suggest Iran's strategy aims to create discomfort for its neighbors and pressure the U.S. and Israel to end hostilities, but this has led to increased regional isolation.
