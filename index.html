Rising Chocolate, Butter, and Egg Prices Drive UK Food Inflation to 18-Month High

2025-08-25 19:10:09 EDT

UK food price inflation has reached its highest level in 18 months, hitting 4.2% this month, up from 4% in July, primarily driven by the rising costs of chocolate, butter, and eggs. The British Retail Consortium's latest shop price monitor indicates this increase, echoing separate data from the Office for National Statistics. Helen Dickinson, BRC's chief executive, attributed significant increases in staples like butter and eggs to high demand, tightening supply, and increased labor costs. Chocolate prices surged due to poor cocoa harvests, exacerbated by climate change and crop disease. This uptick in food prices contributes to an overall increase in UK inflation, prompting households to reassess budgets. (not investment advice)
