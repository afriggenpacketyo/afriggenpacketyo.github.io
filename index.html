Antidepressant Users Face Increased Challenges During Extreme Heatwaves

2026-07-11 13:18:00 EDT

Individuals taking antidepressants may experience heightened heat sensitivity, making summer heatwaves particularly challenging due to the drugs' impact on the body's temperature regulation and sweating mechanisms. Medications like SSRIs and tricyclic antidepressants can affect the hypothalamus, leading to symptoms such as dizziness, nausea, headaches, and dehydration. Some antidepressants can inhibit sweating, while others increase it, further complicating the body's ability to cool itself. Experts advise patients not to discontinue medication without consulting a doctor, instead recommending sensible heat-health precautions like staying hydrated and out of direct sunlight. As climate change increases global temperatures, this issue becomes a growing concern for those relying on these medications.
