Couples Therapists Report Misuse of 'Therapy-Speak' Leading to Relationship Strain

2025-12-12 09:00:00 EST

Couples therapists are increasingly observing the misuse of psychological terms, often termed 'therapy-speak,' by clients who have absorbed simplified concepts from social media. Terms like 'gaslighting,' 'boundaries,' 'triggered,' and 'narcissist' are frequently misapplied, leading to miscommunication and defensiveness rather than constructive dialogue. Therapists note that clients often self-diagnose spouses with conditions like narcissism or ADHD without clinical basis, shutting down conversations and turning partners against each other. While increased mental health awareness is welcomed, the oversimplification and misapplication of these terms can hinder therapeutic progress and even contribute to relationship breakdown. Therapists are working to correct these misinterpretations and encourage more precise communication.
