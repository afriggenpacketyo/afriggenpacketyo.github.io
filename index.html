Understanding Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 Bankruptcy: Liquidation vs. Repayment

2026-02-24 11:51:47 EST

As credit card balances surpass $1.23 trillion and delinquency rates climb, many Americans are exploring bankruptcy, making it crucial to understand the distinctions between Chapter 7 and Chapter 13. Chapter 7, often termed "liquidation bankruptcy," provides a faster path, typically discharging most qualifying unsecured debts like credit card balances and medical bills within three to six months, though it requires a means test and may involve liquidating non-exempt assets. In contrast, Chapter 13 bankruptcy restructures debt through a three-to-five-year repayment plan, allowing individuals to protect assets like their homes and catch up on secured debts. Both options initiate an automatic stay, halting collection efforts, but the appropriate choice hinges on a borrower's income, asset ownership, and specific financial objectives.
