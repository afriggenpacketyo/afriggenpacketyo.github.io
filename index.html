Nike shares surged notably in after-hours trading after the sportswear company announced fiscal first-quarter results that significantly surpassed Wall Street expectations, signaling robust momentum for CEO Elliott Hill's turnaround strategy. The article highlights a 1% year-over-year revenue increase to $11.72 billion and earnings per share of 49 cents, both exceeding analyst forecasts. This strong performance is attributed to a strategic pivot back to key retail partners and a renewed focus on core categories, with the Nike running segment alone achieving over 20% growth. Despite facing ongoing tariff headwinds and challenges in specific international markets, these positive results have instilled increased confidence in Nike's trajectory toward consistent and profitable long-term growth.
