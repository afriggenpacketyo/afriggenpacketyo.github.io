Investigations, including a grand jury report on an inmate's death in Santa Barbara, have repeatedly found negligence by leading providers like Wellpath and Corizon (now YesCare), often linked to staffing shortages. These private equity-backed companies, despite facing hundreds of lawsuits and bankruptcies, have managed to restructure and retain contracts due to their political power and the vulnerability of incarcerated populations. Critics argue that their fixed-fee-per-inmate model incentivizes cost-cutting at the expense of patient care, perpetuating a system where accountability is elusive.
