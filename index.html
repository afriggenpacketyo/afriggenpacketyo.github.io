Data Centers Drive US Clean Energy Growth While Posing Climate Challenges

2026-06-19 07:00:30 EDT

Data centers are paradoxically fueling unprecedented growth in the US clean energy sector, boosting wind and solar companies, even as their immense energy demands create significant environmental challenges. While big tech companies are investing in their own power solutions like battery storage and renewables due to grid connection delays, utilities are also building new fossil-fuel plants or keeping old ones online to meet data center needs. The gas industry, supported by the Trump administration, is particularly benefiting. This surge in clean energy investment, including companies like Nextpower and Bloom Energy, comes with caveats, such as the unpredictability of energy demand and the potential for an AI bubble burst.
