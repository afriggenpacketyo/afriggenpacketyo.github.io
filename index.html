Understanding the Different Types of Drought and Their Impacts

2025-06-17 06:24:00 EDT

Droughts are increasingly impacting crops, economies, and lives globally, with varying consequences depending on the type. Meteorological droughts compare current precipitation to regional averages, while agricultural droughts affect farming due to parched grounds and water scarcity. Hydrological droughts occur when water levels in rivers and lakes drop below minimum levels, often following prolonged meteorological droughts. Socioeconomic droughts affect society and the economy, leading to increased costs or scarcity of goods and electricity, and ecological droughts destabilize ecosystems, causing crop failures and tree death, emphasizing the need for climate change mitigation and efficient water use.
