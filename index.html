Understanding Qualifications and Alternatives for Personal Bankruptcy Filings

2026-02-20 13:14:10 EST

Filing for bankruptcy depends on meeting specific legal standards for either Chapter 7 or Chapter 13, rather than a fixed debt threshold, as many individuals face widening gaps between income and debt due to elevated interest rates and high inflation. Chapter 7, or liquidation bankruptcy, targets unsecured debts and requires passing a means test comparing household income to the state median, while Chapter 13 involves reorganizing debt into a three- to five-year repayment plan for those with steady income. Both types mandate credit counseling and detailed financial documentation, with courts assessing genuine financial hardship versus strategic moves, and prior filings impacting eligibility. Alternatives like debt settlement, debt management plans, hardship programs, or direct creditor negotiation can offer real relief without the long-term credit consequences of formal bankruptcy.
