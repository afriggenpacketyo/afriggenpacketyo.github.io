What Happens to a HELOC After a Decade?

Home equity lines of credit (HELOCs) are an appealing option for homeowners in today's high-rate environment, but they have distinct phases that can create confusion. Most HELOCs have a 10-year draw period, during which borrowers can access funds, followed by a repayment period. After 10 years, access to funds ends, the payment structure changes, and monthly payments increase, often substantially.
