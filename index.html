Understanding the Air Quality Index and Its Impact on Public Health

2025-06-03 11:26:12 EDT

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a tool used to measure air pollution severity and associated health risks, utilizing six color-coded categories ranging from good (green) to hazardous (maroon). The EPA calculates the AQI based on five major pollutants regulated by the Clean Air Act: ground-level ozone, particulate matter, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide. Higher AQI values indicate greater health risks, with "very unhealthy" air posing risks to the general public and "hazardous" levels indicating an air quality emergency. Resources like AirNow.gov provide real-time AQI updates, pollutant information, and activity recommendations based on local air quality conditions.
