Why Cities Experience Higher Temperatures During Heatwaves

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

Cities often become significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas due to the "urban heat island effect," a phenomenon that exacerbates the impact of heatwaves. Dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's heat, slowly releasing it and raising temperatures, especially at night. In contrast, rural areas benefit from the natural cooling effects of vegetation and water. With climate change making heatwaves more frequent and intense, and with a growing majority of the world's population living in urban centers, this effect poses increasing risks like heat stroke and poor air quality.
