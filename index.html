Businesses Minimize Tariff Impact Using the First Sale Rule

2025-05-27 08:27:10 EDT

Businesses are leveraging the "first sale rule," a decades-old U.S. customs law, to reduce the financial impact of tariffs. This rule allows importers to calculate customs duties based on the initial sale price from the overseas producer to a vendor, rather than the inflated price from the vendor to the U.S. retailer. By using the factory price, companies can strip out the middleman's profit and pay a lower import duty. The first sale rule requires at least two sales, independent parties, proof the item was destined for the U.S., and documentation of the first sale price.
