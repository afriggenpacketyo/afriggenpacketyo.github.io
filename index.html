Financial Explainer Details Consequences of Debt Write-Offs for Consumers

2025-11-07 13:08:50 EST

This article clarifies the common misconception that a "debt write-off" means the debt is forgiven. When a creditor writes off a debt, it is an accounting measure that marks the balance as a loss, but it does not absolve the borrower of the obligation to pay. The debt is typically sold to a collection agency, which will then pursue the borrower for the full amount. A write-off remains on a credit report for up to seven years, significantly damaging the consumer's credit score, and any portion of the debt that is eventually forgiven may be treated as taxable income by the IRS.
