Mullin intends to shift FEMA's role from primary responder to a supporter of state and local governments, aiming to streamline approvals and accelerate disaster relief efforts. He acknowledged resident frustrations over ongoing recovery challenges and bureaucratic hurdles, pledging to expedite projects before the upcoming hurricane season. The visit followed FEMA's announcement of $26 million in funding for home buyouts in high-risk flood zones. Mullin also rescinded a previous memo requiring high-level approval for DHS contracts, indicating a move towards greater efficiency and support for local recovery initiatives.
