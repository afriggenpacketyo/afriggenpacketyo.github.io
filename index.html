Argentina's libertarian President Javier Milei faces crucial midterm elections, a significant test for his radical public spending cuts amidst a deepening currency crisis and a controversial U.S. bailout. Milei's party holds a minority in Congress, making legislative progress difficult, while his approval ratings have plummeted due to austerity measures and corruption allegations against his sister. Despite a $20 billion U.S. currency swap, public anger is palpable over 50,000 public sector job cuts and rising food costs. While facing strong opposition and widespread protests, Milei retains a loyal following, particularly among younger voters, who view him as a source of hope for a country that has endured significant economic hardship and instability.
