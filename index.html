New Wealth Tax Proposals Criticized as Unsustainable and Damaging

2026-02-05 13:00:00 EST

Proposals for new forms of wealth taxes, such as California's proposed 5% tax on billionaires' net worth and taxes on unrealized gains, are being criticized as unsustainable and economically damaging. Critics argue that these taxes disproportionately target a mobile group, leading to tax avoidance, capital flight, and reduced investment. Wealth is often tied up in operating businesses and productive investments, and wealth taxes layer additional levies that can function as confiscatory rates on returns. The argument that these taxes "only hit billionaires" is dismissed, as the harm can spread throughout the economy through slower productivity and wage growth. The framing of "one-time" levies is also seen as misleading, potentially encouraging wealthy individuals to leave jurisdictions and leading to the expansion of such taxes over time as revenue falls short.
