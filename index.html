Police are seeking information to identify a suspect in a fatal hit-and-run incident that killed Jose Melesio, 65, who was visiting Los Angeles to see a Cubs-Dodgers game. Melesio was struck by a silver four-door sedan traveling north on Figueroa Street in the early hours of April 27 and was pronounced dead at the scene. The driver failed to stop, notify emergency services, or remain at the scene as required by law. Detectives are offering a $50,000 reward for information leading to an arrest. The incident tragically cut short Melesio's visit with family, leaving his loved ones heartbroken and seeking justice.

CDC classifies hantavirus outbreak on cruise ship as 'Level 3' emergency

2026-05-07 23:10:54 EDT

The U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) has activated its Emergency Operations Centers and classified the hantavirus outbreak linked to the cruise ship MV Hondius as a 'Level 3' emergency response. This classification signifies a low risk to the general public but indicates active monitoring by the agency. Passengers aboard the ship report a calm atmosphere, with doctors on board providing reassurance. President Trump has been briefed on the situation, and a full report is expected. Meanwhile, a congresswoman has urged the State Department and CDC to develop a repatriation plan for American citizens on the ship, expressing alarm over the deteriorating public health situation. The origin of the outbreak remains under investigation.

US State Department to revoke passports for significant child support debt

2026-05-07 22:57:48 EDT

The U.S. State Department will begin revoking passports of Americans with significant child support debt exceeding $2,500, as part of an effort to enforce legal and moral obligations to children. This policy, set to be enforced starting Friday, targets parents with substantial outstanding payments. Those whose passports are revoked will not be eligible for new ones until their debt is cleared. The State Department will collaborate with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services to identify individuals with delinquent child support. This measure aims to provide tangible consequences for non-compliance and support the welfare of American children by ensuring parents meet their financial responsibilities.
