Readers expressed concerns over civil rights rollbacks, deteriorating international relations, and growing domestic opposition, feeling the country has lost credibility and reached a "tipping point." Sentiments range from embarrassment and apathy, particularly among marginalized groups, to a sense that the "American experiment" is failing. While some recall a more optimistic past, others still see the anniversary as a crucial "fork in the road," an opportunity for change if focus returns to working-class Americans and curbing corruption.
