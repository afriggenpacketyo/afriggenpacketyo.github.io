Private Equity's Grip on Youth Sports Breeds Resentment and Emptiness

2026-05-20 07:00:00 EDT

The increasing involvement of private equity in youth sports, exemplified by Black Bear Sports Group's control over hockey leagues and facilities, is transforming the experience from character-building fun into a profit-driven enterprise. This commercialization leads to issues like parents paying for streaming services and leagues prioritizing individual statistics over teamwork. The article argues that this shift from a public good to a commodity breeds "emptiness and resentment," reflecting a broader societal trend of profit maximization over community benefit. This discontent, it suggests, spills into politics, with figures like Donald Trump embodying a symptom of this "spiritual unraveling." The piece calls for a deeper repair of American society beyond electoral politics, focusing on restoring a belief in the common good.

Primary Elections Highlight Trump's Base Strength Amidst Broader Unpopularity

2026-05-20 07:00:00 EDT

President Donald Trump's candidates have performed well in recent primary elections, demonstrating his continued influence over the Republican Party base. Victories in Indiana, Louisiana, and Kentucky saw Trump-backed challengers defeat incumbents or establish strong positions. However, the article notes a contradiction: while Trump's control within the party is expanding, his overall approval ratings are declining. This dynamic is attributed to the low turnout in primary elections, which tends to favor more partisan voters. Reformers suggest that nonpartisan primaries could reduce polarization by forcing candidates to appeal to a wider electorate, thereby potentially mitigating the impact of fringe elements within parties.
