Explainer: Homeowners Retain Ownership and Title with a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

A reverse mortgage allows homeowners aged 62 and older to convert home equity into cash without making monthly payments, but it does not transfer ownership of the property to the lender. The homeowner's name remains on the title, and they are still responsible for property taxes, insurance, and maintenance. Instead of taking ownership, the lender places a lien on the home to secure the loan, which becomes due when the borrower sells, moves out, or passes away. At that point, the homeowner or their heirs can repay the loan to keep the property or sell it to settle the debt.
