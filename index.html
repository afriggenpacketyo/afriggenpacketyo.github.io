The Federal Aviation Administration confirmed that the outage disrupted telecommunications at the Los Angeles Air Route Traffic Control Center, leading to a "no-go condition for launch" to ensure the safety of the traveling public. This significant incident also resulted in a ground stop at Santa Barbara Airport and affected 911 service throughout Santa Barbara County. Fortunately, the rocket and its two TRACERS mission satellites, designed to study Earth's magnetosphere, remained in good condition, and the launch has since been successfully rescheduled for Wednesday.
