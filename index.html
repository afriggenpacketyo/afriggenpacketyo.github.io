Australia's Communications Minister Anika Wells has affirmed the government's unwavering commitment to its "world-leading" social media ban for children under 16, slated to commence on December 10, despite strong opposition from major technology firms. Wells stated that the government stands firmly with parents, citing research indicating significant online harm, and will not be intimidated by companies like Meta. While acknowledging potential intervention from the US, she remains focused on Australian public policy, believing other nations will seek to emulate the ban. Wells conceded that the policy's rollout might be "messy" but emphasized its necessity for social change, drawing parallels to established age restrictions on alcohol consumption.
