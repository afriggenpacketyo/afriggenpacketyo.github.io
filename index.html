A total lunar eclipse will be visible across parts of the United Kingdom, turning the full Moon a deep red color, an event commonly known as a "Blood Moon." The spectacle occurs when the Earth passes directly between the Sun and the Moon, casting its shadow on the lunar surface. While visibility in the UK will be limited by the Moon's rising time, with eastern regions getting the best view, the event offers a chance to observe celestial mechanics in action.
