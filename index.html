Understanding Endemic, Epidemic, and Pandemic Disease Spread

2026-05-11 06:31:00 EDT

The terms endemic, epidemic, and pandemic describe how diseases spread, not necessarily their severity. A disease is endemic when it occurs regularly in a specific region with a relatively constant number of cases over time, like malaria. An epidemic occurs when a disease spreads with unusual frequency in a region for a limited time, exceeding the expected level, such as a localized outbreak. A pandemic signifies a disease spreading across countries and continents, requiring international cooperation for control. These distinctions are crucial for understanding public health responses, especially in light of recent global health events.
