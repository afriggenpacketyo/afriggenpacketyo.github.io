2026-01-16 08:00:58 EST

Mixed messaging from U.S. health officials regarding flu shots is alarming experts, especially as the nation experiences a severe flu season. Recent changes in recommendations for children's flu vaccinations, shifting to "shared clinical decision-making," and comments questioning the vaccine's effectiveness by high-ranking officials have raised concerns. Despite extensive studies demonstrating the flu shot's efficacy in preventing serious illness, hospitalizations, and deaths, vaccination rates have declined. The dominant H3N2 strain is particularly virulent, and combined with rising COVID-19 and RSV cases, hospitals are under immense pressure. Experts stress the importance of vaccination and antiviral treatments, noting that children should not be dying from preventable illnesses.

China reduces US debt holdings amid concerns over Federal Reserve independence under Trump

2026-01-16 08:00:09 EST

China has further reduced its holdings of U.S. Treasuries, reaching the lowest level since 2008, while foreign ownership of U.S. debt globally has hit a record high. Analysts suggest Beijing's move is driven by concerns over the potential politicization of the Federal Reserve under a Donald Trump presidency and the sustainability of U.S. debt. China views the accumulating debt as resembling a "Ponzi scheme" and seeks to limit its exposure. This reduction in U.S. debt holdings signals a strategic shift in China's investment portfolio amidst growing geopolitical and economic uncertainties surrounding the United States.
