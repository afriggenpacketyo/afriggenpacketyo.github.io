Overtime Pay is Generally Subject to Wage Garnishment by Debt Collectors

While overtime earnings increase the total disposable income, the garnishment limits, typically 25% of disposable earnings or the amount exceeding 30 times the federal minimum wage, still apply. This means a larger paycheck from overtime can result in a higher dollar amount being garnished, even if the percentage cap remains the same. Crucially, debt collectors usually require a court judgment before garnishing wages, though certain obligations like child support or defaulted federal student loans have different rules. State laws can also significantly impact garnishment rules, with some states largely prohibiting it for consumer debts. Addressing the underlying debt through options like debt settlement or management plans before garnishment begins is often the most effective strategy.
