U.S. Fighter Jets Intercept Russian Warplanes Off Alaskan Coast

2026-02-19 23:58:00 EST

U.S. fighter jets were dispatched to intercept several Russian military aircraft, including two Tu-95 bombers, two Su-35 fighter planes, and an A-50 spy plane, detected flying within the Alaskan Air Defense Identification Zone (ADIZ). The North American Aerospace Defense Command (NORAD) confirmed that two F-16s, two F-35s, and four KC-135s were launched to escort the Russian formation until it departed the ADIZ. Crucially, the Russian aircraft maintained their position in international airspace and did not infringe upon U.S. or Canadian sovereign territory. NORAD described these intercepts as routine occurrences that do not pose a direct threat, yet they highlight the continuous military presence and vigilance required in this strategically vital region, with similar incidents reported multiple times in recent years.
