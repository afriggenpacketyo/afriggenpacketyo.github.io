Investor Pleads Guilty to Securities Fraud in Case Linked to Hedge Fund Collapse and B. Riley Damage

2025-12-12 14:03:16 EST

Businessman Brian Kahn has entered a guilty plea to conspiracy to commit securities fraud, confessing to concealing over $400 million in trading losses that ultimately triggered the collapse of Prophecy Asset Management in 2020. This criminal proceeding also played a role in the bankruptcy of the retailer Franchise Group and inflicted substantial damage upon the West Los Angeles investment bank B. Riley Financial, which had extended significant financing and held a considerable stake in Franchise Group. Kahn's fraudulent activities, including the alleged diversion of fund money to Franchise Group, resulted in immense financial losses for B. Riley, causing its stock value to plummet dramatically. Kahn faces a maximum of five years in prison, with his sentencing scheduled for April. (not investment advice)
