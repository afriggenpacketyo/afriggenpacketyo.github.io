An Explanation of Methane's Significant Role in Climate Change

While it has a shorter lifespan of about 12 years, it is responsible for up to 30% of the global warming that has occurred since the Industrial Revolution. About 60% of atmospheric methane comes from human activities, including agriculture, landfills, and, significantly, the energy sector through leaks and flaring during oil and gas production. Experts suggest that simple measures, like fixing leaks in fossil fuel infrastructure, could drastically reduce these emissions.
