The Kennedy Center's Board of Trustees voted to rename the performing arts institution "The Donald J. Trump and The John F. Kennedy Memorial Center for the Performing Arts," a move announced by the White House. Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt stated the unanimous vote recognized President Trump's "unbelievable work" in saving the building financially and reputationally. However, the decision immediately sparked outrage from Democrats and the Kennedy family, with Rep. Joyce Beatty disputing the unanimity and legality, arguing Congress must approve such changes to an institution created by Congress. This action follows Trump's appointment of allies to the board and his previous pledges to make sweeping changes to the center, raising concerns about political influence over cultural institutions.
