Senator Steve Daines has requested an investigation into a monkey bite incident and the safety procedures at the National Institutes of Health's Rocky Mountain Laboratories in Montana. Daines expressed concern about potential "lab leaks" and the research conducted at the facility, drawing parallels to the contentious theory about the origin of the COVID-19 pandemic. He cited a whistleblower complaint alleging a researcher was detained for carrying vials of an unknown substance after a trip to Africa. The NIH confirmed an investigation is ongoing regarding the researcher. The lab's associate director stated the employee bitten by the monkey was unharmed and returned to work months ago.

Louisiana approves new congressional map, dismantling a majority-Black district and favoring Republicans

2026-05-29 13:14:27 EDT

Louisiana lawmakers have approved a new congressional map that dismantles one of the state's two majority-Black districts, a move expected to benefit Republicans in the upcoming midterms. This decision follows a U.S. Supreme Court ruling that struck down the previous map as an unconstitutional racial gerrymander, weakening the Voting Rights Act. Governor Jeff Landry pushed for the map's redrawing, arguing for an end to race-based decision-making in districting. While the new map retains one majority-Black district centered around New Orleans, it splits Black populations in Baton Rouge and absorbs Shreveport into other districts. This redistricting effort mirrors similar actions in other Southern states following the Supreme Court's decision.
