New government data for England indicates that pupils who missed school during the first week of September 2024 were significantly more likely to become 'persistently absent' throughout the academic year. Over half of those partially absent in week one eventually missed at least 10% of school, compared to only 14% of full attendees. Education Secretary Bridget Phillipson urged schools and parents to prioritize early attendance for the 2025 term, emphasizing that 'every day of school missed is a day stolen from a child's future.' While overall attendance has improved since the pandemic, it remains higher than pre-Covid levels. The article notes that despite incentives and support programs, unauthorized term-time holidays and medical treatments abroad continue to contribute to absence issues, highlighting the need for a more strategic approach beyond school gates.
