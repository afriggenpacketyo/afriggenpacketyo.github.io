2026-05-31 04:05:00 EDT

During the interwar Weimar Republic, jazz music flourished in Germany, with icons like Josephine Baker and Louis Armstrong becoming widely popular. However, after the Nazis came to power in 1933, they condemned jazz as "degenerate music" due to its African American origins and association with Jewish artists, seeking to control all aspects of culture. In defiance, a youth counterculture known as the Swing Youth emerged in cities like Hamburg and Berlin, embracing jazz, American fashion, and distinct greetings as acts of cultural resistance. Although their opposition was more cultural than political, this movement became a target of Nazi repression, leading to arrests and even internment in concentration camps for those who dared to express their freedom through music.
