The flaw, introduced during an October 2025 system update, allowed unauthorized viewing and editing of other companies' information. Companies House Chief Executive Andy King apologized for the incident, confirming it was resolved by Monday and reported to the ICO and NCSC, with no current reports of data being accessed. While passwords and identity verification data were not compromised, the agency is investigating whether any data was accessed or altered without permission, advising businesses to check their registered email for guidance.
