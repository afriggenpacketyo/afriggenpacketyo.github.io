2026-04-17 14:16:33 EDT

Garret Anderson, a pivotal player in the Los Angeles Angels' 2002 World Series victory and a consistent slugger for 17 seasons, has died at the age of 53. Anderson's career was marked by quiet professionalism and exceptional performance, including a decisive three-run double in Game 7 of the World Series. He holds numerous franchise records for the Angels, including games played, hits, and RBIs. Despite occasional fan criticism for a perceived lack of overt emotion, teammates and observers lauded his intelligence and dedication to the game. Anderson's legacy as a beloved icon of the Angels organization is being honored with memorial patches and tributes.

2026-04-17 14:08:26 EDT

White House officials are closely observing an alleged scandal in Utah involving a relationship between a Supreme Court justice and an attorney who argued against GOP-drawn congressional maps.
