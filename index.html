Companies Employ 'Tariff Engineering' to Reduce Duty Costs

2025-06-18 22:58:40 EDT

Faced with increasing tariffs, businesses are employing 'tariff engineering,' a legal practice involving altering product materials or dimensions to reclassify items under lower-tariff harmonized system codes. This strategy allows companies to reduce duty liabilities by strategically designing products to fit into more favorable tariff categories. For example, some manufacturers bundle steel and aluminum elements or switch synthetic materials for cotton to qualify for lower duties. However, heavily-regulated industries like automotive and aerospace face greater challenges due to extensive validation and approval requirements for even minor changes; this practice dates back to the 1880s and is still used today.
