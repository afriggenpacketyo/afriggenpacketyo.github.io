Fact Check: Claims Linking Statins to Dementia Are Debunked by Medical Research

2026-07-16 14:27:00 EDT

Misleading claims circulating online suggest statins, cholesterol-lowering drugs prescribed to reduce heart attack and stroke risk, cause dementia or cognitive decline. However, a comprehensive analysis by the Cholesterol Treatment Trialists' Collaboration, involving over 123,000 participants across 19 trials, found no increased risk of cognitive impairment in statin users compared to placebo groups. In fact, some researchers, like Dr. Wenzel Glanz, believe statins may even help lower the risk of certain types of dementia, especially vascular dementia, by effectively reducing elevated blood lipids. While the FDA did add a label warning in 2012 regarding generally not serious and reversible memory issues, it did not find evidence of permanent cognitive decline or diseases like Alzheimer's, and experts assert no strong evidence supports statins impairing brain function.

The Joint Committee on Vaccination and Immunisation (JCVI) advises two doses for full protection, noting that teenagers' socially active lifestyles put them at higher risk of contracting this potentially deadly disease. The Department of Health and Social Care will consider the advice, which, if accepted, would extend protection beyond the current baby immunization program, addressing a crucial gap in national vaccination strategy and potentially saving lives.
