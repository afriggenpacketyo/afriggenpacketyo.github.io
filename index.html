Explainer: The Urban Heat Island Effect and Its Impact on Cities

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

Cities often experience significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to a phenomenon known as the urban heat island effect. This occurs because dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, slowly releasing it as heat, which raises air temperatures, especially at night. The lack of vegetation and water, which provide natural cooling in the countryside, exacerbates the effect. As climate change leads to more frequent and intense heatwaves, urban populations are particularly vulnerable to the dangerous impacts of extreme heat and associated air pollution.
