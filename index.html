The UK government plans to fundamentally reform housing ownership by banning new leasehold flats and replacing them with commonhold, a system where residents collectively own and manage their buildings. This initiative aims to alleviate the "terrible stress" and high, often uncontrollable costs faced by millions of leaseholders under the current "feudal" system, which grants significant power to third-party landlords. While experts like Professor Nick Hopkins advocate for commonhold, highlighting its benefits in resident autonomy and ensuring financial interest remains with those living in the building, significant challenges persist. These include difficulties in managing disputes without a freeholder, historical reluctance from lenders to mortgage commonhold properties, and the complex, potentially legally challenged process of converting existing leaseholds, making the reform "easier said than done."
