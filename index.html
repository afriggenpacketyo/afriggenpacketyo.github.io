When a credit card account becomes severely delinquent, creditors typically "charge off" the debt after 180 days of non-payment, classifying it as unlikely to be collected. This accounting measure does not erase the debt but is a crucial step before it enters the collections phase. While some creditors may continue internal collection efforts for a period, many sell the charged-off debt to a third-party collection agency within 30 to 90 days. Understanding this timeline can help consumers strategically address their debt, whether through payment plans, settlement negotiations, or seeking help from a credit counseling agency before the account is sold.
