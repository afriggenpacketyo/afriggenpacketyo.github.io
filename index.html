Photographer Phil Noble recounted the "more luck than judgement" behind his iconic photo of Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor, formerly Prince Andrew, slumped in a car after his historic arrest on suspicion of misconduct in public office. Noble drove six hours to Norfolk following a tip-off, waiting for hours outside a police station. After packing up, a last-minute call from a colleague sent him racing back, allowing him to capture the stunned royal's image as his cars sped away from the station. Noble emphasized the rarity of such a defining moment for one outlet in today's media climate, with the unprecedented nature of the arrest and the clear focus on Mountbatten-Windsor's face securing front-page coverage worldwide and becoming a defining image of the event.
