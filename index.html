Extreme heat exacerbates side effects for individuals taking antidepressants

2026-07-11 13:18:00 EDT

High temperatures, particularly those exceeding 40 degrees Celsius (104 F), can significantly impact individuals taking antidepressants. These medications, such as SSRIs and tricyclic antidepressants, can interfere with the body's natural temperature regulation and sweating mechanisms. Symptoms like dizziness, nausea, headaches, and dehydration can become more pronounced, making it crucial for those on these medications to stay hydrated and avoid excessive heat. It is vital to consult a doctor before altering medication dosages, as abrupt cessation can lead to withdrawal symptoms. General heat-safety advice, including increased fluid intake and seeking shade, is recommended for all, but especially for those with increased heat sensitivity due to medication.
