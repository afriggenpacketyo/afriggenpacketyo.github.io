A multi-agency review into a school stabbing in Ammanford, Wales, found that the 13-year-old girl who attacked two teachers and a pupil had a long-standing fascination with weapons and war, including a previous discovery of a knife and BB gun in her bag. The report indicated that the girl, who was later sentenced to 15 years for attempted murder, could have benefited from "targeted help" if information had been fully shared and assessed. Despite offers of counseling and consideration for anti-terrorism programs, interventions were either refused by her father or not pursued, highlighting missed opportunities and systemic challenges in identifying and addressing high-risk behaviors and mental health issues in children.

Meituan's Profit Plummets Amidst "Irrational" Delivery Price War

2025-08-27 07:12:43 EDT

Chinese tech giant Meituan reported a drastic 98% plunge in total operating profit, with net profit falling to 365.3 million yuan, primarily due to an "irrational competition" and price war in its instant delivery segment. The company's local commerce operating profit decreased by 75.6%, and its operating margin significantly narrowed by 19.4 percentage points. This sharp decline was attributed to a substantial 27% increase in the cost of revenue, mainly driven by higher courier incentives, alongside a 51.8% jump in selling and marketing expenses and a 17.2% rise in research and development spending. Meituan's Hong Kong-listed shares closed lower following the announcement of these challenging financial results. (not investment advice)
