Understanding the Consequences of a Creditor Writing Off Your Debt

2025-11-07 13:08:50 EST

When a creditor writes off a debt, it does not mean the borrower is free from the obligation to pay. A write-off is an accounting measure where the lender marks the debt as uncollectible, typically after 180 days of non-payment, but the debt itself still exists. The original creditor often sells the debt to a collection agency, which will then pursue the borrower for the full amount, potentially with added fees. This action severely damages a person's credit score for up to seven years and can lead to lawsuits or tax liabilities on any forgiven portion. (not investment advice)
