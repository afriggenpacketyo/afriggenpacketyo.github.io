2025-12-10 17:46:52 EST

A bipartisan U.S. commission, the Congressional-Executive Commission on China (CECC), is recommending a significant expansion of Mandarin and other Chinese language immersion programs in American high schools and universities. The CECC argues that deepening linguistic and cultural expertise is essential for the U.S. to effectively confront Beijing's "malign" attempts to increase its influence, even as Congress considers measures to curb Chinese government-linked entities in U.S. education. This recommendation comes despite the Trump administration's previous scaling back of funding for cultural exchange programs to China. The US-Taiwan Education Initiative, established in 2020, is cited as a successful model for expanding access to Mandarin learning and study opportunities in Taiwan.
