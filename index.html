Global Movement for Smartphone-Free Childhood Gains Momentum

2025-12-10 11:02:45 EST

A British couple, Daisy Greenwell and Joe Riory, have sparked a global movement advocating for "smartphone-free childhoods." Their initiative, born from concerns about research linking heavy smartphone use to negative mental health impacts in young people, has resonated with thousands of parents worldwide. The movement encourages delaying smartphone access until age 14 and social media until 16, emphasizing a "pro-childhood" rather than "anti-tech" approach. This grassroots effort has expanded to chapters in 39 countries, inspired by a viral advertisement highlighting the dangers of unrestricted internet access for children.\n\nAustralia has become the first country to enact a ban on social media accounts for children under 16, compelling tech companies to enforce age restrictions. While the UK government is monitoring the situation, it has no immediate plans to replicate the legislation. The movement promotes alternatives like basic "brick phones," sales of which have seen a significant increase. The article highlights the growing parental concern and the societal shift towards prioritizing offline childhood experiences, challenging the norm of constant digital connectivity. The movement's success relies on community-level peer pressure dissolving the need for early smartphone adoption.
