White House Teleprompter Operator Made Over $100,000 on Kalshi Bets on Trump Speeches

2026-07-16 18:58:54 EDT

A White House teleprompter operator, identified as Gabriel Perez, allegedly made over $100,000 from bets placed on the prediction market platform Kalshi concerning President Trump's speeches. Kalshi's surveillance systems flagged these trades as unusual, leading the company to freeze Perez's account and lock more than $90,000 in profits. Kalshi has referred the case to the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) for investigation. Perez is reportedly cooperating with the CFTC and in talks for a potential settlement. White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt confirmed Perez has been placed on unpaid administrative leave at President Trump's direction, calling the situation "a disgrace." The White House had previously issued guidance against using nonpublic information for prediction market bets.
