Originating as a classified Cold War document and launched online in 1997, the publication provided essential facts on countries, governments, and demographics, serving students, scholars, and journalists alike. Its abrupt removal under CIA Director John Ratcliffe, who committed to "strict adherence to the CIA's mission," has sparked concern among academics and former employees regarding the preservation of historical records and public access to governmental communications. This article expresses regret over the loss of such a free, reliable, and comprehensive source of international data.
