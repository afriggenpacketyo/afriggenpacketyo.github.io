Constellation Brands Withdraws 2028 Guidance Due to Economic Uncertainty

2026-04-08 18:48:45 EDT

Constellation Brands, the U.S. maker of Modelo and Corona, has withdrawn its fiscal 2028 outlook, citing prevailing economic uncertainty and limited near-term visibility. While the company reported stronger-than-expected fourth-quarter results and noted positive momentum in its beer and wine/spirits businesses, it anticipates a dynamic operating environment. The company appointed Nicholas Fink as its new CEO, effective April 13. Constellation Brands' decision reflects broader concerns about consumer spending behavior in the face of economic volatility, with overall demand remaining subdued. Shares saw a slight dip in extended trading following the announcement.
