Inherited Debt Generally Does Not Impact Social Security Benefits

2026-05-29 12:16:54 EDT

Inherited debt typically does not directly affect Social Security benefits, as debts are usually paid from the deceased's estate rather than by beneficiaries. Federal law provides strong protection for Social Security benefits from garnishment by commercial creditors. While co-signing loans or holding joint accounts can create liability, these debts generally cannot be seized from Social Security income. The primary risk to benefits comes from an individual's own debts, such as unpaid federal taxes or defaulted student loans, which can be offset.
