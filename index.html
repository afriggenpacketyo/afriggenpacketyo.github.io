An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Powers

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial organ of the United Nations. The court hears legal disputes between states, unlike the International Criminal Court (ICC), which tries individuals. Its jurisdiction depends on whether states have agreed to submit to its authority, either through a blanket declaration, mutual consent for a specific case, or by signing a treaty like the Genocide Convention that designates the ICJ as the arbiter. While its rulings are legally binding, the ICJ has no enforcement mechanism, meaning it relies on the cooperation of the involved parties, as seen in cases like Russia's defiance of an order to halt its war in Ukraine.
