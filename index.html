2026-04-28 00:00:39 EDT

A growing number of U.S. citizens are renouncing their citizenship, driven by dissatisfaction with the country's political direction and complex tax policies. Long waiting lists for renunciation appointments at consulates worldwide indicate a significant surge in such requests. Many individuals cite disillusionment with U.S. foreign policy and domestic divisions as primary reasons for leaving. The financial implications of renunciation, including potential "covered expatriate" status and long-term tax liabilities, are substantial. The article explores personal motivations, from opposition to specific administrations to concerns about military draft registration, highlighting a profound disconnect between some citizens and their homeland.
