As a heatwave affects the UK, this article explains the "urban heat island" phenomenon, which causes cities to be significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. Dark surfaces like asphalt and buildings absorb and retain the sun's heat, while a lack of vegetation and water reduces natural cooling through evaporation. This effect can raise city temperatures by several degrees, particularly at night, exacerbating the health risks of heatwaves, such as dehydration and heat stroke. With global temperatures rising and urban populations growing, this phenomenon makes city dwellers increasingly vulnerable to extreme heat.
