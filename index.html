Trump stands by Bill Pulte's interim intelligence role, jeopardizing spy powers renewal

2026-06-09 19:57:30 EDT

President Trump is maintaining his decision to appoint Bill Pulte as acting director of national intelligence, despite significant bipartisan opposition in Congress that could block the renewal of a key spy authority. Pulte, currently head of the Federal Housing Finance Agency, is slated to begin his new role on June 19. His lack of national security experience and controversial tenure at the housing agency have drawn criticism from Democrats and some Republicans. The appointment has created uncertainty around the reauthorization of Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act, which is vital for national security but faces calls for reform to protect civil liberties. Democrats have stated they will not support the extension as long as Pulte holds the interim position.
