Mainland China's highly competitive retail environment is proving difficult for numerous retailers, including Hong Kong's Mannings health and beauty chain, which has announced its exit from the market. Analysts note a widespread struggle among retailers due to a slowdown in consumer spending and intense competition. Mannings will cease all online and offline operations on the mainland as it adjusts its strategy. The article highlights that retailers, whether local or international, are finding it tough to remain profitable, especially those transitioning from brick-and-mortar to omnichannel models. China's retail sales growth has slowed for six consecutive months, reflecting weak consumer confidence and economic uncertainty.

CDC ends universal hepatitis B vaccine recommendation for newborns

2025-12-16 21:29:37 EST

The U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) has ended its long-standing recommendation for all newborns to receive the hepatitis B vaccine at birth. The agency's advisory panel voted to recommend the birth dose only for infants whose mothers test positive for hepatitis B or have an unknown status. This policy change, described by the CDC as promoting "individual-based decision making," deviates from 30 years of established guidance. Experts express concern that this shift could lead to an increase in preventable infections and undermine parental trust in vaccination. Health Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr.'s administration has been implementing significant changes to U.S. vaccination policies.
