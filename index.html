According to Stefan Szymanski, an emeritus professor at the University of Michigan, the term "soccer" originated in elite Oxford universities in the late 19th century as a slang abbreviation for "association football," differentiating it from "rugby football." The word then spread internationally alongside the sport itself, becoming common in several countries before British newspapers largely phased it out in the 1980s. This linguistic evolution highlights how American football, also developing concurrently, influenced the distinct terminology, making the usage a matter of historical context rather than a modern American invention.
