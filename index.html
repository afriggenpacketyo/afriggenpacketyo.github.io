2026-07-24 12:38:28 EDT

The CNBC Investing Club, led by Jim Cramer, provided an analysis of Intel's recent stock performance, noting its continued decline despite a strong earnings report. The dip was attributed to investor disappointment over the absence of new foundry clients, which the Club views as a future catalyst for growth as companies seek alternatives to Taiwan Semiconductor. While they would add to Intel if not for trading restrictions, the Club expressed a cautious stance on Dover. Despite BMO analysts upgrading Dover to a buy-equivalent rating and its shares recovering after an earnings miss, Jim Cramer was disappointed with the company's execution problems, particularly regarding refrigeration production volume. Navigating Debt Relief Options Following a Job Layoff

2026-07-24 12:31:53 EDT

This article provides practical guidance on whether individuals should consider debt relief after experiencing a job layoff, especially when unsecured debts become overwhelming. It emphasizes that unemployment benefits rarely fully replace a salary, making it challenging to cover mortgage payments, credit card minimums, and household bills. The piece advises a multi-step approach: first, contacting creditors directly for hardship programs, then reviewing emergency savings, severance, and unemployment benefits to assess financial runway. Finally, it suggests creating a revised budget based on current income. If unsecured debt remains unsustainable after these steps, consulting a reputable debt relief company or credit counselor is recommended to understand options like debt management plans or bankruptcy, while also acknowledging the potential impact on credit scores.
