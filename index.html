As a heatwave impacts the UK, urban areas like London are experiencing significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural regions due to the urban heat island effect. This phenomenon is caused by dark surfaces such as roads and buildings absorbing and re-radiating the sun's heat, compounded by waste heat from human activities. The lack of vegetation and water sources in cities exacerbates the issue, preventing natural cooling processes like evaporation. With global temperatures rising and urban populations growing, this effect makes city dwellers increasingly vulnerable to the dangerous impacts of extreme heat.
