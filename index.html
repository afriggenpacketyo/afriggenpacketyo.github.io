Despite a reported rise in football-related arrests in England and Wales, English clubs have notably avoided UEFA bans, surprisingly achieving a high ranking in fair play. The article critically assesses the consistency of UEFA's disciplinary actions, pointing to what appear to be lenient fines for racist abuse and disparate treatment of clubs, such as Young Boys compared to Maccabi Tel Aviv, underscoring the persistent challenges in effectively managing fan behavior across the continent.
