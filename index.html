Explaining the Meteorological and Astronomical Definitions for the Start of Winter

2025-11-23 05:15:38 EST

As temperatures drop and snow begins to fall, many may wonder when winter officially begins. The article clarifies that there are two primary methods for defining the seasons: meteorological and astronomical. Meteorologists use a fixed calendar where winter in the UK always starts on December 1st and ends on the last day of February, a system that simplifies year-over-year climate data comparisons. In contrast, astronomical winter is determined by the Earth's position relative to the sun, beginning on the winter solstice (December 21st) and ending on the spring equinox. This distinction explains why cold, wintry weather can arrive while we are still technically in autumn.
