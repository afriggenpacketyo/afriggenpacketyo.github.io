2026-05-15 14:47:12 EDT

An Army explosive safety specialist has issued a warning that the Pentagon's accelerated effort to develop low-cost, expendable drones for battlefield use may be inadvertently undermining fundamental explosive safety standards, thereby increasing the risk of accidents. A memorandum obtained by CBS News details an incident where a mini-drone detonated, causing injuries to an Army Special Forces soldier, and suggests that the intense drive to innovate is outstripping adherence to long-established safety principles. This critical concern emerges as the U.S. military rapidly scales up its production of lethal drones, a directive stemming from a presidential executive order. The article highlights the inherent tension between the urgent demands of technological advancement in defense and the paramount need to uphold rigorous safety protocols to ensure the protection of military personnel.
