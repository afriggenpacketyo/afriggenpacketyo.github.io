Scientific Explanation Debunks Myth that Turkey Causes Post-Meal Drowsiness

2025-11-27 10:02:38 EST

The common belief that eating turkey on Thanksgiving induces sleepiness is a myth, as other foods like chicken and cheese contain similar or higher levels of the amino acid tryptophan. The real cause of post-feast drowsiness, known as postprandial somnolence, is the large intake of carbohydrates and the sheer volume of food consumed. Digesting a heavy meal diverts significant blood flow to the gut and away from the brain, leading to a feeling of lethargy. Therefore, the accompanying stuffing, potatoes, and pies are more likely culprits for the holiday nap than the turkey itself.
