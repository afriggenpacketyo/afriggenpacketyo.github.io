Understanding Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) for Retirement Accounts

For individuals with $1 million or more in retirement savings, understanding Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) is crucial. The IRS mandates these withdrawals from tax-deferred accounts starting at age 73. The RMD amount is calculated by dividing the account balance by a life expectancy factor, which decreases with age, thus increasing the withdrawal percentage over time. These distributions are taxed as ordinary income and can impact tax brackets, Social Security benefits, and Medicare premiums. Failure to take RMDs incurs a significant penalty.
