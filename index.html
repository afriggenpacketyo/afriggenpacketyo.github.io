Blackstone is imposing limits on investor withdrawals from its Blackstone Private Credit (BCRED) fund, citing a surge in redemption requests that have reached 10% of shares in the second quarter. This move follows a broader sell-off in private markets, triggered by concerns over liquidity pressures. Partners Group also announced restrictions on withdrawals from its private equity vehicles, warning of a spillover effect from private credit into private equity. While Blackstone's shares saw an initial dip, they later recovered. The article notes that these liquidity caps are a designed feature of such funds to protect long-term investors from short-term market dynamics, aiming to ensure returns are driven by asset quality rather than flow fluctuations.

Supreme Court Backs Trump Administration on Telecom Regulation and Data Privacy Fines

2026-06-04 11:06:11 EDT

The Supreme Court has sided with the Trump administration, upholding the Federal Communications Commission's (FCC) authority to enforce data privacy laws on telecommunications companies. The 8-1 decision supports the FCC's ability to impose fines, though the court also granted a concession to companies like Verizon and AT&T, suggesting penalties may not be immediately binding. The companies had challenged the FCC's process, arguing it lacked jury trial rights. The ruling affirms the FCC's regulatory tools while potentially shifting the landscape by allowing companies to contest penalties before payment. This decision impacts how federal regulators can enforce consumer data protection.

Consumer Protection Agency Deletes Thousands of Webpages Amidst Trump Administration's Dismantling Efforts

2026-06-04 11:00:09 EDT

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) has deleted over 2,200 webpages from its website, a move advocates view as part of the Trump administration's effort to dismantle the agency. The removed content, dating back to the CFPB's inception, includes press releases, advisories, and testimonies. This action follows the appointment of Russell Vought, a proponent of abolishing the agency, as acting director. Vought has reportedly ordered a halt to work, dropped enforcement cases, and sought to reduce staff significantly. The deletions, combined with reduced website activity, signal a departure from transparency and a focus on rolling back consumer protections, which advocates argue is detrimental to consumers and the economy.
