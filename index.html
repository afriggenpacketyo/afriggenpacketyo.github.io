The engine, using composite carbon fiber, boasts a maximum thrust of 2,500 kilonewtons, an increase from a previous test. While North Korea claims this significantly boosts its strategic military arsenal, experts are skeptical, suggesting it could be an exaggeration or indicate delays in their program. Solid-fuel missiles offer advantages in mobility and concealment over liquid-fuel counterparts. This development aligns with Kim's goal of enhancing nuclear-capable ballistic missiles, though technological hurdles for a fully functional ICBM remain debated.
