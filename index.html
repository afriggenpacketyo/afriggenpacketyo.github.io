Drug Dealers Sentenced to Prison for Manslaughter in Elderly Couple's Arson Deaths

2026-02-19 08:53:32 EST

Three drug dealers have been sentenced to prison for the manslaughter of an elderly St Helens couple, Sheila Jackson (83) and Eric Greener (77), who tragically died in an arson attack on their home. Kevin Weetman, who ordered the attack, received a life sentence with a minimum of 25 years. Kylie Maynard, who provided information about the address, was jailed for 23 years, and Lee Owens, who helped set the fire, received 13.5 years after pleading guilty. The fire was intended for Jackson's son, who refused to sell drugs for Weetman. The victims' families expressed profound grief, highlighting the devastating impact of the "sordid and brutal world of drug dealing." The judge described it as an "appalling crime" resulting in the untimely death of innocent people. This outcome brings a measure of justice for the victims and their families.

U.S. Jobless Aid Applications Decline to 206,000, Indicating Sustained Low Layoff Levels

2026-02-19 08:53:01 EST

U.S. applications for unemployment benefits fell by 23,000 last week to 206,000, significantly below the 225,000 new applications analysts had forecast. This decline indicates that layoffs in the U.S. remain at historically low levels, reflecting a healthy and stable job market. The four-week moving average for jobless claims, which helps to temper week-to-week volatility, also decreased by 1,000 to 219,000. While the total number of Americans filing for jobless benefits for the previous week saw a slight increase, the overall trend points to continued stability in employment figures, providing a positive economic indicator.
