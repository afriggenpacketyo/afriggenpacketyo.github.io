Understanding the Differences Between Endemic, Epidemic, and Pandemic Disease Spread

2026-05-11 06:31:00 EDT

The article clarifies the epidemiological distinctions between endemic, epidemic, and pandemic diseases, emphasizing that these terms describe the spread of a disease, not its severity. An endemic disease occurs regularly in specific regions with a relatively constant number of cases, like malaria. An epidemic signifies a disease occurring with unusual frequency in a region for a limited time, exceeding expected levels. A pandemic involves the worldwide spread of a disease across continents, requiring international cooperation for control. The piece also notes the metaphorical use of "epidemic" for non-communicable issues, cautioning against misuse.
