The European Space Agency's Proba-3 mission, consisting of two spacecraft flying in precise formation, has achieved a groundbreaking observation of the sun's corona by creating an artificial solar eclipse. The mission involves two spacecraft flying 150 meters apart, with one casting a shadow over the other to block the sun's light. This allows scientists to study the solar corona, the sun's outer atmosphere, closer to the solar rim than ever before. Scientists hope to unravel mysteries such as why the corona is much hotter than the sun's surface and to improve understanding of solar weather, which can affect telecommunications satellites and power grids on Earth.
