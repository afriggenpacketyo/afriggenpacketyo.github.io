This article offers an expert analysis on the efficacy and safety of creatine supplements, which have experienced a significant surge in popularity driven by social media claims extending beyond traditional muscle growth. Decades of scientific research consistently confirm creatine's effectiveness in increasing lean muscle mass and enhancing athletic performance, particularly when integrated with resistance training. However, other purported benefits, such as boosting cognition or protecting against Alzheimer's disease, currently possess only early and less robust scientific evidence. Experts underscore that creatine is not a "magic bullet" and requires concomitant exercise to yield benefits, noting its general safety for adults, especially in its monohydrate form. They advise caution against adulterated products and recommend consulting a doctor for individuals with kidney conditions.
