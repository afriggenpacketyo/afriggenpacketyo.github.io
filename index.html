2026-07-03 10:02:25 EDT

UK government officials are in talks with Pakistan regarding the potential deportation of Shabir Ahmed, a convicted Rochdale grooming gang ringleader recently released from jail. Ahmed, stripped of his UK passport after his 2012 conviction for multiple sexual assault offenses, cannot currently be deported due to a 55-year-old law barring removal of Commonwealth citizens who arrived before 1973. Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer has ordered a review of the case, with calls to amend the Immigration Act 1971. Victims expressed fear and felt unsafe upon Ahmed's release, despite his 24-hour electronic monitoring. The UK faces legislative and diplomatic hurdles, as Pakistan has previously refused to accept the deportation of Ahmed's co-ringleaders.
