California Governor Gavin Newsom rallied Texas Democrats, celebrating Proposition 50's victory in California as a national win and a strong rebuke of President Trump. Newsom accused Trump of pressing Texas Governor Greg Abbott to redraw congressional districts to favor Republicans, an effort Proposition 50 aimed to counteract by potentially adding five Democrats to California's delegation. Speaking with presidential aspirations, Newsom called Trump an "invasive species" and "historically unpopular," asserting that the Proposition 50 outcome demonstrated public rejection of Trump's attempts to "rig" the 2026 midterm election. His trip, part of a broader national presence, underscores his potential 2028 White House bid, as he next heads to a UN climate summit in Brazil to further position himself on the world stage.
