2026-02-17 14:26:37 EST

Amazon's stock is experiencing a historic losing streak, potentially marking its longest series of daily losses on record, which has resulted in a roughly $450 billion reduction in market valuation. This significant decline is attributed to investor concerns over the company's substantial AI spending plans, which include a projected $200 billion in capital expenditures for the year. Analysts are questioning the potential return on investment for these massive outlays, particularly as other tech giants also increase their AI infrastructure spending. While Amazon's CEO defends the investment as crucial for seizing AI opportunities in the cloud, the company is now in a "prove it mode" to demonstrate tangible returns to investors. The stock's performance reflects broader market apprehension regarding the financial implications of the AI race.

Jesse Jackson Hailed as 'Living Bridge' Between Civil Rights Eras

2026-02-17 14:20:39 EST

Jesse Jackson, who passed away at 84, is being remembered as a pivotal figure in the civil rights movement, serving as a "living bridge" between Martin Luther King Jr. and Barack Obama. His two presidential campaigns in the 1980s are credited with expanding the political imagination for Black leadership and shaping the modern Democratic Party through his "rainbow coalition" concept. Jackson's legacy includes advocacy for voting rights, racial justice, and progressive foreign policy, influencing contemporary figures and movements like Black Lives Matter. He witnessed King's assassination and later celebrated Obama's election, embodying a continuous struggle for equality and justice. His impact on American politics and society is widely acknowledged as transformative.

U.S. Air Force VIP Fleet Repainted in Trump's Preferred Color Scheme

2026-02-17 14:18:47 EST

The U.S. Air Force's presidential and VIP fleet is undergoing a repainting process to adopt President Trump's preferred color palette, replacing the traditional Kennedy-era blue and white. Aircraft, including the donated Qatari jet and planes being converted for Air Force One, will feature a new design in dark navy blue, deep red, and gold. This change applies to Air Force One jets and smaller aircraft like the C-32, which transport high-priority personnel. While Trump had previously proposed a similar paint design for Air Force One, it was canceled by President Biden. The repainting is occurring as aircraft undergo scheduled maintenance and repairs, with the first C-32 already painted and awaiting delivery.
