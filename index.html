Antidepressant Users Face Increased Challenges During Heatwaves

Symptoms like dizziness, nausea, headaches, and dehydration can be severely exacerbated, profoundly impacting daily activities and well-being. Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) and tricyclic antidepressants specifically affect the hypothalamus, the brain's temperature control center, and can also diminish the sensation of thirst, further increasing dehydration risk.
