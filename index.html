Understanding Aortic Dissections Following Senator Lindsey Graham's Death

2026-07-12 22:44:55 EDT

Senator Lindsey Graham's recent death from an aortic dissection has brought attention to this life-threatening medical emergency. An aortic dissection occurs when a tear in the inner layer of the aorta allows blood to flow between its layers, potentially obstructing blood flow to organs. Symptoms often appear suddenly and include severe chest or back pain, resembling a heart attack, necessitating immediate medical attention for survival. While rare, it is more common in men in their 60s and 70s, with risk factors including smoking and high blood pressure. Managing blood pressure and avoiding smoking can help reduce the risk of this serious condition.
