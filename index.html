The U.S. Justice Department has charged the governor of Mexico's Sinaloa state, Ruben Rocha Moya, and nine other officials with alleged involvement in the Sinaloa Cartel's operations. The charges include conspiracy to import narcotics into the U.S. in exchange for political support and bribes. This action represents a rare move against sitting Mexican politicians and is expected to increase tensions between the two countries. Mexican prosecutors have launched their own investigation, and the foreign ministry stated it would protest the U.S.'s public announcement of the charges. Governor Moya has vehemently denied the allegations, calling them slanderous and a violation of national sovereignty.
