Giannandrea will retire next spring, having held the position since joining Apple in 2018. This transition comes as experts suggest Apple has lagged behind tech peers in AI, with its Apple Intelligence suite receiving mixed reviews and a key Siri improvement delayed until 2026. Subramanya will lead teams focused on foundation models, research, and AI safety, reporting to software chief Craig Federighi, as Apple aims to intensify its AI development and integrate it more effectively into its products amidst increasing competition.
