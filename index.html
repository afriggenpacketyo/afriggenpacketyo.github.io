Rohingya Refugees Allege Forced Deportation by India, Raising Human Rights Concerns

2025-08-28 18:44:27 EDT

Rohingya refugees and their relatives are alleging that the Indian government forcibly deported 40 individuals, including women and children, to Myanmar, a country from which they had fled civil war and persecution. Accounts describe the refugees being flown to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, then compelled onto a naval vessel, and ultimately forced to jump into the Andaman Sea with life jackets. Thomas Andrews, the UN's special rapporteur, asserts there is "significant evidence" corroborating these allegations, to which India has not yet responded. This reported treatment underscores the precarious status of Rohingya in India, where they are not recognized as refugees and face increasing deportations, raising serious human rights concerns.
