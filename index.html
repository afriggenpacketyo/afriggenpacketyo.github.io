Understanding Heat Exhaustion and Heat Stroke: Prevention and Emergency Measures

As summer brings record high temperatures, it's crucial to differentiate between heat exhaustion and heat stroke and know how to respond. Heat exhaustion, less severe than heat stroke, can lead to the more serious condition if not properly managed, with symptoms including headache, fatigue, nausea, and cool skin. Treatment involves moving to a cool place, drinking water, and applying cold cloths, while heat stroke is a life-threatening condition characterized by impaired consciousness, high body temperature, and potential organ damage. Immediate medical attention is necessary for heat stroke, involving cooling the body and providing fluids, with prevention strategies including avoiding excessive sun exposure, staying hydrated, and wearing appropriate clothing.
