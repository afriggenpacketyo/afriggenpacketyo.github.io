Understanding the Air Quality Index

2025-06-03 11:48:00 EDT

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a tool used nationwide to measure air pollution severity and categorize health risks. The index uses six color-coded categories: good (green), moderate (yellow), unhealthy for sensitive groups (orange), unhealthy (red), very unhealthy (purple), and hazardous (maroon). The Environmental Protection Agency calculates the AQI based on five major pollutants: ground-level ozone, particulate matter, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide. The lower the number, the cleaner the air, with values above 100 posing increasing risks to health.
