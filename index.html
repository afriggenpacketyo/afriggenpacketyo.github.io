This "nostalgia reset" is largely a reaction to today's economic unease, characterized by high prices and unsettled politics, alongside what is termed the "ensh*ttiification" of social media. Young adults are rediscovering the appeal of brick-and-mortar shopping, seeking the comfort and familiarity of the mid-2010s. Experts suggest that brands with high awareness that have lost some cultural relevance, such as Abercrombie & Fitch, are now well-positioned to capitalize on this trend, offering a sense of manageable stability and "peak casual but hot fashion."
