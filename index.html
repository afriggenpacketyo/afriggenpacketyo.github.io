Luther Davis, a former Alabama defensive lineman, has been accused of orchestrating an elaborate fraud scheme, allegedly defrauding lenders of nearly $20 million by impersonating NFL players to secure loans for luxury real estate, vehicles, and jewelry. Davis, along with an alleged co-conspirator, CJ Evins, faces serious felony counts of conspiracy to commit wire fraud and aggravated identity theft. Court documents detail a sophisticated hoax where Davis purportedly utilized wigs, makeup, and head coverings to disguise himself as various NFL players during virtual loan-closing meetings. The specific NFL players whose identities were used, including Xavier McKinney, David Njoku, and Michael Penix Jr., have been explicitly cleared of any involvement in the alleged scheme. Both defendants have indicated their intention to enter guilty pleas at upcoming hearings scheduled for later this month.
