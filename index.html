Explainer: The Role and Function of the International Court of Justice

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based at the Peace Palace in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial body of the United Nations. Composed of 15 judges from different nations, it adjudicates legal disputes between states, distinct from the International Criminal Court (ICC) which tries individuals. Its jurisdiction applies when states consent to it or when a dispute falls under a UN convention like the Genocide Convention. While its rulings are legally binding, the ICJ has no enforcement mechanism, relying on the cooperation of the involved parties.
