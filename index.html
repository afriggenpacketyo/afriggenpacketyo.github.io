Activists in California's Inland Empire are using eye-catching signs that read "ICE stole someone here" to raise awareness about Immigration and Customs Enforcement raids in the region. The Inland Coalition for Immigrant Justice (IC4IJ) utilizes these signs, designed by artist Jaime Muñoz, to make the stories of deported community members visible and tangible, especially in areas with limited local journalism. These efforts aim to counter aggressive ICE operations and alleged inhumane conditions in local detainment centers. The signs serve as a form of remembrance, inspired by the European "stolpersteine" memorial plaques, and empower communities to push back against perceived injustices.
