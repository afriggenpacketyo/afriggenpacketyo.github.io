2025-09-10 06:13:12 EDT

Frightening new details from the National Transportation Safety Board reveal passengers and crew on a Delta Air Lines flight to Europe were violently thrown into the ceiling and floor during 2.5 minutes of severe turbulence. The incident occurred over Wyoming in July, catching pilots by surprise despite route alterations, with the seat belt sign off. Passengers experienced gravitational forces up to 1.75 times their body weight, leading to 24 evaluations and 18 hospitalizations, with two crew members seriously injured. The NTSB is investigating pilot judgment, as the plane flew directly into the worst part of a storm. Scientists warn such serious turbulence incidents may become more common due to climate change, raising aviation safety concerns.
