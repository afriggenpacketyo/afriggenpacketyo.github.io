While SpaceX's rocketry and Starlink satellite network are profitable, the immense valuation appears heavily reliant on its artificial intelligence (AI) business, xAI, and ambitious deep space programs. This IPO is seen as a crucial test of investor optimism surrounding AI's economic potential. Critics question the valuation, with some suggesting it's more tied to Elon Musk's brand than tangible assets, while others raise concerns about the concentration of power in mega-corporations.
