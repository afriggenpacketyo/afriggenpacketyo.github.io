The Historic Introduction of Football's Penalty Shootout in 1970

2026-03-28 02:15:22 EDT

Football's penalty shootout, now a ubiquitous and often dramatic decider in tied matches, made its official debut on August 5, 1970, in a Watney Cup match between Manchester United and Hull City. This innovation replaced earlier, less skill-based methods like coin tosses or drawing lots, which had been criticized for their arbitrary nature, notably after Italy advanced in the 1968 European Championship by coin toss. The idea was championed by Israeli Football Association officials Yosef Dagan and Michael Almog, who proposed a system of five penalties per side to FIFA in 1969. The first shootout saw George Best score and Denis Law miss, with Hull City's goalkeeper Ian McKechnie becoming the first to save and then miss a penalty.
