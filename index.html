UK MPs reject outright ban on social media for under-16s, favoring flexible powers

2026-03-09 18:57:28 EDT

Members of Parliament have voted against an outright ban on social media for children under 16, opting instead for more flexible regulatory powers. The proposal, which aimed to restrict access to platforms like TikTok and Snapchat, was defeated in the House of Commons. Education minister Olivia Bailey argued that a blanket ban could drive children to less regulated online spaces and that the government is consulting on measures such as minimum age requirements and disabling addictive features. The Science Secretary will be granted powers to restrict or ban children of certain ages from social media and chatbots, and to limit harmful features. While some MPs abstained, citing the addictive nature of social media, the majority supported the government's approach to child online safety. score

2026-03-09 18:49:51 EDT

The UK government has introduced a new definition of 'anti-Muslim hostility' aimed at protecting Muslims from abuse while explicitly stating it will not restrict freedom of speech or criticism of Islam. Communities Secretary Steve Reed explained that the definition outlines unacceptable prejudice, discrimination, and hatred targeting Muslims, including criminal acts and prejudicial stereotyping. This definition will guide public bodies in taking action against such behavior. Concerns had been raised by the Equality and Human Rights Commission that a new definition might chill free expression, but Reed assured that legitimate public interest concerns are protected.
