Laryngologist Dr. Inna Husain explains that chronic throat clearing, a common reflex, can become a harmful habit that damages vocal folds and increases throat sensitivity. One cause is laryngeal pharyngeal reflux (LPR), or "silent reflux," where stomach contents irritate the throat without heartburn symptoms. Other causes include allergies, sinusitis, vocal cord issues, aging, asthma, tracheal narrowing, and some medications. Dr. Husain recommends identifying the root cause through an ENT exam, staying hydrated, avoiding smoking and acidic foods, and using saline nebulizers; alternate behaviors like swallowing or humming can also help the throat heal.
