An Iranian eyewitness, Parnia, has provided a harrowing account of the violent crackdown on protests in Isfahan, describing her neighborhood as transforming into a "war zone" as security forces fired live ammunition into crowds. Amidst a government-imposed internet blackout, Parnia recounted seeing numerous people shot and injured, with local clinics struggling to treat the many pellet wounds. The protests, initially sparked by anger over currency collapse, featured chants against Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, who warned against "destructive elements." Despite immense risks, protesters continued, hoping for international intervention, while health workers reported hospitals being monitored and injured individuals facing arrest, highlighting severe human rights abuses.
