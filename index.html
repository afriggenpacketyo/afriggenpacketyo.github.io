The History and Meaning of the Word 'Tariff'

2025-04-09 05:00:00 EDT

The word "tariff," central to President Trump's trade policy, has a rich history rooted in global trade. Originating from the Arabic word "taʽrīf" meaning "notification" or "inventory," the term evolved through various languages before entering English in the 16th century. Tariffs have been a part of U.S. trade policy since the country's founding, initially aimed at raising revenue and protecting domestic manufacturing. However, their use has shifted over time, leading to controversies such as the Tariff of Abominations in 1828 and the Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act in 1930. The word's meaning may be broadening beyond a simple tax rate to encompass broader economic punishment.
