Shares of wind farm developer Orsted experienced a sharp decline, tumbling over 16% to a record low, following a U.S. government order to halt construction on its Revolution Wind Project off Rhode Island. The project, which is 80% complete with 45 of 65 wind turbines installed, now faces significant uncertainty, raising concerns about the company's previously announced $9.4 billion rights issue. Analysts from Sydbank warned of potential double-digit billion write-downs and increased capital raising needs if the project cannot supply electricity. Despite these financial pressures, Orsted confirmed its compliance with the order and stated its intention to proceed with the capital raising, supported by its majority stakeholder, the Danish state. Edinburgh Fringe Festival Grapples with Identity Amid Rising Costs and Mainstream Appeal

2025-08-25 11:42:35 EDT

Edinburgh's Fringe festival, long celebrated as a bastion of alternative and experimental arts, is confronting an identity challenge as escalating costs threaten its foundational inclusive and accessible spirit. Both performers and attendees express concern that rising prices for accommodation and tickets are increasingly making the festival unattainable for independent and emerging artists, and pushing it towards a more affluent demographic. Despite its continued role as a crucial launching pad for talents like Rowan Atkinson and Phoebe Waller-Bridge, many worry the Fringe is losing its "fringe" essence. The article posits that to preserve its original ethos, a new "fringe of the Fringe" might be necessary to ensure its future accessibility.
