The European Central Bank (ECB) has maintained its benchmark interest rates at 2% while acknowledging intensified upside risks to inflation and downside risks to economic growth, largely due to the war in Iran. The ECB noted that the conflict's impact on inflation and activity will depend on the duration and intensity of the energy price shock. Inflation in the euro zone has risen to 3%, driven by energy costs, and economic growth slowed in the first quarter. The ECB emphasized a data-dependent, meeting-by-meeting approach to monetary policy, leaving open the possibility of future rate hikes while prioritizing price stability.
