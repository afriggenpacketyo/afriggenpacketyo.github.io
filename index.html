Explaining Why Government Shutdowns Are a Uniquely American Political Phenomenon

2025-10-03 12:23:59 EDT

Unlike most other Western democracies, the United States experiences frequent government shutdowns due to its unique political structure and budgetary rules. In parliamentary systems like the United Kingdom's, the executive branch controls spending proposals, and a failure to pass a budget can trigger new elections, creating high stakes that ensure passage. In contrast, the U.S. system grants Congress significant power over the purse, and the bicameral legislature with its filibuster rules makes passing a budget inherently more complex. A 1980 legal opinion also made U.S. shutdowns more severe by prohibiting government spending without congressional approval, a problem other nations avoid.
