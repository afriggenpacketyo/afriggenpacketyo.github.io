This incoming system follows closely on the heels of another powerful storm that caused significant travel disruptions over the Thanksgiving holiday weekend across the Midwest and Great Lakes region, including record snowfall in Chicago. Forecasters are predicting accumulations of five to ten inches of snow for areas extending from the Poconos to eastern Maine, with higher elevations in New England potentially receiving over a foot. While major metropolitan areas are not expected to experience the heaviest snowfall, officials are strongly advising residents to delay all non-essential travel due to anticipated hazardous road conditions.
