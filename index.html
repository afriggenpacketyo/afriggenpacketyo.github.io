This opinion article details a pattern of "unchecked abuses of power" by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and Customs and Border Protection (CBP) agents in Minneapolis, extending beyond the fatal shootings of Alex Pretti and Renee Good. The author highlights numerous incidents, including the use of chemical crowd control, brutality against protesters, bystanders, and citizens, and baseless arrests often conducted under administrative rather than judicial warrants. The article describes agents acting like an "occupying force," clashing with local police and employing controversial tactics such as attempting to use a 5-year-old child as "bait" to detain others. These actions raise significant Fourth Amendment concerns and prompt urgent questions about federal accountability in the city.
