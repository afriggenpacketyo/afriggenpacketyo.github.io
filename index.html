Secretary of Homeland Security Markwayne Mullin clarified ICE's role at the upcoming FIFA World Cup in the U.S., stating the agency will prioritize security and customs enforcement, not mass immigration arrests. Mullin explained ICE's long-standing presence at major sporting events focuses on counterfeit goods, tickets, and identifying individuals on terrorist watchlists or wanted for serious crimes. While not ruling out immigration enforcement entirely, he emphasized the primary mission is not to "round up mass individuals," but to target "the worst of the worst." Mullin also discussed DHS's recent shutdown, workforce losses, and its approach to hantavirus, highlighting the department's broader national security challenges.
