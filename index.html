This recommendation emerges amidst parallel efforts by US lawmakers to curb Chinese influence in elementary and secondary schools, often by linking federal funding to the severance of institutional ties with Chinese government-linked entities. The CECC contends that deepening linguistic and cultural expertise will strategically better equip the U.S. to effectively confront "malign" attempts by Beijing to expand its global influence. The US-Taiwan Education Initiative is highlighted as a successful model for expanding access, emphasizing the goal of fostering deeper understanding rather than promoting isolation.
