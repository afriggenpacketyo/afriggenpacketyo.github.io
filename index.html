At just 19, Shay courageously saved countless lives on Omaha Beach, earning a Silver Star for repeatedly plunging into the sea to rescue critically wounded soldiers from drowning, and later received France's Legion of Honor. After World War II, he reenlisted, serving in the Korean War and later working at the International Atomic Energy Agency. Shay, who moved to France to be close to his fallen comrades, eventually broke a 60-year silence to share his powerful testimony and spread a message of peace, though he expressed profound sadness at the return of war in Europe.
