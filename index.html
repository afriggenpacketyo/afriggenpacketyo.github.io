When an individual passes away, their outstanding credit card debt typically becomes the responsibility of their estate, not surviving family members. During probate, the estate's assets are used to pay valid creditor claims before any inheritance is distributed. If the estate lacks sufficient funds, unsecured debts like credit card balances may go unpaid and be written off. Generally, relatives are not personally obligated to pay a deceased person's credit card debt.
