The Krasheninnikov volcano, located on Russia's remote far eastern Kamchatka Peninsula, has erupted for the first time in at least 400 years, sending an ash plume soaring over 3.7 miles into the sky. This significant geological event, which occurred just days after a massive 8.8-magnitude earthquake struck the region, was also accompanied by a 7.0-magnitude earthquake and initially triggered a tsunami warning for three areas of Kamchatka. Although the tsunami warning was subsequently lifted and no populated areas were affected by ashfall, the eruption marks a notable reawakening for the volcano. Experts from the Kamchatka Volcanic Eruption Response Team note that the volcano's last confirmed lava effusion took place around 1463, and anticipate that moderate explosive activity may persist.
