Mistrial Declared in Sacramento's Deadliest Shooting Case

2026-07-21 19:33:49 EDT

A mistrial has been declared in the case against Mtula Payton and Dandrae Martin, accused of carrying out Sacramento’s deadliest shooting, which killed six people and injured at least a dozen in 2022. While the jury could not reach a consensus on the murder charges, both men were found guilty of being in possession of firearms despite having prior felonies. Prosecutors now face the decision of pursuing a retrial for the murder charges. The two-month trial, held just blocks from the state capitol, included dozens of hours of footage and testimony from officers, witnesses, and the accused, but the outcome leaves victims' families unable to begin healing, prolonging their search for justice. Leia Schenk, a victims' advocate, noted that the process of grieving is made harder by the protracted trial.
