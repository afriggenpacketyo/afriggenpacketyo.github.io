However, this article highlights a significant and promising shift at the state level, where lawmakers are rapidly implementing innovative new policies. These include tax credits for caregivers, subsidized respite care, expanded paid family leave laws, and crucial protections against workplace discrimination. Washington State is notably launching an ambitious universal long-term care insurance program, demonstrating that these widespread state-level experiments are building crucial momentum for potential future national change in addressing caregiving as a vital labor and economic issue.
