The Engineering Reason Behind Starboard-Side Islands on Aircraft Carriers

2025-10-06 12:00:00 EDT

The superstructure, or "island," on almost all aircraft carriers is located on the starboard (right) side of the flight deck for a critical aerodynamic reason related to propeller-driven aircraft. Early pilots discovered that the clockwise rotation of most Western aircraft engines naturally causes the plane to yaw to the left during takeoff and landing, a phenomenon known as the "P Factor." Placing the island on the right side provided a clear visual reference for pilots and kept the structure out of the way of this natural leftward drift. This design choice, established over a century ago, has become a standard feature in aircraft carrier construction.
