An Overview of the International Court of Justice's Role and Jurisdiction

2025-07-16 15:25:00 EDT

The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial body of the United Nations, tasked with settling legal disputes between states. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, the court's jurisdiction is not automatic; it applies only when states agree to it, either through a blanket declaration or on a case-by-case basis. The ICJ can also hear cases based on specific UN conventions, such as the Genocide Convention, which has been invoked in cases involving Ukraine, Myanmar, and Israel. However, the court lacks an enforcement mechanism, meaning its rulings are binding but depend on the cooperation of the involved parties for implementation.
