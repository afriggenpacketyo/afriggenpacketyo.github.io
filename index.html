The proposed bill aims to close a persistent and recognized gap in U.S. counter-drone policy, enabling private entities, after undergoing federal training and certification, to take action against drones posing a "credible threat" to designated facilities like power plants and wastewater treatment plants. This legislative initiative follows a series of incidents, including a drone crash near an electric substation, highlighting significant vulnerabilities to inexpensive, commercially available drones. The bill authorizes $250 million over five years for counter-drone systems and provides essential legal liability protections.
