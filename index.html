A man has tragically been found dead in northern Greece as an out-of-control wildfire near Thessaloniki continues to rage, prompting village evacuations and severely challenging firefighting efforts. Over 100 firefighters, supported by dozens of engines and seven aircraft, are battling a rapidly spreading, 5km long wall of flame amidst hot and windy conditions. A woman also suffered burns while reportedly attempting to extinguish the blaze, which erupted and quickly engulfed a nearby forest. This incident marks Greece's latest significant forest fire as the country approaches its peak tourist season, a period increasingly vulnerable to such catastrophes due to recurring droughts and heatwaves.

Legal Rights of Debt Collectors After a Borrower's Death

2026-06-30 14:42:15 EDT

The death of a borrower triggers a distinct legal process for debt collection, wherein outstanding debts typically transfer to the deceased's estate rather than directly to surviving family members. Debt collectors are legally permitted to file claims against the estate during the probate process, and if the estate possesses sufficient assets after covering higher-priority expenses like taxes and funeral costs, qualifying debts may be settled before any remaining assets are distributed to heirs. While collectors can contact the estate's executor or other authorized individuals, they are prohibited from harassing family members or misrepresenting who is legally responsible for the debt. Exceptions for personal liability include co-signers, joint account holders, or surviving spouses in specific states. (not investment advice)
