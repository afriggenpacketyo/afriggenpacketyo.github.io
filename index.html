The demonstrations, which escalated after a government-imposed social media shutdown, tragically turned violent, resulting in 72 deaths, including Praveen Kulung, who died in his brother Mausam's arms. Despite claims from student leaders that the violence was instigated by external infiltrators, the youth remain resolute in their fight for a democratic overhaul. Following the resignation of Prime Minister K.P. Sharma Oli, an interim government has been established, and Gen Z protesters are now actively discussing the formation of a new political party to contest the upcoming March 2026 elections.
