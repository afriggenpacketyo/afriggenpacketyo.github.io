Oracle shares surged by as much as 10% in extended trading after the software vendor reported better-than-expected quarterly results and significantly boosted its fiscal 2027 revenue guidance. The company's total cloud revenue, encompassing both infrastructure and software as a service, climbed an impressive 44% year-over-year to $8.9 billion, surpassing analyst expectations. Oracle also reported a substantial increase in remaining performance obligations, quadrupling to $553 billion, primarily driven by large-scale AI contracts. Management expressed strong confidence in its ongoing investments in data centers and compute capacity, asserting that new AI Code Generation technology is enabling more efficient software development and the creation of more SaaS applications for various industries at a lower cost.
