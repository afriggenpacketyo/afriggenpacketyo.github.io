The ruling explicitly acknowledges the "risks of irreparable harm" to parolees, who face potential separation from families, communities, and lawful employment, and a return to dangers in their home countries. However, the court found an insufficient likelihood of success on the merits to maintain the stay, marking a victory for the Trump administration's efforts to dismantle Biden-era immigration policies, which critics call the "largest mass illegalization event in modern American history."
