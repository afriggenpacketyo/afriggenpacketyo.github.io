Advocates and medical professionals are emphasizing the need to normalize discussions about bowel health, often referred to as 'poop talk,' to overcome the persistent stigma associated with the topic. This reluctance to discuss symptoms can lead to delayed diagnoses, especially for individuals under 45 who may face insurance limitations for preventive screenings. Personal stories and expert opinions highlight the critical role of self-advocacy and open communication in encouraging timely screening and early detection of colorectal cancer.
