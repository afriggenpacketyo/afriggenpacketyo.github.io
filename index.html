The film's themes of expendability, lack of mentorship, and the pressure to conform resonate with broader workplace issues faced by women. The article draws parallels between the film's narrative and Sheryl Sandberg's 'Lean In' philosophy, noting how both emphasize individual agency while potentially overlooking structural barriers. It suggests that Nina's breakdown illustrates the psychological toll of excessive self-reliance in environments that do not adequately support ambition, particularly for women facing systemic inequities.
