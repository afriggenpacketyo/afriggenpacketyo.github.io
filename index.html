Explainer: The Urban Heat Island Effect and Its Impact on Cities

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

As heatwaves become more common, cities often experience significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to a phenomenon known as the "urban heat island effect." Dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, releasing it slowly and elevating temperatures, particularly at night. This effect is compounded by waste heat from vehicles and air conditioning, and a lack of cooling vegetation and water sources. With global temperatures rising and urban populations growing, cities are becoming increasingly vulnerable to the dangerous impacts of extreme heat.
