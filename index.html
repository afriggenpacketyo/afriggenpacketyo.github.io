2026-06-30 10:09:59 EDT

The World Cup has propelled players like Cape Verde's goalkeeper Vozinha and New Zealand's Tim Payne to viral social media fame, raising questions about their ability to convert this attention into lasting financial success. Vozinha's Instagram followers skyrocketed after an impressive match, surpassing NFL legend Tom Brady, while Payne's profile was boosted by an influencer campaign. Experts like Mike Serazio warn that such viral fame can be fleeting, though Brooke Duffy notes that millions of followers can command significant payments from brand partnerships. The article explores how athletes are increasingly leveraging social media to build personal brands and strike deals, moving beyond traditional media endorsements, but long-term monetization remains a challenge for those without sustained elite performance.
