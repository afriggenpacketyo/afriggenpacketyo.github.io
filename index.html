Bumblebees Exhibit Problem-Solving Skills Comparable to Apes and Elephants

2026-06-07 05:00:00 EDT

New research indicates that bumblebees, despite their small brains, possess remarkable problem-solving abilities, mirroring those seen in chimpanzees and elephants. In controlled experiments, untrained bees successfully used a small ball as a tool to reach an overhead reward, demonstrating goal-directed behavior. This finding challenges previous assumptions about the link between brain size and cognitive complexity. The study suggests that such cognitive flexibility is crucial for bumblebees' survival in dynamic natural environments, enabling them to adapt to changing food sources.
