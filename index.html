Scientific Phenomenon of 'Urban Heat Islands' Explained Amid UK Heatwave

As a heatwave affects the UK, this article explains the "urban heat island" effect, the reason why cities are often significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. Hard, dark surfaces like asphalt and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, slowly releasing it as heat, particularly at night. In contrast, rural landscapes with more vegetation and water stay cooler through natural evaporation. This effect, which can make cities 4-6°C warmer, exacerbates the dangers of heatwaves for urban populations and highlights their increased vulnerability as climate change makes extreme heat more common.
