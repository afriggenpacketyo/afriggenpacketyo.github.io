A powerful ocean swell has led to hazardous conditions along the California coastline, resulting in the death of a woman in Santa Cruz and another fatality in Laguna Beach. Authorities warn that dangerous rip currents and elevated surf will persist through Monday evening. In Santa Cruz, two women were swept out to sea near Yellow Bank Beach, with one pronounced dead and the other in critical condition. Lifeguards have responded to numerous rescues across Southern California, with beaches experiencing significant wave sizes and minor coastal flooding. The National Weather Service has issued a beach hazard statement, urging vigilance due to the increased risk of drowning and boat capsizing.

Mumbai women co-direct documentary 'Cool Ladies Club,' showcasing their lives with dignity

2026-06-13 06:56:45 EDT

Ten working-class women from Mumbai have co-directed a documentary titled 'Cool Ladies Club' ('Mast Mahila Mandali'), aiming to showcase their lives and challenge perceptions of helplessness. These women, working as domestic helpers, health workers, and caregivers, learned filmmaking basics to tell their own stories, transforming their 'radical act of resistance' into a 70-minute film. The project, initiated by nonprofit CORO India and guided by filmmaker Shilpi Gulati, allowed them to express creativity and find joy, making them feel 'young again.' The documentary highlights themes of self-expression, friendship, and reclaiming dignity, with personal stories like Rehana Shaikh's dance sequence illustrating their pursuit of passion against societal constraints. The film's success at its premiere and subsequent screenings underscores the power of their voices.
