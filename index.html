Understanding Required Minimum Distributions from Retirement Accounts

2026-05-27 11:08:29 EDT

This article explains Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) from tax-deferred retirement accounts like traditional IRAs and 401(k)s, which generally begin at age 73. RMDs are calculated by dividing the account balance by an IRS life expectancy factor, with the percentage of the account required to withdraw increasing annually. For a $400,000 account, the RMD ranges from about $15,094 at age 73 to $19,802 at age 80. RMDs are taxed as ordinary income, potentially affecting tax brackets, Social Security benefits, and Medicare premiums.
