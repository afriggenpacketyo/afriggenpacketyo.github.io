Birthright Citizenship as a New World Ideal

2025-06-10 08:57:43 EDT

Birthright citizenship, or jus solis, is a common practice in the Americas, granting citizenship to children born within a country's borders regardless of their parents' nationality. This contrasts with jus sanguinis, which assigns citizenship based on parental nationality, often tied to bloodline, race, or religion. Mexico was the first to enshrine jus solis in its constitution in 1814, aiming to create a society of equal members regardless of skin color. The concept of birthright citizenship is deeply rooted in the history and ideals of the Americas, promoting inclusivity and equality.
