Oil and LNG supply disruptions expected to persist through mid-2026 due to Strait of Hormuz closure

2026-04-24 15:55:31 EDT

Baker Hughes, a major oilfield services firm, anticipates that the Strait of Hormuz may not fully reopen until the latter half of 2026, impacting global energy markets. This projection is factored into their financial guidance, assuming the U.S.-Iran conflict continues through June. The company's CFO highlighted the significant uncertainty surrounding the conflict's duration and depth. The closure of this vital trade route, through which approximately 20% of global oil supplies passed before the conflict, has already disrupted 10% of global oil volumes and 20% of LNG supplies, leading to persistent risk premiums for prices. The ongoing conflict has seen both the U.S. and Iran seize commercial ships, further complicating tanker traffic through the strait.

Southern Poverty Law Center faces federal indictment over alleged informant program fraud

2026-04-24 15:48:12 EDT

The Southern Poverty Law Center (SPLC) has been indicted on federal fraud charges stemming from a covert paid informant program that allegedly funneled millions of dollars to individuals affiliated with white supremacist and neo-Nazi groups. The Justice Department claims the SPLC allocated approximately $3 million to informants between 2014 and 2023, including those linked to the United Klans of America and the National Socialist Party of America. Prosecutors allege the SPLC was not dismantling extremist groups but rather "manufacturing the extremism it purports to oppose by paying sources to stoke racial hatred." The indictment includes charges of wire fraud, false statements, and conspiracy to commit concealment money laundering.

Recent graduates express regret over college majors, with liberal arts fields showing highest dissatisfaction

2026-04-24 15:46:53 EDT

A significant portion of recent college graduates, approximately one in five, regret their chosen major, according to a ZipRecruiter survey. Liberal arts fields, particularly political science, international relations, and public policy, reported the highest rates of dissatisfaction at 46.3%. Communications, media studies, and public relations majors also showed high regret at 39.2%. While scientific fields also saw some regret, the study suggests that young workers may feel this more acutely due to the current challenging job market and lower-than-expected starting salaries in their fields. Nursing degrees, however, continue to offer strong career prospects and higher median salaries.
