New York Legislature Approves Temporary Ban on Large Datacenters

2026-06-06 08:00:04 EDT

New York is on the verge of becoming the first U.S. state to enact a one-year moratorium on large datacenters, following legislative approval of a bill that now awaits Governor Kathy Hochul's signature. The proposed ban aims to provide time for the state to develop comprehensive regulations for these energy-intensive facilities, which are crucial for the AI boom but raise concerns about water usage, grid strain, and environmental impact. State Senator Kristen Gonzalez, a co-author of the bill, emphasizes the need to protect New Yorkers' resources and ensure community input. While industry groups express concerns about discouraging investment, proponents argue that responsible development requires a pause to establish necessary standards. The move reflects a growing nationwide backlash against datacenter expansion and a push for greater oversight. This legislative action signals a significant shift in how states are approaching the rapid growth of the tech industry.
