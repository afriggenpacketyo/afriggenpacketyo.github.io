BP Profits More Than Double Amid Iran War-Driven Oil Price Surge

2026-04-28 06:02:19 EDT

BP reported a more than doubling of profits in the first quarter of the year, largely driven by a surge in oil prices following the outbreak of the Iran war. The energy giant's profits reached $3.2 billion, significantly exceeding analyst expectations and the previous year's earnings. This increase is attributed to an "exceptional" performance in BP's oil trading division, which thrived in the volatile market conditions created by the conflict. While upstream production remained flat, the company's customer and products division saw a substantial rise in profits. BP's CEO acknowledged the complex global environment but highlighted efforts to ensure fuel supply and minimize disruption.
