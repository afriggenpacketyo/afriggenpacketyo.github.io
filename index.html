Rise in Rough Sleepers in Hollywood Amidst City's Encampment Removal Efforts

2026-05-22 06:00:00 EDT

The number of "rough sleepers" in Hollywood is increasing as the city's programs to remove encampments gain momentum, presenting new challenges for outreach workers. A recent homeless count conducted by Hollywood 4WRD revealed a shift from tent-dwelling to sleeping in vehicles or on sidewalks, making individuals harder to locate and serve. The Rand Corporation's research highlights this trend, noting that encampment resolution strategies are less effective for those without fixed shelter. Service providers are facing increased difficulties in outreach as the unsheltered population becomes more geographically dispersed. The data underscores the complex and evolving nature of homelessness in the region.
