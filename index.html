Understanding the Seven-Year Rule for Debt on Credit Reports

2025-11-10 11:41:43 EST

A common misconception among borrowers is that debts are automatically forgiven after seven years, but this is not entirely accurate. While the Fair Credit Reporting Act mandates that most negative information, such as late payments and collections, must be removed from a credit report after seven years, the underlying debt itself does not disappear. Creditors can legally continue to pursue collection of the owed amount, including accrued interest and fees. The enforceability of the debt in court is determined by a separate state-specific statute of limitations, which typically ranges from three to ten years. Certain obligations, like federal student loans, are not subject to these time limits.
