2025-08-25 19:15:44 EDT

Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) and several other Southern California airports were subjected to temporary ground stops on Monday afternoon due to the possibility of thunderstorms, according to the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) and National Weather Service. Departures from various airports in nearby states, including Phoenix, Denver, and Salt Lake City, were temporarily halted as a precautionary measure between 3:25 p.m. and 4:45 p.m. The storms are part of a surge of monsoonal moisture and instability across the Western U.S., which has brought dangerous dry lightning, major dust storms, and rare summer rain over the last few days. While the ground stops for Long Beach Airport and John Wayne Airport expired, the weather conditions created temporary travel disruptions in the region, emphasizing safety protocols.

Food Inflation Reaches 18-Month High Driven by Rising Chocolate and Butter Prices

2025-08-25 19:10:09 EDT

Food price inflation has climbed to its highest level in 18 months, reaching 4.2% this month compared with 4% in July, primarily driven by the increasing costs of essential items like chocolate, butter, and eggs. According to the British Retail Consortium's latest shop price monitor, this surge is attributed to a combination of high demand, tightening global supply chains, increased labor costs, and poor cocoa harvests, which are linked to climate change and crop disease. This figure is corroborated by separate official data from the Office for National Statistics, which reported a 4.9% rise in the cost of food and non-alcoholic beverages year-on-year to July, and a substantial 37% increase over the past five years, placing growing financial pressure on households.
