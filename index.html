An Overview of Methane's Role as a Potent Greenhouse Gas in Climate Change

2025-10-22 05:04:00 EDT

Methane (CH4), the primary component of natural gas, is a powerful greenhouse gas responsible for approximately 20-30% of global warming since the Industrial Revolution. Although it remains in the atmosphere for only about 12 years, far less than carbon dioxide, it traps about 84 times more heat over a 20-year period. Human activities, particularly from agriculture, landfills, and the energy sector, account for 60% of atmospheric methane. In the energy industry, methane is released through leaks, intentional venting, and flaring during oil and gas extraction. International efforts are underway to curb these emissions, focusing on improved detection and repair of faulty equipment.
