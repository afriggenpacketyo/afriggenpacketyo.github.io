The Story of Deborah Sampson, a Revolutionary War Soldier

2025-04-19 09:32:03 EDT

Deborah Sampson, a woman from Plympton, Massachusetts, disguised herself as a man named Robert Shurtlieff to enlist in the Fourth Massachusetts Regiment during the Revolutionary War. She served for nearly two years, demonstrating bravery and skill in battle, even taking a musket ball to the thigh and extracting it herself to avoid revealing her secret. After contracting a fever, her true identity was discovered by a doctor who kept her secret until she was honorably discharged. Sampson later became the first woman to receive a military pension and embarked on a year-long lecture tour, sharing her experiences as a female soldier and defying societal norms of her time. The Deborah Sampson Act was passed in 2021 to provide women veterans with greater access to healthcare and resources.
