Polls indicate Labour's support has more than halved in Scotland, while the SNP has marginally improved its standing, and Reform has emerged as a potential second-place party, fragmenting the opposition vote. This shift positions the SNP to potentially secure a majority in Holyrood, an extraordinary achievement that would extend their devolved power for a third consecutive decade and bolster their argument for another independence referendum.
