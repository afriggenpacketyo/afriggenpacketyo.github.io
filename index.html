Understanding the Air Quality Index and Its Impact on Health

2025-06-03 11:26:12 EDT

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a tool used to measure air pollution levels and communicate associated health risks. It uses a color-coded system ranging from green (good) to maroon (hazardous) to indicate the severity of pollution. The AQI is calculated based on five major pollutants regulated by the Clean Air Act: ground-level ozone, particulate matter, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide. High AQI values indicate potential health impacts, ranging from headaches to severe cardiovascular issues, especially for sensitive groups like children, the elderly, and those with respiratory conditions.
