The conflict's disruption of the Strait of Hormuz has critically impacted a third of the world's fertilizer supply and significantly driven up red diesel prices. Farmers assert they cannot absorb these additional expenditures, leading to widespread expectations of rising food prices for consumers and profound concerns about potential business failures within the agricultural sector.
