Explainer: The Specific Criteria Required for an Official Famine Declaration

2025-07-25 13:46:28 EDT

Declaring a famine is a formal process guided by a specific, internationally agreed-upon system known as the Integrated Food Security Phase Classification (IPC). For a famine to be officially declared, three distinct criteria must be met simultaneously: at least 20% of households face an extreme lack of food, at least 30% of children suffer from acute malnutrition, and the daily death rate from non-trauma causes exceeds two per 10,000 adults. Even if these conditions are met, the final declaration is made by high-level government or UN officials. This high bar means many people can be dying of starvation long before a formal famine is announced.
