Their findings demonstrate that birds gradually shed their longer, non-avian dinosaur tails, progressively evolving the pygostyle—a specialized skeletal structure formed by the fusion of the final caudal vertebrae. This compact tail structure serves as a crucial anchor for tail feathers and muscles, vital for flight.
