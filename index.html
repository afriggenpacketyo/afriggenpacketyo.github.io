Scientific Explanation for Why Cities Experience More Intense Heat

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

This article explains the "urban heat island" effect, a phenomenon responsible for cities being significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas. Hard, dark surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's heat, which is then slowly released, elevating temperatures. This effect is exacerbated by waste heat from human activities and a lack of cooling vegetation and water, making urban populations more vulnerable to the impacts of climate change and increasingly frequent heatwaves.
