Despite NHL rules allowing up to 55 names per championship team, the placement and inclusion of non-team personnel have been widely ridiculed as self-serving and disrespectful to those who earned the trophy. Past instances, like an Oilers owner's father's name being covered with X's, highlight the controversy surrounding such decisions, though some owners' family members were designated as alternative governors. Critics argue that only those directly involved in earning the championship deserve to be immortalized on the Cup, rather than an entire family.
