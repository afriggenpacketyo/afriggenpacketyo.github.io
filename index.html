The bear was first spotted on Saturday and was seen in various public locations, prompting city officials to issue warnings and urge residents to stay indoors. The animal was eventually tranquilized and removed without any injuries. This incident is part of a broader trend of increasing bear encounters in Japan, with a record number of fatalities last year. Experts attribute this to factors such as an abundance of food and a growing bear population, leading to more frequent interactions with human settlements.
