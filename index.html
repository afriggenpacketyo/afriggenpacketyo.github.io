Explainer on the Mechanics and Consequences of a U.S. Government Shutdown

2025-10-23 10:51:54 EDT

A U.S. government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass funding legislation before the fiscal year begins on October 1, forcing federal agencies to cease all non-essential functions. Essential services, such as national security, law enforcement, and air traffic control, continue to operate, but employees in these roles work without pay until funding is restored. Programs with permanent funding, like Social Security and Medicare, continue to pay benefits, though administrative services may be curtailed. The economic impact can be substantial, as the 2018-2019 shutdown cost the economy an estimated $11 billion, with $3 billion in permanent losses.
