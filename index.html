Mammoth Brands, owner of brands like Harry's razors, Lume Deodorant, and Coterie diapers, is aiming to challenge legacy consumer packaged goods (CPG) giants by offering high-quality, disruptive products directly to consumers. The company has achieved significant revenue growth and is reportedly considering an initial public offering. Co-founders Andy Katz-Mayfield and Jeff Raider emphasize a customer-centric approach, focusing on innovation and value, contrasting with traditional CPG strategies. Mammoth's success in categories like personal care and baby products demonstrates a shift in consumer loyalty towards brands that offer differentiation and better-for-you ingredients, posing a growing threat to established players.

Dodgers honor LGBTQ+ pioneers Glenn Burke and Billy Bean with permanent exhibit

2026-06-07 08:00:00 EDT

The Los Angeles Dodgers have installed a permanent exhibit honoring former players Glenn Burke and Billy Bean, recognized as the first and second professional baseball players to come out as gay. This tribute, located in the Centerfield Plaza, signifies progress in LGBTQ+ inclusion within sports. Burke, known for initiating the "high five," felt he was traded due to his sexuality, a claim the team denies. Bean, who came out years after his career, also faced challenges in balancing his career and personal life. The exhibit acknowledges their contributions and the importance of visibility and acceptance in sports.
