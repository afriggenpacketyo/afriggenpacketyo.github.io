However, the council sent her proposal to committee, effectively delaying it past the June ballot deadline, following an outcry from labor leaders and community groups. Proponents of Measure ULA defend its $1 billion collection for homelessness and affordable housing, while real estate experts claim it has induced a "recession" in housing construction.
