The study highlights that teens who reported using cannabis had twice the risk of developing serious mental illnesses like bipolar and psychotic disorders, and a higher risk for depression and anxiety. Experts emphasize that these concerning findings challenge the widespread perception of cannabis as a safe product, particularly given the vulnerability and ongoing development of adolescent brains.
