Billions Globally Lack Access to Safe Drinking Water and Sanitation, WHO Reports

2025-08-28 13:40:15 EDT

A recent World Health Organization report highlights a critical global crisis, revealing that over 2 billion people worldwide lack access to safe drinking water, and an even greater number, 3.4 billion, cannot reliably utilize safe sanitation systems. Amaka Godfrey, executive director of WaterAid, provides a poignant personal account of her upbringing in Nigeria, underscoring the severe challenges of living without basic water access and the stark disparities between low-income and wealthier nations. Although substantial progress has been achieved since 2000, particularly in rural regions, rapid population growth in urban areas coupled with inadequate infrastructure continues to present significant hurdles. The article stresses the imperative for a quadrupling of investment to bridge these essential gaps and ensure fundamental human needs are universally met.
