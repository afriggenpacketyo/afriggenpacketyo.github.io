The remaining cost is attributed to state and federal taxes, with the federal tax fixed at 18.4 cents per gallon for highway maintenance. State taxes vary considerably, influencing regional price differences. Seasonal factors also play a role, as summer-blend gasoline, required from June to September, is more expensive to produce. Demand typically increases during warmer months, further contributing to price fluctuations. Gas stations themselves earn an average of 30 to 35 cents per gallon, with margins often shrinking during periods of actively surging oil prices. Understanding these various components provides a clearer picture of what drives gasoline prices at the pump.
