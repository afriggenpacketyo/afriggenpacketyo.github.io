Supreme Court Upholds Finality of Adoption Orders Amidst Parental Appeal

2026-04-22 10:23:36 EDT

The UK Supreme Court has rejected a woman's attempt to revoke the adoption of two sisters, emphasizing the principle that adoption is "final and permanent." The adoptive mother brought the case due to the wishes of the now-adult children, who had resumed contact with their birth mother. Child protection experts expressed concern that overturning adoption orders could destabilize the entire system and deter prospective adopters. The judges affirmed the state's power to decide adoption matters, citing 'parens patriae' powers to protect children from harm. They stressed that adoption should be irrevocable except in rare circumstances of flawed decisions, reinforcing the importance of legal certainty for all parties involved.

Justice Department Indicts Southern Poverty Law Center for Alleged Fraud

2026-04-22 10:18:33 EDT

The Justice Department has indicted the Southern Poverty Law Center (SPLC) on 11 fraud-related charges, alleging the organization paid members of extremist groups to gather information without disclosing this practice to donors or banks. The SPLC vehemently denies these accusations, asserting that its informant program saved lives and that it will vigorously defend its work.
