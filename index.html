Explainer: The Mechanics and Consequences of a U.S. Government Shutdown

2025-09-30 09:11:39 EDT

A U.S. government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass funding legislation before the fiscal year begins on October 1, forcing federal agencies to halt all non-essential functions. Under the Antideficiency Act, agencies cannot spend money without congressional authorization, leading to the furlough of non-essential employees. Essential services, such as national security, law enforcement, and air traffic control, continue to operate, but employees in these roles work without pay until funding is restored. While mandatory spending like Social Security continues, a shutdown can disrupt many public services, from national parks to federal data collection.
