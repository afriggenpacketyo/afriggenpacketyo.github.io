The primary cause is the prevalence of dark, hard surfaces like asphalt and concrete, which absorb and retain the sun's heat more than natural landscapes. This stored heat is released slowly, keeping temperatures elevated, especially overnight. The effect is compounded by waste heat from vehicles and buildings, making urban populations more vulnerable to the impacts of climate change and more intense heatwaves.
