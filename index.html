Analysts argue that while Pakistan can play a facilitating role in low-risk diplomacy like the U.S.-Iran talks, its ties with Afghanistan are marked by "open war," and relations with India remain fraught. The country's internal political climate, including the crackdown on Imran Khan's party, is also seen as mirroring its foreign policy approach. Experts suggest Pakistan's actions are driven by necessity and opportunity rather than a consistent ideological commitment to peace.
