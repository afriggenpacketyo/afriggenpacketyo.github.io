Earthquake off Cuba's Coast Felt in South Florida, No Tsunami Threat Expected

2026-06-08 15:12:18 EDT

A magnitude 6.1 earthquake originating off the northwest coast of Cuba was felt as light shaking in parts of Florida, including South Florida and Miami. The United States Geological Survey reported the epicenter was approximately 65 miles west-northwest of Mantua, Cuba. Residents across Florida, from Tampa to Miami, reported experiencing the tremors. Fortunately, the National Weather Service office in Miami confirmed there is no tsunami threat to South Florida, and there have been no immediate reports of damage or injuries resulting from the seismic event.
