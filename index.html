NBA superstar Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has reportedly taken legal action, sending a cease-and-desist letter demanding the destruction of "Unethical Hoops," a limited-edition board game that satirizes his reputation for frequently drawing foul calls. EU Unlocks €16.4 Billion in Funds for Hungary Following New PM's Reforms

2026-05-29 15:38:56 EDT

The European Union has unfrozen and unlocked a substantial €16.4 billion in funding for Hungary, providing a significant economic boost for the nation's new Prime Minister, Péter Magyar, who assumed office less than three weeks ago. European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen lauded the development, noting a "strong wind of change" and commending Magyar's administration for re-establishing trust with the EU. These funds had been previously withheld due to concerns over democratic backsliding and corruption during the tenure of the former Orbán government. The release, comprising €10 billion from a Covid-19 recovery fund and €6.4 billion from EU cohesion funds, is contingent upon Hungary implementing a series of "long-overdue reforms," including joining the European Public Prosecutor's Office and overhauling public procurement legislation, signaling a new era in EU-Hungary relations.

Blue Origin Rocket Explosion Threatens Delays for NASA's Artemis Moon Missions

2026-05-29 15:38:54 EDT

Blue Origin's unmanned New Glenn rocket was catastrophically destroyed in a launchpad explosion at Cape Canaveral, causing apparently severe damage to the company's sole launch pad and creating significant repercussions for the U.S. space community. This major incident threatens substantial delays for NASA's Artemis moon program, which is heavily reliant on both Blue Origin and SpaceX for launching critical moon landers next year. The explosion occurred during a hot-fire test, and while the root cause remains under investigation, it could also impact United Launch Alliance, which utilizes Blue Origin's BE-4 engines in its new Vulcan rocket. The mishap underscores the inherent dangers and complexities of developing heavy-lift launch capabilities, potentially pushing the Artemis III mission, planned for astronaut moon landings, into 2028 or even 2029.
