Mortgage Rates and Demand Remain Stagnant Despite Federal Reserve Speculation

2025-08-27 08:42:13 EDT

Mortgage demand and interest rates have remained largely unchanged for the second consecutive week, with the average contract rate for 30-year fixed mortgages hovering around 6.69%. Total mortgage application volume saw a slight decrease, though applications for home purchases showed a small gain, marking the strongest week for purchase demand in a month. An MBA economist noted that prospective buyers appear less sensitive to current rates, bolstered by more inventory and cooling home-price growth. Despite news of President Trump's attempted firing of Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook, which could imply future rate cuts, mortgage rates have not yet reacted, indicating ongoing market stagnation.
