Summer Solstice 2026 Marks Astronomical Summer with Longest Daylight Hours

2026-06-16 03:03:42 EDT

The summer solstice, marking the official start of astronomical summer in the northern hemisphere, will occur on Sunday, June 21, 2026, at 09:24 BST. This celestial event signifies the precise moment the hemisphere is tilted maximally towards the Sun, resulting in the longest period of daylight and the shortest night of the year. The solstice date varies slightly, falling on June 20, 21, or 22, due to Earth's orbital period being almost six hours longer than a calendar year, necessitating leap days. Earth's axial tilt is fundamental for distinct seasons; without it, daylight hours would remain nearly constant. The word "solstice" itself originates from Latin, meaning "sun stand still," referencing the apparent pause in the Sun's movement across the sky.
