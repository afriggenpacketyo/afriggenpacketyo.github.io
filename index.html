2025-04-20 05:00:16 EDT

While tongue-scraping is an ancient dental hygiene practice, some experts are warning about potential heart health risks. Tongue-scraping can cause microscopic cuts on the tongue, potentially introducing bacteria into the bloodstream and increasing the risk of endocarditis, an infection of the heart valves. Over-scraping can also lower nitric oxide levels, increasing the risk of high blood pressure, and should not be a substitute for brushing and flossing.
