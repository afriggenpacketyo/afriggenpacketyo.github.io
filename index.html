Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum stated that Mexico will only extradite officials, including Sinaloa Governor Ruben Rocha Moya, to the U.S. if "irrefutable evidence" of cartel links is provided. The U.S. Justice Department recently unveiled charges against Rocha Moya and nine others, accusing them of collaborating with the Sinaloa cartel. Sheinbaum questioned the validity of the U.S. evidence, including alleged bribe payments, and characterized the accusations as "political." She emphasized that Mexico protects no one but requires solid proof in accordance with Mexican law before complying with extradition requests. This stance highlights Mexico's assertion of national sovereignty in dealing with international drug trafficking allegations.
