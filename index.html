Understanding Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 Bankruptcy for Debt Relief

2026-02-24 11:51:47 EST

With rising debt and increasing delinquency rates, many Americans are considering bankruptcy, which offers distinct legal paths for debt relief. Chapter 7, often termed "liquidation bankruptcy," typically allows for the discharge of most qualifying unsecured debt, such as credit card balances and medical bills, within three to six months. However, it requires a means test based on income and may involve the liquidation of non-exempt property. Conversely, Chapter 13 bankruptcy restructures debt into a three-to-five-year repayment plan, making it suitable for individuals with assets they wish to protect, like a home, or those who do not qualify for Chapter 7 due to income. Both options trigger an immediate automatic stay, halting most collection actions. The choice depends on one's income, debt type, and assets, with other debt relief programs also serving as alternatives. (not investment advice)
