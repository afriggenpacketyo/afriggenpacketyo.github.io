The International Court of Justice (ICJ), based at the Peace Palace in The Hague, serves as the principal judicial organ of the United Nations. Comprised of 15 judges from different nations, its primary function is to settle legal disputes submitted to it by states. The court's jurisdiction applies when states have agreed to it, either through a blanket declaration, mutual consent for a specific case, or by being signatories to treaties like the Genocide Convention that designate the ICJ as the arbiter.
