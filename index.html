The 2026 World Cup, co-hosted by the United States, Mexico, and Canada, is poised to highlight the increasing geopolitical divisions within North America, contrasting with the initial vision of unity. Immigration restrictions, trade disputes, and rising U.S. nationalism under President Trump have created an "unusual geopolitical experiment." The renegotiation of the USMCA trade agreement looms during the tournament, with threats of its collapse potentially unraveling economic ties. Visa issues have affected participants and fans from various nations, particularly those with strained relations with the U.S. FIFA President Gianni Infantino's close ties with Trump and his downplaying of controversies have drawn criticism, while concerns over ticket prices and security measures persist, potentially dampening fan enthusiasm.
