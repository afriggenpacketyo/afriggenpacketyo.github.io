Documents released by the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) confirm that the MoD was aware of the necessity to remove such hidden data, a common feature in spreadsheet software, to prevent breaches. This data leak, which has necessitated an emergency resettlement scheme and is projected to cost around £850 million, also sparked internal ICO debates. Staff questioned why the regulator opted against fining the MoD, especially given a previous, smaller Afghan data breach, with the rationale cited as avoiding "additional cost to the taxpayer."
