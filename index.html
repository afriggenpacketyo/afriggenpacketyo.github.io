In the wake of Senator Lindsey Graham's death from an "aortic dissection," this article provides crucial information about the life-threatening medical condition. An aortic dissection occurs when a tear forms in the inner layer of the aorta, the body's main artery, allowing blood to surge between its layers and potentially impeding blood flow to vital organs. Symptoms typically appear abruptly, characterized by severe chest or back pain often described as a "ripping or stabbing sensation," demanding immediate emergency medical attention. More prevalent in men in their 60s and 70s, risk factors include smoking and high blood pressure. Treatment varies from medication to urgent surgery, contingent on the dissection's type and location.
