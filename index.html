Satellite to Measure Carbon Stored in Earth's Forests

2025-04-29 05:38:48 EDT

A new European Space Agency satellite, nicknamed "space brolly," is set to launch to accurately measure the amount of carbon stored in the world's rainforests. The Biomass satellite uses a long wavelength radar to penetrate through clouds and forest canopies, analyzing slices of trees to determine the amount of woody material, a proxy for carbon dioxide storage. This data will help scientists improve climate change models and track deforestation rates, offering a consistent view of forests regardless of cloud cover. The satellite's mission is expected to last five years, with initial maps produced within six months of a successful launch.
