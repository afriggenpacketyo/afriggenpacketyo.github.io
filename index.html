The CNBC Investing Club has added BWX Technologies to its "Bullpen" watchlist, a speculative addition that could benefit from the growing demand for power generation driven by the AI data center boom. BWX Technologies, which primarily serves the Navy with nuclear components, also has a burgeoning commercial business in civilian nuclear power plants, including small modular reactors (SMRs). This focus on nuclear power aligns with the increasing need for reliable, clean energy sources to support AI infrastructure. The club also highlighted advancements in AI chips from Alphabet and the potential growth in CPU spending, benefiting companies like Amazon, Alphabet, and Arm Holdings.

Georgia Congressman David Scott Dies at 80, Fifth Member of 119th Congress to Pass in Office

2026-04-22 15:01:13 EDT

U.S. Representative David Scott, a Democrat representing Georgia's 13th District for over two decades, has died at the age of 80. His death, confirmed a day after he cast his final vote in the House, makes him the fifth member of the current Congress to die in office. Scott, who was seeking his 13th term, was a significant figure, notably as the first African-American to chair the House Agriculture Committee. His passing slightly shifts the balance of power in the narrowly divided House, giving Republicans a marginal advantage. The cause of death has not yet been disclosed. Tributes from fellow politicians have highlighted his dedication to public service and his constituents.
