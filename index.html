Explainer: The Causes and Effects of Urban Heat Islands

2025-07-03 04:03:00 EDT

Urban heat islands are metropolitan areas that become significantly hotter than surrounding rural areas, a phenomenon that intensifies heatwaves for city dwellers. This effect is caused by heat-absorbing materials like concrete and asphalt, a lack of cooling vegetation, and heat trapped by tall buildings and pollution. Temperatures in these zones can be up to 15 degrees Celsius higher, especially after sunset, posing health risks to residents. Solutions include greening cities with more trees and parks, installing "cool roofs" that reflect sunlight, and using permeable pavements to enhance water evaporation and cooling.
