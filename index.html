2026-04-20 15:05:08 EDT

U.S. importers, including major retailers like Target and Walmart, are set to receive over $160 billion in tariff refunds following a Supreme Court decision, as the Trump administration opens a claims filing portal. While the system aims to facilitate these refunds, companies and analysts express cautious optimism about the speed of the process, anticipating potential bureaucratic hurdles. Trade lawyers warn of legal vulnerabilities and the possibility of a last-minute appeal. The portal, known as CAPE, will allow importers to submit declarations for tariffs paid under the now-invalidated emergency tariff authority. Wall Street analysts project substantial refunds for large retailers, though management teams suggest a cautious approach to using these funds, considering business needs, share repurchases, and debt repayment.

Oil prices surge as US and Iran exchange attacks on commercial ships near Strait of Hormuz

2026-04-20 15:04:40 EDT

Crude oil prices rose sharply on Monday amid escalating tensions between the U.S. and Iran, following attacks on commercial ships in the Strait of Hormuz. West Texas Intermediate futures climbed nearly 7% to $89.61 per barrel, and Brent crude futures advanced over 5% to $95.48. The U.S. Navy reportedly fired on an Iranian container ship, taking custody of it, after Iran attacked a tanker in the strait. President Trump characterized Iran's actions as a "total violation" of a truce and threatened retaliation. The situation has created significant volatility in energy markets, with analysts noting a rapid escalation of conflict. Peace negotiations between the U.S. and Iran remain uncertain, with Iran reportedly unwilling to attend talks due to the ongoing U.S. naval blockade.
