Understanding Credit Card Charge-Offs and the Seven-Year Reporting Rule

A credit card charge-off occurs when a lender deems a debt uncollectible, typically after 180 days of missed payments, and writes it off as a loss. However, the borrower still legally owes the money, and the account is often sold to a collection agency. According to the "seven-year rule," this negative mark remains on a consumer's credit report for seven years from the date of the first missed payment, significantly lowering their credit score. While the charge-off's impact lessens over time and it eventually falls off the report, consumers can proactively address the issue through debt relief options like negotiation or management programs to rebuild their credit sooner.
