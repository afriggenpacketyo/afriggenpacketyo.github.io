The International Court of Justice (ICJ), the principal judicial organ of the United Nations, is based in The Hague and settles legal disputes between states. Unlike the International Criminal Court which tries individuals, the ICJ's jurisdiction applies only when states consent to it, either through a blanket declaration, a specific agreement, or by being signatories to treaties like the Genocide Convention. While its rulings are legally binding, the court has no enforcement mechanism, relying on the cooperation of the involved parties, as seen in cases like Ukraine's lawsuit against Russia.
