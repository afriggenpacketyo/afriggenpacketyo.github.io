South Korea's IPO Market Faces Downturn Amid Chaebol Structure Challenges

2026-06-24 19:30:12 EDT

South Korea's Initial Public Offering (IPO) activity has significantly declined this year, with proceeds totaling around $700 million, a sharp drop from previous years. Efforts to boost corporate valuations are encountering obstacles related to governance reforms and the dominance of family-run conglomerates, known as Chaebols. These structures, along with high inheritance taxes, incentivize keeping valuations and free float low. While the Kospi index is performing strongly, the slowdown in IPOs indicates a shift towards a more selective, quality-driven market, impacting fundraising for venture capital funds.
