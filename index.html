China Maintains Benchmark Lending Rates Despite US Fed Rate Cut

2025-09-21 22:49:57 EDT

China's central bank has made the decision to keep its benchmark lending rates, specifically the one-year and five-year loan prime rates (LPRs), unchanged for the fourth consecutive month, holding them at 3.0% and 3.5% respectively. This notable decision comes despite a recent 25-basis-point interest rate cut by the U.S. Federal Reserve last week, highlighting a divergence in monetary policy. The move aligns closely with economists' expectations that Chinese authorities would hold off on implementing major stimulus measures, balancing a recent stock market rally with persistent signs of fatigue in the broader economy. Key economic indicators, such as retail sales slowing to 3.4% and industrial output growth easing to 5.2% in August, underscore sluggish domestic demand and a worsening real estate slump, influencing the central bank's cautious stance.
