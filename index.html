This technology, which includes the Israeli-developed "zero click" spyware Graphite, is being employed by Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) as part of its mission to disrupt fentanyl traffickers and foreign terrorist organizations. The confirmation comes in response to an earlier inquiry from House Democrats, who had expressed concern about the agency's potential use of such invasive tools. While ICE asserts that its use of the tool will comply with constitutional requirements, civil liberties advocates express significant alarm over the potential for abuse and the broader implications for digital privacy, especially given the agency's documented history of surveillance and its ongoing mass deportation campaign.
