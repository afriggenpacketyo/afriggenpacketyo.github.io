Jenkins argues that the British political system typically favors disciplined parties and coherent programs over individual populist "star performers," a model Farage's "one-man show" struggles to fit. The article points to Reform's internal instability, characterized by frequent leadership changes, and Farage's controversial associations with extreme US political figures. It concludes that his singular focus on issues like immigration and unconventional political style may hinder his party's electoral success in an anticipated multiparty hung parliament.
