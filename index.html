A recent scientific paper indicates that the worst-case climate scenario (RCP8.5/SSP5-8.5), which projected over 4 degrees Celsius warming, is now considered less probable due to faster-than-expected renewable energy adoption and global policy changes. While climate scientists emphasize this is positive news, they caution against complacency, as the world is still likely to temporarily exceed 1.5 degrees Celsius warming and could reach approximately 3 degrees Celsius by 2100. President Trump, however, capitalized on the revision to claim scientists were "wrong," fueling attacks on climate science. Meanwhile, a UN General Assembly resolution reinforcing climate change obligations faced notable opposition from the U.S. and major petrostates.
