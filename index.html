2025-08-22 17:54:12 EDT

The Rev Martin Smyth, a former Ulster Unionist Party (UUP) MP and the long-serving grand master of the Orange Lodge during the tumultuous period of the Troubles, has passed away at the age of 94. Remembered as a leader of "unwavering principle and determination," Smyth was a Presbyterian minister who entered Parliament in 1982 following the assassination of the Rev Robert Bradford MP. Although he maintained a less public profile compared to some contemporaries, he wielded significant influence behind the scenes, earning profound respect within the unionist community for his staunch and conservative views. His passing marks the end of an era for a key figure in Northern Irish political and cultural history.
