NATO's Purpose, Creation, and Collective Defense Explained

NATO, the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, was established in 1949 primarily to deter Soviet expansion in Europe after World War II, and also to prevent nationalist resurgence and foster political integration on the continent. Article 5 of the charter, the collective defense clause, states that an attack against one member is considered an attack against all, and has been invoked once, by the United States after the 9/11 attacks.
