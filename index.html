Remains of U.S. Soldier Lost in Korean War Identified After 75 Years

2026-04-15 10:03:44 EDT

The remains of U.S. Army Sergeant Celestino Chavez, who went missing during the Korean War in 1950, have been positively identified 75 years later. Sergeant Chavez was awarded a posthumous Silver Star for his bravery in defending his position near the Chosin Reservoir, refusing evacuation despite being wounded. He was declared missing after his convoy was ambushed. His identification was made possible through advanced anthropological and DNA analysis of remains returned from North Korea. Sergeant Chavez will receive a full military burial in his hometown, bringing closure to his family after decades of uncertainty. Approximately 7,500 U.S. troops remain unaccounted for from the Korean War.
