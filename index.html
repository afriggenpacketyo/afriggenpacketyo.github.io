Warsh, a former Fed chair nominee, has stakes in the Juggernaut Fund, managed by Duquesne Family Office, Stanley Druckenmiller's firm. This exception, established by the SEC in 2011, permits family offices to avoid registration as investment advisors if they manage assets solely for family clients, a category that includes senior employees. Lawyers suggest this structure is common for attracting and retaining talent in the family office sector. Warsh has stated he will divest these interests if confirmed as Fed chair.
