An appeals court has upheld Harvey Weinstein's 2022 rape and sexual assault conviction in California, but has ordered a resentencing by the trial judge. The decision came a day after prosecutors dropped the #MeToo-era case in New York against Weinstein. In California, Weinstein was convicted of one count of rape and two counts of sexual assault and sentenced to 16 years in prison. His defense argued that the trial judge unfairly limited testimony from a film festival head, impacting his defense strategy. Weinstein's lawyers intend to seek review in the California Supreme Court, believing significant legal errors occurred during the proceedings.
