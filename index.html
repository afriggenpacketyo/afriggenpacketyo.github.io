A U.S. government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass the necessary appropriations bills to fund federal agencies before the fiscal year begins on October 1. Without authorized funding, agencies must cease all non-essential functions, as mandated by the Antideficiency Act. Essential employees, such as active-duty military, air traffic controllers, and many law enforcement officers, must continue to work but do not get paid until the shutdown ends. Non-essential workers are furloughed, though a 2019 law now guarantees them back pay. These shutdowns can disrupt a wide range of public services and have historically cost taxpayers billions of dollars.
