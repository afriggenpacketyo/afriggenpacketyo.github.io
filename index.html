Amanda Knox, who was exonerated after spending four years in Italian prison for a murder she didn't commit, is bringing her story to Hulu in an eight-part series titled 'The Twisted Tale of Amanda Knox,' serving as an executive producer. She has notably teamed up with Monica Lewinsky, who also experienced intense public vilification. The series dramatizes the complex events surrounding the murder of Meredith Kercher, showing the profound impact on Knox, her family, and others involved, including her ex-boyfriend Raffaele Sollecito. Knox aims to reclaim her narrative and convince those who still doubt her innocence, having previously written memoirs and spoken publicly. Their collaboration highlights shared experiences of being interrogated and publicly shamed at a young age, with both women now advocating for reclaiming one's story.

Experts Recommend Adding Pine Nuts and Goat's Milk to Mandatory Food Allergy Labels

2025-08-20 07:58:27 EDT

Experts are recommending that foods like pine nuts and goat's milk be added to mandatory food allergy warning labels in the UK and EU, citing frequently occurring and severe allergic reactions. An article in the journal Clinical & Experimental Allergy, based on a review of nearly 3,000 anaphylaxis cases reported in Europe, identified these and other emerging allergens such as buckwheat, peas, and lentils. The Food Standards Agency acknowledges the significant number of foods that can cause allergies and supports providing clear information to consumers. This proactive move aims to enhance consumer safety and provide clearer labeling, especially as new ingredients like pea protein become increasingly prevalent and sometimes unexpectedly found in various food products.
