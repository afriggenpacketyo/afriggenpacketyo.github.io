NPH involves excess cerebrospinal fluid accumulation in the brain, causing difficulty walking, bladder control issues, and memory problems. Diagnosis involves CT scans or MRIs to show enlarged brain ventricles, confirmed by a spinal tap that temporarily alleviates symptoms. Treatment typically involves a surgery called ventricular shunting, with physical therapy potentially improving gait stability after surgery.
