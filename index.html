Poland has significantly bolstered its naval capabilities by signing a substantial €4.2 billion ($4.8 billion) arms deal with Swedish aerospace and defense contractor Saab, ordering three new A26, or Blekinge-class, submarines. These advanced vessels, specifically designed for stealth, intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance in the Baltic Sea, are scheduled for delivery between 2030 and 2038. As part of the agreement, Saab will also establish maintenance, repair, and overhaul facilities in Poland, operated in collaboration with Polish companies, to enhance national defense autonomy. To bridge the gap until the new submarines arrive, Sweden will provide Poland with the use of its HMS Södermanland submarine. This strategic acquisition reflects European NATO member states' intensified efforts to strengthen their defenses amidst Russia's ongoing military aggression against Ukraine and growing concerns over the US commitment to their security under President Donald Trump.
