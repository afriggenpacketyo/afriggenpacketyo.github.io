2026-01-12 01:13:00 EST

Meta has deactivated over 544,000 social media accounts belonging to users believed to be under 16, in compliance with Australia's new law banning under-age accounts. The law, effective December 10, requires platforms like Meta, TikTok, and YouTube to remove accounts of individuals younger than 16. Meta expressed commitment to compliance but raised concerns about the challenges of accurately verifying age online without industry-wide standards. The company advocates for better age verification measures, including app store requirements for parental approval, to ensure consistent protections. Fines for non-compliance can reach up to A$49.5 million. The Australian ban has garnered international attention, with other countries, including Germany, considering similar legislation. The effectiveness and implications of such blanket bans are subjects of ongoing discussion.

California Attorney General Rob Bonta Declines Governor Run, Prioritizing Legal Battles Against Trump Administration

2026-01-12 00:47:19 EST

California Attorney General Rob Bonta announced he will not run for governor, citing his commitment to serving as a "bulwark" against the Trump administration's policies. Bonta believes his role as Attorney General provides a more powerful platform to challenge federal actions impacting Californians, particularly in areas like immigration and welfare funding. He has initiated over 50 lawsuits against the Trump administration. Bonta's decision comes amid an open race for California governor, with other candidates facing scrutiny. His focus remains on his current position, aiming to be a significant check on perceived abuses of power. This stance reflects a strategic choice to prioritize legal advocacy over a gubernatorial bid at this juncture. Bonta's previous consideration for the governorship indicates a dynamic political landscape.
