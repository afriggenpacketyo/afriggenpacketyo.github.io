David Parrish, an ultra-marathon champion and former Royal Marine, has died while attempting to set a record on the Cape Wrath Trail in the Scottish Highlands. Parrish, 35, was found in a remote mountainous area on Saturday night. His fiancée, Jade Lau, shared that they were two weeks away from their wedding, highlighting the tragic timing of his death. Parrish was running to fundraise for Scottish Mountain Rescue in memory of a friend. He was an experienced runner, having won the Cape Wrath Trail race in 2023. The trail is known for its extreme difficulty. Support from friends and family has been vital for Lau during this difficult time.

House Passes Short-Term Extension for Controversial FISA Surveillance Law

2026-04-17 11:36:00 EDT

The House has approved a 10-day extension for Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA), a controversial warrantless surveillance program, after Republican divisions prevented a longer renewal. President Trump had advocated for a clean, longer extension, but a procedural vote failed due to GOP defections. Section 702 allows U.S. agencies to collect foreign communications without a warrant, potentially sweeping up Americans' data when they communicate with foreigners abroad. While proponents argue it's crucial for national security, opponents demand reforms, including warrant requirements for Americans' communications. The Senate also passed a 10-day extension, with further negotiations expected.
