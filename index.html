Explanation of a U.S. Government Shutdown and Its Potential Consequences

2025-09-29 18:55:44 EDT

A U.S. government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass funding legislation for federal agencies before the fiscal year begins on October 1. This constitutional requirement forces all non-essential government functions to cease until a funding agreement is reached. Essential employees, such as active-duty military and air traffic controllers, must continue working without immediate pay, while non-essential workers are furloughed. Programs like Social Security continue, but services may be delayed, and past shutdowns have cost taxpayers billions in lost productivity and back pay.
