Legendary investor Charlie Munger, known for his sharp insights, once stated that "only a total idiot" would be surprised to lose to competitors who demonstrate a superior work ethic and commitment to continuous learning. He cited South Korea's rise in the auto industry as an example, where workers dedicated 84 hours a week for over a decade, complemented by intensive after-school tutoring for their children. Munger consistently championed the compounding power of discipline and consistent effort, urging individuals to "go to bed smarter than when you woke up." He believed success belonged to "learning machines" like Warren Buffett, who exemplify wide reading and clear thinking. (not investment advice)
