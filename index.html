Tesla is slated to release its fourth-quarter earnings for 2025 after the market closes, with analysts forecasting earnings per share of 45 cents and revenue around $24.79 billion. The company has experienced revenue deceleration in recent quarters, largely attributed to intense global competition, especially from China's BYD. Projections suggest a 3.6% year-over-year revenue decline, potentially marking Tesla's first annual drop on record. CEO Elon Musk has attempted to shift investor focus towards emerging ventures such as the Robotaxi business and Optimus humanoid robots, which are yet to be commercialized. The earnings call will also likely address capital expenditures for self-driving and robotics chip technology, as Tesla balances current market pressures with its long-term technological ambitions.
