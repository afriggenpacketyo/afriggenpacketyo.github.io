2025-12-11 16:55:04 EST

A significant gas explosion occurred in Hayward, California, after a construction crew damaged an underground gas line, injuring at least six people. The explosion, which happened around 9:30 a.m. local time, caused extensive damage to three buildings and affected a nearby structure. Firefighters responded promptly to the three-alarm blaze. Pacific Gas and Electric (PG&E) confirmed that a third-party construction outfit was working on expanding bike lanes when the line was ruptured. Residents described the event as shaking their homes and appearing like a "war video." The incident highlights the risks associated with construction work near critical infrastructure.

Federal Reserve Reappoints 11 Regional Bank Presidents

2025-12-11 16:53:31 EST

The Federal Reserve has unanimously reappointed 11 of its 12 regional bank presidents, ending a period of speculation about potential changes. The decision, approved by the seven governors, will see the current leadership of the regional banks continue their five-year terms starting March 1, 2026. The only exception is the Atlanta Fed president, who is retiring. This move comes despite criticism of the Fed from President Donald Trump and concerns raised by Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent about the influence of New York-based officials. The unanimous vote, including a recent Trump appointee, suggests a consensus on maintaining the current leadership structure. The regional presidents play a role in the Federal Open Market Committee's monetary policy decisions.

Senator Warren Criticizes Trump's Decision to Allow Nvidia Chip Sales to China

2025-12-11 16:51:07 EST

Senator Elizabeth Warren has strongly criticized President Donald Trump's decision to allow Nvidia to sell advanced AI chips to China, calling it a "sell out" of American national security. Warren reiterated her call for Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang and Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick to testify before Congress. The announcement permits Nvidia to sell chips to "approved customers" in China, with the U.S. receiving a 25% revenue cut. Critics argue this poses a serious threat to U.S. technological leadership. Nvidia defended the sales, stating they require U.S. government licenses and that blocking them would harm American economic and national security by allowing foreign competitors to gain market share. Warren urged Congress to pass legislation imposing new chip export restrictions.
