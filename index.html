2026-03-17 22:22:30 EDT

South Korea's military has initiated an immediate halt to all shooting drills involving small firearms, including rifles and handguns, after a young girl sustained an injury from a suspected stray bullet at a playground in Daegu city. The elementary schoolgirl, struck near the neck, has since been released from the hospital. The incident took place approximately 1.5 kilometers from a military shooting range where a live-fire exercise was actively in progress. Authorities are currently conducting a thorough investigation to determine if her injury was directly attributable to the military drill. While such occurrences are infrequent, there have been past instances of civilian casualties from live-fire exercises, raising ongoing concerns regarding military safety protocols, particularly in proximity to civilian areas.
