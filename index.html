The 60-day deadline under the War Powers Resolution for President Donald Trump to seek Congressional authorization for military action in the Middle East falls on Friday. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth asserted that a current ceasefire has paused this clock, a claim met with skepticism by some lawmakers who express constitutional concerns. The White House has indicated ongoing "active conversations with the Hill" but has not confirmed plans for a formal authorization request. Congress, currently in recess, has two options: push for an end to hostilities via a War Powers Resolution or pass an Authorization for Use of Military Force (AUMF). Democrats have repeatedly attempted the former without success.
