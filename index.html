Exploring the Complex Origins and Evolution of Daylight Saving Time

2026-03-06 18:35:19 EST

Daylight Saving Time, a practice that causes clocks to "spring forward" an hour, has a complex and often misunderstood history. While frequently attributed to farmers, they did not necessarily support its adoption in the early 20th century. Various figures, including Benjamin Franklin and George Hudson, have been credited with the idea, but British builder William Willet's 1907 pamphlet, "The Waste of Daylight," is a key influence. The practice gained widespread adoption during World War I to conserve fuel, with Germany being the first to observe it in 1916. The U.S. implemented it in 1918, repealed it in 1919, and then established the current system with the Uniform Time Act of 1966.
