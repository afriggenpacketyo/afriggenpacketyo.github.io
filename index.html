Major labor unions are divided over the strategy to end the government shutdown, with the American Federation of Government Employees (AFGE) supporting a Republican funding bill, while other top labor groups remain aligned with Democrats. Many unions, including SEIU 32BJ, the AFL-CIO, and the National Education Association, continue to back the Democratic push for an extension of Obamacare subsidies, viewing President Trump as an adversary of workers. They argue against sacrificing affordable healthcare for millions to reopen the government, despite federal workers missing paychecks and facing layoffs. The article highlights this split, noting that while AFGE seeks a "clean continuing resolution," other large unions emphasize fighting for workers' broader interests and believe Democrats must hold the line against Trump's agenda.

Canadian PM Mark Carney Apologizes to Trump Over Anti-Tariff Ad

2025-11-01 13:34:46 EDT

Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney apologized to President Trump after an anti-tariff ad, run by Ontario's premier, infuriated Trump and led him to end trade talks with Canada. The ad, which cited former President Ronald Reagan's anti-tariff stance, prompted Trump to threaten additional 10% tariffs on Canadian goods. Carney confirmed he advised Ontario Premier Doug Ford not to run the ad, emphasizing the federal government's responsibility for U.S. relations, but Ford's spokesperson did not immediately respond. The article notes that while the ad was pulled, it was shown during the World Series. The Senate has since approved a symbolic resolution to block Trump's tariffs, highlighting ongoing trade tensions that are taking a toll on sectors like steel, aluminum, auto, and lumber.
