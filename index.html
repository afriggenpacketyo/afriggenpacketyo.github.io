Amidst escalating global tensions, notably exacerbated by the US-Israeli bombardment of Iran, fears of nuclear war are reaching an alarming "fever pitch," as articulated by Judith Levine. Experts caution that even a limited nuclear conflict could lead to billions of casualties and extensive environmental contamination. The article points to several contributing factors, including reports of Russia providing intelligence to Iran, China's increasing defense spending, and Europe's more aggressive stance on nuclear weapons. President Trump's past decisions, such as withdrawing from the Iran nuclear deal and allowing the New Start treaty to lapse, are highlighted as significant drivers of this heightened risk. The public discourse, disturbingly, appears to have shifted from denial to an unsettling acceptance of potential nuclear annihilation, with media outlets even publishing survival guides.
