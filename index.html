A U.S. Air Force Thunderbirds F-16 fighter jet crashed in the desert town of Trona, California, during a training mission, with the pilot safely ejecting from the aircraft and reported to be in stable condition. The F-16C Fighting Falcon, assigned to the aerial acrobatic demonstration team based at Nellis Air Force Base in Nevada, crashed around 10:45 a.m., and the cause is currently under investigation. A motorist witnessed an explosion that sent up a thick plume of black smoke, followed by a large response from emergency vehicles. The incident occurred approximately 20 miles from the Naval Air Weapons Station China Lake, a significant Navy base for weapons development and testing. Startup Istari Digital Uses AI to Develop Moon Dust Battery for Blue Origin

2025-12-03 16:34:50 EST

Startup Istari Digital has developed a groundbreaking AI-designed device for Blue Origin that converts moon dust into an energy source, effectively creating a "moon dust battery." Unveiled at Amazon's re:Invent 2025 conference, this critical technology aims to solve a major challenge for lunar missions: the two-week lunar night, during which extreme temperature drops cripple spacecraft hardware without a strong, long-lasting power source. Istari CEO Will Roper, a former assistant Air Force secretary, explained that their AI platform utilizes "guardrails" or a "fence around the playground" to prevent "hallucinations" while generating designs that meticulously meet all engineering requirements. This innovation represents a significant leap for space exploration and highlights AI's transformative role in advanced engineering and overcoming extraterrestrial environmental challenges.
