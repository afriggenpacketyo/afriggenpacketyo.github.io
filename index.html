DPAA investigators, with specialized expert assistance, meticulously matched Duquesne's smiling photograph, particularly focusing on his prominent upper canine tooth and its unique shadow, to the recovered skull. This remarkable case serves as a clear example of how refined older forensic methods, when thoughtfully applied alongside historical records, can ultimately provide clarity and answers decades later, offering crucial closure to the families of fallen soldiers when other identification methods are exhausted.
