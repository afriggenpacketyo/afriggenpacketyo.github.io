A humpback whale that was freed earlier in the week after being stranded for days off Germany's Baltic coast has become stuck again on a sandbank in the Bay of Wismar. Marine conservationists had warned that the whale, likely a young male on migration, might re-encounter trouble due to its movement toward shallow waters. Experts had previously observed the whale in the area, including being freed from a fishing net. The repeated stranding raises concerns about the whale's health or navigational issues, possibly related to underwater noise or its unfamiliarity with the Baltic Sea environment. Authorities are continuing efforts to assist the animal.

Arrest Made After Major Fire Engulfs Historic Leek Mill

2026-03-28 10:52:09 EDT

An 18-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of arson with recklessness following a major fire at The Big Mill, a historic Grade II listed building in Leek, Staffordshire. The fire service responded with six crews at its peak, and part of the structurally unsound building may need demolition. Nearby homes were evacuated as a precaution due to smoke. The fire service confirmed no injuries were reported, but the incident caused significant damage to the building, which dates back to 1860. Local officials had previously raised concerns about safety hazards and antisocial behavior at the derelict site. The arrest marks a significant development in the investigation into the cause of the blaze.
