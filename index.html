Wildfires are historically uncommon in this ecosystem, raising significant concerns about the native plants' evolutionary adaptations and their ability to recover from such a severe burn. The fire, believed to be human-caused by a marooned sailor, underscores the fragility of these unique ecosystems, although a comprehensive seedbank at the Santa Barbara Botanic Garden offers some hope for potential restoration efforts if needed.
