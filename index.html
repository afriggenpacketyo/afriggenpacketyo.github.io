An aortic dissection involves a tear in the inner layer of the aorta, the body's main artery, allowing blood to flow between its layers, which can impede blood supply to organs and potentially lead to rupture. Symptoms typically manifest suddenly, characterized by severe chest or back pain often described as a ripping or stabbing sensation. Rapid diagnosis and emergency medical intervention, including surgery, are paramount for survival and improved outcomes, depending on the dissection's specific location and any associated complications. While rare, the condition is more prevalent in men in their 60s and 70s, with high blood pressure and smoking identified as key preventable risk factors.
