The rise of squishy toys like NeeDoh reflects a growing demand for sensory and fidget play

2026-06-11 07:45:00 EDT

The immense popularity of squishy toys, such as NeeDoh, signifies a broader trend towards sensory and fidget items among children. These toys, designed for tactile engagement, offer stress relief and can aid concentration for some individuals, particularly those with ADHD or autism. Experts note that while fidget toys have always existed, their explicit marketing and widespread adoption have surged in recent years, coinciding with increased awareness of social-emotional learning. The appeal of NeeDoh and similar items lies in their ability to provide a different sensory experience compared to digital devices, fostering hand-eye coordination and fine motor skills. This trend also taps into the growing interest in ASMR and the desire for hands-on, engaging play.
