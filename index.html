The Aeronautical Reason Why Aircraft Carrier Islands Are on the Starboard Side

2025-10-06 12:00:00 EDT

The superstructure, or "island," on an aircraft carrier is almost always located on the starboard (right) side of the flight deck, a design choice rooted in early aviation physics. Pilots on the first carriers requested a visual reference on the right side to help judge landings. This placement was reinforced by the natural left-turning tendency of early propeller-driven aircraft, caused by aerodynamic forces like P-factor and spiraling slipstream from clockwise-rotating propellers. Placing the island on the right gave pilots more room to correct their aircraft's natural veer to the left during critical takeoff and landing phases.
