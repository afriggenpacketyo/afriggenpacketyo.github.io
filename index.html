Understanding the Ownership of a Home with a Reverse Mortgage

2025-10-14 11:24:01 EDT

A common question among seniors considering a reverse mortgage is who retains ownership of the house. With this type of loan, the homeowner keeps the legal title and ownership of the property, while the lender places a lien on it to secure the loan balance. The loan, plus interest, typically becomes due when the homeowner sells the home, moves out, or passes away. At that point, the homeowner's heirs can choose to repay the loan to keep the house or sell it to settle the debt, keeping any remaining equity. This financial tool allows older homeowners to access their home equity without making monthly payments.
