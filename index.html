European policymakers reacted with a blend of relief and caution to US Secretary of State Marco Rubio's conciliatory speech at the Munich Security Conference, where he underscored the intertwined destinies of the US and Europe. German Foreign Minister Johann Wadephul welcomed Rubio's assurance of continued partnership, acknowledging some persistent disagreements on issues like migration and digital services. However, other officials, such as France's Minister Delegate for Europe Benjamin Haddad, urged Europe to prioritize self-reliance and rearmament rather than being swayed by "love words" from the US. The article highlights the complex and evolving transatlantic relationship, marked by European efforts to achieve greater independence amidst varying US diplomatic approaches.
