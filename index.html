2026-03-08 00:18:54 EST

Daylight saving time for 2026 is set to begin on Sunday, March 8, at 2 a.m. local time, necessitating that most Americans advance their clocks by one hour, resulting in a temporary loss of an hour of sleep. This yearly ritual effectively transfers an hour of daylight from the morning to the evening, a practice first adopted in the U.S. in 1918 to conserve fuel during World War I. Although historical analyses, including one by the Department of Transportation, have indicated minimal benefits in energy conservation and traffic safety, the tradition persists. Only Hawaii and Arizona, alongside several U.S. territories, do not observe this time change, with the rest of the nation returning to standard time in early November.
