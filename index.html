Iranian authorities orchestrated large-scale public weddings in Tehran for hundreds of couples who have reportedly volunteered for a state-sponsored "self-sacrifice" initiative amidst the ongoing conflict with the U.S. and Israel. These ceremonies, televised nationally from major capital squares, were explicitly designed to bolster wartime morale, with participants pledging to risk their lives, for instance, by forming human chains outside power stations. The events were part of a broader governmental campaign to project national unity and readiness, also featuring military personnel offering basic firearms tutorials. Couples, arriving in military jeeps, were married on stages adorned with balloons and an image of the Supreme Leader, expressing personal happiness while acknowledging the nation's wartime circumstances.

Publication of Second Batch of Mandelson Files Delayed Until After Recess

2026-05-19 09:43:54 EDT

The release of the second tranche of documents concerning Lord Mandelson's controversial appointment as the UK's ambassador to the US has been postponed until after Parliament's upcoming recess. Chief Secretary to the Prime Minister Darren Jones stated the delay is intended to provide Members of Parliament "sufficient time to review the material." This decision comes after the Intelligence and Security Committee (ISC) chairman raised concerns about certain files being withheld and redacted on new grounds, particularly sensitive personal data. Jones refuted accusations of a government cover-up, asserting that the redaction approach aligns with established precedents. However, MPs, including Sir Jeremy Wright, insist that any alterations to the humble address requiring publication must receive parliamentary consent.
