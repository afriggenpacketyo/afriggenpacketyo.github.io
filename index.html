2025-07-23 17:27:00 EDT

The ubiquitous word "OK" is a uniquely American invention that originated as a joke in 19th-century Boston before being popularized by a presidential campaign. Most scholars trace its first appearance to a Boston newspaper in 1839 as a playful abbreviation for "oll korrect," a deliberate misspelling of "all correct." The term gained widespread prominence during President Martin Van Buren's 1840 reelection campaign, where his supporters formed "O.K. Clubs," referencing his nickname "Old Kinderhook." Its simplicity and versatility allowed it to flourish in the technological age, spreading globally with American soldiers during the world wars and becoming one of the most recognized words on the planet.
