Understanding the Timeline from Credit Card Charge-Off to Collections

This article explains the process and timeline for when a delinquent credit card account becomes a "charge-off" and is subsequently sent to collections. Typically, a creditor will charge off an account after 180 days of non-payment, classifying the debt as unlikely to be collected for accounting purposes, though the borrower's obligation remains. Following the charge-off, the original creditor may attempt internal collection for a few weeks to several months before selling the debt to a third-party agency, often within 30 to 90 days. The article outlines consumer options for dealing with a charged-off account, such as payment in full, negotiating a payment plan, debt settlement, or, in severe cases, bankruptcy.
