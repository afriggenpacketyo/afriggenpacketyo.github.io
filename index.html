UK Health Secretary Wes Streeting "categorically" denied changing a pay deal for resident doctors, despite accusations from the British Medical Association (BMA) that a three-year deal with reduced investment was imposed at the very last minute. Streeting stated resident doctors received a 28.9% pay rise within the first weeks of the current Labour government and claimed the BMA committee "point blank refused" further negotiations, while the BMA insisted it remains open and willing to meet. The dispute continues amid ongoing industrial action over pay and jobs, with the Health Secretary criticizing the BMA's latest walkout and its financial and operational impact on bringing forward 1,000 of 4,500 extra training places.
