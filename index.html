Explainer: Why Cities Experience Higher Temperatures During Heatwaves

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

The "urban heat island" effect is a phenomenon that causes cities to be significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas, particularly during heatwaves. This occurs because dark, hard surfaces like roads and buildings absorb and retain the sun's energy, while natural landscapes with vegetation and water provide cooling through evaporation. This stored heat is released slowly, raising urban temperatures and making city populations more vulnerable to the health risks of extreme heat, a problem exacerbated by climate change and growing urban populations.
