Explaining the Mechanics and Consequences of a U.S. Government Shutdown

2025-10-31 11:55:45 EDT

A government shutdown occurs when Congress fails to pass the necessary appropriations bills to fund federal agencies before the fiscal year begins on October 1. This legal requirement, rooted in the Antideficiency Act, forces all non-essential government functions to cease until funding is restored. During a shutdown, essential employees like military personnel and air traffic controllers must work without immediate pay, while non-essential workers are furloughed, though a 2019 law now guarantees them back pay. While entitlement programs like Social Security continue, other services, including national parks and customer service functions at federal agencies, face significant disruptions or closures.
