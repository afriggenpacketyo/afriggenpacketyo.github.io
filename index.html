Explainer: Why Urban Areas Experience Higher Temperatures During Heatwaves

2025-06-28 21:23:14 EDT

Cities often experience significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to a phenomenon known as the "urban heat island effect." This effect is caused by the prevalence of dark, hard surfaces like asphalt roads and buildings, which absorb and retain the sun's heat more than natural landscapes. This stored heat is released slowly, keeping urban environments warmer, especially overnight. The lack of vegetation and water sources, which provide natural cooling in the countryside, exacerbates the issue. As climate change makes heatwaves more frequent and intense, urban populations are disproportionately vulnerable to their dangerous effects.
