While focusing on preventing AI-enabled biological weapons, cyberattacks, or rogue systems, it seeks to adapt as AI evolves and applies to companies generating over $500 million in revenue. This legislation, supported by Anthropic but opposed by some industry groups citing compliance costs, could set a precedent for federal AI safety laws, despite ongoing debates about risk prioritization.
