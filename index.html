Crude oil prices approached $100 per barrel on Thursday, as tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz remains significantly restricted due to ongoing U.S. naval blockades and Iran's threats of retaliation. While President Trump indicated potential weekend negotiations between the U.S. and Iran, no official date has been set. A two-week ceasefire between the nations is set to expire next week, with no clear pathway to fully reopen the vital shipping lane. European and Gulf Arab leaders suggest a U.S.-Iran deal could take up to six months to negotiate, highlighting the persistent uncertainty and its impact on global energy markets.
