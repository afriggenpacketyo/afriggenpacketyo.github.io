He characterized the situation as "probably the worst pressure the NHS has faced since Covid," with over 2,660 flu cases being recorded daily in English hospitals. A five-day strike by resident doctors is scheduled to commence on December 17, although the British Medical Association is currently polling its members on the possibility of calling off the walkout. Officials have noted that this winter's flu, identified as a mutated H3N2 strain, began circulating unusually early, leading to reduced population immunity and consequently higher hospitalization rates, particularly among the elderly and young children.
