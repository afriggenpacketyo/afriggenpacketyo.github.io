The White House has confirmed that President Trump and his team are discussing "a range of options" to acquire Greenland, including the use of the US military, deeming it a "national security priority." This statement follows European leaders' joint declaration supporting Denmark, which maintains that Greenland belongs to its people and is not for sale. Danish Prime Minister Mette Frederiksen warned that any US attack would jeopardize NATO, while Secretary of State Marco Rubio reportedly suggested buying the island. This ongoing diplomatic crisis highlights severe international tensions, particularly concerning territorial integrity and the stability of transatlantic alliances, as Greenlanders express fear and opposition to the US claims over their land.
