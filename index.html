These reforms include creating and publicly maintaining a comprehensive, up-to-date list of all accused clergy, detailing allegations and investigation outcomes, and banning confidentiality agreements that silence survivors. Survivors' attorneys emphasized that this agreement, following similar large settlements in California, represents a crucial step towards accountability, shifting the burden of shame onto the institution and empowering survivors.
