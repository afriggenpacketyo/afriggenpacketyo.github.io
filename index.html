This concept, originated by Karl August Wittfogel, posited that the extensive construction and administration of large-scale water projects in Eastern societies inherently led to powerful, centralized, and autocratic political systems distinct from the West. However, new studies counter this, suggesting that prehistoric communities across various Chinese regions engaged in collaborative, family or clan-based projects for water storage, irrigation, and flood control. This research offers a revised perspective on the evolution of early Chinese societies and their governance structures.
