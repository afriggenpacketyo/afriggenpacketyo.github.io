2026-06-11 05:00:00 EDT

The word 'algorithm,' now often associated with artificial intelligence and its potential dangers, has a surprisingly ancient origin. It derives from the Latinized name of the 9th-century Persian mathematician Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi. Al-Khwarizmi's influential work introduced algorithmic methods and popularized Hindu-Arabic numerals in the West, laying the groundwork for algebra. His contributions, translated into Latin centuries later, eventually evolved into the modern term 'algorithm.' While the word's modern connotation can be concerning, its historical roots highlight its fundamental role as a set of instructions for solving problems, a concept that has enabled complex technologies for centuries.
