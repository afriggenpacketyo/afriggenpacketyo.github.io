New data on the President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR) has been released, sparking divergent interpretations. Government officials highlight positive figures, particularly an increase in pregnant and breastfeeding women starting PrEP and a slight drop in people on HIV treatment, framing it as a sign of program resilience. However, HIV experts and activists express grave concerns, labeling the situation a 'five-alarm fire' due to perceived drops in HIV testing, prevention, and support services. They argue the data obscures the damage caused by program interruptions and a decline in healthcare workers, leading to a significant decrease in new diagnoses and treatment initiation, which could exacerbate the spread of HIV.

Lebanon-Israel talks resume in Washington amid fragile Hezbollah ceasefire

2026-04-23 06:42:59 EDT

Israeli and Lebanese ambassadors are meeting in Washington for a second round of direct negotiations, focusing on extending the fragile ceasefire that has halted fighting between Israel and the Iran-backed Hezbollah militia. These talks, the first direct negotiations since 1993, aim to shape future discussions on normalizing ties. Lebanon seeks to end Israeli attacks and occupation, while Israel demands Hezbollah disarm. The ceasefire is tentative, with both sides retaining the right to respond to perceived threats. The negotiations occur against a backdrop of ongoing regional conflict and internal pressures within Lebanon, where Hezbollah wields significant influence, complicating efforts towards lasting peace and stability.
